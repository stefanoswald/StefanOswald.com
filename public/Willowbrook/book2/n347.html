Night 347

What the House Hears at Night

Month 12: Bedtime Itself

When everyone sleeps,

the house stays half awake.

Listen to its night sounds:

Tick... tick... (The clock,

counting for everybody.)

Crick. (A beam, settling.

Houses stretch at night too.)

Mmmmm. (The fridge,

humming to the leftovers.)

Plip... plip... (Rain, starting.

The roof will handle it.

Roofs love the night shift.)

The house hears the family too:

Papa's snore. (Rrr-shhh.)

Mama's turning page.

One small sleeper's steady breath,

in... out... in... out...

That's the sound the house

listens for most.

It built its whole quiet around it.

Sleep on, family.

The house has the watch tonight.

Houses always do.

It's what they're for.
