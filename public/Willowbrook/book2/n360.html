Night 360

The Party of Two Candles

Month 12: Bedtime Itself

They all came.

Of course they all came.

Grandpa Joe, hat in hand.

Rosa, cake box held like treasure.

Lily, running hug! "WIWI!"

The aunts, needles finished:

a bear-sized sweater for Bobby.

(He wears it. With dignity.)

The colleagues. The caregiver.

The whole little list, alive

in one living room.

Games. Giggles. The zoomies, shared.

And then...

the lights dim.

The cake arrives. Gold star on top.

TWO candles, dancing.

The song rises, wrong notes

defended by the aunts' harmony...

"...happy birthday, dear Olivia..."

She stares at the two flames.

Last year, Mama blew for her.

This year...

big breath...

SHE blows...

WHOO...

OUT! Both! Herself!

The room erupts.

Two years old.

By her own breath.

The wish? She won't say.

But she looked at everybody first.

Everybody. Slowly. Around the room.

That was the wish.

Granted, said the room.

Granted, granted, granted.
