Night 300

Three Hundred Nights of Both

Month 10: Opposites

Three hundred nights,

this book and this girl.

And look what she is now:

big AND little.

(Big to the baby photos.

Little to the door frame marks.)

Loud AND quiet.

Fast AND slow.

Brave AND shy, taking turns.

Happy and sad and back again,

sometimes all before lunch.

People aren't one thing.

Not even small people.

ESPECIALLY not small people.

She is every opposite,

holding hands,

in one warm bundle

on one warm lap

for the three hundredth time.

The lamp glows. (And the dark holds it.)

The story starts. (And will end.)

Goodnight, everything at once.

You are all of it.

You always were.
