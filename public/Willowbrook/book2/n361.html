Night 361

The Balloon Went Up Again

Month 12: Bedtime Itself

The party is over.

The guests are waved off.

And on the porch: one balloon left.

The tenth one.

Long string.

The balloon man's extra...

"for after. You'll know the moment."

Olivia holds the string.

The balloon tugs, gentle.

It wants to go up.

"Should we let it fly?"

She thinks.

She holds it close one second.

Then...

open fingers...

UP.

Red against the dusk.

Up over the lane.

Up over the rooftops.

Toward the willow line,

where the evening is gold,

where, look, far off...

did something big and gentle

lift its trunk?

The balloon dips once. Courtly.

Then on, and gone, and home.

"Bye-bye!" Both arms.

Some gifts you keep.

Some gifts you send along.

She's learning which is which.

That's two-year-old wisdom.

That's the whole lesson of letting go:

it flies, and it lands

somewhere that was waiting.
