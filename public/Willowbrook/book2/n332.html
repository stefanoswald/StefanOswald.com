Night 332

The Bakery Window Shapes

Month 11: Shapes Everywhere

The bakery window, in the morning,

is the best shape lesson in town.

Look:

The buns: circles. Rows of them!

The braided loaf: a long oval

with a pattern like Tessa's shell.

The sandwich loaves: squares.

Serious, useful squares.

And today, the special tray...

STAR cookies!

Sugar-dusted! A whole sky of them!

"One star, please."

Jingle, says the door.

"For my best customer,"

says Rosa, "the points are extra sweet."

(They are. She's right.)

Eat it point by point by point

by point by point.

Five points. Five bites.

The tastiest star ever counted.

The window refills tomorrow.

Geometry rises fresh, daily.
