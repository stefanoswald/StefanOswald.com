Night 340

When Sleep Won't Come

Month 12: Bedtime Itself

Some nights, sleep is slow.

The eyes won't stay shut.

The legs want one more run.

The crib feels wiggly.

That's okay.

Sleep isn't a door you push.

It's a cat you wait for.

So: the quiet tricks.

The blanket, re-tucked. Snug.

The bear, re-settled. Close.

Mama's hum, low and long.

Hmm-hmm-hmm...

And the big one: breathing.

Slow breaths. Copy Mama.

In... and out...

In... and out...

The wiggles drain out

through the long breaths.

The cat pads closer...

closer...

There.

It curls up on her chest,

soft and heavy.

Sleep came. It always comes.

It just likes to be

waited for kindly.
