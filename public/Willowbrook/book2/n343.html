Night 343

The Whole Town Goes to Bed

Month 12: Bedtime Itself

Olivia isn't the only one.

The WHOLE TOWN goes to bed.

Watch from the window:

The bakery: ovens cooling.

Rosa's light: off. Sweet dreams,

bread lady.

The library: lantern low.

The books: dreaming their stories.

Molly: curled in the wall,

whiskers twitching over sea-pictures.

The farm: barn shut, coop murmuring,

one straw hat on its peg.

The square: fountain still burbling.

(It never stops. Night shift.)

The lane: porch lights out,

one... by one... by one.

Every window going dark

means somebody's tucked in.

The town is a big family

going to bed in shifts.

And the star watches over

every single window.

Including, especially...

this one.
