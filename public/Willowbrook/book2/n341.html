Night 341

Papa's Watch at the Door

Month 12: Bedtime Itself

The story is read.

The song is sung.

The eyes are closed.

And Papa should go now.

He will.

In a minute.

First: the watch.

He stands at the door.

He watches her breathe.

Up... down.

Up... down.

He has done this every night

since the very first night.

When she was brand new,

he checked because he worried.

Now he checks because...

just because.

Because look at her.

The whole day, folded up

in one small sleeper.

The whole world, honestly.

One more minute.

Okay. Now he goes.

(One more.)

Okay.

Goodnight, walker.

The door pulls to. Soft click.

The watch never really ends.

It just moves downstairs.
