Night 354

The Spring Peepers Return

Month 12: Bedtime Itself

A new sound tonight!

Listen, the window's cracked open.

Peep!

Peep-peep!

PEEP-PEEP-PEEP!

The peepers!

The tiny frogs of the brook,

back for spring,

singing their one song

with everything they've got.

The night orchestra is tuning up!

(Somewhere down there,

a certain green someone

is definitely conducting.

"ALTOS! MORE PEEP!")

All winter the nights were quiet.

Snow-quiet. Hush-quiet.

Now: peep-peep-peep, all night!

Spring's white noise.

The warm months' lullaby.

"Peep," says Olivia, settling.

Peep indeed.

Sing them to sleep, little frogs.

Sing us all to sleep.

The green season is here.
