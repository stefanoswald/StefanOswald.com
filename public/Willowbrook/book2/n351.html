Night 351

The Night Before Winter's End

Month 12: Bedtime Itself

Listen. Outside. Very soft.

Drip.

Hear it?

Drip.

The icicle at the eave...

it's melting.

The first melt!

Winter is getting sleepy.

It's been up a long time.

Under the snow,

the bulbs are stirring.

Under the fence,

a leaf-covered someone

is dreaming of trundling.

Down the pasture,

a balloon waits for warm winds.

Spring is coming.

Slowly. Surely.

The way morning comes...

the way everything good comes...

drip by drip,

while you sleep.

"Nigh-nigh, winter," whispers Olivia.

Sleep well, old season.

You were beautiful.

Now it's spring's turn to wake.
