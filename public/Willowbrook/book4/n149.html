Night 149

His Me-Do-It Dawns

Month 5: Little Brother Tags Along

It came at the shoe bench. (The same bench. As it happened the venue and declines to feign surprise. History doesn't rhyme in this house. It reprints.) Theo. The boots. Her hands, moving in to help... the big-sister autopilot, the teaching hands, the doing-for hands...

And two small hands flew up. Stop. "Me do it."... The whole kitchen went quiet. (Mama, at the counter, froze mid-pour... watching not him. Her. The sister. The moment the teacher meets the declaration.

What does she do? She was taught. Her hands were retreated from, once. Slowly. By hands that twitched. Does it transfer? Does the deepest one transfer?) Her hands hovered... twitched... and retreated. Slowly. "... Okay, Fee-oh.

You do it." And she sat on her hands... Literally sat on them, the family's oldest gesture, inherited whole... and watched her brother fight the boot. Wrong foot. Heel folded. Four minutes. Six. And on.

"Me did it! You did it," she said... and then, to Mama, quietly, with the weight of someone recognizing a season: "... His me-do-it came. Everything's gonna take longer now. And matter more. Dat's da deal, right?

Dat's how mine went?" That's how hers went. That's the deal. The declaration, one generation down. The stepping-back, transferred whole... the hardest inheritance, the hands that wait, passed down sat-on and twitching and honored.

This month closes here, where it must: her, watching him, becoming... and letting him. The whole job. Both of them doing it.
