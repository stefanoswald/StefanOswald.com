Night 24

The Little Brother's First Big Game

Month 1: Pretend!

A historic reversal this week. Every game so far: hers. Authored, directed, cast. And then, mid-morning, Theo stood up in the middle of the rug and announced his: "I the bus. Livy... you da rider.

Beep beep." His game. Invented whole. The bus game: he drives, she rides stops are called passengers board And here's the page's whole point, the choice his sister made in half a second, the one that set the next fifteen years: she rode the bus.

Fully. Committedly. "Ding! Dis is my stop!... Wait, I forgot my boot passenger. Beep beep, go back!" (He went back. The route accommodated. He was so proud you could see it from the kitchen.) The author became the player.

The little brother became a maker of worlds... because the first world he ever made, somebody big climbed aboard without hesitating. That's how you grow an imagination: somebody rides your bus. Beep beep. All aboard. Forever after, they took turns driving.
