Night 41

Introducing Baba to Franklin

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

A diplomatic summit this week, long overdue: Baba, meet franklin. (The bear who walks in dreams. The friend nobody sees. The two most metaphysically complicated residents of the household, finally at one table.) The tea was poured.

The introductions, done by the only person fluent in both: "Baba, dis is Franklin. He's a thousand and free. He's invisible but he's real, like how you're a bear but you walk. Franklin, dis is Baba.

He talks in dreams. Don't be scared of his eyes, dey're glass in da daytime, it's fine." The summit proceeded. (Reportedly. The hostess translated both sides: Franklin found the bear "very soft for a walker."

The bear found Franklin "good at tea for somebody with no hands." Compliments were exchanged. A thousand- year-old and a three-year-old bear turn out to have lots in common. Mostly: her.) And the closing rule, invented on the spot and kept ever after: Franklin was invited to the meadow.

"You just gotta get dreamed in. I'll dream you both. Dere's room." There's room. The meadow's whole zoning policy, in two words. Everyone she loves, eventually, gets dreamed in. The invisible, the soft, the far, the thousand-and-three.

There's room. There was always room. That's what she's building in there, night by night: a place with room. It has her whole heart's floor plan.
