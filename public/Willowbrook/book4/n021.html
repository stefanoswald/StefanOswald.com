Night 21

The Puppet Sock

Month 1: Pretend!

The sock drawer's eternal tragedy... the lost mates, the widowed singles... produced this week a miracle: one single sock became somebody. Mama sewed on two buttons. A stitch of red yarn. And onto the hand it went, and, this is the moment, the hand disappeared.

Not a sock on a hand. A character. His name: "Mr. Socko." (Naming department had an off day. The name stuck anyway. They always do.) His voice: scratchy, high, nothing like hers. His personality: Nosy.

Mr. Socko interviewed the entire dinner table: "What are you eating? Why? Is it good? What's your name? I'm mr. Socko." (Papa was interviewed twice. Theo attempted to feed the puppet... a fair response; Mr.

Socko declined graciously: "I don't got a tummy. I got a hand.") And the useful discovery, saved by the watching parents: things she won't say as Olivia, she'll say as Socko. "Mr. Socko is scared of da dentist next week."

(Ah. Is he. Noted. The dentist talk happened that night, puppet included. Socko felt much better. So, coincidentally, did his operator.) Puppets are doors too. Softer ones. Keep a sock ready. Somebody's always easier to be than yourself... and the way in is sometimes the way out.
