Night 114

The Night Walk

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

This month's boldest move, saved for a warm clear night: The night walk. (On purpose. After bedtime. The dark, visited socially... not endured from a bed but walked into, held-handed, as a destination.) The lane, after hours, revealed: The fireflies: at full parliament.

(The lawn's whole summer congress, blinking its slow laws. Both children: reverent. Theo caught one on borrowed technique, the flat palm, and wore the blink like a ring.) The flowers: closed. ("Night-night, daisies"... confirmed at last, in person, on their own schedule.

The garden's bedtime, witnessed.) The gold dog's window: lamplit. (He's in by dark now, old fellow. His silhouette watched them pass. One soft woof through the glass: the patrol, delegated.) The sounds: named live, crickets, the brook's night voice, a far owl, the lane's whole after-hours orchestra.

And overhead: the stars, walked under... not through a window, not from a blanket, but moving under them, the whole bright ceiling keeping pace like the moon always has. "Da dark outside is busy..." She concluded, somewhere past the Hendersons'.

"It's not scary- empty. It's... a whole udder shift." The night shift. The whole other town that runs while hers sleeps... fireflies and owls and closing flowers and old dogs delegating... met now, on foot, befriended.

Home by ten. Both kids: asleep mid-carry. The dark: a place now. Visited. Liked. The best fear-work of this month... and not one tool needed. Just a door, opened outward, at night, together.
