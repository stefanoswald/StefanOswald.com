Night 277

The Bear Book, Decoded

Month 10: Words Are Magic

The bear book. (The bear book. Two years of nightly service. Known by heart... all the hearts.) And this week, at the lamp, a new kind of looking: "Mama. Wait. Da part where it says 'da little bear lost his button'... is dat...

Dese marks? Is da lost living in dis word?" On the actual word. The exact moment... mark it, where memory and print reach for each other. "Sound it out with me. L... o... s... t.

L... o... ss... t. Lost. Da word is lost. The word has been lost the whole time. I knew da story but da story was written down in dere and I never..." (The full realization, arriving like weather: the book she knows by heart has been saying it, in marks, every night, for two years.

The ink has been waiting.) "Do da uddahs! Do bear!" B... bear. Found... f-ound. And the big one, the last-page one, traced letter by letter: "N-igh... nigh. Nigh-nigh. Da nigh-nigh is written too!"

The nigh-nigh is written too. Everything loved is written somewhere. (That's this month's real magic, dawning: the stories were never in the grown-ups. They're in the marks. Which means, the thought arrives, enormous... she could get them herself.

Any story. Any time. Forever. The door isn't open yet. But she's found the handle. And the handle turns.)
