Night 243

The Watch Notes Its Ground Crew

Month 8: Helping Hands

On the last visit of the month, the Great Willow caught Stella's warm light. And Stella's report opened with professional recognition: "The town has a new watch. Whoo? Ground division. Two feet tall. Sock-footed.

She walks rounds every dusk, the fern, the shoes, the brother's cow, the window. I've timed her. She's regular. She checks the Hendersons' light. She checks things I check." The owl blinked slowly. "The oldest law.

The oldest law..." agreed Stella. "The bear taught her. The cow taught him. We taught the cow... well. The dresser years teach everyone. Mmm," hummed the Moon, who has watched ground crews form in this town for two hundred years: the lamplighters, the walk-shovelers, the crust-end distributors, the mystery-oilers of gates.

Every generation, a few of them. "Log her in?" asked Stella. "Formally?" The owl considered the enormity: a mortal, on the watch rolls, at four. "... Log her as apprentice..." He ruled. "Ground division. Provisional. Full job at...whenever she stops needing it logged.

The real ones never know they're on the rolls. That's what makes them real." Logged: apprentice, ground division, provisional, sock- footed. She never found out. She never needed to. Goodnight, ground crew. Goodnight, Willowbrook. All watches: staffed. All lights: checked. Carry on.
