Night 283

The Letter to Lily

Month 10: Words Are Magic

The revelation, mid-week: "Mama. If writing is talking in marks... I could talk to somebody who's not here. Da marks could go somewhere." (The postal service: discovered from first principles. Ten thousand years of civilization, re-derived at a kitchen table.)

The letter. (To Lily. Obviously. Two streets away... whistle range, technically, but that's not the point, the point is the marks going.) The text, preserved in family archive: "Der Lily yu ar my best friend the whistle code is good but this is writing luv Olivia" (Inventive spelling: untouched by editorial hands.

Mama's one contribution: the address, and the stamp, and the walk to the mailbox, where the letter was kissed. Kissed... before posting. "For luck. Da marks are going alone. Dey're brave.") The wait: three days.

Agonizing. Mailbox checked daily, twice. And then: The answer. (Lily's hand. Rosa-assisted. Equally inventive: "Der Olivia yu ar my best friend to I red your letter myself almost all uv it luv Lily") Read aloud on the porch.

Four times. Kept under the pillow a week. Then saved in the treasure box, where it lives yet. The first letter of a correspondence that ran... mark this... decades. Two streets, then two towns, then once, one whole ocean.

The whistle code for the near years. The marks for always. "Yu ar my best friend"... the first thing she ever wrote to anyone. Still true at every distance the far pages hold.
