Night 43

What the Cow Said

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

Theo's dream reports have a style. (Two-year-old style: fragments. Delivered across the whole morning, out of order, as retrieved.) This week's report, assembled by the family like a puzzle: At breakfast: "Da cow talked."

Mid-morning, apropos of nothing: "He gots a bell voice." (A bell voice. The cow talks in chimes. Of course he does. The bell's been in there since the fair.) At lunch: "He said... I'm big now."

At the swing: "He said da bell is for finding. If I get losted in da dream... I follow da ring." (The cow's whole counsel, assembled: you're big now, and if you're ever lost, follow the ringing.

The soft division issues each child exactly the guidance they'll need. The bear got a talker. The cow got a homing bell. The story declines, per policy, to explain how plush knows what it knows.

The story only notes: it always knows.) And at dinner, the last fragment, the one his sister received like treaty news: "Da cow says... da bear is his fwiend." The bear is his friend. The dresser guards, the two jobs, the whole soft division: allied, confirmed, at meadow level.

"Obviously..." ruled Olivia. "Dey're colleagues." Colleagues. The dream-town organizes itself exactly like the real one: on friendship, on bells you can follow home, and on somebody always, always being glad you came.
