Night 173

The Class Learns Maybe

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

The preschool trip to the apple orchard was canceled by heavy rain.

Twelve children sat on the circle rug in boots they no longer needed. Several cried. Marcus asked whether the trip was gone forever.

Olivia raised her hand.

"Maybe it is not all bad," she said. "In my family, maybe means we wait and look for what can still happen."

Ms. Dana asked the class what could happen indoors.

They made paper apples, hid them around the room, and picked them into baskets. They spread the picnic blanket on the floor and ate lunch beneath a paper tree.

The real orchard trip was moved to the following week. By then the leaves were brighter, and the cider press had opened.

At home Olivia told Mama the whole story.

"The rain was like the snowplow in our first maybe story," she said. "It looked as if it ruined the plan, but it moved us toward a different good day."

"And now your class knows the word," Mama said.

Olivia nodded proudly. "All twelve of them. Maybe is spreading.
