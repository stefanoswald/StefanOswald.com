Night 67

Why Does It Get Dark

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

"Why does it get dark?" Tonight: the demonstration. Equipment: one orange, one flashlight, one dark kitchen. "The sun doesn't go anywhere. We turn. Watch..." The orange, spun slowly in the flashlight beam. "Here's Willowbrook...in the light: daytime.

Now the earth turns... turns... and Willowbrook rides around... into the shadow side. Dark. That's the whole night: our side of the ball, facing away." The dot rode around. Day. Night. Day. Night. "... We're the ones moving?!"

We're the ones moving. A thousand miles an hour, right now, all of us, the whole kitchen. (She held the counter. Just in case. Theo held the counter because she did. The kitchen survived its velocity.)

"So da sun never goes to bed?" Never. It's always shining. Somewhere it's always morning... right now, some kid on the far side of the ball is eating breakfast. "... Somebody's always having breakfast?" Always. "Dat's..."

She said, with enormous satisfaction. "a good planet." That's a good planet. Somewhere, always, breakfast. The orange was eaten. (The earth is delicious. Another point in its favor.) The dot rode around forever after in her head... day, night, day, night, the whole town on a turning ball, holding the counter, having breakfast in shifts.
