Night 180

The School Months, Counted

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

Month's. Term's... end. The check. Countables: One backpack: red, practice-worn, bear-capacity. One cubby: adjacent to destiny. One first day: three- beat ceremony, ported; rule: proven daily since. One day-three wobble: ninety seconds, per the professional.

One line led. One chart internalized. One hamster: herded gently, mourned properly, planted under a maple with dandelions. One quiet friend: found at his speed. One fight: rained, repaired, co-painted. One show and tell: a feather and a law; three flower campaigns spawned.

One stage line. One sung line... hers, institutionalized. One teacher: taking notes on them. (The file is mutual now.) Uncountables: That the kit traveled. Every tool this shelf ever built... the porch, the naming, the yet, the maybe, the sitting-close... deployed at the new address by its own owner, unprompted, working.

That the village gained a fence and twelve families. That she left in September holding Mama's hand and walks in now holding the door... for the next kid, for Wren, for whoever's hovering... doorkeeper at the new address too. "Two towns now," per her own count.

Remembered and carried forward: enrolled, befriended, line-leading... at their bedtime world's own pace, walking feet, all the way.
