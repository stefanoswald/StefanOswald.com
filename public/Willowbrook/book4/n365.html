Night 365

The Watch Hands It On

Month 12: Almost Four

The last night. The willow. The full watch... every member, feathered, furred, winged, celestial... assembled over a sleeping town for the year's final session. The minutes, read by Oliver Owl: "Year four of the small one on Maple Lane.

Summary: she crossed to the meadow. She crossed the rope. She crossed the whole distance between not- reading and reading, the longest crossing there is; most never notice they're making it; she noticed everything. She kept a winter's watch on a green room and was there for the opening.

She taught her brother his letter, his first feeling word, and the one-step rule. She learned that brave is lent at interest, that help has a quiet kind, that winter doesn't stop the good things. The tree wrote growing.

The watch concurs with the tree. The watch always concurs with the tree." (The tally book, turned. A fresh page. The heading, inked in owl-careful script: Year five.) "Motion to close the book of Three..." said Stella. "Seconded..." said Bella, wings catching starlight... one year out of her own green room, first vote as a full member. "Carried," hummed the Moon. And Stella... as she does, once a year, on this exact night... reached into her own gold and set a new star in the dark over Maple Lane.

Small. Bright. Fourth in a little line of four, right above a certain roof, where a reader slept with a bear and a key-shaped promise and a letter she read herself. "For da new book..." said Stella. "May it fill slow and good." The watch stood down. The town slept on. The willow held its three gold words up to the stars. Goodnight, Willowbrook. Goodnight, book of Three. Goodnight, growing girl... four years old, four stars up, whole shelf of you still to come.

See you in the next book. The lamp will be there. It always is. The end of From Three to Four.
