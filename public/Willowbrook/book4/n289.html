Night 289

The Word That Fixed a Day

Month 10: Words Are Magic

A hard afternoon this week. (The painting. The spill. Her spill, her own elbow... across an hour's careful work. And an audience for it: Theo, who announced the disaster house-wide, delighted, at volume.) The tears: had.

The stomp: taken. And at the lamp, the work, the kind this family does with words now: "I'm not just mad. Mad's not... da whole shape of it. I need better words. Take your time.

The jar's open... Frustrated. Because da painting was almost done and my own elbow did it. Nobody to even be mad at except me. Dat's frustrated... mad with nowhere to put it. And. Embarrassed. Because Fee-oh told everybody and dey all saw my mess.

Embarrassed is... when your inside mistake gets outside eyes." Mad with nowhere to put it. Inside mistake, outside eyes. "Frustrated and embarrassed. Both at once. Dat's why it felt too big! It was two feelings wearing one coat!"

And, the observable magic, the reason this family stocks words like the pantry stocks flour: Named, the thing shrank. Both halves. Visible things are smaller. Feelings too, feelings most of all. "Da words fixed it.

Not all- da-way fixed. But... handle-d. Da feelings got handles now." Words put handles on feelings. That's the whole tool, little one. The sky still storms, you are the sky, remember, always... but every named weather is a weather you can carry. Two coats. Two handles. Carried. Goodnight.
