Night 132

The First Real Game Together

Month 5: Little Brother Tags Along

Mark this afternoon. It looks ordinary. It's the milestone the whole schedule promised: The first real game. Not her game with him assigned a role. Not parallel play, adjacent kingdoms. A game. Two players. Shared rules.

Both holding them. Hide and seek. He counted: "One, two, fee, nine, ten!" (His count. Improving. The nine is load-bearing.) He sought. Found her. (Behind the curtain. Her old faithful spot. Giggling, per tradition, which helps.)

Then he hid. (Behind the same curtain. The spot is hereditary.) She sought, the full theater, checking wrong places..." Where could he be. "and found him to shrieks. Rules: held, both directions. Turns: taken. The game: Real.

Round eleven, they were found together behind the curtain by Mama, both hiding, having lost track of whose turn, strangling giggles in the same two feet of hiding spot their whole childhood would wear thin. "Da schedule came true," Olivia announced at dinner. "He plays now.

Real plays. Da waiting part is over." The waiting part is over. Two years of fort-spots and fish positions and someday- schedules: arrived. Playmate: delivered, as promised, worth the whole chain of days. The games run daily now. For years. For the whole rest of the shelf.
