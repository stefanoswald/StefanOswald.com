Night 269

The Big Kid Pool Jump

Month 9: Brave Is Scared Plus One Step

Rope day unlocked the deep end. The deep end unlocks, per pool law, per the eternal hierarchy... The jump. (From the edge. Into the deep. The cannonball: the crown jewel of small-kid aquatics, watched from the shallow side for two envious summers.)

The form, coached by its longtime practitioner: "Knees to chest. Arms wrapped. Chin tucked. You're not jumping in... you're delivering a package. The package is you. The splash is the signature." The first jump: feet-first, plain, regulation.

The second: The cannonball. Knees hugged. Midair, one suspended forever- second, the pool rising to meet her... Sploosh. Surfaced to: applause. "Nine! The lifeguard gave it a nine! Tens take years..." said Papa. "I got my first ten at thirty-one.

You're ahead of schedule." The rest of the hour: jump, surface, climb, repeat... seventeen consecutive deliveries, each package signed louder than the last. (The ten came at six, off the low board, in book six, witnessed and scored by the same lifeguard.

The story keeps splash scores now. It's that kind of record.) Two summers of watching. One rope. One month. Seventeen cannonballs. The deep end isn't crossed anymore. It's hers.
