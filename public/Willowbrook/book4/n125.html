Night 125

The Splinter of Being Second

Month 5: Little Brother Tags Along

A splinter this week. The second-child kind... felt by the first child: "It's not fair. I had to learn da flip. He just got taught it. I waited till free for da big swing.

He'll get everything early because I already broke da trail!" (... She's right. Second children inherit trails. Firsts cut them. The unfairness is structural and nobody talks about it at three-almost- four. This book talks about everything.)

The lamp, brought: "You're right. He gets your trail. That's real, and you're allowed to feel the splinter of it. Now, the other tally book: You got things he never will. Two years of only.

Every first, first... first steps nobody compared, first words nobody expected, a mama and papa brand-new and all yours. And one more thing, the biggest: he gets the trail. But you get to be the one who cut it.

Trailblazer. That's a title nobody can inherit." She sat with both tally books. "... Trailblazer." Trailblazer. "And he gets... da trail. And me on it." And you on it. Both tally books, full. Different treasures, by the river's old deal.

The splinter, named, worked its way out... mostly. It revisits. (Siblinghood is long. The tally books get re-read at every age. The lamp stays available.) But the title stuck. Trailblazer. Ask her at any far page. She'll tell you who cut these trails. And who she cut them for.
