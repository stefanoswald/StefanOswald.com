Night 198

The Lost Voice Maybe

Month 7: The Maybe Lessons

A cold's parting gift: The voice. Gone. Croaked out. The loudest citizen in the house: reduced to whisper. (The tragedy of it. So much to say. Zero decibels to say it with.) "...maybe," she mouthed.

And the maybe became: The whisper day. Because the family... watch them, didn't talk over her quiet. They joined it: Everyone whispered. All day. Solidarity volume. And the house at whisper... changed: The jokes got funnier.

(Whispered jokes are contraband. Contraband is hilarious.) The old signs came back out, the more, the all-done, the whole hand- language of the baby years, refreshed and expanded. (Theo: delighted. A language at his level.

He kept half the signs permanently.) And bedtime, whispered entire, story, song, goodnights mouthed... was, all parties agreed, the softest of the season. The slip, whispered to Mama, written down: "Lost my voice. Seemed bad.

Got da whisper day and da signs back and da softest bedtime. Quiet is a whole country. I visit sometimes now on purpose. Caught." (She does. The whisper days recur, voice intact, by choice, family quiet-days, one a season. The lost voice found a volume nobody knew the house had. It kept it.)
