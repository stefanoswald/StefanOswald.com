Night 174

The Halloween Class Party

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

The lantern season came to school: The costume parade. Twelve classmates. The hallway route. The other classes applauding from doorways. The costume decision, weighed for weeks: Something new? Or... The bee. (Third season. Snug now.

The wings, re-wired twice. The antennae, veterans of two treaties.) The bee. ("It's my self costume. New ones are for trying. Da bee is for being.") The parade: flown. (The bee, snug and soaring, buzzed the whole route.

Lily marched as a baker, apron, strawberries, flour on one cheek, a tribute so exact that Rosa, chaperoning, did the dish towel thing in the hallway.) And the after-party ceremony, private, at home, because its wearer had felt the snugness and done the math: The bee's retirement. Hung in the dress- up box's place of honor.

Wings furled. The handing-down scheduled ("Fee-oh gets it next fall. Da buzz comes with it. He's been practicing since last year.") "Free seasons..." She said, patting the stripes. "Best costume ever built. Da aunts made it before dey even knowed who I'd be...

Dey knowed anyway." They knew anyway. Aunts do. The bee rests. The buzz transfers. The season hums on.
