Night 312

The Bird Feeder Winter Shift

Month 11: Winter Wonders

The feeder, the helping month's window-tray, the one that taught that quiet help counts, entered its hard season this week. The winter shift: Snow: swept from the tray. (Every morning now. Before her own breakfast.

"Dey can't sweep. No arms. Dey got wings, which are better, except for sweeping.") The seed: doubled. (Papa's ruling, at the store: "Winter birds burn their food just staying warm. Cold is expensive if you weigh an ounce.")

And the regulars, arriving on the dawn schedule the tray has kept all year: The chickadees (fluffed to spheres, "dey puff, Papa, dey wear demselves as coats!"). And the watcher at the glass, breath-fogging, counting her constituency: "Dey made it froo da night. All of dem.

Da night was so cold and dey're all here." (The morning count. New ritual. The tray sweeper learns what every winter feeder of birds learns: you start doing it for them. You keep doing it because of the count... the small daily miracle of everyone making it through.)

"Da feeder's my watch..." She said, sweeping. "Da birds are my town." The birds are her town. The tray: swept. The seed: heaped. The town: fed, fluffed, counted, carried through one more expensive night... by a watcher who gets up first, in the cold, because that's when the watch matters. The Watch below the Watch. The willow sees it. Approves.
