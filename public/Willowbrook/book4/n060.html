Night 60

The First Why Avalanche

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

It started with toast. "Why is toast brown?" Because the heat toasts it. "Why does heat toast?" Heat changes things. It cooks. "Why?" Because... fire and warmth rearrange what things are made of. "Why?" Because the world is built of tiny pieces that dance faster when they're warm. "...

Why?" And there, four whys deep, at the edge of physics, before eight a.m. The parents met the era. The why year. It has begun. The books forecast it; the toast confirmed it. Every answer is a door to another why.

Every why is real. (That's the secret the tired grown-ups forget: she's not stalling. She's surveying. The whole world, wired together, and her, tracing every wire.) The family policy, set that morning: answer honestly, as far as we know, and when we hit the bottom... say the truest thing: "Nobody knows yet.

Maybe you'll find out." Twelve whys by lunch. The world, under survey. The surveyor: relentless, three, and absolutely right to ask.
