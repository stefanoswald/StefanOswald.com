Night 63

Why Does Grandpa Have Wrinkles

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

Sunday porch. Hand in hand. And the why went straight to the source: "Gampa. Why do you got wrinkles?" (Asked plain. Received plain. The farm has no awkward questions, only unasked ones.) He held up his hand next to hers.

Studied both. "Same reason my barn's got weathered boards and my tools got worn handles. Use. Dis hand's done eighty years of work and weather. Every line's from something, squinting at sun, laughing at your papa, gripping forty winters of frozen gates.

Wrinkles is where da living folded me." Where the living folded him. "Do dey hurt? Nope. Dey're just... da map. Of everywhere I been." She traced one line across his palm. The map of everywhere he'd been. "...

I don't got any maps yet. You got time. Maps come with da miles. Yours'll be a good one. I can tell by how you walk." (By how you walk. The old man's whole blessing, folded in a wrinkle answer.)

"When I get maps..." She decided. "I want laughing ones. Like by your eyes." Rock. Rock. "Best kind. Start early. You already did." She already did. The porch laughed its evening laugh, adding, invisibly, to everyone's maps.
