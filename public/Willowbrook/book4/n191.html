Night 191

The Maybe Jar Reading

Month 7: The Maybe Lessons

Month's end approaching. And the jar, the maybe jar, got its reading. (Dinner. Candles. The slips drawn and read aloud, one by one, like a paper harvest.) The tally: The broken crayon. The rained Sunday.

(The farm came to town.) The wrong book. (The earth is soup with a crust. Everyone knows now. Everyone.) The posted mitten. (It knows things.) The palace Thursday. The multiplied sweater. The burnt-cookie triple.

Eleven slips total. One month. And one empty space, honored out loud: "Da snow globe's maybe isn't here yet. We're still watching. Dat's allowed. Da jar waits." The jar waits. And Theo, two- and-a-half, jar- watcher all month... contributed the closing, his first-ever maybe, caught solo: "My cracker broked... and den it was two crackers."

(The broken-thing maybe. The founding genre. Caught, generation two, at cracker scale.) The slip, his first, went in with ceremony. The jar: eleven and one. Mama said, working, witnessed, hereditary. "Same jar next month?"

Same jar always. It never came down again. It's there in the far books, fuller every volume, the family's whole philosophy in a pickle jar, countable, till it wasn't... till it was just how everyone saw.
