Night 229

The Flyer Crew

Month 8: Helping Hands

Crisis on the lane: The orange cat is missing. (The new one. The wall-sitter. The flat-tire discovery. Three days, no cat. Her family: door to door, worried.) The lane's youngest: Mobilized. The flyers: drawn.

(Crayon. Orange crayon, heavily. "Lost cat. Orange. Blinks slow. Nice." Plus a portrait: recognizable, per witnesses.) Posted: kid-height, every post. ("Kids look low. Kids find things. Da flyers should talk to kids.") (Correct strategy.

As it happened: statistically, lost pets are found by children at wildly disproportionate rates. Kids look low. Kids check sheds.) The search grid: walked. Called. And day two: Found. By the crew. In the Hendersons' shed... shut in by accident during the shovel- crew's cocoa day, living on shed spiders, annoyed but intact.

The reunion: joyous. The reward, offered: declined by the foreman-precedent... "neighbors find neighbors' cats"... but the cat's family delivered cookies anyway, which is how the town settles all its declined rewards. The cat resumed the wall.

Blinked slow at the crew ever after. Cat for: I know what you did. They knew she knew. Everyone slow-blinked. The lane's first search and rescue: in the books.
