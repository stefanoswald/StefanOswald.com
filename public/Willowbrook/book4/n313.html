Night 313

The Longest Night

Month 11: Winter Wonders

The solstice, this week. And the family kept it, simply, per their way: candles at the window, cocoa, and the facts, which are better than any decoration: "Tonight is the longest night of the whole year. The most dark there is.

Tomorrow night: a little shorter. Every night after: shorter still, all the way to summer. Tonight the light turns around. Da light turns around? Tonight? Like, while we're sleeping, da year goes... 'okay, more morning now'?

While we're sleeping... Dat's da best secret I ever heard. Da darkest night is da one where da light starts coming back." (Mark it, record. She found it herself, they always find it themselves, at some window, some December: the oldest human comfort, the one the species has kept with fires and candles and festivals since before it could write the reason down: the dark peaks.

The dark, at its very deepest, is already turning.) "Is dat why we put da candles? That's why everyone puts the candles, love. Every winter light in every window in town, all of it is people saying the same thing: we know.

We remember. It turns around tonight." The candles burned. The cocoa went down. Theo fell asleep mid-carol, per tradition. And at the lamp, the last thought, drowsy: "Da year has a Maybe night. Dat's what dis is.

Da worst dark... dat's secretly da turning- around one... Da year knew da trick before us." The year knew the trick before us. Sleep, solstice child. The light is already coming back. It started while you were saying so.
