Night 89

The Month of Whys, Counted

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

Month's end. The check... by journal, this time. The tally by kind: Answered, with science: the sky, the dark, the worms, two eyes, the bread, the twinkle. A dozen more. Each one: the world, coming into focus, click by click.

Answered, with heart: the wrinkles, the names, the sorry the copying (do good everythings), the older. Held, unfixed: the ambulance worry. The abuela unfairness. (Held is a kind of answered. The best kind, some weeks.)

Kept, by the wonder- file: the willow's gold. The anything- instead-of-nothing. Della's rules. And unanswerable, gloriously: water's wetness. (Loose air. The papers are notified.) this month's finding, written in the journal's margin, both hands: "The whys aren't a phase to outlast.

They're the engine. The kid who asks why the sky is blue becomes the grown-up who asks why things are the way they are, and whether they could be better. Feed the engine. Feed it forever.

Good why-monf?" asked the engine, drowsy. The best. "I got more." We know, love. The journal has blank pages for years. That's the plan. That was always the plan. Remembered and carried forward: asking.
