Night 210

The Uncaught Ones

Month 7: The Maybe Lessons

This month's last question, at the lamp, where the big ones live: "Mama. Do all da bads turn good? Every one?" And the family word earned its deepest hour: "No, love. Not every one. Some bads just... stay bad.

Buttons died. That didn't turn into a better hamster. June's abuela is still sick and far. Some snow globes stay broken longer than we can watch. Here's the whole truth of our word: Maybe doesn't promise the turning.

It promises the watching. The staying open. And here's what I've seen, all my life of watching: even the bads that never turn good... they turn into something. Buttons turned into twelve kids who know how to be sad the right size, and a maple that means something.

The far-sick abuela turned into a girl who knows how to sit with a friend. Not good. But not nothing. The love finds work, even in the unfixable. That's the maybe under the maybe.

That's the whole word." Silence, at the lamp. "... So we watch everything. Even da unfixable ones." Even those. Especially those. "Okay." Sleepy. Certain. "Da jar stays open." The jar stays open. On everything. Forever. That's the word, little one. You have all of it now.
