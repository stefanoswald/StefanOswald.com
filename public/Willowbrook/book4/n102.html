Night 102

Mr. Socko at the Dentist

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

The dentist. The real visit. The one Mr. Socko confessed his fears about, months ago, at dinner. (The puppet knows things first. The puppet always knows things first.) The strategy, deployed: Mr. Socko came along.

And at the chair... the big chair, the bright light, the tray of gleaming tools... Mr. Socko went first. The dentist, a Willowbrook dentist; she knew the drill, pun intact, examined the sock puppet with total professionalism: "Open wide, Mr.

Socko... my, what a healthy yarn mouth... let's count those teeth... zero teeth! Perfect! Zero cavities in zero teeth!" (Socko, via operator: "Dat tickled." The room: giggling. The fear: leaking away through the laughing, the way it does.)

And then, the pivot: "Your turn. Socko will watch. He'll tell you if I do anything he didn't get." The chair ride: Up. The light: sunglasses issued. The counting: twenty teeth, tickled with the little mirror.

The polish: "berry flavor," verified and approved. Zero cavities. One sticker. One new toothbrush, purple. And in the car: "Dat wasn't scary. Dat was Socko's worst fear and it was just... tooth-tickling." Socko concurred, scratchily.

His operator patted him. "You were brave, Socko." The puppet was brave. The puppet's always brave... exactly as brave as the hand inside, which now knows: tooth-tickling. Berry flavor. Stickers. File closed. Next visit: requested. The puppet takes the credit. The puppet's earned it.
