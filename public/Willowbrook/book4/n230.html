Night 230

Theo's First Chart Job

Month 8: Helping Hands

Theo's chart job... Napkin guy, got its training program this month. The trainer: obvious. The lesson: "Okay. Napkins. One each. Dat's four. You can count four... one, two, fee, four. Fold it like a tent.

Tent naps. By da fork. Fork side. Every night. Dat's da whole job. Jobs happen every time, dat's what makes dem jobs and not helping." (Jobs happen every time. That's what makes them jobs and not helping.

The whole labor philosophy, at nap-fold height.) The execution, nightly: four napkins. Tented. Fork side: mostly. And the night he missed, the zoomies ran long; the table sat napkinless... the fern lesson, scaled down: nobody reminded.

Dinner started. He looked. He gasped. "Da naps!" Sprinted. Tented. Delivered mid-soup, breathless, to applause. "Da job waited for you..." said his trainer. "Jobs do. Dat's da udder half of da deal." The napkin guy never missed again.

(Almost. Mostly. He's two-and-a-half. The chart is patient. Charts, like ferns and thrones and towns, have always known how to wait for their people.)
