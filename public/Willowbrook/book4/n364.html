Night 364

The Year of Three, Counted

Month 12: Almost Four

The year's-end check. The big one. Twelve months, named like the old friends they are: The meadow month, the willow's other side, the map on the kitchen wall, the gate seeded far off. Bobby Bear waking, walking, talking, delivering.

The spring of Theo's second year, the cow's bell- voice, da aden era. The waiting- and-growing month. The fear month... storms counted, kit assembled. The school month. Miss Dana, da cubby, Wren on da bench.

The jar month... Maybe franchised to a classroom, a town of jars. The helping month, da ground crew, quiet help, da watch below da watch. The brave month, da deep end, da one-step rule, scared- plus.

The words month, da lights coming on, da card, da book she added to da pile. The winter month, da green room, quiet-but-going, da whole deep year. And the birthday month, da chain, da party, da willow's third word.

Growing. "Dat's da whole book of Free..." She said, at the end of it. "Free hundred sixty- five nights... We read it all. Every night. Nobody skipped." Nobody skipped. (Three books now, cover to cover, night by night.

This family's whole method: show up at the lamp. Every night. The years write themselves when the attendance holds.) "And tomorrow? Tomorrow da last night of da book. Da sky one. Da handing- over one...

And den four's book starts. Empty. Whole. Mine to fill." Remembered. Bound. Shelved beside its two elders. All of it. What a year. What a kid. One night remains. The sky's expecting us.
