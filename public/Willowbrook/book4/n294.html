Night 294

The Catalog Reading at the Farm

Month 10: Words Are Magic

The arrangement's first full session, this week, the farm porch: The catalog reading. (The seed catalog. Spring planning. Forty pages of tomatoes and promises. Grandma's old job. Now... some of the hours, hers.) The method: Her: reading slow.

Finger under words. The hard ones sounded out at whatever speed they need. ("Bur... burp... burpee? Grandpa, dere's a company called burpee. There is. Dat's da funniest thing in agriculture.") Him: listening. Whittling. Correcting nothing, waiting out every long word, patient as soil, because the arrangement's real crop isn't tomatoes.

The work product: Six tomatoes circled. (Two practical. Joe's. One yellow kind... hers, "because yellow tomatoes sound like a made-up thing and I want to check.") The sunflower page: dog-eared. ("Da mammof kind.

It's called mammof, Grandpa. Dey named a flower a mammof. Words are da best thing happening.") And at pickup, Joe to Mama, quiet, at the truck: "She read me eleven pages. Sounded out 'germination' by herself... took her a full minute and she didn't quit it...

Forty years your mother read to me on that porch. I figured those hours were done. They're not done." They're not done. The porch hours, resumed. New reader. Same porch. Same listening. Some inheritances skip nothing... they just wait, patient as soil, for the next voice to come up the steps with something to read.
