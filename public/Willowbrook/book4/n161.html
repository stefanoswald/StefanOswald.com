Night 161

The Turn-Taking Masterclass

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

The easel. The crown jewel. ("Every day if I want," but twelve kids want, and there's one.) The old solution: grown-up assigns. The classroom solution, arrived at this month, witnessed by Miss Dana, reported home with pride: The sand timer.

"Me and Wiwi both wanted da easel. Same time. And nobody grabbed... we're not twos... and I said 'timer?' and she said 'timer' and we flipped it. She painted till da sand ran out. I watched and got my picture ready in my head.

Den me. Den her again, double turns because nobody else wanted it. No grown-up even came." No grown-up even came. (Underline it. The whole arc of the sharing lesson... from one red block circulating at nine-tenths possession, through sacrifice towers and turn- counts and the chart's clothespins... arriving here: two four-year-olds, one contested resource, self-governed, zero casualties, by timer, by treaty, by years of practice nobody knew was practice.)

"And da picture you got ready in your head? Better dan my first idea. Waiting made it cook." Waiting made it cook. The turns lesson's secret final lesson, discovered at the easel: the wait isn't the price of the turn. Sometimes it's the gift.
