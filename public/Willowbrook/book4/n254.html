Night 254

The High Branch

Month 9: Brave Is Scared Plus One Step

The climbing tree... the oak, the low- branch one, sat in since age three... has higher branches. And this season's one-step campaign reached them: Week one: standing on the low branch. (One step past sitting.

Counted.) Week three: the second branch, touched. Week five: the second branch, sat. And this week: The third. Her personal record. Papa- spotted, self- climbed, one rung past every previous stop. And from it: The view.

"Papa. I can see the whole park. Da pond and da kite hill and... I can see the willow from here. It's watching da park too! We're both watching!" (Her and the willow: colleagues now, at altitude.

The watch, discovering its own vantage points. The tree held her the way oaks hold all their climbers: completely, and as if it had been waiting.) The descent: careful, backward, by the academy's eternal law.

The down is the skill that saves. "Da third branch is mine now. Da fourth is a yet." The fourth is a yet. (It fell the next summer. The fifth, the year after. The oak gave her one branch a season for years... the slowest, steadiest ladder in the park, climbed entirely in one-steps, with the whole town's second- best view at every landing.)
