Night 42

The Meadow Tour

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

The cartographer is back. (The map of nowhere, last month. Treasure confirmed. The craft took.) This month's job: a map of the meadow. The dream one. Drawn at the kitchen table, from memory, narrated: "Da meadow is here.

Big. Da grass glows a little at night... dream-grass does dat. Da brook, with da lantern fish. Da willow, it's da same willow, Mama, but da udder side. Baba says trees go all da way froo."

(Trees go all the way through. The purple tree got gold fringes. The story got chills.) "Da firefly field. Dey know Baba dere. And here...is where me and Fee-oh finded each udder dat one time.

I marked it so we can find it again." The map went up on the wall. The family atlas gained its first impossible page... saved between the map of nowhere and the town's real one, which is exactly where it belongs: the geography of a family runs all three kinds, real, invented, and dreamed, and the wise houses frame every layer.

"Is it a good map?" It's a perfect map. "Baba checked it last night. He said I forgot da stone- skipping beach. I'm adding it." She added it. The atlas grows. The meadow, mapped, holds still a little better now... the way all loved places do, once somebody draws them.
