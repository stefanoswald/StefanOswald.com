Night 329

The Winter Things, Put Away

Month 11: Winter Wonders

The warm held. The forecast agreed. And so, this week, the family rite of passage-of- season: The putting away. (The cedar chest. The winter things. The folding... and, in this house, the thanking, because this family doesn't let a season's tools go unacknowledged:) The snowpants: folded.

("Thank you for da sled hill and da fort and da knees dat stayed dry in da museum puddle. You had a big year.") The boots: paired, retired. ("You squelched da whole mud season and crunched da whole snow one.

Two- season boots. Respect.") The stripey mittens' spot: empty, noted, honored. ("Dey're on somebody's warm hands somewhere. Best thing a mitten can be.") The red mitten: Not packed. (The relic. Fort together's flag; the snowman's hat; Theo's contribution to history.

Ruled exempt: "Flags don't go in chests. Flags go in da treasure box." It went in the treasure box.) And the coat, last, sleeves crossed like a bow: "Thank you for da whole warm. See you when da leaves come down."

The lid: closed. Cedar-click. "Why do we thank da things?" she asked... asking the family's catechism, knowing the answer, wanting it aloud, the way the best answers are wanted: "Because things that carry us through seasons deserve words at the door.

And because saying thank you to the winter things is how we say it to the winter." The chest: full. The season: said to. The closet: suddenly spring. (The lid opens again in November, by the far pages, onto everything smaller than she remembers.

That's the chest's whole other magic. But that's November's story. Today the house exhales. Warm is coming.)
