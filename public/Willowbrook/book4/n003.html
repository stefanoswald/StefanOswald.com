Night 3

The Box Castle

Month 1: Pretend!

A box arrived. The box. Big enough to sit in. Big enough to live in. And this year, the difference of this year... it didn't stay a box even one minute. "It's a castle. Papa, I need doors."

Papa's knife made the drawbridge. (Kings are wide.") Her crayons made the stones, grey scribbles, row on row. A flag went up: paper, taped, a duck on it. And the court assembled: Queen Olivia. (Crowned.

The crown was yesterday's paper hat. Repurposed. Royalty recycles.) Sir Theo, the Small. (Knighted with the wooden spoon. The dragon one. It does ceremonies now too.) Baba the Bear, Royal Advisor. The kingdom lasted four days.

It rained inside once (a watering can incident), survived one dragon attack, and fell at last the way all cardboard kingdoms fall: gloriously, under a knight who hugged a wall. "We build a new one next box," declared the queen. Next box. Always a next box. The realm is renewable. The reign never ends."
