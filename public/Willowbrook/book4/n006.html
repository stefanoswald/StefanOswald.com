Night 6

Theo the Dog

Month 1: Pretend!

The pretend is catching. Today, Theo made his own announcement... his first-ever pretend, self-invented, fully committed: "I dog." And down he went, all fours, and barked. "Woof! Woof!" A dog. He's a dog now.

The commitment was total: he panted. He begged at snack. He brought Olivia a slipper. He scratched behind his ear with... an attempt. And his sister, who could have said "you're not a dog, you're Theo"... did the other thing.

The only thing. The thing this whole family always does with a pretend: she believed it. "Good dog. Sit... Sit. Okay, good try. Here's your bone." (A breadstick. Received in teeth. Carried proudly to the fort.)

He was a dog till nap. He was a dog after nap. He was, intermittently, a dog for three weeks. (The gold dog, consulted through the fence about the new colleague, wagged his approval. The patrol accepts all recruits.)

Two children. Two worlds now, hers elaborate, his brand-new... and the doors between them all wide open. Woof, little fish-dog. Welcome to the deep end.
