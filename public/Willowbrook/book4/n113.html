Night 113

Lending Courage at the Bridge

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

Thursday. The brook walk. And at the little bridge: Lily stopped. "I don't like da bridge. You can see da water froo da cracks. What if it breaks?" (A real fear. New... or newly said.

The slat-gaps, the water moving under, the what-if. Bridges have scared better engineers.) And Olivia, who crossed this bridge before she could say bridge, did not say "it's fine, come on." (The dragging move.

The it's-nothing move. She's never once been dragged at a fear. She had no dragging in list.) She ran the kit. Friend edition: The naming: "You're scared of da cracks. Dat's allowed." The information: "Dis bridge holds gampa.

Gampa is like... four Lilys. And da truck went over da big one. Bridges are strong like chairs." The offer: "Want da hand thing?" (The hand thing. The dog-fence technology, from years back, her hand flat under Lily's, at the fear's edge, going first.)

Hand under hand, they crossed: one slat at a time, the water narrated ("it's way down; it's just walking by; it doesn't even see us"), the far side achieved... and re-crossed, immediately, by Lily's own demand: "Again. By myself dis time. You just... be dere."

By herself. Friend adjacent. Crossed. "I did the bridge." She did the bridge. Somebody holds flat. Somebody says allowed. And the bridge... every bridge, forever... gets shorter by exactly one friend.
