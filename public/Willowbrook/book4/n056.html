Night 56

The Storm on Both Sides

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

A big storm this week. The real kind. Night kind. The sky's whole drum corps, close. And though the thunder- count is old fluent craft now (flash... "one two free four"... boom, "four away and leaving")... night storms are night storms, and three is three, and the bear worked his first job hard that night: held tight, squeezed at every boom, soft per job.

The storm passed. Sleep came. And the meadow... this is the discovery... was calm. Perfectly. Star-clear. The glow-grass still, the brook easy, no thunder anywhere in the dream sky. "It stormed here..." She told the bear, walking.

"Loud one. You were dere... I squeezed you. I know. I was on both sides at once. It's a bear thing. But da meadow's not stormy. Da meadow..." said the bear. "keeps shelter weather on storm nights.

Always has. When your side is loud, dis side stays soft. Dat's not an accident, little one. Dat's da design. Everybody needs one country where da storm can't follow." One country where the storm can't follow.

(The cozy corner, the fort, the meadow... the same shape, scaling up, side by side by side. Shelter all the way down. Shelter all the way through.) She slept the storm's second half on starlit glow-grass, and woke to a rinsed blue morning on the loud side, completely unafraid... a girl with shelters at every altitude, including one past the reach of any weather this world owns.
