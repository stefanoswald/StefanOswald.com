Night 155

The First Day

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

The day. The walk there: four blocks, held hands, the backpack riding red. The door: reached. And the ceremony... the three-beat, the one this family built at another door years ago... run at the new address: The kiss.

("Mama goes. Mama comes back. Always comes back.") The wave, held, two seconds, honored. And the window: the classroom's own, found from inside, her face at it, waving the second wave as Mama walked to the corner and back into view.

Twice, seen. The routine, ported whole. And then... the turn. Away from the window. Into the room. Where Lily stood at the adjacent cubby, hanging her jacket... and the whole new country resolved into one known face: "Wiwi!

Livy! We're cubby neighbors forever!" (their bedtime world: entered. Not alone. Never alone. Planned that way by two mamas at a bench, five years back, whether they knew it or not.) Miss Dana rang the little chime.

Circle time. Criss-cross. The hello song... a real one, the school's own... learned by lunch. The mamas: came back. (At noon. The reunion: full- speed, per tradition.) "How was it?" The review, delivered at volume, all the way home: "The easel is real.

I painted. Dere's a hamster. His name is buttons. I got a job... Line leader is coming to me eventually dere's a chart. Lily sat with me at snack. Miss dana likes whys... I asked four...

I'm going back tomorrow, right?" Tomorrow and all the days. School: begun. Their bedtime world: claimed. The bear, consulted in the backpack: unneeded all day. (Carried anyway. Weeks yet. The unneeded carrying is the whole bridge.)
