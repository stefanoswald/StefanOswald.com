Night 18

The Sad Game

Month 1: Pretend!

A quieter page. An important one. This week, the doll Duck played the same game over and over: The goodbye game. Duck went away. Duck's family waved. Duck came back. Duck went away. Wave. Back.

Again. Again. Again. Mama watched a while. Then sat down on the rug and asked the doll, not the girl, the doll; the doll is the door... "Duck, you keep leaving. Are you okay?" And through the doll, it came out: "...

Duck is sad because her fwiend June's gramma is really far and might not come back and June cried at da park and Duck doesn't know how to fix it." There it was. (June's abuela.

The faraway sick one. The bench news had reached the little ones, the way all news does. And a three-year-old, carrying a friend's un-fixable sad, had been processing it the only way three knows how: through a doll, on a rug, in reruns.)

This is what the pretend is for... the deepest thing it's for: the heart rehearses in there. The too-big feelings get handed to dolls, who can survive anything, and played until they're carryable. "Duck can't fix June's sad..." said Mama, to the doll, gently. "But Duck knows the bench trick... Sitting close and staying. Duck's very good at it. So is her owner." The game changed that day: Duck stopped leaving. Duck sat close with a smaller doll, and stayed.

Rehearsal: complete. (Thursday, at the park, she sat with June by the fence a long quiet while. The doll had practiced her.)
