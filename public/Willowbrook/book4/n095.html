Night 95

The Storm Fear Returns

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

A big storm this week. The biggest in a year. Night. Close. Loud... the flash-boom gap down to "one" and once to nothing, the crack right overhead, the house lights blinking. And the old fear... schooled at windows, counted at gaps, saved under magnificent... came back at full size anyway.

(They do that. Fears keep growth spurts too. A three-and-a- half-year-old in a direct-overhead boomer is allowed her whole reaction. The book stamps it: allowed.) The tools, all deployed: The count, tried; the gap too short; "It's at zero, Mama"... true; held anyway.

The bear, clutched. Working. Positioned. The breath, done in threes, Mama's hand rising and falling with hers. The big bed, opened. (The storm part. Some nights the whole family weathers in one harbor. This was one.

No shame at any age. The far books show storm-part nights at ten. The harbor never closes.) And the reframe, whispered between booms: "Remember whose drums?... Da sky's. Is big bad?... Big is not bad.

But dis is very big. It is. And we're very safe. Both." Both. The storm spent itself by two. The family, harbored, slept in a heap. And at breakfast, the survivor's report, delivered with returning swagger: "Da sky did its biggest drums and I was scared and brave and I used free tools and da bear worked overtime."

All true. Every part. That's not a fear defeated. That's a fear ridden... which is the actual skill, the one that works on every weather coming, at every size she'll ever be.
