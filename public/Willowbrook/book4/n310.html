Night 310

Theo and the Melted Snowman

Month 11: Winter Wonders

The warm snap, mid-month. Two days of it. And the casualty, discovered by its co-builder, age two-and-a- half, at the window: "Snowman. Wivy. Snowman sick." (The snowman: slumped. Carrot: down. One coal eye: shed.

The particular pathos of a melting snowman, leaning, patient, dissolving politely into the lawn that made him.) The grief: total. (His first thaw. Everything he builds, towers, snowmen, the world keeps taking down. "Fusstated!" he diagnosed... correctly; the word in service; the professor, watching, proud even mid-crisis... "and sad!")

The consolation... hers; the law: inherited; the kneel: floor- level, eye-level, the method: "Fee-oh. Look. Da snowman's not gone-gone. He's water now. And da water goes in da ground, and da ground drinks it, and da flowers drink dat...

Bella's flowers, in spring... so our snowman is gonna be... Inside da flowers. We built a thing dat becomes uddah things. Dat's not losing da snowman. Dat's da snowman going on." (The sweater- becomes-the-hat.

The water cycle edition. The whole family law... nothing loved gets lost; loved things get remade... translated to toddler, at a window, over a slumping carrot.) "... Snowman in flowers? In da flowers. Spring proof. We'll go look togedder."

(They did. Spring. The crocuses. Doctrinally: unimpeachable. The snowman goes on. Everything loved goes on. Ask the flowers. Ask this family. Same answer.)
