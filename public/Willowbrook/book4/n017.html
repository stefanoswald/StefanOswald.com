Night 17

The Pretend Argument

Month 1: Pretend!

Thursday. And a problem new to this year: Two authors. One living room. Olivia's game: "Da floor is water. Sharks." Lily's game: "Dis is a castle. I'm da queen." Both games: announced simultaneously. Both authors: certain.

"Water. Castle. Water! Castle!" The old ending: a storm. Maybe two storms. The three-year-old ending, watch it work, because it's one of the year's great arrivals: a pause. Two authors glaring. Thinking. And then, from Lily... eleven days of extra wisdom, as ever: "...

What if da castle is in da water?"... A flooded castle. A castle with a moat that's everywhere. A queen who needs a boat. Sharks who guard the throne! The games didn't compete. The games married.

And the merged game... Flood Castle, was better than either original, ran for four Thursdays straight, and entered the permanent repertoire. This is the year's social lesson, hiding inside play: your pretend plus my pretend equals a country neither of us could draw alone.

The negotiation is the game. The best worlds are co-authored. (True at three. True at every age after. Marriage itself is mostly Flood Castle, the far books note. Learn it on the rug.)
