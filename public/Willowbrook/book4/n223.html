Night 223

The Sorting Authority

Month 8: Helping Hands

New chart job, self- petitioned after the bolt triumph: Recycling authority. The bins: hers. Paper here. Cans there. Glass, careful, with help. The standards: bolt- bucket grade. ("Sorted means sorted. No cheating da yogurt cups into paper.

Misc is where sorting goes to die.") The weekly check: performed. The bin- day haul to the curb: co-executed, wagon-assisted. And the why, asked and answered at the curb: "Where does it go?

To places that make it into new things. Old paper becomes new paper. Cans become cans again... Like da sweater became da hat. Nothing loved gets lost. Even cans." Even cans. (The aunts' law, extended to municipal services. The bins never sorted better. Everything, everywhere, remade.)
