Night 247

Speaking Up at Circle

Month 9: Brave Is Scared Plus One Step

A different brave this week. The social kind. The hardest kind: At circle time, a visiting helper said, mid- lesson: "Boys are stronger, so boys should carry the chairs." And the room... twelve four-year-olds, one wrong thing hanging in the air...

And one hand went up. (Hers. Scared... she reported later: "my chest went hot and my words went far away." The full storm- physics of speaking up. She raised the hand anyway. Plus one step.)

"Dat's not right, please. I carry milk. And cushions. And my brother sometimes. Strong isn't a boy thing. It's a practice thing. Anybody who practices gets strong. Miss Dana lets everybody carry." (Respectful. Factual. Milk-credentialed.

The sub, to her credit, took it: "You know what? You're right. Chair carriers: everyone who wants.") Twelve kids carried chairs. And Miss Dana, told after, added a line to the famous file, and one to the class rules, permanent: "Wrong things can be said out loud about.

Kindly. Even to grown-ups." The bravest step of this month wasn't a ladder rung. It was a hand, raised, hot-chested, into a quiet room. (It never got easier. It never does, for anyone.

She just never stopped raising it. Check any far page. The hand goes up. Kindly. Factually. Milk-credentialed. Every time it needs to.)
