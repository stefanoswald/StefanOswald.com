Night 22

The Map of Nowhere

Month 1: Pretend!

Today's project: a map. Not of anywhere. That's the point. A map of nowhere... drawn first, found after. The cartography: Mountains. A lake. A forest of scribbles. A dotted line, wandering through everything. And at the line's end: an X.

"Da treasure. What's the treasure? We hafta go see." And so the map was followed, through the real yard, which cooperated magnificently: The purple mountains: the compost pile. Duck Lake: the wading tub. The scribble forest: the tomato rows.

And at the X... the corner by the fence, where the dotted line ended... they dug. (A little. With the toy spade. Papa may have, at some earlier hour, buried something. Papas do that. It's in their job description, unwritten, universal.)

The treasure: a small tin. Inside: six buttons and one chocolate coin. "The map was true!" The map was true. Maps of nowhere always are... if somebody who loves you walks the line ahead and salts the X.

She drew maps all year. Every X paid out. Nobody ever told her why. Some magic runs on a papa with a tin and a ten-minute head start... which is to say: the realest kind.
