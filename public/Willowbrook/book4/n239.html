Night 239

The Snow Crew Rides Again

Month 8: Helping Hands

Second big snow. And the crew, the Hendersons'-walk founders. Re-formed. Bigger: Mateo: foreman, standing. Olivia, Lily: crew, veteran. Theo: morale, promoted to "salt guy" (a cup of sand-salt, scattered with two- year-old liberality; some of it even on the walk).

And new hires: June and the recruit himself Six workers. Three routes: The Hendersons'. Wren's grandmother's. And Mr. Petrov's shop walk. (The mender. "He fixes da town's stuff. We clear da town's way to him.

Dat's just logic.") The cocoa: tripled. (Three households' worth. The crew drank like linemen at each stop. The route ran on marshmallows by the end.) And the standing, compounding: the lane now expects them. First flakes, and porch lights blink on in a pattern that means: the crew will come.

"We're setup," said the foreman, who learned the word at big school and deployed it with relish. Setup. Six kids, five shovels, one teaspoon. The town's warmest municipal service. Running every snow since.
