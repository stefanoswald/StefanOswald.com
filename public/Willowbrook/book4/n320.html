Night 320

The Frost Ferns

Month 11: Winter Wonders

The cold snapped hard overnight, and morning found the bedroom window... Grown over. Frost ferns. (The whole pane: feathered. Ice- gardens, silver- white, spiraling corner to corner... ferns and feathers and tiny frozen forests, etched in the night by nobody.)

The waking discovery: "Mama. My window grew a garden. In the night. Dere's ferns on the inside of the glass and nobody planted dem." The dawn inspection, noses close: "Who did it? The cold did.

Your window sweats a little... warm inside, cold outside... and last night the cold froze the sweat one tiny drop at a time. And ice, when it grows slow, grows in patterns. It can't help it.

It's how water is, underneath... it's been pattern-shaped all along; the cold just gets it to admit it. Water's secretly fern-shaped?! Da puddles? Da brook? Da bath?! All of it's got ferns hiding inside?!"

(All of it. Snowflakes, same confession. The whole month, one long story: water, under oath, admitting its shapes.) The interaction... discovered, then regretted, then philosophized: a warm breath... Haaah, melts a peephole. ("... I unmade some ferns.

Da looking costs ferns. Dat's... hm. Some looking uses up da thing it looks at.") (Some looking uses up the thing it looks at. Age four. At a window. Before breakfast.) The ferns came back the next cold night.

And the next. All winter: free art, nightly, grown while she slept... peepholes budgeted at two per morning. Winter's whole deal, learned at one pane: it takes the garden. It gives the garden back. Different shape. Same water.
