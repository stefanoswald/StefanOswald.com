Night 222

The Job Nobody Assigned

Month 8: Helping Hands

Month's end. And Mama noticed something... watching, one dusk, from the doorway: The rounds. Nobody assigned them. No chart holds them. But nightly now, before bath, a small figure walks the house: The fern: checked.

The shoes: nudged into their line. The bear's scarf: straightened on his shelf-seat. Theo's cow: retrieved from wherever two- year-olds abandon cows, returned to his bed. The maybe jar: patted. And the last stop, every night: the window.

The lane checked. ("Da Hendersons' light is on. Da gold dog's in. All correct.") The watch. Her own edition. Two feet tall, self-appointed, nightly. "What are you doing, love?" Mama asked, the night she finally asked.

And the answer, shrugged, simple, enormous: "Checking on everything. Somebody soft should always be checking. Dat's da oldest law dere is. Baba told me. He got it from da dresser." (The oldest law. The cow's letter.

The bear's job. The whole soft division's founding text, walked now, nightly, by a four-year-old in sock feet, unassigned, unpaid, utterly faithful. The watch has a ground crew now. It always ends up with one. That's how towns like this keep happening.)
