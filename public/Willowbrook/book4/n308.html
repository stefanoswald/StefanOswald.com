Night 308

The Snow Fort Alliance

Month 11: Winter Wonders

The snow: packing grade. (The good kind. The builder's kind. First of the season.) The trio: assembled. The project: The fort. The division of labor, self- organized; note it, Miss Dana would: June: Engineering. ("Da walls have to lean in a little.

Bugs taught me. Ants don't build straight up either. Straight up falls down.") Lily: Supplies. (Bakery run, via Rosa: rolls, warm, smuggled in a mitten-bag. An army marches on its stomach. A fort garrison doubly so.)

Olivia: Words. (The fort needed a name..." Forts without names fall down, dat's known. "and a sign, snow- carved: Fort togedder. And a founding document, recited at flag-raising... the flag: a red mitten on a stick: "Dis fort is for da free of us and whoever's cold.

Dat's da whole constitution.") (For the three of us and whoever's cold. Nine words. There are nations that do worse.) The garrison served all afternoon: two snowball sieges, one Theo one round of rolls, and one sunset (watched from the walls, gold on snow, the kind of cold that makes warm afterward feel like an invention).

Fort together stood eleven days, melting slowly into legend. (Rebuilt every winter after. Same name. Same constitution. Same red mitten... kept between seasons in the treasure box, promoted to relic. Some forts are snow. Some forts are the building together. Only one kind melts.)
