Night 131

The Matching Everything Phase

Month 5: Little Brother Tags Along

The phase arrived at breakfast: "Same as Wivy." His cup: must be red now. (His was green. A negotiation. He drinks from red's twin.) His boots: "like Wivy's." (Procured. The lane now hosts two pairs of stomping red.)

His bedtime song: "Wivy's verse." His breakfast, his chair angle, his everything: same, same, same. "Why does he copy everything?" the old question, back around. And the old answer, back around with it... but this time she finished it herself, mid-complaint: "...because copying is da biggest compliment.

Because he thinks I'm... da best way to be." (You could almost hear him thinking. The complaint melted, mid- sentence, into something else.) "... Mama. He thinks I'm da best way to be." He does. "Dat's a lot of job."

It is. It's the whole faculty salary: paid in imitation, owed in example. "Do good everyfings," she murmured, her own old rule, re-shouldered. She poured his milk into the red twin. Same as hers.

Exactly as requested. The best way to be, being it, one matched cup at a time.
