Night 94

What Baba Does on Shift

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

The meadow, this week, hosted a professional inquiry: "Baba. You said you work nights. What do you do all shift?" And the bear gave his girl the full log: "First hour: settling watch. You're heaviest into sleep den...

I hold still and warm. It's skilled stillness. Years of training. Deep night: dream duty. If you dream me in, meadow. If not. I stay postbound, on catch. Bad dream comes: I'm da first soft thing your arms find.

Dat's not accident. I position. Small hours: da listening. Da house ticks, da pipes murmur, a car maybe. I know every night sound of dat house. Anything not on my list. I'm awake. (Nothing's ever been off-list.

But I check. Forty… some hundred nights, I've checked.) And dawn: da loosening. You sleep lighter, your grip softens, and I do my last job: be exactly where your eyes open. First thing seen: somebody soft.

Sets da whole day." The whole log. The guarded hours, detailed. "... You do all dat? Every night? Little one. What did you think 'being held' was? It's a job with duties. Best job in da soft division.

I've said it before: I picked da better job." She held him different after that. Not tighter. Prouder. You would too, knowing the log. Somebody soft, on shift, positioned, listening, exactly where your eyes open. Every night. All of them. Working.
