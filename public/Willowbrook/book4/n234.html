Night 234

The Fire Station Thank-You

Month 8: Helping Hands

The class unit on community helpers ended with a field trip. Her suggestion, adopted by vote: "We always learn about da helpers. We should thank dem." The fire station thank-you. The delivery: twelve kids, twelve drawings (trucks, hoses, one duck in a fire hat... provenance obvious), and two dozen buns...

Rosa's donation, solicited by her Saturday staff: "Firefighters need carbs. It's medical." The visit: the trucks, the pole the gear, and the drill... their drill, the real stop-drop-roll, taught by the real crew, practiced by twelve kids on the station floor.

And the chief, at the drawings, at the buns, at the thank-you song: "In twenty years, nobody's come just to say thanks. We go to them. Nobody comes to us." Nobody comes to us.

(The helped, helping back. The town's current, run in reverse on purpose... her whole month's instinct, scaled to a class. The station hung the duck-in-a-fire- hat inside the truck bay. It's there in the far books. Laminated. The crew calls it the mascot.)
