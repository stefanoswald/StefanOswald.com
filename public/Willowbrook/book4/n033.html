Night 33

Telling Theo About Dream-Baba

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

A secret this big needs telling. And the teller knows her audience: "Fee-oh. Come here. Serious thing." (He came. He sat. He is two now, nearly... his birthday is weeks off, and serious things with his sister are his favorite category.)

"Baba walks. In my dreams. He talks. He knows da dream-brook. It's real, dream-real, which is a kind of real." Theo took this in. Looked at the bear Looked back. And asked the only question that mattered in his entire world: "...

Cow too?" The cow. His cow. The dresser guard, the bell-swallowed, the plush colleague. Does the cow walk? His sister considered. (Carefully. This is canon-setting. She takes it seriously.) "... I don't know. I never seen him dere.

But Baba says bears find deir way around when dey're loved enough. Da cow is loved enough. Maybe he's waiting. Maybe you gotta dream him in. Dat's how it works. You dream, dey walk." You dream, they walk.

(The mechanics, established. Book canon now. The whole soft division runs on being dreamed in.) Theo nodded, deeply satisfied, and reported at dinner: "I gonna dweam da cow." Dream well, little one. The meadow has room. The meadow has always had room. That's what it's for.
