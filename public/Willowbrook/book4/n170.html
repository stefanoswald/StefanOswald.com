Night 170

The Chime at Home

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

An import this month, from school to home: The chime. ("Miss Dana doesn't chase anybody. She chimes. And everybody just... changes gears. It's da best magic in da room.") The household edition: a little bell, market-bought, installed on the kitchen shelf.

The rules, established by its importer: One chime: five- minute warning. ("Get your ending ready. Endings need runway... Miss Dana says.") Two chimes: transition. And the rule that makes it work... imported intact: the chime is never angry.

It's information, not thunder. The bell doesn't nag. The bell just knows what time it is. The results, within the week: Cleanup: chimed, not chased. (Compliance: shocking. The bell outranks the voice. Nobody knows why.

Every teacher on earth knows why.) Dinner: chimed. The trike bell, retired from school- pretend, now answers from the yard... Theo's acknowledgment chime. And bedtime's five-minute warning: chimed nightly... "get your ending ready"... the endings, given runway, landing soft all month.

"Da school teached da house something," she noted, satisfied. It did. The flow goes both ways now... home to school, school to home, the two countries trading their best technologies across one red backpack, daily, to everyone's gain.
