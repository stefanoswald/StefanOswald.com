Night 29

The Month the World Doubled

Month 1: Pretend!

Month's end. The check. Countables: One dragon spoon. One drowned living room, with fish position. One cardboard kingdom, four days' reign. One tea service for the invisible. One theater, running nightly. One restaurant, zero-and-five stars.

One store, one hospital, one post office... the table's underside, a whole economy. One map of nowhere: treasure confirmed. One symphony in three movements and a boom. One cape: flightless, essential. One sock: now somebody.

One door, drawable anywhere, and one key, for closing, pocketed nightly. One bus, driven by its first new author. One preschool, rehearsed in advance. One dream, with a walking bear in it. (That one's just beginning.

The whole next month knows.) Uncountables: that the world doubled this month and lost nothing. That the family learned to serve air tea with straight faces and mean it. That the pretend turned out to be the heart's own workshop, where goodbyes get rehearsed and fears get doors and futures get practiced before they arrive.

"Good pretend month?" The best one. "Da games say thank you. Dey told me." The games say thank you. Received, games. The pleasure was entirely, doubly ours. Remembered and carried forward: twice as wide.
