Night 326

The Last Snowman

Month 11: Winter Wonders

The forecast said it plain: last snow of the season. Tonight. Then the long warm. And this family does not waste a last anything. The last snowman: built at dusk, from the thinning cover, a small one, necessarily; the yard gave what it had.

(Knee-high. Modest. "Da snow's almost spent. He's da size of what's left. Dat's not sad. Dat's honest.") The appointments: the old carrot, two coal eyes, and Theo's contribution, solemn: one red mitten, on top.

And the ceremony... because she insisted; because by now the story expects it: "Snowman. You're da last one of dis winter. Thank you to you and all da snows: da fort one, da angel one, da sled hill ones, da quiet-making ones.

You were a good winter. You taught us da whole cold lesson... And when you melt... you know da rule. Da flowers. We'll come say hi in spring. Okay. Dat's da whole speech. Nigh- nigh, winter."

Nigh-nigh, winter. (The season, formally tucked in. By a four- year-old. With full honors. The snowman stood three days, leaning more kindly each one, and went into the ground on a warm Thursday while everyone was at school... politely, off- stage, the way good winters go.

The mitten was retrieved. The carrot was left. "Da ground gets da tip." The ground gets the tip. Winter: thanked. Closed. Loved. The door's open, spring. This family's ready.)
