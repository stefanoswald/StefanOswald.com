Night 325

The Seed Order

Month 11: Winter Wonders

At the farm this week: the season's most hopeful paperwork. The seed order. (The catalog... porch-read, эle circled since autumn, graduating to the kitchen table, the order form, the good pen.) Two practical. One yellow... dat's mine, we're checking if it's real.

Da beans. Da squash. Da carrots, da stubby kind, Grandpa says da soil argues with long ones. And... Da mammof sunflowers. A whole row. You promised. I promised. Write it in." (She wrote it in.

M-a-m-m-o-f. The form is archived. The spelling stands. Burpee filled the order regardless... seed companies, Joe observed, read hope fluently in any spelling.) The envelope: addressed. Stamped. And held, a moment, before the mailbox: "Grandpa.

Dis envelope is weird. It's got da whole summer in it. Da tomatoes, da beans, da sunflower row... dey're all inside dis paper, kind of. We're mailing a letter and getting back a season." Mailing a letter and getting back a season.

"That's farming..." said Joe. "Half dirt, half mail-order hope. Been running on that mix since before my father." The envelope: kissed (per her postal custom, "da marks are brave"), and sent. The summer: ordered.

Deep winter, snow on the fields, frost on the barn... and one envelope heading south with July written inside it in a four-year-old's inventive spelling. Farming. Hope with a stamp on it. The row came up mammoth. The yellow tomatoes: real. Sure enough: worth checking."
