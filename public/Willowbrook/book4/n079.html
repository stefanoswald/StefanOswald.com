Night 79

Why Questions at Preschool Signup

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

A big errand this week: Preschool signup. The real one. The fall one. Forms and a classroom visit. And the visit was done, entirely... in whys: "Why are da cubbies little?" (Kid-sized. Everything here is your-sized.

"My sized?" Your sized.) "Why is dere a easel? Why do da mats live in dat corner?" (Rest time. Everyone rests after lunch. "I don't nap much anymore." Resting counts. Bears may attend. "... Bears may attend." the single most important part in the enrollment packet, confirmed twice.)

"Why do you want to be a teacher?"... aimed point-blank at Miss Dana, the teacher, who crouched to answer it properly: "Because three-and- four-year-olds ask the best questions in the world. Like right now." (Miss Dana.

Note the crouch. Note the answer. She's going to be exactly right. The far pages confirm: she is.) The answer, delivered in the car: "Da school is my-sized. Da teacher likes whys. Bears may attend...

I'm going dere." She's going there. September. With Lily. With the bear, first weeks. The forms went in. The cubby, one of the little ones... has her name's arrow on it by fall. The why-year is going to school, where the whys, Miss Dana promises, are the lesson.

Correct school. Correct kid. September can't come fast enough... and also, per every parent ever, far too fast. Both. Always both.
