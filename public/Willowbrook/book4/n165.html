Night 165

The Show and Tell Debut

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

Friday: Show and tell. Her debut. The selection process: The sparkly rock? The cape? The bear? ("He stays home. School's my job now, mostly.") The choice, finally: The feather. The Duchess's. Speckled, shimmered, impossible. The presentation, per Miss Dana's report at pickup (verbatim, she'd written some down): "Dis is a fevver from da meanest chicken in da world.

Her name is da Duchess. She says no to everything. Nobody ever got a fevver from her. But I gave her a dandelion every day for a whole month. Not to get something. Just because mean ones need more flowers, not less.

And one day dis fevver was just... waiting. At da fence. For me. Dat's da whole story. Da end... Questions?" (Questions: seven. Including Marcus's "can I see da mean chicken"... a farm visit was subsequently arranged; the Duchess received her public with magnificent disdain; Marcus was delighted.)

And Miss Dana's note home, clipped to the feather's return: "In fourteen years of show and tell, the best one I've heard. She taught my whole classroom the flower campaign. Three kids have started their own.

Whatever you're doing at home... keep doing it." Whatever you're doing at home. (Four books of it, Miss Dana. It's all written down.)
