Night 362

The First Night of Four

Month 12: Almost Four

First bedtime of the new age. The list, done from the pillow: "Da room's da same. Da bear's da same." (Confirmed. Job unchanged. Needed different, loved identical.) "Da lamp's da same. Da drawing's dere, hi, Fee-oh.

Da gold light's da same. But I'm different. Four now... Except I don't feel four. I feel like free- plus-one-day." (The oldest birthday discovery. Every age makes it. At the lamp, the family answer, fourth annual delivery:) "That's the secret nobody tells you, love: you never feel the new number on day one.

The number's not a door you walk through. It's a room you furnish. You'll spend the whole year moving things in... new braves, new words, new yets crossed off... and next May you'll look around and the room will be full and it'll feel exactly like four.

Right about then we'll hand you five, empty... Da years are rooms. The years are rooms. And I furnish dem. Nobody else can." (The eyes, going. The grip on the bear, settling. And the last line of the first night of four, mostly asleep:) "...

I'm good at furnishing. Da basement. Da dark. Da whole under-bed... Four's gonna be so full..." Sleep, room- maker. (She was right. But that's tomorrow's shelf. Tonight: the first night's sleep in the new empty room, bear close, lamp low, drawing watching... hands togedder... from the wall.)
