Night 58

The Month of the Walking Bear, Counted

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

Month's end. The check. Countables: Dream-walks: most nights. Missed nights: weathered, by the bear's own law, the arms are the job; the meadow is extra. One brother turned two. (Both candles, first try, self-blown.

The coach remains insufferable. The coach remains correct.) One cow: walking, bell-voiced, allied. One shared sibling dream: confirmed twice. (Twice is everything.) One backstory: told. (Chosen for the flop. The imperfect part was the reason.

The family framed the sentence internally.) One patrol: standing, nightly, perimeter-wide. One friend's meadow: adjacent, confirmed, different door. One whistle-signal: three notes, meadow tonight. One request, one patch: yellow, blanket- sourced, love's receipt made visible.

And one daytime ear- settle in the gold: unproven, unforgotten, kept. Uncountables: That the bear who watched from a dresser, then filled her arms, now walks beside her in a country she builds nightly... and that every word he says there is her own family's love, coming back in fur. That the soft division turned out to be exactly what the first book promised from the first letter: somebody soft, always watching, day or night, seen or not.

"Good month, Baba?" she asked the check's subject, at the lamp. The ear, settled? So far." So far. Remembered and carried forward: walking, on both sides of the willow."
