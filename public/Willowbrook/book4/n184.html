Night 184

The Rained-Out Farm Maybe

Month 7: The Maybe Lessons

Sunday: Pouring. Farm trip: cancelled. The gloom: settling... "... Maybe..." said somebody small, mostly to herself, watching the rain. And at eleven: a truck in the drive. Grandpa. In town. At their door. (. "Farm's just mud today.

Figured I'd come see how town folk live.") Grandpa Joe, who they always go to... Here. In their kitchen. In their fort. Reading the bear book in the big chair Fixing, unasked, per his nature: the squeaky cabinet, the wobbling stool, one drawer nobody had mentioned.

"Da farm came to us..." She marveled. The slip, jar-bound: "Rain broke Sunday. Seemed bad. Gampa came here and read da bear book and fixed free things. Best Sunday inside ever. Maybe caught." (The visit became a winter institution: Rain Sundays = town Sundays.

The truck knows both routes now. The rain, as usual, knew what it was doing.)
