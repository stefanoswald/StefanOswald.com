Night 330

The First Warm Wind

Month 11: Winter Wonders

Porch, this week, after dinner. Light lasting longer now... the solstice promise, paying out daily. And then the wind changed. Mid-porch. Mid- sentence. From the south, soft as a page turn: The first warm wind.

She caught it first. "Papa. Da wind. Feel it. It's... Soft. It's not winter wind. Winter wind bites. Dis one's just... touching. And it smells... what is dat smell?" (The smell: wet ground waking. Green things starting.

Last year's leaves letting go into this year's soil. The whole underneath, stretching.) "Dat's petrichor's cousin! Da rain smell... but dis is da ground smell! Da waking-up one! Does it have a name? If it doesn't..." said Papa. "you know what to do." (She knew what to do. The notebook. The naming: "Da undersmell. When the ground wakes up and breaths out da winter." Saved. The jar's first original entry... not a word collected but a word made, for a thing that needed one.

The collector, turned coiner. The far pages note the date.) They stayed out till full dark, no coats, first time in months, the wind doing its soft new work up and down the lane. "Winter's over, isn't it. Da wind just said." The wind just said.
