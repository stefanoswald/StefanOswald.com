Night 186

The Lost Mitten Maybe

Month 7: The Maybe Lessons

The mitten: lost. (The striped one. Left-hand. Somewhere on four blocks of lane. The string had been retired... big girls, unstrung... and the lane collected its toll.) The mourning: real. ("Da stripes were my favorite.") "...

Maybe." And one week later, on the walk: The fence post. The Hendersons'. And on it, pulled snug over the top: the mitten. Found by somebody. Posted, by the town's oldest custom, visible, where its owner would pass.

Wearing, the week's snow had given it... a tiny white cap. "It's the mitten! The fence kept it! Somebody found it and posted it and da snow gave it a hat..." The slip: "Lost da mitten.

Seemed bad. Da town finded it and posted it and I got to see da lost-and- found fence work real. Da mitten had adventures. Maybe caught." (The mitten was worn the rest of the winter with visible pride.

"Dis one's been out dere..." She'd say. "It knows things." The lost things, in this town, come back seasoned.)
