Night 200

Two Hundred Nights of Book Four

Month 7: The Maybe Lessons

Two hundred nights. Book four's second stone. This hundred held: a school-country, entered and owned. A chart, a porch, a stage, a feather presented to twelve leaning classmates. A quiet friend, found at his speed.

A hamster, mourned under a maple with the right-sized sad. A term of laws, traveling both ways in a red backpack. And a month, this one, still running... of maybes: caught, jarred, franchised, taught to a classroom, played by the sky itself.

The girl at the lamp tonight reads the signs on the page now, points at words she knows: Duck. Mom. The. (The letters are assembling. The reading is coming. Next book's country, visible from this stone.)

"Two hundred..." She counted. "And how many more?" One sixty-five, this book. "And den?" Then book five. "And den?" Then six, then seven. "And den?"... Then you'll read them yourself, love. To whoever you want. "...

Den I'll read dem to fee-oh. And Duck. And my kids someday maybe." (The shelf's whole future, planned at the two- hundredth stone by its own inheritor. As it happened it came true. Every part.

Even the maybe.) Stone: set. Carried forward: half a year deep, reading the signs, planning the succession.
