Night 336

The Annual Interview

Month 12: Almost Four

The fat notebook came out this week. (Papa's. The interview one. Same questions every year, birthday month, since age two. The longitudinal study of one kid, by one father, at one lamp.) Gold. It was red, den yellow, now gold.

Gold's yellow dat means something." (Gold is yellow that means something. The notebook got that verbatim.) "Favorite food? Grilled cheese. Da menu one. Officially forever now. Best friend? Lily. Dat one never changes. Check your book...

Told you. What do you want to be when you grow up? A wever scientist. Or a author. Or da librarian... Can you be all free? Scariest thing dis year? Da deep end. Before. Not after.

Best thing dis year?... Bella coming out. Because I waited all winter and waiting dat long for something dat den happens... dat's da best feeling dere is. Better dan surprises. Surprises are free. Waited- fors are earned."

(Waited-fors are earned. The notebook. Verbatim. Double underline.) And the last question, the closer, every year the same: "Anything you want to tell Four?... Tell Four: I got everything ready for you. Da reading.

Da brave list. Da friends. Da meadow. You're welcome. Don't waste it." Don't waste it. Age three's last telling. The notebook closed soft. The far pages hold interviews through age seventeen... she never once skipped it, even the years she pretended it was babyish. Especially those."
