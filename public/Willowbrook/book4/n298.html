Night 298

The Poem That Happened

Month 10: Words Are Magic

Rain, this week. Window weather. And at the glass, watching it, half to herself: "Da rain goes tap. Da rain goes run. Da gutters drink until dey're done. Mama. Write that. Quick. It came out with a... a bounce in it and it's gonna escape."

Written. Quick. (Word jar paper. The good pen. Read back aloud... and there it was, unmistakable: rhythm. Rhyme. The bounce.) "What is dat? When words come out with da bounce? That's a poem, love. You just made your first poem...

I didn't make it. It happened. I was just saying da rain and it came out in da shape." It came out in the shape. (Poets have described it exactly this way for three thousand years.

None better.) The follow-up questions, rapid: "Do more just happen? Can you make dem happen? Why did tap and run feel like cousins? Is da bounce in me or in da words?" The poem: saved in the jar... then reconsidered, retrieved, and given its own envelope.

("Poems aren't words. Dey're words holding hands. Dey need a bigger house.") The envelope filled. Got a sibling. The poem era: open. (Rain poems. Theo poems. A Duchess poem... savage; suppressed by its author as "too true to publish."

The far pages hold hundreds. But the first one is the first one: tap, run, gutters drinking. It happened, at a window, to a four-year- old who had the sense to yell for a pen. Every poet's origin story. Word for word.)
