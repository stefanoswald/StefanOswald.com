Night 179

The Winter Sing

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

Term one closed with the winter sing. (The whole school. All three rooms. Families packed in coats. The multi-purpose room at maximum purpose.) The program: the season's songs... the snow ones, the candle ones, the light-in-the- dark ones from every tradition in the room..."

Because. "per Miss Dana's welcome, "winter is when every family lights something. Tonight we sing them all." (Every family lights something. The room, singing each other's lights. The town, kid-sized, doing the town's whole work.)

Her class sang the snow song. (Twelve kids. Paper snowflakes. Her solo line... assigned by lottery, kept secret per show rules: "and da dark is just da day resting"... Her own line. From her month.

Miss Dana had asked permission to use it. The law, institutionalized, sung by twelve voices to two hundred people while real snow fell past the windows on theatrical cue.) The applause. The cocoa after. The drive home through the falling white, both kids asleep by the second block, the moment folded and saved, the year's first candles lit in every window on the lane.

"Good term?" whispered Papa, carrying her in. From the shoulder, mostly asleep: "...best town yet. So far." So far. Term one: complete. Their bedtime world: hers. The dark: just the day resting... on the story now, in the school's own songbook, forever.
