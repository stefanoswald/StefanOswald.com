Night 220

Helping Mama Rest

Month 8: Helping Hands

When Mama got the flu, Papa handled the medicine, meals, and phone calls. Olivia and Theo looked for the smaller things they could carry.

They set the table. Olivia watered the fern. Theo delivered a drawing of a purple cow. Together they practiced being quiet outside the bedroom door, which was their hardest job.

At lunchtime Papa filled a tray with soup and tea. Olivia carried the napkin. Theo carried one spoon in both hands.

Mama was awake but tired.

"We can sit without talking," Olivia offered.

For five minutes they did. Theo leaned against the bed. Olivia watched steam rise from the tea.

"This helps," Mama said.

Two days later she came downstairs in her robe. The children tried to give her every job back at once.

"Not yet," Papa said. "Getting better is still her work."

So Olivia watered the fern again. Helping did not mean pretending to be the grown-up. It meant noticing the useful piece that was truly hers to do.
