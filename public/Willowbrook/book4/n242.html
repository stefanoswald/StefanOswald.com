Night 242

What Counts as Work

Month 8: Helping Hands

At the end of helping month, Olivia watered the fern and set five plates on the table without being reminded.

"I used to think real work had to be large," she told Papa. "Like fixing a roof or driving a snowplow."

"What do you think now?"

Olivia looked around the kitchen. Theo was arranging spoons, mostly in the right places. Mama, still recovering from the flu, rested in the next room. Their boots were dry because someone had lined them by the heater.

"Some work is just seeing what needs doing before it becomes a problem."

That month she had shoveled beside the snow crew, worn an apron for an afternoon shift, sorted tools, earned coins for extra jobs, and learned that helping a sick person could mean sitting quietly.

The fern's leaves shone after its drink.

"Also," Olivia added, "work does not always pay money. Sometimes it pays in everybody being less tired."

Papa handed her the last plate. "That is a very useful kind of wealth."

They carried dinner to the table together.
