The Courage Lend-Out Tally book

Month 9: Brave Is Scared Plus One Step

A pattern, noticed at the lamp, month's edge: "You know what's funny, Mama? Dis was my brave month. But half my braves were... Other people's." The lend-out tally book, tallied: The recruit: briefed. (The Duchess scale.

The count. Shot day, survived by proxy rule.) Lily: the ceiling tour. Marcus: the ramp alliance. (Courage to come back to blocks he'd been frozen out of... lent by the freezer herself, thawed public.) The circle room: the raised hand.

(Courage lent to a whole room... eleven kids who watched wrong get corrected kindly and saved it as possible.) And Theo: daily. "Why does lending brave make mine bigger? Dat's backwards mafs." And the answer... this month's whole economics: "Because courage isn't a jar, love.

It's a muscle. Kindness taught you that about kindness. Same law here: lending is the rep. Every brief, every hand-under- hand, every ceiling tour... you're lifting double: their scared and yours. The lenders end up strongest.

It's the only bank where loans grow the lender." The only bank where loans grow the lender. The tally book: balanced. Backwards. Perfectly. Lend on, little one. Compound daily.
