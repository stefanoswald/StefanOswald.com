Night 145

Grandpa's Two-Kid Sunday

Month 5: Little Brother Tags Along

Sunday. Both kids. No parents. (The just-us morning, parents' edition... a first. The farm volunteered. The farm always volunteers.) And Grandpa Joe ran his double Sunday like the conductor he's secretly been all along: Egg duty: Her.

(Six eggs, counted, carried, zero casualties.) Weed patrol: Him. (New recruit. The "weeds" he pulled included two actual weeds, one carrot top, replanted, mostly, and a stick. "Good initiative..." ruled the conductor. "We'll work on targeting.")

The barrow: Shared. (One hauls, one rides. Rotating. The fights over whose turn: refereed at rocking-chair speed. "Time's up when I finish dis verse." He whistles verses. The verses are exactly turn- length. Forty years of refereeing in one whistle.)

Lunch: enormous. Naps: achieved, both, simultaneously... ("Farm air..." He told the parents at pickup, smug as a Duchess. "Works every time. No charge.") And the porch, at golden hour, before the car came: the two of them on the two star chairs, side by side, milk mustaches matching, while the old man rocked and watched his porch and said, to the farm at large, at the moment: "Well now.

Look at my whole point, sittin' right dere." His whole point. Sitting right there. Two chairs. Two stars. The farm's succession: seated.
