Night 322

The Blanket Fort Library

Month 11: Winter Wonders

A week of bitter cold kept everyone indoors, so Olivia built a library beneath the dining-room table.

Six blankets made the walls. Clothespins held the roof. A shoe rack became the shelf, and every book in the house was sorted by color.

"The real library uses the alphabet," Olivia said, "but colors make books look happy."

She drew cards for the whole family. Story hour happened twice a day. The reference desk opened whenever Olivia was awake. Theo's card developed a bite mark but remained valid.

The main rule was whispering.

"Books like quiet," Olivia explained. "They are listening to themselves."

The fort stood for five days and lent eleven books. It charged three hug-fines for late returns.

On the sixth day the family needed the chairs back.

As Olivia removed the last blanket, Theo asked whether the library was gone.

"The blankets are gone," she said. "The library is the idea."

They left one pillow, one lamp, and a basket of books in the corner. The first Willowbrook branch library had found a permanent address.
