Night 9

The Wrong Costume Box

Month 1: Pretend!

This week, the family built setup: The dress-up box. An old chest. Filled with retired treasure: Mama's worn-out scarves. Papa's old hat. The bee costume, outgrown but beloved. One cape. (Sewn by the aunts, overnight-mailed the moment they heard the box existed.

Red. Obviously.) Sunglasses missing one lens. A crown from some cake. Rain boots, retired. A vest. And the first raid produced the year's defining look: the cape, plus the bee wings, plus the firefighter rain boots, plus the one-lens sunglasses.

"Who are you today?" The answer, delivered with total conviction: "Everybody." Everybody. Not a pirate or a fairy or a firefighter... all of it, at once, because three-year-olds haven't learned yet that you're supposed to pick one.

(Don't teach her that. Nobody teach her that. The whole magic of this year is that the categories haven't closed. She can be everybody. For now, for this one wide-open year, she is.) The box never closed properly again. Something was always half out of it. Somebody was always half becoming something else.
