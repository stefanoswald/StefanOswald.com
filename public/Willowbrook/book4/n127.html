Night 127

The Two-Kid Errand

Month 5: Little Brother Tags Along

The errand nobody takes lightly: market, two kids, one grown-up. And the senior partner ran convoy: Her jobs, self-assigned: the list. The counting: "Five apples. I'll count. Fee-oh, you hold da bag open."

(A job for him. Always a job for him. Jobs prevent knocking, the fort law, portable.) The convoy discipline: "Fee-oh. Hand on da cart. Dat's da rule. I invented it just now but it's ancient."

(Hand on the cart: invented on the spot, enforced forever after, adopted by half the bench families by month's end. Some laws are instant classics.) The crust ends at Rosa's counter: Two now, by the standing order's new rule.

("Two customers..." ruled Rosa. "Best ones I got.") And at checkout, to the saintly clerk, the report: "We got everything on da list and nobody got losted and he only licked one apple." (One apple.

Purchased, obviously. Licked goods convert to owned goods. Market law, also ancient.) "Smoothest two-kid run I've seen all week," said the clerk. It was. It runs smoother every year... the convoy tightening, the jobs distributing, the little civilization of a family learning to move through the world as a unit, hand on the cart, list in pictures, everybody counted, all the way home.
