Night 318

The Icicle Orchestra

Month 11: Winter Wonders

The eaves, this week: hung with icicles. (The full set. Finger-length to yardstick. The good winter's whole glass percussion section, waiting.) The discovery... accidental, per the best ones: a mitten-brush, passing, and: Tink.

(A pause. The scientific pause. The one this family knows means an hour is about to happen:) tink. Tonk. Tunk. "Papa. Dey're all different. Da little ones sing high and da big ones sing low...

It's a orchestra. Winter grew us a orchestra." The concert: performed. (Mitten-mallet. Gentle, "you have to play dem soft or dey quit"... a scale, found; a tune, hunted; three notes of the hello song, approximated, to shrieking delight.)

And the physics, dusk-grade, between movements: "Why do da big ones sing low? Big things shake slow. Slow shakes make low sounds. Same reason Papa's voice is lower than yours, bigger strings. So da icicle's size is its voice...

Den da orchestra retunes itself every night! Da drips make dem longer and da longer makes dem lower, winter's orchestra never plays da same twice!" Never the same twice. (The notebook got it all: "Icicls.

Winters piano. Grows its own keys. Retoons nitely. Play soft or dey quit.") The orchestra played till the thaw took it, note by note, key by key, going out the way it came: one drip at a time, music to the last.
