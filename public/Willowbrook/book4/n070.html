Night 70

The Why That Was Really a Worry

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

Some whys aren't whys. This week's clue: the same why, over and over: "Why do ambulances go fast?" Next day: "Why was da ambulance at da blue house?" (Somebody needed help. They got it.) Next: "Why do people need ambulances?"

Then, at bedtime, quietly: "Do mamas ever go in ambulances?" There it is. (The question behind the questions. There usually is one, when a why repeats. The ambulance had passed the park Tuesday. The siren had been loud.

The worry had been burrowing since.) The lamp, brought to it: "You're not really asking about ambulances, are you. You're asking: what if somebody I love needs one." The nod. Small. The real one. "Here's the whole truth: bodies sometimes need big fast help.

That's why ambulances exist, so help always comes. Everyone you love is healthy tonight. And if anyone ever needed help, the town is full of helpers. That siren isn't the sound of scary. It's the sound of help, running."

The sound of help, running. "... Dat's a better sound." Same siren. New file. The whys stopped that night. The worry, named, unburrowed, held.
