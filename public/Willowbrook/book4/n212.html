Night 212

The Watch Catches One Too

Month 7: The Maybe Lessons

At month's end, the first stars appeared above the Great Willow. And a cloudy night... thick, low, starless. Stella: grounded above the cloud deck, unseeable from below. "A whole night off the watch..." She grumbled.

"The town can't see a single one of us. Seems..." She stopped. The word arrived... learned, like the hello song, from below: "... Maybe." And the Moon, humming through the cloud she silvered... turned the night over by the game's own rules: "Mmm."

(Look down, colleague. Look what a starless night does.) Below the clouds: the town, lit entirely by itself. The lane's windows, golder for no competition. The lantern, brightest thing in the world tonight. The porch lights, the school's one glow, a truck's beams sweeping home to the farm... the whole town's own light, visible the way it never is under stars: alone, sufficient, warm.

"On clear nights..." Hummed the Moon, in her way. "they look up at us. On cloudy ones... they look at each other's windows. Both are the watch. Theirs runs on cloudy nights." The slip, had the sky a jar... was composed by Stella, formally: "Clouded out.

Seemed bad. Saw the town light itself. The watch below works. Maybe caught. Whoo..." Approved the owl. "The game scales." The game scales. All the way up. Goodnight, cloud- lit town. Your windows held the shift tonight. The sky, resting, watched you watch. Both. As ever. Both.
