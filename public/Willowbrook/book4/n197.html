Night 197

The Torn Page Maybe

Month 7: The Maybe Lessons

It finally happened. The bear book... five years served, three hundred-plus readings, soft as cloth... Tore. The button page. The page. Right through the gasp. (Theo's turn-taking. Enthusiasm. The page was tired. Nobody's fault.

Everybody's grief.) "...maybe," she said. Wobbly. And the maybe was: The surgery. Done at the kitchen table with full ceremony: the tape, the alignment, the smoothing. And the discovery, mid-operation: the tear, taped, showed.

A scar. A visible seam, right through the button. "It's ruined... Look again. It's healed. There's a difference. Ruined things get thrown away. Healed things have stories. Now the book has a scar from being loved too hard... which is the best scar there is.

Ask the bear's paw. Ask his heart. Ask anybody patched." The slip: "Da bear book tore. Seemed like da end. Now it's healed with a loved-too-hard scar, like Baba's patches. Books can be patched.

Everything loved can. Caught." (The scar became the reading's new ritual: every button-gasp, one finger traced the seam. "Dis is where we loved it too hard." The tear made the book more. Loved things work that way. It's their whole economy.)
