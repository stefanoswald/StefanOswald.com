Night 281

Words That Don't Behave

Month 10: Words Are Magic

Book two of the early readers introduced the criminals. "Papa. Dis word. T-h-e. I sounded it. It should say 'tuh-huh-eh.' But Mama says it says 'Da.' Dat's not what the letters say.

Da word is lying." The hard truth, delivered at the table: "Some words don't follow the rules. The oldest words... the little ones everybody uses most, they've been said so many times for so many hundreds of years, they wore down.

Like river stones. The. Was. Of. Said. They don't sound out. You just have to... know them. On sight. They're called sight words. So da rules have... Exceptions. English is mostly exceptions holding hands." The response... entirely in character: a wanted poster.

(Drawn that evening. Fridge gallery, briefly; her door, permanently: "Wanted: The word 'The.' Crimes: Lying about its letters. Reward: Knowing it anyway." Below: the word the, drawn with a tiny bandit mask.) The gang grew weekly.

Was joined it. Of, "da worst one, it says uv, dat's not even close"... got its own poster. And the system worked: outlaws, once posted, got recognized on sight. (Which is, note, the entire pedagogical goal.

Miss Dana, shown the poster method, adopted it for the whole class. The bandits of English, rounded up one poster at a time, by a sheriff who learned the deepest rule of her language early: love the rules, know the outlaws, read on regardless.)
