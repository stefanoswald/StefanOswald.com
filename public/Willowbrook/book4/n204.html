Night 204

The Rained Recital Maybe

Month 7: The Maybe Lessons

The spring sing... planned for the schoolyard, under the maple, families on blankets... got rained. Moved inside. The gym. Folding chairs. The gloom, class- wide: "da blankets were da whole point...maybe," said one small voice in the line.

And the maybe: The echo. The gym, big, hard-walled, empty- cornered, took twelve small voices and made them enormous. (The vault effect. The library's old acoustics lesson, institutional scale. First note of the first song and twelve kids heard themselves fill a building... and stood up straighter, and sang.)

"We sounded like a thousand kids..." Came the report. "Da rain moved us into da big sound. Outside, da sky eats your voice. Inside, da walls give it back." The slip: "Rain ate da blanket sing.

Seemed bad. Da gym made us sound like a thousand. Da walls give your voice back. Caught." (The sing stayed indoors by choice thereafter, sunny years included. The maybe became the tradition. They often do. Check your own traditions: half of them are old maybes, wearing tenure.)
