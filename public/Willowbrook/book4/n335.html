Night 335

The Countdown Chain, Fourth Edition

Month 12: Almost Four

The chain. (The birthday countdown. Annual. Paper. One link torn per morning. Third edition was Mama-built. This year) "I build it myself. I can cut and I can write now. Da chain's mine dis year."

Thirty links. Self-cut. And, the innovation, hers: Every link gets a word. ("Da year had words in it. Da chain should too. When I tear each one, I get da word back for a day.")

The words, sampled, link by link, in inventive spelling: Medow. Theo. Skool. Maybe. Brav. Deep end. Wisper. Fusstated. Bella. Green room. Sno. Mammof. Pome. Liberry card. Grild cheez. (Thirty words. The year, chained. A calendar made of what happened... which is, note, what calendars were always supposed to be.)

The hanging: doorframe, traditional height, reachable for the daily tearing. And the first link, torn at breakfast next morning... Medow... read aloud, savored: "Da meadow. Dat was da year's first big thing. Da udder side of da willow...

Thirty days, den four." Thirty days, then four. The chain swayed in the doorway draft all month, getting shorter, giving one word back every morning... the year unspooling in reverse, day by day, into the birthday. Best calendar this family ever kept. All the others just count. This one remembers.
