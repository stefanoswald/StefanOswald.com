Night 314

The Mitten Tree

Month 11: Winter Wonders

At the library this week: The mitten tree. (Annual. By the lantern. An evergreen hung not with ornaments but with mittens, hats, scarves... donated, taggless, taken freely by any cold hands that need them. The librarian calls it "the only tree that gets emptier and warmer.")

The explanation, absorbed: "Some kids' hands get cold and their houses can't fix it this year. So the town hangs warm things here. No asking. No names. Cold hands come; warm hands leave." The response: immediate.

"I have da stripey mittens and da red ones. Dat's too many mittens for one me." But then, at home, at the drawer, the catch: the stripey ones are the favorites. (The big think: real.

Logged honestly. She sat with the drawer open a long time. Giving away the spares is arithmetic. Giving away the favorites is the other thing.) The decision, delivered at the tree next visit: the stripey ones.

Hung personally. High branch. "Why the favorites?" Mama asked, gently, after. "Because da kid whose hands are cold... dey should get da best warm. Not da leftover warm. If it's worth giving, it's worth giving da good one."

(The helping month taught quiet help. The winter teaches this: the gift that counts is the one you feel leave. Nobody taught her that line. The drawer taught her. The long sit taught her.) Two weeks later: a kid at the park.

Stripey mittens. Snowball mid-flight. She saw. Said nothing... by the tree's whole law. Glowed the entire walk home. Anonymous is the biggest size of warm. The tree knows. Now she does too.
