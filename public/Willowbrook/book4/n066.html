Night 66

The Why Nobody Could Answer

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

This week produced the question: "Why is dere anything instead of nothing?" Mama tried. ("... That's the biggest question there is, love.") Papa tried. ("Scientists and wise people have wondered that forever.") The honest bottom: "Nobody knows.

Maybe you'll find out." But the question wouldn't settle... and on the Sunday willow walk, at the hill, she took it to the top: The owl was on his branch. (Dusk. He's often there at dusk.

The children see him plainly. Grown-ups see "an owl.") "Oliver. Why is dere anything instead of nothing?" And the owl, who speaks only in questions, whose office is exactly this, blinked his slow blink and answered per his order: "Whoo... and if there were nothing... who would be here to ask why?"...

She stood very still. The gears, visibly turning. "... Da asking needs da anything. Whoo..." said the owl, which meant: closer than most philosophers get, and they take decades. "So da anything is so dere can be... askers?"

The owl ruffled, settled, and gave her the last question, the one she'd carry years: "Whoo knows... but isn't it interesting... that the universe made something that wonders about the universe?" She walked down the hill holding Papa's hand very quietly. "Da owl is smart..."

She said at last. The owl is old, said Papa. "Same thing?" Sometimes, little one. When the old ones keep asking. That's the whole trick of it.
