Night 241

The Quiet Thank-You

Month 8: Helping Hands

Month's end. And the mailbox, flag up, nobody saw by whom... held an envelope. Small. Unsigned. Her name on it, in careful old- fashioned writing. Inside: one gold star sticker. And two words: "We noticed."

That's all. No name. No return address. (The suspects: endless. The Hendersons. The grandmother. Mr. Petrov. The clerk. The fire station. Rosa. The whole noticed, noticing town. They investigated with declined. Some envelopes are like the willow's glow... whys you keep.)

"Somebody saw da secret column," she whispered, holding the star. Somebody saw. (Everybody saw. That's the thing about towns like this one, the invisible list runs both directions. You think you're the only one keeping rounds.

Then one day the mailbox tells you: the town keeps rounds on you. It noticed the shoveling and the carrying and the sock-footed checks. It notices everything. It always did. That's what all those windows are for.)

The star went on the brave list's corner, a document now decorated by the town it was built in. The envelope went to the harbor. "We noticed." Two words. This month's whole wage, paid in full, anonymous, gold.
