Night 323

Valentines for Everybody

Month 11: Winter Wonders

Ms. Dana gave the class one rule for Valentine's Day: if anyone brought cards, they must bring one for every classmate.

"No exceptions," Olivia told the family. "It is a good rule."

The kitchen table became a card workshop. Olivia folded paper and cut half-hearts along the crease. When she opened them, each half became whole.

Theo managed the glitter on a baking sheet. Most of it stayed there. Some reached his hair, his socks, and one mysterious spot on the ceiling.

Together they made thirteen cards.

Marcus received a red ramp-shaped heart. Wren's card had no glitter and only a few quiet words because Olivia knew she disliked things that shouted.

After the class set was finished, Olivia made one more card for home.

YOU ARE MY BEST WARM, she wrote.

On Valentine's morning every child placed a card in every paper bag. Nobody watched the pile beside someone else grow larger than their own.

The rule was small enough for a preschool room and large enough for the world Olivia hoped to live in: when kindness was being handed out, everyone belonged.
