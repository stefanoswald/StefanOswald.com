Night 271

The Brave Teacher

Month 9: Brave Is Scared Plus One Step

Month's-end scene, the park, witnessed: The little slide. And at its base: Theo. Two-and-a-half. Wanting up. Scared up. The deadlock... the exact deadlock, one generation later, same ladder. And the professor: "Fee-oh.

Secret. You don't do da whole ladder. Dat's too big. You do one step. Dat's da whole job today. One step is a win... One? One. I'll be right here. Braves are counted in steps, not tops."

One step. Taken. Celebrated like a moon landing. "Dat counts? That counts. It's da rule. Tomorrow maybe two. Da top waits. Waiting is da top's whole job." (Verbatim. The catcher took his place. The counting began.

His top came nine days later... one step at a time, a catcher at the bottom the whole way, the wind-in-teeth grin at the end of the whoosh an exact genetic reprint of hers.) this month closes here, on purpose: every brave in this book was taught to her by someone. The formula: Mama's. The engineering: Papa's.

The looking-rule: Joe's. The watching-brave: Wren's. And now the whole lesson, running downhill to the next student, professor aged four. Brave is scared plus one step. Pass it on. That's the last rule of the kit: it only keeps what it gives.
