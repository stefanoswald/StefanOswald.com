Night 178

What School Gave Back

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

December. Term one: nearly done. And at the lamp, the list... what the school- country gave back: Wren. The chart. (Fair means waiting some days and shining others. Portable law. Applied at home weekly.)

The timer treaty. (Self-governance at the easel. No grown-up even came.) The porch, self- deployed. The chime. The stage. The hard ones too: day three's tears. The easel fight and its joint painting.

Buttons, under the maple, and the vocabulary no one wants and everyone needs. And the thing under all of it, said at the lamp, half-asleep: "School is like... da town, but kid-sized. It's got a bench and a chart and storms and repairs and a Rosa...

Miss Dana. And everybody learns everybody's weird and likes dem anyway. It's a town. I got two towns now." Two towns. The girl who practiced preschool on a rug last spring owns their bedtime world by December.

They always do. The rehearsals work. The rule held: the mamas came back. Every day. All term. Documented.
