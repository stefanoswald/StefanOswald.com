Night 345

The Invitations

Month 12: Almost Four

The party: nine days out. The invitations: due. And this year... first year... the invitations are written. By hand. Her hand. All thirteen. (Three evenings' work at the kitchen table. The good markers. Mama on spelling consult, used sparingly; da author has her own system and the system is respected.)

The base text: "Please come to my for party. There will be cake and games and a story corner. Saturday. Luv Olivia." Each one personalized to its reader: Lily's: "Yu already no evryfing but this makes it ofishal."

June's: a bug drawn in the corner. Wren's: small letters, quiet colors, and: "Yu can just watch the games if yu want. Waching is playing." (Her own law, handed back to its author. As it happened Wren kept that invitation.

Years.) Marcus's: "Bring yor ramps. Da towers need yu." And the thirteenth, addressed beyond the class list: "Mr petrov. Menders come to partys now. Its a new rule. I made it. Saturday. Luv Olivia." (He came.

He fixed the wobbling picnic table while he was there, out of habit, and ate two slices of the sky. New rule: agreed.) The delivery: cubbies, hand to hand, the postal round of her life so far.

"Thirteen letters. All different. All mine." Know your reader. Sign your work. Invite the menders. The party was already perfect, nine days out, on paper. The rest was just cake.
