Night 245

The Deep End Crossing

Month 9: Brave Is Scared Plus One Step

Two summers of shallow work. The rope: watched. The order: honored. ("The skills go over the rope before the swimmer does.") And this month, at lessons, the teacher said the words: "She's ready. Rope day."

Rope day. The crossing, as done: The skills, inventoried aloud at the rope: "I can float. I can kick- swim da whole shallow. I can blow bubbles and grab da wall. Da skills crossed already.

I'm just... following dem." The teacher: alongside. Mama: at the rope, watching. And the crossing: one push off the wall, into the deep, where the bottom is a rumor... kick, kick, reach... The far wall.

Deep water: crossed. By a swimmer whose skills went first, by the order, per two years of shallow patience. "How deep was it under me?" Nine feet. "And I didn't touch any of it!" That's swimming, little one.

The whole magic: the deep doesn't matter once the skills carry you. (True of water. True of everything. The rope-line law, graduating its first swimmer: yet-lines are crossed by skills, on schedule, almost boring... which is how you know the order held.)

The bounce-out, dripping, glowing: "What's the next rope?" There's always a next rope. That's the good news. That was always the good news.
