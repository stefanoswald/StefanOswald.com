Night 236

Willowbrook Cleanup Day

Month 8: Helping Hands

On town cleanup day, families gathered at the pond with gloves, bags, and long grabbing tools.

Olivia and Lily worked along the park path. They found wrappers, a bent bottle cap, one blue hair clip, and a mitten that was too good to throw away.

The mitten went on the lost-and-found table. The litter went into the proper bags. The marble Olivia found beneath a bench went into her pocket only after Mr. Petrov said it had probably been waiting there for years.

Theo carried an almost-empty bag and called himself the light-trash specialist.

At noon everyone ate soup and bread at long tables beside the pond. The park looked much as it had that morning, only cleaner around the edges.

"It is a lot of work for a small difference," Olivia said.

Mrs. Hollis nodded toward the crowded tables. "Small difference, many hands. That is how a town takes care of itself."

On the walk home, Olivia noticed every clean stretch of path. Someone had bent down for each one.
