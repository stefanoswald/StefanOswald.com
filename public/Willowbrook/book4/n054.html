Night 54

The Far Gate

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

At the meadow's furthest edge, past the firefly field, past where the patrol turns back... she saw it this week: a gate. Far off. Golden. Small with distance. "Baba. What's dat?" And the bear, who answers everything, who narrates the whole country freely... went quiet a moment.

"Da far gate..." He said at last. "It's... for later. I'm not allowed past it yet. Nobody soft is, till it's time. Time for what?... Little one, some doors keep deir own calendars. Dat one's years off.

Years. And when it matters, I'll tell you everything. I promise. Bears keep promises, it's most of what we're made of. For now: it's just a gate, far away, gold, and not today." Not today.

(She let it go... mostly. Three-year-olds hold mysteries lighter than grown-ups do. The gate is for that. The gate has always been for that. Not today. Years off. But on the map now, small and gold, at the edge of everything... the way the biggest doors always are.)

They walked back toward the bright center, paw in hand. "Race you to da brook," said the bear. They raced. He let her win. Some things, on every side of every willow, never change at all.
