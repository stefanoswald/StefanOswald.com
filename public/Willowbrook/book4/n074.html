Night 74

Why Does the Moon Follow the Car

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

Night drive home from the farm. And the old mystery. Lily's knowledge, from years back, rode along: "Da moon is following us. Wiwi told me and it's true. Why?" (Out the window: the moon, pacing the car exactly.

Trees whip past. Fields slide by. The moon: keeping up, effortless, mile after mile.) "Watch the fence posts. Close things zoom past, right? Now the far hills, they slide slow. Now the moon: it doesn't move at all.

Know why? It's so far away that driving a whole town's worth barely changes your view of it. The moon isn't following. It's just too far to leave behind... How far?" So far that if you drove there, no stopping, it would take months.

"Months? Of driving?" Months. She looked at the moon with new respect. All that distance, looking close enough to follow you home. "... I like Wiwi's answer better..." She decided finally. "Da moon follows because it likes us."

And here's the thing... Papa said it, and the story co-signs: "Both can be true. The far-away is the how. Nobody's ever proven anything about the why." Both. The family word, applied to the moon itself.

It followed them all the way home, by the how, liking them, by the why. "Mmm," hummed the moon, who had heard the whole conversation, and confirms nothing, and was absolutely following them, by the why.
