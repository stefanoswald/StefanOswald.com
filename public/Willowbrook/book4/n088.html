Night 88

Why Does the Day Have to End

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

This month's last why, at the dusk window, gold going blue: "Why does da day hafta end? I wasn't done with it." (The best days end hardest. Today was a best one: the fence, the dough, the blanket, the star.

And now... folding. Unfinished, per its owner.) "Can I tell you a secret about endings? The day isn't being taken away. It's being kept. Right now, look... the light's folding it up. Like laundry. Like the map into the atlas.

The day goes into the night crew's hands, and they file every piece of it, the wag and the bread- breath and the twinkle, into forever-shelves inside you. Days that never ended couldn't be kept. They'd just run on, unfiled, unfolded, lost.

The ending is the keeping, love. That's the whole why of it." The ending is the keeping. She watched the last gold get folded behind the Hendersons' roof. "... Den fold it good..." She instructed the dusk.

"Da whole day. Don't lose da bread part." The dusk, presumably, complied. (It has never once lost a bread part. The shelves inside her hold every crumb of this entire book. That's what the shelves are for.)

"Ready to be saved?" asked Mama, at the stairs. Ready. Bath, book, song... the folding ritual, performed. The day, kept whole. The keeper, kept in it. Goodnight, unfinished and complete. Both, as ever. Both.
