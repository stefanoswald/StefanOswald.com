Night 1

The Spoon That Was a Dragon

Month 1: Pretend!

It started at breakfast on an ordinary Tuesday. Olivia picked up the wooden spoon, held it above her head, and discovered that it was not a spoon at all.

"Rawr! This is a dragon. He is flying over the jam mountains!"

The dragon banked around the toast, skimmed the rim of Papa's cup, and landed on the butter dish to rest his wings.

Mama moved the butter knife carefully around him. "Is he a friendly dragon?"

"He is shy," Olivia decided. "He only rawrs at jam."

Papa offered him a crumb. The dragon accepted, which proved that dragons eat crumbs when nobody has remembered to make them breakfast.

By three, Olivia could walk easily between the ordinary world and the one she made herself. A spoon could still stir porridge, but it could also cross a dangerous mountain range before nine o'clock.

At lunch, the dragon became a spoon again. Mostly. Every so often Olivia opened the cutlery drawer and heard a very small rawr from the back.
