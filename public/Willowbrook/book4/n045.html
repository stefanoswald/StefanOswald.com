Night 45

Baba's Backstory

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

A meadow question, long overdue: "Baba. Where were you before me?" And the bear told his backstory. (Every bear has one. Few get asked. Fewer get to answer.) "A shelf. A store shelf, in da town.

Rows of us. All waiting. Dat's what bears do first: wait. Some wait short. Some wait long. Da bear next to me waited two years... went to a boy up da valley; writes sometimes; he's fine.

Me, I waited four months. And den a man with round glasses came in, looking scared and happy at da same time, da new-papa look, we know it on sight... and he picked me up and checked my ear and said, 'Dis one. Da flopped ear.

She'll like dat he's not perfect.'" (... Papa chose him for the flopped ear. The destiny ear. Chosen because imperfect. The story pauses to let that land where it lands.) "And den? Den a dresser. A year of watching you sleep.

Den your arms. Den...walking. Best career a bear ever had. And da whole thing started with a flop dat made me different." The flop that made him different. She held his real ear extra gently that night. The imperfect, chosen part. The reason, it turns out, all along.
