Night 360

The Last Night of Three

Month 12: Almost Four

The last night. (Of three. The birthday: tomorrow. The willow walk: tomorrow. Mama said, tomorrow. Tonight: the family's quietest tradition... The thanking of the year.) At the lamp, the bear in attendance, the routine hers now, learned from three prior editions: "Okay, Free.

Dis is your last night, so I'm saying it all. Thank you for: Da meadow. You gave me da udder side of da willow first thing... dat was a good hello. Fee-oh's words. You made him a talker.

Da whole school. Miss Dana. Wren. Marcus. Da cubby next to Lily. Da jar going to school and da whole town turning out to be jars. Da helping month. Da brave month. The deep end...

Free, da deep end was your best big one. Da letters. Da reading. Free, you were da year da world started talking to me in marks. I'll never be un-able to read aden. Dat's yours forever.

And Bella. Da green room. Da whole winter of keeping company. Da coming out bigger." The lamp held. "... You were da biggest year so far, Free. Dey keep being da biggest year so far. Papa says dat's da trick of years.

Okay. Dat's everything. You can go be a was-year now. Da frame keeps your line. Da folder keeps your pictures. I keep... all of it. Nigh-nigh, Free." Nigh-nigh, Three. (The lamp, lowered.

And the last thought, at the edge of sleep, barely voiced:) "... Four starts in da morning... I'm ready. I got ready all year." She got ready all year. (They always do, little ones. That's what the years are for. Sleep now. Tomorrow the tree writes.)
