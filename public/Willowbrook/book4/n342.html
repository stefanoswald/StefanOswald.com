Night 342

The Hand-Me-Down Box

Month 12: Almost Four

The closet check, pre-birthday, annual: the outgrown things, sorted. And this year, a destination with a name: June's baby sister. (Born last month. The crooked-square blanket baby... June's own bee costume era beginning.

The trio's first satellite member.) The box, packed... and in this family, packing means telling; nothing leaves without its story said: The yellow shirt: "Da paint one. Da spill dat became da Spill Garden's first flower is on here.

See? Dat's not a stain. Dat's history... I'll tell June to tell her." The overalls: "Farm ones. Duchess-approved. Da pocket held a feaver a whole summer." The red boots, size small: "Puddle veterans. Museum window veterans.

Dey know da whole lane. Dey'll teach her feet." (Each thing: folded. Storied. Sent on. The sweater-becomes- the-hat law, scaled to a wardrobe: nothing loved gets lost. It gets a next kid.) And at the bottom, considered long, placed last: the bee costume.

(The bee costume. Hers, then Theo's, antennae resprung twice, the family's most decorated garment.) "... Da bee retires here..." She ruled. "Fee-oh's done with it and da baby should start with da best thing. Bees know da way.

Dat's da whole point of bees." The box: delivered. Porch of June's house. The note, self-written, taped on top: "For the baby. Evryfing works. The storys come with. Ask june. Luv Olivia." The stories come with.

(They did. The far pages catch the bee costume twice more, on kids this record hasn't met yet. Still flying. Still knowing the way. Some garments never retire. They just keep getting smaller childhoods to hold.)
