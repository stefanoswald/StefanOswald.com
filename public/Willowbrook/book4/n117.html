Night 117

Tucking In the Dark

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

This month's last ordinary night. And at the window, before the curtain, a new addition to the goodnight rounds... her own invention, unprompted: "Night-night, dark. You can rest now. Da day is folding. Thanks for holding da fireflies and da stars and da night-shift town.

Thanks for being fuller dan you look. Da gold light will keep you company. No spookies... you're not a spooky, you're just da day resting, I know you now...night-night." The curtain, drawn. The dark, tucked.

And here's the page's finding, this month's whole arc in one gesture: fear ends not when the scary thing is defeated... but when it's known. Named. Visited. Furnished. Walked into holding hands. Until one night you catch yourself tucking it in... the thing that used to loom... kindly, like everything else you love and understand.

The dark, for its part, held the stars up all night, same as ever... fuller than it looks, resting, known. "Da dark sleeps good now," she reported at breakfast. The dark sleeps good now. So, as it happened, does everybody else in the house. This month did its work. Both shifts.
