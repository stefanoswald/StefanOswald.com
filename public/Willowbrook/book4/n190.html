Night 190

The Maybe That Took All Month

Month 7: The Maybe Lessons

Honesty page. Because this month can't be all quick catches, and the book tells true: The snow globe broke. (The aunts'. The tiny town inside... a cottage, a pine, eternal snow. Shelf treasure. Gravity's fault, or a cape- corner's.

Shattered. Water everywhere. The tiny cottage, chipped.) "... Maybe..." She said. And waited. Nothing. Days. A week. "Mama. Da snow globe maybe isn't coming. I keep watching. It's just... broken." And here, this month's hardest, realest lesson: "Some maybes are slow, love.

Weeks. Months. Some take so long you stop watching. And some..." (the true part; the one the word has always secretly carried) "...some we never see turn. Maybe isn't a promise that everything turns gold fast.

It's a promise that we stay watchers. That we don't slam the book shut on a bad page. The watching is the maybe." The slip stayed unwritten. The jar waited. (It turned, for the story.

Months later, next book, even: the chipped cottage, glued, became the meadow map's first 3-D landmark... "da dream cottage"... and started a whole tabletop town. The slip got written the day it turned: "Da snow globe. Longest maybe yet.

It turned into a whole town. Never slam da book." Never slam the book. Some pages just take a season. Keep the jar open.)
