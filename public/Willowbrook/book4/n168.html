Night 168

What She Teaches Theo About School

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

Every school day ends the same way now: The briefing. The fort. Him. The day, retold: "...and den at circle, Miss Dana did da chime and Marcus was still talking and she just waited with her teacher face and he stopped all by himself.

Da teacher face is powerful, Fee-oh. I'm learning it." (She is. It's coming along. Theo reports it's "scary good.") The effects of the briefings, cumulative: He packed a backpack. (Hers, outgrown-ish. Packed with: one cow, one truck, crackers.

The lesson of the heart, his edition.) He demands "cubby" for his coat hook. (Granted. Renamed. The hallway hooks are cubbies now. Fine.) He plays "school" daily. His version: circle time for the plush, a chime, and a hello song that is, per tradition, mostly names.

"When do I go to da real one? When you're free- almost-four. Two more years. Dat's lots. It's some. But Fee-oh, by da time you go, you'll already know everything. Da chart, da timer, da porch trick, da statue drill.

I'm briefing you every day. You'll walk in like you own it." (He did. The far books hold his first day: a kid who walked in fluent, found his cubby, sat criss-cross, and told the teacher "my sister briefed me."

The teacher... Miss Dana still... laughed until she had to sit down. "Of course she did..." She said. "Of course she did.")
