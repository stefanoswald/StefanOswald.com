Night 263

The Fourth Branch

Month 9: Brave Is Scared Plus One Step

The fourth branch... saved as next season's yet... fell early. (A perfect climbing day. Dry bark. Strong grip. The one-steps of a whole season, compounding... and the reach that was two inches short in the spring was, suddenly, autumn-long enough.)

No ceremony. No announcement. Papa, spotting below, watched her test it, take it, stand... and just be there. Fourth branch. New record. The view: wider. (The brook visible now. The school roof. And the willow, gold on its hill, watching the watcher.)

And the quiet up there, she stayed twenty minutes, longer than any climb before, just sitting. Looking. Being at her own new altitude. "What did you do up dere so long?" asked Papa, after the careful backward down. "...

Nothing. Everything. I just was, up high. Some braves aren't for doing something. Some braves are just for getting somewhere new and... being dere a while." Some braves are just for getting somewhere new and being there a while.

(The summit truth. The one every climber learns eventually: the top isn't a task. It's a place. You earn it to sit in it. She learned it at four, on branch four, twenty quiet minutes at a time.

The fifth branch waited, patient, one season out... knowing, the way oaks know, that she'd come.)
