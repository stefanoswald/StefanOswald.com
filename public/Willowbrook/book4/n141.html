Night 141

The Checkup Buddy

Month 5: Little Brother Tags Along

Theo's two-year checkup. And in the room, by petition, on duty: his big sister. Self-assigned role: Support staff. The services rendered: The narration: "Dat's da ear light. It tickles. I know it's weird.

It's fast. I been getting it my whole life... look at my ears, dey're fine." The demonstration: her own ears, offered to the doctor first. Checked. Survived. Visibly. The hand: held out for the squeeze at the pokey part.

(One shot. His squeeze: mighty. Her face: level. The field face. Held for him the way it's been held for her at every poke of her life.) And the after: the sticker counsel. ("Take da dinosaur.

Da star ones run out of sticky. Trust me.") The doctor, who has watched this family since a six-month-old confiscated her stethoscope... wrote something in the margin again. (the story knows what. It's what she always writes, book after book: "Sibling attended.

Best medicine in the room.") "You were brave..." The staff told her patient, in the car. "Scared plus anyway. I saw da whole thing. Both of us," said Theo... Both of them. (He saw her hand too.

He sees everything. Twos do.) Both. As ever. Same waiting room, next year, same staffing. Permanent arrangement.
