Night 203

The Wrong Gift Maybe

Month 7: The Maybe Lessons

The party circuit struck: Wren's birthday. And at the party... the horror... the gift bag was wrong. (Grabbed in haste. The bag contained: the gift for Marcus's next party. A truck. Loud. Wren, quiet, puzzle-souled Wren... does not truck.)

The mortification: complete. "He'll think I don't know him... Maybe." And the maybe unwound like this: Wren opened the truck. Looked at it. Looked at her... stricken at the gift table... and said (forty sentences a year; this was one): "...da wheels come off?"

The wheels came off. And Wren. Wren whose puzzle-soul sees into things... spent the whole party... disassembling it. Wheels, axles, the little door. Blissful. Silent. Screwdriver from his dad's kit. "Best gift..." His grandmother told Mama at pickup.

"He doesn't play with toys. He understands them. Nobody ever gives him something with screws." The slip: "Gave Wren da wrong gift. Seemed so bad. But da wrong gift was da right gift because Wren likes insides of things.

Now I know him more. Da mistake taught me my friend. Caught." (Every Wren gift after: buildable, take-apartable, screwed. The wrong bag knew him first. Sometimes the error is the introduction.)
