Night 309

The Quiet That Snow Makes

Month 11: Winter Wonders

Snow again, at dusk, the falling kind, fat and unhurried. And the listening walk went out into it, collector and father, notebook in the inside pocket The discovery, half a block in: "Papa. Stop... Listen.

Da town's sounds are all... Wrapped. Da cars are soft. Da bell was soft. Even our feet are soft. Snow turned everything's volume down... How?" (The physics, dusk-simplified: snow is mostly air in tiny rooms; sound walks in and gets lost in the rooms; the world upholstered, overnight, in a million small silences.)

"Da snow eats sound. It falls down quiet and it makes quiet. It brings its own." The notebook entry, made under the street lamp, mitten in teeth: "Snow quiet. Not the same as night quiet.

Night quiet = the sounds went to bed. Snow quiet = da sounds are still dere but the snow is holding dem." (The taxonomist of silence, at work. Her collection holds the brook's blblblb and the willow's shhhh, and now this: the year's rarest specimen, the held hush of a snowing town.)

And walking home, the falling flakes catching streetlight: "Some things can't go in da notebook. Dis one's an ears one... And a standing-still one. You can't collect snow quiet. You can only stand in it."

You can only stand in it. (They stood in it. Awhile. Two collectors, pockets full, collecting nothing... which is, now and then, the collector's highest art.)
