Night 363

Bobby Bear's Letter

Month 12: Almost Four

Under the pillow, two nights into four, right on schedule; the bear's mail runs annual. The letter. The text: "Dear four. It is me. You can read dis yourself now which is the best thing dat happened all year and I saw da butterfly come out so that is saying something.

Things I know about the year coming: Da sunflowers will be taller dan Papa. You will lose da training wheels. Spring. A morning. I have seen it. Your Papa runs behind you and then he stops running and you dont notice for a whole block.

The big school comes in the fall. Da real one. It is big but so are you now. Pack the kit. All of it. Dey dont tell you but school is mostly da kit. And the meadow stays open.

Growing up does not close it. That is a rumor. Bears hate dat rumor. Keep the key warm. Love, Bobby bear. P. S. Feo starts dreaming louder dis year. If a small cow shows up in the meadow be nice.

It is probably not lost. It is probably invited." The box just turned into a book. (Every archive does, little one, the day its keeper learns to read it. Yours just came online. Sleep now. Four's room has its first furniture: one letter, read alone, promising sunflowers.)
