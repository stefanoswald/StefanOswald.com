Night 246

The Two-Wheeler Question

Month 9: Brave Is Scared Plus One Step

Olivia watched Mateo ride his bicycle on two wheels and asked when her training wheels could come off.

"Not by the calendar," Mateo said. "First you learn to balance, glide, turn, and stop. Then the extra wheels have less and less work to do."

He used a wrench to raise them slightly. Olivia rode down Maple Lane. On the smooth parts, neither small wheel touched the ground. At the corner, one caught her wobble.

"It saved me," she said.

"It reminded you," Mateo replied. "Your own balance did most of the ride."

For a week she practiced starting and stopping. She coasted down the gentle hill with her feet still. Each day the training wheels rattled less.

"Today?" she asked on Saturday.

Mateo shook his head. "Soon."

Olivia surprised herself by agreeing. She no longer wanted the wheels removed merely to look older. She wanted her body to be ready for the space they would leave.
