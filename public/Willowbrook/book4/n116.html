Night 116

When the Big Kid Was Scared

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

News from up the lane: Mateo, the faculty, the builder, the pit-crew boss, six-and-a-half now and fearless by reputation... has his first bike race Saturday. The kids' race at the fair. Real numbers pinned on.

Real crowd. And Thursday, at the fence, the reputation slipped: "What if I fall? In front of everybody? What if I'm... last?" (The faculty. Scared. Publicly. To an audience of three- and-four-year-olds.

Which took, note it, its own courage. The big kids who can say scared to little kids are the realest faculty there is.) And the little ones... raised on the kit, fluent since forever... deployed it uphill: Olivia: "Scared plus anyway.

Dat's brave. It's da formula. My papa used it on a ladder." Lily: "And if you fall, you get up. Falls are part of da bike. Ask anybody's knees." Olivia: "And here..." (the brave list, her copy, the folded traveling one, pressed into his hand) "...borrow mine.

It's got evidence. You can add yours after." Saturday: the race. The whole lane attended. He fell once. (Turn two. Got up. The knees knew.) He finished fourth. Fourth of eleven. Fell. Got up. Fourth.

Scared plus anyway works. M." The kit's first uphill deployment, confirmed effective at six-and-a-half. It works at every altitude. It always did. That's why you build it low and lend it everywhere."
