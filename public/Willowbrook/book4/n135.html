Night 135

The Bear Meets His Match

Month 5: Little Brother Tags Along

A summit this week, gathered at the rug: Baba and the cow. (Nose to nose. Arranged by their commissioners. The children narrating both sides, as ever... the soft division's diplomacy has always run on ventriloquism.)

The plan, by the narrators: Baba: "How's your boy sleeping?" Cow: "Good. He kicks. Mine kicked too, da whole first year. Dey settle. Does yours... talk to you? In da day? All day. It's da best part.

Yours will more, when his words come... Do we ever retire?" (A pause. The narrators looked at each other. The question had arrived from somewhere neither of them planned.) And Baba, via his girl, slowly, finding it as she said it: "No.

We change jobs sometimes. Dresser to arms. Arms to shelf, someday, maybe. Shelf to... somebody new's arms, if we're lucky. But retire? No. Loved things don't retire. We just get reassigned." Loved things don't retire.

They get reassigned. (The far gate, gold and distant, flickered somewhere at the meadow's edge of this page. Not today. Years off. But the law that will hold that day... it got written here, at a rug summit, by its own future executor.

They remembered it where the big ones go.) The summit closed with the plush shaking hands. Colleagues. Jobs: compared, confirmed, permanent. The soft division: in good order, at every altitude, on both sides of every willow.
