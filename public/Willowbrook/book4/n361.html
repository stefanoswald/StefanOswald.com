Night 361

The Willow Writes

Month 12: Almost Four

The birthday. The walk. (The whole family, golden hour, up the hill... the annual pilgrimage, fourth edition. Theo on Papa's shoulders. The birthday girl walking her own walk, four years old since this morning, star sweater on despite the warm.

Some things you wear to meetings.) The tree: waiting. (It's always waiting. That's its whole office. The long gold branches, the hill's crown, the watcher of the whole valley, and, once a year, on this day, for this family: the writer.)

The approach. The hush. The branches, parting in a wind that touched nothing else on the hill... And there, in the bark, above the old words... Home, gold and settled, age two... Family, deeper gold, age three... the new one, fresh as morning, glowing its first glow: Growing.

(She read it herself. First year she could. Sounded it once, quiet... "G...row... Growing"... and then stood very still.) "Home..." She said finally, reading the sentence so far, top to bottom, finger tracing air.

"Family. Growing... Mama. It's not free words. It's one thing getting longer. Da tree's telling our story in da fewest words dere are. Home. Den family. Den growing... Dat's us. Dat's exactly us. Answer: the tree does not waste words.

Growing. Confirmed in full. The family stood awhile. (Theo, from the shoulders, read it his way: "Tree words! Free of dem!" Also accurate.) And walking down, dusk settling, her hand in Mama's: "Four more birthdays, maybe five, and da sentence gets done...

I can wait. Da tree taught me how. Da tree's da best waiter dere is." The tree writes on, little one. One word a year. Slowest writer in town. Never missed a deadline. Never wasted a word. Growing. Happy birthday."
