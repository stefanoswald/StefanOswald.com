Night 265

The Duchess Detente

Month 9: Brave Is Scared Plus One Step

The Duchess. The meanest chicken in the world. The pecker. The origin of the family pain scale. This month: the detente. The farm visit. Feeding time. And the request, self- assigned: "Grandpa. I'm feeding da duchess.

Myself. It's on my list." The briefing (Joe's, delivered with the gravity of a man who has negotiated with this hen for nine years): "Rules of the Duchess. One: no sudden grabs, she reads grabs as war.

Two: scatter wide, not at her feet... she likes her space. Three: look at her while you do it. She respects lookers. Peckers peck the ones who flinch away." The operation: The coop door. The bucket.

The stare, met. (Hen and girl, eye to eye, one long moment, two veterans of the same old war.) The scatter: wide. Steady. Unflinched. And the Duchess... regarded her a moment longer, then ate. No peck.

A truce. Maybe even... Joe swears it... a nod. "She nodded at me. She respects lookers, I told you. Bravest thing in a barnyard is just not flinching." Looked at her da whole time. She nodded.

We're not friends. We're colleagues." Not friends. Colleagues. (The truce held for years. The Duchess never pecked her again... a distinction extended, as it happened, to almost no one else. Respect, hen-issued, is the rarest kind on the farm. She'd earned it the only way it's issued: one unflinched scatter at a time.)"
