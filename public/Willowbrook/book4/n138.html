Night 138

The Piggyback Era

Month 5: Little Brother Tags Along

A new physical feat this month, petitioned daily until granted: "I want to carry him. Piggyback. Like Papa does me." The physics: dubious. (She's three-almost- four. He's two. The ratio is... ambitious.) The trial, spotted closely: The mounting: from the couch.

The grip: his arms at her neck, "not choking, holding"... coached, adjusted. The stand: achieved. Staggering. Both grinning like maniacs. The voyage: three steps. Three. Full steps. Across the rug... the same rug where he first crawled to her, where the steps were counted, where everything... before the gentle collapse into cushions, both parties shrieking.

"Did you see? I carried my brother! Free steps! Aden!" ruled the cargo. "Aden aden!" Again it was. (Six voyages total. Peak distance: five steps. The era of sister-piggybacks: opened. It runs... the story checks the far pages... years.

The ratio improves. The grinning never changes. There is photographic evidence at every age, including one at the far edge of the shelf that makes everyone cry: same rug, same grip, both of them enormous, still doing it.)

"He's heavy..." She reported, buried in cushions. "But he's my heavy." Her heavy. The best weight in the house. Carried, staggering, gladly, for the whole rest of the books.
