Night 188

The Too-Small Sweater Maybe

Month 7: The Maybe Lessons

The star sweater... last year's, the three-star one... came out of the drawer for the cold... and didn't fit. Wouldn't. The arms stopped short. The stars strained. "But it's my star one..." The grief: real.

(Outgrowing is losing, at four. The river's deal, felt in wool.) "... Maybe." And the maybe took two weeks and one mailing north: The aunts, needles, scissors, genius... Unmade it. And remade it: The three stars: now a hat.

The soft body: now mittens. The trim: a scarf for the bear. ("Nothing loved gets lost..." Read the enclosed card, in the elder aunt's hand. "It just gets remade. Like all of us.") The slip: "Da star sweater got too small.

Seemed bad. Now it's a hat and mittens and Baba got a scarf. Da sweater didn't end. It multiplied. Maybe caught." (Nothing loved gets lost. It just gets remade. The card went in the keepsake harbor.

The law went everywhere... ahead of every outgrowing still to come, of which, the shelf knows, there are so many, and every one survivable, by remaking.)
