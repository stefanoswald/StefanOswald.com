Night 28

The Pretend Rest Day

Month 1: Pretend!

And then, this month's last surprise... a day with no pretend at all. Nobody planned it. She just woke up and... dug. Real digging. In the real garden. For no kingdom. Ate a real snack that was only a snack.

Stacked blocks that were blocks, into a tower that was a tower. Watched real ants for half a real hour. Napped without hibernating. "No games today?" asked Mama, gently, at dusk. "Da games are resting..."

She said. "Dey get tired too." The games get tired too. (The wisest sentence of this month, and she didn't even look up from her ants when she said it.) Even imaginations have seasons. The fields of pretend lie fallow a day, and the real world... which was always the soil under all of it, the ants and dirt and blocks the castles are built from... gets tended, close and quiet.

By Thursday the floor was water again. The games woke up hungry, as rested things do. But mark the fallow day, little maker. Keep taking them, all your life: days where the world is only, entirely, astonishingly itself... which was always the biggest pretend- proof wonder of them all.
