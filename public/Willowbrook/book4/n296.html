Night 296

The Sound Collector's Walk

Month 10: Words Are Magic

The listening walk... old family institution, ears first, since the stroller era... got a new instrument this week: a notebook. The mission, self-assigned: "Da town makes sounds. Sounds can be spelled. I'm collecting da town."

The transcriptions, verbatim from the notebook: The brook: "Blblblblb" ("It doesn't use real letters. Da brook can't spell. It just talks.") The bakery bell: "Ting-a-ling" The market door: "Weeee-bang" The gate at Number Twelve: "Skreeeek" ("Dat gate needs Mr.

Petrov.") Wind in the willow: "Shhhhhhhh... Hushhhhh" ("Da willow spells quiet. It's da only thing dat can.") And, page last. Papa's whistle, the evening-come-home one, three notes, attempted in letters four ways and finally rendered: "Twee-dee-doo (But better dan dat.

Letters can't hold it.)" Letters can't hold it. (The collector's first encounter with the limit of her new power, and the family remembered how she took it: not as defeat. As information. "Some things go in letters.

Some things only go in ears. Dat's why we got both." Some things only go in ears. The notebook kept both kinds ever after: the spellable town, spelled; the unspellable, described, pointed at, honored from the edge of the alphabet.

Which is, as it happened, the entire job description of a writer. She has the whole kit now. She just doesn't know the word for herself yet. The far pages know it.)
