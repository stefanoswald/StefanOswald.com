Night 84

Why Does Theo Copy Me

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

The complaint arrived mid-afternoon: "Fee-oh copies everything. I hop, he hops. I say pickle, he says pickle. Why? Come here. I'll show you why." The album. The old pages. Photographic evidence: Little Olivia, at the fence, hand flat... exactly like the bigger hand beside hers.

Little Olivia in the big kids' sprinkler line, studying their leaps. Little Olivia wearing two hair bunches... "like Lily had! You copied your whole way up, love. Everyone does. Copying is how little ones learn, it's the oldest school there is.

You watched the big kids. Lily watched her cousin. And now... You're the big kid. You're not being copied. You're being attended. He's in your school. You're the whole faculty." The whole faculty. She turned and watched her student, mid-hop, mid-pickle, radiant at her attention. "...

Den I should hop good..." She said slowly. "If he copies everything... I should do good everyfings." (the story underlines it. Three years old: the entire ethics of being watched, self-derived. If he copies everything, I should do good everythings.

Somebody put it on the school's wall. Somebody put it on every wall.) She hopped. Good. He hopped. Good. School, in session. Faculty: aware now. Lesson: everything. Forever.
