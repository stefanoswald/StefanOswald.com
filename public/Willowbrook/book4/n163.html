Night 163

The Painting Phase

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

The easel access has produced: a body of work. Daily paintings. Home in the backpack, damp- ish, magnificent. The refrigerator: full. The freezer door: annexed. The critics have identified periods: The Duck Period. (Weeks one-two.

Ducks. All ducks. The eternal subject, now in tempera.) The Purple Period. (Week three. Everything purple. Asked why: "Purple was da full jar." Availability- driven art. Honest.) The Family Period. (Ongoing. Four figures, always: big, middle, small, smaller.

Plus a fifth blob, lower right, every time. "Baba." Plus, occasionally, an orange smudge. "Franklin came dat day.") And this week, the piece that went straight to framing: a tree. Enormous. Trunk up the whole paper.

And the branches... half green, half gold, with tiny dots below the gold half. "It's da willow. Both sides. Da dots is da lantern fish." Both sides of the willow. Painted from... memory. (The frame: immediate.

The wall spot: prominent. The questions: politely not asked. The town's records note the piece with interest. First documented artwork of the other side. The archive, growing, in tempera, age four.) "Miss Dana says I'm a real artist."

Miss Dana is correct. The gallery expands. The periods roll on. The second appliance won't be the last.
