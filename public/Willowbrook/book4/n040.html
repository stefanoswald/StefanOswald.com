Night 40

The Night Baba Couldn't Come

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

A hard morning this week: "Baba wasn't dere." The dream: dreamless. Or wrong-dreamed... something about a bus and socks, no meadow, no bear. "I dreamed him in. I tried. He didn't come." (The first missed meeting.

It was always coming. Dreams are weather, not machinery, but try telling that to somebody who found a door this good and expected it open nightly.) The disappointment: real. Held. (Named: "You missed him. You wanted da meadow."

The old craft. It works on dream-grief too.) And that night... the meadow, restored, per dreams' own fickle mercy... she asked him straight: "Where were you?" And the bear, walking: "Little one. Dreams are like da brook.

Some nights da water goes udder ways. Not away, just udder ways. I'm never gone. Da meadow's never gone. Some nights you just get da bus-and-socks dream instead. Even I get dose. You dream?

Everything loved dreams. It's how we're paid." (Everything loved dreams. It's how we're paid. The story underlines it and moves on, because some lines don't need anything after them.) "So when I don't find you...

Hold me tighter in da morning. Dat's where I always am. Da meadow is extra. Da arms are da job." The arms are the job. The missed nights stopped mattering after that. The mornings never missed once.
