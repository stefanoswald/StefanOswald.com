Night 244

The One-Step Rule

Month 9: Brave Is Scared Plus One Step

The formula, this family's oldest: brave is scared plus anyway. This month adds the engineering: The one-step rule. Taught at the base of the big slide. (The tall one. The big-kid one. Wanted for a year.

Feared for a year. The wanting and the fearing, deadlocked.) "Here's the secret nobody tells you about brave..." said Papa. "You don't have to do the whole thing. Brave is just one step past where scared says stop.

One rung today. That's a complete brave. Tomorrow, maybe two. The top will still be there. Tops wait. That's their whole job." One rung today. She climbed one rung. Stood on it. Scared, plus one step.

Came down. "Dat counts?" That counts. That's the whole unit of it. Braves are counted in steps, not summits. (Rung two: Thursday. Rung four: the next week. The top: seventeen days out, and the slide itself, when it finally came, almost beside the point: the staircase was the education.

The whoosh was just the diploma.) The rule went in the kit, permanent: one step past stop. Count it. Come back tomorrow. Every mountain on every far page got climbed exactly this way. Start counting, little one. One.
