Night 4

Tea for Everyone Invisible

Month 1: Pretend!

The tea party today had a guest list. The visible attendees: Baba. The knit doll Duck. The plush cow. And the invisible ones: "Dis chair is for Mrs. Feathers. Dis one is for da Balloon Man.

And dis one is for Franklin." (Franklin. Nobody knows who Franklin is. Franklin attended every gathering for two months. Franklin liked two sugars. The family served Franklin without question, which is the correct rule for Franklins.)

The tea: air, poured with precision. The cakes: blocks, served on saucers. The conversation: done in all directions, hostess voicing every guest... Mrs. Feathers gossiped; the Balloon Man spoke only in whooshes; Franklin was "having a hard week" and received extra cake.

(Even imaginary friends get the bench treatment in this family. Franklin was sat with. Franklin felt better.) Three's mind is a crowded, generous place. It sets extra chairs. It always has room. Pull one up anytime... just know the tea is air, the cake is wooden, and the company, every bit of it, is completely real where it counts.
