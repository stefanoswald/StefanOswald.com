Night 343

The Year in Pictures

Month 12: Almost Four

The fridge gallery rotated all year. The retired works went, per family archival law... into the folder. And this week, pre-birthday, the folder came out. The floor became a museum. The retrospective, artist-led, chronological: "Dis one's from way back.

Da meadow, when I first dreamed it. Look. I drew da willow with arms. I didn't know trees yet... I mean I knew dem. I just drew what dey felt like." (Drew what they felt like.

The entire history of art, casually, en route to the next piece.) "Da preschool one. Everybody's names. I couldn't write den, so dose are pretend letters. Look how hard pretend-me was trying... Good job, pretend-me."

The Buttons memorial drawing. (Held a moment. Placed gently. "Da hamster one stays in da folder forever. Some pictures aren't for walls. Dey're for keeping.") The Maybe jar, drawn in autumn. Fort Togedder. The green room, December edition, "I drew da inside even doh nobody can see da inside.

Artists get to." and the emergence, drawn the same Tuesday it happened, wings still wet in both worlds. And the latest: signed, punctuated, titled. Scribble to signature, one floor's length. "Dat's a whole me..." She said, surveying it.

"Da year, but in pictures... Everybody should keep deir folder. How else do you prove you were all dose people?" How else do you prove you were all those people. (The folder went back in the cabinet.

A new one, labeled four, went in beside it, empty, waiting. The museum of her: under one roof, accepting acquisitions, never once deaccessioned. The far pages hold folders through seventeen. The proof holds. She was all those people. The story can confirm: every one.)
