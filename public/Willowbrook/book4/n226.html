Night 226

The Cocoa Stand

Month 8: Helping Hands

The enterprise line, crossed at scale: The cocoa stand. (Sledding hill traffic. Cold customers. A market inefficiency, spotted by the foreman: "Everybody's cold and nobody's selling anything. We should sell da warm.") The operation: Mateo: management, signage, the thermos logistics.

Lily: pouring. Olivia: the counter. (Greeting, change- making, coins counted by the five-craft... and the crust-end instinct, applied: one free cup to the crying kid who wiped out on the ice. "Some cups are business.

Some cups are da town.") The take: four dollars and change, split three ways. Her share's fate, announced at home: half to the earned jar. And half... "for da casserole route. Da wagon needs a new wheel.

Business should feed da town back. Rosa says. Da jar with bread." (The kindness jar with something. Rosa's whole economics, implemented at the first opportunity, age four, on a snowy corner.) The stand ran every big snow for years.

The free-cup line item never came off the books. It was, all three founders agreed, the best-performing asset.
