Night 305

Where Did Bella Go?

Month 11: Winter Wonders

The cold came early this year. And with it, a missing person: Bella. (The butterfly. The rainbow-winged one. Garden regular since the beginning of memory. Landed on Theo once... his word fly exists because of her.

Family, as far as this household saves things.) The search: thorough. The garden: bare. The flowers: done. The air: empty of rainbows. "Mama. Bella's gone. Winter's coming and she's just... Gone. Did she leave? Did something..."

(The voice doing the thing. The Buttons lesson lives in this house now; she knows what winter can mean; the question costs her.) And then, under the milkweed leaf, at looking- closer height: The bundle.

Small. Silk-strapped. Jade-green, gold- flecked, hanging still as a held breath. "... What is dat? That's a chrysalis, love. And if I know this garden... that's Bella's kind. She didn't leave. She went inside.

Inside dat? She doesn't fit in dat! Not the way she was. That's the mystery. That's the whole month's mystery, if you want it: what happens inside the little green room." (She wanted it. The bundle got a name, "da winter cradle"... a daily visit schedule, and a guard rotation per family tradition.

This month had its mystery. The mystery had its watcher. And inside the green room, quietly, the oldest magic in the garden began doing what it does: its slow, secret, certain work.)
