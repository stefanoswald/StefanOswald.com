Night 284

The Word Jar

Month 10: Words Are Magic

A new jar on the shelf this week. (Next to the Maybe jar. The shelf of jars: growing. This house governs by jar.) The word jar. Not useful words. Beautiful ones. "Words dat taste good when you say dem."

The criterion: hers. The spelling: inventive. The founding collection: Lullaby. ("It sings itself. Lul-la-by. Da word does da thing it means.") Whoosh. (Slide vocabulary. Onomatopoeia before she'll know the moment... the jar collects first, names later.)

Colleague. (The Duchess treaty word. "It means we-work-togedder- but-carefully. Dere's no uddah word for dat.") Willow. ("Our tree. Also da word is droopy, like da tree. Say it. Willllow. It hangs down in your mouth.")

And Papa's contribution, inducted by family vote: Petrichor. ("Da smell of rain on warm ground." The jar's first grown-up word. She made him say it eleven times. "Pet- ri-kor. Da rain smell has a name.

What else has names dat I don't know?!") What else has names. (Everything, little one. Everything has a name, and the ones that don't yet are waiting for namers exactly like you. The jar filled.

Got a second jar. Became, eventually, a notebook, then a shelf of notebooks... the far pages know her, pen out, collecting still. Every writer starts as a collector. The jar knows. The jar was always step one.)
