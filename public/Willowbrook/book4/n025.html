Night 25

The Pretend That Practiced the Future

Month 1: Pretend!

A new game appeared this week, unannounced, and the watching parents exchanged the eyebrow: "Preschool." (The real one starts in the fall. It's been mentioned. The cubbies were peeked at once, months ago. The mention lodged.)

The living room version: Cubbies: the bookshelf's bottom row. Assigned. Circle time: gathered on the rug. ("Everybody sit criss-cross. Now we sing da hello song." A hello song was composed on the spot. It was mostly names.)

Snack time: distributed. And "da mamas leave part," played carefully, repeatedly, with the toys' mamas walking to the "door" and waving: "Bye! I always come back. Dat's da rule." Wave. Exit... Return. Reunion. Every session.

Look at what the pretend is doing... the same thing it did with Duck and the goodbyes: Rehearsing. The fall is coming. The big new country. And her mind, wise before she is, is walking the route in advance, the cubbies, the circle, above all the leaving-and-returning... until the shape is known, until the rule is load-bearing: they always come back.

By the real first day, she'll have attended preschool a hundred times. Play is the heart's dress rehearsal. The show opens in the fall. The cast is ready already.
