Night 93

The Nightlight Negotiation

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

This week: the summit. Because "the dark is resting" is a good thesis, and also, three-and-a-half is three-and-a-half, and the room's night settings needed renegotiating. The options, tested like mattresses: Full lamp: too bright.

("Da day can't rest with dat on. And me neider.") Full dark: too much, currently. (Honestly stated. Honored without one ounce of shame: "Lots of people your size renegotiate the dark. It's allowed.") The nightlight: the little gold one.

Tested... approved. ("Like Della came inside.") Plus the door-crack: one inch. (The governing inch. Hallway light, distant voices, the sound of the house being awake below... civilization's hum, audible.) The final calibration, hers: nightlight on.

Door: one inch. Bear: armed. Curtain: pinned... ("so da car-spooky can't do his show.") "Deal?" Deal. And the page's note for every negotiating household: the settings aren't forever. They're scaffolding. The dark gets renegotiated smaller every year on its own, the nightlight dims to habit, the inch closes by itself someday...

If the scaffolding goes up without shame when it's needed. Fear, met with respect, retires on schedule. Fear, met with "you're too big for that"... digs in. The inch stayed a year. The gold light, three.

Both retired, eventually, with honors, unpushed. The dark, by then: just the day, resting. Exactly as renamed.
