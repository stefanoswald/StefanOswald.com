Night 214

The Wilting Lesson

Month 8: Helping Hands

Week two of the chart: The failure. The watering job... hers, real-staked... went three days unwatered. (The trio Thursday. The snow fort. The forgetting that forgets it forgot. Three days.) And the fern... the thirsty one, the always-thirsty one...

Drooped. Visibly. Dramatically. (Ferns are the theater kids of plants. It performed its neglect.) And Mama, who had seen it drooping day two, who could have reminded, who chose, deliberately, the harder kindness... said nothing.

Until: "Mama. The fern. It's all sad. Who forgot... I forgot. Free days I forgot. My job and I forgot it and da fern paid." The rescue: immediate. (Water. The mist bottle. An apology, verbal, to the fern.

It recovered by morning, ferns forgive like toddlers, and performed its recovery as dramatically as its decline.) And the lesson, landed the only way real ones land: "Reminders make da job yours-wif- help. Forgetting makes it yours- yours.

Da fern taught me. Da fern is da real boss of da job." The fern is the real boss of the job. (She never missed again. Not from guilt, from ownership. The job had stakes, and the stakes had fronds. Real jobs teach. That's why you give them real.)
