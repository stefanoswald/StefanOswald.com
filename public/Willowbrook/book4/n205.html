Night 205

Papa's Trip Maybe

Month 7: The Maybe Lessons

Papa went away. (Work. Four nights. The far city. The first long trip of their whole remembered lives.) The ache: real. The window's post: unmanned at dusk. The wrong-note song: sung by Mama... right notes, apologetically.

("It's not da same." It wasn't. It wasn't supposed to be. Some jobs are one person's.) "...maybe," she said, night two, uncertain. And the maybes, arriving: The postcards. (One per day. Mailed day one so they'd land right.

The far city's bridges, its towers... and on each, one duck drawn in Papa's hand, touring the landmarks. The mailbox flag: up four days straight. The mail is love with a stamp. The old lesson, airmailed.)

The calls. (The window's post, kept by phone. The song, sung down the wire, wrong notes restored.) And the airport. (The pickup. The arrivals door. The waiting... signs made: Welcome home Papa, one duck, one scribble, and the moment the doors opened: the double sprint, both kids, the drop of the bags, the four-way collision-hug that airports were invented to host.)

The slip: "Papa went away four sleeps. Seemed long and bad. Got duck postcards and phone songs and the airport hug which is da biggest hug kind dere is. Missing somebody shows you da size of dem.

Caught." (The size of him: four sleeps wide, airport-hug deep. Measured once. Never doubted again.)
