Night 216

The Snow Shoveling Crew

Month 8: Helping Hands

Snow again. And this time, organized by the faculty, staffed by the lane... A work crew. Mateo, Olivia and Lily, and Theo. The job: The hendersons' Walk. (The old couple. The ones whose walk gets mystery-shoveled by a certain farmer.

He's got the whole town's walks and a bad hip this year. The lane's youngest noticed. The lane's youngest mobilized.) The work: Real. Forty minutes. The whole walk, plus the steps, plus a path to the mailbox ("mail is love; da mailman needs feet-room").

The wage, offered by Mrs. Henderson at the door: dollars. The wage, negotiated by the foreman, who has been raised by this exact lane: "No thank you, ma'am. Neighbors shovel neighbors. But if dere was cocoa..."

There was cocoa. (The good kind. Whipped cream. Served on the cleared steps to a crew of four, steaming in the cold like tiny linemen.) And the standing... the real wage: the Hendersons told everyone.

The lane's list updated: the crew exists. The crew shovels. The crew takes cocoa. (Three more walks that winter. The crew held for years, growing, swapping shovel sizes, absorbing new recruits. The town's next generation of mystery-shovelers, apprenticing in plain sight.

The farmer heard. "Well now," at the moment. His hip healed. His routes stayed shared. Succession: begun.)
