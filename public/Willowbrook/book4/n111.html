Night 111

The Solo Sleepover, Night

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

The day: perfect. (Chickens, barrow, workshop hours, corn checked, supper enormous. The farm's whole ritual, solo edition. "Ran the place. Duchess ate from her hand. I mostly supervised.") And then: night. The real test. The guest room... the one that held her papa once, and her before, but never her, alone, in the dark, away.

The tools, deployed in order, by their owner: The gold light: plugged at the sill. The bear: positioned. (On shift. The job traveled fine. It always travels. That's the design.) The farm's sounds: Named, with help from the resident expert, at tuck-in: "Dat creak: da barn, cooling.

Dat rustle: da corn talking to itself, it does dat on growing nights. Dat far-off who: da owl. You know da owl." (She knows the owl. The dark filled up with knowns.) Da dark is different here.

It is. Farm dark's older. C'mere... I'll show you something." The porch. Two minutes. The whole sky... farm-dark sky, no lane lights, stars past all counting, the lots-lots-est edition she'd ever seen...

"Da dark's not empty out here, little one. It's fuller. You just gotta get away from da lamps to see what it's been holding all along." The dark is fuller. Back to bed. Out in minutes.

Slept till the roosters. The dark, met, away, alone... and found full. Brave list: the big entry. The one with stars on it."
