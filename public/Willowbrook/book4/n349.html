Night 349

The Remembering Game

Month 12: Almost Four

A request at dinner this week, pre- birthday, traditional: "Tell me babies-me. Da stories from when I was little. All da favorites." The remembering game. Papa went first: "The night you were born, it snowed out of season.

The nurse said she'd never seen May snow. I held you up to the window to show you and you... brand new, five hours old, looked." (You looked. First entry in the whole record. Still true.

Never stopped.) Mama: "Your first word was duck. Not mama, not papa... Duck. At the pond. Pointing. Like you'd been holding it in for a year and the duck finally earned it. Da duck earned it..."

She confirmed, solemn. The greatest hits, on request, each polished by annual telling: the spaghetti helmet. The flush heard round da house. The first Maybe night, snowplow edition. The bear's arrival in the crib... "night free- sixty-five, first book of you..."

Per Papa, who keeps the numbers. And Theo's contribution, new this year, his first-ever entry in the game: "Wivy! Da time you... you fell in da mud! And said a word!" (Recent history. Last Tuesday, in fact.

Mama said, unrecorded officially. The family: crying laughing. The archive: accepts all eras.) And at the lamp, after: "I like da remembering game best of all da games. Why's that, love?... Because I can't remember babies-me.

But you can. So babies-me is safe. You keep her for me. Da family is where you keep da yous you can't carry." The family is where you keep the yous you can't carry.
