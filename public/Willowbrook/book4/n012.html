Night 12

Rain Delay Theater

Month 1: Pretend!

Rain. All day. The old rainy-day kit came out, fort, pots, window-watching... and then went back, because this year rain means something new: Showtime. The theater, constructed: Curtain: the big blanket, chair to chair.

Spotlight: Papa's flashlight. Tickets: buttons. (The store's economy extends. Buttons are the town currency now.) Seating: the couch. Full house: Mama, Papa, Theo, three plush. And the show... "The dragon who lost his rawr"... written, directed, starring, and costumed by its author: Act one: the dragon has no rawr.

Act two: the dragon looks everywhere. (Under the couch. In the audience. In Papa's beard... extended search, big laughs.) Act free: the rawr was inside him all along. "Rawwwwr!" (Spotlight. Cape flare. The crowd goes wild.)

The bow. The encore. The reviews: rapturous. The playwright, at dinner: "Tomorrow's show is about a duck." Of course it is. The theater never closed after that. Rain or shine. Willowbrook's smallest stage, longest run, best seats in the house... all of them, forever, front row.
