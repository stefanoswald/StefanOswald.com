Night 293

The Word for Theo

Month 10: Words Are Magic

Scene, this week: Theo. Blocks. Tower, four high, personal record attempt. Down. The response, age two-and-a-half: total systems failure. Scream, stomp, block thrown, tears, the floor-flop. (The old country. She remembers it.

The feelings bigger than the body, no words to bleed the pressure off.) And the intervention... not a grown-up's. Hers. Kneeling, floor-level, the exact posture Mama used on her: "Fee-oh. Fee-oh.

Look at me. You're not bad. You're frustrated. Frus-tray-ted. Say it. It means mad-because-it- almost-worked. Da maddest kind... Fuss-tated. That's it. You're fusstated. Da tower fell and you're fusstated and dat's da right feeling for it.

It's allowed. Now we breathe. Like dis." (One breath. Two. The small stormy system, regulating... visibly, around a word and a breath given him at eye level by the person he trusts most in the world.) "...

Fusstated..." He confirmed, calmer, inspecting the word like a new block. "Build aden? Build aden. I'll hold da bottom. Bottoms are da secret." (At the lamp, the report: "I gave Fee-oh frustrated. His first feeling word.

Now his maddest thing has a handle.") His maddest thing has a handle. (The vocabulary, passing downward. The kneel, the eye level, the it's-allowed... the whole method, inherited whole. Words are the family trade now.

Both children are apprenticed. The house speaks feelings fluently, one generation teaching the next. Fusstated. First word of his emotional alphabet. Given by his sister. On the floor. Where the best teaching happens.)
