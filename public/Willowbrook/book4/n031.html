Night 31

Stella Learns the Hello Song

Month 1: Pretend!

Last night of this month. And up through the dark, on the warm air that carries such things, came a song. A small one. New. Invented this month on a rug, for a circle of stuffed animals: the hello song.

(It's mostly names. It's entirely names, actually, everyone she loves, in a row, to a tune that wobbles. The best songs are just love, listed.) Stella caught it at altitude. Learned it in one pass.

(Stars are quick studies. They've been collecting the town's small songs forever, the wrong-note heirloom, the washing anthem, the whole homemade songbook of every family below.) And near midnight, over the sleeping lane... softly, in star-register, which no instrument catches but every sleeping child hears... she hummed it back down.

The hello song. Over the house at number seven. Over Lily's window seat. Over June's new room and the recruit's and Mateo's. Every name on the list, hummed over its own sleeping owner. "Mmm?" asked the Moon.

"New one..." said Stella. "From the rug. It's mostly names. Mmm." The best ones are. The song drifted down like the gold does, settling over the town that makes such things daily and gives them away upward, free.

Hello, sang the star, to everyone by name. Hello. Hello. Goodnight, Willowbrook. This month of doubled worlds is sung to sleep.
