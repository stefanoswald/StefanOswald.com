Night 348

The Cake Consultation

Month 12: Almost Four

The bakery. After close. (Staff privileges: Lily's, extended to the birthday client. The counter: floured. The consultation: annual.) The constellation cake, fourth edition, design conference: "Da base is da sky..." said Rosa, sketching on parchment.

"Dat's tradition. Deep blue, like every year. Now: what stars go in your sky dis year? Da cake tells da year. Dat's how we've always done it. Da meadow... can frosting do gold? Da willow gets gold branches.

And a little green room... da chrysalis, Rosa, right dere, hanging off a frosting leaf, and den next to it, coming out of it... Rainbow wings..." said Rosa, already drawing. "I heard. Da whole lane heard.

Dat butterfly gets da cake's best corner. And a tiny book. Open. Because of da reading. And, can you do a little jar? With stars inside it?" (A jar with stars inside it. Rosa looked at that request a long moment... the Maybe jar, rendered in sugar... and said, quietly, "Yes.

Dat one I can do with my eyes closed. I've watched you fill it all year.") The design: finalized. Parchment: signed by client and baker. And the client's closing note, at the door, sweater stars catching the streetlight: "Rosa.

Make da sky big dis year. Da year was big. Da sky should fit it." Make the sky big. The year was big. (She did. It did. Saturday's record: the cake came out under a cloth, the room went quiet, and the whole year was there in frosting... meadow, willow, green room, wings, book, jar of stars... and nobody wanted to cut it and everybody did anyway, because that's what years are for. Eaten. Shared. Down to the last gold branch. Best sky yet.)
