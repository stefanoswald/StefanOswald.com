Night 206

The Broken Zipper Maybe

Month 7: The Maybe Lessons

The red backpack... term-and-a-half old, beloved, bear-rated... Broke. The zipper. Split. Unclosable. "We'll get a new... No. Dis one's mine. It knows my cubby... Then maybe we can fix it." And the fixing led to: The shop.

(The cobbler's corner. Old Mr. Petrov's. The dim little shop by the square that fixes... per its ancient window sign... "Shoes, zippers, & most things." She'd walked past it her whole life. Never in.) Inside: the workbench world.

Awls, thread, leather smell, a hundred repairs in progress... and Mr. Petrov, who examined the zipper over his glasses like a doctor, said "is nothing. Come Tuesday..." And. Tuesday... handed back the backpack with a zipper better than original, plus, unasked, the fraying strap re-stitched.

Cost: four dollars. Lesson: priceless and the whole point: "Mama. Dere's a whole shop for not-frowing-fings- away." The slip: "Da zipper broke. Seemed like da end of da backpack. Founded da fixing shop.

Da town has a place where things get healed instead of replaced. Everything loved can be patched... da bear knew, da book knew, now da whole town knows. Caught." (She brought Mr. Petrov a duck drawing Thursday.

He put it in his window. It's there in the far books. Faded. Kept. The mending economy runs on exactly such currency.)
