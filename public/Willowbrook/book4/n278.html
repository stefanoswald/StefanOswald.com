Night 278

The Grocery List Scribe

Month 10: Words Are Magic

A promotion this week: Family scribe. The document, preserved: "Mlk" (milk... vowels are expensive; she budgets) "Apls" + a drawing of apples "Brd" + a drawing of a loaf "Egs" + an egg, drawn so round it required a label lest it be read as an O "Chez" (cheese... the scribe's favorite entry; she read it aloud in a French accent invented on the spot, nobody knows from where) And at the market: The reading back. Every aisle, the list consulted, squint-decoded, executed: "Mlk... dat's da milk.

Check. Apls, apples, also dere's a picture, doubly check..." (The clerk... the saintly one... watched the whole operation and said, at checkout: "You know that's the first list in this store's history written by somebody who can't read yet.

I can read my writing..." corrected the scribe. "Dat's da first kind of reading. Reading uddah people's comes next.") Reading your own writing is the first kind of reading. (Developmentally: exactly correct. The scribes always come before the scholars. Ten thousand years ago and every kitchen since. Chez. Check.)
