Night 46

The Two-Bear Patrol

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

The second shared dream came this month. (The rare kind. The twice-a-year kind. Both children woke with matching reports, down to the details.) And what the meadow held this time: a patrol. The bear and the cow... colleagues, allied, walking the meadow's whole perimeter together... "like da gold dog does da lane..."

Per Olivia's report... checking everything: The glow-grass: fine. The lantern fish: counted. (The cow counted. The bell rang once per fish. Eleven fish.) The firefly field: "all lit, all known." The willow's other side: greeted.

And behind the patrol, following like two small deputies: the dreamers themselves, learning the route. "Why do dey patrol?" Mama asked, over the matching breakfasts. And Theo, fragment-style: "Cow says... so da dreams stay nice."

And Olivia, filling in: "Dey check da meadow every night. Even when we're not dere. Dat's da whole soft job, Baba says... day or night, seen or not: somebody soft is always watching over you.

It's da oldest law dey got." (The oldest law. The cow's letter said it, a book ago, from the dresser: somebody soft is always watching. Now it walks the perimeter, nightly, in plush and bell.)

The deputies finished their toast. The patrol, presumably, walked on... other side, all hours, all children, by the law. Sleep easy, dreamers of Willowbrook. The meadow is patrolled. It always was.
