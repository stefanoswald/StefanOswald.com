Night 44

The Rainy Meadow

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

A discovery this week: It rains in the meadow. The dream came silver... rain falling warm (dream-rain is always warm, she reports; "it's da bafwater kind")... and she and the bear sheltered under the dream-willow, watching. The lantern fish rose in the brook to sip the drops.

(Fish drink rain on the other side. Glowing rings spread where they sipped.) The firefly field dimmed to embers, waiting it out. And the bear, watching the weather with her: "Even here, it rains. Good thing too.

Why good? Same as your side. Da rain is how da meadow drinks. No rain, no glow-grass. And..." (the bear-pause; he has the pause; he learned it from her) "...shelters is where da best talks happen.

Nobody talks like dis in da sunshine. Rain builds da huddle. Da huddle builds da talk." Rain builds the huddle. The huddle builds the talk. (True on both sides of every willow. The fort knew it.

The porch knew it. Now the meadow's on record too.) They huddled till the dream-rain passed. Talked about everything. Nothing. The bafwater rain on the leaves. She woke to real rain on the real roof... the same silver sound... and lay there a long minute, listening to both sides at once, holding the bear who'd just been holding the weather with her.

"It's raining in da meadow too," she whispered. It was. It does. The weather, like the willow, goes all the way through.
