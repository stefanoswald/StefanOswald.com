Night 13

The Invisible Door

Month 1: Pretend!

The newest development needs no box, no cape, no props at all: Olivia drew a door in the air. With one finger. A rectangle. Careful corners. Then turned an invisible knob... click... and stepped through. "...

Where are you now? The snow land. Brrr. You can't come in unless you do da door." (Mama did the door. Drew it. Turned the knob. Stepped through. The temperature dropped immediately, you could tell by the shivering, which was mandatory.)

The snow land had: invisible snow, an invisible bear (friendly; not Baba; "a wild one; he knows Baba"), and weather that required huddling. They huddled. It was June. It was freezing. Both true, the family specialty, now with geography.

Other doors followed, daily: The jungle door. The under-da-sea door. The grandma-aunts land. No props. No sets. Just a finger, a rectangle, and a mind that furnishes whole countries on the far side of drawn air.

The pretend has gone portable. She carries every world now, one door away, wherever she stands. Knock anytime. She'll draw you in.
