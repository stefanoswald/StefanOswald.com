Night 341

Theo's Secret Project

Month 12: Almost Four

A mystery this week, behind the couch: Theo. Working. (Paper: ordered. Crayons: five, clutched. Location: the couch-back gap, his office. Security: total. "No wooking, wivy. Is secret.") (A secret. His first. Held... this is the remarkable part, for nine days.

A two-and-a- half-year-old, holding a secret nine days. The strain was visible. He vibrated near her daily. Twice he announced, unprompted, "Is not a picture," which was, of course, intelligence of the highest grade.)

The security breaches, managed: Mama: recruited as consultant. Spelling assistance: one word, whispered, behind the couch. The bear: relocated twice for "wooking." Two figures. Big one, small one. Holding hands, the hands rendered as one shared scribble, because hands are hard and love is approximate.

Above, in letters he made Mama draw dotted so he could crayon over them: "Wivy + feo." And one T... one enormous, sideways, self-made T... signed in the corner. His letter. His mark. First gift he ever made anyone.

(It's framed. The far pages confirm: it hangs in every home she ever keeps. Outlasts frames. Gets reframed. The scribble- hands hold. They always held.) Nine days, behind the couch, tongue out, guarding wonder. The little ones give the biggest way there is: everything they've got, in crayon, signed with their whole alphabet. One letter. Enough.
