Night 292

The Reading Fort

Month 10: Words Are Magic

Discovered this week, at 8:40 pm, forty minutes past lights-out: a glow. Under the blanket. The investigation: The under-blanket den. The flashlight The pillow shape. The bear, propped as audience. And the early reader, open, page six, being worked, whispered, finger under words, past bedtime, alone... "...dog... runs... home.

Dog runs home!" (The parental dilemma, eternal: it's past bedtime. It's also, look at it, a child who taught herself to prefer books to sleep. Forty years of far pages hang on how this moment gets handled.)

The handling: Mama did not turn on the light. Did not confiscate. Knocked, softly... on the doorframe, and said, into the glow: "Ten more minutes, reader. Then lamp off for real... Fort privileges are for finished books and kept promises...

Ten. Promise... Mama? Da dog got home. I read it myself in da dark. That's the best place to read it. Ten minutes." (Ten minutes. Kept, eleven, technically; grace was extended. And the precedent, set that night, held for a childhood: reading earns the extra minutes.

Always. It's the house's one negotiable bedtime, the loophole installed lovingly, on purpose, forever. She found the loophole at four. She used it... the story has counted... four thousand times. The house regrets nothing.)
