Night 90

Oliver Owl's Favorite Question

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

On the last visit of the month, Stella blinked once over the listening tree. And this month of whys closed with the watch's own why... Stella asked it: "Old friend. Centuries on this branch. Every child in this town's history has brought you questions.

Which was the best one?" Oliver Owl was quiet so long the moon hummed a prompting hum. "There was a girl..." He said at last. "long ago, before the lantern, before the bakery, who asked me: 'Why do the askers stop?'

She'd noticed it. The grown ones around her had stopped asking whys. Stopped wondering. Just... walked past the glow, yawned at the stars, buttered the toast without once asking why it browned. 'Why do the askers stop?'

And I've held that one ever since, because it's the only question that guards all the others... And what did you answer her? Per my office: a question back. 'Whoo says you have to?'" The branch was quiet.

"She didn't stop..." The owl added. "Ever. She's the one who hung the first lantern on the library. Went looking for answers her whole long life and left the light on for the next askers."

(The lantern. The library's own gold. Hung by a girl who kept asking. The town's records, somewhere, hold her name. The light holds it better.) "And the small one below?" asked Stella. "The current asker?

Mmm," hummed the Moon... who reads the whole tally book, who countersigns everything... and the hum meant: this one won't stop either. Look at the journal. Look at the wonder-file. Look who's already learning which whys to open and which to keep.

The lantern's next keeper, somewhere down the years. The light stays on. "Log this month..." said Oliver, settling. "Log it under the lantern's own line: the askers, unstopped.", both sides. Goodnight, askers of Willowbrook. Whoo says you have to stop? Nobody. Nobody ever did.
