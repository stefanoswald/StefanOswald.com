Night 68

Why Do We Say Sorry

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

The whys turned inward this week, at the family's own customs: "Why do we say sorry? Da words don't fix da tower." (A real objection. The words don't fix the tower. She's watched it. Sorry plus nothing equals a still-knocked tower.)

The answer, honest as always: "You're right. Sorry doesn't fix towers. Hands fix towers. Sorry fixes something else, the invisible thing that also got knocked. When you hurt somebody, two things fall: the tower... and the trust.

The little bridge between you. Sorry is how you start rebuilding the bridge. That's why our sorry comes with a flower and a fix, the fix for the tower, the flower for the bridge. Words alone are half a repair.

You invented the whole one." She sat with that. Her own ritual, x-rayed, found sound. "... Da flower is for da invisible broken part." The flower is for the invisible broken part. "Den we should always do both."

You always have, little one. Since the first dandelion. You built the whole law before you could explain it. Now you can explain it. That's what the why-year is for: catching up, in words, to everything your hands already knew.
