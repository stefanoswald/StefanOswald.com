Night 306

The Chrysalis Questions

Month 11: Winter Wonders

The library run: made. (Card: used. The butterfly book: borrowed. "For research. Bella's in a green room and I need da facts.") The facts, read at the lamp... and they are, sure enough, the strangest facts in any book this shelf owns: "Inside da chrysalis, da caterpillar...

Papa. Read dat part again. Da part I don't believe. Inside, the caterpillar's old body breaks almost all the way down. Nearly everything melts. And from the melt, a new body builds... wings, new eyes, new legs.

It keeps only a few deep pieces of itself. Scientists call them imaginal discs. The blueprint parts. It melts?! She melted?! On purpose?! And she's not... scared in dere? Nobody knows what it's like in there.

But here's the part I hold onto: it's not breaking. It's rebuilding. The melt isn't the end of the caterpillar. It's the middle of the butterfly." (The middle of the butterfly. Saved. You could watch it get saved.)

"And da... imaginal pieces. Da blueprint ones. Dose stay? Da whole time? The whole time. The deepest parts don't melt. They're what the new body gets built from... So she's still Bella in dere. Even melted.

Da Bella-est parts are da ones dat stay." The Bella-est parts are the ones that stay. (this month's first theorem. It gets bigger. Wait for it.)
