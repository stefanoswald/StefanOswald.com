Night 133

The Tattle Question

Month 5: Little Brother Tags Along

A new dilemma this week, brother-generated: "Mama. Fee-oh's standing on the couch." (He was. Fourth report of the day. The reports were becoming a genre.) Time for the sort: Tattling vs telling. "Here's the line, love.

One question: Is somebody in danger, or is somebody just bothering you? Danger: Tell. Always. Fast. That's not tattling, that's guarding. Couch- standing near the edge? Tell. Something in his mouth? Tell. The street, water, high places: Tell, tell, tell.

Bothering: try your tools first. He took your crayon? You've got words, turns, trades... a whole kit. Come to me when the kit's empty, not before." The test cases, run: "He's touching my tower? He's got a button in his mouth?

He's looking at me funny? He climbed da gate by da stairs?" Line: drawn. Held... imperfectly, humanly, with weekly appeals... but held. And the deeper teaching under it, said at the lamp: "You're his guard, not his police.

Guards watch for danger. Police watch for everything. Nobody wants to live with police. Everybody's glad of a guard." His guard, not his police. She kept the badge. Retired the ticket book. The couch-standing reports dropped to danger-only.

The tower disputes went to the kit. The house, de- policed, guarded well, ran happier within the week.
