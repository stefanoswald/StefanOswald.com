Night 225

The Bird Feeder Round

Month 8: Helping Hands

The kindness detectives closed this case long ago: the church bird feeder. Always full. Filled by whom: Grandpa. (The invisible list. The secret-kindness man. Case sealed, told no one.) This month, with the hip slow and the mornings cold, the apprentice petitioned: "Gampa.

Da feeder. I know it's yours... Can it be ours?" And so, Sundays now, before the farm: the seed round. The sack (she carries the scoop; he carries the sack; the other end, held). The filling.

The retreat to the truck to watch the birds descend... chickadees first, always, the brave little ones. "Why secret, Gampa? Still? Told you. Telling turns gifts into bills. But *I* know now." Rock. Rock. "Dat's not telling.

Dat's training." Training. The invisible list, gaining its apprentice... one scoop, one Sunday, one secret at a time. The chickadees, for the story, know exactly who feeds them. Birds always know. They're just better than towns at keeping it quiet.
