Night 164

The Birthday Party Circuit

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

Preschool brought a new institution: The party circuit. (Twelve classmates. Twelve birthdays. The calendar, suddenly social. Marcus's was first: a bounce house party. The invitation itself caused a household event.) The preparation: The gift: chosen by her, for Marcus.

("He likes trucks and loud. Dis truck is loud. Perfect gift." Correct methodology: the receiver's weird, honored.) The card: a duck driving a truck. The party itself: Loud. Wonderful. Too much, eventually... the bounce house, the sugar, the twelve-kid roar... and at the wall, mid-party, the old flag: hands near ears.

The glassy eye. And the self- deployment... witnessed by the mamas, celebrated quietly forever: she found Mama, took her hand, and said: "I need a porch." I need a porch. (The overwhelm kit, self-administered at a bounce house.

The porch was found, a quiet front step. Five minutes. The world re-sized. Back in for cake.) "Good party? Great party. Loud, den porch, den cake. Dat's da way to do a party." Loud, then porch, then cake.

The circuit ran all year on that formula. Every kid should pack a porch. Hers travels.
