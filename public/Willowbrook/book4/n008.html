Night 8

The Grocery Store Under the Table

Month 1: Pretend!

Under the table today: a store. The tablecloth: walls. The stock: real cans, play fruit, one actual potato The sign: paper. It says, she'll tell you it says, "Olivia's store. Everybody welcome. No sharks." The currency: buttons.

The customers: Papa (bought the potato; paid four buttons; received "change" of nine buttons, the store's economics are generous). Theo the dog (attempted to buy a can with a bark; store accepted; the can was a bone now, apparently). Baba And Mama, at the moment's busiest hour, buying six things she already owned with buttons she'd been issued for the purpose.

"Thank you! Come again!" (Every transaction ends with it. The grocer says it. She's studied the grocer her whole life. Shopkeeping is mostly kindness with a counter, she learned that at the source.) The store ran three days.

Closed for nap daily. It reopened as a post office the next week. Then a bakery. Then a vet. The table's underside became the town's whole economy, one pretend shop at a time... which is exactly how real towns started, if you go back far enough. Ask the market. Ask anywhere.
