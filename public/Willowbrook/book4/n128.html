Night 128

When He Ruined It

Month 5: Little Brother Tags Along

The truth-telling page of this month: he ruined it. The drawing. The drawing, the best duck of her career, an hour's work, intended for Grandpa's birthday... scribbled across. Green crayon. Corner to corner. While she was in the bathroom.

"He ruined it! It was for gampa and it's ruined and he always..." The storm: full, fair, earned. And the handling... note the order, because the order is everything: First: the loss, honored. "That was an hour of your best work.

It's wrecked. That's worth being mad about." (No "he's just little" First. The little-defense can wait. The loss can't. Grief before context. Always.) Then: the context. "And, he's two. Two sees a drawing and wants in it.

He wasn't wrecking. He was joining. Badly." Then: the repair... his (led through a two-year-old sorry: a pat, a flower..." Sowwy Wivy. "the routine, downhill again)... and hers, harder: the forgiving. Which took until dinner.

(Forgiving is allowed to take until dinner. Sometimes longer. It's not a light switch. It's a walk.) And the ending nobody planned: the ruined duck, reconsidered at the easel... "...actually da green makes it look like grass.

Like da duck is in grass." Finished together. Retitled: "Duck in grass, by Olivia and Fee-oh." Grandpa framed it. (Of course he framed it. It hangs in the workshop. One of 'em on purpose.")"
