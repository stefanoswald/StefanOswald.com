Night 76

Why Can't We See the Wind

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

Gusty day. Kite hill. And the why of the week: "Why can't we see da wind? Because air is invisible, even moving air. But...we can see everything it touches. Come on. Wind-detector walk."

The detectors, catalogued on one gusty hill: The grass: bending in long silver wa ves. The kite: climbing, tugging, alive on it. The dandelion seeds: riding it, visible as traffic. Her own hair: streaming. The flag at the library: pointing the way the wind runs.

The clouds: herded slowly east. "Da wind is invisible..." She summarized, hair whipping. "but it can't keep a secret. Everything tattles." Everything tattles. And then Papa gave her the keeper, the one for the shelf of big thoughts: "Lots of the realest things are like wind, little one.

You never see them directly. Only what they move... Like love?" Like love. Exactly. Nobody's ever seen it. You only see what it moves: the casseroles, the held doors, the waiting arms, the whole tattling town.

She stood on the hill in the invisible river, watching everything it touched bend and ride and stream... "Da world is full of invisible movers." It is. You're learning to see every one... by their footprints, by their kites, by what bends when they pass.
