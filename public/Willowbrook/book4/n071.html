Night 71

Why Does Rosa Give Us Extra

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

The why-year reached the bakery: "Rosa. Why do you always give us extra? Da crust ends and da celebration buns and da free tastes. Isn't dat... losing bread?" (An economics question. Asked over the counter, at eye level with the eclairs.

Rosa put down her towel and gave it the full answer.) "Come here, miss. Business lesson. Yes: I give away maybe a basket a day. Crusts, ends, buns for birthdays and bad days. And you know what comes back?

Everything. Your mama's been buying my bread since before you were born... partly because it's good, partly because this counter is kind. The whole lane shops here. When my oven broke, four neighbors showed up with tools by noon.

When I got sick two winters back, this town fed my family for a week. A basket a day, out. A whole town, in. That's not losing bread, little one. That's the best investment on this street."

The extra comes back. "... Da kindness jar!" she said. "But with bread!" The kindness jar, with bread. "Every good business..." said Rosa, handing over, obviously... a crust end. "is a kindness jar with something. Find your something.

Fill the jar. The town does the rest." Saved. Deeply. (The far books show a grown Olivia's whole way of working. It traces here. To a counter, a crust, and a baker who opened the books.)
