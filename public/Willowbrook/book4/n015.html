Night 15

Franklin's Birthday

Month 1: Pretend!

An announcement at breakfast: "It's franklin's birthday." Franklin. The invisible regular. Two sugars. Recently having a hard week. Well, hard weeks deserve good birthdays. The family mobilized. (Completely. Sincerely. This is the house policy on Franklins and it never wavered: you attend the party.

You sing. You mean it.) The preparations: One real cupcake. (Rosa's, from the freezer stash. A candle in it. Real flame.) The chair of honor: draped in a scarf. A crown, placed on the seat.

A card: drawn. A duck, wearing a party hat. ("Franklin loves ducks." Of course he does.) And the song, the whole family, around an empty chair: "...happy birthday, dear franklin..." The candle was blown out by the birthday boy himself.

(Olivia leaned over and did it "for him, because he's shy about fire.") The cupcake was eaten on his behalf, too. (Franklins can't eat. Hostesses must serve. The system works.) "How old is Franklin now?...

A thousand. And free." A thousand and three. The party was ruled his best in a millennium. He seemed, everyone agreed, and nobody could say how they knew, very happy. Invisible friends are real practice for real love: you give the party.

You expect nothing back. You get everything back anyway. Happy birthday, Franklin. A thousand and three looks good on you.
