Night 73

Why Do I Have Two Eyes

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

"Why do I got two eyes? One sees fine." Tonight: the experiment. "Cover one eye. Now... grab my finger. Quick. Now both eyes. Grab. Why?! Each eye sees from a slightly different spot. Your brain takes both pictures and builds depth, the near-and-far of everything.

One eye: the world's a flat picture. Two eyes: it's a place." Then the thumb trick: "Thumb out. Look past it. Close one eye... now switch eyes fast... The thumb jumped! It didn't move. Your eyes live a little apart.

That jump, that's the gap your brain sews shut all day, every second, for free." She switched eyes for ten straight minutes. The thumb jumped hundreds of times. The brain, sewing, sewing. "So my eyes are a team."

A team of two. Like most good things. "Like me and Wiwi. We see different and togedder we see da whole thing."... (the story stops. Rereads that. Two eyes, two friends, stereo vision of the world: the depth comes from the difference.

She derived it from a thumb trick. The why-year is paying dividends nobody budgeted.) Yes, little one. Exactly like that. The difference is where the depth comes from. Keep both eyes. Keep the friend. See the whole thing.
