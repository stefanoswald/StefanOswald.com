Night 307

First Snow on the Cradle

Month 11: Winter Wonders

First snow, this week. Overnight. The town: sugared. The morning: white. And the first thought of the watcher, launched from bed, boots half-on: "Da cradle. Bella's in the snow..." The garden run. The milkweed leaf.

And there: the chrysalis. Frost-jeweled. Snow-capped. Hanging in the white hush, still as ever... "She's freezing. Mama, we have to bring her in, we have to..." And the fact... the impossible, checkable, book-confirmed fact: "Look at the book again, love.

Her kind winters outside. The chrysalis makes its own antifreeze... that's real; that's the actual word... and the cold isn't hurting her. The cold is part of the clock. Her kind needs the winter. The long cold is what tells the body when spring is real.

Bring her inside and the clock breaks, she'd wake too early, into a world with no flowers. The kindest thing we can do is the hardest thing: leave her in the cold. Trust the green room.

It's older than we are." (The watcher, at the leaf, absorbing the hardest kind of caring: the kind that doesn't pick the thing up.) "... So da cold is for her. Da winter's not happening to Bella.

It's happening... For her." (Say it, record. Seven words, age four, frost on the milkweed: the whole Maybe philosophy, found independently, in a garden, about a butterfly. The far pages will say it bigger. None will say it truer.)

The cradle stayed out. Snow-capped. Trusted. The watcher visited daily. Mittened. Unworried now. Winter kept its clock.
