Night 169

The First Friend Fight at School

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

It happened at the easel. The timer turned. Lily said Olivia's turn ran long. Olivia said the sand was slow today. Words got pointy. "You're not da timer boss. You're not da sand." And then: the not-talking.

A whole day of it. Parallel cubbies, zero words. Snack apart. The circle sat with a gap in it. (Miss Dana, veteran, let it run. Some weather has to rain. She watched the radar and kept the room.)

The repair came at the fence, at pickup, where the walls are lower, via the old tools, rustier at the new address but working: The weather reports, exchanged: "... You made me mad at da timer. You made me mad at da sand."

The second-sorry grade truths: "I did watch da sand slow. I wanted more turn. I did boss. I wanted da easel." And the treaty, per four books of precedent: "... Build again? Build again." (At the easel, next day: a joint painting.

One paper, two brushes, the timer retired for the session by mutual treaty. The result, half ducks, half flowers, signed twice... hangs in both houses. Copies. Rosa laminated them.) First school fight: weathered. The friendship, proofed at the new address... same as the old address, same as every address coming: storms pass. Towers rebuild. Best friends are the ones you paint the joint one with, after.
