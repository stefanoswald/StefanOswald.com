Night 98

The Brave List

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

A new document this week, posted by the cozy corner, next to the body map: The brave list. The rule: every scared- plus-anyway, written down. Evidence. For consulting on dark nights. The founding entries, spoken: "Da ear light at da doctor.

(Baby times. Mama says it counts.)Da big slide. Da pool. Da whole first day. Da big flush. Da storm with zero gap. Rode it. Free tools. Da under-bed. (It was fluffy. I own it now.)Da shadow.

(It was a curtain. I do da show myself now.)Da first sleepover try." (Failed gloriously. Counts double, ruled Mama. Attempts are brave squared.) And the list's use, proven within the week, a hard bedtime, a new noise, the old what-if rising...

"Bring da list." The list, brought. Read aloud. Entry by entry. "... I did all dose?" Every one. "Den dis noise is just... da next one." Just the next one. (That's the list's whole machinery: fear says 'you can't.'

The list says 'you have. Repeatedly. It's documented.') The noise was investigated. The list gained its ninth entry by morning. It gained entries for years... new schools, big stages, deep water, harder things... the crayon giving way to pencil to pen, what they saw compounding, the answer to every 'you can't' growing longer and longer: I have. Repeatedly. It's documented. Start your kid's tonight. Start yours.
