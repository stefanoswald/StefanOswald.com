Night 37

What the Bear Knows

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

The dream walks have become conversations. And the bear, it turns out, knows things. (Of course he knows things. He's been listening for three years. Every secret, every bedtime, every cried-into night. Bears are vaults... and now the vault talks.)

This week's meadow questions: "Baba. Why does June's gramma have to be far away sick?" And the bear, walking slow, ear flopped... "I don't know why, little one. Bears don't get da whys. But I know what you do about far-away sad.

You learned it already. Da bench thing. Sitting close and staying. Dat's it. And when da person is too far to sit with, you sit close to somebody who loves dem. June's close. Sit dere."

(She did. Thursdays. The bear's counsel, walked out in daylight.) "Baba. Are you scared of anything?" A long bear-pause. "... Moths. Just one moff, really. We had an incident. Da dresser years. I don't want to talk about it."

(A bear with a moth incident. The dream dissolved in giggles. Even vaults have comedy. Especially vaults.) The answers, note them, are all hers... her own tools, her own bench, her own family's wisdom, spoken back by the softest voice she knows.

That's what loved bears are for, on the far side: they keep everything you gave them, and hand it back exactly when you need it, walking beside you, at meadow pace.
