Night 150

Keeping Theo

Month 5: Little Brother Tags Along

At bedtime Mama asked Olivia what she remembered most about the month she had spent learning to be Theo's big sister.

Olivia counted on her fingers.

"I taught him the jacket flip. I gave him my red tricycle. He put his handprint beside mine in the fort. I pulled out his splinter even though he yelled."

She paused.

"I also told him too many rules. I missed him when he slept at the farm. And I repaired his duck after pretending I did not care that it broke."

From the other room came Theo's voice. "Livy! Water!"

Olivia sighed with enormous seriousness and carried him his cup.

When she returned, Mama asked, "So what have you decided about having a little brother?"

"He is a lot of work," Olivia said. "He touches my things, asks why forever, and needs water after bedtime."

Mama waited.

Olivia climbed beneath the blanket. "But I am keeping him."

Across the hall, Theo called, "Keeping Livy too!"

Olivia smiled into the dark. That settled the matter.
