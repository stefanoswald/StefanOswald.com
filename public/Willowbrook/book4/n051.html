Night 51

The Dream Picnic

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

The third shared dream... the rarest kind, the whole-family-of-dreamers kind... came this month, and it came as a picnic. Both reports agreed: a blanket on the glow-grass. (Checked: "da yellow one, but da dream one."

The softest blanket in the world has an other-side edition. Of course it does.) The company: Olivia. Theo. The bear, presiding. The cow, bell chiming softly with every gesture. And one warm spot on the blanket corner where the grass bent slightly: Franklin, attending.

The menu: dream food. ("It tastes like warm. All of it. But da berries taste like warm-sweet.") The entertainment: lantern fish, leaping at dusk. The firefly field, rising all at once "like da sky practicing."

And the bear's toast... raised with a berry, reported verbatim by his girl: "To da whole soft family. Da seen ones and da unseen ones. Da real ones and da real-in-here ones. Dere's room at dis blanket for all of it.

Dat was always da plan." That was always the plan. Two children woke smiling on the same morning, in rooms across one hall, having picnicked together in a country their parents can't visit... and told it all at breakfast over real berries that tasted, everyone agreed, a little warmer than usual.
