Night 228

The Shelving Volunteer

Month 8: Helping Hands

The Thursday library run gained a duty: The returns cart. (The picture-book shelf. Low. Hers- height. The librarian's offer: "My volunteer could shelve the returns. If she shelves like she sorts.") She shelves like she sorts.

The standards, absorbed instantly: Spine out. ("Books talk with deir spines. Backwards books are mumbling.") Right section. (Animals with animals. Trucks with trucks. "Misc is where shelving goes to die," the law, third deployment.) And gently.

("Day-old books deserve dignity too. Especially da loved-soft ones.") Twenty minutes, Thursdays. The cart, emptied. The shelf, mumble-free. And in the wainscoting, the list updated by its permanent staff: volunteer, second generation, shelves true.

The collection is in good paws. (Molly's endorsement, the town's records note, is the library's highest. It has been issued eleven times in ninety years. The list is kept behind the wall. Her name is on it. She'll never know. The real ones never do.)
