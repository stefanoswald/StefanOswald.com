Night 154

The Night Before School

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

The night before school, Olivia laid out her clothes and checked her backpack twice: lunchbox, sweater, and Bobby Bear's tiny cape, though the bear himself would stay home after the first visit.

Everything was ready. Olivia was not. "I feel excited-scared," she told Mama in the dark.

"Both at once?" Mama asked. Olivia nodded. "The excited part wants morning now. The scared part wants morning never."

Mama sat on the bed, and Olivia asked the question she had saved all evening. "Will you really come back after school?"

"Yes. Ms. Alvarez will be with you, and after dismissal I will be waiting at the same door where we say goodbye."

Olivia checked every possibility. What if she cried? Mama would come. What if she had fun and forgot to miss her? Mama would still come.

School could contain tears or fun or both. None of those things changed the ending: Mama at the door. Olivia held Bobby Bear close. "All right," she whispered. "Morning may come."

Outside, the town went on preparing it.
