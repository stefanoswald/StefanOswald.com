Night 354

The Jar Harvest

Month 12: Almost Four

The morning after Maybe night: The harvest. (The other half of the rite. The jar, emptied on the rug. The year's slips... all of them, dated, saved... read back, one by one, to see what the year did with them.)

The check, slip by slip: "Maybe preschool is happening for me." (Autumn slip. Answer, shouted: "It was. Miss Dana. Wren. Marcus. Da cubby next to Lily. Confirmed.") "Maybe da watching is happening for Wren." (Winter.

"Confirmed. She read two ghosts out loud. Kind of loud even.") "Maybe da green room is happening for Bella." (The big one. The whole winter's wager. Held up. Held quiet a moment. "... Confirmed da most.

She came out bigger. Da melting was da making. I watched da whole thing.") "Maybe da moving is happening for Lily." (Old slip. Two years old, carried forward annually. "Confirmed forever. Window seat. Whistle code.

Two streets made us better.") Mama. Dis one's yours. It is. Confirmed?" And Mama... looking at the rug, the slips, the whole checked year of this family... "Confirmed, love. Every hard night so far. Turns out they were building this."

The slips: bundled, ribboned, boxed with the three elder bundles. The jar: washed. Reshelfed. Empty. "Ready for Four's maybes," said the harvester. Ready for Four's. (The jar never once went hungry. The far pages confirm: this family never ran out of maybes. That's not luck. That's the practice. Harvest. Reseed. Repeat.)"
