Night 77

Why Do Songs Get Stuck in Heads

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

The morning's crisis: "Da hello song is stuck. In my head. It keeps singing without me!" (The hello song. Her own composition. Mostly names. Now looping, apparently, on internal repeat.) "Why do songs get stuck?

Because your brain loves circles. A good song is a circle, it ends by pointing at its own start. And your brain, when it likes a shape, runs it again. And again. Like the zoomies, but inside.

The stuck song is your brain doing laps with something it loves. How do I make it stop? Two ways. Sing it all the way to the end, big finish... sometimes the circle just needs to land.

Or...give the brain a different circle." The replacement was deployed: the washing anthem. It worked. For an hour. Then the washing anthem was stuck. Then, by dinner, via contagion, it was stuck in Mama.

Then Papa. Then Theo hummed it in his sleep, verified, monitor, 9:40 p.m. One family. One loop. Four heads. "Da songs move in with us," she concluded. They do. The whole family songbook, the wrong notes, the anthems, the hello song of names, lives in there now, doing laps, circling forever, which is, if you think about it... exactly what it was written to do.
