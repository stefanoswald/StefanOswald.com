Night 106

Upstairs Alone

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

A quiet milestone this week: "I'm gonna go play upstairs. By myself." By herself. Upstairs. Alone. (The house's second floor, solo, is a real frontier at three-and-a-half. It's not the rooms. It's the between... the being where the family sounds go faint, where the house's own sounds get louder, where alone becomes audible.)

The first expedition: eight minutes. (Then the stairs, casually: "I just came to get a thing." The thing: unspecified. The real thing: a top-up of family noise. Legal. Wise, even.) The second: fifteen. And the tool that made it work, taught at the first expedition: The sound names.

"The house talks upstairs. Learn its words: That creak: the hall floor, cooling. That tick: the attic, warming. That hum: the pipes, working. That's not spooky talk. That's house- talk. It's saying 'I'm here. I'm holding everything.

Carry on.'" House-talk. "And if you hear a sound that's not on the list? Come tell me. We'll lamp it." (The lamp rule: portable, eternal. Zero off-list sounds, all year. The list holds.

The house talks. She answers, some days: "I know, house. Carry on.") Twenty minutes, alone, sovereign, unafraid... brave list, entry eleven. The house held her. Houses do. It's what they're for... and now she speaks enough house to know it.
