Night 347

The Aunts' Package

Month 12: Almost Four

The package: arrived. (The aunts'. Annual. Brown paper, twine, postmarked far, and opened, per family tradition, early: "Da aunts' box doesn't wait for da day. Da aunts say love ships when it ships.") (The star sweater.

Fourth edition. This year's: deep evening blue, and on it... counted instantly, fingers to each... Four stars. "One... two... free... Four. Dey knitted me da new number! Da sweater knows before I'm even dere!") (Four gold stars on evening blue.

The night sky, wearable. The aunts' whole theory of birthdays, in wool: you are accumulating light. Dress accordingly.) And the letter... read aloud by the reader herself this year, first time, slowly, the aunts' looping hand decoded word by patient word: "Dear Olivia.

Four stars now. We knit them on knowing you earned every one. Your Mama tells us: The deep end. Da letters. Da butterfly you kept company all winter. Good girl. Keep company with waiting things.

It's da family trade. All our love, da aunts." Keep company with waiting things. It's the family trade. The sweater: worn immediately. The letter: saved in the treasure box with its three elders. And at the mirror, turning, stars catching lamplight: "Every year anudder star.

By da time I'm big I'll be a whole constellation." That's the plan, little one. That was always the aunts' plan. Wear your years. Shine accumulating.
