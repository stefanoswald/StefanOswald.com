Night 332

The Morning Bella Came Back

Month 11: Winter Wonders

It was a Tuesday. (as it happened it because she notes it: "Da best things happen on regular days. Dat's da trick of dem.") Morning. Sun already warm on the south bed. The routine cradle check, oatmeal still on the spoon... and then the shout that pulled the whole family to the garden in pajamas: "It's open it's open she's coming out she's...

Everybody!!" The emergence, as witnessed by four humans, one bear, and the entire morning: The split silk. The slow, careful, upside-down arrival, legs first, then the crumpled everything of her, wings packed wet and small as folded letters...

"Is she okay?! Da wings are wrong... Wait. Watch. This is the part." And the pumping: slow. Patient. The wings filling... visibly, minute by minute, creases becoming curves becoming... Color. The full rainbow, opening in the sun for the first time.

Bigger than before. Brighter than before. An hour, the whole unfolding. Nobody moved. Oatmeal: abandoned. And then Bella... new wings, old self, the Bella- est parts and then some... lifted off the milkweed, circled the garden once, twice... and landed, one long moment, on a small outstretched hand that had reported to her all winter. "...

Hi..." whispered the watcher. "You heard. You heard da whole time." (Then gone, up, over the fence, into the spring... the way rainbows go. Not gone- gone. Never gone-gone. The garden's hers all summer.

This family knows how seasons work now.) And Theo, into the silence, pointing, with his whole arm, with his whole self: "Fly!" Fly. Her word. Kept warm all winter. Given back.
