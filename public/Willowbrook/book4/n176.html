Night 176

The Thanksgiving Program

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

The school's gratitude program: her first stage. (A real one. The multi-purpose room. Folding chairs. Every family. Phones aloft like a vigil.) The class's number: the thankful song. (Composed by Miss Dana. Twelve verses were threatened; four were performed; each kid got a "thankful for" solo line.)

The rehearsal intelligence, leaked at dinner all week: Marcus: thankful for trucks. Lily: "my mama's bakery smell." Wren: (negotiated down to a wave. The wave, Miss Dana ruled, counts as his verse. Everybody gets liked how they like, the law, institutional now.)

And hers, kept secret..." You hear it at da show. Dat's da rules of shows. "The performance: twelve kids, paper feathers, assorted keys. Her line, when it came, clear, loud, aimed at the third row where her whole row of people sat: "I'm thankful for my whole family... da big ones and da little one and da far ones and da soft ones and da town."

(The big ones, the little one, the far ones, the soft ones, and the town. The complete count. Baba, counted, publicly, from a stage. The aunts, attending by earlier train, wept in row three. Theo yelled "Wivy!" at the silence after her line and brought down the house.)

The program: twelve minutes. The pride: measureless. The macaroni-and- feather headpiece: preserved by the aunts, framed, shipped north, displayed forever. First stage. First public count. The town: thanked. It thanked her back, in folding- chair applause, the only way towns can.
