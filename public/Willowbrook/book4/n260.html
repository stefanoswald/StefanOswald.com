Night 260

The Brave List, Read Whole

Month 9: Brave Is Scared Plus One Step

Month's-end ritual, new this year: The brave list, read whole. (It runs two pages now. Crayon giving way to Mama's print, one guest entry in big-kid letters. M's, from the race. The document of documents.)

Read aloud, entry by entry, at the lamp: The ear light. The big slide. The pool. The flush. The under- bed. The farm dark and its fuller stars. The solo night. The bridge, hand under hand.

The gutters. The race. And this month's harvest, fresh: the deep end. The raised hand at circle. The solo line, sung scared-and. The booster, watched, 0.5 Duchesses. The basement, furnished. The market plan, run scared and perfect.

The sticky-mean words, unmadesticky in public. The reader chair. "... Dat's a lot of scared-plus..." She said at the end, at the weight of it. A lot of scared- plus. "And look what none of dem are anymore."

(None of them. Every entry: a retired fear or a won stage or a crossed rope. The list is a museum that keeps becoming a résumé.) "Da list is getting long." The list gets long, little one.

That's the design. Long enough, eventually, that when some far- page fear says you can't... the answer isn't even brave anymore. It's just: the story. Have. Repeatedly. Documented. Next entry, please.
