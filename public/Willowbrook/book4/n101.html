Night 101

The Big Unknown Dog

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

A new dog on the lane this week. A big one. Unknown. Loud-barked. Straining its leash toward everything. And the fear... reasonable, correct fear, arrived on schedule. (Note the difference, because she did: the gold dog is a friend.

This is a stranger. Fear of strange big dogs isn't a fear to fix. It's judgment, working. This month isn't about erasing fear. It's about sorting it.) The rule, taught at safe distance: Stop your feet.

Quiet your body. No reaching. Ever. Not even friendly- looking ones. And the magic words, aimed at the owner: "Can I say hi to your dog?" (The owner answers for the dog. Owners know. Some say yes.

Some say "he's nervous, better not"... and that answer gets a thank-you too, because it's a gift: the truth about this dog.) This owner said: "He's still learning manners. Better just wave." They waved.

From distance. The big dog barked. The wave stood. "Dat dog is a yet..." She ruled, walking on. "He can't do gentle... yet. Somebody's teaching him." A yet-dog. Correct filing. Respected boundary, zero panic, judgment intact.

The fear did its one good job... kept her feet stopped... and then handed the moment to the rule. That's the whole system, working: fear as smoke alarm, not as fire. Alarm heard. House checked. Walk continued.
