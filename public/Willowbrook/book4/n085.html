Night 85

Why Is Water Wet

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

Bath night. And the why that broke the system: "Papa. Why is water wet?... Because it's... water. But why is it wet? Wet is... what water is. Wet is water touching things. So water isn't wet till it touches?...

I. Hm." (Papa, physicist of oranges and flashlights, conqueror of bounced skies, stalled. Completely. Is water wet, or is wet what water does? The question has broken better men. There are internet arguments. There are philosophy papers.)

"Let me try again. Wet is the feeling of water... Den is da water wet when nobody feels it? Okay. New position. Water is wet the way... the way silly is silly. It just is its own thing...

Dat's not a why, Papa. I know it's not a why." (Collapse. Both of them. The bath water, wet, or whatever it is... splashed appreciatively.) The final ruling, issued from the bubbles by the question's author: "Water is wet because if it wasn't, it would just be... loose air."

Loose air. (Water: the alternative to loose air. The story submits this to the philosophy papers. It's as good as anything they've got.) The why-journal took the entry under a new heading, first of its kind: "Unanswerable.

Gloriously. See also: wonder-file." Some whys open doors. Some whys open laughing. Keep both kinds coming, little one. The bath is wet either way. Probably.
