Night 75

Why Do We Have Names

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

"Why do we got names?" Dinner. The whole table took it. "So we can find each other..." said Mama. "Imagine calling 'Hey girl' at the park... forty heads turn. 'Olivia', one head. Yours. A name is an arrow that only hits you."

And then, better... the stories: "Your name, we chose from a list a mile long. We said hundreds out loud. And when we said 'Olivia'... you kicked. Inside. Hard. Three times. I picked it?! You voted, anyway.

We counted it. Theo means 'gift.' We knew by then what was arriving. Papa's name was his grandpa's. Names travel down like chairs. And Mama's means 'wished-for.' Grandma waited a long time for her."

The table sat in its own name-stories a moment, four arrows, four exactly- one-persons, each word carrying its little history. "... Baba's name? You made that one. Age one. It's yours and his. And franklin?"

Franklin, presumably, named himself. A thousand years back. Nobody's asked. "Names are little homes..." She concluded. "dat words live in." Names are little homes that people live in, love. Yours has you. It always kicked when called.

It still does... watch her, any park, any day: "Olivia!" One head. Every time. Home.
