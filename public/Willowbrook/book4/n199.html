Night 199

The Missed Nap Maybe

Month 7: The Maybe Lessons

Theo skipped the nap. "... Maybe?" offered his sister, dubious. (Some materials resist the lens. A napless two-year-old is dense material.) But: the crash came early. Dinner-early. Bath moved up. Bed at six-thirty... except...

"Papa. It's getting dark and he's still awake and he's never awake at dark-dark..." The realization: Theo, early-to-bed, summer-scheduled, his whole life... had never really seen stars. The detour: five minutes.

The yard. The blanket. One exhausted two- year-old, held against Papa's chest, blinking up as the first stars came out... "... Lights..." He said. Croaky with tired. Pointing with his whole arm. "Wivy. Da sky gots lights."

The sky's got lights. (His first stars. Age two-and-a-half. Delivered by a ruined nap. His sister showed him the gold one... "dat's Della; she's ours; wave"... he waved; the story declines, as ever, to explain what twinkled.)

The slip: "Fee-oh skipped da nap. Seemed very bad. He met da stars. First ones. He waved at Della. Some maybes cost a whole afternoon and pay in sky. Caught."
