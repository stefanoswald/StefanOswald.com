Night 291

The Unanswerable Spelling

Month 10: Words Are Magic

It had to happen. She met the ough words. "Papa. Dis word is t-h-r-o-u-g-h. Through. Okay. Weird but okay. But dis one is t-h-o-u-g-h... same letters almost exactly... and it says doh?!

And Mama said dere's more?!" There's more. The demonstration... Papa at the whiteboard (yes, the house has acquired a whiteboard; the letter era demanded it): Tough. Through. Though. Thought. Bough. Cough. "Six sounds. The same four letters.

Six sounds. Who did dis?! Nobody did it. Everybody did it. English is a river that swallowed five other rivers. The spellings are fossils... each one still spelled the way some drowned river said it eight hundred years ago.

The letters are loyal to dead sounds. It's not logic. It's archaeology... So every weird spelling is a fossil? Da word's got its old bones still in it?" Still in it. "Den da wanted posters are wrong.

Da words aren't outlaws. Dey're... Ghosts. Dey're haunted by how dey used to sound." The posters came down that week. Replaced by a new taxonomy, her own: "Words with ghosts." (Etymology, age four: every irregular a haunting, every silent letter a small polite ghost, still holding its place in line centuries after its sound went quiet.

She never hated an irregular again. You can't hate a ghost. You can only learn its name and let it haunt you politely. Knight. Silent K. Ghost armor. Saved. Loved.)
