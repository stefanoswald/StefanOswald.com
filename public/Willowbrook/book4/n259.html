Night 259

The Try-Out Brave

Month 9: Brave Is Scared Plus One Step

The library's story hour tried something new this month: "Would any children like to share a page? Read, or tell?" The hands: none. The silence: long. And then, scared, plus one step, hot-chested, hand up: "I can't read da words yet.

But I can tell da bear book. I know it by heart. All da hearts." (All the hearts. The librarian wrote that down mid-sentence.) The telling: the reader chair. The bear book, held facing out per the professional method.

The tale, told... lost button, gasp, found button, nigh-nigh... to eleven littler kids, rapt. The applause: real. And in the wainscoting: the list's oldest entry type, made once a decade, maybe: "Volunteer, age 4: first public reading.

The lantern's succession is not in doubt. Note: 'all da hearts.' Keep that phrasing. It's the whole job description." The reader chair knew her ever after. The letters came, in their season. The telling never stopped. It's where the reading lived all along.
