Night 327

The Cradle Check, Late Winter

Month 11: Winter Wonders

The daily report run, this week... routine, mittens, "End of report"... except. Except. "Mama. Mama come. Come now. The cradle. It changed." The chrysalis... jade-green all winter, opaque, patient... has gone thin. Dark. And through the silk wall, unmistakable, folded like a promise half-unwrapped: Color.

Wing color. Rainbow, packed tight, pressing its pattern against the inside of the green room like a secret that's done being one. "Dat's her. I can see her. Da wings are in dere and dey're made and dey're...

Mama, dey're folded, da whole rainbow's folded up like... like da letter in da envelope! She's almost done." (Almost done. The book confirms: the transparency is the last chapter. Days now. Not weeks. The melt is over.

The building is over. What's left is only the waiting for the right warm morning.) The report, delivered that evening, voice low, mitten on the milkweed stem: "Bella. I saw you. Through da wall. You kept da Bella- est parts and you made new ones.

I saw da colors, dey're yours, dey're still yours, dey're more yours... Da book says soon. Da warm mornings are coming. I ordered da whole summer, me and Grandpa, it's in da mail. Come out when you're ready.

We're all here. End of report." (The watch, all winter, kept. The green room, all winter, working. Both nearly done now. Some nights the whole garden feels like the week before a birthday. This is one. Soon, watcher. The story promises: soon.)
