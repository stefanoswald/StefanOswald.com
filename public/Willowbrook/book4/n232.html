Night 232

The School Plant Break

Month 8: Helping Hands

Winter break loomed. And Miss Dana asked the classroom: "Who can take the plants home? Two weeks. They need a waterer." Twelve hands. One chosen, on badges: "Olivia. You have... references." (The fern. Its wilting.

Its recovery. Its boss-hood. The story had reached school via show-and-tell, naturally. The fern is famous. The fern has a following.) The plants, the classroom's three: the spider one, the succulents, the avocado experiment... came home in a box with instructions.

The service, two weeks, flawless: watered per schedule. Rotated toward light. Talked to ("plants like knowing you remember dem"... the jar law, extended to flora). And returned, January, green, thriving... the avocado experiment having grown a whole new leaf over break, presented like a diploma.

"Da fern taught me everything," she told Miss Dana, professionally. The fern's territory: two towns wide now. The references: impeccable and growing.
