Night 268

The Splinter Extraction

Month 9: Brave Is Scared Plus One Step

The fourth branch sent her home with a souvenir: a splinter. The options, presented honestly: "We can take it out, tweezers, a pinch, ten seconds. Or leave it and it'll work itself out in a few days but might get sore.

Patient's choice... Take it out. But I want to watch. It's my hand. I watch what happens to my hand." The extraction, kitchen table, lamplight: The tweezers. The breath. The still hand... Still, note, the whole ten seconds, watching her own repair like a junior surgeon... and: Out.

Held up. Examined. "Dat little thing made all dat hurt? It's barely a crumb! Most hurts are smaller than they feel, once they're out where you can see them." (Said about splinters. True about splinters.

Not only about splinters... the lamp has run this exact procedure on worries for two years: out, into the light, where they're barely a crumb. Same table. Same method. The tweezers are optional.) The palm: washed, bandaged.

The splinter: kept. ("Evidence. Da fourth branch bit me and I climbed it anyway. Dat's a list entry with a exhibit.") A list entry with an exhibit. The museum grows.
