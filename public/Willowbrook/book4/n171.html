Night 171

The Rest Time Truce

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

The one hard part of school, admitted at week six: Rest time. ("I don't nap, Mama. I stopped napping forever ago. But da mats come out and everybody has to lie dere. For a thousand minutes.")

Forty. It's forty minutes. At four, horizontal and awake, it's a thousand. The truce, negotiated with Miss Dana (who has met every non-napper in fourteen years): The quiet resting tier: for non- sleepers. Requirements: on the mat.

Still-ish. Silent. Allowed: one book. One bear. Your own head. And the reframe, delivered at home, that turned the truce into a skill: "Rest time isn't fake-sleeping. It's stillness practice. Your body idling.

Your head free-playing. Some of your best castles will get built lying still with your eyes open. Watch." And so it went: forty minutes, thrice weekly, of mat-bound stillness, the bear in the crook, the book read twice, and then... the head, free- playing.

(The boat weeks got plotted at rest time. The show-and-tell speech: composed on the mat. Whole meadow expansions: architected horizontal. The stillness, mastered, became the studio. It does for everyone who ever learns to lie quietly with their own head... rarest skill in the loud world, taught by a mat, forty minutes at a time.)

"I builded a whole castle today..." She reported. "At rest. With my eyes open." The best ones get built there, little one. Rest on. The studio's always open.
