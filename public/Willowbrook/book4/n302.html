Night 302

The Last Page Problem

Month 10: Words Are Magic

A new grief this week. The reader's own kind: The last page. "... Dat's all? But I know dem now. Da dog. Da ball. Da whole family in da books. And it just... Ends? Books end?"

The consolation, at the lamp... the library's own law: "Books end, love. That's part of what makes them books. A story that never ended wouldn't be a story, it'd just be weather. The ending is where the book hands you back.

But here's what every reader learns tonight, on some night exactly like this one: the shelf doesn't end. There's always a next book. Always. You will never, in your whole life, run out. You couldn't if you tried...

Never? Dere's dat many? There's that many. And more being written right now. Somebody somewhere is writing your next favorite book tonight." (The tears, drying into the only thing big enough to dry them: appetite.)

"Den we go to da library tomorrow. Four at a time. Forever." Four at a time. Forever.
