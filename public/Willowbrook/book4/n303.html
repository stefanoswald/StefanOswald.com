Night 303

The World in Words

Month 10: Words Are Magic

During words month, Willowbrook began to look different to Olivia.

Letters she had always seen became things she could use. She wrote her name, helped Theo find the T in his, received her first library card, and read a whole sentence without help.

She made a tiny book, a letter, a poem, and a menu. When a favorite page tore, she learned that books could be mended instead of discarded.

One afternoon she and Mama walked past the bakery.

"Fresh bread," Olivia read from the window.

At the corner she sounded out STOP. At the library she recognized RETURN BOOKS HERE.

"Those signs were talking before," she said. "I just couldn't hear them."

At bedtime she added one word to the maybe jar and wrote the label herself.

Words did not make the world new. They opened doors that had been standing in front of her all along.

Olivia turned off the lamp and pictured the town outside, quietly covered in messages she would learn to read tomorrow.
