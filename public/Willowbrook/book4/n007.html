Night 7

The Band-Aid Hospital

Month 1: Pretend!

The hospital opened at nine. The couch: the ward. The patients, admitted in order of drama: Baba: "broken ear. Da floppy one. It's extra floppy today." The cow: "swallowed a bell." (He has always had a bell inside.

Today it's a medical condition.) Duck the doll: "too warm." (Fevers are big this season.) The rabbit: "sad." (Also admitted. Sad counts. This hospital learned from the bench.) The doctor made rounds with her kit: a real band-aid budget, the toy stethoscope, and the treatment every patient received regardless of diagnosis: "You'll be okay.

I'm here. Dis will help." (The words. The words. Poured into her at every fever and bump of her whole life, pouring out now onto plush.) The ear was bandaged. The bell was "operated" The fever was cooled with a washcloth.

The sad was sat with. All patients recovered by lunch. (They always recover by lunch. Best hospital in the county. The doctor has the touch. She learned it by being the patient... which is how all the good ones learn.)
