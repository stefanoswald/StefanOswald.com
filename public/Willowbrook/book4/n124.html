Night 124

The Slow Walk, Slower

Month 5: Little Brother Tags Along

The walk to the corner. With Theo walking. The pace: archaeological... times two. Every pebble: His now. (She's seen these pebbles. Years of these pebbles. But he's meeting them... and meetings take time.) Every gate: tested.

Every crack: stepped over twice. The corner: forty minutes out. And the impatience... real, fair, rose... and met the old teaching, coming back around: "Mama. Is dis how slow I was? Slower. You once spent an hour on one block.

There's a whole story about it somewhere." (There is. Book three. The archaeology pages. Her own pebble era, documented.) "... An hour?" An hour. She looked at her brother, crouched, rapt, interviewing a crack in the sidewalk... and something settled.

"Take your time, Fee-oh..." She said, crouching down beside him. "Pebbles are good. Dis one's got sparkles, look." The corner: reached, eventually. Or not. Nobody remembers. The pebble had sparkles. That was the walk. That was always the walk.
