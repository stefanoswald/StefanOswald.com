Night 105

The Deep End Line

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

Swim lessons, year two. And the pool has a geography now that she reads: the shallow steps. The middle. (Lesson country. Teacher-held.) And past the rope... the floating rope line, the buoys... The deep end.

Where the blue goes down. Where the big kids cannonball. Where the bottom is a rumor. "Is da deep end scary?" The honest sort: "It's not scary. It's early. Deep water is for swimmers with more skills than you have...

Yet." Yet. "Da rope is a yet-line." The rope is a yet-line. (File that. The whole month's sorting, mastered: some lines are danger-lines... the street, the stove. Some are yet-lines... the deep end, the big slide, the two- wheeler.

Danger-lines you never cross. Yet-lines you grow across. Knowing which is which is half of childhood. Maybe half of everything.) "When do I cross it? When your swimming crosses it first. The skills go over the rope before the swimmer does.

That's the order. Every big kid over there did it in that order." She watched the deep end a while, the cannonballs, the easy crossers... not with fear. With schedule. "Okay. Shallow work today. Rope later.

I'm growing at it." Shallow work today. Rope later. (Two summers later, as it happened. Skills first, per the order. The crossing itself: almost boring. The best crossings are. That's how you know the order held.)
