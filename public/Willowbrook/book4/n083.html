Night 83

Why Do We Get Older

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

At the door frame, studying the ladder of marks: "Why do we only get older? Why not backward sometimes?" (Why not backward. The whole species has asked. She got there at three.) "Time's a river, love.

It only runs one way. Nobody knows why, that one's in the wonder- file with the willow. But here's what I know about the river: every stretch has its own treasure. Baby-you could be carried everywhere... but couldn't whistle.

Now-you whistles... but fits in fewer laps. Seven-you will read alone, and miss being read to. Grown-you gets the whole world, and keeps a little homesickness for this exact kitchen. Nobody gets to hold all the treasures at once.

The river's deal is: each one, fully, as you pass." She thought about it. "... So we should grab da now-treasures. While it's now." Grab the now-treasures while it's now. "What's a now-treasure?" she asked, testing.

"This..." said Mama, and scooped her up... still liftable, still lap-sized, this exact year, this exact kitchen... "this is one. I'm grabbing it." It was grabbed. It was held a long time. The river ran on regardless, per its deal... but that stretch, that exact golden stretch, got fully, entirely passed through awake. Which is the only thing the river ever asked.
