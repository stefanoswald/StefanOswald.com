Night 108

What Papa Is Scared Of

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

Gutter-cleaning day. The tall ladder. "Papa. Are you scared?" And here's the fork every parent meets on every ladder: the "no, I'm fine"... or the truth. He took the truth: "Yes. A little. Heights make my tummy drop and my hands sweat.

Always have... Papas get scared?? Everybody gets scared, remember? Same list, bigger coats. Watch what I do with mine: I check the ladder twice. (Scared makes me careful, that's its one good job.) I do the breath.

I don't look down more than I need. And I do it anyway, because the gutters need me and the scared isn't the boss." Scared-plus-anyway. The formula. Live. At altitude. In a grown man.

"You're being brave right now. I'm being brave right now. It doesn't retire, kiddo. You don't grow out of scared. You just get more practiced at the anyway." She held the ladder after that. (It didn't need holding.

It absolutely needed holding. Both.) And at dinner, at the feeling check, Papa said "proud" and she said why and he told the table about the ladder, all of it, sweaty hands included... and the brave list gained its first grown-up entry, by unanimous vote: "Papa.

Da gutters. Scared plus anyway. Da formula works on big ones too." It works on big ones too. It works all the way up. Every ladder of it.
