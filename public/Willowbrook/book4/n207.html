Night 207

The Spilled Seeds Maybe

Month 7: The Maybe Lessons

Garden prep. The seed packets. And... the drop. The whole packet. (Wildflower mix. A hundred seeds. Scattered, gone... into the grass along the fence, unfindable, un- plantable, un- rowable.) "Dey're lost. Da whole flower plan...

Maybe." (The longest-game maybe of this month. Because seeds, the paper- cup lesson, years old now, are buried yets. And these just buried themselves.) The waiting: all winter. (The slip: unwritten. The jar: patient.

The fence line: watched with proprietary suspicion.) And spring... The fence line erupted. A ribbon of wildflowers... un-rowed, un- planned, gloriously random, the whole hundred-seed spill blooming where it fell: poppies in the grass, cornflowers against the posts, something yellow nobody could name, all of it wild and wrong- placed and better than any row ever planted.

The slip, finally, triumphantly: "Spilled all da seeds in fall. Seemed like da end of da flowers. The fence is a rainbow now. Da spill planted better dan I would have. Some things grow best un-planned.

Longest caught yet." (The fence line reseeded itself yearly ever after. The Spill Garden, it's called, in the family maps. Nobody ever planted it again. Nobody ever had to. The best accident in the yard's whole history: permanent.)
