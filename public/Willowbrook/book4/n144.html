Night 144

The Second Library Card

Month 5: Little Brother Tags Along

A civic milestone this week: Theo's library card. (Two-and-a-half. The age she got hers... well, she got hers at four months, fist-signed. His fist-signature is at least legible-adjacent.) The ceremony, at the lantern desk... done half by the librarian, half by the veteran patron at his elbow: "Dis is da desk.

Dat's da stamp... it goes thunk. You get five books. Five is da rule. Da rule makes you choose good." (The whole system, transferred. The librarian let her do most of it. Librarians know a docent when they see one.)

His first five, chosen: a tractor one. A cow one. A duck one. And two chosen by his method, newly revealed: by weight. (The heaviest books. Both of them. "Big books is more book." The librarian wrote that down.

The librarian writes everything down. There's a file. It's generational now.) The stamps: Thunk, thunk, thunk, thunk, thunk. And in the wainscoting, the list updated: second patron, second generation, chooses by weight, attended by docent.

The lantern's future: doubly secure. Five books rode home in the wagon. Two readers now. One town's light, passed down and down and down.
