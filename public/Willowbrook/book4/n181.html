Night 181

The Watch Adds a Building

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

At the month's quiet finish, gold light gathered in the Great Willow's branches. And an administrative matter, raised by Stella: "The rounds have grown. I'm adding a building. Whoo? The school. The little one, three rooms, by the maple.

I've been passing over it nightly since September and I'm making it official: it's on the watch now. It was always on the watch..." said the owl. "Every school is. Where else do you think the town keeps its entire future?

Three rooms. Thirty-some small futures. A teacher who takes notes on the weather the seedlings grow toward. The watch has covered schools since schools began. We just don't announce it. Teachers get proprietary. Mmm..."

Hummed the Moon, who lights the multi-purpose room's skylight nightly, who heard the winter sing entire, who countersigned, so to speak, the snow's theatrical timing. "Then log the addition formally..." said Stella. "One school.

Twelve in her room alone. The girl taught them a word this term, the family one. It's spreading. Rained-out Tuesdays all over town are turning to snowplows. Log it..." agreed the owl. "And log the older truth under it: towns build schools to teach the children.

Then the children teach the schools. It's the oldest trade route there is... and it's running heavy this year, in both directions, through one red backpack." Logged. Sealed. The school under the gold with everything else... the farm, the bakery, the lantern, the lanes, and now the three rooms where the town's whole future practices its hello songs. Goodnight, futures. Goodnight, Willowbrook. Term two at first light.
