Night 34

The Waking Test

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

A scientist lives in this house. And scientists test. This week's experiments: Can daytime-baba be caught? Trial one: the pretend- sleep. (Eyes shut. Fake snore, dramatic, unconvincing. One eye cracked open suddenly... Peek.) Result: a bear.

Sitting. Composed. Precisely where placed. Trial two: the quick turn. Result: bear. Sitting. Possibly. Possibly... one degree rotated? Trial three: the trap. (A cracker placed just out of bear-reach before nap. If moved after: Proof.)

Result: cracker intact. (Though, as it happened, slightly... dented? "That's a bite mark." It was probably a thumbprint. It was ruled a bite mark. Science bends for hope, at three.) And at bedtime, the direct interview: "Baba.

I know you walk. Why not in da daytime?" And that night, in the dream, on the meadow, the answer... which is the answer, the true one, the one the book gives too: "Daytime is for being held, little one.

If I walked all day, who'd be soft in your arms all night? A bear can't do both at once. I picked da better job." I picked the better job. The experiments ended. Not because the science failed... because the answer was better than the proof.

Some things are truest with your eyes closed. The bear is one. The bear was always one.
