Night 316

The Snow Angel Count

Month 11: Winter Wonders

Fresh snow, deep enough, and the proposal at breakfast: "Today da whole family goes down. Snow angels. Everybody. Even da grown-ups. Especially da grown-ups." (The negotiation: brief. Resistance: token. Papa's "I'm too old" was struck down by precedent... the puddle- stomping ruling of two springs past: nobody's too old; the almanac was cited; he went down first.)

The flock, as censused from the porch after: One Papa-sized angel. One Mama-sized. One Olivia-sized. (Deep-winged... "I flapped extra," with, beside it, a handprint: "signing my work. Angels need authors.") And one Theo- sized.

(Chaotic. Beautiful. Less angel than event, he rolled mid- make; the result has been described as "a angel doing a somersault..." Which, the family agreed, some angels surely do.) Four angels. Four sizes. One yard, printed with the family's whole shape...

"Look. Da snow's got us in it now. Da yard remembers us. Till it melts. And den da snowman rule says we go in da flowers anyway." The yard remembers. The flowers are forecast. (The flock lasted nine days, softening nightly, the family watching itself blur from the window... already planning next snow's count.

Annual now. Everything good here becomes annual. That's how this family keeps things: they make the calendar hold them.)
