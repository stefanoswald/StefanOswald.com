Night 250

The Sleepover at Lily's

Month 9: Brave Is Scared Plus One Step

The old glorious failure, the sleepover attempt, age two, giggles till ten-thirty, the walk of shame... got its rematch. The sleepover at Lily's. The new house. The window seat. Age four- and-a-half.

The kit, packed by the farm- overnight rules: the gold light the bear, the brave list. The night, honestly logged: The evening: Perfect. (Rosa's dinner. The window-seat stories. The whisper-plans for a trio clubhouse.)

Lights-out: the test. The giggles: present. The wobble: Also present, the strange-ceiling moment, the missing- Mama wave, quietly weathered with the bear and the breath and one whispered consult: "Wiwi. Your ceiling is different dan mine.

I know. Want me to tell you what da cracks look like? I named dem all. Dat one's da dragon..." (The ceiling tour. Friend-to-friend. The dark, furnished by its resident, handed over crack by named crack.

The wobble, walked through, together... which is the entire difference between two and four-and- a-half.) Morning: woke at Lily's. Both of them delighted by the fact of it. Breakfast: the bakery-smell kitchen.

Rosa's Saturday rolls, first batch, staff privileges. The pickup report: "We did the whole night. Da rematch is won." The rematch is won. (Sleepovers: unlocked. The far books hold dozens, each house, both directions, the trio's whole circuit.

The failed first try, age two, got retold at every one, fondly, forever: the giggles that lost the battle and started the war.)
