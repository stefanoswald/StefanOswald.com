Night 65

Why Do I Have to Sleep

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

The why-year reached bedtime. Inevitably: "Why do I hafta sleep? I'm not tired." (She was tired. Beside the point. The why was real underneath: why does the good day have to stop?) The full honest tour: "Because the night crew needs you still.

Remember them? The builders? While you sleep: your legs get longer... they only pour the growing at night. Your bumps get fixed. Your brain sorts the whole day... every why you asked, every answer, saved onto shelves so tomorrow-you can find them.

And the meadow opens. The bear's waiting. None of that runs while you're awake. Sleep isn't the day stopping. It's the day being saved." The day being saved. "... Like da almanac?" Exactly like the almanac.

Your body writes everything down at night. Skip sleep, lose the entry. She considered the stakes. Today had been a good entry... the swing-flying, the worm sidewalks, twelve answered whys. "... Okay. Save it." Okay.

Save it. The lamp clicked. The crew came on. The day, in full, was written down... and somewhere in the filing, a meadow opened, and a bear looked up, and said what he says every night: "Dere you are. Good day? Tell me everything. We got all night to file it."
