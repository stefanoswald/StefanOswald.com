Night 359

The Just-Us Morning

Month 12: Almost Four

Dawn, two days after the party. The house: asleep The kitchen: candle-lit, pancake-scented. The just-us morning. (Annual. Sacred. No party, no town, no sharing. Just: the original three, the early light, and the letters.)

Read aloud once, this morning, then sealed in the drawer. The drawer that waits. ("For someday. For eighteen. For whenever-you- need-dem. Da drawer decides when it's full.") This year's reading: Mama's (excerpted; the full text is the drawer's business): "...

You learned to read this year, so someday you'll read this yourself, and I want you to know: we watched you keep company with a waiting thing all winter, every day, mittens on, faithful... and we knew, watching, exactly what kind of person we were raising. The kind who reports to the cocooned. The kind who keeps words warm for people who can't say them yet.

Don't ever lose that job. It's the best one you'll ever have..." Papa's: "... Four things about four: It's the age I first remember being. So from here on, you're building the memories you'll keep. Build good ones.

Second: your wrong notes are already better than my right ones. Third: the willow writes tomorrow... your mama and I don't know the word either; we never know; that's the deal. Fourth: whatever it says... it's been watching you all year, same as us.

So it's going to be a good word. It can't not be..." The sealing. The drawer. (Eight envelopes now. The drawer: patient. The someday: coming, the far pages confirm, and worth every year of the waiting.)

Pancakes. Candle out. Day begun. "Same time next year?" Same time every year, little one. The drawer and the dawn and us. Standing appointment. Life's best kind.
