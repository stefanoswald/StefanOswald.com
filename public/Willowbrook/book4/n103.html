Night 103

The Lost Plan

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

A fear worth keeping... and planning for: "What if I get losted? At da market?" It happened to a bench kid last month. Found in four minutes, at the pickles. The answer: not reassurance. A plan.

Drilled calm, like the fire one. "If we ever get separated: One: Stop. Right where you are. Turn into a statue. (Lost people who wander get loster. Statues get found. I will come back along everywhere we walked.)

Two: Shout. My name... not 'Mama', every mama turns for Mama. Shout 'Elena!'... loud as the roar spot. Louder. Free: if I don't come fast, find a helper: a worker behind a counter, or a mama with kids.

Say 'I'm lost. My mama is Elena.' They'll bring the whole market to finding us." The drill, run at the actual market, lightly, like a game: "Statue drill! Shout drill... Quiet version. Helper drill: point me two helpers."

(The clerk, the saintly one, and a mama with a stroller. Both correct.) Plan: installed. And the fear? Shrank to fit its plan. That's what plans do, they're containers exactly fear-sized. "If I get losted, I got free jobs..."

She summarized. "Statue. Shout. Helper." Three jobs. But carried like the flashlight under the bed: stationed. Every kingdom needs a lamp. Every kid needs a plan.
