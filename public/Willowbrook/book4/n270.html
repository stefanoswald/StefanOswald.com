Night 270

The Scared-Of-Nothing Night

Month 9: Brave Is Scared Plus One Step

A strange report at the lamp this week: "Mama. I checked everywhere. I'm not scared of... Anything right now. Is dat allowed?" The tour that prompted it: The under-bed: kingdom. The closet: costume country.

The ceiling: named cracks. The basement: furnished. The vacuum: Crumby. The dark itself: home address. Thunder: front-row entertainment. The deep end: hers. The Duchess: colleague. "Every room said friendly. Da whole checklist. Nothing's scary and it feels...

Weird. Like I lost something?" (Like I lost something. The strangest true thing about courage: the fears, conquered, leave a silence where the work used to be.) "That's not losing, love. That's graduating. But here's a secret, so the quiet doesn't fool you: new scareds come.

They always come... bigger ones, life- sized ones, made to fit each new size of you. Tonight's quiet isn't the end of brave. It's the rest between sets." The rest between sets. "So I should... enjoy da quiet?"

Enjoy the quiet. Sleep in the house you furnished, room by room, fear by fear, two whole years of work. You built this quiet. Lie down in it. (The new scareds came, in their seasons, life- sized, by the forecast.

And met the list, the kit, the formula, the story. But that's the far pages. Tonight the house is friendly, every room, checked twice. Sleep, graduate. Sleep.)
