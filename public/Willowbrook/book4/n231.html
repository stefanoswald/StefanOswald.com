Night 231

The Letter Writers

Month 8: Helping Hands

The aunts write monthly. (Real letters. Stamps. The mail-is-love law, northern edition, faithful for years.) And this month, the realization: "Dey write us always. Do we write dem?" (... The mail tally book, checked: incoming, faithful.

Outgoing: thank-you drawings, sporadic. The imbalance, discovered, deplored.) So: The letter institution. Monthly. First Sunday. The kitchen table: Her letter: spoken, Mama's hand. (Contents: the fern's status, the cocoa stand's take, Buttons' maple, one joke.

The essential news.) Her enclosure: drawn. (A duck. In snow. "Dey like knowing da duck's okay in winter.") Theo's: a scribble, annotated by the scribe: "He says it's da cow. He says da cow says hi."

And the ceremony: the envelope, licked, the flag, raised by four hands. The return volley, two weeks later: a letter each. Addressed individually. First mail of their own names' arrows. (Kept. Both. The keepsake harbor's mail wing: founded.

The correspondence ran for decades... the far books hold letters in her hand, then typed, then her kids' scribbles annotated in turn. Mail is love. The flag goes up both ways now. Forever.)
