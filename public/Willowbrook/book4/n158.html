Night 158

The Snack Trade Exchange

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

Preschool has an economy. It runs at snack. The currency: everything. "I traded my crackers for Wiwi's apple slices. She likes crackers best. I like apples best. We both got best." (The good trade. Textbook.

Both- best. Adam Smith weeps with joy at the snack table.) But the economy, like all economies, has its lessons: "Marcus traded Ben a raisin for his whole cookie. Dat's not fair, right? Ben's little.

He didn't know. What makes a trade fair?... Both people happy after. Not just at da trading part." Both people happy after. "And Ben wasn't? He cried at rest time. For da cookie. So what could happen?"

The intervention, self-executed next day, per her report: "I told Marcus da trade was sneaky-unfair and he should do a do-over. And he did. He gave da cookie back and Ben gave da raisin back and den dey traded a fair thing: half da cookie for free raisins.

Both happy after. I checked after twice." Both happy after. Checked twice. The snack economy, regulated... by its youngest and fairest commissioner, who checks happiness at t-plus-one, which is more than most regulators ever managed.
