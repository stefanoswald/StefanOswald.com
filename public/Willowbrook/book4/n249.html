Night 249

The Shot Day

Month 9: Brave Is Scared Plus One Step

The checkup came with news: Booster day. (A shot. The real kind. She's old enough now to know in advance, and the family policy held: no ambushes. Truth, with tools.) The prep, honest: "Will it hurt?

Yes. Like a pinch. For about three seconds. Then it's done and your body learns to fight a whole sickness. Three seconds of pinch buys years of armor. That's the trade." The tools, chosen by the patient: The breath: ready.

The squeeze hand: Mama's, offered. The counting: hers... "I'll count da seconds. Counting makes things visible. Visible things are smaller." And the choice... new this year: "Watch or look away?... Watch. It's my arm. I want to know what happens to my arm."

(She watched. The needle. The three seconds, counted aloud, steady: "one... two... Free." Done.) "That was the pinch? I've had worse from da Duchess." (The Duchess pinch comparison entered family medical vocabulary permanently. All future shots: rated in Duchesses.

This one: 0.5 Duchesses. Barely a peck.) The brave list, that night, gained its most literal entry: "Da booster. Watched it. Counted it. 0.5 Duchesses. Armor installed." Armor installed. Both kinds. The list knows which one matters more.
