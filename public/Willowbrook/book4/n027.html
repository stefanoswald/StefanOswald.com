Night 27

Bobby Bear in the Dream

Month 1: Pretend!

At breakfast, a report. Delivered slowly, with the wonder still on it: "Mama. Baba was in my dream last night. Not like holding him. He was walking. He talked to me." (... The letter. The birthday letter.

"In your dreams lately... have you noticed?... I've been walking... watch for me." The paperwork, apparently, cleared.) The dream, as reported: a meadow. Moonlit. The willow on its hill, glowing gold "like always but more."

And Baba, walking beside her on his own two bear feet, flopped ear and all, patchwork heart catching the moon. "What did he say? He said... he's been practicing. And dat he can only do it in dreams for now.

And he showed me da meadow. He knows where everything is. He said da fireflies know him. And Mama...he said when I dream, he gets to be dere. So I should dream lots. So he gets more walks."

So he gets more walks. The bear was consulted at bedtime that night. Held up. Examined. His glass eyes, his stillness, his eternal daytime composure. "I know it's you..." She whispered. "See you in dere." Month two is coming. The bear is almost through the door.
