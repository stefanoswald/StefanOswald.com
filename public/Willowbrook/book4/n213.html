Night 213

The Real Job List

Month 8: Helping Hands

Olivia wanted a real job in the house, not a pretend one invented for practice.

The family made a list at the kitchen table. Olivia would set the table, water the fern, and match clean socks. Theo would carry napkins and put spoons beside the plates.

"How much do we earn?" Olivia asked.

"These are family jobs," Papa said. "We all do them because we live here. Extra work can sometimes earn money, but caring for our home belongs to everyone."

That evening Olivia placed four plates on the table, then remembered Grandpa Joe was visiting and added a fifth. Theo put two spoons at one place and none at another.

They fixed it together.

After dinner Olivia watered the fern until one drop appeared in the saucer.

"Done," she announced.

Nobody applauded. Nobody needed to. The table had been ready, the fern had water, and the house had leaned a little on her.

That was what made the jobs real.
