Night 78

Why Is the Willow Gold

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

The Sunday walk. The hill. The tree at dusk, doing its thing: glowing. Faintly. Gold. The way it has all three books. The way the town has never once remarked on. And the why-year, inevitable: "Why does da willow glow?"

Silence. (The parents looked at the tree. The tree glowed. The town's records, wainscoting, bark, starlight... held their breath.) And Mama gave the answer this book has been waiting three volumes to write down: "You know what?

Nobody knows. Nobody's ever known. Grandpa's grandpa asked the same question on this same hill. Some things in Willowbrook just... are. The willow glows. Bella's never late. The brook knows things first. And here's what the town figured out, a long time ago: some whys aren't doors to open.

Some whys are the treasure itself. If somebody explained that glow tomorrow... would the hill be better? Or smaller?" She looked at the gold a long time. "... Smaller..." She said finally. "Some whys we keep."

Some whys we keep. The wonder-file: officially founded. Contents: the glow. The balloon over the willows. Franklin. The ear that settled. The moon that follows because it likes us. Kept. Unopened. Treasured exactly as is.

(The tree, for its part, glowed a shade warmer as they left. The records decline, per eternal policy, to explain. That's the whole covenant. It holds.)
