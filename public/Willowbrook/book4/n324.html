Night 324

The Thaw Report

Month 11: Winter Wonders

Mid-month, and the light... by the solstice promise, has been coming back. And this week it brought muscle: The first real thaw. The inspection tour: The eaves: orchestra at half strength. ("Da piano's losing keys.

Going out low note first. Tunk... gone.") The lane: puddled. (The frozen museum window: Closed. Leaves: released. Bubbles: "da brefs finished, Papa. Months late. But dey finished." The puddle, back in the stomping business by afternoon.)

The snowbanks: shrinking, going grey and honest. ("Snow gets old. It comes down young and fluffy and leaves old and tired. Same as anybody after a big job.") And the brook... The brook: still lidded, still white... but underneath, audible, kneeling, mitten to ear: Running.

"It's awake under dere. Da brook's been running under the lid da whole time! Blblblb... I can hear it, winter never stopped it, it just... Covered it!" (The notebook, urgent, mitten in teeth: "The brook ran all winter.

Under the ice. Quiet but going. Like Bella. Like everything. Winter doesn't stop the good things. It just makes dem quiet for a while.") Winter doesn't stop the good things. It makes them quiet for a while. (this month's theorem, complete.

Saved above the brook, kneeling, one ear to the lid of the world. Spring is not a return. It's an uncovering. Everything good kept going. You just get to hear it again.)
