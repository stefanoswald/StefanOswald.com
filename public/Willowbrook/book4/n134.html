Night 134

The Brook Together

Month 5: Little Brother Tags Along

Summer. The brook. And a ceremony two years in the waiting: Theo's first wade. (Hers was at almost- three, hand in Papa's. His is at two-and- some, hand in hers... Papa one step behind, the insurance layer, by the scripture: hands on, always, any depth.)

The walk-through, done by the approved: "Okay Fee-oh. Boots off. Da water's cold first, dat's just its hello. Den it's nice. Pebbles are wobbly. Grip with your toes. Hold my hand da whole time.

Ready?" Step. The gasp. The second step. The grin arriving. "Da water's moving, Wivy! It always does. It's going somewhere. It doesn't mind us standing in it. We're just a thing it says hi to on da way."

(The brook: burbling its hellos. The pebbles: wobbling per tradition. Two kids, ankle-deep in the town's oldest teacher, one introducing, one being introduced, the water running its eternal lesson around four small feet.) And from the rock, the announcement: "I taught both of dem..."

Yelled Freddy, to nobody, wrongly, loyally. "The whole family. Approved." Approved. Close enough. The brook school's enrollment: complete. Next generation: waded. The stepping stones wait, by the rules, for bigger feet... hers next summer, his in three. The water keeps the schedule. The water keeps everything.
