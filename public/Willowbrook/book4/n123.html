Night 123

Teaching the Jacket Flip

Month 5: Little Brother Tags Along

Big lesson this week. The crown jewel of her whole self-doing kit: The jacket flip. "Fee-oh. Watch. Floor. Upside down. Arms in da sleeves... Now you." Attempt one: jacket on floor. Arms in.

Flip... jacket lands on his head. Entirely. A jacket ghost. "Dat's how my first one went too. Again." (It's exactly how her first one went. The teaching manual is autobiographical. The best ones are.) Attempt four: the flip clears.

Half on. Attempt seven: Whoosh. On. Right- side up. Arms in. "I did it! Wivy! I did the magic! You did the magic!" The victory lap: jacket flipped eleven more times. (The jacket has never been on and off so much in its life.

Jackets expect this now. It's a flipping family.) And at pickup-time, for Mama, the demonstration... and the teacher, standing back, arms folded, glowing like a lantern: "I taught da flip. Rosa taught Mama, Mama taught me, I taught him.

Da flip goes downhill forever." The flip goes downhill forever. Everything does, in this family. That's the whole plumbing of it.
