Night 19

The Longest Game

Month 1: Pretend!

A milestone this week, easy to miss: the game that didn't end. Monday: the couch became a ship. (Sailing to "da faraway island. It takes six days." Six days. A schedule. Note it.) Tuesday: the ship... still a ship.

The game, resumed at the exact plot point: "We sailed all night. Now dere's a storm." Wednesday: the storm's aftermath. Repairs. Thursday: "Land! But it's da wrong island." Friday: the right island. Treasure. Saturday: the sail home.

Sunday: Home. The voyage: complete. Six days, as scheduled. One game. One week. Held entirely in one small head, the plot, the crew roster the list, the unfinished business... carried across six sleeps and resumed each morning without one dropped thread.

The books call it narrative persistence. The family called it "da boat week." What it means: her worlds have time in them now. Yesterday and tomorrow. Plots that wait overnight, like the puzzle waited, like the seeds waited.

The pretend has grown a calendar. Next: it grows chapters. (It did. The far months are full of sagas. The boat week was just the maiden voyage.)
