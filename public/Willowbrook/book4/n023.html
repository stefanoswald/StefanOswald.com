Night 23

The Orchestra of One

Month 1: Pretend!

A composition this week. An original. Title: "The song of everything." The instruments: Two pots. The bike bell, detached. A shaker. (A jar of dried beans. Sealed. Triple-checked. The check never sleeps.) The whistle.

And Theo on "da drums." (An overturned bucket. He has one move. It's a good move.) The premiere, from the porch steps, audience assembling per lane custom (two parents, one gold dog through the fence, the Hendersons on their porch, waving): Movement one: pots.

Loud. Movement two: the bell, with whistle. (The gentle part. "Dis part is da morning..." By the program notes, shouted mid-piece.) Movement three: Everything at once. (. "Dis part is da whole day!") Finale: one bucket hit, solo, by the guest artist.

Boom. The applause: the whole lane's. The encore: performed. (Same song. It's the only song. It's a good song.) A symphony of one day, in three movements and a boom: morning, everything, bedtime. Honestly? Better structured than most operas.

The lane agreed. The composer bowed. The drummer bowed at the wrong time. The piece entered the permanent repertoire, performed seasonally, evolving always... the town's own unofficial anthem, first performed this ordinary golden evening, admission free forever.
