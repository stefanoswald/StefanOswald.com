Night 219

The Tool Belt

Month 8: Helping Hands

Sunday. The workshop. And on the bench, un-birthday, unexplained: a tool belt. Her size. Real leather. Real tools: a small hammer. A screwdriver, stubby. A measuring tape, retractable. A pencil, flat, carpenter-style. And the sanding block.

Her block, the original... holstered in its own loop. "Every apprentice gets a belt when dey're ready..." said the maker. "You been ready. Da hip says I need hands. You got two." The apprenticeship. Official.

Her first jobs, that same Sunday: Sanding: the new project's edges. Measuring: boards, for cutting. ("Measure twice. Cut once. Da oldest law in dis room. Older dan me.") And holding: the other end. Of everything.

("Half dis trade is holding da other end steady while somebody works. Learn it now. It's also most of being married, but dat's a later lesson." rock, rock) The belt went home. (Worn to dinner.

Worn to school show-and-tell... "Real tools"... Marcus fainted with envy, nearly.) And the Sundays changed shape: egg duty, still. But now, after: shop hours. Two aprons of sawdust. The other end, held. Measure twice. The apprenticeship of everything, begun at the source.
