Night 115

The Fear Museum

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

Month's end approaching. And per family tradition... a new exhibition: The fear museum. The retired fears, displayed on the low shelf with full honors: The vacuum: represented by a photo of Theo patting it. (Plaque, spoken by the curator: "Crumby.

Retired monster. Now a pet. He eats crumbs only.") The shadow: one curtain clip. ("Da wall spooky. It was a curtain plus cars. I do da show myself now.") The under-bed: the stationed flashlight, on loan.

The big flush: a drawn picture of a toilet with a speech bubble: "Roar." The giant duck: the high-five photo. The farm dark: one star sticker. ("It's not empty. It's fuller. You gotta leave da lamps to see.")

And one empty pedestal, labeled, by the curator's instruction: "For the next one. Because dere's always a next one and dat's okay. Dis museum has room." This museum has room. (The pedestal filled and re-emptied for years, new fears arriving, touring, retiring to plaques.

The museum's whole teaching, standing in that one empty spot: fears come. Fears go. The shelf keeps the proof.) Admission: free. Docent tours: hourly, unsolicited, thorough. The gift shop sells courage. It's always been free.
