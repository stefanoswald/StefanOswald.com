Night 358

Theo's Gift

Month 12: Almost Four

He couldn't wait for the party. (He tried. Nine days of trying. At the party itself: too loud, too many, not right, a courier knows his moment and that wasn't it.) The moment came the morning after.

Quiet house. Streamers half-down. Cake for breakfast And across the kitchen, with both hands, with ceremony, with nine days of heavy finally lifting: "Wivy. Is for you. I made it. Is not a picture."

(It was a picture. It was also, per his denials all week, correctly not just a picture, as the opening revealed:) The unwrapping: slow. (The paper survived nowhere.) And inside: the two figures. Big one, small one.

The hands, one shared scribble, because hands are hard and love is approximate. Wivy + feo, crayoned over Mama's dotted letters. And the enormous sideways T, signed. (The silence that followed was the good kind. Then:) "...

Fee-oh. Dis is us. Is us! Da hands are togedder. Hands togedder!... It's da best thing I got. Of everything. Da whole birthday." (She meant it. The scribble- hands win.) The framing: immediate. (Mama.

The good frame. The one from the drawer of frames- waiting-for- things-worth- framing.) The hanging: her wall. Lamp-side. Where the last thing seen at night lives. "Now you're dere every night..." She told the artist.

"Even when you're in your own room. Da drawing does da staying." The drawing does the staying. (It did. It does. Sixty years of walls, later, and counting. Hands togedder. Structurally tape. Permanently love.)
