Night 319

The Big Sled Hill

Month 11: Winter Wonders

Olivia had spent two winters watching the big sled hill from the little slope below.

This year she looked at the little slope and said, "That is nearly a lawn."

Papa agreed to begin halfway up the big hill. The first run was fast enough to make Olivia shout. The second began three quarters of the way up.

For the third, they climbed to the top.

Mateo gave his advice: lean back on the bumps, steer with your feet, and yell the whole way down. The yelling was not required, he said, but it was tradition.

Olivia pushed off.

For eleven seconds the hill was wind, snow, bumps, and one long whoop. She reached the bottom dusted white and laughing.

"Again."

She made eleven more runs, gladly paying the four-minute climb for each eleven-second ride.

At the top of the last run, she looked down at the little slope.

"That hill taught me this hill," she said.

Then she pushed away, carrying everything the smaller hill had taught her into the larger one.
