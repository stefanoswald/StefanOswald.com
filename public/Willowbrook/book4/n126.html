Night 126

Theo's Whys Begin

Month 5: Little Brother Tags Along

It happened at snack. Theo, age two, looked at his cracker and said his first: "Why?" (Why what? Unclear. The why is ambient at first. The engine doesn't care. It just starts.) And then: everything.

"Why juice? Why cup? Why wivy?" (Why Livy. The best one. Unanswerable. Kept.) And the household's resident why-veteran... one year past her own ignition, journal still filling... took the questions like a professional: "Juice is squeezed fruit.

Dey squish da apples. Cups is so da juice doesn't be a puddle. And why me? Because... I'm your Livy. Everybody gets one big kid. You got me." (He accepted all three. Saved them wherever twos file things.

Asked four more.) And at dinner, her report, half warning, half pride: "Fee-oh's whys started. I answered nine... We're gonna need another journal." Another journal was procured. The engine, ignited, one generation down... fed by the person whose own engine never stopped.

"Why?" asked Theo, at the lamp, at nothing, at everything. "Because..." said his sister, with total authority. "da world is interesting. Dat's da whole why under all da whys. Now go to sleep. More whys tomorrow." More whys tomorrow. The truest bedtime promise in the house. Always kept.
