Night 288

June's Alphabet Trade

Month 10: Words Are Magic

The trio's park summit this week produced a treaty: The knowledge trade. (June: behind on letters, her house is bug-rich but book-light. Ahead on: everything with six legs. Lily: mid-pack letters, expert in bakery operations and older-kid intelligence.

Olivia: the letter-hoard, freshly rich, itching to teach.) The exchange rate, negotiated: One letter lesson = one bug fact = one bakery secret. The market, open air, park table: "Da B. Bumpy back, den belly. B is for bug, so it's your letter, June.

Ants leave a smell trail. Dat's how dey walk in lines. You can rub your finger across it and dey get confused and do a dance. Da bakery frows away da broken cookies at closing.

Frows them away. Unless you're standing dere at 4:55 looking hungry." And the certificates... because this administration documents everything: "June: Sertifyd in letters A B and J." Signed, o-l-i-v-i-a, harder because it's a document.

"Olivia: Sertifyd in ants." Signed with June's careful J. (Miss Dana, finding the certificates in cubbies, added them to the file with a note: "They've founded a school inside my school. Tuition is knowledge. Recommend non-interference." Non-interference held. The school-inside- the-school ran all year. Everyone graduated everything.)
