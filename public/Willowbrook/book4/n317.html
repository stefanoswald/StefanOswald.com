Night 317

What the Cradle Hears

Month 11: Winter Wonders

A theory this week, from the cradle's watcher: "Mama. When I was in your tummy, you talked to me. You said da words went in... Da chrysalis is Bella's tummy dat she made herself. So... words should go in dere too."

(The logic: unassailable. The precedent: family record, book one, the talked-to belly. The application: approved.) The cradle reports began that afternoon: Daily. At the milkweed. Mittened, crouched, soft- voiced, the watch report, delivered through silk: "Bella.

It's me. Report: it snowed aden. Da fort is holding. Da birds made it froo da night... all of dem, I counted. Fee-oh says fly now, dat's your word, he keeps it warm for you.

Da garden's sleeping but it's dere. Everything you need is getting ready, same as you. Take your time. Melt good. Keep da Bella- est parts. Spring's coming. I checked da calendar myself. I can read now, dat's new since you went in.

I'll read to you when you come out. End of report." (End of report. Every day. All winter. Snow or clear. The bear attended twice weekly... "visitors are good for patients.") Does the sound go through the silk?

The book doesn't say. But it notes: of all the chrysalises in all the gardens of that town, one spent the winter being told, daily, that the world was getting ready for it. That it was waited for.

That its word was being kept warm. Unprovable. The watcher knows what she knows. End of report.
