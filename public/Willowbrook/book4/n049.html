Night 49

The Moth Incident

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

The meadow, this week, produced a moth. A dream-moth. Big. Soft-winged. Glowing faintly, per meadow custom. Harmless, obviously. And the bear... the guardian, the patroller, the vault of three years' courage... Froze. "... Baba?

Da moff..." He said, very quietly. "Da incident. I told you. Da dresser years. It landed on my ear. In da dark. For an hour. I couldn't move. I'm a bear, we don't move when watched, it's da code..."

(An hour. A moth. On the destiny ear. Unable to move, by the code. And his girl... who has been taught one formula her whole life, at ear- lights and pool steps and every scared threshold since... knelt down to bear height and gave it back: "Baba.

Listen. Brave isn't not-scared. Brave is scared... Plus anything. Plus anything..." She maintains. Both work. Hers might work better.). "Da moff is soft. Like you're soft. Watch. I'll go first. Hold my hand." She went first.

He held her hand The moth, approached, fluttered up... and landed, briefly, on her raised finger. Soft. Dusty. Glowing. Gone. "... Dat wasn't so bad..." said the bear, who had squeezed her hand throughout. "You were scared plus anything..."

She said. "Dat's da whole thing, Baba. You taught me by being dere. Now I taught you by going first." The formula, come full circle: taught to her at thresholds, taught back at a moth. That's how courage circulates in a family. Even the fur members. Especially them.
