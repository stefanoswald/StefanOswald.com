Night 64

Why Can't I Fly

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

The cape settled this question once. The why-year reopened it: "But why can't I fly? Birds fly. Bella flies. Why not me?" The honest physics, kid-sized: "Flying needs wings big enough for your weight, and bones light as straws, and chest muscles bigger than your whole body.

Birds paid for flying by being mostly feathers and air. You got other equipment: legs that run, hands that build, a brain that makes castles. Nobody gets everything. Birds can't build castles... Birds can't build castles..."

She repeated, absorbing the trade. "And, humans found the cheat codes. Watch." The swing. Pumped, she pumps herself now, the big-kid skill, legs out, legs in, higher, higher, to the story's very top, where the chains go light and the tummy floats and for half a second at the peak...

Half a second... "I'm flying..." Half a second at a time. The human way. Swings, and jumps, and someday planes and dreams... where, as it happened, she flies regularly, over the meadow, with a bear who says the same thing every time: some countries give you the wings your side couldn't. Both sides, between them: everything.
