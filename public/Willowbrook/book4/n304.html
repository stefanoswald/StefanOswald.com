Night 304

Molly Mouse and the Written Acorn

Month 10: Words Are Magic

When the final night arrived, the first stars appeared above the Great Willow. The words month, closing. And tonight, a rare presenter: Molly mouse. (The satchel mouse. The acorn-cap courier. Smallest member of the watch, and, it turns out, its postmaster.)

"The small one below mailed her first letter this month," Molly said. Mice write?" asked Stella. "Mice have always written. Field mice in the next valley. Barn mice at Honeywell. A very literary vole. We write small, one acorn cap holds forty letters... but we write everything.

News. Recipes. Where the good seeds are. Which cats are bluffing. And love letters. Mice write mostly love letters, if you weigh it out. Why?" asked the owl, who suspected the answer and wanted it on record.

"Because we're small and the world is big and winter is long..." said Molly. "Writing is how the small stay large across distance. A mouse with a letter in her satchel is never only where her feet are.

She's also wherever her words already arrived. The child below learned that this month," Molly finished. "Her first letter went two streets. Mine have gone four valleys. Same magic. Log it: the reader writes now.

The words go where she can't. She will never again be only where her feet are." Goodnight, letter-writers of Willowbrook... large and small and four valleys wide. The mail goes out at moonrise. Write everything."
