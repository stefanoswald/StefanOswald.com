Night 152

The Backpack

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

The fall came. And with it, at the shop, the great equipment purchase: The backpack. Requirements, per its future wearer: "Red. With room for Baba. And my brave list. And a snack. Dat's da whole list."

(That's the whole list. Note what's on it: courage, comfort, crackers. The complete first- day kit, self- specified. The books couldn't pack it better.) Found: red, bear- sized, snack-pocketed. Worn out of the store.

Worn to dinner. Worn, negotiated off only at bath... for three consecutive days. "Practice wearing..." She explained. "So it feels like mine before it goes to school." Practice wearing. (The preschool game of last spring, still running its rehearsals.

The backpack joins the cast. By the first day it won't be equipment. It'll be armor, broken in, smelling like home.) Six sleeps to school. The backpack sleeps by the door, packed, practiced, red as everything brave in this family.
