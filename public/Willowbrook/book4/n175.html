Night 175

The Report Card That Wasn't

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

November: the parent conference. (The little chairs. The grown-ups folded into them, knees high, by the eternal comedy of the venue.) Miss Dana's report... no scores at four; just the real stuff, delivered in stories: "Academically: fine, ahead, whatever... she's four; the letters will come; they always come.

Here's the actual report: She noticed Wren. Took two weeks and did it his speed. They're inseparable now, in the quietest way I've ever seen. She regulated a bounce-house overwhelm by herself. Asked for a porch.

I've had teachers who can't do that. She taught my room a word... you know the one, and I watched twelve kids try it on a rained-out Tuesday. She sat with the boy who cried at the hamster funeral.

Didn't fix. Just sat. Where does a four-year-old learn to just sit with someone?" (The parents, knees high in the little chairs, looked at each other. Four books, Miss Dana. A bench, a bear, a bakery, a farm.

It's all written down.) "Anyway." She closed the folder she hadn't opened. "Your kid's the weather in my classroom. The good kind. The kind the other seedlings grow toward. Conference over. Take the macaroni art.

There's a lot of it." The macaroni art was taken. The report... storied, scoreless, exact... was framed in the family's memory next to frame thirty-one: the weather the seedlings grow toward. Their kid. The good kind.
