Night 55

The Naptime Visit

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

A discovery this week: the meadow has an express entrance. Naps. (Naps are short. Dreams there are quick, when they come at all. She'd assumed the meadow was nights-only... too far for the little sleeps.)

But this Tuesday, mid-nap, briefly: the meadow. Flickering in, the way nap-dreams do. And there, at the tea rock, as if he'd been waiting exactly there... the bear. "You came da short way!

Welcome! How long do I got? Five minutes, maybe. Nap-visits are little. Dat's okay. Little visits count. Come sit." And they sat. Five dream-minutes at the tea rock. No adventure. No patrol. Just sitting, the way the bench taught, close, quiet, together... while the lantern fish drifted and the grass glowed its low afternoon glow (the meadow has an afternoon; it matches hers; the sides keep the same clock).

Then the flicker, the pull, awake... the whole visit shorter than a cartoon. "I saw Baba! Just little. We just sat." Just sat. Don't save love up for da big long times only. Five minutes at da rock is five minutes at da rock.

Da door doesn't measure. It just opens." The door doesn't measure. It just opens. Nap-visits joined the rotation. Five minutes at a time. All of them, counted. All of them, enough."
