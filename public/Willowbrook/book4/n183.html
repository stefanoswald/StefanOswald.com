Night 183

The Broken Crayon Maybe

Month 7: The Maybe Lessons

Maybe number one came fast: the red crayon. The red. The duck- drawing one. Snapped. In half. (By whom? The story pleads the fifth. The suspect is two.) The wail: instant. "It's ruined... Maybe." The watching: brief.

Because look: where there was one red crayon... there are now two. One for each hand. Or... "...one for me and one for fee-oh." (The suspect, handed half what they saw, beamed. Two artists, one color, zero fights, the red- crayon wars, ended, by the very snap that started the wail.)

The slip, spoken for the jar: "Da red crayon broke. Seemed bad. Now dere's two reds and nobody fights. Maybe caught." First slip in. The jar: begun. This month: rolling. (Crayons, as it happened, work better broken.

Every preschool teacher snaps them on purpose. Don't tell the wailers. Let the maybes teach it.)
