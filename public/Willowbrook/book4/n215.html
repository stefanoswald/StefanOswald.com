Night 215

The Bakery Apprentice Hours

Month 8: Helping Hands

A Saturday institution, formalized this month: Bakery hours. (She's helped Rosa forever, the dough scraps, the braid lessons. But this month, Rosa made it official:) an apron. Her size. Strawberry- printed, by the house uniform.

And stitched on the pocket, in Rosa's own thread: Olivia. ("Staff get names on aprons. You're staff now. Saturday mornings, one hour, if your mama says.") The duties, real: The napkin dispensers: filled. The day-old shelf: arranged.

The crust-end basket: Stocked, and, on Rosa's nod, distributed: "You know who gets them. You've watched your whole life. Now it's your counter." (The crust ends. Hers to give now. The tired man got one.

The kid pressed to the window got one. The whole inherited generosity, handed its first deputy.) The wages: one celebration bun, one hour of Rosa's company, and the thing money can't touch: "I work at da bakery.

Saturday is my shift." Her shift. (It ran for years. The apron got re-stitched at every size. The far books hold a sixteen-year-old in the same strawberry print, Saturday mornings, by then running the counter... but that's ahead.

This month: hour one. Napkins. Dignity for day-olds. The whole career, in miniature, begun.)
