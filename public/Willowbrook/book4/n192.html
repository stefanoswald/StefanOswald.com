Night 192

Teaching Miss Dana's Class the Jar

Month 7: The Maybe Lessons

The classroom caught wind of the jar. (Via its usual correspondent. The rained-out-trip maybe had primed them. The law had beachhead.) And Miss Dana... who has stopped interfering and started note-taking, per her own file... asked the question that franchises philosophies: "Should we have one?"

The class maybe jar. Installed by the easel. Decorated by committee. (Ducks feature. The consultant had influence.) The first classroom catches, by the pickup-line reports: Marcus's tower, knocked by accident → rebuilt as a bridge "which is harder and cooler."

The paint spill → "marbled paper. We made twelve. It's a technique now." Wren's lost turn at the easel → a double-turn Thursday..." And he painted da best one of da whole year with da extra time.

"And the big one, the one Miss Dana retold at the fence with the dish-towel look: the broken class fish tank → the empty tank became... a terrarium. Moss, stones..." A whole tiny land. "The class voted to keep it.

The fish, upon return, got a new tank. The room got two worlds. "Da maybes work at school too..." She reported. "Dey work everywhere. Dat's da whole point of a philosophy, Mama says." It is. Franchise: open. Jars: two towns wide. Spreading.
