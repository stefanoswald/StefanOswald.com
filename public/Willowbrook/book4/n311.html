Night 311

The Ice Window

Month 11: Winter Wonders

The cold came back hard after the thaw, and made, overnight, on the lane... Art: The frozen puddle. (The big one. The gutter-fed one. Frozen clear, black ice, glass-grade, and beneath it, locked mid-float: three oak leaves, a twig, and a constellation of bubbles, paused.)

The discovery: boot-level. The inspection: knees, then belly, mittens flat, nose an inch off the ice: "Mama. It's a window. Winter made a window. Da leaves are in dere, you can see dem but you can't touch dem... it's like... a museum.

Da puddle got framed." The puddle got framed. And the bubbles: "Dose are brefs. Da puddle's brefs, frozen mid-bubble. Winter stopped da clock with da air still in it." (Frozen breaths. This holds up.)

The questions, belly-down: "Will da leaves be dere till spring? Do da bubbles pop when it melts... like, do da brefs finish? Does da puddle remember being a window?" (Yes. Yes... months late, in a warm week, the breaths finish.

And the last one: unanswerable, therefore saved by the asker herself: "I'll remember for it. Dat's what watchers do.") I'll remember for it. The walk resumed, eventually. The knees of the snowpants: soaked. Worth it.

Winter makes windows. The trick... her words, notebook, that night... "is being short enough and slow enough to look froo dem." Short enough and slow enough. The season's whole lesson. She's fluent.
