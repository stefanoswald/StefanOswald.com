Night 159

Buttons Gets Out

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

The event of the season, reported breathless at pickup: "Buttons got out." The hamster. Loose. Mid-morning. Twelve four-year-olds. One open cage door. (The light helper. An overreach of office. Forgiven, eventually. The chart giveth power; power corrupteth, briefly, at four.)

The chaos: instant. Shrieking. Chasing... "...and chasing is wrong for hamsters, dey're little, chasing scares dem under things..." (she knew; of course she knew; the gentle lesson covers all species)... And into the chaos, Miss Dana's voice: "Statues.

Everyone. Right now." And twelve kids froze... and one kid, statue-still, whispered the rule sideways to her neighbor statues: "Quiet body. No reaching. He'll come out when it's calm. Small scared things always do."

(The strange-dog rule. The worm summit. The whole gentle kit, deployed at hamster scale, in a whisper, to a frozen classroom.) Buttons emerged... four minutes of statue-silence later, from under the easel. And the calmest hands in the room helped herd him, gently, cup-and- cardboard, home.

The cage door got a clip. The light helper got a hug. (He cried. The office had overwhelmed him. Power is heavy at four. The commissioner of fairness sat with him at lunch.) And Buttons got... per unanimous class vote... a second hamster song, composed at circle time, about staying home safe. It's mostly his name. The best songs are.
