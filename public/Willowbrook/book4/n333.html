Night 333

Winter Goes Deep

Month 11: Winter Wonders

Near the end of winter, Olivia and Mama walked through the garden looking for what had changed.

The pond still wore ice at its edges, but water whispered beneath it. Crocuses pushed through the soil. In the shed, seed packets waited beside the shovel.

"Winter looked asleep," Olivia said. "But it was busy underneath."

She thought of the season's long days: building Fort Together, hanging mittens for anyone who needed them, making soup, listening to icicles chime, finding library books beneath blankets, and watching Bella emerge from her chrysalis.

There had been snow angels and sled tracks, a lopsided snowman, and the shortest day's small candle.

Mama pressed a hand to the cold earth. "Some growing is easier to see than other growing."

Olivia knelt beside the crocuses. "Maybe winter is the year going deep before it comes back up."

That night the temperature fell again. In the morning there was fresh snow, and three purple flowers standing through it.
