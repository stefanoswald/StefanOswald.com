Night 258

The Getting-Lost That Happened

Month 9: Brave Is Scared Plus One Step

It finally happened. The drilled thing. The real one. The market. Saturday- crowded. One turned back, the cheese counter, thirty seconds, and: Gone. Both directions. Separated. Four minutes. (The longest four in the parental record.

The almanac declines to describe Mama's four. It describes hers... because hers ran like a drill, because it was a drill, bought cheap on sunny days, paying now at full price:) statue. (She stopped. Mid-aisle.

Turned to stone by the pickles. "Lost people wander. Statues get found.") Shout. ("Elena!"... twice, market- loud, the roar- stone training paying compound interest.) And, as the shout carried... Helper: the clerk. The saintly one.

Already moving: "Lost your mama? I know exactly who you are. Stand right here with me. She's two aisles over... I can see her hat." The reunion, at the pickles, four minutes total: the running kind.

Both directions. The clerk, saintly to the last: "Textbook. Both of you. I've watched worse from grown-ups." And the debrief, at home, at cocoa: "I was scared da whole time. And you did the plan the whole time.

Scared and. Da plan worked scared. Dat's why we drilled it boring... so it works scared." That's why you drill it boring. So it works scared. (The drill, validated. The plan, retired undefeated... never needed again. Once was enough. Once is what the drills are for.)
