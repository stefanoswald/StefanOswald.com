Night 130

The Defender, Downhill

Month 5: Little Brother Tags Along

The park. The sandbox. And a bigger kid... a stranger-kid, four-ish, having a hard day of his own probably... shoved Theo. Over a shovel. Down he went, wailing. And before any grown-up crossed half the distance... she was there.

Planted. Feet set. Between. (The blue-jacket move. Lily's move, from the ancient sandbox case. Received then. Deployed now. Downhill, like everything.) "That's my brother. We don't shove. He's two." The bigger kid blinked. Mumbled.

Retreated to his own grown-up. And she turned and ran the kit: the scoop-up, the check, the "you're okay, I got you"... and then, the part that made the bench mothers put down their coffees: she walked over to the bigger kid, five minutes later, and said: "You having a bad day?" (... He was.

It came out in a mumble. New baby at his house. Nobody playing with him. The shove had a story. They always do.) "Wanna dig? You can do da roads. My brother does da knocking part.

He's good at it." Three diggers by snack time. The defense and the password, one incident, one girl. Protect the little. Include the lonely. Both jobs. Same sandbox. She runs them both now... the whole inheritance, operational.
