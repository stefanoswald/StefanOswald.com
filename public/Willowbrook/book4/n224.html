Night 224

The Book Box Keeper

Month 8: Helping Hands

The little free library... the flat-tire discovery, the box on the post... got a keeper this month. Self-appointed. Weekly rounds: The box: checked. (Tidied. Rain-swollen ones triaged home for drying. "Books hate wet.

Everybody knows.") The stock: managed. Outgrown books from her own shelf, chosen at the handing-down ceremony's full rites... donated in: "Dis one taught me colors. Somebody little needs it now." And the finds: curated out.

(One per visit, by the take-one law. Chosen, per tradition, sometimes by smell.) The librarian at the lantern library, told of the deputyship, made it official: a tiny "Volunteer" sticker, worn on the coat for a month.

"Two libraries now..." She reported. "Da big one and da little one. I work at both." The town's book economy: staffed at every scale. The lantern's light, by the old story: kept on, now with a branch office, one post tall.
