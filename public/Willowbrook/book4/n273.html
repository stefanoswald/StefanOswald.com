Night 273

Oliver Owl on the One Step

Month 9: Brave Is Scared Plus One Step

On the final evening, gold light gathered in the Great Willow's branches. The brave month, closing. And Stella asked the expert: "You fly, old friend. Highest of any of us who started grounded. At the beginning?"

Oliver Owl was quiet a long moment. "There is a night..." He said. "every owl remembers and none discuss. The first branch. The mother gone quiet. The dark below... and we love the dark, you understand, the dark is home... but the dark below, with nothing under your feet yet but trust...

Every owl sits on that first branch scared. Every single one, since owls began. And then... One step..." said Stella. "One hop..." corrected the owl. "We hop before we fly. Nobody tells you that about flight... it starts as the smallest, most terrified hop, one branch down, wings out mostly in panic.

And the wings... this is the secret... the wings only learn the air by being in it. You cannot practice flying on the branch. One scared hop. That's how every soaring thing you've ever admired began.

Mmm..." Hummed the Moon, who has watched every first flight this valley ever launched, feathered and otherwise. "Then log this month..." said the owl. "Log it: the small one below is hopping. Branch by branch, rope by rope, hand- raise by hand-raise.

The air is learning her. Flight: on schedule. The usual schedule. One scared hop at a time." Goodnight, hoppers of Willowbrook. The air is patient. The air is waiting to learn you. Hop.
