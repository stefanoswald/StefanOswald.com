Night 274

The Letter That Followed Her Home

Month 10: Words Are Magic

It started with the bakery sign. "Mama. Dat circle letter. Da O one. Dat's my letter... da first one in my name. Miss Dana showed us. That's the one." And then, the walk home, forever changed: "Dere's another one.

On da door! And on dat license plate! And. Mama. Da O is on everything. Was it always on everything?!" It was always on everything. "Den why didn't I see it?!" And the answer... this month's whole doorway: "Because letters are invisible until you learn them.

Then they turn on. Like lights. One letter at a time, the whole world starts... glowing with them. Every sign, every box, every page... it's all been talking this whole time. You're just starting to hear it."

The rest of the walk: an O hunt. (Counted.) And at the lamp, the wondering: "If da O turned on... da other letters can turn on too?" All of them. Twenty-six lights. "And den I could read da words?"

And then you could read the words. (The sentence landed the way the biggest ones do: quietly, eyes wide, whole future rearranging. This month had found its magic. The lights were coming on.)
