Night 52

Teaching Baba to Whistle

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

A meadow project this month, her idea: "Baba. I'm gonna teach you something. You taught me everything. It's my turn." The lesson: The whistle. (Hers. The year-long yet. The hardest-won skill in her whole kit.

Naturally the one she'd teach.) The lessons, meadow- nightly: "Lips like dis. Small hole. Blow soft, not spit-blow, wind-blow..." The bear's attempts: honest. Furry lips are shape whistles never planned for. Night one: "pfffff."

Night two: "pffTH." Night three: a sound like a kettle giving up. And the teacher... watch her; this is the important part... never once laughed wrong, never once fixed it for him, and deployed, at his lowest kettle-moment, the entire inherited law: "Baba.

You can't whistle..." (the pause; she has the pause; she is the pause now) "... Yet. Yet means it's coming. Mine took a whole year. Yours can too. We got forever here. Dat's da meadow's whole deal."

And some nights later... week two, maybe three... thin, wavering, unmistakable, from a bear's impossible lips: a whistle. One note. True. The meadow rang with cheering. The lantern fish leaped. "I whistled..." said the bear, stunned.

"You taught da teacher." She taught the teacher. The circle, fully closed: everything poured in, poured back, both directions, forever. They whistled together at the dream-moon. It hummed back. Both notes. Perfectly wrong. Completely theirs.
