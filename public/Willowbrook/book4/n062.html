Night 62

Why Do Worms Come Out in Rain

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

Rain walk. Worms out. And this year, the crouch comes with a why: "Why do da worms come up when it rains?" The answer, at worm level: "They live in tunnels, in the dirt. When rain fills the tunnels... they can't breathe down there.

So they come up for air, and travel while everything's wet, because wet keeps them safe. Dry is dangerous for a worm. So rain is deir... Up time?" Their up time. Their traveling weather. "Den we should be careful where we step on rain days.

It's deir sidewalk time." (New family rule, self-issued, kept forever: rain days, watch your step. It's their sidewalk time.) The why, answered, became a kindness. That's the why-year's secret bonus: understanding is the front door of care.

She steps carefully now because she knows the tunnels flood. Every why answered true makes the world easier to love correctly. The worms, unaware, traveled on, safer by one educated three- year-old.
