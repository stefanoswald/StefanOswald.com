Night 139

The Snack Chef for Two

Month 5: Little Brother Tags Along

The sandwich-making... her old sovereign skill, expanded this month: Service for two. His order, taken seriously: "Fee-oh. What kind? Nana. And... cheese." (Banana and cheese. Together. On bread. The chef paused. The chef did not say "dat's weird."

The chef has watched Rosa take orders for years: the customer's weird is the customer's right.) Built to spec: banana coins, cheese squares, bread folded. Served. Devoured. Entirely. ("Nana-cheese" entered the household menu permanently.

It's... actually fine? Nobody admits it. Everyone's tried it. The chef takes no position. The chef serves.) And the service standard, set: his plate like her plate. His weird like her weird. The little customer, honored at the same counter, by the same rules, daily.

"Thank you! Come again!" the old shopkeeper's close, delivered per tradition. "Aden!" the standing reorder, delivered per his. He always comes again. Best regular she's got.
