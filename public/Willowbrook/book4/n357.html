Night 357

The Party

Month 12: Almost Four

The day, as kept by the story, which attended all of it: The arrivals: everybody. (The law of everybody, fulfilled. Thirteen classmates. The families. Mateo, fence-vaulting. Grandpa Joe, truck and all. Wren, early, by arrangement..."

Before da loud starts. "And Petrov, in his good coat, bearing his own tools "just in case..." Who fixed the picnic table by 2:15 out of pure reflex.) The games: The bug hunt: won by, upset of the season, grandpa joe. (.

"Fifty years of knowing where bugs live. Not a fair fight. Da trophy"... a jar; of course a jar, "goes to June. Steward of bugs. Trophies belong with da experts.") Musical cushions: chaos, rolls, glory.

The cake: the reveal. (The cloth. The hush. The sky, meadow, willow, green room, wings, book, star- jar, all of it, and the gasp, and then the four candles, and the song, and the wish... sealed.

Drawn, not spelled. Blown in one.) The cushions. Eleven littler kids and several un-littler ones The bear book, held facing out, professional grip: "Dis book taught me da shape of stories. Now I read it to you.

Dat's how books work. Dey're not yours. Dey're just your turn." (Books are just your turn. Said at a fourth birthday party, cushion-throned, cake-smudged. The librarian heard it Monday via three separate sources.

The wainscoting got it word for word.) The reading: flawless. The gasp at the lost button: eleven- voiced. The nigh-nigh: delivered to a yard gone soft and golden. Best one yet. (They're all da best one yet.

Confirmed again. Four candles down. As it happened its own lump in its own throat and lets it stand.)
