Night 48

The Bear's Request

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

At the end of a meadow walk this week, the bear made a request. His first ever. (Three years of giving. One ask. Note the ratio. That's the soft division's whole union contract.) "Little one.

My paw. Da right one. Dere's a fin spot coming... da fur's going tired. Could somebody... patch it? Like my heart one? I'd like matching." He'd like matching. She checked the second she woke: the right paw.

And there it was... the worn place, the fur rubbed thin by three years of being held exactly there, by exactly her, every night. (Worn spots are love's receipts. The bear knows it. He didn't ask for repair.

He asked for a patch... a mark, like the heart. Love's receipts, made visible.) The mending: that afternoon. Mama's needle. And the patch, chosen by the patient's representative from the scrap basket: a small square of her old yellow blanket.

(The blanket. The softest one in the world, book two, night one. Retired to the scrap basket last spring. Now: part of the bear.) "Now he's got my blanket in him..." She said, watching the stitches.

Now he has her blanket in him. The heart patch and the paw patch. Matching, as requested. That night, on the meadow, the bear held up his paw in the glow-grass light: "Look at dat.

Good as new. Better, new was never dis loved." Better. New was never this loved. Neither, little one, were any of us. That's the patch system. That's the whole economy of worn.
