Night 331

The Crocus Patrol

Month 11: Winter Wonders

The warm wind held. And this week, the year's first green: The crocuses. (Spears first... found by the patrol, hands and knees, at the south bed: "Green! Green coming up! Everybody! Da ground's doing it!")

Then, days later, the blooms: purple, white, gold... small, early, absolutely unbothered by the cold nights, the way crocuses are. (First up. Bravest of the flowers. One- step rule, botanical division.) And then, the appointment.

The one made at a window, over a slumping snowman, months ago: "Fee-oh. Come. It's time." (The little brother, led by hand to the gold ones. The law, redeemed:) "Da snowman. Remember? Water, den ground, den flowers?...

Dese flowers. Da snowman's in dere now. Spring proof. Like I promised." And Theo... two-and-a-half, theologically flexible, absolutely certain... leaned down to the crocuses and greeted them: "Snowman! You got little! And purple!"

(You got little and purple. The story, as previously ruled, lets it stand. The law holds: nothing loved gets lost. It gets little and purple and comes back gold.) The patrol logged the count: eleven crocuses, two snowdrops, one brave early tulip spear.

The garden: officially awake. And under the milkweed... checked last, checked daily... the thin dark cradle, rainbow pressing its walls, waiting for exactly this: warm wind, gold flowers, a world worth coming out to. Any morning now. Any morning.
