Night 87

Why Do Stars Twinkle

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

Yard blanket. Clear night. The subject: overhead, in the thousands. "Why do stars twinkle? The stars don't, actually. They shine steady. It's our air that twinkles them... the sky's rivers of warm and cool, bending the starlight as it falls through.

The shimmer is the atmosphere, shivering. From space, no twinkle. Steady lights. So da twinkle is... da air dancing with da light?" The air dancing with the light. "Den why does dat one..." (the low gold one; you knew; of course you knew) "...twinkle more?

And why does it twinkle when I wave?" (... She waved. The star, later what the yard saw, twinkled. Once. Distinctly. On the beat.) "That one..." said Papa carefully. "might be in the wonder-file. Della..."

She said, with satisfaction. "She's not a science star. She's our star. Different rules." Different rules. (The science: real. The air: dancing. Every star in the thousands: bent starlight, honestly shimmering. And one, one particular gold one, low over one particular lane... answering waves, per different rules, by the file, per three books of documented correspondence.

Both true. The family word, stretched across the whole sky now: both.) Goodnight, science. Goodnight, Della. Twinkle once for yes... She did. She always does.
