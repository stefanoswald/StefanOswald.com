Night 275

The Name Written Whole

Month 10: Words Are Magic

The O she could draw. The L arrived at school. The I: easy..." A stick with a dot hat. "The V: "a bird going down." The A: "a ladder roof." The final A: "aden!" And this week, at the kitchen table, unprompted, tongue out, marker gripped: O.

L. I. V. I. A. The whole name. Wobbling. Unmistakable. Hers. "... I wrote me. Mama. Papa. I wrote my whole me." I wrote my whole me. (The fridge gallery... curator: Mama; standards: none; love: total... received it at center position, eye height, where it stayed so long the tape yellowed and got ceremonially replaced, twice.)

And Papa, studying the six letters like a museum piece, which, in this house, they were: "You know what you just did? Ten thousand years ago somebody figured out you could make sounds visible. Marks that talk.

And tonight you joined them. Every book in the library starts exactly here: somebody, one table, one marker, writing their own name and feeling the power go on." The power: on. The name: written. The writer: made.

(She signed everything for a month. Drawings. Napkins. Theo's arm, once, in washable marker... a diplomatic incident, resolved at the fence. Writers gonna write. The story signs off the same way she now does everything: O-l-i-v-i-a. Whole me.)
