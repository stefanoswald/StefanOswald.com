Night 290

The Bedtime Story, Reversed

Month 10: Words Are Magic

Announced at dinner, formally: "Tonight, da reverse. I read the bedtime story. You two get tucked. I do da lamp." The staging, directed precisely: Mama and Papa: In the reading chair and the small chair, respectively.

The bear: on Papa's lap. The lamp: hers. The reading: "Dog... sits. Dog sits down... Dog sees... da ball." (Slow. Finger under words. Grandpa's method. Every page turned with the outward- facing flourish of a professional who has watched a thousand turnings.)

Eight pages. Four minutes. The longest four minutes of concentration in her documented life. And the closing, executed to tradition: "Da end... Nigh- nigh, Mama. Nigh-nigh, Papa. Sleep when you're sleepy. Da lamp will be here."

(Her lullaby lines. Given back. Word for word, the way they were given to her, four thousand nights running.) as it happened what the parents did after lights- out, in the hallway. The story respects them enough to note only: barn dust.

Both of them. Lots of it. (The reverse became a weekly institution. Sundays. Her lamp. Their turn. It never stopped... check the far pages: a teenager reading Theo's favorites over his protests; a grown woman, one distant Sunday, reading to her own listener. The lamp passes. That was always the plan. Night 290: first passing.)
