Night 172

The Buttons Funeral

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

A hard page this month. A true one. Buttons died. (The hamster. Old, by hamster arithmetic. Found still on a Tuesday morning, peaceful, in the cedar corner of his cage.) Twelve four-year-olds. One empty wheel.

And Miss Dana... who could have disappeared the cage over a weekend, the easy way, the way some classrooms do... did the other thing. The whole thing: The telling: plain and soft. "Buttons died in the night.

His body stopped working. He was old and comfortable and it didn't hurt." The questions: All taken. ("Is he sleeping?" No... died is different from sleeping; sleeping ends. "Will you die?" Someday, a long time from now.

"Will I?" the room held its breath, "Everything that lives, dies, love. It's part of being alive at all. Mostly it comes after a long, long life full of wheels and sunflower seeds." Plain. Soft.

True. No fibs. The fibs, the books all agree, cost more later.) And the funeral: a shoebox. The school maple. Twelve kids, one song, and flowers, dandelions, naturally, brought by the girl who knows what flowers are for.

Her report, at home, at the lamp, processing: "Buttons died. Died is when da body stops for always. We put him under da maple so da tree keeps him. I'm sad. Miss Dana says sad is da right size feeling for it...

Mama. Da gold dog is old." (... There it is. The generalization. The gold dog is old. This month knew this page was coming; the book has known for volumes. Not yet. But the vocabulary is stocked now... died, always, the tree keeps him, sad is the right size... laid in gently, hamster-scale, against the bigger pages ahead.)

"He is old," said Mama. Just that. True. Held. The dandelions went to the fence next morning. Extra ones. Just because.
