Night 38

The Daytime Wink

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

The story swore off explaining things. It keeps that vow. It only reports: Late afternoon. Gold light through the nursery window, the long kind, the kind the town does best. Olivia, at her shelf, mid-tidy, talking to the bear the way she always talks to the bear... "...and den Franklin said da tea was too hot, which is silly, because it's air..." and she stopped.

Because the bear's flopped ear... the left one, the destiny one, flopped since the factory... settled. Moved. A half-inch. Gently. The way things settle. In the exact pause of her sentence. At the exact angle of the gold.

Like a nod. Like, she stood very still, a wink, if ears could wink. Now: stuffing settles. Physics exists. Ears flop further at four p.m. For a hundred boring reasons. The book knows all the reasons.

The book also knows what she did: she didn't run to tell anyone. She just smiled... slow, private, entire... and said: "... I knew it." And went back to tidying. Some moments aren't evidence. They're gifts.

You don't test them. You keep them. She kept it her whole life... never proved, never doubted, the afternoon the ear settled mid-sentence in the gold. (The bear, for his part, said nothing. Daytime is for being held.

But as it happened: he picked the better job. And even the better job, apparently, gets one wink a year.)
