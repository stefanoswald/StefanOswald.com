Night 104

The Giant Duck Problem

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

An ambush this week. From the least expected direction: a duck. The new market wing's grand opening. Balloons. And out front, waving: a mascot. A person- sized, a person-sized... duck costume. And Olivia, the duck girl, the founder of the whole duck canon, first word duck- adjacent, duck art on every wall... took one look at seven feet of waddling yellow and went behind the legs.

Full retreat. The old shy position. Wide, wide eyes. (And fair. Fair. Ducks are palm-sized. A duck the size of Papa is a category error. The wrongness of scale is a real fear, old, deep, sensible.

Nobody's brain accepts a giant duck calmly the first time. Nobody's should.) The handling: her pace. Entirely. No "but you love ducks!" No dragging forward. Just the legs, held still for hiding behind, and the narration: "That's a big one, huh.

It's a costume... a person inside, being a duck for the store. Like your bee, but giant. We can watch from here as long as you want." They watched. Five minutes. Ten. The duck waved at kids.

Danced badly. Removed its own head once, revealing a sweaty teenager, waved with the head under its arm, put it back on. "... There's a kid in the duck." There's a kid in the duck. (The head-removal: accidentally the perfect exposure therapy.

The wrongness, explained by its own seams.) The final approach: her call, day three, different visit... a high-five with the giant duck. Photographed. Framed by the aunts, obviously. "Ducks should be little..." She maintained ever after.

"But costume-ducks are okay. Dere's kids inside." Correct on all counts. The category, repaired. The canon, intact. Scale matters. Seams help. Her pace, always.
