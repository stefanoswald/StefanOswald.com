Night 109

The Meet-at-the-Mailbox Drill

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

This week: the family drill. (The fire one. Every house needs it. Most houses postpone it. This house did it on a sunny Saturday, calm as a game, which is exactly the craft: practice the scary thing when nothing's scary.

Boring is the goal. Boring is armor.) The plan, taught in three pieces: If the loud beeper ever really means it: Get low. ("Smoke lives up high. Good air lives down low. Crawl like the old days, you were fast, ask the book.")

Get out. ("Nothing else. Not toys. Not even..." the hard part, said plain "...not even Baba. Bears are replaceable. You are not. He'd tell you the same... he'd insist."... A long pause at that one.

A nod, finally. The hardest part, held.) Meet at the mailbox. ("Our spot. Everybody goes there. Nobody goes back in. The firefighters do the going-back-in. It's their whole job and they're great at it.")

The drill, run twice: crawl-low across the living room, out the door, to the mailbox. Family count: four. High-fives at the mailbox. Done. Total time: six minutes. Never mentioned fearfully again... just re-run each season, casual as the statue drill, boring as intended.

"Da mailbox is da meet-spot," she'd tell anyone who'd listen, for years. It was never needed. It was always there. That's the whole economics of drills: you buy them cheap on sunny days so you never pay full price in the dark.
