Night 72

Why Do Flowers Close at Night

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

Dusk. The garden round. And a noticing: "Mama. Da flowers are closing." (The little ones... the daisies, the dandelions, folding their petals in, slow as sleep.) "Why do dey close?" The answer, at flowerbed level: "They're tucking in.

Really. Petals are delicate, night is cold and damp, and moths bumble around, and there's no sun to work with anyway. So they fold up everything precious and wait for morning. Flowers keep the same hours you do.

The garden has a bedtime." The garden has a bedtime. She walked the beds after that like a ward nurse: "Night-night, daisies. Night-night, dandy-lions. Da tomatoes don't close?" Tomatoes are fruit... they're not delicate, they're busy.

They ripen all night. "Da tomatoes work da night shift?" The tomatoes work the night shift. (Delight, total. The garden: a town. Day workers, night workers, everyone on their own clock... exactly like the real one, exactly like everything, the closer you look.)

"Night-night, garden..." At the gate. "Good work everybody. See you at opening." The garden, tucked and shifted, worked and slept. The nurse went in to her own folding. Same hours. Same care. Same morning coming to open everyone back up, petal by petal by girl.
