Night 119

The Month of the Friendly Dark, Counted

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

Month's end. The check. Countables: Fears retired: the shadow, the under-bed, the giant duck, the vacuum. Fears respected (sorted, kept, correct): the strange dog, the deep end. Plans bought cheap: the lost drill, the mailbox drill.

One nightmare: rewritten at 2 a.m. By its author. One grown-up fear: named on a ladder. (The formula works at altitude.) One uphill loan: the brave list, to the faculty. One bridge: crossed, hand under hand.

One night walk: the dark, befriended outdoors, found busy. And one solo night... the crown of it... away, alone, under farm dark and its lots-lots stars: "Da dark's not empty. It's fuller. You gotta leave da lamps to see."

Uncountables: That the fears were never the enemy. This month never fought them. It sorted them... alarm from fire, yet from never, fun-scary from real... and met each kind with its own right tool.

That courage turned out to be a lending library. And that a girl who started this month renaming the dark ended it tucking the dark in... kindly, Nightly. Known. "Good dark-monf?" The best one.

"Da dark thinks so too. It told me." The dark thinks so too. Received, dark. Remembered and carried forward: friendly, on both shifts.
