Night 211

The Maybe Month, Counted

Month 7: The Maybe Lessons

Month's end. The check. Countables: Twelve slips. (Eleven hers-and-family's. One his, the cracker, the founding catch of generation two.) One franchise. (The classroom jar. Twelve new catchers. The marbled-paper technique, spreading to other rooms.)

One slow maybe: honored, open, watching. (The snow globe. The jar waits. The book stays unslammed.) Uncountables: That the word changed jobs this month. It used to be Mama's word, applied to her, at spilled milk and crashes.

Then it was hers... said back, at three, at the lamp. Now? Now it's a reflex. Said in advance. Half-believed before the turn. The gap between bad-thing and maybe shrinking all month, until, at the burnt cookies, it arrived during the smoke alarm.

That's not a word anymore. That's a lens, grown into the eyes. Life doesn't happen to us. It happens for us. And the ones who believe it early... the story has watched this town long enough to say it plain... they don't just survive their bad pages.

They farm them. "Good maybe-monf? Da best. And da snow globe one's still coming. I can feel it." (She could. It was. Never slam the book.) Remembered and carried forward: watching, catching, farming the gray for gold.
