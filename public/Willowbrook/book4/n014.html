Night 14

When the Pretend Gets Too Real

Month 1: Pretend!

A wrinkle this week. An important one. The invisible wild bear... the friendly one, from the snow land... followed her out. At dusk, at toy-cleanup, suddenly: "Mama. Da wild bear is in da hallway.

I don't want him dere now." The pretend, tipped over. Too vivid. The author, spooked by her own character... which happens, at three, when the world-making power runs ahead of the world-closing power.

Here's what Mama didn't say: "There's no bear. It's not real." (Useless. Worse than useless. The bear is real enough, that's the whole gift and the whole problem. Dismissing the bear dismisses the gift.) What she said instead: "You made the bear.

That means you're the boss of him. Authors close their own doors. Want to send him home?"... Send him home? She could do that? "Draw his door. Right there. Open it. Tell him it's bedtime in the snow land."

The door: drawn. The knob: turned. "Bear. It's bedtime. Go home now... He's going. He waved. He's not mad." The door: Closed. Locked, even, an invisible key, invented on the spot, pocketed. The hallway: empty.

Hers again. The author's full powers, restored: not just making worlds... Closing them. The key stayed in her pajama pocket for months. Used nightly. She closes them. The boss of the bears sleeps soundly.
