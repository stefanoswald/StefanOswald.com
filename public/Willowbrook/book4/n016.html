Night 16

The Farm Pretend

Month 1: Pretend!

Sunday. The farm. And an interesting collision: what happens when the pretend visits the real thing? Olivia brought her kit, the toy tools, the star barrow... and announced: "I'm a farmer today." And Grandpa Joe... an actual farmer, of actual decades... did not smile the indulgent smile.

He nodded the colleague nod. "Good. I'm short-handed. Row four needs weeding. Real weeds. You know da difference now." (She does. He taught her. Feathery carrot tops stay; everything else goes.) And the pretend and the real ran the same rows all morning: Her barrow hauled real weeds.

Her watering was real water on real thirsty rows. Theo, a dog today, naturally..." Guarded. "the coop. (The hens were unimpressed but tolerant. Henrietta has seen every game this family owns.) And at the break, on the porch with real lemonade: "Am I pretend farming or real farming, Gampa?"

Rock. Rock. "What's da difference?" She thought hard. "... Real farming, you hafta come back tomorrow." The old man laughed his whole quiet laugh. "Forty years..." He said. "and that's the best definition I ever heard."

Real is what you come back to. The pretend teaches the shape. The coming-back makes it true. She comes back every Sunday. Farmer. Real one. Small so far.
