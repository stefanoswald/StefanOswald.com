Night 351

The Photo Album Night

Month 12: Almost Four

The albums came out this week. (Birthday-week tradition. The big bed. The whole family. Four years, spine by spine.) The audience: two children. One historian apiece: For her: the deep cuts. ("Dat's you at one day.

Dat's da May snow out da window behind you. Dat's da duck. The duck. The first-word one. Dat's you meeting Lily. You're both bald. You'd known her eleven days and you're already holding hands... look, we didn't pose dat.

You two just did dat.") For Theo: the captions, simplified, shouted back: ("Wivy little! Dat's right. Wivy bald! Also right. Duck!! The very one.") And the strange magic, working on her, page over page... watchable at the lamp after: "Mama.

Da albums are weird. Dat baby doesn't know anything dat's coming. She doesn't know about Lily-forever or Fee-oh or da meadow or reading. I wanted to tell her. I kept almost saying it to da picture: 'just wait.

Just wait. You won't believe it.'And if you could tell her one thing?" (The long think. The gold-star sweater sleeve, worried between finger and thumb.) "... Nothing. I wouldn't tell her nothing. Da not-knowing is da good part.

Everything got to surprise her... Everything got to surprise me. Dat's da best way to get a life: one page at a time, no peeking." One page at a time. No peeking, little one. Sleep.

The next page is Saturday. It's a good one. They're all good ones. That's the one thing the story would have told the baby, if telling were allowed: they're all good ones.
