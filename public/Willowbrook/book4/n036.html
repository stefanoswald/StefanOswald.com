Night 36

The Cow Walks

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

Two days after turning two, at breakfast, Theo made his report. (Slowly. His words are fewer. The wonder was the same size.) "Livy. Da cow. Da cow walked." The table went still. "In your dream?!

Uh-huh. He gots... a bell. Inside. It ringed when he walked." (The bell. The swallowed bell. The one that's been inside him since the fair. In the dream: Ringing. Nobody taught him that detail.

Nobody could have. They remembered it and declines, per policy, to explain.) "Where did you walk? Da meadow. Big one. Dere was...a bear dere too. Walking. He waved." He saw baba. The meadow is shared.

The two dreamers stared at each other across the toast... the enormity landing: same meadow. Both bears, bear and cow. The soft division, awake, together, on the other side. "Fee-oh. Listen. Tonight, we both dream.

Same time. Maybe... maybe we find each udder." (Did they? The story is honest: dreams are slippery, and mornings-after brought mixed reports for months, "I saw your foot maybeda cow said you was just dere," near misses, mostly.

But twice, twice that year, per two breathless matching breakfast reports... the meadow held all four at once: girl, boy, bear, cow, by the glowing brook, skipping stones. Twice is plenty. Twice is everything. Some doors only need to open twice to stay real forever.)
