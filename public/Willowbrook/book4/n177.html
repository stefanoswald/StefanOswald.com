Night 177

The First Snow at School

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

It came mid-circle- time. One kid saw it first. Wren, naturally, the noticer, and pointed, wordless, at the window. Snow. First of the year. Fat flakes, falling slow. And twelve four- year-olds levitated.

(Circle time: lost. Unrecoverable. Miss Dana, fourteen winters wise, didn't even try. She declared it: "Emergency recess. Coats. Go.") Emergency recess. (The institution's finest hour. Some lessons outrank the schedule. First snow is one. It's maybe all of them.)

The yard, in the falling: Twelve tongues out. The flip-taught jackets, flipped in record time. ("I taught free kids da flip today. Da flip is spreading.") The first snowball... Marcus's, obviously... sailing, per school law, at no person.

(The fence took it. The fence takes everything. Fences are the town's oldest goalies.) And the quiet moment, reported by Miss Dana, who collects these, of two cubby neighbors standing still at the fence, faces up, catching flakes side by side in the together-quiet: "It's da hush kind..." said one. "Da library of weathers," said the other. (The library of weathers. Fourteen winters, said Miss Dana, and the file grows every year.) The snow kept falling through pickup. The walk home took forty minutes.

(Four blocks. Archaeology, snow edition. Every flake, apparently, required greeting.) Winter: opened. At institutional scale. Emergency recess: the best class of the moment. It always is.
