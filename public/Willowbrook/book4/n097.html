Night 97

Theo's First Fear

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

Theo's turn this week. His first real fear, arrived at two-and- some, on schedule: The vacuum. (The roar. The lunging. The cord like a tail. A reasonable monster, honestly. The vacuum has scared better men.)

At first roar: flight. Full toddle-sprint, tears launching... not to Mama. (Mama was holding the monster.) And the harbor... watch her run the whole inherited kit, scaled to two... received him: The scoop. (Onto the couch.

High ground. Monsters don't climb couches; everyone knows.) The naming: "Dat's da vacuum. It's loud. Loud is scary. You're scared. Dat's allowed." The reframe: "But Fee-oh, it's not a monster. It's a cleaner. It eats crumbs.

Watch, it can't even see us. It only wants da floor." The exposure, dosed: watched from the couch-harbor, roar and all, narrated... "dere it goes... eating da crumbs... dat's your cracker crumb, say bye..." until the roar stopped and the monster was wheeled to its closet..."

Where it sleeps. It sleeps all day. It only wakes up for crumbs. "By the third vacuuming, Theo watched from the floor. By the fifth, he followed it. (The vacuum: demoted to pet. Its name, per its handler: "Crumby."

It answered to nothing, being a vacuum, but the name stuck for a decade.) The kit, passed down whole. The chain, unbroken: comfort flows downhill, scaled to fit, harbor to harbor, forever.
