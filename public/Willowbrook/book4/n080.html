Night 80

The Why Journal

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

Month's end approaching. And the almanac... keeper of firsts, storms, and stones... gained a column: The why journal. This month's best, entered verbatim: "Why is toast brown? Why is da sky bounced?" (Answered. Beautifully mangled.

Kept mangled.) "Why do worms get sidewalk time? Where did da living fold Gampa? Why is dere anything instead of nothing?" (Open. Referred to owl. Owl referred it back to the universe. Standing.) "Do mamas go in ambulances?"

(The worry one. Named. Held. Closed.) "Why do we keep some whys?" (The willow one. Kept, per its own answer. The wonder-file.) And the journal's purpose, written at its head in Mama's hand: "Questions are how she loves the world.

Keep them all. Someday she'll want to know what she wondered... and we'll want to remember when every single thing was worth asking about." The column ran for years. Hundreds of entries. Two handwritings, then three, she adds her own now, the far pages show, in wobbling letters: her whys, self-saved.

The book of wonder, open. Never closed again. Every family should keep one. Start tonight: what did yours ask today? Write it down. It's the world, being loved, in question marks.
