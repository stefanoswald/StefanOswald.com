Night 301

The Word Willow

Month 10: Words Are Magic

A pilgrimage this week, up the hill, reader and mother, to the oldest listener in town: The willow. And beneath it... the realization, arriving with the force letters now give everything: "Mama. Da willow words.

Home when I turned two. Family when I turned free... Da willow is writing something. One word a year. Da tree is da slowest writer in town." (The slowest writer in town. She's the first to say it out loud as writing... because she's the first to stand under it as a writer herself.)

"What's it writing? A sentence? A poem? Mama... what if it's a list? What do you think?" (The thinking: long. Willow- shaped. The branches moving their gold a little, the way they do.) "... I think it's a sentence.

Because home and den family... dose aren't list words. Dose are... building words. A sentence dat builds. And it's got years and years to finish. Trees aren't in a hurry. Dat's da whole thing about trees."

A sentence that builds. Years to finish. "And my birthday's coming. Word free comes on my birthday... I'm gonna be dere when da willow writes." (She was. Night 361. But it confirms: the tree is writing.

It's a sentence. It builds. And the slowest writer in town has never once missed a deadline.)
