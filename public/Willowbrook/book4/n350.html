Night 350

The Town Delivers Week

Month 12: Almost Four

Birthday week opened, and with it, the tradition the town started back when she turned two and never once dropped: Town-delivers week. (No organizing. No list. Just: the week before her birthday, the town... delivers.

Small things. Daily. Doorstep-grade. It began as coincidence. It became ritual.) This year's tally book: Monday: a flower on the step. (First crocus- patrol gold, transplanted to a jar. Unsigned. Handwriting of the note...

"For da birthday girl"... suspiciously June-adjacent.) Tuesday: rolls. (Warm. Rosa's. "Baker's dozen means thirteen. Birthday dozen means as many as fit da bag.") Wednesday: from the library... held at the desk with her name on it... a book.

Thursday: at the fence, from Mateo, gruff, ceremonial: one perfect whistle-acorn. Friday: Petrov. The wheelbarrow... the birthday one, the star- stenciled one... returned from "maintenance" nobody requested: oiled, tightened, one new star added to the stencil.

("Four stars now..." said the tag. "Menders keep count too.") And all week, the reflection, gathering: "Mama. Da town knows me. Not just knows... Keeps me. Da librarian keeps my reading. Mateo keeps my code.

Petrov keeps my stars. Everybody's keeping a different piece." And Mama, at the lamp: "That's what a town is, love. A place that agrees to keep pieces of everybody. You've been handing yours out for four years, every hello, every jar, every letter.

Birthday week is just the town handing them back so you can see how many there are." The town, handing her back. Piece by piece. All week. (She kept every note. It's full. It stayed full. Some people bank in towns. This family taught her how.)
