Night 162

Mateo at the Big School

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

The lane's other schooling news: Mateo started big school. First grade. The brick one. The bus one. And the fence reunions, afternoons, became the lane's best programming: "What's it like?" The reports, delivered to his faithful fence audience: "Dere's a gym.

A whole room just for running. On purpose. Lunch is in a cafeteria. You carry a tray. And I got homework." He showed it: a worksheet. Letters to trace. The audience regarded it like a relic.

"Is homework bad? Big kids on tv say... It's kinda... good? It's like da school misses you and sends a little school home with you." (A little school home with you. The single best reframe of homework ever recorded.

Teachers: take it. It's free.) "Will I go dere? After preschool. Everybody flows up. Like da hand-me-downs. I'll be in third grade when you come. I'll show you where everything is." The promise, fence-witnessed: he'll show her where everything is.

(He did. The far books hold it: her first day at the brick school, and a third-grader materializing at the bus line... orange sneakers, tool belt outgrown, loyalty intact... "dis way, little Oswald." The lane's escalator: everybody flows up, nobody flows alone.)
