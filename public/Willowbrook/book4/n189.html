Night 189

The Burnt Cookies Maybe

Month 7: The Maybe Lessons

Baking day. And then: the forgetting. (The phone rang. The timer didn't. Somebody, the story protects the guilty; it was Mama... lost track.) The smoke alarm. Full shriek. Both kids: hands on ears. The cookies: black.

Utterly. Twelve small charcoals. The gloom, the waste, the ringing ears... "... Maybe?" shouted somebody, over the alarm, fanning it with a towel. And the maybes, counted later, came in threes: One: the alarm lesson. Live.

("Dat's da sound. Dat's it doing its job. Loud on purpose, it has to wake sleepers. Now you know it, and it's not scary, it's just rude. Rude on purpose. For love." The drill, refreshed free of charge.)

Two: batch two... made together, double chocolate, "with free timers dis time"... the best cookies of the winter. Three: the charcoals themselves, crumbled, per Rosa's consulted wisdom, over ice cream: "burnt crumble. Fancy restaurants charge for dat."

(They do. It was delicious. Nobody tell the fancy restaurants the recipe is 'forget, panic, crumble.') The slip: "Burned da cookies. Seemed very bad and loud. Got: da alarm lesson, better cookies, and fancy crumble. Free maybes, one bad batch. Caught caught caught."
