Night 238

The Chart Grows a Column

Month 8: Helping Hands

An new rule this week, proposed by the workforce: "Da chart's missing stuff. Missing what? Da real jobs. Da ones nobody assigns. Checking da fern isn't on it, dat's just watering. But checking is different.

And da rounds. And noticing when Fee-oh's sad before he cries. And da secret ones, dey can't go on da chart or dey stop being secret, but dey should count." The summit, reconvened. The new rule, drafted: a new column.

Crayon-headed: "The noticing jobs." No clothespins. No checkmarks. Just listed, acknowledged, real: - checking on things - noticing sads early - da rounds - secret ones - receiving help when you need it (the newest hire's own addition, still warm from its lesson) "Why list them if we can't check them off?

Because..." ruled the workforce. "unlisted jobs get invisible. And invisible jobs get dropped. Mama does a thousand of dem. I counted some. Dey should be seen even if dey can't be checked." (... Mama does a thousand of them.

She counted some. The story pauses. The noticing jobs, noticed. The invisible labor, made visible, in crayon, by a four-year-old counter. The column stayed on every chart this family ever made. Economists take decades to find this column. She found it before kindergarten.)
