Night 286

Olivia Writes a Book

Month 10: Words Are Magic

On Tuesday Olivia announced that she was making a real book.

Mama folded four sheets of paper and stapled the middle. Olivia wrote the title: THE BEAR WHO LOST HIS WHISTLE.

"It sounds a little like the bear-and-button story," Papa said.

"That book taught me the shape," Olivia explained. "Something gets lost. There is a sad part. Then it gets found, and everybody sleeps. My whistle goes inside that shape."

She drew a bear searching beneath a chair, behind a tree, and finally under a leaf. Her nine-word story ended: NIGHT-NIGHT, BEAR. THE END. BY OLIVIA.

At dinner she held the pages facing outward and read to the family. Theo demanded it again four times.

"The first reading is free," Olivia told him. "The others cost one hug."

At bedtime she placed the little book beside Bobby Bear.

"There is a book in the world that was not there this morning," she said. "Mine is small, but all the books together are bigger because I added it."

Mama kissed the author goodnight. "That is how every shelf grows.
