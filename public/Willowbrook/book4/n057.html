Night 57

The Count of the Dreamed-In

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

Month's end. And the kitchen map, the meadow atlas page... got its count box. (Real maps have them. Hers should too. She's seen the town sign: Willowbrook, pop. And a number. The meadow deserves the same.)

The roster, counted in crayon: "Baba. He lives dere now, kind of. Number one. Da cow. Colleague. Two. Me. Free. Fee-oh. Four. Franklin. Five... draw him as a warm spot. Dat's what he looks like."

(A small orange smudge was added. Franklin, rendered. His first portrait in a thousand and three years.) "Wiwi's rabbit... he's in da country but a different door. Does he count? Da lantern fish: eleven. Da cow counted.

Da moff: one. He's okay now. Baba's working on it." And the count concluded, the number written big at the map's corner: Meadow, pop. Lots (Numbers were attempted. Numbers were abandoned. "Lots" was ruled more accurate... because the count's own final line, spoken for the story, made counting beside the point:) "Population: everybody I love, whenever dey get dreamed in.

Da number keeps going up. Dat's da whole kind of country it is." The number keeps going up. That's the whole kind of country it is. The map went back on the wall. The count stands open. Forever, by design.
