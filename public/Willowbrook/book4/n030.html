Night 30

Oliver Owl and the Doubled Town

Month 1: Pretend!

On the final evening, gold light gathered in the Great Willow's branches. And Stella arrived with a surveying problem: "The town below has grown. Substantially. And I can't map it. Whoo? There's a castle on Maple Lane now.

Cardboard, officially, but I've watched it at dusk and I'm telling you, some nights it has towers. There's a sea in the living room, Tuesdays. An island six days out by couch. A snow land.

I felt the cold myself, passing over. And doors. Doors drawn in plain air, opening onto countries that aren't on any chart I hold. Mmm..." Hummed the Moon... who sees into dreams, who countersigns bear paperwork, who knew all of it already.

And Oliver Owl asked this month's question: "Old friends, when a child doubles the world... where does the second world go? When she's grown? When the castles flatten and the doors stop being drawn?" The branch was quiet.

The willow let down its gold. "It doesn't go..." said Stella slowly, working it out. "I've watched this town for generations. It goes under. The second world sinks in and becomes... the way they see.

The grown ones who built castles, they're the ones who look at a bare hill and see an orchard. A stranger and see a friend. A closed door and draw a new one. The pretend doesn't leave.

It becomes imagination, and imagination becomes the town. Mmm..." said the Moon: correct; it built this one; I watched it laid, castle by invisible castle, a hundred years of children deep. "Then log this month..." said Oliver, settling. "The town grew again. Twice its mapped size. Right on schedule." Logged. Sealed. Below, in a dream, a girl and a bear walked a meadow off all maps. The town, doubled, slept in both its worlds. Goodnight, makers. Goodnight, Willowbrook... both of you.
