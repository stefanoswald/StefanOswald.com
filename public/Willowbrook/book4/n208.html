Night 208

The Maybe Theo Taught

Month 7: The Maybe Lessons

Ice cream day. The corner shop. Two cones. And, the physics of two-year-olds and gravity... Theo's fell. Scoop-first. Sidewalk. Gone. The wail: loading... loading... and then... watch him... the wail... held. One breath.

Two. (The jar-watching. The months of slips. The sister's reflex, overheard a hundred times. Something in the small machinery, turning over) "...maybe?" His maybe. Solo. Aimed at his own splattered scoop. And his sister... fast, proud, ready... made it true: "Yes maybe!

Here... we share mine. One lick you, one lick me. Shared cones taste better anyway, everybody knows, it's law..." One cone. Two lickers. Alternating. (It lasted twice as long that way, somehow. Shared- cone math.

Real phenomenon.) The slip, spoken by him, his second ever, written down in Mama's hand with the date circled: "My ice cream falled. I said maybe. Den sharing. Sharing is more licks somehow. Caught. Theo, age 2, law holder."

(Law holder. Age two. The lens: hereditary, confirmed. This month's whole mission, complete one generation early. They remembered it and takes, so to speak, the rest of the afternoon off.)
