Night 201

The Yellow Boots

Month 7: The Maybe Lessons

In the middle of puddle season, Olivia's red boots began to pinch her toes.

She wore them for one last walk and came home with sore feet.

"They still look like mine," she complained.

"They are still yours," Papa said. "Your feet are simply growing beyond them."

Theo tried the red boots. They were too large for him, but he walked three proud steps before sitting down with a thump.

At the shop, Olivia chose yellow boots that reminded her of the first pair she could remember. They felt roomy and strange.

On the next rainy day, she and Theo went outside together. He wore the red boots with thick socks. She wore the yellow ones.

They jumped into the same puddle from opposite sides.

The splash reached both coats.

"Your old boots still know how," Theo said.

Olivia looked at the red pair, then at the yellow pair beneath her own feet.

Nothing had been lost. One child had grown, another had caught up, and the puddles were ready for both of them.
