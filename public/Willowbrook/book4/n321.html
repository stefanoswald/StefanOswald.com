Night 321

The Soup Day

Month 11: Winter Wonders

Deep-winter Sunday. Grey. Cold. The lane: hunkered. And in this family, that weather has a name: Soup day. (The big pot. The all-afternoon kind. The house filling slow with the smell that means winter is losing.)

The brigade: Mama: knife work, stove command. Olivia: the small-chop. (Carrots, celery... the butter-knife cuts, ragged and devoted. "Soup doesn't care if da pieces are pretty. Soup cares dat you came.") Theo: bean sorter.

(A bowl, two kinds, one job. Sorted mostly. Sung to throughout... his own composition, "Da Bean Song..." Lyrics: "bean." The classics.) The pot: built. Onions first... "da smell dat tells da house something good started," then the roots, the broth, the beans, the long afternoon simmer, tasted at intervals by the whole brigade with enormous seriousness.

And at dusk... the family custom, the part that makes it soup day and not just soup: The jars. Four of them. Filled warm. Lidded. And delivered... the whole family, hats on, down the blue-cold lane: One: Mrs.

Alvarez. One: Petrov's shop. One: the new family, Number Nine. One: Grandpa Joe's truck seat, for the drive home Sunday. "Why do we always make too much?" she asked, walking home, empty-handed, full. "We don't..." said Mama. "We make exactly enough. The pot knows the size of the lane." The pot knows the size of the lane. Soup Day. Winter's answer. The house smelled like victory till Tuesday.
