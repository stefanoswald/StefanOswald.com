Night 96

The Under-Bed Question

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

It arrived on schedule. The classic: "Papa... is dere something under da bed?" (Every bed. Every child. Every century. The under-bed is the imagination's oldest rental property.) The rule, run full: "Let's go look.

Together. You hold the flashlight." (She holds the light. Critical part. The inspector, armed, outranks the fear.) The expedition, done on bellies: The beam swept the territory. Findings: One sock. (The lost mate! A reunion!

The fear-check turned rescue mission!) Dust, in quantity. ("Da under-bed is fluffy.") One book. And in the far corner: a button. Total monsters: zero. Total treasure: four items. And then the annexation... the move that retired the fear for good: "If it's my under-bed... can I put things dere?"

She could. The under-bed became a kingdom: the treasure tin moved in. The spare flashlight, stationed. A drawing, taped to the slats above ("so da under-bed has art"). Territory, annexed. Fears can't rent rooms in places you've furnished.

The under-bed, from that night: not the dark's property. Hers. Inspected, treasured, decorated, and, the last laugh... checked nightly thereafter not for monsters but on her stuff. The flashlight stayed stationed. Every kingdom needs a lamp.
