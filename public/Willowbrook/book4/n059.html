Night 59

The Willow's Other Side

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

At month's end, the Great Willow caught Stella's warm light. And tonight, the watch addressed the tree itself, because the children below had figured something out, and the watch owed the story honesty: "They know..." said Stella.

"About the other side. The bear told her: same tree, other side. The little one's cow confirmed it. They've mapped it. It's on their kitchen wall. Whoo..." said Oliver... not a question. An exhale. Some secrets, even owls are relieved to set down.

"Mmm," hummed the Moon... who travels both skies nightly, who has always countersigned the paperwork of both sides, who knew before knowing was. One tree. Roots in the real. Branches in both. "Log this month..." said Oliver Owl at last. "Log it: the town found its other half. Or remembered it. Towns like this one always had two sides. Takes a three-year-old and a bear to get the door open again."

Logged. Both-side gold. Goodnight, Willowbrook. Goodnight, other- Willowbrook. Same tree. Same watch. Same love, all the way through.
