Night 110

The Solo Sleepover, Packed

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

The promise, three years old now: "Anytime..." said Grandpa Joe, holding a four-month-old, handing back a sleeping parcel. "Anytime." And the three-year-old turn of "anytime" came due this week: The solo sleepover.

The farm. Overnight. Alone-alone. No parents. First ever. The packing, done by its traveler: The bag: her own. (Chosen. Packed. Inspected by Mama after, quietly... veteran craft... and found... complete. Genuinely. Pajamas, toothbrush, the star sweater, spare everything.

Three years of watching the packing pays out.) The night kit, by this month's whole lesson: The bear. The nightlight... the gold one, unplugged and packed. ("Da farm has dark I don't know yet. I'm bringing my own gold.")

The brave list... Copied, by Mama's hand, folded in the bag's pocket. And one photo: the family of four, frame thirty-one, mid-laugh. The nerves, named at bedtime, night- before: "I'm excited-scared." The old pair.

The first-day feeling. Both flags flying. "Perfect weather for an adventure..." ruled Mama. "That's the exact right feeling for the size of the thing." The exact right feeling for the size of the thing.

Bag: by the door. Bear: on top. Morning: coming. The farm: waiting, per three years of anytime... one lamp already planned for the guest room sill, one old man whistling in a workshop, building nothing, just whistling, the way he does when a big small thing is coming.
