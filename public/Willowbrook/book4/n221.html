Night 221

The Extra Job Exchange

Month 8: Helping Hands

The chart: family, unpaid, by the summit. But this month: the first extra. The garage's shame: The bolt bucket. (Years of orphan hardware. Bolts, screws, washers, mystery pieces. A bucket of entropy. Papa's guilt in metal form.)

The contract, negotiated at the workbench: The job: sort it. By kind. Into the compartment box. The wage: one coin per compartment filled. The standard: "sorted means sorted. Washers with washers. No cheating da little weird ones into 'misc', misc is where sorting goes to die."

(Papa's standards. The apprentice absorbed them along with the wage. That's the hidden salary of extra jobs: the standards come free.) The execution: three afternoons. The tongue out. The tool-belt pencil, checking off kinds.

The little weird ones: Researched. ("Papa. Dis one's not misc. It's a wing nut. Da chart in your book says. Wing nuts get deir own spot.") (She read the hardware chart. She gave wing nuts their dignity.

The day-old shelf law, applied to bolts. Rosa would weep.) The payout: eleven coins. Counted twice. Kept in a jar labeled... what else... "My earned." (The maybe jar's cousin. The shelf of jars grows.

This family jars everything: kindness, maybes, earnings. The economies, visible, side by side.) "What will you buy? Nothing yet. I'm watching it." Watching it. (Savings, at four. As it happened the far books: the jar became a box became an account.

The first eleven coins never got spent. They're in the far books, taped to a page, labeled by an adult hand: 'the wing nut money. Where it all started.')
