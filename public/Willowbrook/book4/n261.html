Night 261

Theo's First List Entry

Month 9: Brave Is Scared Plus One Step

An new rule this month, proposed at the reading: "Da list should have fee-oh's braves too. He's got some now. Everybody's braves should get papers." The second-generation section. Founded. His first entries, spoken (his words, her transcription- supervision, Mama's hand, his scribble- signature): "Da vacuum.

It's Crumby now. He eats crumbs only. I pet him. Da big slide. Little one first, den da big one, with Wivy at da bottom." (The one-step rule, apprentice edition. The catcher: standing by, by the permanent arrangement.)

"Da storm. I counted. Fee away." Three entries. One scribble- signature each. The document: a family register now, two generations of scared-plus, accumulating. "When he's big..." She explained. "he'll read his own braves back.

Like me. Da list will tell him who he is when he forgets." The list will tell him who he is when he forgets. (The whole function of every list, every almanac, every book on this shelf, stated plain by its co-author: the records are for the forgetting days. Write everything down. The forgetting days come for everyone. The records hold.)
