Night 2

The Floor Is Water

Month 1: Pretend!

Announcement at ten a.m: "The floor is water." Just like that, the living room drowned. The rug: ocean. The cushions: islands. The couch: the big safe boat. And the crossing, island to island, leap by careful leap, became the morning's whole heroic work.

"Don't touch da water! Dere's sharks!" (There were sharks. Invisible ones. The most dangerous kind.) Theo, almost two, following her everywhere, the way little brothers do, watched from the boat, then attempted the leap, missed the island entirely, and stood in the middle of the shark-filled sea, delighted.

"Fee-oh. The sharks." He looked down. Considered the invisible ocean he stood in. And said his newest word: "Swim!"... Swimming. He was swimming. Not drowning... Swimming. The game amended itself on the spot: Theo was a fish.

Fish can't drown. Fish are fine. "You're a fish now. Dat's your job. Fish!" agreed the fish. The ocean lasted till lunch, which was served on the big safe boat. Pretend rules are soft rules.

They bend for little brothers. The best games always have a fish position available. Hers always, always did.
