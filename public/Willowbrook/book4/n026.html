Night 26

The Day Everything Was Something Else

Month 1: Pretend!

This month's crescendo: a day, one whole ordinary day, where nothing stayed only itself. The log: Breakfast: "a royal feast." The hallway: a canyon. (Crossed on the rope bridge. Single file. Don't look down.) The stairs: a mountain.

(Climbed with the expedition grunt. Summited daily anyway; today it counted.) The bath: the sea. (Standard. But today with a shipwreck plot and a rescue.) The nap: "hibernating." (Bears do it. She's bear-adjacent by association.

The hibernation was convincing and ninety minutes long... the pretend's finest practical achievement to date, per Mama.) Dinner: the restaurant. (Mud-free edition. Papa was the customer. The tip was generous.) And bedtime, the day's last conversion: "Da bed is a boat.

Da blanket is da sail. We sail to da morning." The whole real day, lived double. Nothing lost, she ate, napped, bathed, learned, loved, all of it, full measure. Everything gained: the same day, twice as wide.

That's the year's whole gift, delivered in one Tuesday: the world, and the world she makes, running side by side... and her, walking both at once, sovereign of the seam. Sail to the morning, little maker. Both worlds will be there when you wake.
