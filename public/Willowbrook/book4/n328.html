Night 328

Mud Season Opens

Month 11: Winter Wonders

Between winter and spring, the calendar hides a secret season... and this week it opened: Mud. (The thaw finished what it started. The yard: bootlace- deep. The lane edges: chocolate. The world: one big squelch, after four months of crunch.)

The christening: immediate. (Boots: on. Permission: negotiated... "outside clothes, back porch hose-off, no arguments"... terms: accepted before Mama finished them.) The squelch report: "It goes shloop when you step and shlop when you pull and if you stand still it tries to keep you... da mud likes company, it's been alone under da snow all winter..."

(Theo: knee- down in it within the minute. Then palm-down. Then, the story averts its eyes... significantly more down. His answer, muddy to the dimple: "Good.") The engineering hour: a mud kitchen, a boot-suction experiment, and one channel dug lane-to- puddle, watched run like a public works triumph.

And at the hose-off, dripping, brown, entirely satisfied: "Winter's clean. Spring's pretty. But mud is da honest one in between. It's da ground saying everything it couldn't say frozen. Da whole yard's talking again."

The whole yard is talking again. (Mud season: short, loud, gloriously unphotogenic... the year's thaw made touchable. This family never once missed it. Boots by the door. Hose on the porch. Come in honest.)
