Night 166

The Sick Day

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

The plant-house economy, preschool edition: a cold. A couch day. A school sick day... her first. And the discovery, made from the blanket pile: she missed it. "Is it circle time now?... Now dey're at da easel.

Marcus gets line leader today and I'm not dere to see da chart move..." (The chart moves without her. The first hard lesson of belonging to things: they run in your absence. The classroom, beloved, does not pause.

She grieved it properly. A whole morning's worth.) And then, at pickup time... the knock. Lily. Cubby neighbor. Courier. Bearing: the day. "I brought you the whole day. Look: my painting... you can borrow it.

And a get-well card from everybody... Miss Dana made da circle sign it. Wren drew you a puzzle piece. And... I saved you half my crackers from snack. Da good kind day." The day, delivered.

The classroom, paused after all... in the only way places pause: somebody carries them to you. "Da school missed me back," she said, at the card, at all twelve scribbled names. It did. Belonging runs both directions.

That's how you know it's real. Back by Thursday. The chart had moved. Her clothespin had waited. Some things pause after all.
