Night 282

The Sign Reader

Month 10: Words Are Magic

The O hunt, one month later, has become something else entirely: The town reads itself to her now. The walk to the market, narrated: "S-t-o-p. Stop! Da red sign says da thing it means!

Dat's good design, Mama. B-a-k... bakery! I knew it was da bakery but now it says so. Everything says so now. M-a-p-l-e. Maple. Mama. The lane's name is written on the pole.

Da street knows its own name!... We live on a word." We live on a word. (The town, turning legible, one sign at a time. What was scenery is becoming sentence. The lights coming on street by street, exactly as forecast, the bakery-sign morning, one month and one lifetime ago.)

And trailing behind, the apprentice, pointing at everything: "Dat say? Dat says park. Dat say? Dat says open. Dat say? Dat's just a scratch, Fee-oh. Not everything says... But most things say." Most things say.
