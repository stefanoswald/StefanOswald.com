Night 91

The Dark Gets a Name

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

The fears found her this month. (They find everyone at three-and-some. The imagination that builds castles builds shadows too. Same engine. Both directions.) So this month opened with a renaming. At the dusk window, watching the light fold: "I want to tell you the dark's real name.

Ready? The dark is just the day, resting... Resting? Look at your room when the lamp goes out. Nothing leaves. The chair is the chair, resting. The dresser: resting. Your toys: the whole shelf of them, resting.

The dark isn't a thing that comes. It's just the light lying down. And everything the light loved all day... it's all still there, resting beside you." The lamp clicked out, as a test. The room went dark... and got narrated: "Da chair... resting.

Da shelf... resting. Baba...working. He works nights." He works nights. The dark, renamed, lost a little of its teeth that night. Not all. (Months of this ahead. The book knows. This month is budgeted.)

But the thesis was planted where all this month's work would grow: nothing leaves when the light does. The day is resting. And somebody soft is on shift.
