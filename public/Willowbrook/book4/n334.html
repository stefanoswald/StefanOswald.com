Night 334

Stella and the Green Room

Month 11: Winter Wonders

At the month's quiet finish, Stella watched from over the Great Willow. Winter, closing its book. And tonight the watch had a guest of honor: Bella. (New-winged. Rainbow at night, the firefly glow catching her wings like stained glass.

First flight up the hill since the emergence. The whole watch turned to look. Even the Moon opened one eye fully.) "Report..." said Oliver Owl, gently, knowing, as the whole hill knew, what the report had been.

"I went into the green room..." said Bella. "I melted almost all the way. I kept the deepest parts. I built the rest new. It took the whole winter and it was dark and slow and I was never once alone, there was a voice, most days, through the wall.

Small. Faithful. It told me the snow was falling and the birds made it through and my word was being kept warm. I came out bigger. That's the whole report. That's the only report there ever is, from a green room.

Log it..." said Stella, gold and quiet. "Log it next to the brook that ran under its lid, and the seeds in their envelope, and the small one below, who learned to read in the dark months and taught the watch its own oldest law: winter doesn't stop the good things.

It makes them quiet for a while," hummed the Moon, who has watched ten thousand winters do exactly that. Goodnight, green rooms of Willowbrook... seen and unseen, silk and snow and small brave hearts. Do your slow work. Spring keeps every appointment. The watch can confirm. The watch has minutes.
