Night 315

The Cocoa Meeting

Month 11: Winter Wonders

Snow day, this week. School: closed. The lane: white. And the summit, gathered at Lily's, window seat, the good blanket, two cocoas: The year review. Unscheduled. Unprompted. Two four-year-olds with cocoa watching snow fall on their two streets just... started doing what old friends do.

Da whistle code fixed it. And da mail does da rest now. Remember da sleepover dat failed? Baby us. Giggling till ten-firty. And da rematch. We're sleepover people now. Baby us would be so proud.

Remember when I got two streets and you got a brother and we both fought da uddah one got da harder thing? We were both right." (Cocoa. Snow. The particular silence of best friends who don't need the next sentence yet.)

"Six years..." said Lily finally. (Their whole lives. Eleven days apart. Cribs in the same music class. The math is theirs and they guard it.). "Six years..." Confirmed the delegation. "And we're only four- almost-five.

Dat means da friendship is older dan us. It started before we could even remember it." The friendship is older than us. (Older than memory. The best ones are. The summit done for the day at dusk, no resolutions, no treaties; the old alliances don't need new paper.

Just cocoa. Snow. Two streets. And the whole rest of the far pages, which sure enough: they walk all of them. Together. Both directions.)"
