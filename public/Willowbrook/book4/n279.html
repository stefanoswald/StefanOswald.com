Night 279

The Library Card

Month 10: Words Are Magic

The librarian had been waiting. "You can write your name now, I hear. My whole me. Six letters. Then it's time." The card. (Her own. The form: filled, gravely. The signature line: O-l-i-v-i-a, tongue out, best work to date, you sign harder when it's a document.

The lamination: watched like a moon launch. Warm from the machine. Held like treasure... because it is one: the first id of her life, and the only one that matters yet.) The rules, conferred like knighthood: "This card means the library trusts you.

Books go out; books come home; the lantern stays lit either way. Four books at a time until you're eight." Four at a time. The first borrowing: done solo. The checkout: her card. Her hand.

The stamp... Thunk, four times. And in the wainscoting, the entry: "Card issued. Age 4. Signed 'harder because it's a document.' The lantern's long game proceeds exactly on schedule." Walked home hugging four books. Owner of nothing in them.

Keeper of all of it. That's the card's whole magic. That's the library's whole deal.
