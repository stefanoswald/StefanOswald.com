Night 129

The Nap Gap

Month 5: Little Brother Tags Along

A discovery this month, hiding in the schedule: The nap gap. Theo still naps. She doesn't. (Mostly. "Rest time" holds, but sleep comes rarely now.) Which means: ninety minutes, daily, where the house is hers.

Just hers. The only-child hours, discovered inside the sibling life. And the gap became an institution: Some days: the big puzzles. Some days: the fragile pretends. (The tea sets. The tall towers. The games too breakable for a two.)

Some days: just Mama. The two of them, at the table, drawing or dough-ing or nothing... the just-us, daily edition, ninety minutes at a time. And some days... the wisest days... nothing at all.

The quiet ten minutes, stretched to sixty. Her own company, kept. "Da nap gap is my time," she ruled. It is. (And the page's note for every multi-kid house: guard the gaps. The big ones need the only-hours... not as escape from the little ones, but as return to themselves.

The best big siblings are the ones who still get to be just-themselves, somewhere, daily.) He wakes at three, calling her name. She's always glad to hear it. Gladder for the gap. Both. As ever. Both.
