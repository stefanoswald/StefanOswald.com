Night 50

Franklin Gets Dreamed In

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

The promise, kept: "I'll dream you both in. Dere's room." This week: Franklin's visit. The report, from the morning after: "He came. To da meadow. I dreamed him in and he came. What does Franklin look like?" asked Mama, the question three books of tea parties never answered.

And the answer, from the far side: "... You still can't see him. Even dere. But you can feel where he is. Da grass bends a little. Da tea rock had a warm spot. Baba talked to him da whole time... dey knew each udder, Mama.

From somewhere. Baba wouldn't say where." (Bears and Franklins, acquainted from somewhere. They remembered it in the drawer of things the soft division keeps to itself. The drawer is deep. The drawer is probably the whole point.)

The visit's program: tea at the tea rock (The flavor is warm). Stone skipping. (Franklin skipped one thirteen times. Nobody saw the stone leave. The splashes were counted. Thirteen.) And at the leaving: "He said thank you.

He said it was da best invitation in a thousand years." The best invitation in a thousand years. There's room. There was always room. Even for the ones nobody can see... Especially for them. The meadow's count grows. The heart's floor plan holds.
