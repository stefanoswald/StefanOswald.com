Night 5

The Puddle Ship

Month 1: Pretend!

Rain last night. The pothole puddle: returned. Faithful. Last year, the puddle was for stomping. (It got stomped. History records the splashing.) This year... "Shhh. Dat's not a puddle. Dat's da sea." And onto the sea went a leaf.

One oak leaf, curled at the edges. A ship. "Dere's a storm coming..." The captain announced, crouched at the sea's edge in her boots. "Da ship has to get home. Da sailors are scared. But da captain says hold on..."

(A stick stirred the sea. Waves! Enormous ones! An inch high!) "...and da ship goes up da wave, and down da wave..." The leaf survived. Barely. Gloriously. It docked at last on the far shore, sailors saved, captain triumphant.

Mama watched the whole voyage from two feet away, where the grown-ups stand, at the edge of seas they can't quite sail anymore, watching somebody who still holds the passport. "Da sailors made it," Olivia reported. The sailors made it.

They always will... every leaf, every puddle, every storm she narrates for the rest of this gorgeous year. The sea dried by Thursday. The captain kept the leaf.
