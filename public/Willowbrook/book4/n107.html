Night 107

The Bad Dream and the Key

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

A real one this week. A nightmare. (The imagination that builds meadows builds these too. Same engine. This month has said it. The night proved it.) The wake: crying, tangled, 2 a.m. "Da dream was bad.

Dere was a... a something. Chasing. And you weren't dere and I couldn't find..." The kit, deployed in order: The holding. First. Always first. Words wait. The lamp. (Low. The room, verified: chair resting, shelf resting, bear on shift..."

He was catching, Mama, I found him first thing... "correct; positioned; the log holds.) The telling: the whole dream, out loud. And then the tool this family forged a year ago, at a hallway, for a wild bear: The key.

"You're the author, remember. Even of the bad ones. Want to rewrite it?" The rewrite, spoken sleepily: "Da something chases... and den it trips. And it's actually... little. It was little da whole time, with a big shadow.

And it's lost, dat's why it was chasing... it wanted help. And I take it... to da meadow. And Baba checks it in. And it gets a little bed." (The monster: re-cast as lost.

Given a bed. In the meadow. It cannot be improved on.) Back to sleep by 2:30. The rewrite, apparently, took: morning report... "da little something is staying. Baba's teaching it manners." The meadow count: plus one.

The author's key: proven at 2 a.m. Even the bad dreams, hers to hold the pen on. Especially those.
