Night 217

Helping That Isn't Wanted

Month 8: Helping Hands

A hard helping lesson this week: Theo's puzzle. (His. The wooden three-piecer. Her old one. He was struggling, the circle piece, wrong hole, again, again) And she helped. Swooped. Popped it in. Done! And Theo...

Burst into tears. "I do it! Mine! You do-ed my puzzle!" (... The visitor's mistake. The pop-it-in. The one done to her, years back, at the star piece, the one that un-solved the puzzle on principle.

Done, now, by her. The lesson, coming around, wearing her own hands.) The unpacking, at the counter, tearful on both sides: "I was helping... You were finishing. Those are different. Remember your star piece? The visitor?...

Da working part is da game. I stole his working part." She stole his working part. The repair: the puzzle, un-done. The apology, the flower, and the new offer, the right-sized one: "Fee-oh.

You do it. I'll just... be here. If you want a hint, ask 'hint.' Dat's all I got unless you ask." (The hint rule. Self-invented. Correct. The helping that waits to be wanted, the whole month's hardest lesson, learned by stealing one working-part and giving it back.)

He did the puzzle. Alone. Four minutes. "I do-ed it!" He did it. And his sister... hands sat on, hint unrequested, glowing anyway... learned the deepest helping rule of all: sometimes the help is the not-helping. The hardest hands to give are the ones you hold back.
