Night 297

The Menu Reader

Month 10: Words Are Magic

The diner. Saturday. The family four. And the laminated kids' menu, which for four years has been a picture document... read tonight, top to bottom, out loud, finger under words, while the table waited and the waitress, bless her, leaned on the counter and let it happen: "Grilled...

Cheez. Grilled cheese! Chik-en fin... Fingers. Chicken fingers! (Dey're not real fingers, Fee-oh, don't worry.) Mac... And... Cheez. Pan-cakes. You can get breakfast at night here, it says so, this place is lawless."

And then, the crown of it... the order, placed directly, first time in her life, no parental relay: "I would like da grilled cheese please. It's da first thing I ever read on a menu so it's da one I'm having. Dat's fair to da menu."

(Fair to the menu. The waitress wrote it down like a normal order, which it was, and also wasn't, and everyone at the counter knew it.) Some sandwiches are rated by the doorway they came through.

First menu. First self-placed order. The town keeps turning legible, one laminated page at a time... and every reading buys her another small piece of the world, handled directly, no relay, her own voice to the waitress and the waitress writing it down. That's not dinner. That's citizenship. Onward.
