Night 92

The Shadow That Moved

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

Night two of this month: a shadow moved. On the wall. By the dresser. It slid... she saw it, then stopped. "Papa. The wall. Something moved." And Papa came... not with "it's nothing" (nothing doesn't slide)... but with the lamp.

The old rule. The inspection. "Let's catch it moving. Watch the wall with me." They watched. Two minutes. Three... It moved. Slid and stopped. "There! See! I see it. Now watch the window when it happens."...

Headlights. A car, turning at the corner. Its light swept the curtain... and the curtain's shadow slid across the wall and stopped when the car did. "... Da curtain?" The curtain. Plus cars. Working together. The test: Papa wiggled the curtain.

The shadow wiggled. She wiggled it. Wiggled. She made it slide, stop, slide, the whole scary show, run by her own hand. "I'm da spooky!" She was the spooky. The shadow: demoted from mystery to toy.

(She played wall-shadows a week after. The fear became a puppet theater. They often do, once caught.) The lamp went back. The rule logged its win. Every investigated shadow, all month, every year: a curtain, a branch, a coat. Every single one. The lamp is undefeated. Bring the lamp.
