Night 157

The Line Leader Chart

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

The classroom runs on a sacred document: The job chart. (Clothespins. Rotating. Every kid, every job, in turn: line leader, light helper, hamster monitor, snack passer, calendar kid.) And the chart is studied, daily, by every four- year-old in the room, like tide tables: "Two more days till my line leader.

Lily had it today. Marcus gets tomorrow. Den me." (The math of turns, scaled to a classroom. The whole month's lesson... turns and shares... running on one laminated chart and twelve clothespins.) Her day, when it came: Line leader.

The full glory: first in line. The door held The walking-feet pace set for eleven followers. The heady, brief, total power of it. And at pickup: "I led the line. I did walking feet.

Nobody ran. Marcus tried and I said 'walking feet' like Miss Dana and he did it." (A born line leader. The bench mothers, hearing the retelling, exchanged looks. The far books exchange them too.) And tomorrow?

"Tomorrow I'm nothing. Marcus leads. I'm just in da line." And that's okay? "Dat's da chart, Mama. Everybody gets da turn. Da chart is fair. Fair means waiting some days and shining udders." Fair means waiting some days and shining others.

The chart teaches it in clothespins. It takes some people a lifetime. She got it in a week, in line, walking feet, both directions.
