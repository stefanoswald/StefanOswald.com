Night 195

The Power Outage Maybe

Month 7: The Maybe Lessons

Mid-dinner: Click. Dark. The whole lane. (A transformer, somewhere. Hours, said the phone.) Two kids, one dark kitchen... "... Maybe!" in chorus now; the reflex is communal... And the outage delivered: Candle dinner. ("Fancy," by the burnt-pasta precedent.

Fancier tonight: the whole meal gold-lit, faces flickering, voices gone soft to match.) Shadow puppets. (The flashlight... under-bed kingdom's, ordered... and the wall: ducks, obviously. A dog. A "volcano"... two hands erupting. Papa's rabbit: legendary.

Taught.) And the closet guitar. (Papa's. Dusty. Pre-kids. Tuned badly by candlelight and played, badly, wonderfully, for an hour: the wrong- note song, the hello song, one old one that made Mama put her head on his shoulder.)

The lights came back at nine. Everyone was almost sorry. The slip: "Da power went out. Seemed bad. Got candle dinner, shadow ducks, and Papa plays guitar which nobody told me. Da dark had a guitar in it.

Caught." (The guitar stayed out. Friday songs: founded. The outage, thanked in absentia. Some maybes rewire the whole house... including, this once, literally not.)
