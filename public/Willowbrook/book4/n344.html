Night 344

The Spring Peepers Night

Month 12: Almost Four

A warm night this week... the first one that stayed warm after dark. And on it, from every wet place in town, the year's loudest small sound: The peepers. (Spring peepers. Frogs the size of a thumbnail with voices the size of the night.

The whole brook-edge: ringing.) The expedition: launched. (The collector, the notebook, Papa, flashlights, boots. The listening walk's night division.) The approach: deafening. ("How can something so loud be so invisible?") The stalk: attempted. (The peepers' oldest trick: silence at ten feet.

The chorus parts around you like water and closes behind. "Dey can hear us listening!") And then... Papa's woodcraft: stand still. Two minutes. Lights off. Be a tree... And the chorus closed back over them, both inside it now, the dark ringing floor to stars...

"Papa..." At a whisper. "We're in da sound. It's all da way around. Da spring is... Loud. Winter was da quiet months and spring is da loud one and we're standing in da exact door between dem."

The exact door between them. The notebook, by flashlight, mitten season over: "Peepers. Da springs doorbell. Tiny frogs. Huge news. Dey ring till summer answers." They ring till summer answers. And walking home, the chorus fading streetlamp by streetlamp behind them: "Da year's turning aden.

I can hear it turn now. I used to just see it... da leaves, da snow. But da year makes sounds when it turns. You just have to be out in da dark with somebody who stops walking."

Out in the dark with somebody who stops walking. (they remembered it under: fathers, best practices. And birthdays, the week before: the peepers rang her into four. The door between seasons stood open all week. She went through it listening.)
