Night 61

Why Is the Sky Blue

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

The park. Backs in the grass. The subject directly overhead: "Papa. Why is da sky blue?" (The classic. Every parent's exam. He'd been preparing for three years.) The answer, true and small: "Sunlight looks white, but it's really every color mixed.

When it hits the air, the air scatters it, bounces it everywhere. And blue bounces most. So everywhere you look... bounced blue." Silence. Processing. "... Da sky is bounced?" The sky is bounced. "Off da air?"

Off the air. "Den why is da sunset orange?" (A follow-up. A real one. The light going sideways at dusk, traveling farther, the blue bounced away before it arrives, leaving the long slow colors) "Da orange is da leftover light."

The leftover light. "... I like da leftovers best," she decided. Same, said the whole town, which gathers on porches nightly for exactly those leftovers. Physics: survivable. Wonder: fully intact. That's the why-answering craft: true enough to build on, small enough to hold, and always leaving the wonder where you found it.
