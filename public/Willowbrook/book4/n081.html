Night 81

Why Do Dogs Wag

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

The fence. The old friend. The daily slow visit. "Why do dogs wag? Dogs can't talk... so their feelings run out the other end. The tail is the heart's flag. Watch his: Slow, wide wag... that's 'content.

Old-friend happy.' Fast wag, whole body in it, that's 'Joy. You came!' Still tail, ears up... 'thinking. Checking.' Tucked, 'worried.' He's got a whole language back there." She watched the current flag: slow, wide, steady as his breathing.

Old-friend happy. "What's my tail?"... Your tail? "My heart-flag! Where do my feelings run out? Your face, mostly. Your bounce. Your voice. Humans leak everywhere." She tested it: made her joy face, bounced her joy bounce... and the dog, reading her flags right back, stood up and wagged the fast one, whole body in it.

You came. Two languages, one fence, total fluency both directions... the way it's been since a stroller paused here years ago and a tail said welcome, small one, and meant it, and never once stopped.
