Night 266

The Wrong Turn Walk

Month 9: Brave Is Scared Plus One Step

A strange drill this week. Papa's invention: The wrong turn walk. "We're walking to the library. But at Cedar Street, we're turning the wrong way. On purpose. And then you're getting us back... Why would we get lost on purpose?

Because lost is a skill. The market taught us the staying-still kind. This is the other kind: the finding- your-way kind. Better to learn it holding my hand than not." The wrong turn: taken.

Three blocks of unfamiliar. (New fences. A dog she'd never met. The particular wobble of streets that don't know you.) And then: the work. "Okay. Da library lantern. I can see it over dere. So da library is dat way.

And our house is past da library from here, so... dis street should bend left... " (Landmarks. Logic. The lantern... visible from almost anywhere in town, which is, note, the entire reason lanterns exist.) Four turns, one consultation ("Does da brook sound closer or farder?

Closer. Den we're too far east")... and: Maple Lane. Home. Navigated. By the four-year-old. From lost. "Da town has handles..." She concluded. "Da lantern. Da brook sound. Da willow on its hill. If you know da handles, you can't be all- da-way lost.

Just... temporarily misplaced." Temporarily misplaced. The confidence of a person with handles. (It never left her. Any town, any trail, any far-page city: first hour, she finds the handles. Old habit. Cedar Street habit. The best drills are the ones that feel like adventures.)
