Night 295

The Book Hospital

Month 10: Words Are Magic

An emergency this week: The bear book. The spine, two years, two thousand readings, one recent era of being read by its owner nightly... Let go. Page seven: loose. The cover: hinged on threads. The grief: real.

("It's not just a book. It's da book. It taught me da shape of stories. It was dere before I could even understand it.") And the response... because this town has setup for exactly this: Mr.

Petrov's. (The mending shop. Fixer of the wheelbarrow, the umbrella, the town's whole category of too-loved-to- replace.) The consultation, done across the counter with full surgical gravity: "Spine's gone soft. Page seven's loose at the root.

This book has been read, young lady. Two thousand times. Probably more. Best thing a book can have wrong with it. Cloth tape. New hinge. She'll take another two thousand." The operation: watched. Assisted. The tape: held.

And Petrov's benediction, at the moment, waving off half his fee per his custom with the small: "Books that fall apart from love get mended at cost. Bring me the next one you wear out. I expect it'll be soon."

The bear book: home. Spine firm. Page seven: rooted. Read that night... carefully, ceremonially, by its owner, to its bear... and retired to the shelf's place of honor soon after, when the early readers took the nightly shift.

Not gone. Retired. (It's in the far pages still... the shelf of every home she ever kept. Cloth-taped. Twice more, over the years, by her own hands. Petrov taught her the hinge trick before he retired. Some books you keep until the keeping is the point.)
