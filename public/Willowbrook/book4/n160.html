Night 160

The Kid Who Plays Alone

Month 6: Preschool, Turns, and Shares

There's a kid at school who plays alone. Marcus is loud. Lily is hers. The twins are a weather system. And wren... small, quiet, corner-dwelling Wren, does puzzles. Alone. Every free-play. Every day.

"Is he sad?" she asked Mama. "Maybe. Or maybe alone is how he's comfy. Some people fill up in the quiet. Remember the together-quiet? Some kids live there... How do I find out which?

The slow way. The shy way. No swooping." (The stranger-file rules, inverted: she's been the harbor-hider. Now she's the approacher. The craft transfers.) The campaign, run at Wren-pace, over two weeks: Day one: puzzled nearby.

Different table. No words. Day three: same table. Far end. Parallel. Day six: "Dat piece is sky. I saw one like it by you." (One sentence. Placed like a dandelion at a fence. Left.) Day nine: Wren pushed a piece toward her.

Wordless. It fit. Day twelve: two puzzlers, one table, mostly silent, entirely together... the together- quiet, found by its second- ever member in this book's records. "Wren's not sad..." She reported. "Wren's quiet. Dere's a difference.

He does puzzles with me now. We don't talk lots. Dat's how he likes company. Everybody gets liked how dey like." Everybody gets liked how they like. (Wren's in the far books too. Of course he is.

The quiet ones found early stay found. He speaks maybe forty sentences across three volumes. Every one of them lands.)
