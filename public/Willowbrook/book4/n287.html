Night 287

The Question Marks

Month 10: Words Are Magic

A new discovery in book three of the early readers: "Papa. What's dis mark? Da curly hook one with da dot. That's a question mark. It turns a sentence into a question. 'Dog sits', that's telling.

'Dog sits?' that's asking. Hear it? The voice goes up at the end, like the mark is a little hook pulling the words up. The marks have voices. Do da uddah one! Da excited stick!

Exclamation point. It means say it big. 'Dog sits.' 'Dog sits!' Same words. Different size." The next hour: every sentence in the house, re-punctuated aloud. "Dinner's ready? Dinner's ready! Dinner's ready." (Papa's flattest deadpan.

Declared "da boring one... but probably da most honest.") And the insight, at the lamp, that made the story sit up: "So when I write, I can tell da reader how to say it. Even if I'm not dere.

Even if I'm... old. Or far. Da marks keep my voice in da words." The marks keep your voice in the words.
