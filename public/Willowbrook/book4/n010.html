Night 10

The Quiet Pretend

Month 1: Pretend!

Not every pretend is loud. Today's was the quietest one yet. In the garden, by her lettuce row, Olivia lay down in the grass. Curled small. Still. "... What are you, love? Shhh. I'm a seed."

A seed. And then, slowly, so slowly, the way only a three-year-old playing a seed can move... she grew. One arm: a root, creeping out. The other: a shoot, reaching up. Her whole body, unfurling in slow motion, knees to standing, arms rising, fingers spreading wide, face lifting to the sun... until she stood at her whole full height, swaying slightly, branches out.

"I'm a tree now. It took a hundred years." It took about four minutes. It was the best theater the garden ever hosted. (The real lettuce, two feet away, growing at actual seed-speed, was deeply upstaged and did not mind.)

"Trees can't walk..." She added, from inside the tree. "So I hafta stay here. Bring me water sometimes." Water was brought. The tree was watered. The tree giggled... which real trees almost never do... and stood there, swaying, being enormously old, until lunch called and a hundred years ended in a sprint for sandwiches. Some pretends roar. The deep ones grow.
