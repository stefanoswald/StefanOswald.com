Night 99

The Fear That Was Fun

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

A sorting this week. An important one. Because some scary, the family noticed, gets requested: "Chase me! Do da pounce peekaboo! Turn da fort lights off!" Why is some scared delicious? The sort, worked out at the fort with Papa: "Fun-scary has three ingredients: You chose it.

You can stop it. And somebody safe is right there. Chase: you started it. 'Stop' stops it. I'm the monster and your papa. That's not danger. That's danger's costume, with all the sharp parts removed.

Your body gets to practice big feelings in a safe suit. And real-scary? Didn't choose it. Can't stop it. That's why it needs the tools instead of giggles." The shelf, labeled: Fun-scary: chase, pounce-boo, dark forts, high swings, Papa's monster voice.

Real-scary: storms, new noises, vacuums, the under-bed. The wisdom, hers, at the sort's end: "Fun-scary is practice-scary." It is exactly that. Every shrieking chase: a rehearsal, in a safe suit, for a brave list entry years away. Scream on, little ones. The practice is the play. It always was.
