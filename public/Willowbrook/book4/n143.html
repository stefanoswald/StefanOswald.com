Night 143

The Room Question

Month 5: Little Brother Tags Along

A proposal this month, floated by its enthusiast: "Can me and Fee-oh share a room?" (Enthusiast: him, via her, via him... the provenance circular, the enthusiasm total on one side.) The summit, gathered. The cases: For: all-night company.

The fort but forever. Whispers after lights-out. "Da sleepover dat never stops." Against: "... But my shelf is in my room. Da high one. And my gold light is calibrated. And sometimes I need da nap-gap kind of alone but at night.

And he still kicks... da bear says." (The bear says. Expert telling. The kicking is documented.) The answer, self-rendered, gentle: "Not yet. But someday maybe. When he's free and doesn't kick. And we can do practice sleepovers.

Fridays. In my room. Sleeping bags." Not yet, but practice Fridays. (The compromise of the year. Adopted instantly. The Friday floor- sleepovers ran for years, sleeping bags, flashlights, whispers past lights-out, the sleepover that never stopped needing to be permanent because it was better as a treat.

The far books confirm: they never did share a room. They never needed to. Fridays were the room.) "Both things..." She explained at the lamp. "My own door and da whisper nights." Both things. The family word, solving the family floor plan. As ever. Both.
