Night 240

What Helping Isn't

Month 8: Helping Hands

A sorting, at the lamp, month's edge... because the helper's kit needs its edges, and better early: "What isn't helping, Mama? It feels like everything is." The edges, drawn: "Helping isn't fixing people. Sad friends don't need repairs... they need benches.

You know this one best of anybody. Helping isn't doing what's theirs. You learned that at Theo's puzzle. Stolen working-parts. The hint rule. Helping isn't for making people like you. Help that needs applause is a performance.

The secret column knows. And helping isn't emptying yourself till you crack. The receiving lesson. The jar pours both ways or the helper breaks. Helping is just: seeing what's needed, asking or offering, doing your share, and letting people stay the boss of their own working- parts."

She counted the edges on her fingers. "Not fixing. Not stealing. Not showing off. Not emptying." Four edges. One craft. "Dat's a lot of nots. Every good craft is mostly nots, love. The nots are what make the yes mean something."

The nots are what make the yes mean something. Saved. Deep. The helper's edges: drawn at four, held for life... which is the only reason the far books' Olivia, who helps everyone, never once burned out. The edges. Drawn here. At a lamp. In time.
