Night 39

The Bear at Preschool Rehearsal

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

The preschool game got a staff upgrade: Baba is the teacher's helper now. His duties, by the director: He sits at circle time where everyone can see him. ("Because new kids feel better when dere's a bear.

Dat's science.") He demonstrates criss-cross. He holds the hello song's rhythm. ("He nods it. Watch."... He does appear to nod it. The propping is skilled.) And, his real job, the one she assigned with full seriousness...

"He's for if somebody misses deir mama. You hold him and he's soft and you remember da mamas come back. He knows about waiting. He waited on a dresser free hundred and sixty-five nights."

He knows about waiting. His whole résumé, deployed. (And a note, saved by the watching mama: the real preschool allows one comfort item, first weeks. Guess who's enrolled. The bear's dream-walks may pause come fall... he'll be busy days, in a cubby, on call, doing the job he trained three years for: being soft in a new place while the rule proves itself.

The mamas come back. The bear stays till they do. Best teacher's helper in the county, pending.)
