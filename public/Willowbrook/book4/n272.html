Night 272

One Brave Step

Month 9: Brave Is Scared Plus One Step

At bedtime Mama asked Olivia to choose the bravest moment of the month.

"The deep end," Olivia said first. Then she changed her mind. "No, the solo at school. Or the storm. Or when I got lost near the market."

She had raised her hand when unsure, sung alone, slept away from home, played through a wrong note, crossed the dark basement, and apologized after hurting someone.

"Which one felt least brave?" Mama asked.

Olivia thought of the reader's chair at school. "Reading aloud. I was scared, but then I started and forgot."

"Does forgetting the fear make it less brave?"

"I don't think so. The brave part was the first sentence."

That seemed to be true of the other moments too. Courage was not a grand feeling that arrived first. It was the small movement made while the frightened feeling was still there.

Olivia tucked Bobby Bear beneath her chin.

"One step while scared," she said. "Then see what the next step is."

It was a rule small enough to remember in the dark.
