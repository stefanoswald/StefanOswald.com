Night 227

Teaching June the Town

Month 8: Helping Hands

June is ours now... trio-approved. But this month came the realization: "June doesn't know da town yet. Da secret parts." So: The orientation. One grand walking tour, guide-narrated: The gold dog's fence: introduced.

(Flat hand. The old rule. The tail accepted her on the first sniff. He's efficient now. He knows the guide's endorsements are sound.) Rosa's counter: "da crust ends are real. Just be a regular. Regulars get da town rate."

The book box: "take one leave one. I'm da keeper. Wave at it." The pothole puddle: "da best one. It refills. Bring boots always." The statue drill: taught. ("If you get losted: statue, shout, helper.

Da clerk at da market is a helper. Da saintly one.") And the willow, from the hill's base: "Dat's da town's tree. It keeps words. You'll get to give it one someday. Everybody ours does."

(Everybody ours does. June, hearing herself inside the word, glowed the whole walk home.) Orientation: complete. Citizen: installed. The town, handed on... the way towns actually transfer: kid to kid, secret by secret, one walking tour at a time.
