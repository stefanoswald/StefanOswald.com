Night 300

Night Three Hundred

Month 10: Words Are Magic

Night three hundred of the year. (The family marks the round ones. Standing tradition. The tally book came out, and this year, for the first time: "I write it. It's my number and I can write numbers now.")

3-0-0. Her hand. The tally book's first entry not in a parent's script. (The tally book felt it. Tally books do.) The stocktaking, at the lamp... what the year holds so far, counted by its owner: "Dis year I got: da dream meadow.

Da preschool. Da Maybe jar went to school. Da helping month. Da brave month... da deep end, Mama. And now da letters... The whole letters, I can read menus, I wrote a book, I made a poem happen..."

Three hundred nights hold a lot when you're four. The lamp burned patient through all of it. And at the end, the arithmetic... because she can do this now too: "Free hundred done. Dat leaves... sixty-five.

Sixty-five nights till da new book of me." Sixty-five nights. "Is da next year gonna be big like dis one?" And the answer, which is the only honest answer this family ever gives: "Bigger.

They're all bigger, love. That's the design. You're not done, you're never done... you're just always beginning at a higher floor." Always beginning at a higher floor. Three hundred nights up. Sixty-five to the landing. Climb on, reader. Climb on.
