Night 276

Theo's Letter

Month 10: Words Are Magic

The signing spree had a witness. And the witness... two-and-a-half, justice-minded, permanently aden-hungry... lodged his claim: "My yetter. Fee-oh yetter. Want." The lesson, table, professor and recruit: "Yours is da T.

It's da easiest good one. Two sticks. Down stick. Hat stick. Dat's da whole letter." Down stick. Hat stick. (Attempt one: both sticks, different zip codes. Attempt four: acquainted. Attempt nine: a recognizable T. Sideways.

But recognizable.) "That's the fee-oh!" And then, the epidemic, second generation: T's on every drawing. T's in the steamed-up window. T's in the sandbox, stick- drawn, house-sized. A T on her drawing, added without permission... (a summit gathered; a treaty drafted: artists sign their own work; collaborative works get both letters; the t-and-O joint signature debuted that week and ran for years on every co-production the household issued)...

And at the lamp, the professor's report: "He thinks da T is a prize. He doesn't know it's a key yet. What's it a key to? Everything. Da whole reading door. But he can think it's a prize for now.

Prizes get kept. Keys get used later." Prizes get kept. Keys get used later. (Pedagogy, age four: let the little ones love the shapes before the shapes go to work. Miss Dana, told of the law, wrote it down. She had a whole file by now. This one went on top.)
