Night 35

Theo Turns Two

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

The day came. Theo's second birthday. The birthday season, back to back. The house barely un-decorates between. His party, by the constitution: small and ours. His cake, per Rosa: a cow on it. (His request, made in his own words: "Cow cake."

Rosa's finest bovine work. The bell was frosting. He checked.) His guest list: the family, Lily-and-June ("dey're my fwiends too," true; the circle holds all sizes), and the gold dog at the fence, attending in spirit and for dropped cake.

The candles: Two. The coaching, delivered by the veteran at his elbow: "Big breath. Both at once. I'll help if you need but you won't need. Ready? One... two... Free..." Whooo. Both. Out. Himself. (The room erupted.

The coach loudest: "He got them! First try! I taught the breath!") His gifts: a wooden barn (the workshop; you can guess the carving), a knit cow-sweater, and from his sister, wrapped in her own paper, chosen with her own buttons at the market: a small yellow duck.

His own. Not Esquire. Not the block one. His. "Every kid needs a duck..." She explained. "It's da rules. Duck!" ruled the king, delighted. Two years old. The boy of the whole day. "Well now..." said Grandpa, at the moment. "Two of 'em walking and talking. House is officially outnumbered." Officially. Wonderfully. The outnumbering is the whole point.
