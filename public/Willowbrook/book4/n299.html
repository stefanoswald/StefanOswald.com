Night 299

The Sight Word Bee

Month 10: Words Are Magic

School news this week: Miss Dana ran the sight word game. (Not a competition... she was careful, the room's whole weather depends on it, "a team game. The class against the deck. Every card anyone reads is a point for everybody.")

The deck: the ghosts. The. Was. Of. Said. Come. The gang. The haunted regulars. The engagement, by the report at dinner: "When da of card came up, I said 'dat's da worst ghost, it says uv,' and da whole class remembered because of da posters, Mama, my posters, Miss Dana put dem on da wall... and Marcus got said, and Wren...

Wren read two, out loud, kind of loud even... and we beat da whole deck. Da class beat da deck twenty- free to nothing." (Twenty-three to nothing. Against a deck of cards. The score reported with the gravity of a championship... which, for twelve four- and-five-year- olds meeting the haunted words of their own language, it was.)

Kind of loud even." (The whisper, one month on, gaining decibels. The bench watcher, reading ghosts to a cheering room. Growth compounds quietly and then out loud... that's the whole physics of it.) "Da best part..."

She finished. "was da cheering. Reading alone is good. But reading where everybody wants you to get it... dat's da best weather dere is." Reading where everybody wants you to get it. May every room you ever read in, little one, have exactly that forecast. (Most of hers did. She learned early to bring the weather with her.)"
