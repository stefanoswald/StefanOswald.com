Night 148

The Unposed Portrait of Two

Month 5: Little Brother Tags Along

The aunts ordered it: a portrait. The siblings. Formal. For the mantel-of- mantels. The production, mounted per tradition: good outfits, the window light, the bribery snacks staged. The results, per tradition: Frames one through nine: him licking the window.

Her directing him not to. Him licking her directing arm. Frames ten through twenty: the great collapse. (Giggles. The set lost. The outfits, untucking themselves in real time.) Frame twenty-one: both looking... Looking! And Theo sneezed.

(The sneeze frame is its own genre. The aunts got a copy anyway. They frame sneezes. They frame everything.) And frame twenty-six, production abandoned, snacks distributed, camera lowered and fired once from the hip: piggyback.

Mid-voyage. Step two of three. Her stagger, his grip, both faces cracked wide with the laugh... the rug, the light, the whole ropes- between-them month of them, in one frame. The one. (It's always the one.

Frame nineteen taught them. Frame thirty-one confirmed. Frame twenty-six joins the dynasty: the unposed law, three generations of frames deep, undefeated.) Mantel: mounted. Aunts: wept by mail. And the workshop got a copy too... hung next to "Duck in Grass," the gallery of both-their-names growing wing by wing.

The formal portrait of these two does not exist. It never will. They don't hold still. That is the portrait.
