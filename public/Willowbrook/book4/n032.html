Night 32

The Second Dream

Month 2: Bobby Bear Wakes Up

The dream came back. (She'd been dreaming lots, per instructions. So he gets more walks.) This time: the meadow again, and past it... new territory... the dream-brook. Like the real one, but the fish glow.

Little lanterns, swimming. And Baba walked beside her on the bank, sure-footed, chatty: "Da fish here know me. I come sometimes when you're dreaming udder dreams. A bear finds his way around. What do you do here without me?

Wait, mostly. Practice walking. Talk to da willow, it's here too, you know. Da dream one. It's da same tree, udder side." (The same tree, other side. File that. The town's geography runs deeper than its maps.

The books have suspected it for volumes.) They skipped dream- stones. (His went nine skips. "Bears are natural at dis. It's da paws.") And at the dream's edge, where waking starts pulling: "Same time tomorrow?

I'm always here, little one. Dat's da whole job now. Dream me in." She woke with her arms around him. Glass-eyed. Still. Daytime-Baba. "I dreamed you in," she told him. And she'd swear... she'd swear all year... the flopped ear flopped a little further, like a nod.
