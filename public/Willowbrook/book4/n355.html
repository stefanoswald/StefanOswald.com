Night 355

Grandpa's Almanac Entry

Month 12: Almost Four

The almanac. (Joe's. The farm one. Fifty years of weather, plantings, prices, and, one page per year, back pages, the family section... People. Every grandkid gets entries. It's the farm's own record.) And this year, at the kitchen table, the tradition turned a corner: "You write your own entry dis year..." said Joe, pushing the book across. "You can. So you do. Dat's how it goes with everything." (The book: enormous. Year four.) Lerned her leders. Reeds to me on the porch now... Burpee, germinashun, da whole catalog.

Fed da duchess and got the nod. Planted da mammof row herself, forteen seeds, fum-pressed. Kept company with a chrysalis all winter which is the kind of farming most people never lern. The farm is proud.

Da farmer is prouder." (The farmer is prouder. He spoken that part slowly, looking out the window, and she wrote it without comment, and neither of them made it a moment, which is how the biggest moments go, at farms.)

The blotting. The closing of the book. And the one addendum, hers, penciled small at the page's foot after he'd turned away: "P. S. Da farmer taught me everything on this page. O." (He found it in November. Barn dust. Substantial. Farmers don't dog-ear. Grandfathers do.)"
