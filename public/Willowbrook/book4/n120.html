Night 120

Oliver Owl, Who Loves the Dark

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

On the final evening, Stella settled her glow over Willow Hill. And this month of dark- work closed with telling from the watch's own expert... the one member who lives there: "You all guard the night..." said Stella.

"But you, old friend... you love it. The small ones below just spent a month making peace with the dark. Give them the owl's side." And Oliver Owl, feathers settling, gave it: "The dark is my morning.

When your side's light lies down, mine gets up. The dark is where I see best, these eyes were built for it; the night is full of shapes and softness and supper that day- eyes never catch. The dark is quiet enough to hear a mouse's heartbeat, a leaf's decision, a child's breathing three lanes off.

The day shouts. The dark listens. And the dark...is where the stars live. The day hides ten thousand of them behind one. The dark is not the absence of light, little ones asleep below.

It is the honesty of it. All the lights, shown at their true size. The dark never scared me. The dark raised me. I am what the night grows, given time: something that sees in it, loves in it, and keeps watch from inside it... so the day-things can rest."

The branch was quiet. "Mmm..." Hummed the Moon at last... the night's other resident, agreeing with her whole silver self. "Log this month..." said the owl. "Log it: the town's smallest made peace with my country.

Tell her, someday, when she's old enough for the telling, their bedtime world noticed. Their bedtime world is glad. The dark keeps its friends forever." Logged. Sealed. Dark-gold, both. Goodnight, day-things. Rest easy. The night is up, and seeing, and yours.
