Night 256

The Basement Light

Month 9: Brave Is Scared Plus One Step

Mama needed the keepsake box from the basement, and Olivia offered to fetch it alone.

At the top step, the darkness changed her mind.

"You may take the flashlight," Mama said. "And I will stay right here."

Olivia turned on the beam and counted each stair. Halfway down, the furnace rumbled.

She froze.

"Furnace," Mama called from above.

"I knew that," Olivia answered, though she had needed the reminder.

At the bottom, the basement smelled of dust and laundry soap. The box sat on the second shelf. Olivia tucked the flashlight under her arm, lifted the box with both hands, and climbed back slowly.

In the kitchen she set it down and let out a long breath.

"Was the basement less scary than you expected?" Mama asked.

"No," Olivia said. "But I knew what the sounds were, and I knew where you were."

She looked back at the open door. It was still a dark room beneath the house. Now it was also a room she had crossed.
