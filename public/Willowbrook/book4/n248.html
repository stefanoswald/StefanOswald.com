Night 248

The Recital Nerves

Month 9: Brave Is Scared Plus One Step

The spring sing assigned her a solo line. (A whole line. Alone. Two hundred people. The multi-purpose room's biggest stakes yet.) The nerves: real. Week-long. Appetite- adjacent. ("What if my words go far away on da stage?")

The kit, deployed in full: The practice: nightly. (The line, she'd tell you it's secret; show rules, drilled at the mirror, to the bear, to Franklin, to the fern.) The visualization: Mama's trick. ("Close your eyes.

See the stage. See the line going right. Your brain rehearses what you show it. Show it the good version.") The one-step rule: applied. ("You don't sing to two hundred people. You sing to one person, two hundred times.

Pick your person. Sing to dem.") (She picked Theo. "He thinks everything I do is da best. Good audience.") And the cape: worn under the dress. Secret. The inside kind of wearing. The night: the line came.

She found Theo's face in row three. Sang it to him... clear, loud, hers: "...and da whole town wakes up singing!" (He yelled "Wivy!" in the after-silence, per his tradition. The house came down, per its.)

"I was scared da whole time..." She reported, glowing, after. "And? And. Dat's da whole word. Scared and. I did it scared." Scared and. The formula's shortest edition yet. It fit on a stage. It fits everywhere.
