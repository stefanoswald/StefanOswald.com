Night 280

The Three-Word Page

Month 10: Words Are Magic

The early reader. It told her Tuesday. At the lamp. Unprompted: "I'm trying da free-word book. Don't help unless I do da signal." (The signal: agreed. One tap = stuck, help. No tap = silence, please, work in progress.)

Page one. Three words. The work... and it is work, the realest kind, watchable on her whole face: "D... o... g. Dog. (I knew it was dog, dere's a dog right dere in da picture, but I read it too, both ways, check.)

S... i... t...s. Sits... Dog... sits. D-o-w-n. Down. Dog sits down." Silence. Then, very quietly, not to Mama, not to anyone, just to the room, to the lamp, to the whole rearranged world: "...

I read it. Dat was reading. I just did reading." (Three words. The first sentence of her life read entirely alone. The biggest doors open quietly. Ask any library.) The rest of the book: seven more pages, conquered over four nights, signal used twice, sovereignty absolute.

And at the end, the back cover closed with the gravity of a completed voyage: "Da book told me when. Da librarian was right... What's next?" What's next. The reader's eternal question. First asked here, night 280, lamp on, door open one real inch... and never, not once, in her whole life, asked out of anything but joy.
