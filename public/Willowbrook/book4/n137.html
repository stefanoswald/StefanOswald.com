Night 137

The Day He Wasn't There

Month 5: Little Brother Tags Along

An experiment nobody designed: Theo went with Papa to the far cousins. Day trip. Just the boys. And she had... everything. The house: hers. The toys: unknockable. The nap gap: All day. Mama: undivided. The whole only-child kingdom, restored for one entire day.

By ten a.m: glorious. By noon: quiet. By two: "... Mama. It's too big in here." Too big in here. The tower stood unknocked, and looked, she reported..." Weird. Like it's waiting. "The fort held one... "but da spot's empty."

The hide and seek: "you can't play it alone. I checked. I hid and... nobody." (She hid. Nobody sought. The loneliest sentence in the book. The curtain kept her secret until she gave it up herself.)

By four, at the window, watching for the car: "I miss my shadow." The car, at five. The door. And the reunion, the running kind, both directions, the collision-hug in the hallway like Thursday's greeting, like the crash that started everything: "Wivy!

Fee-oh! I hid and you weren't dere and da tower just waited and..." The house, re-sized instantly to correct. "Good day, just-us?" asked Mama at bedtime. "Good day. But... I like da full house now.

Da just-us is for gaps. Not for whole days. He's... part of da size of it." He's part of the size of it. The shadow, missed at full weight, measured at last: exactly one brother wide, which turned out to be most of the room.
