Night 338

The Sunflower Row Goes In

Month 12: Almost Four

The envelope came back. (The seed order. Mailed in deep winter with July written inside it. And this week, at the farm: July arrived. In packets.) The list, done on the truck gate: tomatoes beans, squash, stubby carrots... and the packet.

The one she'd have known blindfolded: Mammoth sunflowers. (Spelled right on the packet. "Dey fixed my spelling..." She noted. "Dat's fair. Burpee knows deir own flower.") The planting, row of honor, south fence, full sun: The furrow: Joe's hoe, her line- walking behind.

The seeds: striped, seed- big, one per thumb-press... "Dis deep? Knuckle deep. Firm it after. Seed likes to know the ground means it." (Seed likes to know the ground means it. The notebook got that one through the fence, later.)

Fourteen seeds. Fourteen thumb- presses. Fourteen firmings. And the water, and the stakes for later... "Later? How tall is mammof? Taller than your papa. Taller than the truck. Come August, this row will be a wall of faces, every one following the sun across the sky like a crowd at a slow parade...

I planted a wall of faces taller dan Papa. With my thumb." With her thumb. (The row came up. The wall happened. But that's ahead. Today is fourteen thumb-presses and a promise firmed into spring dirt: the summer, planted, by the shortest farmer on the payroll. Grow on, birthday girl. Race your row.)
