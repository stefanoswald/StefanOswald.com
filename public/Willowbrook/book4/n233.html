Night 233

Carrying for Wren's Grandmother

Month 8: Helping Hands

Wren's grandmother does Tuesday pickups. (The cookie-recipe one. The line's adopted elder. She walks, two blocks, cane some days... and carries Wren's bag plus her market sack.) And this month, a Tuesday shadow appeared at her elbow: "I can carry da market one.

I'm strong. I carry milk." The Tuesday institution, founded: the two- block carry. The volunteer, the grandmother, Wren, and the sack... walked slow, at cane pace, at gold-dog pace, at the pace where the talking happens.

And the talking: her stories. (The old country. The boat over. The bakery she worked at sixty years ago, "like your Rosa's, but smaller"... the recipe she's teaching Rosa, her mother's, older than the town.)

"Why do you carry for me, little one?" she asked once. And the answer, shrugged, complete: "Kindness goes your speed. Dat's da rule. Plus your stories are da good kind. Da carrying is how I get dem."

The carrying is how I get them. (The elder repeated that to the whole pickup line. Twice. The line, reportedly, dissolved. Kindness that goes your speed, paid in stories: the town's oldest exchange rate, rediscovered every generation, two blocks at a time.)
