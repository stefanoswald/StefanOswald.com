Night 118

What the Nightlight Sees

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

One more account of this month. The smallest witness's. The gold nightlight... plugged low, faithful, Della-come-inside... saw all of it, from the outlet: It saw the shadow caught. (The curtain show, demystified at lamp-point, then performed for fun above its own glow.)

It saw the under-bed annexed. It saw the storm night, the whole family harboring past its little reach, the booms, the three tools, the heap asleep by two. It traveled, the farm, the sill, the older dark... and came home approved: the gold works anywhere.

It saw the 2 a.m. Rewrite: a monster re-cast as lost, given a bed, escorted to a meadow it will never see but fully believes in. And nightly, every night, all month... it saw the last scene, the one it exists for: a girl, breathing slow.

A bear, positioned. The day, folded and saved. The dark, tucked and resting. And itself, small, gold, steady... holding the one job of every nightlight ever plugged: being the proof, all night long, at kid's-eye height, that the light never fully leaves.

It just gets small, and stays. Goodnight, little gold. Good work this month. Good work every night of this whole shelf. The dark is just the day resting... and you're the day's night watchman, two inches tall, undefeated.
