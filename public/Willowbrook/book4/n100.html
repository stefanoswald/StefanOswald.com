Night 100

One Hundred Nights of Book Four

Month 4: The Dark Is Just the Day Resting

One hundred nights. Book four's first stone. This hundred held: a world, doubled. A bear, walking. (The meadow mapped. The count open. The far gate, gold and distant, saved for a far book.) A why-engine at full burn.

(The journal, filling. The wonder-file, founded. The lantern's next keeper, apprenticing.) And now a month of dark-work: the day renamed resting, the shadows caught, the under-bed annexed, the brave list posted and growing.

One quarter of the pretend year, banked. "A hundred?" she asked at the number. "Since da birthday?" Since the birthday. "Dat's...lots-lots of stories." Lots-lots. "And how many left?" Two hundred sixty-five, this book.

Then three more books. Then... "Den more books," she finished, with total confidence. (the story smiles. The shelf she means ends at seven. The shelf she'll build, the read- alone years, the chapter years, the whole life of books this nightly lamp is quietly constructing... that one doesn't end at all.

So: correct, little one. Then more books. Always more books.) Stone: set. Lamp: lowered. "Nigh-nigh, story." Goodnight, story. Same time tomorrow. All two hundred sixty-five of them, and then the more.
