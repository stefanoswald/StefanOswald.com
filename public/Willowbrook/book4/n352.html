Night 352

The Dream Meadow, Almost Four

Month 12: Almost Four

The meadow came this week. (The dream one. The willow's other side. It comes when it comes... but it has never once, as it happened, missed a birthday week.) The crossing: familiar now. (The gate.

The gold. The grass that knows her weight. Spring on both sides this year... the dream flowers doing their extra colors.) And on the path: Bobby bear. Walking. (Upright, the way he does here. One ear flopped, patchwork heart catching the gold.

And: something held behind his back.) "You're early..." She said. "Da birthday's Saturday. Bears deliver early..." said the bear. "It's da whole reputation... Almost-Four. Come walk. I have a job to do and a thing to say."

The walk: the meadow's long edge, to where the far gate stands. (The far gate. The one seeded in memory since the meadow's first map. Closed. Always closed. Not locked... closed the way books are closed.)

"Da thing to say..." said the bear, stopping. "You're reading now. Writing. Deep-ending. Your years are getting big, and bears know what dat means: you'll need me different soon. Less carrying. More... keeping. Dat's allowed.

Dat's da design. Bears are built for every size of needing... I'll always need you. I know..." said the bear, comfortably. "Different is not less. Write dat down; you write things now." And the thing behind his back, presented: a key.

(Small. Gold. Warm as a held hand.) "Not for da far gate..." said the bear, before she could ask. "Not yet. Yets have schedules... you taught da whole meadow dat. It's for keeping. Some doors open years before you reach dem.

Da key comes first. Da key is da promise." She woke with her hand closed. (Empty. Of course. Dream keys don't cross. But as it happened that her hand knew the shape of it, ever after.

Ask the far pages what the far gate opens onto. Book seven knows. The bear knows. The promise: saved. Kept warm. Like all this family's words.)
