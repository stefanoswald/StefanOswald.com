Night 267

Wren's Loud Day

Month 9: Brave Is Scared Plus One Step

News from school, reported at dinner like a front-page story: "Wren talked at circle." (Wren. The quiet friend. The bench watcher. Deep-end listener, joke- keeper, six-month holder of the watching-is-playing law.

Has never... Never, raised a hand at circle.) "Miss Dana asked who knows why leaves change. And Wren knows... she told me at da bench once, da whole science... and I saw her hand start... and stop... and start... and I whispered 'I'll watch you do it'... and it went up."

The answer: delivered in a whisper. (The room leaned in. Twelve kids, one teacher, total silence, leaning at a whisper about chlorophyll.) "And da whole class said whoa because da answer was so good. And Wren did da smile.

Da real one. Da whole- face one." The analysis, at the lamp: "Her brave is a different size dan mine. My hand goes up easy, my brave is da water and da high branches. Her brave is da hand.

One whisper for her is like... da whole deep end for me." Her one whisper is the whole deep end. (Courage, correctly measured at last: not by the size of the act but by the size of the fear it crossed.

The smallest voice at circle did the biggest brave of this month. And the friend who said "I'll watch you do it," the exact words from her own bridge, her own rope, her own every-brave... lent the step that crossed it. The kit travels. That was always the design.)
