Night 209

The Maybe That Was a Person

Month 7: The Maybe Lessons

This month's last big catch came as a realization, at the trio's third Thursday: "Mama. June is a maybe... How so?" The tracing, worked out loud: "June's family moved here because her papa's old job ended.

Dat seemed bad, she told me. Moving seemed bad. Da new town where you don't know anybody: So bad. And den...she hovered at da sandbox. Which felt bad. And we passworded her. And now she's ours.

Da trio. And her baby sister is coming to a whole town of us with a blanket already made. June's whole family is a caught maybe. Da bad thing in anudder town was da good thing in ours." (...the story sat back.

The lens, aimed at people now. At migration, loss, arrival, welcome, the whole human weather system, read at four years old through one friendship: every newcomer is somebody's seemed-bad, turning. The town caught them.

The town has always caught them, check the records; everyone here arrived as somebody's maybe once.) The slip, this month's longest: "June is a maybe with a face. Da best catch in da jar. Towns are jars too.

Caught forever." Towns are jars too. Saved. Framed, honestly. Somewhere the bench mothers are crying and they don't know why.
