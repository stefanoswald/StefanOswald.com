Night 86

The Hard Why About June's Abuela

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

The hard why came at bedtime, where hard whys live: "Why did June's abuela get sick? She's nice. Nice people shouldn't get sick." (The justice objection. The oldest one. She's three; philosophers are eighty; the objection is identical.)

The answer, held honest: "You're right that she's nice. And here's the hard true thing: sick doesn't check who's nice. Bodies are like weather, storms land where they land, not where's deserved. It's nobody's fault.

It's not a punishment. It's just... bodies, being bodies, sometimes. Dat's not fair. No. It isn't. Some true things aren't fair. You're allowed to be mad at that. I am too." (Allowed to be mad at the unfairness of things.

Licensed. Together. Half the comfort is the company in the objection.) "But here's the other true thing: her abuela has good doctors, far away, working hard. And June has us. And you already know the whole job: ...

Sitting close and staying." Sitting close and staying. "And Mama, dis week... June said her abuela did a video call and sang to her. In da odder language. June cried but da good kind." The far lap, singing down the wire.

The wire, doing its one great job again. "Den da abuela is doing da job too..." She said. "From far. Everybody's sitting close. Just... some sitting is froo da phone." Some sitting is through the phone. The hard why: not answered. Held. Which was always the assignment.
