Night 82

Why Does Bread Rise

Month 3: Why? Why? Why?

Thursday. Rosa's kitchen. Dough day. And the why went to the professional: "Rosa. Why does da bread get big? You put it in da bowl little and it gets big." Rosa lifted the cloth off the proofing bowl like unveiling a sleeping animal.

"Best secret in baking. Ready? The dough is alive... Alive?! There's a tiny living thing in it, yeast. Smaller than you can see. Millions of them. And they're in there right now, eating the flour's sugar and breathing out tiny breaths.

Every breath: a bubble. Millions of breaths: the bread rises. Bread is flour, water, salt... and breath." The dough, examined at nose range. It smelled alive. (It is alive. The smell is the proof. Every bakery is a barn of invisible livestock.)

"So when we punch it down... They start breathing again. Patient little things. They just get back to work." (The dough law and the family law, converging: knocked down, get back to work. The yeast has been running the try-again program since bread began.)

"Rosa. Your whole bakery... is breathing?" The whole bakery is breathing. Every loaf in the window: a held breath, baked golden. "Dat's why it smells like dat..." She whispered. "Da bread smell is... Bref." The bread smell is breath.

Never told better, ruled Rosa, in thirty years of telling it. The crust end that day tasted, everyone agreed, especially alive.
