Night 62

Marcus Thinks in Ramps

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

The study turned to Marcus this week, with his permission ("you can put me in the notebook if the notebook's fair"). The interview, at the blocks: "The letters still swim sometimes. B's and d's park crooked when I'm tired. Reading's a uphill for me, probably always.

But..." And he built while he talked, hands running their own program. "I see loads. Where the weight goes. I look at a tower and I see the pushes inside it, like arrows. Nobody taught me.

The arrows are just there. You see stories everywhere? I see arrows everywhere. The school grades the stories. Nobody grades the arrows." The fair notebook took it fairly: "Marcus sees the arrows. The pushes inside things.

Nobody taught him. Nobody grades it. His uphill is my flat. My flat is his uphill. The hills are dealt out different and the school only maps some of them." And the exchange rate, negotiated at the blocks: he teaches her arrows (she saw one by Friday, "the bookshelf pushes down and out, Marcus, I saw it..."); she keeps his b's parked. The trade routes of the differently-hilled.
