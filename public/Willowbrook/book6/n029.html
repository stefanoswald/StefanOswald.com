Night 29

The Flight of the Willowhawk

Month 1: How Things Work

The day. The hill. The wind: right. The crowd: everybody. (The town talks, by the forecast. Half the lane came. Rosa brought a tray.) The go check, at the top, per rule: "Mateo. The Hawk's ready.

Are you?" and his answer, helmet on, eyes clear: "Been ready since the chalkboard." The run: down the mown strip, rope crew pacing, wheels singing, bedsheet wings drinking the hill's wind, and then... The lift. Three seconds.

The story counted, and the story does not exaggerate: Three full seconds of daylight under all three wheels, the Willowhawk flying, actually flying, Mateo whooping, the crowd's roar lifting it maybe a fourth second, before the nose dipped, the tail argued, and the whole beautiful contraption came down in the long grass in a cartwheeling, bedsheet-shredding, entirely survivable tumble. Silence. Then Mateo's arm, up out of the grass: "Did you see it?! She flew!

Three seconds! The bandages, please, and the notebook! Log it! She! Flew!" Logged: "The willowhawk. First flight: Three seconds. First crash: Glorious. Pilot: Laughing. Crowd: Roaring. The laughed-at machine flew over the laughing and everybody was cheering by the time it landed."
