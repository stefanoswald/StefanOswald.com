Night 86

The Hard Case

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

This month's hard case, brought to the lamp: a kid at school, older, sharp, uses other kids' differents as ammunition. The stutter mimicked. The chair blocked. The quiet mocked as stupid. This month's whole lesson, weaponized in reverse.

"And here's my puzzle, Mama. This month taught me: everybody's got a basement. The quills guard soft. Mean is a alarm somebody forgot to turn off. So I know he's probably got a whole hard story..."

The breath. "But knowing his basement doesn't unblock Zoe's chair. Understanding him and stopping him, do they fight?" Mama took the big one whole: "No, love. They're two jobs, and you can hold both. Understanding is for how you think about him, it keeps your own heart from going hard, and it tells you what might fix him, someday, upstream.

But stopping is for what you do, today, at the chair. The basement explains the mean. It never licenses it. You can hold 'he's probably hurting' in one hand and 'this stops now, and here's the pattern in a stack for the grown-ups whose job it is' in the other. Kindness without a spine isn't kindness. It's surrender with nice manners."
