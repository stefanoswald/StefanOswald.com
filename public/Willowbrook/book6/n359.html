Night 359

Willowbrook at Gloaming

Month 12: Almost Six

Two nights before her birthday, Olivia walked through Willowbrook with Mama during the gold hour.

Rosa waved from the bakery window, hiding the sky-cake somewhere behind her. The library lantern was lit, and the librarian's shape moved among the returns.

Mr. Petrov worked beneath his shop lamp. The community garden glowed with string lights and full beds. School stood dark and quiet, waiting for September.

At the crossing, no guard remained for the evening, but the corner still seemed to hold the shape of everyone who had stood there to keep children safe.

They passed the Great Willow without climbing the hill.

"Two more sleeps," Olivia told it. "Save my place."

The tree made no sign. It did not need to. Some appointments become certain because they have been kept so many times before.

Olivia and Mama walked home as the last gold left the windows.
