Night 215

The Dana Door

Month 8: School Stories

The Dana file, worked this month's way, by door, not lecture: The invitation: "Fort together, Saturday. Whole crew. You're invited." (Dana's flinch, visible: a group? Where her one claimed friend dilutes into many? The scarcity machinery, grinding.)

She came anyway. (They mostly come anyway. Lonely beats scared by a nose, usually. The whole second grid banks on it.) And Saturday did the teaching no lecture could: the fort's whole candle economy, witnessed.

Lily belonging to everyone and shorting no one. The constitution recited ("the three of us is permanent; the whoever's cold is infinite; both forever"). Room made without measurement. By dusk, Dana, who'd spent the day braced for the subtraction that never came, asked Lily, quietly..."

So nobody here... loses their spot? Ever? "And Lily, eleven-days-younger wisdom at full power: "There's no spots. There's just the fort. Forts don't run out. That's why we built one instead of a pie."
