Night 73

The Allergy Table

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

The study mapped the lunchroom's strangest geography this week: The allergy table. (Nut-free. Three residents. Set apart, for safety, but apart is apart, and the study asks what things build and also what they cost.)

The interview, done as a guest (hands washed, lunch checked, rules honored, "their table, their rules, the whole point"): "The apart keeps us safe..." said its senior resident. "but it also keeps us... apart. Kids think the table's contagious. It's not a sick table.

It's a careful table." The finding, taken to Ms. Alvarez with the usual paperwork: "The safety's right but the lonely's a side effect nobody's treating. Proposal: the table doesn't need less rules. It needs more visitors.

A rotation. Kids with safe lunches sit at the careful table, by turns, with the checking done proper. The apart stays safe and stops being lonely. Safety and company aren't enemies. They just need scheduling."

(Adopted. The rotation ran. The careful table became, within a month, the lunchroom's most-booked real estate, the checking ritual itself became the draw; kids like rules; rules are belonging with steps.)
