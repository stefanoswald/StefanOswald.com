Night 166

The Family Goal Board

Month 6: Hard Things Take Days

Olivia covered the kitchen corkboard with four names and the things each person was learning.

OLIVIA: PIANO. BACKSTROKE.

THEO: LETTERS. THE BIG ROCK.

MAMA: COMMUNITY GARDEN PLAN.

PAPA: REPAIR THE SHED.

Beside Papa's goal she added: THIRD SUMMER OF SAYING "THIS SUMMER."

Papa laughed when he saw it. "Public embarrassment is a powerful tool."

"It is not embarrassment," Olivia said. "It is company. Everybody has a hard thing."

They marked small steps instead of perfect streaks. Olivia practiced one difficult measure. Theo learned three new words. Mama found two garden volunteers. Papa replaced one rotten board.

By June, the shed was sound. Papa credited the board. Olivia credited the many small Saturdays that had finally become visible.

No one's mountain disappeared. It was simply easier to climb with the family watching from nearby.
