Night 138

The Fishing Lesson

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

Joe took her fishing this week. (His boyhood pools; his father's rod, cut down; the full inheritance.) The morning's lesson: the cast. The wait (heron school paying off, "you've got the stillness already, darlin'. Most kids fight the quiet.

You moved in years ago"). The catch: one perch, hand-sized, furious, gleaming. And at the bucket: the decision. Joe laid it out plain, farmer-honest: "Keeping's legal. Pan-size, just barely. Eating what you catch is old and honest.

I've done it all my life. Throwing back is honest too. Your catch. Your call." The long look at the perch, the gills working, the eye like a gold button. "... Back. Not because keeping's wrong, the coop taught me the honest eating; I gavver eggs from ladies I know personally.

But this one's the first. The first of anything is for learning, not keeping. He taught me the cast and the wait. Tuition's paid. Swim on, professor." (The perch went back with a flip like an insult, which both of them agreed was fair.)
