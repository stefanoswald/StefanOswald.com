Night 142

The Frozen Waterfall

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

The deep cold delivered this month's crown wonder: The falls, frozen. (The upstream drop, a modest three-footer, the brook's one waterfall, caught mid-leap by the hard freeze: columns and draperies of white-green ice, the whole motion of falling stopped and kept.) The pilgrimage: family-scale, thermos-equipped. The viewing: reverent.

"It's the water's statue, Papa. Of itself. The fall was too fast to ever see proper, and the cold just... Paused it. We're seeing the shape falling water always had and never showed." And underneath, the ear to the ice, the routine, the thread of it still running, secret, behind the sculpture: the falls not stopped at all, just... showing one frame of themselves while the film ran on behind.

"The statue's temporary," Theo observed, gravely, deputy-wise. "Spring takes it. Spring takes the statue. The falls stay. The temporary part is the showing, not the thing." (they remembered this under this month's whole teaching, and notes: they visited the frozen falls every deep winter after.

The statue was always different. The falls were always the same. Never the same brook twice. Never a different brook. The water says it every way it can.)
