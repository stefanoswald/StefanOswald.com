Night 231

Valentines for Everybody

Month 8: School Stories

Theo ran his own valentine-making table that year. He had paper, scissors, a carefully fenced glitter area, and one rule.

"Everybody gets one," he told his stuffed cow. "That is the rule because the rule is good."

Olivia made cards for her class and several Willowbrook friends. Wren's used quiet colors. Gus-Senior's showed the old marble from the garden and said, THE GROUND KEEPS ITS CHILDREN.

At school, Olivia discovered that every class now followed the same practice: bring cards for everyone or bring none.

"Our kitchen-table rule reached the whole school," she told Mama.

"Good rules travel," Mama said.

Theo held up two gluey hands. "Mine traveled to the cow already."

The cow received three valentines, which was two more than anyone had planned. The children received one apiece. No desk was left empty.
