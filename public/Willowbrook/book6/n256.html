Night 256

The Eulogy for the Duchess

Month 9: Truth With Kindness

The story asks for quiet on this page. Saturday. The coop. The count came up one short, and this time the search ended at the roost's warm corner, where the duchess lay still. Old. Fat.

In her own good time, on her own farm, exactly as promised. The truth of it, held the family way, no softening, no "she went to a farm" (she was the farm): the burial, by the fence row, Scout's descendants standing over. The eulogy, hers, because Joe asked and couldn't: "The Duchess was the meanest chicken in the world, and that was the first thing everybody knew and the least true. She was a alarm that never got to turn off, guarding a sister three years gone.

She was a promise wearing fevers. Grandma picked her last and said be patient, and Grandpa was, for nine years, and the promise got kept on both ends. She pecked everybody and nodded at almost nobody and sat on exactly one boot, once, for eleven minutes, and I will keep that boot forever. She laid seven eggs this spring at a age when hens don't, for reasons nobody explains because hens don't explain.

She was my first colleague. The farm is quieter and the farm is less and that's the true size of it. Nigh-nigh, Duchess. The alarm can turn off now. The sister's that way." (Joe's hat was off the whole time.

Theo left a sorry-flower, for nothing he did; "some flowers are just sad-flowers, the crop covers both." they remembered this page beside Buttons and the empty carrier, in the drawer where the true weights live.)
