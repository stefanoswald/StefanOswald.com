Night 30

The How Month, Counted

Month 1: How Things Work

Month's end. At month's end, Olivia could count one clock (opened; full of town). One bakery. One zipper. One doorbell (buttons complete, they don't send). One nest (four blue maybes; four tooth keys; one fledge day; Fourth flew farthest).

One piano. One compost bin. One almanac. One wind tunnel. One Willowhawk: Three seconds of sky, one glorious tumble, one town converted from laughing to roaring. Other changes resisted numbers: that nothing works alone, not in the clock, not anywhere.

That the magic looks like a rota from inside. That most broken things are one crooked tooth. That the opening comes from inside or it doesn't count. That the wait is the ingredient. That the world, opened up, didn't get less magic, it got more: "Every thing I looked inside of, Mama, had another whole world in it.

The insides have insides. It's workshops all the way down. Good how-month? The openest one. And the best part: the year's just started. Eleven months of insides left. The whole town's a clock with its back off now. I can see everything touching everything." Remembered. Everything touches everything. On we go.
