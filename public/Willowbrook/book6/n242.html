The School Stories Month... Year, Counted

Month 8: School Stories

At the end of the month, Mama asked Olivia what she would remember about the school year.

Olivia expected the answer to be one thing. Instead, the whole year arrived at once: Lily's hard week, the class treaty they made together, the reading corner, the wall they changed, the test that felt like a monster until somebody offered a second try.

"There were unfair things," Olivia said. "Some were one person's mistake. Some were rules nobody had bothered to question. Those take different kinds of fixing."

Papa turned his mug in both hands. "And what did you fix?"

Olivia began counting on her fingers. The class jar. The quiet corner. The clinic table. The new rule that had traveled all the way to the district office.

Then she stopped counting.

"I used to think school was a place I went," she said. "Now it feels like a place all of us make. My fingerprints are on it. Not real fingerprints," she added. "Mostly."

Mama laughed. The answer went into the family notebook without a grand lesson underneath. Olivia had already said the part worth keeping.
