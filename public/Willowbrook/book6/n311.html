Night 311

The Entry Theo Requested

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

Night's entry, chosen by the four-year-old: "May 1979. The pig got out. Three words, and beneath, added in stages over apparently days: Still out. Seen at the church picnic. Seen at the school. The pig is enjoying himself.

Caught at last in widow kane's roses. The widow kept him. The pig knew what he was doing all along." The full oral edition ran twenty minutes and included: the church picnic raid, the school incident ("the teacher screamed; the kids voted him class president; the file, Mama, every era's got miss dana's file"), and the roses, where the fugitive, cornered at last, simply lay down among the blooms and looked up at the widow "like he'd booked the room."

She kept him. Fourteen years. Named him Roosevelt. (Theo's answer, breathless, tear-streaked with laughing: "The pig knew what he was doing. The best ones always do," Joe agreed. "Animals and people both. Some escapes are arrivals, darlin'.

Roosevelt wasn't running from the farm. He was running to the roses. Worth remembering when people bolt too.")
