Night 113

The Toy Drive Logistics

Month 4: My Jobs

The school's winter toy drive met its match this week: a first-grader with logistics. The bins (labeled by age band, "a thirteen-year-old getting a rattle helps nobody; sort at collection, not at delivery; the cart taught me"). The counts (public, chalked, updated, "numbers make generosity catching; people give to a rising count; the bean jar economics"). The routes (to the shelter, the clinic, the far school, no-backtrack, Zoe-dispatched).

And at home, the harder half: the own-shelf check. Theo's, first, coached gentle ("the still-loved stays. The outgrown goes. The box tells the stories on the way out, the hand-me-down law").

And hers, where someone saw the mitten-tree lesson come due at scale: the good stuffed horse. Not the outgrown one. The good one. ("The kid whose winter this is should get the best warm.

The drawer taught me at four. The shelf learns it at five-going-six. Same lesson, bigger furniture. If it's worth giving, it's worth giving the good one.")
