Night 135

The Rain Race

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

Storm day, and the brook's story came to the window: raindrop races. (The old game, pick your drop, cheer it down the pane, upgraded by a month of hydrology:) "Theo, watch what they do. The drops don't race straight, they wander, they stall, and then they hit another drop and merge and the merged one goes faster. The big drops eat the little ones and win.

That's not cheating. That's the whole secret of rivers. The brook is a thousand raindrop races that all merged. The pane is a watershed. We're watching the brook get born, every storm, on our own glass."

The races ran all afternoon. And the finding, for the notebook: "The little wandering ones that merge become the thing that carves valleys. Nothing big starts big, this month keeps saying it. Even the flood on the bridge stone started as pane-racers. Respect the drops."
