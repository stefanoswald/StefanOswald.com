Night 213

The Friendship Wobble

Month 8: School Stories

This month opened with the wobble nobody maps for: Lily went distant. (Three lunches elsewhere, the new girl's table. Shorter answers. The window-seat Saturday: "busy.") The old scripts, not run: no interrogation. No counter-distance ("cold answering cold is how friendships freeze, the fight physics").

No web-gossip triangulation. The run script: the treaty's own tools. One direct, unloaded offer at the fence: "Just ears available. No hands. Whenever." And then, the hardest tool. Waiting. ("Friendship wobbles are either about you or not about you, and asking-too-hard makes both kinds worse.

The heron rule: the stillness is the fastest move sometimes. She knows where the bench is. She's known for six years.")
