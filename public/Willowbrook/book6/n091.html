Night 91

The Employment Question

Month 4: My Jobs

First grade opened this week (new room, new teacher. Mr. Okafor, the dog-yelling legend himself; the wall at the playground, eyed; the whole machine running on five years of practice), and with it, this month's declaration, at dinner: "I want jobs. Real ones.

Not chores, chores are the rent for living here; I know the way things worked; the feeder and the table and the room are rent. I mean posts. Things that are mine, that somebody counts on, that the world runs worse without. The difference is: chores end when they're done.

Posts never end. Posts are the thousand-answers-a-minute kind. I'm six in nine months. It's time." The negotiation produced this month's charter: three posts, real ones, with real counting-on. To be found, not assigned.

("Posts can't be given, Papa says. Posts are where your showing-up meets somebody's needing. You have to go find the meeting spot.")
