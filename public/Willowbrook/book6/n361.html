Night 361

The Willow Writes: TRUE

Month 12: Almost Six

The birthday. The walk. The whole family, golden hour, up the hill, sixth annual pilgrimage. The secret-star sweater, worn to the meeting (the stars visible when the light moves; the light, climbing the hill, moved).

The hinged box, in her pocket, with a slip of paper and the good pen. The tree: waiting. The hush. The branches, parting in their private wind... And in the bark, beneath home, beneath family, beneath growing, beneath kind, the fifth word, fresh gold, first glow: True.

(She read it. Stood still. And whispered, the story close enough to catch it: "... I knew. The whole truth month, I knew. You watched the umpire calls and the kind and honest and the mirror sessions and the kitchen wall, kindest true thing first, and you were writing this the whole time.")

The sentence so far, read aloud, her finger tracing air: "Home. Family. Growing. Kind. True... Mama. Look at it build. The place. The people. The direction. The how. And now, the condition. The thing all the others run on.

Kind without true is a currency crisis. Growing without true is just... taller. The tree put true fifth because true is what makes all the other words spendable. One more word, Mama. One more birthday.

The sentence ends next year and I already can't..." She stopped; the gold moved; the family stood in it. "I can wait. The tree taught the waiting. The tree taught everything." (And then, new tradition, first execution: the slip of paper.

The good pen. True, written small, her best letters. Folded. Placed in the hinged box, the reliquary's first resident, exactly as its builder designed. Theo watched his gift begin its work with the satisfaction of every engineer at every successful launch. The box clicked shut on the word. The keeping, kept.)
