Night 61

How Noor Prays

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

Noor mentioned it matter-of-factly this week, leaving the bench early: "Prayer time. We pray five times a day." The collector's follow-up, respectful, notebook-backed: "Five? Every day? Tell me the way things worked.

I keep appointments too, the lamp, the Fridays. Systems are my whole collection." Noor explained it the way she maps: precisely, warmly. The five times, sun-anchored (dawn, midday, afternoon, sunset, night, "the clock is the sky.

Like your gloaming, but five of them"). The washing first. The facing, one direction, everywhere on earth..." Like every compass in the world agreeing. "The words in Arabic, old ones, her grandmother's and her grandmother's grandmother's.

"So five times a day, everybody in your whole... your whole web, everywhere, faces the same spot and says the same old words at the same sky-times. Noor. That's the biggest together I ever heard of. It's a web that covers the world and it meets five times a day.

That's exactly what it is..." said Noor, pleased the way cartographers are when someone reads the map right. "Most people ask if it's a lot. You asked what it builds."
