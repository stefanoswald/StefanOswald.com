Night 150

The Brook Month, Counted

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

Month's end. At month's end, Olivia could count one source (a dinner plate of quiet; everything starts about that size). One calendar. One minnow school (no boss; match your neighbors; the second grid has fins).

One dam (built, checked, gated, the pool and the river; check whose water). One heron. One flood-line lesson. One cedar float (launched untracked; the sending is the message). One cleanup plus one bin. One count.

One otter. One skim ice. Other changes resisted numbers: that the music is only where something's in the way. That the tap and the brook are cousins and the always-things go invisible unless a house refuses.

That the stayer and the goer are one system, and the town lives in the conversation. "Good brook-month? The longest-story one. Mama, the brook's story started before the town and ends at the sea and never actually ends at all, and I got to read one summer's page of it.

That's what the big stories are like. You don't read them start to finish. You read your page, well, and hand the book on. The brook taught me how to be in things that are bigger than my whole life.

Gently. That's how. Gently, and leave the bend better, and don't tell where the otter lives." Remembered. Read your page well. Hand the book on.
