Night 177

The Teacher's Own Mountain

Month 6: Hard Things Take Days

This month's best discovery, made via a slipped folder at the corner: Mr. Okafor is taking a class. (Night school. Spanish. The homework, spotted, dog-eared, red-marked, fell out of his bag at dismissal.)

The exchange, at the desk, carefully: "Sir. Your homework's got more red pen than mine ever gets." And Mr. Okafor, the dog-yeller, the legend, the man who gave her a corner and a budget, laughed and owned it whole: "Three nights a week.

I'm the worst student in the class, the plateau's eating me alive, my accent makes the teacher wince, and last Tuesday I nearly quit in the parking lot." He tapped her folder. "I assign you people hard things all day. Seemed honest to keep one of my own.

A teacher who's forgotten the toll booth teaches like the tolls are free... The hate-the-bike day. In a parking lot. Every mountain has one. Yours come with better coaching, I hear."
