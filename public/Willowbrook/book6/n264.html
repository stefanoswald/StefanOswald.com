Night 264

The Kindest True Thing

Month 9: Truth With Kindness

This month's distillation session, at the lamp: "Mama. Six years of truth rules, the kind and honest, the doors, the time-axis, the true dodge, the asking-hinge, the honesty rule. I think they're all one rule wearing six coats. Listen: Every situation's got a hundred true things in it.

The haircut had 'it's bad' And 'it's sudden' And 'hair regrows', all true. The craft was never true-versus-kind. The craft is: Of all the true things, say the kindest one first. Not instead.

First. The harder truths can follow if they're needed; they follow better behind a kind one. Four words, Mama: Kindest true thing first. That's the whole month. That might be the whole mouth, like, the instruction manual for having one."

(Mama sat with it. The story sat with it. "The instruction manual for having a mouth," Mama said finally. "Four words. Your grandmother would have stitched that on something. Then we should stitch it. The aunts taught me.

The needles are upstairs..." It got stitched. Crooked, gold thread, framed in the kitchen: Kindest true thing first. It hangs there yet. The story checks on it sometimes, the way you check on steady things.)
