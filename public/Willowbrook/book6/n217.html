Night 217

The First Spelling Test

Month 8: School Stories

The first spelling test made half the class nervous. At lunch, Marcus said he felt sick enough to forget every letter he knew.

Olivia shared the tools that helped her. They took three slow breaths. Then they followed the worst possibility to its ordinary ending.

"If you miss several words," she said, "Mrs. Hollis knows which ones to teach again. You practice them. Maybe you take another test."

Marcus frowned. "That is all?"

"That is all. Worry puts a dragon costume on a small problem."

The class's scores varied. Marcus got seven out of ten. More importantly to him, every b faced the correct direction after months of practice.

"The test saw some of what I know," he said.

"One shelf," Olivia replied. "Not the whole library."

He kept the paper, corrections and all, because it showed both what needed work and what had already changed.
