Night 128

The Heron

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

Dawn expedition (the brook's calendar says the good hours; the postholder rises early now by trade), and the reward: The heron. Grey, tall as Papa's chest, standing in the shallows like a folded umbrella. Motionless. Ten minutes: motionless.

Fifteen: one step, slower than the minute hand. Then, faster than seeing, the strike, the silver flash, the swallow, and back to stone. "Papa..." At a whisper. "that's the best waiter I ever saw. Better than the willow, almost.

The tree waits growing. The heron waits hunting, every muscle loaded the whole time and nothing shows. The stillness isn't rest. The stillness is the fastest thing at the brook, holding its breath. Twenty minutes of statue for two seconds of strike..." said Papa. "That's the trade the heron makes every meal of its life... The bean jar counting. The not-filling the pause. The hands-in-pockets at the bridge kids. All my hardest skills are heron skills.

The doing-nofing that's actually the whole doing." The bird fixed them with its gold eye, ruled them harmless, and returned to its work. "I'm gonna come practice with it. The brook's got a stillness professor and office hours at dawn."
