Night 291

The Icicle Piano Duet

Month 10: The Long Winter

The icicle orchestra's final tuned week hosted its first duet: the eave, played four-handed, her mittens on the melody keys, his on "the bass monsters..." The cold sonata's second movement premiered to an audience of one snowman (this year's; the dynasty holds; the carrot ceremonial). The pedagogy, mid-performance: "Soft, Theo, the keys quit if you bang. Soft is the whole instrument.

(Everything this family plays runs on soft, honestly, the lamp, the not-digging, the near-not-wif. The piano's the only loud thing we own and even it does its best work quiet.)" And his contribution, unprompted, mid-tink: "The icicles are the winter's teeth. And we're brushing them. Musically." (they remembered the image unimproved. Some images arrive finished.)
