Night 170

The Hundred Days of Practice

Month 6: Hard Things Take Days

The slot hit one hundred days this week. (Not perfect, the story and the rule both insist on this: two fevers covered by "listening practice" (records, Papa's collection, the wrong-note archive). One vacation stretch done on a paper keyboard, "which is silent piano, which is still piano, the fingers don't know the difference; the fingers just want their walk.") "Faithful, not perfect..."

She confirmed at the board, flagging the century. "The fevers got included. The paper keys counted. Perfect would've died at fever one, perfect's brittle; it was always gonna be faithful or nothing. And Mama, the scales sing now.

A little. The plateau paid out last Tuesday: the C scale ran while I thought about dinner, and the needle'd been moving the whole time, and the doorframe was right again. The doorframe's always right. Growing is invisible daily and enormous annually and the piano is just a doorframe with strings."
