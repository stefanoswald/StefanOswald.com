Night 75

The Rich Kid Question

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

The study's uncomfortable file: The ridge house kid. (The big house. The new bike every season. The kid, Preston, whom the fort's assembled had quietly saved under "has everything.") The check, forced by an accident of benches (he sat down at the park alone; the feet said heavy; the file said impossible; the feet outrank the file): "Everybody thinks the house does the friending for me..."

He said, once the company-or-no was asked and answered. "Kids come over for the pool. Then summer ends. My birthday parties are full and my Saturdays are empty. Nobody just... shows up for the me part."

The fort court, that evening, revised its saves in shame and in policy: "We saved him under has everything without checking the columns. He's got the comfort column full and the company column... Saturdays-empty. Different supplies, Lily.

The money buys one and can't touch the other. We of all people, the second-grid people, should've checked before filing." (The repair: a Saturday. Just showing up. No pool, the fort, the hill, the loaner kite.

The me part, attended. It took three Saturdays for him to believe it. The story understands entirely. Believing is the slowest supply of all to restock.)
