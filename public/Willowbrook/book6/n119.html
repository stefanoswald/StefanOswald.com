Night 119

The Jobs Month, Counted

Month 4: My Jobs

Month's end. At month's end, The easy-to-count part included three posts hunted and held plus one taken deliberately at the bottom of the market plus one that found her. One short count (survived; the latch fixed; the realest lesson). One cart logic.

One succession. One birthday. One pile-up wall. One pay envelope (care in, eggs out, coins after, all three columns fed). Beyond the numbers, there was that chores are rent and posts are where showing-up meets needing.

That the post gets realer on hard days, never easier. That holding isn't hoarding, the post is yours because you made sure it survives you. That the salary is being counted on, and citizen is a counting-on, not an age. And the Duchess, the story lets her have the last countable: Five eggs now.

Nobody explains hens. Some paydays just arrive, three years late, when the right Saturday hands start holding. "Good jobs-month? The realest one. Mama, the whole year's been insides, clocks, kites, ways of working. This month was my insides: what runs me.

Answer: the counting-on. It was always the counting-on. The lamp counts on me to show up and I count on it to be lit and that's the first post I ever held and I've held it my whole life. The family was the training coop all along." Remembered. The family was the training coop. On we go.
