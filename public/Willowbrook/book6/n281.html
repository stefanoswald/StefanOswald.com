Night 281

The Family Interview Tapes

Month 10: The Long Winter

The long winter opened the year's deepest project: The tapes. (The second fat notebook, collecting the parents, upgraded to Papa's old recorder: "voices keep what handwriting can't. The marks keep the words; the tape keeps the person.") Session one, candle-lit, the questions prepared all year: Mama's childhood kitchen (the grandmother's letters; the drawer tradition's origin; "she wrote me one every birthday and I read them still, you knew that.

What you don't know: I answer them. Every year. In my head, at the window. The correspondence never closed"). Papa's first wrong note (age seven; the recital; the freeze; his father's four sentences that founded the dynasty, recorded now, in Papa's voice, breaking slightly at "play it again on purpose and it becomes style").

("The tape got it, Mama. The break in his voice. That's the part no notebook keeps, the place where the telling costs. The archives need the costs kept too. Especially the costs. The costs are where the love shows its receipts.")
