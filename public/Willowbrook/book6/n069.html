Night 69

The Picky Eater Meeting

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

The study hit the lunch table: The food map. (Every kid's eating, charted without judgment, the peas law, scaled up.) The findings: Rosa-No-Relation: no wet-touching-dry ("the foods can be neighbors, not roommates," compartment trays are civilization). Ben: everything, but slowly.

A four-square kid: beige-only ("the beige is safe. The beige never surprised anybody," and his mama, consulted at pickup, says the list grows one color a year; he's added yellow; respect the pace). Ava: kosher rules ("the food's got a whole system, some foods don't mix, some don't come to the table at all. It's not picky.

It's kept. Like the five prayers. The eating is part of the believing"). And her own entry, honestly saved: mushrooms ("the real-gag. Textures are real. The body votes and the vote counts"). The table's new law, drafted by summit: "Nobody polices anybody's plate.

The mouths belong to their owners. Comments cost a cracker." (The cracker tax. It worked. Plate commentary died out in a week, nobody at that table could afford it.)
