Night 328

The Sixth Chain Hangs

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

The season arrived: Almost-six. And the chain, cut since summer, staged all winter, the densest ever. Hung at last. (Eighty-some links, doorframe to doorframe; the tearing begins tomorrow; one word back per morning, the year unspooling into the birthday.)

The hanging ceremony's new element: The apprentice's parallel chain. Theo's first, his almost-five arriving on the same season's heels; the family's birthdays cluster like the family; his links, self-cut with the lefty scissors, self-worded in his campaign-hardened letters: "The ferry. The sheep win. The dock jump.

The big rock..." ("The rock's this summer, Theo, it goes on next year's chain... It goes on this one. I'm manifesting." The family let it stand. The story lets it stand. Manifesting, it turns out, is just the Maybe jar wearing ambition. The rock, for the story, fell in July. The chain knew.)
