Night 237

The Last Month of Five Begins

Month 8: School Stories

The chain's cutting began this week, sixth edition, staged early, because the year's density broke the paper budget: "Mama. The links. I need more strips. The year won't fit." The words, sampled as cut: "The insides year.

The willowhawk. The kite with the cargo. The squadron. The posts. The duchess on my boot. The garden. The marble. The dahlias. The bench. The wall. The gentle brook. The otter (the link just says 'Guarded.'

The chain keeps secrets too)." And the check, hanging it: "Sixty-some links last year. Eighty-plus this year. The years get denser, still both, the years and me, but Mama, look at this year's links: half of them aren't mine.

The garden's everybody's. The squadron's everybody's. The bench is the old ones'. The chain used to be a list of what I did. Now it's a list of what got built, with me somewhere in the crew.

That's... I think that's the whole difference between five and six, right there on paper."
