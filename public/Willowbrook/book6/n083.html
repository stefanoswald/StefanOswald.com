Night 83

The Different Inside the Same

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

The study's final field case: The twins. (Second grade. Identical, coat, face, freckle-map identical. This month's control group, she figured: Same everything. What's left to differ?) Everything, it turned out. The observation log: one twin leads walking to school; the other leads leaving.

One collects; the other races. One's a volume-knob-low; one narrates his own four-square games. Same face. Same house. Same grandmothers. Opposite ways of working. "That's this month's last piece, Lily. I fought different came from different, different countries, different hands, different roads.

But the twins got identical everything and the different is still there, all the way down. The different doesn't come from the outside stuff. The outside stuff just decorates it. The different comes standard. Every person ships with their own approach, from the factory, no two alike, even when the factory ran the same order twice."
