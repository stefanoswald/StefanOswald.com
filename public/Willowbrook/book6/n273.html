Night 273

The Owl's Confession

Month 9: Truth With Kindness

At month's end, gold light gathered in the Great Willow's branches. The truth month, closing, and Oliver Owl, keeper of the tally book these many decades, closed it with a confession the hill had never heard: "The tally book has a gap." The spectacles came all the way off. "Forty years ago.

A night I was... young, for an owl. A storm took the west valley, and the watch's records of it, three pages. I wrote them from the willow's shelter, secondhand, from what the wind told me. I never flew the valley myself.

Too rough. Too scared, colleagues, there. Said. The three pages read as witnessed. They were not. It is the tally book's one gap, and I have carried it forty years, and the small one's month, the mirror sessions, the banked compliments, the locked pages, has made an old bird's carrying look like what it is: a lid that wanted lifting."

The hill held very still. Then Stella, gold and gentle: "The correction, keeper? Entered tonight." The quill moved. "'Pages 1,140-1,142: Secondhand. The keeper was afraid. The fear was reasonable. The mislabeling was not. Corrected, with apology to the story, forty years late.

Late kindness counts. Perhaps late honesty counts double.'" The spectacles went back on. "The tally book is now true. Entirely. It is the lightest this old bird has been since the storm. Mmm," hummed the Moon, who flew the west valley that night, the story knows, and has kept the owl's secret forty years, and hums now the way old friends hum when a carried thing finally sets down.

Goodnight, keepers and confessors of Willowbrook. The truth weighs nothing. Even forty years late, especially forty years late. Sleep light. All of you. Sleep light.
