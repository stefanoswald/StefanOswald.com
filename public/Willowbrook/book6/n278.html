Night 278

Letters by Candlelight

Month 10: The Long Winter

On the fourth snowed-in evening, Olivia discovered that she owed five letters.

She spread paper, envelopes, stamps, and the good pen across the kitchen table. Mama brought cocoa. Candles made the room feel warmer than the thermometer claimed.

Olivia wrote to Jordan about the sound of snow before it falls and the coming garden. She wrote to the aunts, Ms. Halloran, and Dev, who now lived two towns away.

Her final letter went to Mrs. Hollis, only two streets from home.

DEAR MRS. HOLLIS,

SOME LETTERS CROSS DISTANCE. THIS ONE IS TO THANK YOU FOR SEVENTY-THREE FRIDAYS.

YOUR FRIEND,

OLIVIA

"You could tell her on Friday," Mama said.

"I will. The letter is not instead of telling. It is something she can keep after the sound is gone."

Five envelopes waited by the door when the snowplow finally came.
