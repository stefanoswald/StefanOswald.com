Night 261

The Willow Word Speculation Market

Month 9: Truth With Kindness

A phenomenon reached her at the counter this week: The town speculates. (The willow's words are town-famous now. Home, family, growing, kind, read by every hill-walker, and the bakery's under-conversation, Rosa reports, runs a friendly market on word five: "Brave, the crossing guard says. Patient, says Petrov.

Gus-Senior's got two coins on together. Mrs. Hollis declines to bet but smiles like a person who knows.") The keeper's position, held at the counter, flour-dusted: "Everybody's guessing the word they need, Rosa.

The guard guesses brave because he guards. Petrov guesses patient because mending is patience. The guesses are the town telling on its own hearts, it's the best survey anybody ever ran and the tree didn't even mean to run it." A pause.

"And mine stays sealed. The tree says it best. But I'll say this much for the market: everybody's right. Whatever the word is, everybody's guess is somewhere inside it. The tree's words are like that. They hold everybody's."
