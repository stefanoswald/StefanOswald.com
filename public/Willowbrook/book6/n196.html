Night 196

The Marble Returns

Month 7: Team Willowbrook

It happened at the shed, a Tuesday gloaming, the story in its chair: Gus-Senior, browsing the museum box (the marble, the odd hinge, three interesting rocks, the count of finds), and stopping. At the marble. Picking it up with a hand that shook slightly. "... This is a Master Shooter.

Nineteen-forties glass. You found this here? Six inches down, by the east bed..." said Theo, its finder, proud. "A kid gardened here before the before. Wivy says the ground keeps its kids." The old man turned the marble a long time.

"It does..." He said finally. "It kept this one seventy years. I buried it with a spoon the night we moved. So part of me'd stay." He looked at the beds, the barrels, the bench, the whole grown lot.

"Part of me stayed, all right. And look what the rest of you built on top of it." (The marble: His, by unanimous instant ruling of the museum's entire staff. His counter-ruling, made through the barn dust: it stays in the box..."

On loan from the original kid. Permanent loan. A museum needs its founding piece. And I need a reason to keep coming. "he didn't finish. He didn't need to. The bench holds his Tuesdays now. The story holds the rest.)
