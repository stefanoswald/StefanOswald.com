Night 294

The Power Outage Night

Month 10: The Long Winter

The ice storm took the power Thursday, one whole evening, the house on candles, and they remembered it among the year's best nights, as these nights always file: The rules, deployed like the family had drilled (it had, in pieces, for years): the candles (the letter-writing stock; "the light that gets in"). The fort as capital (warmest room; blankets consolidated; the districts united under emergency federation). The radio drama, live, no fan, all mouth-wind. The piano by candlelight ("the Gentle Brook sounds older in the dark, like the song remembering where songs come from").

And the window discovery, at full dark: "Mama. The stars. Without the town's lights there's... Double stars. Triple. The lights we run every night are eating the sky and nobody knows because the eating's always on..."

(The whole family, coats over pajamas, five minutes in the yard's deep cold: the sky at full volume. The always-sound of light, heard by its absence, once, entirely.)
