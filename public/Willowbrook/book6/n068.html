Night 68

The Hearing Aid Kid

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

At story hour: a younger kid, new, with sky-blue hearing aids, and fielding the stares with the weary patience of a five-year-old who's done this tour before. The study's approach (ask what it builds; the method holds): "Those are the best color I ever saw on a ear. Do they turn the world up like a volume knob? What's it build you?"

And the kid. Theo-aged, delighted to be asked right for once, gave the full tour: the aids ("they turn up the parts I want. The teacher wears a little microphone. I hear her over the whole loud room.

You wish you had that"), the batteries, the whistle they make when hugged too hard. And the off switch: "when the world's too loud, I can turn it down. Can you turn the world down?" (She could not.

The envy was genuine and it showed and the kid glowed.) And the memory, surfacing from a Friday tea: "Mrs. Hollis's class learned sign language once. A whole secret class, at recess, for months, for one deaf kid.

Thirty hands sang at the concert." The librarian, shelving nearby: "That kid..." She said. "grew up two streets from here. Teaches at the deaf school upstate now. Sends the library a card every year." (The chains. They never stop surfacing. The town is made of them.)
