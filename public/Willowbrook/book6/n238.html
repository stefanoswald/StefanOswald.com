Night 238

What Six Might Bring

Month 8: School Stories

Before her birthday, Olivia made a list of things she hoped to do at six.

Read from the library's deepest shelf. Play a second year of piano. Manage a full Saturday at the farm. Help Theo move from three-letter words toward chapter books. Bring the neighborhood skill exchange from idea to reality. Bargain for twenty minutes of reading on school nights.

Each item needed more than wishing. The piano required practice. The exchange required follow-through after everyone praised the idea. The farm required Grandpa Joe's trust and Olivia's attention from dawn to closing.

At the bottom she added one more line:

MISS FIVE SOMETIMES.

Five had built the community garden, opened school, and brought a hundred other changes. Becoming six did not require pretending the year before had stopped mattering.

Olivia left room beside every hope for the actual year to surprise her. Lists were maps, not contracts. The best roads always added turns.
