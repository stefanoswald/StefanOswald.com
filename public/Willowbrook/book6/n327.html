Night 327

The Spring Uncovering

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

The uncovering came all at once this year, one warm loud weekend, the whole town's sleeping things waking on the same schedule: The garden (the mulch drawn back; the beds blinking awake; the tulip spears up in the corner bed, "the dahlia couple planted tulips for the early glory too; the September people learned the April trick; everybody teaches everybody here"). The brook (the lid gone, the blblblb public again, the annual sermon's shortest edition, delivered to Theo at the bank: "See? Running. The whole winter.

Told you. You tell me every year. And every year it's true. That's why it's a sermon and not a news"). The kites (the fleet, un-hangared; the first flight of the season; the willow kite's tails needing one repair, the leaf patch's third companion; "the kite's collecting scars like the tree now; they match on purpose").
