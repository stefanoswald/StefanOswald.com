Night 195

The Surplus Stand

Month 7: Team Willowbrook

The surplus got its institution: The stand. (A table at the lot's gate. The signage, hers: "Garden extra. Pay what works. The box takes coins, promises, and trades. The helpers.") The economics, checked weekly, delighting its designer: the box collects coins, trades (eggs, once, circularly, "somebody paid the garden in my own eggs, Theo, the economy's a circle"), and notes.

Mostly notes: "Took tomatoes. Left cookies on your bench. Took beans. Fixed your gate hinge, it squeaked. Took lettuce. Will weed saturday. The new family, number 14." (Biscuit the Third's people. Woven in via salad. The web works every door.)

"The stand runs on warm promises..." The check concluded. "and the promises keep, the hinge got fixed, the Saturday weeding happened. Petrov's columns, Rosa's weather prices, and now the stand: the town's whole other economy, and it clears its books better than the word one. Neighborly trust's got better credit."
