Night 181

Steady's Story

Month 6: Hard Things Take Days

On the final evening, Stella watched from over the Great Willow. And Oliver Owl opened the tally book to an unusual honoree: "The watch typically closes a month on one of its own. Tonight the nomination, hers, relayed through channels..." A nod to Molly, whose channels are the walls. "is for a colleague of the mechanical persuasion: the metronome.

Named Steady. Resident of the piano top. And the citation, in her words: 'It does a thousand invisible answers a minute and never gets a crowd.'" The watch considered its mechanical colleague from afar. "The nomination stands examined..." said the owl. "and the watch finds it sound, because look at the roster, colleagues. What are we? A moon that pulls every tide on schedule, uncrowded. A hedgehog who walks every round, unthanked. A tree that writes one word a year for three hundred years.

A star-keeper who sets one star per child per year, forever. The watch is a metronome. The town keeps its music because something faithful keeps its time, and the faithful timekeepers never get the applause. That was always the arrangement.

The music gets the applause. The time gets the music. Mmm..." Hummed the Moon, in tempo, as it happened. Always in tempo. "Log it..." said Stella. "For Steady, and for every uncrowded faithful thing: the time gets the music.

That's the pay. It was always enough." Goodnight, timekeepers of Willowbrook, brass and bone, celestial and small. A thousand invisible answers a minute. The music is yours. Sleep.
