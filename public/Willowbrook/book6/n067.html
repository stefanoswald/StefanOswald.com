Night 67

The Two Houses Kid

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

Study subject, self-volunteered at lunch: Casey, of the two houses. ("Mama week, papa week. The backpack does the moving. You can put it in the notebook, kids ask wrong about it all the time; maybe the notebook can ask right.")

The right asking, learned by doing: not "which house do you like better". But: "What's the best thing about how yours works?" And Casey, who'd braced for the usual, unbraced: "... Two Christmases, everybody says. But really?

Two goodnights. Papa calls at mama-house bedtime. Mama calls at papa-house. I get the voice and the tucker-inner, every night, both houses. The family didn't get smaller. It got... two addresses." At the lamp, saved with care: "Mama, her family runs different than ours and it runs.

The love found new plumbing when the old plumbing changed. That's this month's whole finding again: the machines matter more than the layouts. Goodnights are goodnights. The address is just... the address."
