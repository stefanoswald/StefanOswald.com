Night 243

The Owl's School News

Month 8: School Stories

When the month's stories were told, the first stars appeared above the Great Willow. The school year, closing, and Oliver Owl, keeper of tally books, delivered the annual education report: "The town ran two schools this year, colleagues, the brick one and the other one. The brick one taught letters, numbers, the water cycle. The story shows it functioned well."

The spectacles came down. "The other school, the one with no building, taught: that repairs compound. That blurs wear hate costumes. That teachers have basements and the soft landing runs upward. That a card requires a name, not an address.

Its faculty included a crossing guard, a librarian, a mender, a baker, a farmer, two retired teachers, one warden of the nothing-hours, and a bench. Its star pupil..." The spectacles glinted. "is also its most prolific junior faculty member, and the distinction stopped mattering some years ago. In the other school, everyone holds both posts eventually.

That is the other school's entire design. And the tuition?" asked Stella, who knows the answer and loves to hear it read in. "Showing up..." said the owl. "Payable daily. Waived for the fevered, by the faithful part.

The other school has never once turned away a student, never once issued a number, and never once closed for snow, the snow days, the story shows, are its finest sessions. Mmm," hummed the Moon, alumnus. Goodnight, both schools of Willowbrook. The other school convenes at dawn, as always. Everywhere, as always. Sleep, class dismissed.
