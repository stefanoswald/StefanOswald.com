Night 302

The Last Snow Angel Count

Month 10: The Long Winter

The season's last snow came wet and heavy and perfect, and the family rite gathered: The angel count, sixth annual. The flock, as censused from the porch: one Papa-angel. One Mama-angel. One Olivia-angel, and the porch measurement stopped there a moment, because the angel was big this year, the wingspan startling, the growth spurt printed in snow: "The shoes knew, Mama.

Now the snow knows. The angel's a doorframe you lie down in." And one Theo-angel: no longer chaos. Deliberate. Even-winged. Signed, with a T, in the corner, one letter, his mark since before he could read it.

("The somersault era's over..." His sister noted, at the porch rail, with the exact both-feelings the story has come to expect of her at the passings. "The angels grow up too. The count keeps the whole flock, every year, every size. That's what the count is for.")
