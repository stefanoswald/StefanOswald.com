Night 46

The Kite Festival

Month 2: The Kite That Wouldn't Fly

The festival, as kept by the story, which flew nothing and saw everything: The hill: fifty kites. The town: underneath, necks craned, the whole sky a argument of colors. Theo's red rocket: High (the boy has one gear; the gear delivered; "Highest four-year-old" is not a category but the judges wrote it on a ribbon anyway because judges know things). Lily's bakery-palette diamond: legitimately gorgeous, third in Most Beautiful, celebrated with rolls.

Wes's stunt kite: looping the loops that started a squadron. Mateo: flying the hawk's sail, re-rigged as a kite..." The Hawk flies every year now, one way or another. "to the town's roaring approval. And the willow kite: Up.

Up into the good numbers, twelve tails streaming, gold on blue, the leaf scar catching sun, four painted words riding the wind over the town that grew them, and holding. Holding. Steady through the gusts, a thousand invisible answers a minute, while below, on the real hill, the real willow's gold branches moved in the same wind, and every person who looked from one to the other felt the thing the story cannot improve upon and so simply reports: the tree, flying. The tree, seeing everything.

Finally. After three hundred years of holding still. Best homemade: the blue ribbon. (The judges' note, kept forever: "For craftsmanship, for the leaf, for the cargo.")
