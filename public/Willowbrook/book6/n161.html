Night 161

The Practice Slot

Month 6: Hard Things Take Days

The lesson's real assignment wasn't the scales, it was the slot: daily practice, and the teacher's warning ("the practice dies without a home; give it an address or lose it by March"). The shape, built per five years of system-craft: fifteen minutes, at the gold hour, after office hours, before the gloaming window ("the day's got steady beams; you bolt new things to them, not near them"). Theo's slot, parallel: his letters at the table while she plays. The metronome: named (Petrov repaired it; it's called Steady; "it does a thousand invisible answers a minute and never gets a crowd, hi, colleague").
