Night 111

The Post Trade

Month 4: My Jobs

An experiment this week, Lily's proposal: The post trade. One week: Lily walks Theo's Wednesday; Olivia takes Lily's bakery Saturday shift. The findings, exchanged at the window seat after: Lily, on the Wednesday: "The walk's not a walk. It's a whole show, the letters, the worries, the firework.

I thought you walked him. You run a program. Also he told me his ladder theory. I'm sworn not to tell. It's good, Olivia. The kid's cooking something." Her, on the bakery: "The counter's not a counter.

It's a listening post with bread. The customers tell the counter everything, the flour's like the lamp, somehow; people talk to a person with dusty hands. Rosa runs the whole town's under-conversation from behind twelve dozen rolls and nobody knows. Your mama's the other Mrs.

Hollis, Lily. The window and the counter. Two watchtowers, one lane." The trade's conclusion, mutual: posts look simple from outside. All of them. Every post is a program wearing a chore's costume.
