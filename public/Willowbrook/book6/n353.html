Night 353

The Maybe Night, Sixth Keeping

Month 12: Almost Six

Night three-fifty-three. (The night. The standing one. Sixth keeping. The story in its usual chair.) the routine: the candle. The jar. The window. The story, the snowplow, six years polished, the parts redistributed by the annual quiet treaty: Papa's blizzard (shorter every year, better every year, "the tellings compress like the years do").

Mama's window. Her snowplow-goose. Theo's "Perfect timing," plus, new, the whole plow soundtrack, which he has decided the story always needed, and which, the family votes with its laughing, it always did. This year's maybes, into the flame: Hers: "Second grade.

The fat-spine era. The word coming in three nights. Maybe it's all happening for me. (The data's six years deep now. But you say it anyway. The saying is the keeping. The keeping is the whole religion.)"

Theo's: "The big rock. This summer. For real this time." ("Maybe it's happening for you..." The family confirmed. The chain already knows. The flame took it smiling, if flames smile. This one seemed to.) And the parents', low, together, the grown-up kind, and one aloud, deliberate, the annual new rule: "Maybe the whole point was always this exact table."

("It was..." said their daughter, six years of evidence behind her. "That one's not a maybe anymore. That one graduated. Put it in the confirmed bundle." They put it in the confirmed bundle. The story witnessed. Some maybes graduate. That's the jar's whole hope, fulfilled.)
