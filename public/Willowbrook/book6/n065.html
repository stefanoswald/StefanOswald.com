Night 65

The Left-Handed Discovery

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

The study found a difference in-house this week: Theo. The crayon. The left hand. Consistently, checked across a week of drawings, "Mama. Theo's a lefty. The whole time. Nobody noticed because nobody checks which hand a scribble comes from."

The world, re-examined from his side: the scissors (built righty; his cuts wander because the tool argues, not the boy). The spiral notebooks. Door handles, ladles, the desk at Miss Dana's..." The whole world's got a secret handedness, Mama, and it's not his.

He's been playing a away game his whole life and never said. "The fixes: lefty scissors. The notebook flipped. And the reframe, delivered at his eye level: "Theo. Lefty means your brain sets up its workshop on the other side.

Same tools, different shop. Grandpa says the best ballplayers are lefties, the world can't predict you. You're not backwards. You're rare. One in ten. I checked. Rare..." He repeated, cutting a perfect circle with the green scissors. "Like the snow leopard."
