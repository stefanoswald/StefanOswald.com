Night 365

The Watch Sets the Sixth Star

Month 12: Almost Six

The last night. The willow. The full watch, assembled over the sleeping town for the year's final session. The minutes, read by Oliver Owl, the tally book true now, entirely, every page: "Year six of the small one on Maple Lane.

Summary: she opened everything, and found attention at the bottom of everything, and named it correctly: the love. She grew a garden from a forty-year nothing and proved the nothing was soil all along. She became the fourth hand in a fifty-year book. She buried a colleague with honors and learned the grief is sized to the love.

She kept a month of fires. The tree wrote true, the condition the other words run on, and a five-year-old built the word a house before anyone knew it would need one. The watch concurs with the tree. The watch always concurs with the tree."

(The tally book, turned. The fresh page. The heading, inked in owl-careful script: Year seven. The last of the first shelf. And beneath it, in smaller script, the old bird's own addition: "The gate year.

All hands.") "Motion to close the book of Five..." said Stella. "Seconded..." said the bear, against the trunk, velvet and ready, one job left, one walk he was made for, one year out. "Carried," hummed the Moon.

And Stella reached into her gold and set the sixth star in the little line above a certain roof, six now, a whole small constellation, bright over a sleeping reader with a bear, a warm key, a hinged box holding true, and a letter promising the biggest year of all. "For the last book of the first shelf..." said Stella. "May it fill true."

The watch stood down. The town slept on. The willow held its five gold words up to the stars, one space waiting, one year to fill it. Goodnight, Willowbrook. Goodnight, book of Five. Goodnight, true girl, six years old, six stars up, the gate year dawning.

See you in the last book. The lamp will be there. It always is. It always, always is. The end of From Five to Six.
