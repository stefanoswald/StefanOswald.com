Night 120

Harry's Rounds Get a Trainee

Month 4: My Jobs

At month's end, Stella settled her glow over Willow Hill. The jobs month, closing, and Harry Hedgehog arrived with news that stopped the tally book: "The rounds..." said Harry. "have a trainee." (The hill went still.

Thirty years, one hedgehog, every night, alone. The watch had stopped imagining otherwise.) "A young one. Found her doing my hedge-line out of order last month, checking the heavy-footed houses on her own, no training, no badge.

Doing it wrong, mind you. Backwards route, missed the fog window entirely." The quills rustled, a hedgehog's laugh. "Same as me, thirty years ago. The rounds don't recruit, colleagues. The rounds get found. Somebody small and warm starts passing close on purpose, and one night the old roundskeeper falls in step and says: 'the fog window's on this street, and here's why, and here's the whole map, and mind the gate at Number Twelve, it creaks.'And the succession?" asked Oliver Owl, quill ready, because the tally book loves a succession.

"Begun. Ten years of training in it, maybe. The rounds take a decade to give properly, every heavy-foot house, every window's story, every leaf worth carrying. But begun." Harry looked down at the town, at all its small lit faithfulnesses.

"The small one below learned it this month in her own words: the post is yours because you made sure it survives you. Log the rounds compliant. Thirty years, and the rounds will survive me. That's not sad, colleagues.

That's the whole point of every post there ever was. Mmm," hummed the Moon, who trains the tides nightly and has never once feared succession. Goodnight, postholders of Willowbrook, day shift, night shift, feathered, quilled, six years old and gaining. The counting-on continues. It survives us all. Sleep.
