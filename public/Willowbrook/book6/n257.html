Night 257

The Grief Truth

Month 9: Truth With Kindness

The lamp held the after-days: "It's a chicken, some kids would say. The sad feels too big for a chicken, Mama. Is the sad wrong-sized?" And Mama, true-voice: "The sad is sized to the love, not the species.

Nine years of Saturdays, four years of slow-and-front, one boot, eleven minutes, the sad is exactly the size of all that. Feelings check true even when the world would mark them down. Never let anybody grade your griefs by the pound." The processing, kit-deployed, honest: the tears (had; sized right).

The remembering game (the peck scale; the detente; the boot, told and retold; "the retelling is the rescue; the stories are where the gone ones keep office hours"). And the old law, arrived at freshly, the way the true ones always re-arrive: "She's not gone-gone. The sweater rule. The compost rule.

The eggs became kids became eggs, half the coop is her line. The farm keeps her the way the ground keeps its kids. Over isn't lost, Mama. The duchess is distributed."
