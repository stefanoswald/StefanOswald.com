Night 207

The Council Thank-You

Month 7: Team Willowbrook

The council closed the civic loop this week: a commendation. (Official. Sealed. Read aloud at the meeting: "...to the Willowbrook Community Garden, its bed-holders, its bench, and its youngest founding petitioner..." the councilman winked at the gallery; the gallery's occupant maintained professional composure until the parking lot.)

The certificate: hung in the shed, beside the honor board, below the trowel. And the question, asked by three separate council members at the reception: "So what's next?" The answer, which she'd been holding since the summer, notebook-drafted: "The bench worked so good the town should check: where else is fifty years of knowing sitting with nowhere to put it? The retired teachers (I know one; she's got forty years and one window).

The retired menders. The mill men. Proposal coming this winter: the town needs a... a knowing exchange. The old ones and the young ones, scheduled together, on purpose. The garden proved the pattern. Now build it everywhere."

(The proposal landed in February. The story will let the far pages tell it, except to note the program's eventual name, voted by its members, over her objection that it was "too much": The Willowbrook bench.)
