Night 323

The Fire Goes Quiet

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

The week after: The quiet. (The spare room, made up and empty. The fire, lit from habit, un-read-to. This month's shape, visible by its absence, absence is data; the oldest finding; it never stops finding.)

The processing, family-wide: Theo's ("Who does the entries now?" answered by institution: the family book, begun that night, his first entry spoken: "Grandpa went home. The fire is cold. I miss the pig story. T."

The almanac's first grandchild, born of missing it). Hers, at the lamp: "this month was a whole extra grandpa, Mama, the daily kind, the fire kind. And now the Saturdays go back to being the whole ration and the Saturdays are... enough, but they're enough the way the ration is enough. I want the abundance back.

Is that greedy? That's not greedy, love. That's love doing its arithmetic. You can want the abundance and honor the ration. Both hands. You know this one."
