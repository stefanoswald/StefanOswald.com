Night 258

The Insurance Question

Month 9: Truth With Kindness

An overheard word this week..." Insurance. "Papa's end of a phone call, the serious voice, and the old rule ran on rails: walked in, asked for the honest size of it. ("Guessing had it house-size by the stairs, the old way.

The new way skips the stairs.") The answer, table-laid, at six-year-old depth by request: insurance is the town's oldest machine wearing paperwork, "everybody pays a little into a jar, always, so that whoever's roof falls gets the jar. Most years you pay and get nothing, and getting nothing is the good year. It's the shared bed, kiddo.

It's the mitten tree with actuaries." The processing: "So the phone call was you... putting our roof in the big jar. Not a emergency. A un-emergency, on purpose, paid monthly. Then the serious voice was just... the voice grown-ups use for jars they hope to never open.

Okay. Saved. Papa, the whole grown-up world is jars, isn't it. The bank, the insurance, the taxes-thing you grumble at. Jars all the way up. Jars all the way up," Papa confirmed. "And the grown-up secret is the same as the kid one: check what the jar's for before you grumble at the filling of it."
