Night 14

The Gear Ratio Revelation

Month 1: How Things Work

The bike went upside down this week and became a laboratory: pedal turned slow by hand, back wheel spinning fast. "One pedal-turn makes... Three wheel-turns? Where do the extra turns come from? You can't get free turns.

The clock taught me, everything pays." Papa's chalk, on the driveway: the big gear pulls the little gear; the little gear must spin three times to keep up; the wheel rides the little gear. "You don't get free turns. You trade.

Three easy pushes for one hard one, or one hard push for three fast turns. Gears don't make power. They exchange it. Every machine is a currency exchange." The hill test, right side up: low gear up, high gear down. The legs, learning the tally book.
