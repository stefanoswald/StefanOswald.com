Night 309

The Entry About the Lost Crop

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

Night's entry: "July 20, 1996. Hail. Twenty minutes. The year's work on the ground. And underneath, added later, different ink: Winter came. We ate. The valley saw to it." The story: the hail year. The whole harvest, flattened between lunch and milking.

The winter that followed, and the different-ink line's cargo: the anonymous hams. The feed that appeared. The seed money, spring, in an envelope, no name, "in handwriting I knew and never once let on I knew, because the not-letting-on was the point, the dignity engineering; you know the work; your month of detectives found it. The valley ran it on us that whole winter and nobody ever spoke of it at church.

The boots man..." She said softly. "The envelope case. The weather adjustments. Grandpa, it's the same machinery. Fifty years back. Different valley. Same machinery everywhere there's people, darlin'. It don't get invented. It gets inherited, and it gets inherited by whoever had a winter where the valley saw to it. I've filled a few envelopes since. Handwriting disguised. The wheel goes around."
