Night 304

The Moon's Long Watch

Month 10: The Long Winter

On the last visit of the month, Stella settled her glow over Willow Hill. The long winter, closing, and the watch, having heard the Moon speak once this year already, was astonished twice: "The long winters..." said the Moon. "are mine. The summer nights are short.

I barely clock in. But the deep winter... sixteen-hour shifts, colleagues. I have kept more long winters than the tally book has pages. And I will tell the watch what the long ones hold, because the small one below found it this month and the finding deserves a second witness:" The hill leaned in.

The snow held still. "The long dark is where the town does its true building. The summer builds what shows, gardens, kites, walls taken. The winter builds what holds: the tapes of the parents' voices.

The skill that crossed a snowed street. The fort where a brother and sister governed three blanket rooms and learned each other whole. I watch it all through the windows, sixteen hours a night, and the windows in deep winter are the most honest windows of the year. Gold squares in the blue dark, each one holding a family building what holds."

A pause, moon-wide. "The calendar calls them the dead months. I have watched ten thousand of them. Nothing dies. Everything roots. Tell the calendar it has it backwards. Tell everyone. Logged," said Oliver Owl, quill steady.

"'Nothing dies. Everything roots. The calendar has it backwards.'Mmm," hummed the Moon, going back to its shift. Gold squares in the blue dark. Goodnight, deep-winter builders of Willowbrook. Nothing dies. Everything roots. Sleep. The thaw is ticking. The bird has somewhere to fly.
