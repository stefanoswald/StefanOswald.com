Night 144

The Almanac's Water Pages

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

Deep-winter farm Saturday, coop held, and after: the fire, and the water pages. (The almanac, mined for this month: fifty years of rain tallies, well depths, the brook's moods in Joe's capitals.) The treasures, surfaced: the drought of his young-family years ("The well held. Barely.

Dug the stock pond that august. Hardest summer of my life, darlin', and one line. The almanac keeps the facts; the man keeps the weight"). The flood spring ("Margaret's garden drowned. She replanted in june saying 'The water gets one vote, not the whole election'", the story pauses; the family's entire philosophy, one wet June, one grandmother).

And the oldest water entry, his father's hand: "Spring ran all drought. Never failed yet. 1947. Never failed yet..." She read. "The source. The dinner plate. Seventy-plus years of never failed yet. Longer. That spring's the reason the farm's here.

My grandfather chose the land for it. The whole family, darlin', every egg, every catalog, every porch hour, sits on top of water that never failed yet."
