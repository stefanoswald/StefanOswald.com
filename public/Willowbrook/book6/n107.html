Night 107

The Lost Dog Assignment

Month 4: My Jobs

The lane's Tuesday crisis: Lost dog. (Flyers on poles by breakfast, the new family at Number Fourteen; a spaniel named Biscuit..." Another Biscuit; the town's on its third; the name has a dynasty. "gone since dawn, gate latch old, wind strong. As it happened the town's latches are a recurring villain and Petrov can only be everywhere eventually.)

The response, organized by the kid the town now semi-officially organizes around: The grid search. (Agency methods, publicized: the town map, the bilingual masterwork, third edition, squared off; searchers paired; routes assigned no-backtrack, cart-logic; the dog's habits interviewed from the family first, "search the dog's head, not the map: where would biscuit go? He's new. New ones go toward the familiar.

What's familiar? The moving van route. The old smells. Check the roads they drove in on.") Found: two hours, the creek bend along the van route, muddy, delighted, entirely unrepentant. The reunion: the crying kind. The latch: fixed by Thursday.
