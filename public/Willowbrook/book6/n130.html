Night 130

Cleaning the Brook Bend

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

On Tuesday, Olivia, Lily, and Mateo found a torn rubbish bag at their favorite bend in the brook. Bottles and wrappers had caught among the reeds, and an old tire sat half in the water.

They told their families, gathered gloves and litter grabbers, and returned with a wagon on Saturday. The tire required all three children and one great deal of muddy argument.

"Worst egg we ever collected," Mateo said as it finally rolled free.

The brook looked itself again, but Olivia was not satisfied with cleaning it once. She called the town office.

"Could the bend have a bin?" she asked. "People should still carry their rubbish home, but a bin makes the right choice easier."

A covered bin appeared before summer, with a small sign about protecting the water.

The bend stayed clean. Part of the credit belonged to the children who gave up a Saturday. Part belonged to a better place for bottles than the reeds.
