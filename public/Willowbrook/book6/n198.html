Night 198

The Weed Philosophy Story

Month 7: Team Willowbrook

Weeding hour, shared bed, three generations on knees, and this month's deepest session, sprouted from Theo's law: "Theo says weeds are only weeds if you got no job for them. But Grandpa, who decides? The dandelion's a weed in the lettuce and a crop in his corner. The mint's a herb in a pot and a invasion in a bed.

Scout was almost pulled as a weed and he was the best sunflower of the year. The plant never changes. Just... the map around it." Joe worked a thistle loose, considering. "Fifty years, and here's where I landed: a weed's just a plant where your plan is.

The plant's got its own plan, they all do; they're all just growing where the falling put them. Weeding's not a judgment on the plant. It's you standing up for your plan, gentle as you can manage. And every so often..."

The thistle went in the bucket, but the volunteer sunflower by the fence stayed, unremarked, protected all season by common consent. "every so often the plant's plan is better than yours. The skill is telling which. Fifty years, darlin'. Still learning which."
