Night 57

The Kite Retires for the Season

Month 2: The Kite That Wouldn't Fly

Season's turn: the winds going autumn-restless, school looming, the hill's summer chapter closing. And so, with care: The retirement ceremony. (Seasonal. The kite flies again in spring, but this family closes chapters properly or not at all.)

The willow kite: cleaned, checked (spar: strong at the break; patch: proud; bridle: locked at the found finger-width), and hung on the hangar wall beside the Hawk's sail. The twelve tails: coiled. The blue ribbon's spot: empty on purpose ("the ribbon lives in the bark. The kite knows where its ribbon is.

That's enough for both of them"). And the hangar plaque: "The Willowbrook air fleet. The willowhawk: Flew three seconds, crashed glorious, flies again every festival. The willow kite: Carried the tree's words over the town.

Both built by hand. Both broke. Both fixed. Both better for it. The squadron. Goodnight, fleet," said the ceremony's junior officer, saluting with the wrong hand, entirely correctly.
