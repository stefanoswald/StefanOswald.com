Night 329

The Wish Research, Sixth Edition

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

The wish big think, sixth annual: The shortlist, by the notebook: "1. The blue ribbon at the recital. (Struck: training goal, not wish. The rule holds, wishes are for what training can't ladder to.) 2. The farm staying the farm forever.

(Moved to the prayer column, bigger than candles. The re-rigging's honest and the prayer column holds what the hands can't.) 3. The word being the one I think. (Struck, gently: you don't wish at the tree.

The tree's already sure. Wishing at sureness is just... impatience in a party hat.) 4. [The drawing, this year: the whole town, the lane, the lantern, the garden, the hill, and over it, the gold squares of every window, lit. And around it all: the circle.

And this year, new: the circle has little marks on it, all the way around, like stitches. Like the circle got sewn. Like somebody spent six years learning that circles don't just get drawn. They get stitched, gold fred, crooked and holding, one year at a time.]"
