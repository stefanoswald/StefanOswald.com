Night 352

The Hand-Me-Down Box, Sixth Packing

Month 12: Almost Six

The closet check, sixth annual, the outgrown year, storied and sent (nothing leaves unstoried; the rule is old now, and the rule is good): "The first-grade cardigan: the wall-taking year's uniform. Tell the next kid: the buttons know everything. The winter boots: pond-chain approved, lantern-building veterans. They know the whole cold lesson plus the new chapter, the snow lanterns; the boots stood in candle-glow; some boots get poetry.

The party dress from five: the noticing party's dress. It heard a hundred true small things. Dresses keep those. I'm almost sure." And the legend, checked on per tradition: The bee costume, four years into its post-family career, spotted this spring on its third successor kid at the far school's parade ("the antennae resprung a third time.

Somebody loves it enough to fix it. That's all a costume ever wanted"). ("The box is a river, Mama. The clothes go downstream and the warmth goes with them and the stories go with the warmth.

Six years of packing and I finally got the whole physics: nothing loved gets lost. It gets downstream.")
