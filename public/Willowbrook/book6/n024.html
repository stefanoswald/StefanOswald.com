Night 24

The Compost Engine

Month 1: How Things Work

Farm day, and the lens found the humblest machine on the property: The compost bin. ("The rot box. The... Grandpa, why do you keep garbage in a house?") Joe turned the pile with the fork, and the inside steamed.

Working. "Not garbage. Groceries, halfway back. Everything that falls, peels, stalks, leaves, last year's whole garden, the breakdown crew takes it apart. Worms, bugs, things smaller than seeing. They unbuild it all the way down to soil.

And the soil builds the next garden. This bin is where endings get turned back into beginnings. Slowest, most important machine on the farm." The long look into the steam. "So nothing on the farm actually ends.

It just... changes departments. The sweater-hat rule. The snowman rule. The row that distributed. It's not a nice idea, Grandpa, it's equipment. The farm built the rule a house. Every farm does. The rule needs housing. That's half of farming: keeping the rule's machines running."
