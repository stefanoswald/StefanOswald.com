Night 190

The Night Watch Rotation

Month 7: Team Willowbrook

The vandal response, phase two, and as it happened she vetoed the fence-and-lock proposals first: "A garden behind locks isn't a town garden. It's a town safe. The whole point dies in there. Lock-things invite lock-breakers.

Attention-things invite attention." The alternative, organized in one bench session: The attention watch. Gus-Senior's window (he's watched the lot for decades; now it's official; he got a logbook and the title "Warden of the Nothing-Hours" and, later, took it more seriously than any job of his working life). The evening dog-walkers, rerouted past the lot by request ("dogs are attention with legs").

The porch lights of the three nearest houses, re-aimed a few degrees. And the squadron's dusk flights moved lot-adjacent. (No second vandal night. Ever. Sure enough across all far pages: the lot was held by nothing but aimed attention, for decades.

"Locks guard things..." The notebook ruled. "Attention guards places. Places are things plus love. Love needs witnesses, not walls.")
