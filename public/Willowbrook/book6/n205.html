Night 205

The Garden Book

Month 7: Team Willowbrook

The season got its book (everything gets its book; the story long ago stopped noting this as unusual and started calling it the town's luck): The garden annual, volume one. Compiled at office hours, bound in yarn, shelved in the shed beside the marble: Contents: the proposal (the original one page, "the founding document; every thing that grows has a seed and this was the lot's"). The bed map. The bench faculty's collected sayings (Gus-Senior's olla lore; the elder Alvarez's bean spacing; Joe's "wasn't the soil that changed..."

Attributed, dated). The vandal night and the fifty who came. The zucchini rules. The dahlia lesson. The frost race. And the last page, for volume two's authors, whoever they turn out to be: "This book is a relay.

The garden outlives its gardeners. That's the whole design. Write your year. Leave the book better. The ground keeps its kids and the shelf keeps its seasons. Volume one, with love."
