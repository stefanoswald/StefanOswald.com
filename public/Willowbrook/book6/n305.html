Night 305

The Almanac Month Opens

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

The month began with a suitcase: Joe, come to town. (The farm's quiet season; the arthritis's counsel; the family's standing invitation, finally taken: a month of town-living, the spare room, the fire. And the almanac, carried in both arms like the relic it is.) this month's format, proposed by its beneficiary and agreed by its source: One entry a night. ("Fifty years in there, Grandpa.

This month's got thirty nights. You pick the entries. I keep the questions. The fire does the rest.") Night one's entry, read in his farmer's capitals by firelight: "April 12, 1974. Planted in mud against advice. Lost the whole row.

The advice was mine, to myself, ignored. The first entry I ever wrote, darlin'. Started the whole book to catch my own ignored advice. Fifty years on: it caught plenty."
