Night 87

The Mirror Page

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

The study's last interview: Herself. By family panel. The panel's findings, delivered at dinner, her cheeks doing the thing: Papa: "You can't leave a system unimproved. Doors, games, lunchrooms, you see the better version instantly and your hands start moving.

It's a gift. It's also..." Kindly; the panel was kind. "why the not-fixing is your lifelong hill. The seeing never turns off." Mama: "You collect people the way collectors collect anything: because forgetting them feels like losing something the world needs kept.

Your different is that you appointed yourself the keeper. Nobody assigned it. Age two, you were already doing it." And Theo, tenderhearted, going last and going deepest the way he does: "Your different is you make everybody feel like their different is the good kind.

That's why everybody tells you everything. You're the... you're the place where people put demselves for keeping." (The place where people put themselves for keeping. The story, which knows exactly what she becomes; which has always known, saves the five-year-old's telling as the most accurate forecast in the entire series and says no more about it.)
