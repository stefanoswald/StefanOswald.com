Night 189

The Vandal Night

Month 7: Team Willowbrook

The hard page came Thursday: Vandals. (Overnight. Two beds trampled, a barrel tipped, the class bed's stakes snapped. No thieving, just the pointless kind of breaking. The story has no explanation. The story never does for this one.

Some breakings are just somebody's alarm going off in the wrong direction.) The morning's grief: real, communal, standing-in-the-wreckage. And then the thing this month was built to prove: By noon, the lot was full. Word ran the lane and the town came, re-staking, re-righting, re-planting from the bench faculty's own seed stashes.

The damage, assessed at dusk: two weeks of growth lost. The gain, assessed at the same dusk, by the girl on the bench: "Thirty-one people came to the first workday, Mama. Fifty came to the breaking. The town shows up harder for a wound than a party.

That's... that's the whole secret grid, visible, in one afternoon. The vandals tested the wrong garden."
