Night 23

How the Piano Remembers

Month 1: How Things Work

Duet night, and the piano's top came open (Papa's idea; the insides year has infected everyone): the hammers, watched while playing. Every key: a lever. Every lever: a felt hammer leaping at a string. The song, visible, a whole small factory of soft mallets, dancing.

"Play the wrong note..." She demanded. "I want to see where it lives." He played it, the beloved one, the thirty-one-year one. The hammer leapt. The string sang the wrongness. And the revelation: "It's a real string, Papa.

The wrong note isn't a mistake floating in the air, it's a hammer and a string, same as every right note. The piano doesn't know it's wrong. The piano plays everything with the same whole heart. The wrong is just... a address.

We decided that address is wrong. The piano never voted. Thirty-one years..." said Papa. "and you just found the actual philosophy. The piano plays everything with the same whole heart. That's why the wrong notes could become mine. The instrument never judged them. Only the sheet music did."
