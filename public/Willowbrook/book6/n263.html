Night 263

The Hard Case Returns

Month 9: Truth With Kindness

The dark file's old subject resurfaced this week: the kid who weaponized differents (the stutter-mimic, the chair-blocker, reported in stacks, helped upstream, two years back). At the court. Asking, awkward, sideways, the way the re-entering ask, to play. The court's memory: Long.

The kids' faces: doors, closing. And the umpire's call, this month's last big one: "He plays. Pairs draft, he's with me." And quietly, to the objectors, after: "The stack worked. The upstream help landed, you can see it; the hooks are out of his laughing; watch him a whole game before you keep the door shut.

The truth about people isn't a photograph, remember. Kids happen forwards. If the court won't let a fixed thing be fixed, then the fixing was pointless and the stacks are pointless and the whole believing-in-better is pointless, and I've checked, and it's not." (He played.

Clean. Nervous. Clean. By month's end: a regular. By next year: the kid who taught the little kids four-square, gently, hooks nowhere. Sure enough across all far pages: the fixing held. They mostly hold, when the door reopens. The doors are half the fix.)
