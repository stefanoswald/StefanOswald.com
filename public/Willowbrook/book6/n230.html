Night 230

The Snow Day Governance

Month 8: School Stories

The big snow came, two feet, school-closing, historic, and the park became, for three days, fort city: the whole kid-town, building. And someone saw five years of systems self-organize in the drifts: Fort together. The castle rule (two rival crews, pre-treatied via the wall-between law, "castles, not wars; the target range settles disputes; best of five"). The loaner shovels.

The hand-chain runs to the sled hill. The little-kid district (Theo, mayor by acclamation, governing with dandelion-farmer benevolence: "Everybody builds. Nobody wrecks. Snacks at the big fort"). And the warming rotation. Rosa's counter, cocoa at cost, the frozen rotated indoors on a schedule somebody chalked on the bakery window.

(Somebody. The story smiles. The somebody wall's oldest author remains at large, as ever.) "Free days of city..." She reported at the lamp, thawing. "Nobody assigned anything, Mama. The systems just... Stood up, out of everybody who'd ever used them.

That's the whole test of five years of building: the builder wasn't needed. The city ran itself."
