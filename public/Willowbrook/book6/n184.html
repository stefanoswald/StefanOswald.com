Night 184

The Bed Assignment Diplomacy

Month 7: Team Willowbrook

The beds got assigned this week, and as it happened the diplomacy was pre-built, because its architect has been building fairness machines since the roll wars: the way things worked: sign-up, first-come for quantity but lottery for location ("the corner beds get sun; everybody wants the corners; wanting-order shouldn't beat luck-order for the things nobody earned, the dice got no memory and no favorites; that's exactly why they're fair"). The family beds, the solo beds, the class bed, the shared bed ("for whoever's passing, the whoever's-cold part, in dirt. You can weed it or eat from it, either, no tally book"). One dispute: settled by the cutter-chooses-last principle, garden edition, "you two draw the bed lines between your plots.

Whoever draws, the other one picks sides first." (The lines came out generous to the inch. The pattern works. It always works. Nobody has to be good when the rule does the goodness.)
