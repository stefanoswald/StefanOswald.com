Night 333

The Almost-Six Check

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

The birthday season opened with the year's grand check, the chain half-torn, the words coming back morning by morning, and the whole insides year weighed at the lamp: "The year opened a clock, Mama, and never stopped opening things: the bread. The kite. The posts. The brook.

The ways of working (the factory only makes originals). The practice (the underground takes everything eventually). The garden (the caring was in the line). The truth. The long winter. And the almanac, the inside of time itself, and the finding at the bottom of all the insides, every single one: The attention is the love.

The whole year, one sentence. You look close enough at anything, a zipper, a hen, a grandpa, a town, and the mechanism at the very bottom is always somebody paying attention on purpose. That's the gears of everything. I opened everything and it was attention all the way down.

And tomorrow the tree, which has been paying attention longer than anybody...says its word. Yeah." The grin, arriving slow and sure. "I know what I think it is. The gold month runs first, and at its end, the surest writer in town settles it." Remembered. Attention all the way down. The gold month opens.
