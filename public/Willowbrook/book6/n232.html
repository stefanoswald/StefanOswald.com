Night 232

The Spring Concert Solo

Month 8: School Stories

The spring concert brought the year's stage summit: the music teacher, hearing about the recital through channels (the town has no other kind of channel), asked her to accompany, "The Gentle Brook," adapted, played under the class's water-cycle song (her own play's anthem, recycled; the loops close; they always close). The performance: the gym, the families, twenty-two voices over eighty-eight keys. One near-train-wreck (verse three, the class rushing, the piano holding tempo like Steady taught..." The accompanist is the metronome with feelings; you don't chase the singers; you hold, and they find you. "they found her).

And the wrong note: claimed, looped, made a feature, family-grinned to row three where the whole dynasty sat, three generations of wrong-note holders, if you count the bear, and they counted the bear.
