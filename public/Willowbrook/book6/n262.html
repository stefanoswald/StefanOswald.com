Night 262

The False Modesty Check

Month 9: Truth With Kindness

A self-catch this week, saved for its rarity: a neighbor praised her piano, and she deflected: "oh, I'm not really good, I just..." and stopped. Mid-sentence. Hearing it. The check, run on the spot: "Papa.

That was a lie. A polite one, a tiny one, but I've had a hundred days of practice and a recital and a concert, and 'I'm not really good' is just... False. Why does the mouth do that?

The compliment comes and the mouth frows it back like it's too hot..." Papa, from the bench: "Because somewhere kids learn that owning your good is bragging. It isn't. Bragging is spending your good at people, the puffed thing, the gloat.

Owning it is just... accurate books. Try: 'thank you. I've practiced a hundred days and it's starting to show.' True, humble, and the practice gets its credit, which it earned. False modesty stiffs your own hundred days. They worked hard. Pay them."
