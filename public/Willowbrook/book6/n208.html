Night 208

The Winter Planning Bench

Month 7: Team Willowbrook

The bench went indoors for winter (Number Nine; Mrs. Hollis hosts; the faculty migrates like sensible birds): The planning sessions. Fridays, extended. Tea, catalogs, the annual's volume one consulted like precedent. Next season's list, drafted by firelight: the fourth barrel.

The bee treaty formalized (a pollinator bed, all flowers, "the staff gets a wing"). The question row's town edition expands (votes taken; the winner, overwhelmingly: Pumpkins, "the town wants pumpkins; the town shall have pumpkins; democracy is occasionally very easy"). Theo's bed: doubling. And Gus-Senior's motion, carried unanimous, tea raised: "That the lot's gate get a small sign, reading: Forty years of weeds was just the soil getting ready.

Because somebody said something like it in June, and an old man's been chewing on it since, and it's the truest thing he ever heard said about waiting."
