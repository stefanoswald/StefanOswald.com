Night 106

The Homework Post

Month 4: My Jobs

A quiet post grew at the office-hours table this week: Marcus, Wednesdays after the walk, invited for homework alongside. (His letters still climb their uphill; first grade raised the grade; his mama and hers arranged the table-share the way mamas arrange the steady things, over one phone call, in four minutes.) The method, learned by immediate hard experience: "The first day I basically did his sheet, Mama. It felt like helping.

It was stealing, the bridge rule; I know better; the fix I hand him isn't his." The correction, self-issued by day two: the bed trick, re-drilled. The finger under the words, his finger. The waiting, the runway rule, ported from Ben, while his sounds assembled themselves at their own dignity.

Her pencil: Down. Her job: the sitting-near, the confirming, the one hint when the wall was real ("gear down, check the neighbors, the sentence around the word is the free clue"). By month's end: his sheet, his. Slower than hers.

Entirely his. "The uphill's still his uphill. But he's got a table now, and a table changes a hill's whole weather."
