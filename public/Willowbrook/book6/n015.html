Night 15

The Two-Part Story Begins: The Nest

Month 1: How Things Work

The bear's letter said the two-part stories start this year. This week, the first one arrived, in the porch eaves: a nest. Robin-built, mud-and-grass engineering ("She's a detail person, look at the mud work, Mama, the inside's smooth"). And inside, when the porch chair dared the angle: Four eggs.

Blue as the exact best blue. The watch: established instantly. (Distance rules, by the bird book: "watchers don't loom. The porch corner, the quiet, the brass glass at full zoom.") The mother: back and forth, patient, warming her four blue maybes.

And at the lamp, the new discipline, the one the letter forecast: "The story doesn't end tonight, Mama. The eggs take days. Weeks maybe. I have to go to sleep without the ending. The nest is a... a chapter book the porch is reading me, one page a day, and nobody can skip ahead.

Not even the robin." Not even the robin. Sleep, watcher. The story runs long. That's not the story breaking.
