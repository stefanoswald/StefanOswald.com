Night 312

The Entry She Wasn't Supposed to Find

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

The book fell open Tuesday to a page Joe reached to turn past, and didn't, because her eyes had already caught it, and this family's law is the rule: "March 1999. Margaret sick. The word the doctor used. I wrote it and crossed it out.

The crossing-out didn't help. Nothing in this book helps. Fifty years of knowing what to do and there's nothing to do." The fire burned a while on its own. "Honestly?" she asked, finally, gently, her own law, offered back.

"Honestly..." He said. "Two years of that ink. The book near ended, you can see the gaps; a man who logs everything, logging nothing, because nothing felt worth catching. And then..." He turned pages; the entries resumed, thin, then steadier.

"April 2001. Planted the row anyway. She'd have had my hide else. The first entry after. The row anyway. Darlin', that's the only wisdom the worst pages hold, and it's the whole of it: The row anyway.

You plant it not because the grief's done, it's never done, but because the not-planting doesn't honor anybody. She wanted both, remember. The whole life. The whole one includes the after. I'm still in the after. The rows still go in."
