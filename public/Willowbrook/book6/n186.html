Night 186

Water for the Garden

Month 7: Team Willowbrook

The community garden had no tap, and summer beds needed water every day.

The nearest spigot belonged to the library, across the fence. Olivia, Mateo, Lily, the librarian, and Gus-Senior gathered to find a better answer.

Mr. Petrov donated three rain barrels. With permission, a gutter carried water from the library roof into them.

"Libraries water minds," the librarian said. "This one may water beans too."

Families signed up to carry buckets to their own beds. The shared plot rotated among volunteers, and Olivia's crew covered any missed day.

Gus-Senior added buried clay pots that released water slowly near the roots. His wife had learned the method from her mother in a much drier place.

By July, rain from the library roof was feeding tomatoes, pumpkins, and the free bed. The answer had not come from one clever child. It came from many people bringing the piece they knew.
