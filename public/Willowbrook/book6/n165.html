Night 165

The Scarf at Last

Month 6: Hard Things Take Days

Five weeks of wool: The scarf, cast off. (Honest assessment, delivered at the needles: "One end's wider than the other. There's a hole from row ten I patched with the leaf-patch spirit. The color's the willow gold.

It's the worst scarf the aunts' Dynasty ever produced and I made every stitch of it and I know where all eleven mistakes live.") The destination, decided before the last loop: The mitten tree. ("The first-of-anything rule says the first is for learning. But the drawer rule says the give should cost.

Both rules point the same way: the first scarf goes to the tree. Some kid gets the warmth and the mistakes, and the mistakes are the warm part, honestly. Machine scarves don't carry anybody's five weeks.")
