Night 136

The Well Visit

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

The councilman (the bench; the bin; the fountain petition, the town office has a file on her now, the good kind) arranged it: a well-house tour. The town's water, met at its source. The tour's wonders, logged: the pump house hum ("the town's fridge-hum, the always-sound nobody hears; hi, always-sound; I hear you"). The depth: the well drops past all the digging anybody's ever done..."

Down to the aquifer. A underground lake, Papa. Under the whole town. Under the school and the bakery and the willow's hill, we've been walking on a lake my whole life. "The age of the water: the engineer's jaw-dropper, some of it fell as rain centuries ago, filtering down slow, "so the tap might pour rain that fell on the willow when it was a sapling..."

(She held her next cup of water for a long minute before drinking it. The story understood entirely.)
