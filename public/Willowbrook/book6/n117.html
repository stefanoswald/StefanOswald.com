Night 117

The Councilman's Question

Month 4: My Jobs

Civic week at school brought a visitor: an actual town councilman, asking: "What does our town need, kids?" The hand. The veteran hand. And the plan, delivered from the accumulated saves of a five-year career: "Three things, sir, from field data.

One: a latch count. Eleven flagged on our lane alone, the lost dogs and the swung gates all come from latches; Mr. Petrov can't be everywhere; the town should hire the fixing. Two: the crossing guard's corner needs a bench, the little kids wait standing and the old ones don't come at all because there's nowhere to sit; one bench buys the corner a whole new population.

Three: the mitten tree works but it's winter-only. Needs a summer cousin, a water fountain by the ballfield; the hot days have their own cold hands, if you follow." (The councilman, later, stopped smiling the crayon smile at "field data" and started writing at "latch count." The bench went in by spring, the plaque, small, reads "Requested by the students."

The fountain took two years and a petition, hers, of course, with signature-gathering run on route logic. Democracy, she reported at the lamp, "is just the suggestion box with longer cooking times. Crockpot governance. The jar rules apply.")
