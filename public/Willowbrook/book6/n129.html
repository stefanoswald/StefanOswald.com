Night 129

The Brook in the House

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

This month came indoors this week, via the tap: "Mama. The faucet water. Is it... Related to the brook?" The trace, run with Papa's diagrams and one call to the town office (she calls town offices now; the councilman took the call personally; the bench plaque has consequences): the town's wells, drinking from the ground's own dark..."

The same dark the spring rises from. The brook and the tap are cousins. The hill exhales in two directions: one makes the brook, one fills the wells, and the house drinks from the same underground everything. "The ceremony that followed: the tap, thanked at dinner.

("We say gloaming at the window and goodnight to the lamp. The tap carries the hill's dark gift through the wall into my cup and nobody's ever once said thanks. The always-things go invisible. Not in this house. Not anymore.")
