Night 92

The Chicken Post

Month 4: My Jobs

Post one found her at the farm: Saturday chicken duty. Full charge. Joe's standard. ("Not helping. Holding. Helping is hands. Holding is the whole worry, the feed math, the water check, the egg count, the head count, the gate.

The birds are yours on Saturdays now. I'm just the appeals court.") The routine, learned to standard: feed scattered wide. Water: checked, hauled, topped. Eggs: gathered, counted, logged in the almanac's coop column ("the chart talks if you keep it.

Thursday hens lay less; why? Saved. Attention, banking"). The head count: All, every time..." Because the count is the whole job, a farm's just counting things that matter, daily, forever. "And the Duchess, at week's end, accepting her scatter with the old stare, and then, witnessed by Joe from the appeals bench: laying, for the first time in nearly three years, one egg.

(Joe looked at the egg a long time. "Well," he said. And that was the whole speech, and they remembered it as one of the great ones.)
