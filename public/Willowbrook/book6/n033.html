Night 33

Day Two: The Blame Phase

Month 2: The Kite That Wouldn't Fly

Day two: failure again. And with it, as it happened honestly, the blame phase: "The string's cheap." (The string was fine.) "The wind's wrong." (The wind flew two other kites that afternoon. Visible. Mocking.) "The tail's too long.

Or short. The instructions lied." (The instructions, consulted by Lily, said, in diagram form, the thing neither of them wanted to hear: the bridle. The little string triangle where the line meets the kite. Angle-critical.

Fiddly. Skipped on day one because it looked pre-set.) "... It's not the wind, is it..." She said finally, wind in her hair, other kites overhead. "It's the fiddly part we skipped..." said Lily. "The fiddly part we skipped." this month's first law, paid for in two days of blame: "You always blame the wind first.

The wind is big and can't argue. The real problem is usually small, close, and yours."
