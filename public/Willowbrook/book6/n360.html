Night 360

The Thanking of Five

Month 12: Almost Six

The last night. The thanking, sixth annual, the routine hers whole: "Okay, Five. Last night. Everything out loud: Thank you for the insides, the clock that started it, the gears, the breves, the whole opened world.

The Hawk's three seconds and the Hawk 2's nine. The kite month, the blame phase, the half-inch hinge, the tree flying at last. The posts: the coop, the cart, the Wednesdays, the counting-on. The garden.

Five, you grew a garden out of a parking lot of weeds; that's the biggest thing either of us ever did. The wall, taken on schedule. The hundred days of piano. The long winter's whole lobby.

The fire month. Grandpa's fifty years, opened one night at a time; the fourth hand; the seed stock. The Duchess..." The pause; the bear pulled closer; the weights get thanked too; that's the whole rite. "thank you for the boot.

Thank you for eleven minutes. Thank you for teaching me the grief is sized to the love and the love was big." The breath. The finishing: "You were the openest year, Five. The year everything showed me its gears.

Go be a was-year. The frame keeps your line. The folders keep your pictures. The box, the new box, the hinged one, waits for tomorrow's word. And I keep... all of it. All of you. Nigh-nigh, Five."
