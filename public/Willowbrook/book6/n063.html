Night 63

The Two Birthdays

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

Ava (Purim-basket Ava; the hamantaschen alliance holds) compared calendars this week, because the study asks everyone now: Her year: Rosh Hashanah's apples-and-honey ("the year starts sweet on purpose, you eat the hope"). Yom Kippur ("the sorry day. A whole day for the sorries, everybody at once. The rule, but a national holiday").

Hanukkah's eight nights. Passover's questions, "the youngest kid asks the questions, it's built in, the whole night runs on a kid asking why is this night different..." (The collector nearly upended the table. "A holiday that runs on the youngest kid's questions?!

Who designed that?! That's the best design I ever, my whole notebook is that holiday...") The comparison, concluded at the lamp: "Mama, her calendar and ours, different festivals, different foods, different words. But underneath: the same machines.

Days for sorry. Days for sweet-hoping. Days for questions. Days for light in the dark months. Everybody's grandmothers built the same machines out of different parts. The underneafs match again. The underneafs always match."
