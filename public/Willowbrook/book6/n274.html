Night 274

The Boredom Door

Month 10: The Long Winter

Deep winter arrived and locked the doors, and with it, day three, the ancient words, spoken by the four-year-old to the ceiling: "There's nothing to doooo." And Mama, folding laundry, delivered this month's founding law without looking up: "Good. Stay there a while." (The outrage.

The appeal to the sister. The sister's ruling, which surprised him: "She's right, Theo. I checked this years ago with the poems: the bored is a door. The wanting-something-to-do is the beginning of the something, but only if nobody rescues you too fast.

The boredom's not a emergency. It's a lobby. Sit in the lobby. The ideas come through it, they got no other door.") (He sat in the lobby eleven minutes, an eternity at four. Minute twelve: the couch cushions became a truck-cow ferry system of great complexity. The lobby delivered. It always delivers, if the rescuing holds off.)
