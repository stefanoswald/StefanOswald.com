Night 239

The School Year Closes

Month 8: School Stories

First grade closed this week, the second school-year goodbye, run with veteran honors: The corner: handed on (Owen, new-kid Owen, matched-crackers Owen, a whole year woven in, takes the Tuesdays; "the corner belongs to the chair; do the welcoming parts warmest; you know why"). The worm farm: summered to the garden (the Warden of Worms transporting his charges in a procession the story can only describe as ecclesiastical). The jar: stays (Room 6's now; setup outlives engineers; the room's slips confirmed publicly at the closing circle, the bus one still undefeated across two rooms and three years). The somebody wall: one last unsigned deed ("Somebody had the best first grade in recorded history.

Records vary. This one doesn't"). And Mr. Okafor at the door, at the last bell, with the handshake he gives departing colleagues: "Second grade doesn't know what's coming. Neither does the town, I suspect. Keep the file on the good ones, whatever it is. I know I'm in it. It's the honor of my year too."
