Night 102

The Pay Envelope

Month 4: My Jobs

Month's end at the coop brought a ceremony nobody warned her about: The envelope. (Small. Brown. Her name in Joe's capitals. Inside: egg money. Her share, by his arithmetic: the Saturday eggs, market rate, minus nothing, "the feed's my column; the holding's yours.")

The stunned arithmetic: "Grandpa. This is real money. The eggs I counted turned into coins. That's a farm, darlin'. Care in, eggs out, coins after, the whole machine, and you're in it now. First wages.

Spend them like they know where they came from." (They knew. The tally book's three columns received them with care: Saved, the bulk..." The fleet fund; the Hawk 2 needs canvas. "Spent: one bakery run, rolls for the whole Wednesday walk..."

The post treats the post. "And given: a coin for the mitten jar, early as always, "the coop taught me the cold math: winter's always coming for somebody. The given column knows it first.")
