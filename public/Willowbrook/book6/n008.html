Night 8

The Zipper Autopsy

Month 1: How Things Work

The jacket zipper died this week, and instead of the donation bag, it met the brass glass. The autopsy, done at the kitchen table: the slider, examined under Hollis-issue magnification. The teeth..." They're not teeth, they're little hooks, and the slider's a tiny machine that marries them two at a time, and unmarries them on the way down...

"And the diagnosis: one tooth, bent. One. The whole jacket dead of one crooked hook. The referral: Petrov's. The repair: ninety seconds, one squeeze of pliers, no charge ("diagnostics were done for me, the detail person gets trade rates").

"One bent tooth, Mr. Petrov. The whole zipper quit over one. That's most broken things, detective. One small crooked piece, and everyone declares the whole thing dead. My entire trade..." He handed the jacket back. "is finding the one tooth. Remember that when things look all-the-way broken. They almost never are."
