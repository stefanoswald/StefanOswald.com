Night 77

The Quiet House

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

The study went overnight this week: Sleepover at noor's. (First one. New house rhythms, learned as a guest, "their house, their approach; the whole month in one overnight.") The differences, honored one by one: shoes off at the door ("the house stays clean for the praying, the floors are part of the way things worked.

I like a house where the floors have a job"). The prayer times, witnessed from respectful distance (the washing, the facing, the old words, "the biggest together, running right there in the living room, twice before bedtime"). The food (halal; her hosts explained the kept-rules; the guest ate gratefully and asked what it builds and Noor's mother, as it happened, sent her home with extra dessert and a standing invitation, because the right question opens every door in every approach there is). And the quiet hours.

Noor's house runs quieter than hers; less radio, more reading; "a whole family of volume knobs set gentle, and the gentle is full, not empty." The finding, saved at her own lamp, home again: "Every house is a country, Mama. Ours has the forecast and the office hours and the lamp. Theirs has the floors-wif-jobs and the five appointments and the gentle-full quiet.

Visiting isn't checking whose country's right. Visiting is learning that countries work lots of ways, and coming home loving yours more and the world's wider, both at once. Two coats. Travel is two coats."
