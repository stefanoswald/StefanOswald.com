The Old Language at Number Nine

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

Friday tea delivered the study's biggest shock: arriving early, she heard Mrs. Hollis through the door. On the phone, in another language. Fluent. Fast. Laughing in it. The interview, tea poured, the right question ready: "Mrs.

Hollis. The phone language. You got a whole other language and forty-one Fridays never mentioned it?!" The chapter, opened at last: Polish. Her parents' tongue, she came to this country at six..." Younger than you, child, with no English at all.

Sat in a classroom like a sealed jar for one whole year. The teachers thought I was slow. I was full, full to the brim, in the wrong currency. "The phone: her sister, weekly, seventy years running.

"The Polish holds the childhood. Some memories only live in their first language. When I want my mother's kitchen, English can't take me there. Only Polish knows the way." (The sealed jar, full in the wrong currency.

The study sat very still. "That's why you saw the quiet ones your whole career. The watching. The windows. You were one. I was one. The teachers who finally saw me, that's why I became one.

Every teacher is paying somebody back, child. You knew that already. Now you know my somebody.")
