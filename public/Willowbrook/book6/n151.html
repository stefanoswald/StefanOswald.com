Night 151

Tessa's River Memory

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

On the last visit of the month, the first stars appeared above the Great Willow. The brook month, closing, and Tessa Turtle, sixty years a resident of the subject, gave the decade's telling: "I have lived in that water since before the bridge was rebuilt..." said Tessa, in the pause-heavy way of the long-lived. "I remember the flood that signed the high stone.

I remember the drought summer the pools shrank to puddles and the heron's grandmother stood guard over the last deep one and let no fisher near it, herons keep posts too; nobody logs them. I remember when the otters left. I was young. The water went quieter for forty years, you'd only notice if you were in it."

A pause, ten years wide. "And this summer, a small dry creature with a notebook found the slide marks, and confirmed the return, and told no one. Guarded the bend. And the otter stayed." The hill absorbed this.

Freddy took off his hat entirely. "So log this month with the river's own arithmetic..." said Tessa. "Sixty years: one flood, one drought, one leaving, one return, ten thousand quiet faithful days between. The water's long story is almost all quiet faithful days.

That's not the boring part, colleagues. That's the story. The floods are just punctuation. Mmm," hummed the Moon, who has pulled that water gently for longer than turtles. Goodnight, long stories of Willowbrook, the stayers, the goers, the returned. Almost all quiet faithful days. That's the story. Sleep.
