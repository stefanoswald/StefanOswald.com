Night 66

The Slow Talker

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

A new study subject arrived in class this week: Ben. Who talks slow. Long pauses mid-sentence, the kind that make grown-ups finish his words and kids drift off before the verb. The observed damage: everyone answers for him.

His sentences get taken from him mid-carry..." Helpfully. "daily. He's stopped starting most of them. The bench rule, adapted: Don't fill the pause. (Harder than it sounds. The pause aches to be filled. She sat on her hands with her whole heart up, the training transfers.)

And Ben, given the whole silent runway, finished his own sentence, and the sentence was worth it: asked about the class jar, given all his time, he said: "Maybe... the maybe is... the jar's way of... letting the bad day... stay warm... till it's ready to hatch." (The bad day, staying warm till it's ready to hatch. The best description of the jar ever rendered, the founder ruled on the spot, and it took forty seconds of everyone not helping.)
