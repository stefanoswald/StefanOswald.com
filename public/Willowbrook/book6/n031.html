Night 31

Oliver Owl Explains the Watch Itself

Month 1: How Things Work

On the month's last evening, Stella blinked once over the listening tree. The how month, closing, and Stella posed the recursive question: "The small one opened everything this month. Clocks. Pianos. The lantern. So, for the story, before she someday asks us: how does the watch work?

What are our insides?" Oliver Owl removed his spectacles, the gesture of the biggest answers, and gave the mechanism plainly: "One Moon, for the long memory. One star-keeper, for the marking of years. One owl, for the tally book.

One bear, for the needing. One hedgehog, for the rounds. One postmaster, for the words that must travel. Assorted specialists, wings, shells, hats. And one tree, for the holding of all of it." He polished the spectacles slowly.

"But that's the parts list, not the mechanism. The mechanism is simpler and she already found it in a doorbell: the caring is always in the line. Every member, every night, completes some small circuit, a round walked, a star set, a letter carried, a session held, and the power that was always waiting flows. The watch doesn't make the love, colleagues.

The town makes it. We're just..." he settled the spectacles back, "...the wiring. Mmm..." Hummed the Moon, oldest wire of all. "Log it..." said Stella. "For when she asks. She'll ask. She asks everything eventually, it's the best thing about her."

Goodnight, wiring of Willowbrook, seen and unseen, feathered and celestial. The caring is in the line. The circuits close all night, every night. Sleep. The power flows.
