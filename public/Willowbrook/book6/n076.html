Night 76

The Wheelchair Racer

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

The park gained a racer this week: Zoe. New to town. Fast chair, faster mouth, tired of the wrong questions before she'd finished a lap. The right question, deployed on arrival: "That chair's got cambered wheels.

Mateo, look, they lean in like a race car, what's it build you? What's the top speed?" And Zoe, who'd braced for the usual, lit up like the hill at festival: the chair tour (the cambers, the push-rims, the turning radius..." Tighter than any bike; watch this. "it was; Mateo took notes).

The top speed. And the races, rerun with the handicap engineering flipped, on pavement, Zoe spots everyone else the head start. ("Fair is same chances. The math just runs the other way here. The chair's not her limit, Theo.

On pavement, the chair's her superpower. Limits and powers trade places depending on the ground. Everybody's do.")
