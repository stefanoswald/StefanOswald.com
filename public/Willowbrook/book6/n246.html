Night 246

The Two Grandmas Problem

Month 9: Truth With Kindness

Case two, via the lunch table: a classmate, caught between grandmas. (Both knit her sweaters. Both ask "which is your favorite?" Both mean it. The kid: paralyzed weekly.) The consult, from the veteran of grandma-soup diplomacy: "The question's a trap, but not a mean trap, it's a love-trap; they're both asking 'do you love me' in sweater language.

So don't answer the ranking, answer the asking. 'This one's the warmest for snow. That one's the best for school.' Different jars! Both first place! The bests come in kinds. Lily settled that one years ago.

Nobody's soup loses. And here's the deep part: it's not a dodge. It's the truer truth. Ranking sweaters was always the wrong math. The love doesn't run on a ladder. Tell them where each sweater wins. That's the honest they were both actually asking for."
