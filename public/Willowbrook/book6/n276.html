Night 276

The Radio Drama

Month 10: The Long Winter

The fort's broadcasting division launched this week: The radio drama. (Performed from inside the fort, audience outside, listening only; "that's the whole form: the pictures happen in their heads; we just send the sounds. It's chapter books, but live.") The production: "The willowhawk rides again".

The sound effects, engineered all afternoon: the wind. The wheels. The crash (the glorious crash, couch cushions plus a cymbal improvised from two pot lids, kitchen sovereignty briefly and diplomatically suspended). Theo as "The hawk's engine" (a role consisting entirely of committed humming; he prepared for two days).

The family, listening from the dark living room, reported: the pictures in the head beat television. "That's the radio secret..." The playwright explained at the lamp. "The listener builds the whole world demselves, so it's exactly as good as their imagination, which is always better than anybody's budget. The books knew it. The radio knew it. The fort knows it now."
