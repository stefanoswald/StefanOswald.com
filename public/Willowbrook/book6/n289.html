Night 289

The Winter Photo Album

Month 10: The Long Winter

The album tradition met the tape project this week: The recorded tour. (The albums, turned page by page, but this time the recorder ran, and the captions got kept in voices: Papa's telling of the May snow. Mama's of the duck. Theo's of his own arrival..."

I came in winter and wivy waited at the window. "third-hand memory, first-person pride, the archive accepts all eras.) And the session's discovery, made mid-tape: the album's gaps. ("Mama, there's no pictures of the ordinary days. The birthdays, the firsts, the trips: documented.

The Tuesday-ness of us, the office hours, the forecast at breakfast, the firework at the corner. Zero pictures. The albums keep the peaks and lose the weave. And the weave is the whole... the weave is what the peaks stand on.")

The correction, instituted that week: The tuesday roll. (One film roll, Papa's old camera, spent entirely on ordinary: the table at homework gold. The bag by the door. The forecast mid-delivery. The lamp, lit, mid-chapter. Developed: the album's new best page, instantly, unanimously. "The weave, kept at last.")
