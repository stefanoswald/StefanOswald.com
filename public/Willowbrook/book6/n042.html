Night 42

The Steady Practice

Month 2: The Kite That Wouldn't Fly

Festival minus twelve days: Practice era. Daily hill sessions, and the skill someone saw assemble: Steadiness. Not the launching, not the height, the holding. The kite steady in shifting air, through a hundred small constant answers: feed, check, ease, answer, answer, answer.

"It's not one big skill, Papa. It's a thousand little ones per minute. The line never stops asking and you never stop answering and from the ground it looks like nothing's happening. Steady looks boring from outside.

Inside it's the busiest thing I ever did. Now you know the secret of every steady thing you've ever trusted..." said Papa, watching the gold diamond hold its patch of sky. "The marriages. The farms.

The Fridays. Steady is a thousand invisible answers a minute, forever. It only looks like standing still."
