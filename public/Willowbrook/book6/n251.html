Night 251

Grandpa Joe's Slower Steps

Month 9: Truth With Kindness

Olivia had noticed that Grandpa Joe walked more slowly. He used both hands on the porch steps and rested more often during farm work.

At last she asked, "Is something wrong? Please tell me the real size. My guesses have made it enormous."

Joe set down the seed catalog. "It is arthritis. My joints are wearing, the way old hinges do. The doctor and your mama know, and treatment helps. It is not the kind of illness you are imagining. It is a slowing change."

Over time, he explained, more farm work would move to other hands.

"Yours among them, if you keep wanting the Saturdays."

Olivia sat with the answer. It was sad, but smaller and clearer than her fear.

"The Saturdays keep," she said.

Joe smiled. "Your grandmother used to ask for the real size too."

The truth did not make his steps faster. It let Olivia walk beside them without being chased by guesses.
