Night 5

The Willowhawk's First Problem

Month 1: How Things Work

Build day two, and the Willowhawk hit its first wall: the wings, bedsheet over lath, bent the frame just sitting there. Mateo's response: push harder, brace more, add wood. (Add weight. To fix weight. The story has watched grown engineers do it.)

The detail person's response: the notebook. "Wait. The clock taught me, everything touches everything. The heavy wing pushes the frame, the brace adds heavy, the heavy needs more brace, it's a circle going down. You can't brace your way out of a weight problem.

You have to make the wing lighter or the frame's shape stronger. Shape, Mateo. Triangles. The bridge book at the library, every bridge is secretly triangles. Triangles hold shape without adding much heavy." The rebuild: triangled. The frame: held. The detail person's stock: soaring before the pattern did.
