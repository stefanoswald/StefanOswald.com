Night 241

The Interview at Almost-Six

Month 8: School Stories

The fat notebook. The sixth interview. "Favorite color? Gold. Tenured. Retired, almost. The question can retire with honors, it's gold forever; the whole town voted with its string lights. Favorite food? The harvest table. Not a food, the whole table.

Write it anyway. You always let me bend the questions; that's why the interviews work. Best friend? Lily. Six for six. The before-memory spot is taken forever. But Papa, write the full answer this year: Lily, and then the web.

The answer's got layers now. Best friendships do. What do you want to be? The looking-close-and-telling-true job, still. Plus: whatever the person is who builds the benches and the exchanges and the funds-nobody-knows-about maps.

Is there a job that's both?... Then I'll build the job too. Jobs are just posts somebody proposed once. Scariest thing this year? The vandal morning. For about two hours. Then the fifty came. Best thing?

The fifty coming. Anything to tell Six?... Tell Six: the chain says it best. It stopped being a list of what you did and became a list of what got built. Keep building. The town keeps what you build, the ground keeps its kids, the shelf keeps its seasons, the bark keeps its words.

Everything here keeps. That's the whole town, in one word. It keeps. So build things worth keeping and then, this is the part. Let them go into the keeping. That's six's whole homework. I can feel it coming."
