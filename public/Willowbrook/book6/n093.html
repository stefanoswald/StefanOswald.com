Night 93

The Library Post

Month 4: My Jobs

Post two: The returns cart. (Asked for, at the desk, formally: "I want a post here. Real. Counted-on. I know the alphabet, the sections, the spine labels, and the whisper law. Six years I've been withdrawing from this building.

I want to deposit.") The librarian's response: the apprenticeship. Tuesdays after school. The returns cart..." Shelving is the library's whole bloodstream, child. A misshelved book is a lost book. The cart is trust in its heaviest form.

"The standard: exacting (spine out, flush, section-true, and the cart's order preserved..." The cart has a logic; learn it before you improve it. "which she wrote down with her whole face red because of course the cart's logic got questioned first). And the post's secret privilege, discovered week one: the returns cart knows the town's whole reading life. Who's into dinosaurs this month.

Whose family checked out the hard-weather books. Which kid just found chapter books, the level-ones coming back with that particular reluctant-return look.
