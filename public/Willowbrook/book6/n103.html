Night 103

The Post That Found Her

Month 4: My Jobs

The trimmed schedule lasted eleven days, and then the fourth post found her, the way posts do: a new kid at school. Mid-year arrival (the hardest kind; she holds the Dev file; she knows what mid-year costs). Lost in the hallways, lost at lunch, lost in the particular way of kids whose maps are all dragons. Mr.

Okafor's ask, quiet, at the door: "You're the one with the welcome systems, I'm told. The bench codes, the approach book. Walk him in?" The walk-in, done from the full kit: the tour (handles first, the lantern-visible corner, the mural bench, the bathroom map "because it's the worry nobody says").

The approach book, shown ("your page is waiting whenever you want it. Blank till then. The blank's yours too"). The lunch table. The loaner spot at the hill, mentioned for Saturday. "The trim was right..."

She reported at the lamp. "And the new post was right. That's the arithmetic's last piece: you trim to your good numbers so there's room when the real one walks in lost. The space wasn't empty. The space was ready."
