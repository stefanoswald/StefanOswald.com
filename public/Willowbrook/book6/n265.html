Night 265

Theo's Camp Stomachache

Month 9: Truth With Kindness

On the morning of camp, Theo said his stomach hurt.

Mama checked him carefully. He had no fever or other sign of illness, and the pain vanished during cartoons but returned beside the packed bag.

"The ache is real," Olivia said. "Sometimes worry lives in the stomach. What is it worried about?"

Theo admitted that nobody at camp knew he was left-handed, liked a blue cushion for sitting, or needed time to feel before he could explain.

Together they made a small card:

THEO. LEFT-HANDED. FEELS FIRST, THINKS AFTER. LIKES TRUCKS, COWS, AND THREE. IF LOST: STAND STILL, SHOUT, FIND A HELPER.

Theo showed it to one counselor, who thanked him for explaining what helped.

The first day was hard. The second was better. By Wednesday, his stomach was quiet and he had a friend at the block table.

If the pain had persisted or changed, Mama would have checked again. This time, listening to the worry helped the body settle.
