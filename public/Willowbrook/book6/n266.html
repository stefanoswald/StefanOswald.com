Night 266

The Photo That Flattered

Month 9: Truth With Kindness

The school photos came back: Two options. (The retake system: photo one, mid-real-laugh, gap-toothed, one curl escaping, alive. Photo two, the retake, posed, smoothed..." Nice. "The choice: hers.) The big think, mirror-consulted: "Photo two looks like the kid the picture-man wanted.

Photo one looks like me on a Tuesday. The nice one's not a lie exactly, it happened; the camera saw it, but it's a... a true dodge of my face. All-true roads around the actual me." The choice: Photo one.

("The year was gap-toofed and laughing and escaping its curl. The photo should match the year. Archives want the true one, ask any archive. Ask mine.") (Mama ordered photo one in every size. The story, which is itself an archive and has opinions, endorses the answer entirely: the far pages hold that photo, and it out-lives every posed one in every album it shares.

The alive ones always do. That's the whole physics of photographs, and six-year-olds who know it early save their archives decades of nice.)
