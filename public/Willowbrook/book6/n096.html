Night 96

The Cart's Logic

Month 4: My Jobs

Three Tuesdays of holding her tongue paid off: The cart's logic, decoded. (Not alphabetical. Geographic. The cart loads in shelf-walk order, one lap of the building, no backtracking. "The cart is a route, not a list.

The alphabet's for finding. The route's for carrying. Different jobs, different logics.") The decoding, presented at the desk, and then, the earned moment: "Now that I know the logic... one improvement, offered humble: the kids' picture books load last but shelve first on the route.

Swap their cart spot and the heaviest stack rides shortest. The route stays; the loading flips." The librarian looked at the cart a long moment. "Forty years..." She said. "and the picture books have ridden long the whole time."

The flip: adopted. Permanent. ("Improvement accepted..." Entered in the wainscoting list, sure enough, with the note: "She learned the logic first. The order of operations is the whole apprentice test. She passed it the slow way, which is the only way it passes.")
