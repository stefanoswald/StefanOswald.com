Night 148

The Hot Spring Rumor

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

A legend reached the squadron: Steam in the west woods. Warm water in deep winter. ("A hot spring..." The playground insisted. "A dragon," the younger benches preferred. The detective packed for both.) The investigation: the steam, real, visible, rising from a seep by the old mill ruins.

The temperature: warmer than the brook. Not hot. The source, traced with the well-tour's education: groundwater. "The underground lake's water holds the summer's warmth all year, Mateo, the earf's a slow thermos. The seep's not a dragon.

It's the aquifer breaving out, and its breath is warmer than the air, so: steam. The dragon is... the hill exhaling. Which was the brook's whole secret and the spring's whole secret... So no dragon..." said Mateo, slightly mournful. "Mateo. The hill breaths. It holds summer in its chest all winter and breaths it out warm by the old mill. That's not no-dragon. That's the dragon being real and bigger and asleep under the whole town.

The truth demoted the monster and promoted the world. It always does. That's the whole business of finding out."
