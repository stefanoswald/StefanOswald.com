Night 38

The No-Wind Day

Month 2: The Kite That Wouldn't Fly

And then: The no-wind day. Perfect sky, free afternoon, kite packed, hill climbed, and the air: Still. Dead still. Flag-limp, dandelion-fluff-hanging still. The runs: tried. (You can drag a kite into fake flight if you sprint forever.

It's not flying. It's towing. The kite knows. You know.) The waiting: attempted. The truth: accepted, eventually, sitting on the hilltop next to a grounded gold diamond: "You can fix a bridle. You can unpick a tangle.

You can't argue with a calm. The wind's the one part of kite-flying that was never yours. It comes or it doesn't. The whole sport sits on top of a thing nobody controls." So the afternoon became what it insisted on being: cloud-shapes.

Dandelion clocks, blown, the fluff hanging in the dead air like slow snow. The hill, lain on. The sky, read without a string for once. "The calm was the lesson..." She reported at the lamp.

"The kite teaches wind days and no-wind days both. Some days the sport is the waiting. The tree's whole lesson, but with grass stains."
