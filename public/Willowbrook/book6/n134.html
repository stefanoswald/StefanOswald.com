Night 134

What the Brook Taught the Willow

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

This month's last field trip connected its two oldest subjects: the hilltop, the willow at her back, the brook silver below, the town's two elders, in one view, for one long think: "You two know each other..." She said to the tree, watching the water. "Three hundred years of neighbors. The brook waters your hill from underneath, the spring's dark and your roots' dark are the same dark.

You're not two elders. You're one system wearing two costumes: the stayer and the goer. You hold the town still and the brook carries it forward and between you... that's the whole job of anywhere, isn't it. Something that stays.

Something that flows. Both faithful. Both resting in their season. The town lives in the conversation between you." The gold moved. The water shone. (the story, which has listened to that conversation for three hundred years, confirms her written-down words as substantially accurate.

The stayer and the goer. The whole job of anywhere. The two of them have been saying it to each other, in root and current, since before the first wagon, and waiting, the way elders wait, for someone small to climb the hill and hear it.)
