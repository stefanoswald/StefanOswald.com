Night 340

Birthday Week on the Doorstep

Month 12: Almost Six

During birthday week, Willowbrook arrived at Olivia's door one piece at a time.

Monday brought a wheelbarrow of dandelions from the children who grew sorry-flowers. Tuesday brought Rosa's largest basket of rolls.

On Wednesday, the librarian set aside a great thick book with a note: THE DEEP SHELF IS YOURS NOW. SHELVE THIS YOURSELF WHEN YOU FINISH.

Mateo delivered a smooth workshop pulley for the fort's bucket. Mrs. Hollis gave Olivia the old leather case for her brass magnifying glass.

On Saturday, Mr. Petrov returned the wheelbarrow after its yearly maintenance. Six painted stars ran along its side. A small brass plate on the handle read: HOLDER: OLIVIA. SINCE AGE THREE.

No gift was grand by itself. Together they formed a map of the places and people that had watched her grow.

The doorstep emptied each evening. The feeling of the town arriving stayed.
