Night 51

The Wind Journal

Month 2: The Kite That Wouldn't Fly

This month's data went formal this week: The wind journal. The entries, shown at the farm porch: "Mornings: light, unreliable, kite-lazy. Two pm: the hill's thermal wakes (Wes found it; the journal maps it: over the blacktop, rises like a invisible fountain). Gloaming: the day-wind lies down.

Storm-front days: the restless kind, gusty, tangle-weather, fly low or don't. And: The flag on the library tells the truth first. Check the flag before the hill. The flag's the town's free forecast."

Joe read the journal twice, went into the house, and came back with something: a small brass wind gauge, old, pocket-sized. "Your grandma's. She kept her own wind pages, kite-flyer as a girl, she was. Champion, if you believe her sister."

A pause. "The pages are in the almanac. March section, 1961. Go read what she logged." (She read it. Margaret's girlhood hand: gusts, calms, thermals, and one line, aged nine: "The wind is the sky's mood.

Learn the moods and you can dance with anything." The journals, sixty years apart, agreeing. They remembered them side by side.)
