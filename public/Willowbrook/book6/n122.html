Night 122

The Brook's Calendar

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

The study formalized: The brook's calendar, built with the almanac's fifty years of water notes: "Spring: the big drink, snowmelt, the flood shoulders, the brook loud and brown and busy. ('The spring brook is a teenager,' Joe says. 'Strong, muddy, in a hurry, won't listen.') Summer: the low clear months, stepping stones showing, minnow country, the brook polite.

Fall: the leaf-freight season, the water carrying the whole wood's mail downstream. Winter: the lid. Quiet-but-going. (Known since the fourth year. The brook never stops. It just goes private.)" And Joe's summary, entered in both books: "Fifty years watching this water.

It's never once been the same brook twice, and it's never once been a different brook. Chew on that one, darlin'. It'll feed you your whole life."
