Night 224

The Substitute Legacy

Month 8: School Stories

The Portuguese substitute returned this week (Mr. Okafor's night-class exam day; he requested her specifically, as it happened, on intelligence from his most reliable source), and Room 6 was ready: The greeting, rehearsed in secret via the window code and the lunch table: "Bom dia, professora!" Twenty-two voices. Her face, the story keeps a shelf for faces, and this one went on it beside the crossing guard's: the whole day, taught bilingual by mutual delight.

The counting reviewed and extended (to twenty; the teens, she confirmed, are haunted in Portuguese too, "all languages have the haunted numbers; it's a comfort, honestly"). The mimicry incident of last year: not mentioned, entirely repaired, compounded into lesson. "The ten words grew, Mama..." The lamp heard.

"That's the thing about the right response to the mean moment, the cheap laugh would've died in a day. The repair's still paying, a year later, in vocabulary. Repairs compound. Meanness just... spends. The whole tally book of the whole world might come down to that one line, honestly."
