Night 214

What the Wobble Was

Month 8: School Stories

Saturday, week two: the whistle. Two long, come when you can, no running. (She ran anyway. Some rules you honor in the breach.) The window seat. The whole thing, out at last, just-ears held throughout: the new girl, Dana, had latched hard onto Lily.

Sweet, then clingy, then controlling: sit here, not there. Don't do bakery talk with her. "You're my best friend now," the possessive machinery, and Lily, kind to a fault, caught in it: "I didn't want to hurt anybody, so I hurt you, slow, instead of her, fast. That's the math I did.

It was bad math." The response, ears first, then tools, by the eternal order: "The math was bad but the heart was yours, you were trying to be kind to everybody at once. That's not a crime; it's a overload. Here's the old finding: love's not pie, but time is.

Time's the one pie there is. Dana can be a friend, a whole real one, without owning your lunches. Owning isn't friendship; it's... it's scared-generous, from the other side. Somebody taught her friendship's a scarce thing you gotta fence. Somebody should show her it's candles."
