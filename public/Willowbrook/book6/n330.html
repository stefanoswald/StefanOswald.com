Night 330

The Almanac Month, Counted

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

Month's end (the story grants the almanac month its check late, the way this month itself would prefer, in the different ink of the added-to pages). The check: Olivia could count thirty fire-nights. One suitcase month. The fence that didn't care and the barns that did.

One drought's pipes, still running forty years on. One yes under the willow. One row-anyway. One pig named Roosevelt. One petrichor, delivered fifty-two years late, logged in different ink. One honorable retirement (the sour you can't garden).

Three generations of wrong notes, one firelit room. One re-rigging, faced at true size. One family book, born of missing. One exchange, launched. One fourth hand, confirmed. Other changes resisted numbers: that the book is seed stock, not memory, you keep it to plant.

That the carried things want company, not vaults. That most good days get discovered years late, in the missing, unless somebody trains you to catch them live, and somebody did, thirty nights running, by firelight, before the suitcase and the thaw called him home. "Good almanac-month? The deepest-rooted one.

Mama, the whole year was insides. This month was the deepest inside there is: the inside of time. Fifty years, opened one night at a time, and the lesson under all the lessons: The attention is the love. The book is just love, wearing a tally book's clothes.

All the books are. Mine too. All of them, all the way down." Remembered. The attention is the love. All the books are. This one too, little one. This one especially.
