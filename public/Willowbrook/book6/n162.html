Night 162

The Plateau

Month 6: Hard Things Take Days

Week three: The plateau. (Every mountain has one; the maps all show it; living it is different.) "Papa. The scales sound the same as last week. Fifteen minutes times seven days times three weeks and the needle's not moving."

Papa brought the answer from the family's oldest instrument, the doorframe: "Growing's invisible daily and enormous annually. Remember? You never once felt yourself grow a hand-width. The practice is the same machine: the days deposit; the account compounds in secret; and one week, trust the map, the scales come out and surprise you.

You don't feel roots going down. You notice the wind stops moving you." (Week five, for the story: the C scale ran itself, underground, while she thought about dinner. The needle had been moving the whole time. It always was.)
