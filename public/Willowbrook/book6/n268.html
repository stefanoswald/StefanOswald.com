Night 268

The Weather Forecast Trust

Month 9: Truth With Kindness

A jurisdictional dispute this week: The picnic forecast. (The radio: "clear all Saturday." The almanac. Joe's bones plus fifty years of Octobers plus the behavior of the cows: "rain by two.") The family, caught between authorities, asked its resident methodologist.

The answer, delivered with full care: "The radio's got satellites. The almanac's got fifty years of this exact valley. The radio knows the county. Grandpa knows the hill, the way the wind wraps it, the way the fog sits.

For the valley, at this season: the almanac's local knowledge beats the radio's big knowledge. Picnic in the morning, home by one. And, this is the method part, we check. Whoever's right gets logged. The authorities earn their trust one forecast at a time, same as everybody."

(Rain: 1:40 pm. The almanac, logged victorious. Joe's entire celebration: one hat adjustment. "The cows knew," he said. The cows, as it happened, declined to comment. They usually know. They always decline.)
