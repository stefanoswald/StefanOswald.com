Night 123

The Minnow School

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

The summer-low brook opened its aquarium this week: The minnow pool. Bellies down on the warm rocks, faces at the water, the school below, forty silver slivers moving as one. "Lily. Watch. Nobody's the boss minnow.

They turn together, instant, all at once, no leader calling it. How?!" The library answered (the brook has a shelf; everything has a shelf): each fish watches its neighbors, just the nearest few, and matches them. That's the whole system.

No boss. No plan. Forty little match-your-neighbor rules adding up to one silver thought. "... The school is a web with fins. Nobody runs it and it runs. Match your neighbors, that's the whole law, and the whole thing turns like one animal."

The long watch. "The town does this, Lily. The shoveling. The soup jars. The wave at the crossing. Nobody's the boss kindness. Everybody matches their neighbors and the whole town turns together. The second grid is a minnow school."
