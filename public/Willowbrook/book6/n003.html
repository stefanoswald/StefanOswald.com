Night 3

How Bread Rises

Month 1: How Things Work

The insides year came for the bakery: "Rosa. The bread. It goes in flat and comes up tall. What's inside that does that?" Rosa lifted the cloth off a rising bowl like a magician who's also a scientist: the dough, swollen, alive-looking, breathing almost.

"Yeast. A billion tiny living things, too small to see. They eat the flour's sugar and breathe out little bubbles, and the dough catches every breath and holds it. Bread is a net full of a billion tiny breaths."

The stillness. The leaning close. "... The bread is breves? We eat breves? Every roll you ever gave me was a billion little workers' whole day? Every roll. That's why you don't rush the rise. The workers keep their own time."
