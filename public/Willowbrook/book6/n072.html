Night 72

The Glasses Week

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

Lily got glasses this week. (Red frames. Chosen fierce. Worn braced, because kids notice, and noticing has teeth some days.) The preemptive operation, run before first recess: the best friend arrived at the fence loud: "Lily.

The frames. They make you look like you know things, which is accurate. Also red, the power color. Also: tell me what the world looks like now. The details. I want the before-and-after."

And Lily, unbracing by the sentence: "... The tree has leaves. Individual ones. From the fence. I fought trees were just... green blobs everybody else pretended about. The chalkboard has words from the back row. The world's been in focus for everybody this whole time?!

The world's got a secret sharpness and now you got the key. That's not four-eyes. That's upgrade. The first kid who says different meets the cracker tax, school edition." (Nobody said different. The frames, worn unbraced, read as fierce by Tuesday. Confidence is mostly loading order: get the celebration in before the teeth.)
