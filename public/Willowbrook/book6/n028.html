Night 28

The Night Before the Hill

Month 1: How Things Work

The Hawk is staged. Tomorrow: the hill. The final garage session, dusk: the checklist, run three times (wheels, tail, wings, ropes, the pilot's helmet. Mateo's, non-negotiable, his mama's one condition). The chalkboard's philosophy section, read aloud like scripture: "Everything walks before it flies.

Inventions get laughed at first. Doubt plus a library card is basically engineering. The angle decides what the push becomes." And the last system, examined honestly at the lamp that night: "The nerve, Mama. Mateo's the pilot, it's his machine, his hill, his nerve to spend.

My job tomorrow is the detail person's last detail: the go check. Looking him in the eye at the top and asking the real question, not 'is the Hawk ready.' The Hawk's ready; I checked everything checkable. The question is 'are you ready', and then believing his answer either way.

Even if the answer's 'not today.' Scheduling isn't scared-quitting. The pole taught me. The detail person guards that too. The nerve is a system. You check it like hardware. You never, never shame it."
