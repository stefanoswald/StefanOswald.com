Night 116

The Job Interview Game

Month 4: My Jobs

Dinner invented a game this week (this family's dinners have a research division): The job interview game. Imaginary posts; family applicants; rigorous questions. The written-down words's best moments, preserved: Post: Cloud shepherd. (Theo, applying: "I would keep the clouds in a herd.

The rain ones go west where the farms are firsty. I know west. It's where the sun goes to bed." Hired, "for knowing where west is and what farms need. The qualifications were secretly real.")

Post: Keeper of lost socks. (Papa: "Twenty years' experience losing them. I know all their hiding thoughts. Set a sock to catch a sock." Hired, with reservations about method.) Post: Town gloaming coordinator. (Mama: "I would simply continue doing what I do, stopping at the window each evening and making sure it happens properly.

Attendance is the coordination." Hired unanimously; as it happened the post was invented to describe what she already does, which is the game's secret genius: half the family's real posts got named tonight, disguised as jokes.)
