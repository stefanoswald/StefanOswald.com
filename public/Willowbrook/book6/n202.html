Night 202

The Late Bloomer Bed

Month 7: Team Willowbrook

One bed shamed the season quietly all summer: the corner plot, an older couple's, planted entirely in, nothing, apparently. Green nubs. No harvest. No flowers. Passersby politely didn't mention it. (The town is kind. The bed looked like the lot's one dud.)

September answered: Dahlias. The whole bed, at once, dinner-plate blooms, wine-red and gold and impossible, the lot's undisputed glory precisely when everything else was going brown and done. "They planned the late..." She reported at the lamp, awed.

"The whole summer of looking-like-nofing was the plan. Dahlias spend the season building underground, the tubers, the roots, the whole bank account, and then they spend it all in September, when the garden needs glory most and expects it least. Sound like anyone you know?" The pause.

The slow grin. "... Ben. The slow talkers. The late readers. Marcus. Fourth, who waited longest and flew farfest. The whole file. The late bloomers aren't behind, Mama. They're dahlias. They're on the september schedule, and September always comes, and when it comes, they own the whole garden."
