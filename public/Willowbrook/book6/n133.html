Night 133

The Brook's Winter Preview

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

The cold came early to the water this week: Skim ice on the still pools, the first pane of the coming lid, thin as breath, gone by noon. The annual observance (fifth year running; the brook's winter is old family ritual now): "It's starting, Mama. The going-private. By January: the full lid, and the blblblb running secret underneath, and everybody who doesn't know the brook thinks it stopped.

And you'll tell them. I always tell them. It's my oldest sermon." (It is. The story has heard it delivered to Theo, to the trio, to Owen, to the story-hour carpet, to at least one town councilman: winter doesn't stop the good things.

It makes them quiet for a while. The brook is the proof that runs all year.) "But this year I got the new half, from this month: the lid isn't the brook's enemy. The lid is the brook's sunday.

The rest that the running runs on. Even the water gets its part. Everything faithful rests, the willow's winter, the heron's stillness, the lid. The resting isn't the opposite of the faithful. It's the proof of it."
