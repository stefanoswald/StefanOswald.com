Night 172

The Long Division Preview

Month 6: Hard Things Take Days

Fence summit, and Mateo, fifth grade now; the fence holds, showed his homework: Long division. (The tower of numbers. The bringing-down. The remainders. The playground's whispered dragon: "everybody says it's where math gets impossible.")

The dread, examined: real. The defusing, self-administered at the fence: "Okay but. Mateo, look at it with this month's eyes. It's not impossible. It's a multi-day. It's got phases (the estimate, the multiply, the subtract, the bring-down.

Four systems, learned separate, married late. The hawk rule, it's the Hawk rule with numbers). It's got a plateau. It's got a hate-the-bike day on the map. Everybody calling it impossible is just... reading the toll boof as the wall.

I got three years till it's mine. By then I'll have the piano summited and the backstroke and whatever else, the atlas just keeps growing. Long division's gonna meet a kid with a whole atlas. That's the weirdest thing about you..." said Mateo, not unkindly, doing his own homework. "You look at the dragon and count your maps."
