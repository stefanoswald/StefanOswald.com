Night 139

The Bath Physics Revue

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

This month closed its laboratory series in the oldest lab of all: The bath. The revue: Displacement ("watch the water line. I get in, the water goes up. The water has to go somewhere. I'm not in the bath, Theo.

I'm the bath's new stone. The brook taught me: every body in the water is a stepping stone the water rewrites itself around"). The whirlpool (stirred to spinning; "the water remembers the stir after the hand stops, the water's got momentum, the lightest memory there is"). And the drain: the spiral, watched to its gurgling end, "it always spins, Mama.

Never straight down. The water leaves dancing. Even the drain is a dance if you watch the whole exit." (as it happened this month's whole arc, closable in one tub: source to sea, stone to spiral, and the water leaving dancing. The assistant demanded the drain be refilled and re-drained twice. The grown-ups permitted once. Science lives within limits.)
