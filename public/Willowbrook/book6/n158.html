Night 158

The Swimming of the Big Rock

Month 6: Hard Things Take Days

Pool day (winter lessons; the family tradition holds), and Theo, mid-kickboard, declared his far mountain: "The big rock. At the swimming hole. The one you jumped. When summer comes: Me." The coach's response, and as it happened she didn't say "you're too little"; the load-rating law says ages aren't insults but also maps beat answers: "Okay.

The map, then. The rock needs: the deep-water float (you got half). The climb-out (you got none, the rock's got a scramble). The jump itself (the nerve system; can't build it in the garage; grows from the other braves).

That's a whole-spring map, Theo. Lessons, the pool ladder for climb practice, the little dock first. Summer's the test, not the training. The training's now... The training's now?" (Delighted. The boy loves a campaign. It's the chain year. He's living loud, by the requirement.)
