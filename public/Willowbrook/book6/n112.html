Night 112

The Broken Streak

Month 4: My Jobs

Fourteen Saturdays, unbroken. Then: the fever. Real, flattening, non-negotiable. Saturday came and went without her. Joe holding the coop solo, the eggs counted by older hands, the route run by Papa-and-Theo ("the backup rules held..."

By the debrief. "because you built them," Sam's Tuesday lesson, generalized). But the lamp got the residue: "Fourteen and broken, Mama. The streak's dead. The counting-on got... uncounted." And Mama took this month's last big one: "Listen close, love, because this one guards your whole life if you let it: the counting-on was never the streak.

Streaks are numbers, they break, all of them, eventually; that's what numbers do. Faithful is different. Faithful includes the fevers. Faithful is: the post was covered, because you built the covering. The hens drank warm.

The route ran. Your body did its one job, the being-sick, and the way things worked you built did yours. That's not a broken streak. That's a faithful post passing its hardest test: the test of you...

Faithful includes the fevers. It includes everything, love. That's what makes it faithful and not just... perfect. Perfect is brittle. Faithful bends."
