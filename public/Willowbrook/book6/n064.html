Night 64

Wren's Volume Knob

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

The study's most careful interview, requested by its subject, which is the whole miracle: Wren, at the bench, wanting the notebook to get her right: "Everybody thinks the quiet is shy. Or scared. Or broken-waiting-to-be-fixed. It's not.

It's a volume knob, and mine's just set low, and, this is the part nobody gets. I hear more there. The loud kids hear demselves. I hear... everything. The room's whole weather. Who's sad under the laughing.

Which grown-up's tired. Where the trouble's starting, two minutes early. The low volume is why the watching works." A pause; the knob turned up one notch, deliberately, for the story: "You knew that already.

You're the only one who never tried to fix me louder. The bench, the crayon, the near-not-wif. You treated the quiet like a language. So the notebook can say this, from me, exact: The quiet is a language.

Some of us are native speakers. Stop translating us. Start listening in it." (Taken down word for word. Read back for approval. Approved. The story adds only: it has never received a clearer dispatch from their bedtime world of the quiet, and it has been collecting dispatches a long time.)
