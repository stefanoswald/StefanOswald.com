Night 210

The Team Photo

Month 7: Team Willowbrook

The season's last act: The photo. (Gus-Senior's motion again, the man's on a roll: "before the winter scatters us to our stoves. Every team gets a picture. This was a team.") The assembly, at the gate, under the new sign: the bed-holders, four to eighty-one.

The bench faculty, seated front (the chairs brought out for them, the honors run correct here). The squadron, kites furled but present. The dog (bowed; third Biscuit; center front, an arrangement he negotiated personally). Rosa with a tray even now ("photos need rolls; everything needs rolls").

The whole impossible cast of one empty lot's first year. The photographer (the councilman; his contribution; he does weddings, it turns out): "Say garden!" (They said garden. Forty-some voices. The story has the picture, it hangs in the shed, in the library, in Number Nine, on the council's wall, and in one treasure box, and it is, the story can confirm from decades on, the single most-reprinted photograph in the town's whole archive.)

"Everybody's in it..." She said, examining the print. "That's the whole caption. Everybody's in it. That's the garden. That's the town. That's the... that's just the caption, of everything, if you build things right."
