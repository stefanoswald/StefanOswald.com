Night 70

The Fast Kid Slows Down

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

The study's surprise subject: Priya. (Second-Saturday Priya. Woven-in since. And the class's fastest, first done, every worksheet, every time.) The discovery, made from the reading corner: Priya, done in four minutes... secretly erasing and redoing.

Not fixing errors. Redoing correct work. Third time. The careful ask: and it came out in a rush: "Fast is my thing. Everybody knows it. First done. But if I'm not first I'm... nothing? So I go fast, and then I check, and then I'm scared it's wrong because I went fast, so I redo it, and I'm still first because even my redoing's fast, and nobody knows the fast has a... a hamster wheel in it..."

The bench wisdom, ported: "Priya. The fast is real, you think quick; that's your approach; keep it. The wheel's the problem. The wheel says fast-or-nofing. But you're not the fast kid. You're a whole Priya who happens fast.

If you came in last tomorrow you'd still be the whole Priya... Also, the redoing? That's not checking. That's the wheel eating your recess. Do it once, fast, your way, then come help me with the corner.

The corner needs fast hands and the wheel can't follow you there." (It couldn't. Wheels can't follow you into usefulness. The corner gained a fast hand; the eraser retired to erasing actual errors.)
