Night 233

The Kid From Column Three

Month 8: School Stories

Column three got a face this week. (The fair-unfair tally book's hard column, the born-different-decks column. The story has been waiting for this page, and dreading it a little, the way she was.) Story hour.

A new kid, shelter van kid; the gloaming bread route's stop; the librarian's nod said everything in the old dignity code. And the reader in the chair read the room by the whole manual: the kid, at the carpet's far edge, wanting in, unable to ask, the stop-start feet of it. The offer, five feet, no looming: "front spots are open. The gasp parts land better up close."

The kid came close. Gasped at the gasp part. Stayed for both books. And after, the librarian, quietly, watching the van pull away: "That one's card application lists no fixed address. The lantern's policy, my policy, forty years, is that the card requires a name, not an address.

Books don't check where you sleep." (The lamp got all of it that night, and the lamp barely held it: "Column three has a face now, Mama, and the face gasped at the gasp part, same as every face. The decks are different and the gasp is the same. That's the whole reason the homework matters. Nobody's underneath is column three. The columns are all on-top. All of them.")
