Night 228

The Field Trip to the Mill

Month 8: School Stories

Field trip: The old mill. (The ruins by the warm seep; the town's working past. The scheduled guide: a laminated pamphlet. The actual guide, recruited by a certain student through bench channels: Gus-senior. Forty years a mill man.

The pamphlet never stood a chance.) The tour, as only the real thing gives it: the wheel pit ("she turned three hundred days a year, rain-fed, exactly where the brook narrows, the town's first machine and the water ran it; your whole lane exists because this wheel could turn"). The grinding floor. The whistle mount ("she blew at noon sharp for eighty years.

Set every clock in the valley. When the mill closed, the town ran late for a month, nobody'd ever needed their own clocks"). And his own initials, found for them, cut in a beam at boy-height, sixty-odd years old. ("The marble, the beam, the wind pages, E.

W.'s drawing..." She inventoried at the lamp. "The town keeps its kids in every material. Wood, glass, paper, bark. The whole town is a treasure box that people live inside of.")
