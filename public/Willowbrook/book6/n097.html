Night 97

Theo's Fourth Birthday

Month 4: My Jobs

Theo turned four. (the story remains for this one. The dandelion courier. The bell operative. The tenderhearted. Four.) The day, kept whole: the truck cake (the cow era closes; Rosa managed the transition with a small frosting cow waving from the truck's window, "eras don't end here; they ride along").

The candles: four, one breath, wish sealed (drawn, not spelled, the tradition transfers; his drawing, glimpsed: a ladder. Nobody knows. He knows). The doorframe: his fourth line, measured with full ceremony. And the inheritance, presented after cake, wrapped in last year's chain-paper: His first countdown chain kit.

Paper strips, the good scissors, and the founding brief, delivered faculty-to-freshman: "The chain starts at your almost-five, next year. Between now and then, your job is the requirement: live a year worth chaining. Big things, small things, brave things, the chain keeps what you live. So live loud, Theo.

The paper's waiting." ("Live loud," he repeated, four years old, cake-smudged, holding scissors like a knighthood. As it happened: he did. Check his whole book. He did.)
