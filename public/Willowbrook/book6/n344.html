Night 344

The Six Party

Month 12: Almost Six

The six party, as attended by the story, in its chair: The guests: everybody. (the rule. Elders included; Petrov arrived with the thing his hinge-face had promised: a music box, shop-built, that plays, the story verified by ear, the first eight notes of the Gentle Brook. "Menders make too, sometimes..." He said, and would say no more.)

The Hunt's successor game, by the year: The noticing. Cards at the door; one true small specific thing per guest, noticed before cake. And the reading, at the solar-system sky: "Somebody noticed: Wren hums when she draws now. Somebody noticed: Gus-Senior's trowel got polished.

Somebody noticed: Theo let the little kid win the race, and then raced him again for real, so the win would count. Somebody noticed: Mrs. Hollis wore the valentine's colors. Somebody noticed: the birthday girl engineered herself to the edge of every group and the middle found her anyway."

(That one. Someone saw her hear that one. The room's oldest finding, aimed home: the middle finds her anyway. It always did. It always will.)
