Night 100

Night One Hundred: The Post Review

Month 4: My Jobs

Night one hundred, the round-number rite, themed as ever to its season: The post review. The portfolio, checked: The coop (Saturdays; twelve hens; one short count survived; one latch fixed; one impossible egg, the Duchess has laid twice more since; Joe has stopped saying "well" and started saying "huh"). The cart (Tuesdays; the route, mastered; the flip, adopted; the succession, proven. Sam covers; the post survives her).

The wednesdays (Theo delivered, letters drilled, fireworks performed; the salary, collected in walk-talk, this week's: his whole theory of ladders, which the story suspects connects to the wish, and which his sister is patiently not digging at, by the not-digging arts). Plus the unlisted fourth: The sink. And the finding, delivered from the pillow: "this month asked what posts pay. Answer: Being counted on.

That's the whole salary and it's huge. Somebody's Saturday runs because I show up. The difference between a kid and a... a citizen, Mama, it's not the age. It's the counting-on. I'm six in eight months. But I've been a citizen since the coop."
