Night 307

The Entry About the Drought

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

Night's entry: "Aug 1988. No rain 51 days. Dug the stock pond. Wells low valley-wide. Ran the hose to the petersens tuesday and thursday. They ran theirs to the widow kane sundays. The water went around."

The story: the drought summer, the one the water pages logged in one line ("The well held. Barely") and the man carried in weight. And the detail the entry kept that the memory needed: The rotation. Every farm's failing well, shared on a schedule, the whole valley running its water like the hand chain runs wobble, thinned, spread, survived.

"The spring never failed..." said Joe. "but it came close, and close teaches what plenty can't: the water was never mine. I was holding it. Same as the coop. Same as everything. The drought made the whole valley learn it at once, and darlin', here's the entry's real cargo: the valley never un-learned it.

The sharing outlived the emergency by forty years. Hard times build the pipes that good times run through after."
