Night 209

The Snow on the Beds

Month 7: Team Willowbrook

First snow took the lot this week, and she walked it (the Warden waved; the gate knows her mitten now) to see the year under white: The beds: clean rectangles, sleeping. The bench: capped. The shed: frosted, marble warm inside ("things in museums don't get cold; being kept is a temperature"). The whole season, the proposal, the marble, the vandals, the dahlias, the table, the race, under one smooth cover, like the year got tucked in.

"It looks like nothing again..." She said at the gate. "Like the forty years came back. But Mama, that's the whole difference between empty and resting, and now the whole town knows it: under that nothing is drip lines and tubers and mulched beds and a museum and a plan.

The nothing-before was waiting. The nothing-now is charged. Same white. Different underneath. You can't tell by looking, you can only tell by remembering. That's why the annual matters. That's why all the books matter. The remembering is how you tell charged from empty, in gardens and in everything."
