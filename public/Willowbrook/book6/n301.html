Night 301

The Maple Tapping

Month 10: The Long Winter

The thaw's first farm rite: The tapping. (The sugar maples along Joe's north line, his father's taps; the buckets; the old sweet arithmetic of freeze-thaw:) "The trees saved all summer," the lesson ran, spile by spile, "...the sugar, banked in the roots, the whole gold season deposited down. And now, at the turn, the freeze-thaw pumps it up, the tree spending its savings on the spring buds. We borrow a little at the turn.

Forty gallons of sap to one of syrup, the boiling's the patience part; everything at this farm has a patience part." And the connection, arriving over the first bucket's slow tink... Tink: "Grandpa. The trees are dahlias.

The whole winter of looking-dead was a bank. The maples, the dahlias, the lobby, the long winter, the year keeps teaching one lesson in a hundred costumes: The quiet seasons are the saving seasons. And the spring is just... the spending. The sweet, sweet spending."

(The syrup, boiled down that weekend: one gallon, gold as everything. The pancakes: historic. The lesson: banked, like everything at that farm, at compound interest.)
