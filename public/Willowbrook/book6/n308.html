Night 308

The Entry About Margaret's Yes

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

Night's entry, the one she'd found years ago at the anniversary, read aloud at last by its author: "Margaret said yes. Under the willow. Told her the farm came with me. She said 'Good. I wanted both.'"

The story, told whole for the first time, the fire low, the room still: the courting (two years; the county dances; her wind pages already famous). The willow walk ("I picked the tree because it was the only thing in the valley steadier than how I felt"). The yes. And the part no entry ever held, offered now because this month was built for it: "She said 'good, I wanted both', and then she said the rest, which I never wrote down because some things you keep in the original: 'The farm, you, and every hard and gold thing the two of you come with.

I'm not choosing a easy life, joe. I'm choosing a whole one.'" (The fire held. The story held. "A whole one..." She said finally, quiet. "That's the... Grandpa, that's the whole shelf. That's what everybody's choosing who chooses right.

Not easy. Whole. That's what the book's for, darlin'..." He said, the way he says it. "Somebody's telling you now.")
