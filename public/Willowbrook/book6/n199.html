Night 199

The Rain Day Shed Raising

Month 7: Team Willowbrook

The shed raising. (Rain-dated twice, then finally: one wet Saturday, tarps rigged, cocoa urn stationed, and the build, lumber donated, hinges Petrov's, plans drawn by Mateo and his papa, "the bridge-builder; the ramps and the arrows run in that family too.") The raising, as logged: the frame (triangles throughout; the detail person inspected; "the Hawk's rules hold at shape scale"). The walls.

The roof, raised in the rain's one merciful gap, the whole crew hauling on count. Petrov calling the joints like a conductor. And the door hung last, with ceremony, its little window facing the bench ("the shed watches the garden; the bench watches the shed; Gus-Senior's window watches everything. Attention, all the way down").

The museum box: added on its shelf. The marble: front and center. The first tool hung: the trowel. Gus-Senior's wife's, donated by its keeper to the wall of honor, "where working things retire to keep advising."
