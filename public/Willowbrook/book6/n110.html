Night 110

The Coop in the Cold

Month 4: My Jobs

The coop's first winter under her Saturday charge, and the post, per its own law, got realer: The cold lesson: the water, frozen by nine (broken, hauled warm from the kitchen, twice per visit, "the birds can't drink ice; the first job of winter is water, everybody thinks it's warmth; it's water"). The warm mash. The Duchess, fluffed to a sphere, accepting her scatter from a heated hand, and, on the coldest Saturday, doing the thing the story must report because it happened: stepping onto the boot of the girl who has fed her slow-and-front for four years, and settling. Sitting.

On the boot. Warming her feet on the enemy that never was. (Neither moved for eleven minutes. Joe watched from the barn door and didn't say "well" or "huh." He didn't say anything. Some Saturdays the farm just pays you in full, on the spot, in hen.)
