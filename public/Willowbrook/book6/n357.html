Night 357

The Last Saturday of Five

Month 12: Almost Six

The last Saturday of five, at the farm, run whole: the coop (open to close, the full charge, the six-list's item three, rehearsed one year early, flawless; "the post grew with the postholder all year; the growing just got official"). The count (the Duchess's line, thriving; the love, counted). The porch (the catalog; the reading; his eyes closed, listening, the arrangement's oldest posture). The row (Margaret's okra, knee-high and opinionated; the glass corn's sealed promise; the question row's whole green congregation).

And at the truck, the send-off. Joe's birthday tradition, the words changing yearly, the form eternal: "Six tomorrow-ish. The tree writes. And darlin', whatever it writes, I'll tell you what the farm wrote, in the only ink it's got: eggs, rows, porch hours, and a hen that sat on your boot.

The farm's word for you took nine years to write and it's the same word every year. The farm's word is: Welcome. It was always welcome. You always were."
