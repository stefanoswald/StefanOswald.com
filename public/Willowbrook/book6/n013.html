Night 13

How Scabs Work

Month 1: How Things Work

A driveway spill delivered a skinned knee, and the insides year did what it does: studied it. "The blood comes and then it stops itself. Then the scab builds. Then under the scab. Mama says.

New skin, whole new skin, built in the dark, and the scab falls off when the job's done like... like a construction fence coming down." The rebrand, announced to Theo (who takes knee-spills hard): "Theo. Scabs aren't hurts. Scabs are the body's petrov.

The mending shop is inside you, open all night, no charge. The bandage is just the fence around the job site. You don't cry at construction fences. You say 'good work in there' and let the workers work."

(He said "good work in there" to his next scrape. Solemnly. Directly to the knee. The household adopted it permanently, the story has heard grown members of this family address their own injuries with it, decades on.)
