Night 74

The Stutter and the Song

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

Music time delivered the study's strangest data: a classmate whose words catch, the stutter, the repeated starts, the hard consonant gates, sang the whole hello song clear. Not one catch. Bell-clear, note-perfect, gate-free. The investigation (careful; the subject consulted first; "you can ask, nobody ever asks; they just look away from the catches, which is worse"): the answer, from the music teacher, is real science, singing and speaking run on different roads in the brain.

The talk-road has the gates. The song-road doesn't. Same words, different highway, no toll. "So the words aren't broken..." The study concluded, aloud, at the table, for everyone. "The words are all there, perfect, whole, waiting.

One road's got construction. The other road's wide open. That's not a broken kid. That's a kid with a secret highway." (The kid, Miles, grinned the grin of the correctly described. And the class, as it happened, stopped looking away from the catches after that.

Construction zones are just construction zones. Everyone's got a road with cones on it somewhere. Miles sings the announcements now, when he feels like it, by class petition.)
