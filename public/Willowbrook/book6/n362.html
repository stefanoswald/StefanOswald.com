Night 362

Six, Night One

Month 12: Almost Six

On her first bedtime as a six-year-old, Olivia counted what had changed.

The room was the same. Bobby Bear still fit beneath her arm. Her oldest drawing remained on the wall, all wild lines and brave color.

The new wooden box sat on the nightstand. Inside was the Great Willow's fifth golden word: TRUE.

"I think true is the floor of six," Olivia told Mama. "Games, promises, friendships, even mistakes, nothing stands for long if it is built on pretending."

The warm brass key lay beside the box. Bobby Bear had said the dream-meadow gate would open during the coming year.

"So six is the gate year too," Olivia murmured. "Everything here was always a door."

Her eyes closed before she could explain what she meant.

Mama tucked the blanket around her. The box caught a line of night-light, and the word inside waited to be lived one ordinary day at a time.
