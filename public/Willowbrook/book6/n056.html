Night 56

The Calm Evening Flight

Month 2: The Kite That Wouldn't Fly

And once, the story keeps this one for its own shelf, there was the evening. Barely enough wind. The gloaming coming gold. The hill: empty but for two. And the willow kite went up soft, no fighting, no thrum, just a slow float into the last light, the twelve tails hanging almost still, the four painted words turning gently over the town.

No skills required. No answers per minute. The line barely breathing in her hand. "Mama. It's not flying flying. It's just... hanging there. Being up. Being gold." The long quiet, mother and daughter and forty feet of string and the whole day going down orange behind the far trees.

"The fourth branch taught me this one: some braves aren't for doing something. Some flying's not for flying. It's just for getting somewhere beautiful and... being there a while." They stayed till the light went.

The kite came down like it was sorry to, and forgave them on the way.
