Night 282

The Cabin Fever Break Glass

Month 10: The Long Winter

Week three of deep freeze, and even the lobby's deliveries thinned: Cabin fever. (Real. Documented. Theo bit a couch cushion for no reason he could name. The puzzle got glared at. The fort's districts declared minor wars.)

The family's break-glass rules, deployed: The bundled march ("cold is weather, not a wall, twenty minutes, all lungs, around the block; the cold resets the walls; nobody knows why; nobody needs to"). The furniture rotation (the living room, rearranged; "new room, zero dollars; the eyes get a whole fresh country"). The bath in the afternoon (decadent; unscheduled; "the rules bend on purpose so we remember they're ours"). And the opposite hour (everyone does the other person's thing: Theo curated the reading fort; she hummed engine for his ferry epic; Mama built; Papa drew, "the ways of working swap for one hour and everybody comes back liking theirs better and understanding everybody's. The cheapest vacation there is").
