Night 104

The Post Boundary Lesson

Month 4: My Jobs

A temptation this week: the paper-route kid (thirteen; the lane's dawn fixture; the anxious dog's daily treat stop) is quitting in spring. The route: Opening. And the post-hungry heart leapt..." A route, Papa.

A dawn one. With a dog on it... "Papa's counterweight, applied gently at the fence: "You're six in seven months. The route's a thirteen-year-old's post, the dark mornings, the weather, the distances. Some posts have ages on them, and the ages aren't insults.

They're load ratings. You don't put a wagon's wheels on the Willowhawk." The disappointment: real, logged. The extraction, better: "... Load ratings. The posts I hold are rated right, that's why they hold. The coop fits six-almost.

The route fits thirteen. Wanting the bigger post early isn't ambition. It's... wrong-rated hardware. The route'll still be a route when I'm rated for routes." (It was. Sure enough: age eleven, the dawn route, the third dog of that dynasty, the whole winter of it. Rated right by then. Worth the wait entirely.)
