Night 317

The Entry About Papa

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

Night's entry, with its subject in the room, which as it happened made the reading twice the event: "1975. The boy took to the piano at the grange hall like the cows take to oats. He's no farmer. The hands are for something else.

Telling myself early so the telling don't hurt later." (Papa, coffee stopped: "You wrote that when I was seven? Wrote it the night of the grange dance. You played the wrong notes on purpose even then, grinned at the crowd like the flubs were the show.

I knew that night the farm wasn't keeping you. Took me some years to like it. The entry was me... practicing.") The son and the father regarded each other across fifty years of different ink.

"You never once said I should stay. The entry did my saying. Books are for the things that'd come out wrong as speech. You went to your pianos and your town girl and your whole life, and the farm found other hands..."

A nod at the room's smallest Saturday-holder. "and the almanac's got a 2001 entry says 'The boy's girl plays the grange piano like he did. The hands came back around.' Everything at this farm comes back around, son. It just takes the long way. The long ones always land."
