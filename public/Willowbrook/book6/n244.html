Night 244

The Nuance Month Opens

Month 9: Truth With Kindness

At the start of the month, Olivia said, "Mama. The truth rules I got at four, kind and honest, the doors-not-walls, say-the-true-part-loud, they're good rules. But the cases are getting heavier. Six-year-old truths have more moving parts.

This month's job: the rules grow up with the cases." The syllabus, drafted: when the truth helps nobody (does it still get said?). When two people need opposite truths. When the kind thing and the true thing pull hard apart.

When the truth is yours to know but not yours to tell. "The four-year-old rules were arithmetic..." She saved. "This is the long division of honest. Good thing..." The grin. "I got a atlas."
