Night 59

The Wind's Own Story

Month 2: The Kite That Wouldn't Fly

When the final night arrived, the first stars appeared above the Great Willow. The kite month, closing, and tonight the watch gathered inside its subject: a night wind, one of the traveling ones, the far-business kind, moving through the willow's branches for the whole meeting. The tree's gold: streaming. The minutes: taken loud.

"Fitting..." called Oliver Owl over the rush. "this month belonged to this colleague. Let the story show the wind attended its own review. And let the story show what the small one learned of it..." said Stella, her gold flickering in the gusts. "That it cannot be argued with. That it cannot be owned. That the whole sport of the string, and the whole sport of quite a lot else, sits atop a power that comes and goes on its own business.

And that the correct responses are exactly two: fly when it's flying weather, and wait well when it isn't. She also learned..." added Bella, wings folded tight and riding the sway like a professional. "the angle. That the same push can shove you flat or lift you high, and the difference was never the wind.

The difference was always how you met it." A gust took the hill; the whole watch held on; the butterfly, as it happened, looked entirely at home. "Every creature with wings knows it. Now a girl with a string knows it.

The knowledge spreads on the wind, like everything." The gust passed. The branches settled. Far off, the traveling wind went on to its farther business, and the town below lay still under the little line of five stars over one roof.

"Log it..." said Stella, quiet now. "Mmm," hummed the Moon, who pulls the tides but has never once commanded the wind, and respects it accordingly. Goodnight, flyers and waiters of Willowbrook. Meet the push at your best angle. Sleep.
