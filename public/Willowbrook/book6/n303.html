Night 303

The Long Winter, Counted

Month 10: The Long Winter

Month's end, the thaw ticking in the gutters, honest this time; the almanac concurs. The check: On paper, they could list one boredom door. One fort of all forts. One radio drama. One thousand-piece sky (the bird placed by its archivist; the room the bird needed).

One letter marathon (five paid by candle). One someday-box. One tape archive begun (the costs kept; the breaks in the voices). One cabin fever. One skill circuit. One false spring. One invention manifest. Some changes belonged outside the count: that the ideas got no other door than the lobby.

That the winter was the sky and the spring's the bird. That the long dark months, the ones the calendar dreads, produced more of the year's keepers than any gold month did. "Good long winter? The fullest empty ever.

Mama, this month looked like nothing from outside. Snowed in, nothing to do, the long dark. And it built: the fort, the tapes, the circuit, the manifest, the whole spring pre-loaded. The winter's the dahlia of seasons, building underground, spending it all later.

I'll never dread a long winter again. I'll rub my hands at them. The lobby's where everything good checks in first." Remembered. Rub your hands at the long winters. The lobby's open. On we go, the thaw is ticking.
