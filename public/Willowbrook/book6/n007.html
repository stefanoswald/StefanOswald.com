Night 7

Theo's Why Engine

Month 1: How Things Work

Theo's why chains (age almost-four; the engine runs hot) evolved this week, audibly, at dinner, mid-chain: "Why bath? Because muddy. Why muddy...no. How muddy? How does the mud stick? Is mud glue?"

(The table stopped. The founder of the question notebook put down her fork with ceremony.) "Theo. That's a how. You just upgraded. Whys go down forever, they're good but they're a bottomless stairs. Hows have floors.

Hows you can check. Mud sticks because water makes dirt grabby, we can test that, tonight, with the hose..." (They tested it. Dirt: not grabby. Wet dirt: grabby. Dry again: lets go. The boy stood at the hose watching mud dry on his arm with the expression of a person watching a door open.) "Hows have floors," he reported at his bath, triumphant, filthy, scientific.
