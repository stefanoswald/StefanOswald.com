Night 267

The Recipe Adjustment Confession

Month 9: Truth With Kindness

A confession, carried to the bakery with data: "Rosa. The roll recipe you gave our family. I... Changed it. A little honey in the dough. I've been making them modified for a month and everybody likes them better and I felt like a criminal every batch because it's your recipe and, here."

Rosa ate it slowly. Professionally. The answer: "Better for your family's table. Mine stays mine for the bakery, the honey'd fight my jam line. But child..." The flour-laugh. "you thought you sinned? Recipes are letters, not laws.

My mama's roll recipe was her mama's with the butter doubled. The changing is the tradition. A recipe nobody adjusts is a recipe nobody's using, it's the almanac rule: the books stay alive by being written in. You didn't steal my recipe.

You answered it. That's the whole correspondence of cooking, generation to generation: letters, answered, with honey."
