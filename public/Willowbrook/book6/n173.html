Night 173

The Handstand Sisters

Month 6: Hard Things Take Days

Olivia and Lily decided to learn handstands together. They practiced against a wall, then on the grass, almost every day at recess.

After four weeks, Lily balanced alone for three seconds.

Olivia cheered, and felt jealousy knock at the same time. Her arms were not ready yet. For a moment, Lily's success felt as if it had taken something from her.

Olivia checked the feeling before it could choose her words.

"Three seconds!" she shouted. "You did it!"

The happiness was real. So was the jealousy. Two feelings could arrive together without receiving equal authority.

Two weeks and one day later, Olivia held her own handstand for three seconds while Lily counted aloud.

Their dates did not match. Their work had never promised that they would.

On the walk home, both girls agreed that one person's success was not a slice removed from somebody else's plate. It was more like another candle lit in the same room.
