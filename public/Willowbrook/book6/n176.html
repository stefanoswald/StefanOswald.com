Night 176

The Backstroke Ribbon

Month 6: Hard Things Take Days

The winter swim session closed with its little meet, and the backstroke campaign saved its summit: Straight. (Two laps, lane-true, the rope unattacked. Third place of six, "the ribbon's white, which is the honest color for a fixed-arm season. The blue ones go to the kids on their day four hundred.

I'm on day sixty. The map says blue's two seasons out and the map's been right about everything else.") The real prize, claimed at the pool exit, dripping: "The arm, Mama. It went underground this week.

I stopped steering and it stayed straight and I thought about, dinner. The dinner test. Everything that runs while I think about dinner is mine forever. The collection grows: buttons, laces, the route, the C scale, and now one whole arm."
