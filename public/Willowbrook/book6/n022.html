Night 22

The Fledge Day

Month 1: How Things Work

Part four came in its own season, by the era's law: Fledge day. The nursery had gone crowded and loud (Peep, Repeat, Loud One, and Fourth: feathered now, jostling, wing-testing on the nest rim daily). And on a bright loud afternoon, the watch was summoned by a change in the peeping, and the porch got its finale: One by one, flappy, graceless, one scared hop each, wings out mostly in panic (Oliver Owl's exact forecast, as it happened, from a session two books ago: nobody tells you flight starts as the smallest most terrified hop), the nursery emptied. Peep to the rail.

Repeat to the hedge. Loud One straight down, a bounce, then up, indignant. And Fourth, the smallest, the longest waverer, a whole extra hour on the rim while the watch held its breath and didn't help (the shell rule; the opening comes from inside or it doesn't count), Fourth flew farthest. Cleared the fence.

Gone into the wide green world like the wait had been fuel. The empty nest, at dusk. The strange full-empty feeling, held at the lamp: "Nine days of eggs. Three weeks of nursery. One afternoon of leaving.

The long stories end fast when they finally end... The mud room stays up dough. The book says she might come back next year. The porch keeps the shape. That's what porches are for."
