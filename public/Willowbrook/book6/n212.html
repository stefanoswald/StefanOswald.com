Night 212

The Marble's Provenance

Month 7: Team Willowbrook

When the final night arrived, Stella settled her glow over Willow Hill. The garden month, closing, and the watch turned, unusually, to its oldest member. Because the question on the tally book was the marble's, and only one colleague was on duty the night it went into the ground. The Moon, who speaks perhaps once a decade, in actual words, and whom the whole hill went silent to hear, said: "Nineteen fifty-two.

A boy. The lot was his family's yard then, before the mill closed and the fences came. He buried the marble the night they moved away, dug the hole with a spoon, put in his best shooter, and said: 'so part of me stays.'" A pause, moon-length.

"He grew up two towns over. He is eighty-one years old. And in June, the watch may check the workday logs, he came back with his own trowel and cleared weeds beside a girl with a wheelbarrow, on ground he last touched with a spoon." The hill did the arithmetic.

Gus-senior. The trowel. The window. The Warden of the Nothing-Hours, warden, all along, of his own buried childhood. "He has not found the marble..." The Moon continued. "The small one's brother found it.

It sits in the shed marked first exhibit. And one evening soon, an old man will look in the museum box, and know his own shooter instantly, and the ground will have kept its kid for seventy years and handed him back at last." The Moon closed its eye. "Log it however the owl likes.

I only came to say: I watch the long ones. The long ones always land.", and something older than gold. Goodnight, buried marbles of Willowbrook. The ground keeps its kids. The long ones always land. Sleep.
