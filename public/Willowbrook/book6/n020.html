Night 20

The Willowhawk Wing Test

Month 1: How Things Work

Wing day. The Hawk, complete now, wheels, tail, triangled frame, bedsheet wings taut, went to the park for the ground test: staked ropes, a strong June wind, and the question: do the wings even want to? The wind came up the kite hill the way it does, and the Willowhawk answered. Strained the ropes.

Lifted its nose. Went light on its wheels, two inches, three, the stakes creaking, the whole bedsheet contraption leaning into the sky like a dog at a leash... "She wants to," Mateo screamed over the wind. "She actually wants to..."

The detail person, clipboard flapping, checking her list through the gusts: "Wings: Pass. Frame: Holding. Tail: Steering the strain. Look, she's pointing herself at the wind without us..." (the pattern, weathervaning. The tail doing its quiet job.

Systems, passing, one at a time.) The answer, wind-yelled, recorded: "All systems pass. The hawk wants the sky. Remaining: The hill. The day. The nerve."
