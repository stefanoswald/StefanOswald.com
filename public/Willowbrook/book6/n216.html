Night 216

The Unfair Homework Night

Month 8: School Stories

A systems failure this week: Both first-grade classes assigned major projects, same due date, a calendar collision nobody caught. The load: genuinely unfair. The kid-grumbling: universal. The response, run by the book: not the grumble, the channel.

A note, drafted at office hours, co-signed by eleven classmates: "Dear mr. Okafor and ms. Price: The calendars collided. We checked: Both projects are good projects. Both due thursday is the problem. Proposal: Stagger them.

Respectfully, the undersigned (Who will do both projects either way, but better if staggered)." The result: staggered. Plus, the part she saved deepest. Mr. Okafor read the note aloud, and said: "This is what fixing things looks like, people.

Name the problem, skip the blame, propose the repair, commit either way. The way things worked made an error. The way things worked thanks you." ("the way things worked apologized, Mama. Systems can. They just need the complaint to arrive shaped like a repair.")
