Night 50

The Loaner's First Customer

Month 2: The Kite That Wouldn't Fly

The charter's loaner part got its first customer this week: a kid at the hill's bottom, watching. (The watching kind that isn't chosen, she can tell now; five years of feet and benches; the wanting-in reads different from the resting.) The offer, made per rule, from five feet, no looming: "Hill rule: everybody gets offered the line. The gold one's the loaner.

Bridle's set. You want the flying part?" The shy yes. The coached launch. And by dusk: a new flyer, forty feet of borrowed gold overhead, wearing the exact first-line face (there is only one first-line face; the hill's veterans all wear the memory of it).

"Keep the loaner till Saturday..." The squadron ruled. "First week's free. The hill's a library, basically. Kites are just books with more wind."
