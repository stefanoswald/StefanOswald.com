Night 143

The Water Cycle Play

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

Mr. Okafor assigned the water cycle. The class, knowing its resident systems-dramatist, voted for a play. (She wrote it in two office-hours sessions. The story has obtained the script.) "The long trip: a water story."

Cast: the whole class as drops. One sun (the beige kid, "the most reliable person for the most reliable job"; he took it as the honor it was). One narrator. The plot, by the script: the drops rise (sun does the up), ride the sky, fall on the hill, split, some to the brook, some to the dark, all to the sea, all up again.

Forever. No drop lost. No drop new. "The water you drank today..." The narrator's closing line, delivered to a gym of families. "Has been everywhere and everyone's. The dinosaurs drank your bath. The whole earf shares one cup.

Treat the cup kind. The end." (Standing ovation. The councilman attended, he attends her things now; the file grows. The dinosaur line entered town vocabulary within the week, by the bakery's listening post.)
