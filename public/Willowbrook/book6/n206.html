Night 206

The Empty Beds Ceremony

Month 7: Team Willowbrook

The season closed the way this family closes things: With care. The tucking (mulch, bench-supervised, every bed blanketed, "the beds sleep like the brook sleeps: not stopped; private"). The tool cleaning. The barrels drained.

The stand's box emptied one last time (final note, unsigned: "Took nothing. Left this: Best thing the town built in forty years. See you in spring"). And the thanking, row by row, led by its smallest bed-holder, because Theo asked to lead this year, and the routine passes down warm: "Thank you, beds.

Thank you, dirt. Thank you, bees, you're the staff. Thank you, worms. Grandpa says you're the real staff. Thank you, sorry-flower corner, you made eleven apologies happen. Sleep good. The winter's your Sunday. We'll come back at the uncovering.

The garden says nigh-nigh." (The garden said nigh-nigh. The string lights held gold a moment past their timer, the story noted it and offers no explanation; the story has stopped explaining the gold. It comes when this family thanks things. It always has.)
