Night 293

The Board Game Tournament of Houses

Month 10: The Long Winter

The someday-box's game went town-scale this week: The tournament of houses. (Snowed-in bracket; each house plays its local matches; results relayed by phone and window-code; the standings chalked on the bakery's window by the grown-ups. Rosa's counter is the town's scoreboard now; the listening post accepts all franchises.) The bracket's drama, as relayed: Mateo's house fell to the sheep strategy remotely ("Theo's farm method traveled, somebody leaked the patent," she leaked it; sure enough; skills only keep what they give and she gives everything, it's pathological and correct).

The final: her house versus Mrs. Hollis's ("she plays with Gus-Senior on Tuesdays now. The bench games; nobody told me the bench winter'd at Number Nine with board games"). The result: Number Nine, by three points, on a move the story can only describe as forty years of teacher-cunning.

("We lost the bracket and won the reveal, Mama: the bench runs a whole winter game circuit and nobody knew. The second grid's got a games division. The town keeps growing new rooms. You never finish the map. That's the map's whole point.")
