Night 313

The Entry About the First Tractor

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

Night's entry: "1968. Bought the tractor. Retired the team. The work goes faster. The fields go quieter. Both true. Don't let anybody tell you progress is simple." The story: the horse team (Duke and Bell; his father's training; the field work paced to hooves and breath for forty years).

The tractor (the arithmetic was undeniable; the fields fed more people faster; "the hungry don't care how poetic your plowing is"). And the both-true, held whole for fifty years: "I never regretted the tractor and I never stopped missing the team. People'll tell you to pick one feeling. Don't.

The picking is the lie. Progress is a trade, darlin', you get, and you give, and the honest ones keep both receipts. The tractor receipt says: more fed. The team receipt says: the fields used to breathe. I keep them in the same drawer. That's the whole entry."
