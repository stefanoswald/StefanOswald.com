Night 16

How Eggs Work

Month 1: How Things Work

The watch continued (day four; the robin knows them now; the porch corner has been ruled non-looming), and the waiting filled, per family method, with the how: "The egg is free things at once, Theo: a room, a lunchbox (the yellow part is food, the whole pantry packed before the journey), and a door (the shell looks locked but it's built to open exactly once, from the inside, when the bird's ready, the bird has a special tooth on its beak just for the opening, a one-time key, it falls off after). A tooth key," Theo repeated, undone. "The bird comes with a key. Everybody comes with their key, the book basically says.

The opening's gotta be from the inside, dough. That's the rule. You can't open an egg for a bird, it needs the pushing to get strong enough for the air. The fight with the shell is the first lesson. The door and the teacher are the same thing."
