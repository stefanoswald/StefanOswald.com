Night 178

The Recital Piece Assembles

Month 6: Hard Things Take Days

Assembly week: the four phrases of "The Gentle Brook," learned separate, per law, got married. The first whole run-through: lumpy at the seams ("the phrases know each other but they're not family yet, the marrying takes its own days; the map allotted them"). The tenth: smoother. The twentieth, Thursday, gold hour, Steady ticking its thousand answers: It flowed.

Start to finish, the little piece running like its namesake, riffle, pool, riffle, home. And from the doorway, unplanned audience: Papa, coffee stopped halfway to his mouth, doing the barn-dust face at "the whole thing, played whole, by his kid, on the family instrument where the wrong notes live. The seams went underground..." She reported, hands still on the keys.

"Somewhere around run fifteen. The piece is mine now, dinner-test mine. Two weeks to the recital and the map's got nothing left but polish and nerve. The nerve system's already trained. The nerve system's been training for three years."
