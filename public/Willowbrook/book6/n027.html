Night 27

How Freckles Happen

Month 1: How Things Work

Summer brought the annual freckle bloom, and the year's lens turned on skin: The science, library-checked: freckles are the skin's tiny sun-painters, each dot a spot where the melanin crew works extra. "They're not damage, Lily, the book says they're protection. Little umbrellas, painted on. Your skin watches the sun and answers it.

The freckles are a conversation." The mirror session, comparing constellations: "Yours make the Big Dipper if you squint. Mine's more of a... small confused triangle." And Lily's contribution, the one the story keeps: "My grandma says freckles are where the summer signs its name.

Every year it signs again. The kids who fade in winter, the summer just wrote in pencil. Mine's pen. The summer really means me."
