Night 157

The Row That Behaved

Month 6: Hard Things Take Days

Day nine of wool: The row that behaved. Twenty stitches, even, unswollen, correct, and somewhere in its middle, the thing the multi-day mountains all hide: The rhythm. ("The hands went underground for a minute, bear. Loop-through-off, loop-through-off, the fingers ran it and I just... watched them go.

One whole minute of the craft doing itself.") The minute ended (row ten grew a mystery hole). But the minute happened, "and once the underground takes the first minute, it takes the rest eventually. The buttons proved it. The laces. The reading. The minute is the foot in the door."
