Night 322

The Month's Last Entry

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

The suitcase reappeared (the farm calls; the thaw's chores; this month spending its last fire), and the final session wrote itself into the book, Joe's hand, while the family watched: "[Date]. A month in town with the grandchildren. Read the book aloud for the first time in its life. Findings: The entries hold up.

The grief pages lightened in the reading. The carried things want company, not vaults. The pig still brings the house down. The girl writes in the book now. Fourth hand. The boy hums the engine parts.

The town came to a reading and four new books got started. Fifty years I fought this book was the farm's memory. This month learned me: It's the family's seed stock. You don't keep seed to keep it.

You keep it to plant. Planted plenty this month. J." (the story reproduces it entire because the story could not summarize it better, and knows when it's outmatched. The suitcase left Saturday. The book, as it happened this carefully, left with him.

It wasn't time yet. The Saturdays keep. But the fourth hand's pages stay in it, and the ink, as the man said, is drying on the decision.)
