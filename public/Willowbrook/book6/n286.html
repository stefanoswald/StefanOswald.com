Night 286

The Puzzle's Bird

Month 10: The Long Winter

The puzzle-season closed this week: The sky, complete. (Nine hundred ninety-nine pieces of gradient blue, assembled across the whole deep winter, some days three pieces, some days none, the table faithful throughout.) And the last piece, the bird, the sky's one inhabitant, hunted since January, was found... exactly where the youngest searcher had put it, week one, "somewhere safe" (his pocket, then the treasure box, then forgotten, the apprentice archives everything now; the family accepts the hazards). The placement ceremony: His.

(The finder-loser-finder, lifted to the table, one piece, the sky's whole point, pressed home.) "The bird's in the sky..." He announced, and the winter, a quick check confirmed the window, chose that exact hour to begin, at last, honestly, to melt. "A thousand pieces of empty and one bird..."

She said at the lamp. "And the bird's the whole picture. The sky was always just... The room the bird needed. That's why the hard puzzles are all sky, Mama. You build the enormous patient empty, piece by piece, all winter, and then the bird has somewhere to fly.

The winter was the sky. The spring's the bird. It's coming. I can hear the brook thinking about it under the lid."
