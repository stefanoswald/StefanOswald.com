Night 318

The Weather Rhymes Lesson

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

Night's session went to the back pages: The weather rhymes. (The folk forecasts, collected over fifty years, each one field-tested and graded, because of course Joe graded them:) "'Red sky at night, shepherd's delight', grade: B-plus. The red evening means dry air west, and weather comes from the west, mostly. Real physics in folk clothes.

'Low swallows, rain follows', grade: A. The bugs fly low when the pressure drops; the swallows follow the bugs; the birds are reading the barometer with their dinner. 'Cows lie down before rain', grade: C. Cows lie down before rain, after rain, and instead of rain.

Cows lie down. Don't build on cows. The rhymes are the almanac's grandparents..." The lesson closed. "Attention, banked, before writing was cheap, the data compressed into poems so it'd carry in heads. Half of them hold.

Grade them yourself, always. The folk knew things and the folk guessed things, and the respect is in the checking, not the swallowing."
