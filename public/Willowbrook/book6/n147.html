Night 147

The Icicle Farm

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

The icicle orchestra went agricultural this year: The icicle farm. ("Why wait for random? The eave's a instrument and instruments get tuned. Papa, the drip lines. If we clear the gutter there and pack the snow there, the drips concentrate and the keys grow long...")

The cultivation: managed drip lines. The harvest: the eave's best season on record, a full two-octave run of ice keys, bass monsters to tinkling babies, tuned nightly by the cold itself, retuned every thaw-freeze. The concerts: nightly, mittened, the family routine expanding (the hello song; Twinkle; one original composition, "The cold sonata..." Which is mostly rhythm and entirely serious).

And the farm's philosophy, delivered to its junior farmer: "The winter grows instruments, Theo, if you set up the conditions and then let the cold do the craft. That's all farming, the coop, the rows, the icicles. You don't make anything grow. You make the conditions and stand back respectful. The growing was always the world's half of the deal."
