Night 131

The Stepping Stone Count

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

Setup week at the brook: The stepping stone count. (Seven stones, the classic crossing, used by every kid in town history, and never once documented. "The town's oldest bridge and it's got no paperwork.") The count, stone by stone: "One: the starter.

Flat, faithful, never wobbled in recorded history. Two: the wide one. Rest stop rated. Three: The wobbler. (Everybody knows. Nobody wrote it down. Written down now: 'Lands left-side-safe. Right edge is a liar.')

Four and five: the quick pair, momentum stones; don't stop; they're a sentence, not two words. Six: the tall one. Dry in all waters. The emergency island. Seven: the lander. Home free." And the teaching crossing, Theo's first solo, coached from the bank with the count in hand: "Stone three lands left, Theo!

Four-five is a sentence, don't stop in the middle of it!" (Crossed. Dry. Both fists up on stone seven, the whole brook applauding in riffle.)
