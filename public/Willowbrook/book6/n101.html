Night 101

The Overcommitment Wall

Month 4: My Jobs

This month's reckoning arrived on schedule: The pile-up week. Coop Saturday plus cart Tuesday plus Wednesday walking plus the sink plus homework plus the corner plus Fridays at Number Nine plus the bench plus, and by Thursday the pattern of her hit the wall machines hit: the snapping-at-Theo, the skipped notebook, the tears over nothing. The lamp took the whole check: "I'm doing everything and enjoying nothing. The posts were supposed to pay in counting-on and this week they just...

Cost. What broke? Nothing broke, love. You found the other wall, not the can-I wall. The should-i-all-at-once wall. Listen: even the willow doesn't hold every bird every night. Posts are real, so they weigh real.

And the arithmetic of a person is the same as a kite's: past a certain load, nothing flies. The skill isn't holding more. The skill is knowing your good numbers, like the wind. Eight to twelve, gusting fifteen.

You're flying in a twenty-five." The trim, negotiated: the sink post handed to its next volunteer. The rest: held, spaced, breathing.
