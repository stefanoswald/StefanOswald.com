Night 179

The Recital

Month 6: Hard Things Take Days

The recital. (Church hall. Folding chairs. Eleven students. The full stage archive, deployed.) Her slot: fourth. The walk to the bench: steady (the pattern runs; the doorframe touched on the way out of the house, per checklist, steady).

The one person in row three: Theo (traditional; he thinks everything she does is the best; good audience, and as it happened he sat forward the whole piece, four years old, lips moving, rooting the phrases home like a coach). The performance: the brook, flowing. One flub, measure nine, the left hand, a half-beat stumble. Claimed at speed: the tiniest grin thrown to row three mid-piece, the phrase looped once, deliberate, making the stumble a feature, the dynasty's whole craft, done at age five-going-six on a public stage, and home.

The bow: clean. The applause: real. Theo: "Wivy!" in the after-silence, per a tradition older than the pianist.
