Night 292

Summer in the Seed Catalogs

Month 10: The Long Winter

The seed catalogs arrived while snow covered the fields. Grandpa Joe called them July in envelopes.

At the farm table, he and Olivia chose the yellow tomatoes and Margaret's okra again. The community garden needed pumpkins and wildflowers for pollinators.

For the question row, they picked two plants neither fully trusted: glass gem corn, whose jeweled kernels looked impossible, and luffa, the vine that eventually became a sponge.

"We grew luffa once," Joe said.

"Some strange things should be grown twice so we know the first time was not a dream."

Joe's hands moved slowly over the order form. Arthritis made small boxes and thin lines difficult. Olivia did the writing while he chose varieties and quantities.

The work had once belonged mostly to his hands. Now his knowledge and her handwriting shared it.

Outside, the farm slept beneath ice. On paper, summer had already begun.
