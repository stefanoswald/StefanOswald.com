Night 235

Six Years of Lily

Month 8: School Stories

Olivia and Lily marked six years of friendship at the window seat with cocoa and their old photograph.

They had been eleven days apart, bald, and holding hands in a music class. Neither remembered it.

They remembered the failed sleepover and the successful one, the move to two different streets, nine whistle signals, the just-listening agreement, and the autumn when their friendship had wobbled but not broken.

"Six years," Lily said. "We never ran out."

Their friendship had made room for new people without becoming smaller. June, Wren, Noor, Dana, and the others had not taken pieces away. Each new connection had added another path through the web.

They sat without speaking for a long time, the comfortable silence that had become one of their best traditions.

Some friendships begin with a memorable first meeting. This one began before memory and kept becoming more visible as the girls grew able to understand what it held.
