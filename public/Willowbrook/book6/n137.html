Night 137

The Puddle Ecosystem

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

The great puddle (the museum one; the stomping one; the lane's oldest institution) got the full survey this week, because this month asks about all waters, even the temporary: The findings, over its six-day life: day one: rain-fresh, sky-mirror. Day two: the first visitors (sparrows bathing; "the puddle's a bird bath with no subscription"). Day three: mosquito wigglers. Day four: a butterfly drinking at the muddy edge ("Bella's cousin, the mud's got minerals; the book says butterflies need puddle-edges; the puddle is butterfly groceries").

Day five: shrinking, ringed with its own tide lines..." A flood-line bridge stone, but in miniature, but in fast-forward. "Day six: gone. The dried bed, printed with bird tracks and one boot heel.

"Six days, Lily. A whole pond life, visitors, groceries, tide lines, the end, in six days. The puddle's a brook's whole story at flip-book speed. The temporary things aren't lesser things. They're the same things, faster. Care for all small guests, the hamster rule. It covers puddles."
