Night 121

The Source Expedition

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

This month opened with the oldest unanswered question in the notebook: Where does the brook start? The expedition: family-scale, boots, thermos, the bilingual map (dragons marked upstream, "the unchecked parts; today we check them"). The journey: two hours up the narrowing water, the brook going from town-brook to wood-brook to a silver thread in the bracken, to the source: a spring. A dinner-plate of clear water, rising quietly out of the hill's own dark, no ceremony at all. "...

That's it? The whole brook? The stepping stones and the peepers and the skating pond and the mill history and the sea eventually, it all starts at a plate of water coming out of a hill? Everything starts about this size..." said Papa. "Rivers. Towns. Families. You've seen the photo of your mama and me at one table for two."
