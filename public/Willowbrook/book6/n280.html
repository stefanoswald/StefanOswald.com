Night 280

The Window Fern Farm, Revisited

Month 10: The Long Winter

The cold snapped proper this week and the windows grew, and the old wonder got its handing-down: Theo's first frost-fern morning, guided by the veteran curator: "Theo. The window grew a garden in the night. Nobody planted it, the cold did, one breath-drop at a time, and ice grows in patterns when it grows slow because water's secretly fern-shaped all the way down, the snowflakes are the same confession. Now: the rules.

Peepholes cost ferns, two per morning, budgeted; the looking uses up the looked-at, so spend careful. And the haaah, is both the destroyer and the only way in. Everything's like that a little. Be gentle with your HAAAHs."

(He budgeted his peepholes like a banker all winter. "The ferns came back," he reported daily, amazed daily. They come back. That's the whole magic. It never once got old, a quick check confirmed both children's whole childhoods: it never once got old.)
