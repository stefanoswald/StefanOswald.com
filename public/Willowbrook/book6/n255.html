Night 255

The Honest Look Inward

Month 9: Truth With Kindness

This month's hardest customer, faced at the lamp: Herself. ("The kind and honest goes out all day, Mama. Tonight it goes in. The mirror gets a session.") The self-check, kind and honest, both barrels: "True: I fix too fast still.

Year three of the growth jar and the hands still twitch at wobbles. True: I like being the one who knows. The umpire, the consultant, the founder, there's a puffed thing that purrs when the town needs me, and I feed it, and it's not all bad but it's not all good and it wants watching. True: the not-digging holds for everybody except, sometimes.

Me. I dig at my own basements at two a.m. The worry inflation. The am-i-too-much. Kind..." And here Mama watched her switch registers, deliberate, the way she'd switch for any friend. "kind: the fixer saved a garden and a lunch line this year.

The knower gives it all away free, the kit only keeps what it gives, and I give it. And the two a.m. Digger... needs the same rule everybody gets: the basement explains; it never licenses. Be as gentle with the me-basement as the everybody-basements. That's At month's end, Signed, both of us."
