Night 249

The Umpire Summer

Month 9: Truth With Kindness

The court drafted her this summer: Umpire. (The four-square disputes had outgrown best-of-five; the players wanted a neutral; the neutral they wanted was the town's known teller-of-true.) The post's hard part arrived week one: Lily, out, on her call. (Line ball.

Out by an inch. Best friend's eyes meeting umpire's eyes across the chalk.) The call: Out. (The truth doesn't check the friendship first. That's the whole post.) And the after, at the window seat, both of them careful: "The call was right..." said Lily, eventually. "I checked the chalk after. By a inch. I'd have paid a week of window seats to call it in..." said the umpire. "And if I ever start paying that price, take the whistle away from me, because the whole court runs on the umpire's calls costing her something and her paying anyway. The day the calls are free is the day they're worthless."
