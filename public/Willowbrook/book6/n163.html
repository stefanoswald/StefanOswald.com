Night 163

Marcus's Chapter Book

Month 6: Hard Things Take Days

The table's other campaign summited this week: Marcus finished a chapter book. (His first. A short one. Entirely his, finger under the words, gears one through three, the tap signal used twice in eighty pages.)

The last page turned at the office-hours table, and the room held still the way rooms do when a long thing lands. "... Done..." He said. And then, testing it: "I read a chapter book."

And then, the part the story keeps: "The b's parked straight the whole last half. I didn't even check the fists. The bed trick went... underground. The underground takes everything eventually..." The table's proprietor confirmed, producing, because she'd had it ready for three weeks, because maps include their summits, a bakery roll with a candle in it.

"The uphill kids get the biggest summits, Marcus. The flat kids never get this feeling. Trade you nothing for it."
