Night 197

The Garden Postcards

Month 7: Team Willowbrook

The garden went postal this week, because seasons this good get mailed: To the aunts: photos plus the report: "The town grew a garden and the garden grew the town. Also I knit now. The dynasty's safe. O."

To Jordan, pen pal of three years: the before/after of the lot, plus: "Remember the creek that sounds different before rain? Our lot sounded different before growing. Empty things do that. Listen for it in your town.

Then write a proposal. It's one page. The lever's free." And to Ms. Halloran, author to author, annual correspondence standing: "The underneath of the garden story is: The caring was in the line all along.

Feel free to steal it. That's how the trade works, a great author told me. O." (Jordan's town, later, proposed its own lot the following spring, creek-adjacent, kid-drafted, council-approved. The lever travels by mail. It always did.)
