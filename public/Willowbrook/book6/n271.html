Night 271

The Month's Last Case: The Compliment She Believed

Month 9: Truth With Kindness

This month's last case closed at the lamp, quiet as the biggest ones close: Mrs. Hollis, at Friday tea, had said, mid-conversation, factual as weather: "You are, child, the finest listener I have met in seventy years, and I include the professionals." And the reflex, the deflection, the too-hot toss-back. Loaded... and didn't fire.

She'd looked at the old teacher, run this month's whole machinery in one breath (true? She thinks so, and she's got seventy years of data. Kind? Yes. Mine to take?... Mine to take.), and said: "Thank you.

I practice it. On Fridays, mostly. With you. And it landed, Mama..." The lamp heard. "All the way in. First compliment in maybe my whole life that I didn't dodge or discount or spend. I just...

Banked it. And you know what it took? The whole month. The mirror session, the false-modesty check, the accurate books. Turns out the hardest truth-wif-kindness was never telling it to other people. It was letting it in when it comes back."
