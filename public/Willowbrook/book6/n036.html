Night 36

Theo's Kite Day

Month 2: The Kite That Wouldn't Fly

Theo's turn. (His kite: the old red daypack's cousin, small, sturdy, chosen for forgiveness. The coach: experienced now, four days deep in kite law.) The lesson, delivered his-size: "The bridle's set. I did the fiddly part; you get the flying part.

The wind does the up. Your job is the line. When it pulls, give. When it sags, run. The kite tells you what it needs through your hands. Listen with your fingers." The launch: coach-assisted.

The climb: wobbly, then real. And the moment, the story was there, when the line went into the small hands alone, and the kite held, his, forty feet of sky on a string in a four-year-old's fists: "I got the whole sky! Wivy! I'm holding the whole sky!!"

(Heard, the story attests, at the duck pond. Possibly at the farm. The yell of a person meeting the line for the first time. Everyone's first-line yell is the same yell. The park's veterans all smiled the same smile.)
