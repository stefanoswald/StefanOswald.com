Night 6

What Makes the Whistle Whistle

Month 1: How Things Work

The carved whistle went under the year's lens: "Papa. Why does it whistle? It's just wood with a slot." The physics, dusk-grade: the air comes in as one river and hits the sharp edge, and the edge can't decide which side, so the air flutters, fast as anything, and the flutter is the sound.

"A whistle is air arguing with an edge, very fast, on purpose. The sound is a argument?! Then the carving matters because. Mateo shaped the argument. He tuned the disagreement till it sang." A long look at the little wooden thing.

"That's what instruments are, aren't they. Shaped arguments. The piano. Papa's wrong notes. The peepers. Everything that sings is air fighting something beautiful."
