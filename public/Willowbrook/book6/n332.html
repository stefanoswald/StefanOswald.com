Night 332

The Bench Faculty Photo

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

The exchange's first season closed with, obviously; the town has learned its forms. A photo. The library steps. The faculty entire: twelve elders, twenty kids, the librarian at the door, the lantern above them all, lit, as always, by the rota, by the promise.

The composition, arranged by the councilman-photographer with wedding-grade care: the mending hands and the map hands. The story chair. Gus-Senior with the marble in hand. Petrov holding, at the kids' insistence, one wrench, ceremonially.

And the youngest founding petitioner, they put her in the middle and she engineered herself to the edge between shots ("the program's the picture, not me, the whole point is it runs without..." and the photographer caught that, mid-protest, mid-gesture, half-turned toward her whole faculty, and that frame, later, is the one they printed. It hangs beside the garden photo. The caption, chosen by the elders over her objection, which the story and the far future both overrule: "The one who asks.")
