Night 188

Theo's Bed

Month 7: Team Willowbrook

The lot's smallest bed went to its youngest holder: Theo. (Solo. His name on the stake. T-h-e-o, the ghost letter standing proud. The bed: one square yard. The seriousness: immeasurable.) His planting plan, spoken to the family and done to the letter: "Sunflowers (the tall kind, like the wall of faces.

My wall now). Carrots (the stubby kind; the soil argues with long ones; Grandpa said). And a dandelion corner." (A pause at the dandelion corner, family-wide. Dandelions are weeds. Nobody said it. He said it for them:) "Dandelions are only weeds if you don't got a job for them.

My dandelions are the sorry-flower supply. They're a crop. I'm farming apologies." (Farming apologies. The story has covered agriculture on four continents of this family's doing and rules this the finest crop declaration on file.)
