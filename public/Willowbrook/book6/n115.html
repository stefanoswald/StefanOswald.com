Night 115

The Library Lock-Up

Month 4: My Jobs

A Tuesday privilege this week, earned by a season of faithful carts: The closing. (Staying past hours, Mama reading in the stacks, while the librarian ran the shut-down, "every keeper should see a building go to sleep once. Buildings have bedtimes. Somebody runs them.")

The routine, witnessed: the stragglers, gently shepherded ("closing a library is mostly closing people, books don't resist"). The returns, final sweep. The thermostat, the windows, the wainscoting corners checked ("old building..." said the librarian, entirely straight-faced, leaving a corner biscuit. "for the mice," the postmaster's toll, paid nightly, the two keepers' eyes meeting over it in the way of professionals who both know and both don't say).

The lights, section by section, the shelves going dark like a town lying down. And last, always last, the rota's oldest line, the stair, the little room, the lantern: checked, fed, burning. "Goodnight, building..." said the librarian at the door, and turned the key.

"Does it hear? Forty years I've said it. The building's never once failed to be here in the morning. Draw your own conclusions, child."
