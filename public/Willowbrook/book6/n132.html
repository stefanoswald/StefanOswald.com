Night 132

The Otter Rumor

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

A rumor reached the patrol: An otter. Upstream, past the source trail. ("An otter" in this county is an event; Joe hasn't seen one since his boyhood; the almanac's last otter entry is forty years cold.) The investigation, run right: three dawns.

What they saw, assembled: the mud slide on the far bank. The tracks: five-toed, webbed. The half-shells at the flat rock: a diner's table. And on dawn three: The otter. Two seconds of brown arc through the pool, one liquid loop, gone.

Confirmed. The decision, made on the walk home, weighed like a heavy stone: "We don't tell, Papa. Not the town, not the paper, not even the notebook's public pages. A crowd would come. Otters leave when crowds come, the book says.

The blank-page rule: some things you guard instead of witness. Joe gets told, he's waited forty years; he's earned the knowing. The almanac gets one line in the private column. And the otter gets the bend, undisturbed, with its slide and its shells and its two-second loops. That's the whole report."
