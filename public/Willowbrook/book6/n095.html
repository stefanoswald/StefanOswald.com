Night 95

The First Failure of Office

Month 4: My Jobs

Week two of the coop: The short count. Rain, dusk, the head count run quick because of the wet. Eleven. Twelve hens. Eleven counted. The cold drop in the stomach, the posts have real floors under them; this is the difference; a missed chore is a scolding, a missed hen is a hen.

The search: coop, run, hedgerow, rain hardening, and Joe, appeals court, at her shoulder, not taking over. Found: under the barn ramp, wedged, wet, indignant, alive. (The gate had swung; the wind's doing; the latch worn..." The crooked tooth. "diagnosed on the spot.

Petrov-fixed by Sunday.) The debrief, dripping, hen restored: "I ran the count quick because of the rain. But the rain's exactly when the count matters most, the weather that rushes you is the weather that takes hens. The post doesn't get easier on hard days.

The post gets realer. Now you know what holding is..." said Joe. "Fifty years, and that's the whole of it: the job is realest exactly when you least feel like doing it right."
