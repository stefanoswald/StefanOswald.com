Night 4

The Rain Machine

Month 1: How Things Work

Rain day. Window. And the insides question, aimed up: "The rain machine. I know the parts, puddle, up, cloud, down, again. But what powers it? Machines need a motor." The answer, traced on the steamed glass: The sun.

The sun lifts the water. The cold lets it drop. The whole sky is a machine with one motor ninety-three million miles away, running on light. "The sun carries the ocean upstairs? Every day?

Theo, the sun is the strongest thing there is and it works invisible. You never see the carrying. You only see the rain when it lets go." And Theo, five months from his own chain now, saved his version: "The sun does the up.

The down does itself." (The sun does the up; the down does itself. The story has heard physicists say it worse.)
