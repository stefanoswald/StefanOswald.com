Night 250

The Compliment Inflation Crisis

Month 9: Truth With Kindness

An economics case, spotted at the court: Compliment inflation. (A sweet kid, genuinely sweet, calls everything "Amazing." Every serve: amazing. Every sandwich: amazing. Result, observable: her amazings buy nothing now. The kids' faces don't even flicker.

The word spent itself to zero.) The intervention, gentle, economist to economist: "Your nice is real, everybody knows it. But words are money, and 'amazing' got printed too much. Try the specific-true, it's the gold standard: 'your serve got spin now.'

'The sandwich trade was fair.' Specific-true can't inflate, every one's minted fresh from a real thing you really saw. One of those is worth fifty amazings. I mapped it years ago; the physics hold."

(The kid converted. Slowly, then wholly. By August her rationed, minted compliments moved kids visibly, the flickers returned. "The nicest kid on the court got a currency reform..." The notebook saved. "and now her nice reaches.

That was the whole point. Kind without true is a currency crisis. True is what makes kind spendable.")
