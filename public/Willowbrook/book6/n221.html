Night 221

The Class Pet Campaign

Month 8: School Stories

Room 6 wants a pet. (The campaign: student-launched, petition-formatted, the room learned from the homework note; she's created a monster and the story enjoys it immensely.) The species debate, run with full machinery: The hamster faction. The fish faction (low maintenance; "but you can't love a fish the same..."

Argued the floor;. "Speak for yourself..." Argued June's cousin, who has a beloved goldfish, and the chamber conceded the point). The lizard lobby. And the dark horse, proposed from the corner after research: a classroom worm farm. (.

"Hear me out. Worms: the real staff, Grandpa says. They eat the lunch scraps, the room's garbage becomes the garden's dirt, we watch the whole compost engine work, and nobody has to take them home for summer. The worm farm is a pet that's also a machine that's also a lesson that's also free.")

(The worm farm won. Democracy, presented with a pet that is also a machine that is also a lesson that is also free, knows its business. The lizard kid got appointed Worm Warden as a coalition gesture. The scraps route to the garden. The loops, as it happened, keep closing. This whole town is loops.)
