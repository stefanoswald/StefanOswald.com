Night 169

Theo Jumps from the Dock

Month 6: Hard Things Take Days

Theo had walked to the end of the pool's low dock and turned back for three lessons.

This time, he reached the edge and stayed. His toes curled over the wet boards. Olivia waited at the rail.

She did not shout, "Jump!" She did not promise to catch him. She gave the small nod they had agreed upon: You practiced. You are ready to choose.

Theo looked at the water once more. Then he jumped feet first.

The splash reached Olivia. Theo surfaced with his eyes wide and let out the same wild yell she had used on Kite Hill years before.

"The big rock is next!"

It was not next. His map still held two smaller steps before the high rock at the swimming hole. Olivia did not correct him immediately.

Some victories needed one full minute to be enormous before they became part of a longer road.
