Night 343

The Sky Reveal

Month 12: Almost Six

The parchment came out of hiding this week (the bakery, after close, the counter floured, the consultation's sixth season), and Rosa's poker hand, revealed: The sixth sky: all five previous skies, miniaturized, orbiting, the meadow-gold year, the green room, the open book, the magnifying glass, the gold town, five small constellations in a ring, and at their center, new: a fire. Small, warm, gold-orange, the almanac month's fire, with tiny figures around it. "The years don't replace each other..." said Rosa, tapping the ring.

"That's the design's whole argument. They orbit. Every sky you ever were, still turning, around whatever's warmest now. This year the warmest was the fire and the fifty years read aloud beside it. Next year the center changes and the ring grows.

The cake's a solar system of you, child. Six years in, it finally got big enough to show the mechanism." (the story, which has watched every consultation, every parchment, every reveal, rules the sixth sky the dynasty's masterpiece and notes: she hugged the baker over the counter, flour and all, and the flour transferred, and she wore it home like the aunts' stars. Some receipts you wear.)
