Night 54

The Kite Mail

Month 2: The Kite That Wouldn't Fly

Joe came to the hill this week and taught the old trick: Kite mail. "Paper ring around the line. The wind pushes it up the string, all the way to the kite. We sent messages up when I was a boy.

Your grandma sent wishes. Said the kite was closer to wherever wishes go, so the postage was cheaper up there." The service, inaugurated immediately: rings torn from the notebook, messages written small, launched one by one up the singing line: Theo's: "Hi sky. T."

Hers, considered longer, written smaller: "For M. The wind pages still work. The moods still dance. Your gauge rides in my pocket now. O." (The ring climbed, caught the high river, spun at the kite's knot like it was deciding, and the wind, the story swears, took the hill just then, one warm gust out of a clear sky, and Joe took off his hat.)
