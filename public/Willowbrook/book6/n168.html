Night 168

The Recital Announcement

Month 6: Hard Things Take Days

The piano teacher dropped this month's horizon: Spring recital. ("One piece. Memorized. Performed. All students." The date: two months out. The piece: "The Gentle Brook," as it happened the universe's filing system remains impeccable.) The response, drawing on a five-year stage archive: "The nerves'll come, the solo-line rules apply: pick one person, play to them, two hundred times.

The wrong-note rules: Loaded. If I flub, the flub gets claimed fast and played again on purpose, the family thing, the dynasty's whole craft, now with eighty-eight keys of ammunition. And the practice map: the piece breaks into four phrases; one phrase a week; the last two weeks are assembly, not learning. The pieces of hard things get learned separate and married late. The Hawk taught me. Systems pass one at a time."
