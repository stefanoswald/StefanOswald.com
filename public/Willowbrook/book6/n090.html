Night 90

Bella and the Moth

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

When the month's stories were told, the Great Willow caught Stella's warm light. The differences month, closing, and Bella brought a guest: her cousin. A moth. (Dust-brown. Thick-bodied. Feather-antennaed. The hill went politely quiet, the way rooms do around the unfamous relative.)

"Say what you're all thinking..." said Bella, rainbow wings catching the firefly glow. "She's not colorful. She flies at night. She's the plain cousin." The moth held still, patient, she'd done this tour before. "Now hear the file: she navigates by the moon, steers by that colleague there, personally.

She hears with her antennae, whole feathered instruments; I have stubs. She flies in cold that would ground me, pollinates the night flowers nobody thanks anybody for, and her family's been doing the night shift for fifty million years longer than mine's done the day. The garden runs twenty-four hours. You only clap for my shift.

Mmm..." Hummed the Moon, and as it happened the hum had a warmth in it, the tone of a navigator greeting a sailor who's steered by him for fifty million years. "Log it..." said Stella. "this month's last case, saved by the day shift on behalf of the night: the plain cousin runs the harder route.

The wealth was never in the colors. The wealth is in the fleet, every wing built different, every shift covered, the whole garden held around the whole clock. The small one below..." added Oliver Owl. "would ask the moth the right question.

She'd ask what the night shift builds. She'll get to..." said Bella. "The moth summers here now. The garden's web grows too." Goodnight, day shift and night shift of Willowbrook, bright wings, dust wings, all wings. The whole clock is covered. It always was. Sleep.
