Night 25

How the Almanac Predicts

Month 1: How Things Work

Same visit, second investigation: "Grandpa. The almanac called the pond freeze. It calls the frosts. How? Is it magic or is it math?" Joe slid the big book over. "Neither. It's attention, banked. Fifty years of writing down what happened, first frost dates, pond freezes, which winds brought which weather.

Enough Octobers in the tally book and the next October stops being a stranger. I don't predict, darlin'. I recognize. The almanac's just attention, compounding... Like the feeder chart. The hawk that the missing birds announced.

Enough data and the absences talk." The two keepers of records regarded each other across the table. "Grandpa. The notebook, the chart, the almanac, the rings in the willow, it's all the same machine. Write enough down and the world starts rhyming.

The rhymes are the predictions. Fifty years..." said Joe. "and that's the cleanest anyone ever said it. Put it in the front of the book." (She did. The almanac's flyleaf, her printing: "Attention, banked. The world rhymes if you write enough down." It's there yet.)
