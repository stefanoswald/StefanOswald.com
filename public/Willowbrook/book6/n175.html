Night 175

The Green Hooks

Month 6: Hard Things Take Days

Day eleven of watered nothing: The green hooks. (Dawn; the window cups; the yell, his, this year, the deputy checks first now: "Wivy. The dirt. The dirt's doing the thing...") The tomatoes: up, hooked, unbending toward the light by noon.

The okra: two days behind, then up stubborn (the family line holds; "she comes up like she's got opinions," Joe says of the whole dynasty). The tomatillo: up with its seed-hat still on..." Wearing the shell like Fourth wore the nest, the last-out ones always keep a souvenir. "And the lesson, closed live: "Eleven days of nothing, Theo.

You watered nothing eleven times and never skipped. That's the whole month. The nothing was never nothing, the nothing was the plateau, and under it: This. Remember the dirt-days when the piano goes flat and the bike goes hateful. Everything's got dirt-days. Everything green came through them."
