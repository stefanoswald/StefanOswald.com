Night 99

The Job Nobody Wants

Month 4: My Jobs

Mr. Okafor's room runs on a job wheel, and one job, sure enough, is universally dodged: Brush washer. (Art days. The sink. Twenty brushes, paint-stiff. Cold water. Nobody's post; everybody's punishment.) She took it.

On purpose. Volunteered for a month of it, to the room's open bafflement, because this month asks what posts build, and the study needed the bottom of the market: The findings, from the sink: "Week one: it's as bad as advertised. Cold, orange-fingered, last-out-the-door. Week two: the technique arrives (warm water first, the soap after, the bristles worked flat-handed, there's a craft in here nobody stayed long enough to find).

Week three: the secret dignity shows up. The brushes come out soft. Next art day, twenty kids paint with brushes that work, and nobody knows why art day got better. It's the quiet help again, Mama.

The unwitnessed good. The worst jobs are where the second grid hires. That's why nobody wants them: the pay's all in the invisible."
