Night 247

The Surprise Party Stress Test

Month 9: Truth With Kindness

This month's technical exam: Mateo's surprise party, and Mateo, suspicious, testing her at the fence, direct: "Is something happening Saturday? You're being weird. Truth." The don't-tell held without one lie, the story diagrams the whole maneuver for posterity: "Something's always happening Saturday, it's saturday.

I'm being weird because I'm thinking about the festival kite mods. (True, she was, among other things.) And if you keep fishing, I'm gonna tell your mama you're the kind of person who shakes his presents. " The debrief, at the lamp: "Surprises have opening dates, the old rule held. The new skill is the true dodge: every sentence true, the secret untouched.

It's not lying, Mama. It's... driving around the secret on all-true roads. The roads exist. You just gotta know the map. Should I feel weird that I'm good at it? You should feel careful that you're good at it.

The skill's a tool. The don't-tell it guards has to deserve guarding, a surprise, a dignity, an otter. Guard a rotten secret with it and the same skill goes rotten. Tools take the color of their jobs."
