Night 298

The Snow Lantern Night

Month 10: The Long Winter

Noor's family brought the deep freeze its best import this week: Snow lanterns. (Her mother's tradition from a cold country the family passed through: snowballs, stacked in hollow pyramids, a candle set inside, the whole structure glowing from within like a small patient moon.) The lane's adoption: Instant. By Saturday dusk: eleven lanterns, porch to porch, the whole street lined with glowing snow, the circuit building them house by house, the technique traveling the web's usual way.

And the standing in it, full dark, the lane aglow: "Mama. The snow was already the quiet-maker. Now it's the light-keeper too. The exact same thing that buries the lane is holding candles up and down it.

The winter's not either-or. Nothing's either-or. The whole year keeps saying it and the lanterns are saying it in fire: the hard season will hold your light for you, if you build the holding right."
