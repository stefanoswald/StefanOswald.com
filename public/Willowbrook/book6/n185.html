Night 185

The Old Ones Arrive

Month 7: Team Willowbrook

The bench went in Tuesday (council surplus; Petrov re-slatted it; the plaque, small: "Sit. Advise. The garden's listening"). And the forecast held: The old ones came. By Friday the bench ran a rotating faculty: Mrs.

Hollis. The elder Alvarez. A retired mill man nobody'd met ("the lot's neighbor since before the weeds, he's been watching the nothing out his window for decades; he brought his own trowel the first day"). And Joe, Saturdays, holding seminar.

The knowledge, flowing bench-to-beds: which corner floods. Where the old path ran (its packed line still bakes hard, "plant nothing there; put the path back; the ground remembers its jobs"). Tomato spacing, bean poles, the moon-planting arguments. "The bench cost nothing..."

She reported at the lamp. "and it's the most productive thing in the garden. We planted seats and harvested fifty years of knowing. The old ones weren't waiting to be helped, Mama. They were waiting to be asked."
