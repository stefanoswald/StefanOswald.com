Night 140

The Ice Story Retold

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

The lid advanced this week, pool by pool, the winter closing its patient hand, and the sermon got its annual tour: Owen, Noor (second, but the brook's part is new), and one trailing four-year-old deputy, walked the bank for the telling: "Look: the still pools freeze first. The riffles fight longest, the moving water won't take the lid till it has to. (The busy parts of everything freeze last. Schools.

Kitchens. The bakery at 4:55.) By January: the whole lid. And here's the part everybody gets wrong..." (the kneel; the mitten to the ear; the students, kneeling likewise) "... Listen." (The blblblb. Under the ice. Running. The faces, each in turn: the going-wide.

The story has watched her deliver this exact revelation on this exact bank for three winters and the faces never once disappoint.) "Winter doesn't stop the good things. It makes them quiet for a while. The brook runs all winter, private, under the lid.

Remember it in February when everything feels stopped. Nothing good is stopped. It's all down there. Running. Waiting for the uncovering."
