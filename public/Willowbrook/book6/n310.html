Night 310

The Entry About Nothing

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

Night's entry, chosen by Joe with visible mischief: "Oct 14, 1990. Fixed the gate. Margaret made soup. Rain at four. Good day." The audience: "... That's it? That's it. And I'll tell you why it's the best entry in the book."

The fire took a log; the lesson took the room: "Fifty years on, I can't remember one thing about most of the big days, the fair ribbons, the good prices. But I can walk every inch of that nothing Tuesday: the gate's rusted hinge-pin. The soup was potato. The rain came while I washed up and Margaret hummed at the stove and I stood in the doorway and thought.

I remember thinking it, 'this is the whole of it, right here, and I'm in it.' I wrote good day because I noticed on the day. Most people's good days get discovered years late, in the missing. The almanac's real job, darlin', its whole fifty-year job, is training you to catch them live."
