Night 342

The Hawk 2 Flies

Month 12: Almost Six

The hawk 2. (Sunday. The hill. The crew summons honored, detail person, hardware checked thrice; the winter wing's chalkboard math made real in lath and fresh canvas; the crowd bigger than the first flight's, because the town talks and the town remembers.)

The go check, at the top, per rule: "Mateo. The Hawk's ready. Are you? Two years readier." The run. The lift, and this time not three seconds: Nine. Nine full seconds, a genuine glide, the winter wing carrying its builder halfway down the hill's whole face before the flare and the landing.

On the wheels. Rolling. Intact. The crowd's roar; the crew's pileup; Theo's commentary ("The engine worked!" he hummed it the whole flight, tradition now, the Hawk flies on boy-hum and triangles). ("Nine seconds and a rolling landing..."

The detail person logged, hands shaking slightly with the joy of it. "The first Hawk taught us everything by crashing. The second one learned it all by not. Both flights perfect, Mama. The crash-one and the roll-one. The education and the diploma. Two years apart. The maps work.")
