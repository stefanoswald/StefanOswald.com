Night 79

Theo's Different

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

The study got checked by its youngest reader this week: "Wivy. Everybody in the notebook's got a different. Noor's appointments. Marcus's arrows. Wren's knob. The lefty, that's me, but that's my hand. What's my... my me different?"

The file, read to him at his lamp, compiled over four years of professional observation: "Theo. Your different is: You go first with your heart. The dandelion to the stranger toddler, you were three. The bell in my sightlines, a whole year, secret.

The sorry-flowers you invented. The 'good work in there' to your own knee. Everybody else in this family thinks first and feels close behind. You feel first, instant, whole, out loud, and the thinking catches up after.

That's not littler than thinking, Theo. That's rarer. The tenderhearted are the ones the second grid runs on. You're gonna spend your whole life getting to the hurt things ten seconds before everybody else even sees them.

The town's gonna lean on that someday." (He absorbed this the way he absorbs the big ones: silently, completely, one thumb in the old spot. "... Tenderhearted..." He said at last. "Like a snail. Exactly like a snail, and snails always know where the rain is. Goodnight, Theo.")
