Night 44

The Night Wind Question

Month 2: The Kite That Wouldn't Fly

The lamp took this month's gentlest question: "Mama, where does the wind go at night? The hill goes still at dusk, almost always. The flags drop. Does wind... sleep?" The answer, window cracked so the subject could listen: mostly, yes, the day's wind is the sun's doing (the up-carrying, the warm rising, the cool rushing in, "the whole sky-machine with the one far motor"), and at dusk the motor idles, and the air lies down like everything else.

"So the wind is diurnal. Like me. The sun makes us both run and the dark makes us both quiet." The listening pause; the night air, doing its still small nothing at the curtain. "Except, there's night winds sometimes.

The storm ones. The restless ones. Some winds have farther business. They're crossing, not visiting. Even the air has its travelers... The kite sleeps in the garage and the wind sleeps on the hill and they'll both wake up with the sun. Goodnight, wind. Big day coming. Festival in nine sleeps. Rest your gusts."
