Night 336

Planning the Noticing Party

Month 12: Almost Six

Olivia planned her sixth birthday with Lily and the rest of the fort group.

The guest rule stayed simple: everybody.

For a new tradition, Olivia wanted a noticing party. Each guest would receive a card at the door and write one small, specific thing they appreciated about another person. The cards would be read after cake.

"No empty compliments," Olivia said. "Not just nice or cool. Something you truly noticed."

Lily wrote the first example: MATEO ALWAYS CHECKS THE KITE KNOTS TWICE, EVEN WHEN EVERYONE WANTS TO LAUNCH.

The cake remained a secret. Rosa had hidden her sketch beneath bakery parchment and claimed six years of cake-making had earned her surprise rights.

Olivia agreed.

They left the fort with a guest list, a stack of cards, and absolutely no knowledge of the cake. For a child who collected answers, Olivia was becoming surprisingly good at letting one sweet question wait.
