Night 229

The Report Card, Winter Edition

Month 8: School Stories

The winter report card came home, and got read by a kid who owns the whole methodology now: "Reading: high. Math: high. Handwriting: caught up, the grip rebuild finally reached the grade; the photograph caught up to the parade, like forecast. And the comment section, 'a natural leader who is learning to let others lead.'

Mama. That's the growth jar, still filling, honestly reported. The not-fixing. Year two of the hardest skill. He sees the practicing." The family's reading, per policy: the doing praised, not the smart. The growth jar toasted with cocoa.

And one line of Papa's, saved by the story for its complete accuracy: "Best report card line you'll ever get, kiddo, and I want you to hear it right: Learning to let others lead isn't a criticism softened. At your speed, with your systems? It's the whole difference between a person people follow and a person people escape. He's not grading your past. He's guarding your future."
