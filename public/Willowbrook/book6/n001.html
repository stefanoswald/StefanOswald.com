Night 1

The Insides Year Begins

Month 1: How Things Work

Five began with a broken clock and a question.

The clock had been silent for a year, but Papa had kept it because he liked its brass hands. Olivia spread newspaper across the kitchen table.

"Can I see its insides? Broken things are the only things that let you look."

Papa found a small screwdriver. Together they removed the back and discovered a hidden country of gears and springs. Olivia turned one wheel with the tip of the screwdriver. Another wheel moved, then another.

"Nothing in here works alone," she said.

"That is true of machines," Papa replied.

Olivia looked through the clock toward Mama making tea and Theo building a tower on the floor.

"Maybe it is true of other things, too."

The clock never ticked again. It did something better: it opened. Olivia drew the gears in her notebook and labeled the page, How Things Work.

That night, the clock rested on the mantel, still broken and newly useful, while the little house grew quiet around it.
