Night 58

The Kite Month, Counted

Month 2: The Kite That Wouldn't Fly

Month's end. At month's end, Some changes fit neatly on the page: one kite. One blame phase (survived; the wind exonerated). One tangle (an hour; unpicked on the bench; the knot laws extracted). One no-wind day.

One rival. One willow kite. One festival. One ribbon. One squadron (chartered; thermal held in commons; loaner stocked). What mattered most could not be totaled: that the real problem is usually small, close, and yours.

That the flying lives in a finger-width. That steady is a thousand invisible answers and never gets a crowd. That the wind was never yours, the whole sport sits on a thing nobody controls, and you fly anyway, and wait well. That patches are features.

That the lessons come in costumes, and the costume department has been open every night since page one. "Good kite-month? The persistentest one. And the secret's out about the costumes, so I'll just say it plain: this month taught me that the should-be-easy thing that isn't easy is the best teacher in the whole school.

The kite fought me for free days and gave me the whole sky for a year. Fair trade. Good trade. The best things fight you a little first. That's how they check you're serious." Remembered. The best things fight you a little first. On we go.
