Night 71

The Grandpa Interview

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

The second fat notebook (collecting the parents, and the grandparents; the project runs all year) took the study to the farm: "Grandpa. Your times. Was different... Allowed, back then?" Joe worked the porch rail awhile.

The honest answer, taken down slow: "Less than now, darlin'. My times sorted people hard, the lefties got switched, the slow talkers got finished-for, the quiet ones got 'come out of your shell' till some of them broke shells they needed. A boy like your Marcus, arrows in his head and letters that swam, my times would've just called him slow and let that be his whole name. Nobody asked what it builds.

That question hadn't been invented on this farm yet... Who invented it? On this farm?" The rail took more weight. "Your grandma. Margaret asked it about everything and everyone. The mean hen. The wild row.

Me. I was the quiet one, you know that? The whole county's quiet one. And she listened in it instead of translating." He looked at the fields a long time. "You didn't inherit the question from nowhere.

You should know that. The asking runs in the family. It skipped to you through her wind pages and her weather-eyes, and don't let anybody tell you the dead don't teach. They teach the longest courses of anybody."
