Night 319

The Entry She Got to Write

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

Night's session reversed the chairs: the book, opened to today. The pen, handed over. "this month's got you in it, darlin'. The book should too. Write the entry." The entry, as written: "[Date]. Grandpa's month in town.

Thirty nights of entries at the fire. Learned: The fence don't care, the barns do. The row anyway. Some escapes are arrivals. Don't build on cows. The word came for grandma fifty-two years late.

The book is attention, banked, and this month it paid a whole generation's interest to me. Signed: O, age almost-six, the fourth hand in this book." (The fourth hand. His father's. His. Margaret's, the wind pages, the corrections, the "she means 2" of the whole tradition.

And now hers. Joe read the entry twice, blotted it, and said the whole speech: "Well." And then, because this month had loosened even his ink: "The book was always gonna be yours someday, you know. Books like this pick their keepers.

It picked you years back. I just been letting the ink dry on the decision.")
