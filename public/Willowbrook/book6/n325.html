Night 325

The Family Book's First Month

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

The family book closed its first month full, and the story checks its opening pages with professional delight: Theo's entries (spoken, then increasingly self-written, the letters campaign paying out): "The fire is cold. I miss the pig story. Made the ferry go all the way. No crashes.

Saw three dogs. All good ones." (The four-year-old's tally book: griefs, triumphs, dogs. The essential categories. The story has kept worse books.) Mama's ("Fog Tuesday. The house drove slow around me. They've gotten so good at it that the fog lifts faster now, someone should study this").

Papa's ("Wrong note in the shower this morning. Kept it. Some habits are steady"). And hers, nightly, the notebook's public cousin: the weave, kept. The Tuesdays, logged. "Fixed the gate latch. Lily made the braided loaf solo.

Rain at four. Good day." (The format, inherited whole. The best entry in the book, on purpose, weekly.)
