Night 248

The Bad Gift Reckoning

Month 9: Truth With Kindness

A relative brought her a gift: a princess diary. Pink, locked, glitter-clasped, a gift for some other six-year-old, some hypothetical one assembled from wrong guesses. The live threading, family watching: the thanks (real..." Thank you for thinking of me on a regular Tuesday; that's the rare part. "gratitude aimed at the true target: the thought, not the object).

The honest, delivered gentle: "I keep question notebooks, three volumes; I'll show you, so the diary part's perfect. The princess part's not really my country. But the lock, the lock is genius. Some pages want guarding.

This'll be the private volume." (It became exactly that: the private volume. The princess cover got a gold paper jacket, willow-drawn. The relative, next visit, brought a plain notebook and said "I did better research."

The honest, threaded gentle, had taught the giver, which flattery never once managed in the whole history of gifts.)
