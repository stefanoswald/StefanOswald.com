Night 19

How Money Works, Sort Of

Month 1: How Things Work

The tally book prompted it: "Papa. Where does money come from? Not your pocket, before that. Before the bank. The beginning of it." Papa poured coffee with the face of a man who knows the next hour of his life.

The attempt, jar-based: money is a promise token. "Long ago: you fix my fence, I owe you eggs. But maybe you don't want eggs, so we invented a token everyone agrees to want, so promises could travel between strangers. Money is portable promising.

So the coins are... Frozen promises? The whole market is people trading promise-ice?" The wheels, visibly turning: "Then the real money is the agreeing. If everybody stopped agreeing, the coins would just be... circles."

A long pause. "Petrov's columns. The hug-fines. Rosa's weather prices. They're not bad at money. They're money with the promises still warm. Unfrozen. The second grid runs on warm promises and the first grid runs on frozen ones and they're both promise-systems..."

(Papa, at this point, quietly got the fat notebook. Some dinner theories get archived in real time.)
