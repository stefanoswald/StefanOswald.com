Night 285

The Thaw Tease

Month 10: The Long Winter

The false spring came mid-month (two warm days; the eaves dripping; the town's coats unzipped, hopeful), and the veteran called it at the porch rail: "The tease, Papa. March lies. Grandpa's law. The eager pays.

The real thing's weeks out; the almanac says; the cows say; the flag says." The two days, spent correctly anyway: mud reconnaissance. The icicle orchestra's farewell tour. One glorious coatless hour ("the tease is still warm, even if it's lying, you can enjoy a thing and know it's temporary; that's the whole skill of teases, and of a lot of things, honestly").

And when the cold slammed back Thursday, the town grumbling, betrayed, the porch philosopher held serene: "Nobody got betrayed. The winter never left; it stepped out. The grief is for people who believed the tease. Believe the almanac.

Enjoy the tease. The two things fit fine together, that's the whole trick of hope, actually: hold the warm day in one hand and the forecast in the other and don't let either hand lie to the other one."
