Night 40

The Build Begins

Month 2: The Kite That Wouldn't Fly

The build: opened. The kitchen table: commandeered (office hours extended; the homework does not mind sharing with aviation). The design, drafted with full detail-person rigor: the frame, split cane, light and springy ("the triangle lesson where it helps, the bending where it doesn't. Kites want a little give.

Stiff things snap; bendy things ride. The willow taught that too, watch it in a storm sometime; it never fights the wind; it dances it"). The sail: Rosa's gold paper, doubled at the stress points. The bridle: measured three times, adjustable knot..."

The fiddly part gets the most time now. I learned this month's lesson forward for once. "And the tails: Twelve. Long gold streamers, cut with ceremony, one per month of the year, "so when it flies, the whole year streams behind the tree.

The kite's not just a willow. It's the willow. It's got the calendar in its hair."
