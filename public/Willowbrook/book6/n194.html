Night 194

The Harvest Dinner

Month 7: Team Willowbrook

This month crowned itself: The harvest dinner. (One long table, boards and sawhorses, Petrov-engineered, down the lot's center path. The whole bed-holding town. The food: the lot's own, cooked by everyone: the soup (shared-bed greens; her family's pot; the pot knows the size of the lane and scaled).

Rosa's zucchini bread (the surplus, transfigured). The class bed's carrots, marigold-guarded to perfect. Theo's stubby ones, served with maximum ceremony. The elder Alvarez's beans. Salad from six beds, tossed in a washtub.) The gloaming came on while the table ran, the string lights coming up gold, the whole lot glowing like the year's best sky-cake, and Joe stood, at the bench-faculty's nod, and said the grace of a man who's fed people from dirt for fifty years: "Forty years this lot grew weeds.

One year it grew all this. Wasn't the soil that changed." He sat down. (The whole speech. They remembered it beside "Well." The man's collected works run four sentences and every one's a fence post.)
