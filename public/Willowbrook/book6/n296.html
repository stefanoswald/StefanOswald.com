Night 296

The Groundhog Debate

Month 10: The Long Winter

Groundhog Day raised a jurisdictional question at breakfast: "Papa. The groundhog. A rodent predicts the season and the whole country reports it. The almanac earns trust one forecast at a time, what's the groundhog's record?!"

The check: the groundhog's accuracy..." About a coin flip, the books say. Worse some decades. "The answer, though, arrived nuanced, because the year taught nuance: "So it's not forecasting. But Papa, it's not trying to be.

It's a... a holiday of hope with a rodent mascot. The town gavvers mid-winter and asks 'how much longer?' Together, and the asking-together is the whole product. The groundhog's a excuse. The gathering's the point.

Same as half the holidays: the work underneath is always people needing to stand in the cold together and say 'we're halfway.' The rodent's just... the bell that calls the meeting."
