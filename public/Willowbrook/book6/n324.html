Night 324

The Saturday Restored

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

First Saturday back at the farm, and the story logs the restoration in full: the coop (her charge; the Duchess's line laying steady; the count run slow and complete, rain or no). The porch (the catalogs; the reading; the arrangement, resumed mid-sentence like this month was a comma). The far fields (the Petersen kids' fences going up, the re-rigging, visible, orderly, "the land staying worked; the receipt in the drawer"). And the book, on its shelf, the fourth hand's pages settling in among fifty years. "this month changed the Saturdays..."

She reported at the lamp. "Not the shape, the coop's the coop; the porch is the porch. The weight. I used to come as the helper, then the postholder. Now I come as... the fourth hand.

The apprentice-keeper of the seed stock. The Saturdays got heavier and the heavy is the good kind, the cargo kind. Some things you carry glad. The book taught me the phrase and then it went and became one."
