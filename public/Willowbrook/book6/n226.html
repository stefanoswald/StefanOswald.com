Night 226

The Kid Who Hates School

Month 8: School Stories

This month's hard file: a classmate who hates school. Daily. Stomach-aches-at-the-door hates. And the room's reflex..." He's just lazy. "met her oldest rule: check before pulling. The gentle work: the root, finally surfacing: he can't see the board.

Squints. Has squinted for a year. Told nobody, because glasses cost money and he knows it (the lunch-money file's cousin; the basements connect; they always connect), and because "school = the place where everything's blurry and everybody else gets it" had calcified into "school = bad" so thoroughly that even he'd stopped remembering the blur came first. The lever, pulled through channels (the nurse; the district's glasses fund, it exists; nobody knows it exists; "the town's full of funds nobody knows exist; somebody should make a map..." the story watches the next proposal being born in real time): the exam.

The glasses. (Sky-blue frames. His choice. The hearing-aid kid's endorsement carried the fitting.) And school, three weeks later, unblurred: not loved yet. But the stomach-aches: gone. "The hate was never school, Mama.

The hate was the blur. Half the hates are blurs wearing hate costumes. Check the blur first. Always check the blur first."
