Night 32

The Kite Arrives

Month 2: The Kite That Wouldn't Fly

This month opened with a purchase: The kite. (Saved-for. Tally book-tracked. Gold, obviously, with a tail like a comet.) The assembly: flawless. The wind: present. The hill: the famous one. The launch: ... Nothing.

Runs, drops, nose-dives, tangles. An hour of it. The Hawk flew three seconds on bedsheets and lath, and this store-bought, purpose-built, instructions-included kite would not take six feet of air. "The hawk flew, Papa.

This is a real kite and it won't. That's backwards. That's insulting. Welcome..." said Papa, untangling string with the calm of a man who has met this month before. "to the kite. Everyone meets one. The thing that should be easy and isn't. This month's just getting started."
