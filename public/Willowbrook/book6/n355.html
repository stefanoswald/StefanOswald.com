Night 355

The Just-Us Morning, Sixth Dawn

Month 12: Almost Six

Dawn. Candles. Pancakes. The original three. The letters, sixth annual reading: Mama's (excerpted; the drawer keeps the whole): "... This was the year you learned that attention is the love, you said it at the lamp and I wrote it down and I've thought about it every day since, because it means you finally have the word for what you've been doing since you were two.

You attend. It's the rarest verb. Most people visit their lives. You attend yours, and ours, and the town's... Don't ever learn to visit. The attending is the whole you..." Papa's: "... Six things about six: One, you're the fourth hand in a fifty-year book, and Joe cried telling me, which stays between us and this letter.

Two, the wrong note in measure twelve was the proudest I've ever been at a concert, and I've been going to concerts since before you could sit up. Three, your brother built you a reliquary, and I watched you understand it instantly, and I understood something too: we didn't raise two kids. We raised a keeping, with two keepers. Four, the willow writes tomorrow-ish, and for the sixth time we don't know the word, and for the sixth time it doesn't matter: it's been watching the same year we have.

Five, the gate opens next year, the bear tells me, don't ask how I know what the bear tells; papas have channels. Six: whatever's behind it, you'll be ready. You've been packing for seven years. The whole shelf was the packing..." The sealing. The drawer: Twelve envelopes. The someday: patient as everything in this family.
