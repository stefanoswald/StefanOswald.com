Night 272

The Truth Month, Counted

Month 9: Truth With Kindness

Month's end. At month's end, The month's visible changes were one haircut. Two grandmas. One surprise. One bad gift. One umpire season. One currency reform. One plain diagnosis. One Duchess (the story lowers its voice: distributed; the boot kept; the alarm off at last).

One rumor chain. One draft reform (cruelty is never steady). One returned kid (the doors are half the fix). One locked page. One compliment. The harder part to measure was that the sad is sized to the love, not the species.

That the truth isn't yours to apologize for. That recipes are letters and the changing is the tradition. And the four words on the kitchen wall now, crooked and gold and steady: Kindest true thing first. "Good truth-month?

The grownest-up one. Mama, the four-year-old rules said don't lie. The six-year-old rules say something bigger: The truth is a tool for loving people. That's what it was for the whole time.

The honesty was never the destination. The honesty was the road, and the destination was always the people, safe, seen, told true, and kept." Remembered. The truth is a tool for loving people. Stitch that one too, someday.

(She did. The story's seen the wall. It's crowded now, and every line is true.)
