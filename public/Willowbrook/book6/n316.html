Night 316

The Entry About the Bad Neighbor

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

Night's entry, chosen by Joe "because the book owes you its failures too": "1985-2003. The carver place. Eighteen years of sour. Tried the fence-mending four times by my count. He wasn't having it. Sold up and left sour.

Some fences stay down." The hard story, told level: the neighbor who never warmed. The four attempts. The nothing that came back. "Darlin', your whole kit is repair, the rules, the doors, the stacks. So the book owes you this entry most: some repairs don't take.

Not because you built them wrong. Because repair is a handshake and some hands stay in pockets. Eighteen years I wondered what I did. Answer, best I ever got: nothing. His sour predated me. I was just the nearest fence."

The fire ticked. "The peace I made wasn't with him. It was with the unfixed thing. You can retire a repair attempt with honor, after real tries, after real time, and the retiring isn't quitting.

It's the three questions, darlin'. Your own check. Hard-stop or done-stop? Done. I done what a neighbor does. The rest was his row to plant, and he let it go to weeds, and that..."

The oldest shrug in the valley. "that was his weeds' right to happen. You can't garden another man's sour."
