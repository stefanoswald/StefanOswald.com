Night 320

The Entry Reading Goes Public

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

This month's format went public for one night (her proposal; his grumbling consent; the grumbling, as it happened, was pure theater): The open reading. The living room, packed, the trio-plus, the squadron, Mrs. Hollis and Gus-Senior, Theo front row with the cow. The set list, curated for the room: Roosevelt the pig (brought the house down; Gus-Senior wept; "I remember the church picnic.

I was there. The deviled eggs were my mother's..." the town's memory, interlocking in real time). The hail year and the different ink (the room went church-quiet; eleven kids learned the work's name at once). The weather rhymes (graded; "Don't build on cows" chanted by the under-ten delegation for a week after).

And the closing, Joe's, to a room of the town's incoming keepers: "Every family's got a book like this, whether they write it down or not. The ones that write it down get to keep the lessons with the names still on. Start your books. Tonight's soon enough.

The first entry can be this one: 'Went to a reading. The old man said start the book. Started it.'" (Four kids started books that week. The story has read three of them since, decades of them.

The almanac franchises like everything else in this family: by fire, by invitation, one night at a time.)
