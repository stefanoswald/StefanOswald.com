Night 218

The Teacher's Bad Day

Month 8: School Stories

A Tuesday the story keeps: Mr. Okafor had a bad day. (Visible by 8:20, the shortened patience, the rubbed eyes, the coffee refilled twice before circle. Teachers are people. This month's whole thesis, arriving in person.)

The class's veteran feet-reader made the call, and the room, trained by a year of her systems, ran the soft landing, upward, for the first time: the volume, self-lowered. The line, self-straightened. The worksheet questions, batched and brought up once instead of eleven times ("the interruptions are the tax on tired people; we waived the tax"). And at day's end, on his desk, unsigned: a drawing of the class, and three words.

Elias's format, the faithful-things format: "You teach good." (Wednesday's Mr. Okafor, restored, held up the drawing: "I don't know who. I know it worked. For the story, people: teachers have basements too, and yesterday somebody in this room read the feet and rerouted the whole day around mine.

That's a citizenship no test measures." The room, twenty-two faces of perfect innocence. The story, in its chair, filing.)
