Night 53

The High Wind Day

Month 2: The Kite That Wouldn't Fly

A high-wind day this week, the restless kind, the journal's tangle-weather, gusting past the good numbers into the arguing ones. The squadron's call, made at the hill's base, flag consulted, journal cited: Fly low or don't. (Half height. Short lines.

And when the western sky went the color the almanac calls "business," down. All kites, reeled home, fifteen minutes before the front hit the hill with weather that would have shredded everything airborne.) From the fort, rain hammering, kites safe: "That's the other half of brave, isn't it. This month taught fly-anyway and wait-well.

Today was know-when. The sky posts its warnings, the flag, the color, the restless gusts, and reading them isn't scared. Reading them is respect. The flyers who last are the ones the sky never had to teach twice."

Mateo, watching the front eat the hill: "The academy logs it: she called the down fifteen minutes early. The detail person reads weather now."
