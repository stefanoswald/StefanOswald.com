Night 126

The Flood Line

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

Joe walked her to the bridge this week and taught her to read the stones: The flood lines. (The stains, chest-high on the pilings, "the brook's biggest days, signed in mud. That one: the flood of my boyhood. That higher one: before me.

The bridge keeps the brook's records the way the doorframe keeps yours.") "The brook gets that big? The polite summer brook? The minnow brook? Every few decades. The water's got moods bigger than its banks, darlin'.

The town knows, look." (The lesson's second half: the town's shape, reread. The houses, all set back from the banks. The bridge, arched high. The park on the floodplain, "grass floods cheap; houses flood dear.

The town gave the brook its arguing room on purpose. That's not fear. That's a town that listened, once, the hard way, and wrote the listening into where it put its walls.")
