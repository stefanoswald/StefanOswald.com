Night 39

The Kite Festival Question

Month 2: The Kite That Wouldn't Fly

The poster went up in the bakery window this week: The Willowbrook kite festival. (Annual. Kite hill. End of this month. Categories: highest, steadiest, most beautiful, best homemade. "All ages.") The planning, instant, over rolls: Lily: most beautiful ("the bakery colors.

Rosa says I can borrow the frosting palette idea, not the frosting"). Theo: "Highest!!" (The boy has one gear. It's up.) And her, the long think, the notebook out: "Best homemade. Obviously. But not just a kite, Rosa, listen, a willow kite.

Gold. Long tails like the branches, lots of them, so when it flies it looks like... like the tree finally got its wish to see the town from the top of the sky. The tree watches everything from one hill its whole life. The kite can take it up.

I'm gonna fly the willow." (Rosa put down her tray. "Five years of skies on your cakes..." She said. "and now you're baking one out of paper and wind. The trade approves. The trade loans you the gold paper, the good kind, the roll in the back.")
