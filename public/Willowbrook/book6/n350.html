Night 350

The Last Week's Rounds

Month 12: Almost Six

During the last week of five, Olivia paid extra attention to the ordinary rounds.

She waved to the crossing guard and smelled cinnamon from the bakery. She climbed to the office reading perch and checked the pulley she had helped repair. At recess, she sat with her friends on the familiar bench without needing to fill the silence.

At the fence, Mateo rolled the ball to her twice.

"See you when you're seven," he said.

"See you when I'm seven. First Saturday still counts."

On Friday she knocked at Mrs. Hollis's door for tea. Mrs. Hollis admired the leather case Olivia carried and said, "Come back as a six-year-old, colleague. I will expect a full account."

Saturday brought the chicken count at Honeywell Farm. Grandpa Joe claimed that one of the Duchess's granddaughters had begun studying his boots.

Nothing extraordinary happened on any of the rounds. That was why Olivia wanted to remember them. A year was not only its firsts and emergencies. It was also the places that kept opening when you arrived.
