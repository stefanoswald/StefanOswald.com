Night 314

The Entry About the Marble... Almost

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

Night's entry, and someone saw her sit up at the date: "1952. The mill cut the third shift. The west families moving out. Good people. The valley gets smaller every time the whistle shortens. Grandpa. 1952. The west families.

Was one of them... a boy? The lot by the library, was that..." Joe consulted the page, the memory, the fifty-year index behind his eyes: "The Grubers. Boy about my age. I knew him from the county games.

Gus. They went two towns over when the shift cut." A pause; the fire ticked; the connection closed across seventy years and one buried marble: "You're telling me the garden's window-warden is gus gruber? Darlin', I played horseshoes against that boy in 1951.

He had..." And here the story swears the old man smiled the smile of the very long game. "the steadiest throwing hand in the valley. Some things you don't forget." (The reunion, arranged the following Saturday, two old men and a marble and seventy years, at the garden bench, ran four hours.

The story attended. The story declines to summarize four hours of that except to say: the long ones always land, and sometimes they land twice.)
