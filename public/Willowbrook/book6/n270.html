Night 270

The Diary Lock's First Secret

Month 9: Truth With Kindness

The private volume received its first locked entry this week, and the story, for the first time in six books, reports a page it cannot read. (The lock works. The story respects locks. That was always the arrangement.)

What the story can report: the entry took forty minutes. The bear attended. The lamp burned late. And at the end, the lock clicked, and she said, to the bear, to the room, to nobody: "Some truths aren't for telling or even for keeping-where-people-can-find.

Some are just for... Saying once, to paper, so they're out of the two-a.m. Basement and into a jar with a lid. The writing is the whole transaction. No reader required. The lock's not hiding it from the world.

The lock's just... the lid." (they remembered the metadata and honors the seal: even archives have doors they don't open. Especially archives. That's what makes them trustworthy enough to hold everything else.)
