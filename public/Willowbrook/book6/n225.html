Night 225

The Wall at Last

Month 8: School Stories

The wall. (The big kids' wall. The playground's summit yet, saved two summers ago by the academy's actuarial tables: "two summers out." The schedule, sure enough, arrived this week, on time, because her yets keep their appointments.)

The attempt, run by the full book: the arms (grown; the bars and pole and two years of campaigns in them). The chalk. The route (scouted from below for a week, "the wall's got a sequence; the big kids brute it; I'm gonna read it, the holds are a sentence and the crux is the verb"). Lily at the catching end.

Mateo calling beta from the side ("Left hand to the horn. Trust the feet. The feet do the climbing, the hands just vote..."). And the top: Taken. First try. (Not lucky. Scheduled. Two years of deposits, withdrawn in forty seconds.)

The view from the wall's top, looked over levelly: the whole playground, the school roof, and, she checked; she always checks, the willow, gold on its far hill, watching its colleague summit.
