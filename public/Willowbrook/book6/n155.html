Night 155

The Wrench and the Letting Go

Month 6: Hard Things Take Days

Saturday. The wrench (ceremonial; Theo turned the last bolt himself, lefty scissors having prepared him for reverse threads, as it happened, in ways nobody planned). And then the lane, and the run-alongside. Papa's post; some posts don't transfer; the coach stood at the mailbox with Mama, hands in pockets, whole heart up, and the seat held, and the seat released, and: The block.

The whole block, ridden alone, unnoticed, exactly as the bear's letter once forecast for her, the family physics repeating a generation down: he didn't notice for a whole block. "I did it alone! Since the mailbox!"
