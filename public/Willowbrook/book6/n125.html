Night 125

What the Brook Sounds Like

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

The sound collector returned to her first subject with five more years of ears: The brook's full vocabulary, written down on site: "The riffle: the talking parts, shallow, stony, busy. The brook does its thinking out loud here. The pool: the quiet parts. Deep water says almost nothing.

(The volume knob rule. The deep ones hear more than they say. The brook has Wrens in it.) The undercut bank: the secret hollow sound, the brook talking to the roots, private. The stepping stones: the interruptions.

The water says something different around every stone, every rock gets its own syllable. The stones are the brook's consonants." And the finding, read to Mama at the bank: "The brook only makes sound where something's in its way. The smooth parts run silent.

All the music is the water meeting its obstacles. All of it, Mama. The whole song is the arguing." (The whistle lesson, surfacing after two years: everything that sings is fighting something, beautifully. They remembered them together, where they always belonged.)
