Night 124

The Dam Project

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

The oldest project in kid-history got its turn this week: The dam. (Stones, the shallow neck, three engineers, one afternoon. The pool formed behind it, knee-deep, magnificent. The story has watched ten thousand years of children build this exact dam.)

And then: the minnows. Downstream of the dam, in the thinning water, circling the shrinking pools. The dam's fine print, arriving on fins: "... We built the pool by unbuilding their river. The water we're keeping is the water they run on.

Mateo. The dam works and it's wrong." The redesign, argued and built: The fish gate. A channel through the dam's middle, the pool stays, the river keeps running, the minnows thread the gate by evening like they'd voted on it.

"Everything we build sits in somebody's river..." She ruled, wet to the knees, moving the last stone. "The build isn't done when it works. The build is done when you've checked whose water you're standing in. The councilman should hear this one too."
