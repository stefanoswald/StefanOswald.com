Night 159

The Piano Question

Month 6: Hard Things Take Days

This month's biggest maybe, raised at the piano: Lessons. Real ones. ("The school music teacher says I got the ear. The ear wants the hands to catch up.") The big think, run with Papa at the family instrument: the fear, named honest: "If I learn it proper... do the wrong notes die?

The dynasty's whole heart is the wrong notes. I don't want lessons that fix what isn't broken." Papa played his beloved wrong one, thirty-two years old now, and gave the answer the story's been waiting a book for: "Kiddo, I never took the lessons, that's why my wrongs stayed wrongs instead of becoming choices. Learn it proper, and your wrong notes get promoted: from accidents you kept, to choices you make. The dynasty doesn't die from skill. The dynasty graduates."
