Night 94

The Theo Post

Month 4: My Jobs

Post three landed closest to home: Wednesday walker. (Mama's mid-week early shift; somebody walks Theo to Miss Dana's; the somebody, negotiated and agreed with parental escort at distance: Her. "Half a block behind," Mama's condition. "Half a block is a post radius..."

Her counter. Settled.) The Wednesday rule, run to standard: the corner crossing (looked both ways, twice, out loud, teaching by doing. "the crossing guard's forty years start with somebody's Wednesday"). The walk's lesson (his letters, drilled off signs, the campaign continues; he's at three-letter words and gaining).

The drop-off: the cubby check, the blue cushion claimed, and the firework, fists together, fingers spread, three steps backward, "See you at the table." And the return walk, solo-ish, tall with office: "The post pays backwards, Mama. I thought I was giving Wednesday. Wednesday's giving me, the whole walk of him telling me his everything, the letters landing, the firework. Best salary in town."
