Night 341

The Remembering Game, Sixth Round

Month 12: Almost Six

The remembering game, sixth annual. With the recorder running (the tape project's greatest session; the archive keeping the archive): The classics, performed for the tape: the May snow. The duck ("The grandfather of all the words," Theo's theology, canon now). The flush.

The snowplow (five voices; the parts redistributed again. Theo runs "Perfect timing" And the plow sounds now; the boy multiplies). And the year's new entries, minted live: the boot (the Duchess's eleven minutes, told by Joe via speakerphone, the farm attending its first remembering game by wire). The petrichor delivery.

The word-season's whole approach. And the session's discovery, made by its engineer: "Mama, the game's different on tape. Everybody tells better, the stories stand up straighter when they know they're being kept. That's not showing off.

That's... the stories knowing they matter. The keeping makes the telling truer. The attention is the love, and the tape is attention with a memory."
