Night 81

The Substitute From Elsewhere

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

A substitute this week, kind, capable, and carrying an accent from somewhere far. And one kid did the mimic. The cruel little echo. The room's oldest cheap laugh. The hand went up, veteran, hot-chested as ever, five years of practice behind it: "'Scuse me.

Before the laughing settles: that accent means she's got a whole other language at home. A complete one. The accent's not a mistake in her English, it's a receipt for her other world. She thinks in two currencies.

The kid doing the copying..." (mildly; the symmetry rule; repair the room, not the kid) "...has got one. Copying the accent is a one-language kid teasing a two-language grown-up for being richer. The math's embarrassing when you run it." (The room ran the math.

The mimic, to his credit, and the story always credits, ran it too, went red, and asked the substitute at recess what her first language was. Portuguese. She taught the class to count to ten in it. The room went home ten words richer, which is, the story calculates, ten more than the cheap laugh was ever going to pay.)
