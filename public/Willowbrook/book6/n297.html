Night 297

The Reading Fort Count

Month 10: The Long Winter

The reading wing's winter count, done with the bear: Fourteen books, the deep season's haul, rated in the house style: "The fat-spine one (The librarian's forecast held, the era opened on schedule). The dog book, reread (annual; chapter nine still gets me; the books don't stop getting you. Hollis's law, confirmed at six). The sequel's sequel (new room, same hallway, the page-free test passed at the shelf).

Two duds (abandoned at the fifty-page line: 'the line exists because the shelf never ends, quitting a dud isn't losing; it's shelf helpers'). The poetry one (the barn cats of books: you don't read it; you leave the door open and it gets in your lap). And the atlas, a atlas, read like a novel, cover to cover ('every page is a place that's real and I haven't checked. The whole book is dragons. Best cliffhanger structure in the building')."
