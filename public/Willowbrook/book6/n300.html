Night 300

Night Three Hundred: The Gold Squares

Month 10: The Long Winter

Night three hundred, and the rite went to the window: the lane at full dark, the gold squares of the neighbors' windows in the blue evening, the family watching the town's evening the way the Moon describes watching it (the session leaked, the story admits; the sessions always leak; the town and the watch have never been as separate as either pretends). The count, from the sill: "Number Nine's square: Mrs. Hollis's reading lamp. The bakery's late square: Rosa's proofing the morning's dough, the breves rising in the dark.

Gus-Senior's square, garden-side: the Warden's shift. The new family's squares, all lit: they're woven now; a year does it. And ours..." She looked at their own window's glow spilling onto the snow. "ours is somebody else's gold square right now.

Somebody's walking the dusk and counting us. The lane watches the lane, Mama. Every window's both a lamp and a lighthouse. That's the whole town, in one shape: Everybody's light is somebody's landmark."
