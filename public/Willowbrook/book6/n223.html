Night 223

The Show and Tell Exchange

Month 8: School Stories

Show-and-tell evolved this year: Show-and-teach. One kid weekly. Not an object, a skill. Small, teachable, ten minutes. The season's lesson, as delivered by the room to the room: the bed trick (Marcus, teaching his own uphill's tool, as it happened the full circle and saves it warm).

The horse-drawing basics (Rosa-No-Relation; "the legs are the secret; everybody draws the legs too short"). Whistling through an acorn cap. The five-times table on fingers (Priya, fast kid, teaching her speed instead of racing it). A card trick (Casey, both-houses kid, "my papa's; he does it at both addresses; some things travel").

And hers, when her week came, not reading, not systems, not any flagship: The nine-second take-back. ("The most useful thing I own. 'Wait, take-back. Say it again, I'll listen right.' Nine seconds.

It's saved every friendship I got. You can all have it free. The kit only keeps what it gives. That's the real show-and-tell, forever, everywhere.")
