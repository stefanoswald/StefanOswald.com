Night 10

How the Lantern Stays Lit

Month 1: How Things Work

The year's lens turned on an old mystery: The lantern itself. "It never goes out. Five years I've checked, fog, storms, the big blackout last winter. How?" The librarian took her up, the spiral stair behind the staff door, the little room at the top where the light lives.

The mechanism: old glass, a modern bulb, and, this is the part, a backup oil lamp, ancient, polished, ready. And a logbook: every keeper's initials, every night checked, going back, she turned the pages with two fingers, a hundred and some years. "The light has a keeper. Every librarian before me climbed these stairs.

During the blackout I sat up here till two in the morning with the oil lamp burning and my coat on. The town assumes the lantern's magic." She smiled the banked-coals smile. "It is. This is what the magic looks like from inside. It looks like a rota."
