Night 275

The Fort of All Forts

Month 10: The Long Winter

The lobby's first great delivery: The fort of all forts. (Four days. Three rooms. Every blanket, sheet, and clothespin in the house, plus structural furniture and two Petrov-loaned clamps. Mama's one condition: "the kitchen stays sovereign."

Treaty signed.) The shape: the reading wing. The meadow room (the gold blanket ceiling; "for thinking and woolgavvering; no loud"). Theo's garage district. The tunnel network (engineered post-and-lintel; Marcus consulted by phone: "arrows say the couch cushions can't span that, use the chairs," remote structural engineering; the web works snowed-in too).

And the flag, raised through a gap in the blanket-roof: the red mitten, fetched from the treasure box, flying its third structure. ("Fort together, Fort City, and now this. The mitten's a whole fleet flag now. Some relics keep working. The best ones never stop.")
