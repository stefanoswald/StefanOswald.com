Night 290

The Valentine Blizzard

Month 10: The Long Winter

Valentine's week met a blizzard, school closed, the party postponed, and twenty-two cards plus the special editions stranded undelivered. The four-year-old's crisis: "the rule of everybody says everybody. The snow can't beat the rule." The operation (parental clearance; route-cleared streets only; the sled as mail van): The valentine run.

The web's houses, hit in route-logic order, cards in zip bags, delivered to porches with the classic rule (ring, leave, run, "the zucchini method; it works for love too"). Mrs. Hollis's, first. The far kids' cards mailed ("the post office beats the drifts; that's their whole post").

And the finding, thawing at cocoa after: "The blizzard improved it, Lily. A card in class is nice. A card that came through a blizzard on a sled says the rule's real, says you're on somebody's list that weather can't edit. The hard deliveries carry extra cargo. They always do. The effort is the envelope."
