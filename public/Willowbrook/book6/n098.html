Night 98

The Substitute Shelver

Month 4: My Jobs

A cold took her Tuesday this week, and the post met its first test of absence: "The cart doesn't care that I'm sick, Mama. The returns pile eider way. That's the whole difference between posts and chores again, chores wait. Posts pile."

The solution, phoned in hoarse, done by proxy: Sam. (Library boy. Carpet regular. Trained, as it happened, in one afternoon two weeks prior, because a good postholder trains her backup before the cold comes; the succession law; the agency taught her; everything teaches everything.)

The route, taught. The logic, explained. The picture-book flip, transferred. Sam's Tuesday: clean. The cart: emptied to standard. And the librarian's report, relayed at the lamp: "'Tell her the post held. Tell her that's the final apprentice lesson: the post isn't yours because you're irreplaceable.

The post is yours because you made sure it could survive you. That's the difference between holding a job and hoarding one.'"
