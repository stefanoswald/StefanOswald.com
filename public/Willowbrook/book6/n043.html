Night 43

The Rival

Month 2: The Kite That Wouldn't Fly

A new variable at the hill this week: a kid. New to town, festival-bound, flying a kite that made the whole hill stare, a store-bought stunt kite, two lines, swoops and dives on command, expensive as a bicycle. The first feeling, logged honestly: the green one. ("My kite's paper and tape and a leaf-shaped scar.

His does loops.") The blame phase's cousin, arriving on schedule: "Store-bought fancy. Anybody can buy loops..." And then the kid, Wes, it turned out, crashed. Hard. Repeatedly. And sat down next to his expensive wreckage looking exactly like day-one-of-the-month her.

And this month, five weeks deep in her now, did the thing instead: "Bridle-check?" she offered. "Mine wouldn't fly for free days. The fix was half a inch." (It was, for his, a quarter inch and a swapped line.

Mateo consulted. The stunt kite looped for real by dusk, and Wes, it emerged, knew thermals, the invisible warm elevators, and taught the hill where the hill's own thermal lived, and by dark three kites were riding it in a slow shared spiral, paper and fancy together, and the rivalry had quietly become a squadron.)
