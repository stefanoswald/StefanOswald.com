Night 48

The Squadron Plan

Month 2: The Kite That Wouldn't Fly

The festival's afterglow organized itself (things organize themselves around her; the story has stopped noting it as unusual): The hill squadron. Charter, drafted at the fort, agreed at the hill: "Members: whoever's flying. Roles: Mateo, engineering. Wes, stunts and fermals.

Lily, beauty division. Theo, altitude. Me, steadiness and bridle clinic. (The fiddly part is a service now. Nobody skips it on my hill.) Common property: the thermal. The repair kit. The knot laws (yanks tighten; walk toward each other; bring both kites down together).

Visitors: get a loaner. (The old gold diamond, refurbished, lives at the fort. Nobody watches from the bottom of this hill unless watching's their choice. Watching is playing, but so is flying, and everybody gets offered the line.)"
