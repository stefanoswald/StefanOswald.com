Night 283

The Midwinter Reading of the Chain

Month 10: The Long Winter

A midwinter ritual, invented this year: The half-reading. (The cut links, staged since summer, unhung, waiting for almost-six, read by firelight at the year's deep middle: "the yearbook at halftime.") The reading, sampled: "The willowhawk. The cargo kite.

The posts. The garden, all of it. The duchess..." The pause; the small silence; the link held longer than the others. "the boot link. The guarded link. The wall. The hundred days. The kindest-true-thing-first."

And the finding, fire-lit: "The year's not done and it's already the fullest chain ever, and Mama, reading it midway is different than reading it at the end. At the end, the links are memories. At midway, they're... Momentum.

The back half of the year gets to know what it's building on. The half-reading isn't looking back. It's fueling."
