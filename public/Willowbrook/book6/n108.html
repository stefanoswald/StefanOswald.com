Night 108

The Reading Corner Returns

Month 4: My Jobs

The old post came up for renewal at the new school: The corner. (First grade has one, bigger, dustier, underused. Mr. Okafor runs a louder room; the corner had gone to seed.) The pitch, delivered at his desk with references: "Sir.

The reading corner. I ran Room 4's. Tuesdays, sixty-some sessions, Ms. Alvarez will confirm. My metfods: the books face out (covers sell; spines don't). The cushions get territory (a corner without cushion law is just a shelf with a rug).

The little-kid gasp spots get marked in the margins. And the corner adjusts to whoever's having the hard day, that's not in the manual; that's the whole real manual. References: the wainscoting, the elbow kid, the whole carpet of Room 4. I'm asking for Tuesdays."

Mr. Okafor, who yelled at a dog once and has been legend since; who reads his room like weather too, the good ones all do, looked at her a long moment: "The corner's been waiting two years for somebody to ask. Tuesdays. And the budget has eleven dollars, spend it on whatever the corner needs."

(Eleven dollars. A budget. Her first. Someone saw her walk home holding the responsibility like a full cup.)
