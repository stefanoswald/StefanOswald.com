Night 315

The Entry About Rain Smell

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

Night's entry, and the word jar's keeper made a noise when he read it: "June 1971. The smell before rain on warm ground. Margaret says there's a word for it somewhere. Told her the smell don't need one.

She says everything deserves its name. We've agreed to disagree. The smell continues either way. Petrichor! Grandpa. The word exists. Pet-ri-chor. Papa gave it to my jar when I was four. Grandma was right, everything deserves its name, and you were right, the smell don't need it.

Both, because that's how everything in this family resolves, apparently, always both..." (Joe took the word like a late letter. Said it twice. "Petrichor. Fifty-two years she'd have waited for that." He wrote it in the almanac, that night, beneath the old entry, in the different ink of the added-to pages: "The word came.

Petrichor. Delivered by our granddaughter, june of her sixth year. Margaret: You were right. J." The correspondence, as it happened, never closed. It never closes anywhere in this family. That's the family.)
