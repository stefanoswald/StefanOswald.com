Night 219

The Reading Group Shuffle

Month 8: School Stories

The reading groups shuffled this week (mid-year assessment; kids moved up, down, sideways, the room's quiet earthquake; every kid knows the groups are "not ranked" and every kid can rank them). Her lamp-work covered all three weathers: the moved-up kid (glowing, and promptly insufferable for a day, "the puffed thing; winning has one; somebody should tell him the winner cleans up"). The moved-down kid (crushed; found at recess; given the September-bed law personally: "groups measure where you are, not where you end. The dahlias got moved down in july, basically, and owned September.

The group is a photograph, one jar, from behind, and kids happen forwards"). And her own non-move (top group, unchanged, and the strange flatness of that: "no summit if there's no climb, Mama. The comfortable spot's got no story in it. That's not a complaint.

It's just... noticing that the top of one ladder is mostly a waiting room for the next ladder").
