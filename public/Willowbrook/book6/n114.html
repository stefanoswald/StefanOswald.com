Night 114

The Deputy Wednesday

Month 4: My Jobs

A shift on the Wednesday walk this week, small and enormous: Theo walked ahead. Half a step. Narrating the route himself: "Corner. Look both ways. Twice. Out loud. The guard waves, wave back, it's the rule.

Cinnamon means halfway..." (The program, running in its student. The teacher, walking half a step behind for the first time, hands in pockets, whole heart up.) "Someday..." He announced at the cubby, mid-firework. "I walk this walk with my little kid.

The walk's gonna be mine when you go to the big-big school. Right? The posts pass down. You said. The chain thing... The posts pass down. That's right. Wednesdays'll be yours, the whole program, the letters, the firework.

You're already deputy. The deputy walks half a step ahead so the postholder can check the handoff." (She invented that rule on the spot, as it happened, to make the half step official instead of heartbreaking. Both. Two coats. The whole era's flavor, forever.)
