Night 364

The Year of Five, Counted

Month 12: Almost Six

The year's-end check. The big one. Twelve months, named like the old friends they are: "The insides month, the clock, the breves, the lantern's rota. The kite month, the hinge, the steady, the tree flying with its words for cargo.

The differents month, the ways of working, the empty jar of normal, the factory of originals. The jobs month, the posts, the counting-on, the envelope of egg money. The brook month, the source plate, the heron, the guarded bend. The practice month, the maps, the plateau, the hundred days.

The garden month. The garden, whole, from weeds. The school stories, the wobble, the blur, the wall. The truth month, the kindest true thing first, stitched and hanging. The long winter, the lobby, the sky puzzle, the gold squares.

The almanac month, the fires, the fourth hand, the attention that is the love. And the gold month, the word. True. In the bark and in the box. That's the whole book of Five. Three hundred sixty-five nights.

Nobody skipped. Six books now, Mama. The shelf of me is six books deep, and the bear says the seventh finishes the set, and I'm not ready to think about that and I'm completely ready, both, two coats, always two coats." Remembered.

Bound. Shelved beside its five elders. (the story, which is the book of Five, and the books of all the years, hello again, little one, from inside your own shelf, one shelf-spot left, signs here: witnessed. All of it. The openest year.

The gears of everything, and the love at the bottom of all the gears. One night remains. The sky's expecting us. It always is.)
