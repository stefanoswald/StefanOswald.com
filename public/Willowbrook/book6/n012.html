Night 12

The Steering Problem

Month 1: How Things Work

The Hawk's next system: Steering. Mateo's plan: rope to the front axle, cart-style. The detail person's worry, noted in the log: "Carts steer on ground. The Hawk leaves the ground. What steers air?" The library run (the bridge book's shelf had neighbors): Tails.

Rudders. "Planes steer from the back, Mateo. The tail pushes the air and the air pushes back and the whole machine swings around its middle. Front-steering is for ground. Tail-steering is for sky.

The Hawk needs both, wheels-rope for the runway, a little tail for the air part." The tail: built. Bedsheet and lath, a hand-span tall, hinged with two of Petrov's smallest hinges (donated; "aviation gets shop rates"). "How'd you know to check the books?"

Mateo asked, screwing the hinge. "I didn't know. I doubted. Doubt plus a library card is basically engineering."
