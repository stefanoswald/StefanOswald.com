Night 17

The Doorbell Circuit

Month 1: How Things Work

The doorbell went under the lens: "The button's here. The bell's there. The finger pushes and the ding happens far away, instant. What carries it?" The trace, Papa-guided: the wire in the wall. The loop.

The electricity, waiting in the line like water behind a tap, the button doesn't send anything; the button closes a gap, and the waiting power flows the instant the road connects. "The push doesn't make the ding. The push completes the circle. The power was already there, wanting to go, and the button just... finishes the road."

The pause the story waits for: "Papa. The whistle code. The wave code. The bench. They're circuits. The caring's already in the line, always on, waiting, and the signal just closes the gap and the whole warm flows. I've been building circuits the whole time."
