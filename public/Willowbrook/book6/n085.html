Night 85

The Superpower Draft

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

This month went playground-viral this week, mutated into a game: The superpower draft. Kids drafting each other's differents like trading cards: "I draft wren's hearing-the-room. I draft zoe's pavement speed. I draft miles's singing road.

I draft the beige kid's, his knowing what he likes. He's never once been talked out of a lunch. That's unshakeable. That's a power." (The beige kid, hearing his pickiness drafted as unshakeability, second round, high, stood taller for a week.

The draft, as it happened, kept doing this: every "weird" trait, re-priced by the market as a power, came back re-priced in its owner's own books. The economics of this month, completing themselves at recess, unsupervised, in game form.) Her own draft stock, for the story: the collecting went first round. But the trait drafted hardest, three-way bidding war, surprised her: "The asking-right.

The what-does-it-build question. I draft that. No, I draft that... I drafted it first..."
