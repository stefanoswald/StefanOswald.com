Night 277

The Hundred-Piece Sky

Month 10: The Long Winter

The winter's marathon project opened on the card table: a thousand-piece puzzle. (The image: a sky. Just sky, clouds, gradient, one bird. "The hardest kind..." By the box. "Good..." By the puzzler.) The method, month-matched: the edge first.

The sort by shade (. "a hundred blues, Mama, and they're all different blues, the puzzle's a paint-chip wall of one sky; somebody at the puzzle factory did the color-naming job and I respect them"). And the pace, declared on day one: "No deadline. The puzzle's a season, not a task. Some pieces a day.

Some days no pieces. It lives on the table and the table lives with it and by spring: a sky. It's the slowest campaign on the board and that's the point, the long winter needs one thing that's allowed to take the whole winter."
