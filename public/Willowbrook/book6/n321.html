Night 321

The Arthritis Conversation

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

This month's hard session came at its own pace, the book open between three generations: The farm's future, at true size. The facts, laid out in Joe's level voice: the joints. The farm. The options, weighed like seed orders: selling.

Leasing the far fields to the Petersen kids ("likely; good hands; the water-sharing families; the land stays worked"). And the house, the coop, the near garden, the porch: "mine while I'm mine. The Saturdays hold. The arrangement holds.

We're not ending anything, you understand, we're re-rigging. The Hawk flew with rebuilt wings. Farms do the same or they don't fly at all." ("The re-rigging isn't the crash..." She said at the lamp after, working it the way she works the big ones.

"The re-rigging is how it keeps flying. The tractor entry, Mama, you get and you give and the honest ones keep both receipts. The far fields receipt says: the land stays worked. The porch receipt says: the porch stays ours.

Both in the same drawer. The farm's just... learning the long division of itself. And it's got a atlas. We're the atlas.")
