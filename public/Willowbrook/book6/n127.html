Night 127

The Message in a Bottle Question

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

The old dream surfaced: The bottle. A message, downstream, toward the sea..." The brook's the sea when it's little; the mail should ride. "The ethics, checked first: glass, ruled out. The solution: a cedar float, Petrov-shop-made, the message burned in: "This float rides from Willowbrook.

The brook is the sea starting small. If you find this: The town with the gold tree says hi. Be kind to the water. It's carrying everybody's mail. O & T." The launch: from the bridge, both hands, one each.

The float, spinning off downstream, past the flood lines, around the bend, gone, into the whole unknowable rest of it. "Where will it get?" Theo asked. "That's the whole gift, Theo. We'll never know. Some mail you send without tracking.

The sending is the message." (the story knows where it got. The story isn't telling either. Some mail belongs to the water.)
