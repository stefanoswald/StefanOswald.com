Night 363

Bobby Bear's Letter to Six

Month 12: Almost Six

Under the pillow, two nights into six, the annual mail: The letter, the longest yet, because the bear had the most to say: "Dear six. It is me. The longest letter yet, because the year coming is the biggest one yet, and bears believe in proportional mail. Things I know about the year of seven: The sentence ends.

The tree's last word comes at your seventh birthday and the watch has voted not to peek and the moon hummed three times when the tree chose it. (The third thing, almost. The third thing the moon ever hummed three for. You'll understand at the bark.)

The gate opens. The key knows its season. I will walk you there myself. It's the walk I was made for. I've known since the crib, the way bears know their jobs. Your brother's paper-chain year runs alongside yours.

Help him live it loud. The rock falls in july. (The chain knew. Chains know.) Second grade has a teacher you'll fight with and then love. Both parts matter. The fighting is this month three story.

The loving is the rest. Trust the story. And the big one, almost. The thing I've waited six letters to say: This is the last year of the first shelf. The books of you. The nightly ones, the lamp ones.

The seventh one finishes the set. After that, the lamp keeps burning but the books change shape. They become the ones you write. You've been practicing your whole life. The pile gets bigger because of you, remember.

You said it at four. It was always the forecast. Keep the key warm. Keep the box filling. Keep the meadow's rumor false. Love, Bobby bear. P. S. The word in your box. The tree meant it about you, too.

It means every word about you, too. That's the part nobody guesses. Check the sentence again with that key. B. B." (She checked the sentence again with that key. Home. Family. Growing. Kind. True. Someone saw her read it as a description, of a town, of a family, of a year, and then, slowly, as an address.

The tree has been writing to someone all along. The letter went in the box beside the word. The box, two residents deep, clicked shut on the whole discovery.)
