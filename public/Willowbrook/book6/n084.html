Night 84

The One Who Didn't Want Studying

Month 3: Different Is Interesting

Truth page, this month's edition: one kid said no. A classmate, the study reached her in rotation, heard the pitch ("everybody's different is in the notebook; yours can be too; the notebook's fair; Marcus vouches") and said, simply: "I don't want to be in your book." The old reflex: persuade. (The pitch was good.

The notebook is fair. The) The trained response, done instead, with effort the story honors by recording: "... Okay. Not everybody wants witnessing. The blank-page rule. I got a whole case that's blank on purpose; it's my best work, the agency says.

Your page stays blank. And the blank's not a hole. The blank is your page, exactly how you want it kept." And at the lamp: "It stung, Mama. The collector in me hates a gap.

But this month's been saying it all along and I finally heard the whole sentence: the differences belong to their owners. The studying was always borrowing. And borrowing means the no is always available. A collection where nobody can say no isn't a collection. It's a cage with nice labels."
