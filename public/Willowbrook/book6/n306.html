Night 306

The Entry About the Fence

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

Night's entry: "June 3, 1981. Harsh words with abel over the line fence. Both right. Both wrong. The fence don't care." The story behind it, fire-drawn: the boundary dispute (two feet of pasture; two stubborn men; one whole summer of silence between farms).

The repair, and this is why the entry survived fifty years of pages: "Sept 9, 1981. Abel's barn took lightning. Was there by the smoke with every man in the valley. The fence line never came up again.

The fence don't care. The barns do. The fence don't care; the barns do..." She repeated, filing it whole. "Grandpa, that's the whole fight physics. The thing you're fighting over never cares. The things that burn.

Those care. And the smoke settles every line dispute in the valley, always, instantly. Somebody should tell people before the smoke. That's what the book's for, darlin'. Somebody's telling you now."
