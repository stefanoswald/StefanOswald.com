Night 288

The Sick Week Reading Aloud

Month 10: The Long Winter

The flu took Mama this week. (The house's axis, horizontal. The strangeness of it: total. Mamas don't get sick, except they do, and the house discovers what it's made of.) What the house was made of, by the story: the soup (Papa's hands, her recipe-reading.

Grandma's card, the wrong flour and all, "she means 2, everybody knows"). The forecast, run by the children. The quiet rules, run upward (the soft landing, the waived taxes. Theo policed the volume like a four-year-old librarian).

And the lamp, reversed entirely: her, at the parental bedside, reading chapters aloud, the dog book, the retired classic, "because when I was sick you read to me, and the sick person gets the lamp brought to them; that's the lamp's whole law; it travels." ("Best medicine in the house," Mama said on day four, mending. "The soup was close. The chapters won." the story concurs and saves the scene where it saves the great reversals: the lamp travels.

It was always going to. That was the plan from page one, book one, whether anyone said it or not.)
