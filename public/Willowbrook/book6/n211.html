The Team Month, Counted

Month 7: Team Willowbrook

Month's end. At month's end, The notebook could hold one proposal. Thirty-one ground-breakers; fifty wound-responders. One bench. One marble. Three rain barrels drinking a library's roof. One deadlock dissolved by introduction. One smallest bed.

One vandal night; zero second nights. One harvest table, one lot long. One stand running on warm credit. No list could quite hold that the lot farmed itself waiting, forty years of unscheduled compost under the weeds, which is, she noted..."

Probably true of every empty-looking thing and every empty-looking person: the nothing years were secretly soil years. "That the old ones were waiting to be asked. That wasn't the soil that changed. "Good team-month?

The provingest one since the chain. Mama, the whole year I studied insides: clocks, kites, brooks, ways of working. This month I saw the town's insides. And it's the doorbell again, always the doorbell: the caring was in the line the whole forty years.

The lot just needed somebody to push the button and complete the circuit. Any somebody. The button doesn't care how tall you are." Remembered. The button doesn't care how tall you are. Push on.
