Night 240

The First Morning of Summer

Month 8: School Stories

On the first free morning of summer, Olivia and Mateo climbed to their favorite branch above the park.

Below them waited the community garden's second season, full Saturdays at the farm, the kite festival, Mateo's redesigned Willowhawk, Theo's approach to the big swimming rock, the brook, the bench, and a stack of unread books.

"A whole summer," Olivia said.

At the end of it, she would turn six and the Great Willow would add another word to its golden sentence.

"What do you think the word will be?" Mateo asked.

Olivia had guesses. She kept them to herself.

"The tree is better at saying its own words."

They sat with their legs swinging over the branch. The season did not need to be planned down to every hour. It only needed to be entered.

Mateo jumped first. Olivia followed, and the long gold morning began.
