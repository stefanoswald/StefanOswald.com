Night 26

The Fan and the Sail

Month 1: How Things Work

Pre-Hawk research, living-room division: The wind tunnel. (The box fan. Paper boats with paper sails, floated in the long hallway on a bedsheet "sea." Theo: sea operations. The couch: grandstand.) The findings: sail angled flat to the wind: pushed straight.

Sail angled sideways: the boat slides across the wind, "Across, Theo! The wind goes one way and the boat goes another! The sail steals sideways power from straight wind! That's not obeying the wind, that's negotiating with it!"

And the transfer, logged for the hill: "The Hawk's wings are sails, basically. The wind'll push, and the shape decides what the push becomes. Flat gets shoved. Angled gets lifted. The whole difference between being blown around and flying is... the angle you meet the push at." (the story underlines that last sentence and says nothing.

Some sentences about wind are never only about wind. This family produces one about weekly.)
