Night 204

The First Frost Race

Month 7: Team Willowbrook

The almanac called it Tuesday: First frost, thursday night. The race: word ran the systems (the counter, the window, the codes, the honor board's chalk: "Frost thursday. All hands. Gloaming harvest wednesday"). And Wednesday gloaming delivered the season's last great scene: the whole bed-community, stripping the tender crops by string-light, tomatoes green and red alike ("the green ones ripen in paper bags; bench knowledge; nothing's lost that's looked after"), the last beans, the basil by armloads, Theo's final stubby carrots, pulled and counted like treasure.

And Thursday night, the frost came exactly on schedule, and found the beds empty, tucked, mulched, done. "The cold got nothing, Mama. Not because we thought it, you can't fight cold; the kite taught the wind rules, because we listened to the almanac and ran. The deadline made the best workday of the year.

Deadlines do that. The cold's the gloaming of the whole season: it doesn't ruin the day. It makes the day count."
