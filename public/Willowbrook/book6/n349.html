Night 349

The Question Row at Year Three

Month 12: Almost Six

The question row's third season, reviewed at the lot pre-birthday (tradition now; the dragons get their check before the word): The glass gem corn: up, thriving, its promise still sealed in green husks ("the catalog swears the kernels come out like stained glass. August tells. The row's whole religion: August tells"). The luffa, returned: sprawling with sophomore confidence ("the second-year dragons grow like they own the fence.

They kind of do"). The okra: Margaret's line, year three, stubborn and tall and entirely family. And Theo's bed, doubled: the sorry-flower crop industrial, the sunflower wall his now, and one new corner, self-planted, unlabeled, "My question row..." He explained.

"The seeds from the mystery packet at the exchange. Nobody knows what they are. The old lady who donated them forgot. That's the best kind of question." (The mystery corner, later for the impatient: Moonflowers.

They bloomed at night, all August, white as the moon's own crop, and the evening garden became a destination, and the forgetting old lady, brought to see them, remembered everything, all at once, at the first bloom: "My mother grew these." The best kind of question. The boy was right. He's usually right. Tenderhearted.)
