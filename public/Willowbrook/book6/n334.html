Night 334

The Watch Before the Word

Month 11: Grandpa Joe's Almanac

On the final evening, Stella's light touched the willow's highest branches. The word-season's opening session, sixth annual, the full watch assembled the way they assemble only for this: "The year in summary, for the pre-word record..." said Oliver Owl, tally book open. "She opened the town's every mechanism and found attention at the bottom of all of them.

She held four posts and handed two on. She kept a month of fires with the almanac's keeper and became its fourth hand. The exchange runs. The garden enters year two. The family book accumulates in four hands.

And the bear reports..." A nod to the velvet colleague against the trunk. "the meadow holds, the key stays warm, and the far gate's season approaches. One more birthday, give or take. The gate knows.

And the word?" asked Harry, in from the rounds, leaf bright with spring. The watch turned, as one, to the tree above them, the bark smooth and ready in its gold-lit place beneath home, family, growing, kind. The tree said nothing, per three hundred years of policy. But the branches moved, the sure, unhurried movement of a writer who finished being sure eleven months ago and has simply been enjoying the wait.

"Mmm..." Hummed the Moon, who watched the word get chosen and has hummed about it privately all year. "Log the opening..." said Stella, gold banked and glowing. "The town below sleeps. The chain unspools morning by morning.

And at the gold month's end, at gloaming, a girl who spent her whole sixth year learning that attention is the love walks up this hill, and the oldest attention-payer in the valley shows her its receipt." Goodnight, Willowbrook, at the opening of the word season. The tree is sure. The tree was always sure. Sleep. Soon: gloaming.
