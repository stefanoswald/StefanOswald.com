Night 149

The Month's Last Crossing

Month 5: The Brook's Long Story

This month closed where months close when they're water months: The crossing. The seven stones, winter edition (ice-checked, recounted: "Stone three's liar edge is worse iced. Stone six wears a snow hat but holds true. The sentence-pair reads slower in boots").

And at stone seven, home free, the winter brook running its private blblblb below, this month's formal thanks, delivered midstream style, out loud, by the family's whole religion of thanking the carrying things: "Okay, brook. This month's over, so: thank you for the source plate and the minnow school and the heron's office and the otter's bend, guarded, still guarded, always guarded. Thank you for running under the lid. Thank you for the mail service and the tuition perch and for being the sea starting small and patient with it.

You're the oldest goer in town and the willow's the oldest stayer and I got to read one page of each of you this year. Keep going. That's your whole job and nobody does it better. See you at the uncovering."

The water, running, said what it always says. (Everything. Nothing. Blblblb. The story has never needed it translated and neither has she.)
