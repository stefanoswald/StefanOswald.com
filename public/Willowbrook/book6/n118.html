Night 118

The Post Holder's Rest

Month 4: My Jobs

This month's last town rule, proposed by the grown-ups (Mama; the grown-ups watches its postholder the way postholders watch hens): The sunday part. One day. No posts. No coop, no cart, no route, no corner, no drives, no count.

Rest..." As a post. "the framing that finally sold it: "the holding of nothing, faithfully, once a week." The first Sunday: Hard. (the story logs it honestly: the hands twitched for jobs. The eyes found three fixable things by ten a.m.

The trained instinct paced its cage.) The learning, by the third: the woolgathering hour, reclaimed. The reading with no corner to curate for. The floor-lying. The bear time, needed different, still needed, Sundays are for the old needings.

The being a kid who is counted on by no one for one whole rotation of the lane. "The rest is a discipline..." She conceded at the lamp. "The hardest post yet. But Mama, the Monday coop was sharper.

The Tuesday cart was gladder. The rest isn't stolen from the posts. The rest is what the posts run on. Even the willow, you said it and I didn't hear it right the first time, even the willow doesn't hold every bird every night.

The willow rests all winter. The willow is the best postholder in town and it takes three months off."
