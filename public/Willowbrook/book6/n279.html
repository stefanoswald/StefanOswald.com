Night 279

The Board Game Renaissance

Month 10: The Long Winter

The game shelf's deep cut came down this week: the complicated box. (The one with the rulebook. The one saved for "someday." The long winter is someday, that's this month's whole economy.) The conquest: three nights.

Night one: the rules, fought ("the rulebook's a hard page, gear down, check the diagrams, the sentence around the rule is the free clue"). Night two: the first real game (Papa won; the constitution held; the winner cleaned up). Night three: The upset. Theo, age four, playing "the farm strategy," won.

Legitimately. The table checked the math twice. ("The fourteen-game trap's little brother..." His sister ruled, awed. "He's been watching us learn for two nights and building the sheep thing quiet. The tenderhearted got a thinking longer inside it now.

The apprentice is becoming a whole department." The handshake across the board: solemn, traditional, gloat-free. "Good game, Wivy. Good game, Theo. Rematch." The war, sure enough, remains eternal, and remains the healthiest institution either of them owns.)
