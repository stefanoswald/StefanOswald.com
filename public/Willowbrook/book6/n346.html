Night 346

The Meadow at Almost-Six

Month 12: Almost Six

The meadow came, birthday-faithful, gold as its whole tradition, and the bear was waiting on the path, and the walk went, for the first time in six years, all the way to the far gate. Close up: old wood. Warm. A keyhole shaped exactly like the shape her hand has kept warm for two years.

And the bear, at her shoulder, velvet and sure: "Not tonight. But look at it proper, you've earned the looking. The gate opens the year you're seven. The seventh year, Almost. The last book of the first shelf.

What's behind it..." The bear's ear flopped in the gold wind. "is the meadow's whole point, and the key's whole job, and I've kept the secret six years and I'll keep it one more, because the tree taught everybody the same thing: the wait is part of the word. One more year. One more year.

And Almost..." The bear's annual report, delivered at the gate this time, which made it different, which made it more. "the needing changed shape again this year, and the meadow held, and the rumor stayed false, and it stays false forever. Even the year the gate opens. Especially then.

Write it in the new box when the tree gives you something to put beside it: The meadow stays open. Growing up is a door, not a wall. It was always a door. Everything here was always a door."
