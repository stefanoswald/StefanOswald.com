Night 182

The Town Garden Proposal

Month 7: Team Willowbrook

This month opened with the year's biggest build: The empty lot. (Beside the library. Weedy, fenced, town-owned, unused since anyone's memory. Sure enough: forty years of nothing.) The proposal, drafted at office hours, presented at the council's public comment: "A town garden, sirs and ma'ams.

The lot's just sitting there holding weeds. Plan attached: beds for whoever signs up, a shared tool shed, a question row for the whole town (that's a row where you grow things you never grew, it's a system, it works, ask my grandpa), and a bench (benches make the old ones come; the old ones make the gardens work, they got fifty years of dirt-knowing and nowhere to put it). Budget: small. Labor: free.

I got a squadron, a class, and an egg route. The town provides: the yes." (The town provided the yes. Unanimous. As it happened the councilman's motion used the phrase "field data.")
