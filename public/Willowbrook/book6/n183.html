Night 183

The Ground Breaking

Month 7: Team Willowbrook

Workday one: The clearing. Turnout: thirty-one humans, ages four to eighty-one, plus one dog and one wheelbarrow. The weeds fell to the crowd. The soil, met underneath, got the expert assessment. Joe, on knees, crumbling a handful like a man reading a letter: "Better than it has any right to be.

Forty years of weeds, darlin', is forty years of compost nobody scheduled. The lot's been farming itself, waiting." (they remembered the metaphor where the whole month can see it.) And the day's find, six inches down, Theo's shovel: a marble. Old glass, swirled, buried who-knows-when by who-knows-who.

("A kid gardened here before the before..." ruled the finder's sister. "E. W.'s rule: the ground keeps its kids too. The marble goes in the shed when there is a shed. First exhibit. Every garden needs a museum.")
