Night 21

How Sleep Works

Month 1: How Things Work

The lens turned, inevitably, on the lamp's own institution: "Mama. What does sleep actually do? I close the eyes, the night disappears, I'm new. What happened in the gap?" The night shift, explained: the body's repair crews.

The brain's librarians, "this is real, love; while you sleep, your brain sorts the whole day: keeps the keepers, saves the lessons, sweeps the scraps. Dreams are partly the sorting, the librarians trying combinations, seeing what shelves things belong on. My brain has a night librarian?! The day gets returned and shelved every night?!

That's why hard things look smaller in the morning. The night staff saved them. That's why you always say 'sleep on it', you're not saying wait, you're saying let the librarians at it..."
