Night 337

The Sixth Line

Month 12: Almost Six

The doorframe. Shoes off. The book. The pencil. The sixth line, and the snow angel's forecast confirmed in wood: the biggest gap on the frame. ("The growth spurt's receipt. The shoes knew, the snow knew, now the frame knows.

All my archives agree, that's how you know a thing's true. Cross-referenced in three materials.") The frame, read entire, six years of ladder: the baby line. The wobbly two. The proud three. The fourth line's lesson.

The fifth's school year. And now six..." The insides line. The year I opened everything. "And Theo's fifth line, drawn beside, and his check, delivered with the gravity of a boy who reads now: "The frame's gonna run out of door someday."

("Then we start on the other side..." His sister ruled, instantly. "and when that runs out, the kitchen's got more doorframes, and when the whole house runs out..." The pause; the grin. "we'll be too tall to measure and somebody smaller'll be starting at the bottom. The frame never runs out, Theo. It just changes who.")
