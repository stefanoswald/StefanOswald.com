Night 9

The Witching Hour

Month 1 — Arrival

Every evening around dinnertime, Olivia came undone. Fed, dry, warm, held — and crying anyway, as if the day had simply been too much day.

It has a name, this hour. Parents call it the witching hour, and nearly every small baby keeps the appointment.

Mama and Papa learned to meet it like weather. Lights down before it began. A bath, sometimes, to change the subject. The sway-and-shush, walking the hallway like a slow ferry crossing a narrow river.

And they learned to take shifts. Twenty minutes of pacing, then hand her over and step outside to breathe. A crying baby in calm arms does better than a crying baby in frantic ones, and calm is easier to keep in twenty-minute pieces.

It always ended. That was the secret the clock knew. By eight, Olivia lay sleeping like nothing had ever been wrong in the history of the world.

The evening forgave everyone. Evenings do.
