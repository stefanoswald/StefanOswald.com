Night 13

Growth Spurt Week

Month 1 — Arrival

Suddenly, Olivia was hungry all the time. She fed, dozed for a blink, and woke up outraged that no one had ever fed her, not once, in her entire life.

Mama checked the calendar. Two weeks old, nearly to the day. Right on schedule.

Growth spurts arrive like little storms — around two or three weeks, again near six, again near three months — a few days when a baby seems to want to eat the whole world. It is not a supply problem. It is a construction project: bones lengthening, brain wiring itself by the mile, and all of it billed to the kitchen.

So Mama stopped fighting it and built a nest instead: water within reach, snacks in a bowl, phone, pillow, footstool. Feed, doze, repeat, and let the week be what it was.

By Friday the storm passed, and Olivia slept a long, heavy, workmanlike sleep.

She woke up measurably more baby than before. Storms do that.
