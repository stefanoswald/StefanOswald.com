Night 268

The Photograph Question

Month 9: The Edge of Everything

The far aunts' spring petition, renewed monthly since the festival, carried at last: the nine-month photograph, the formal one, the portrait, the frame-and-mantel edition, and the household staged the attempt on the month's final Sunday.

The production, logged in the almanac's driest hand. The setup: the good blanket, the window's true light, the festival outfit's fourth deployment, one bear (dresser leave granted, supervised). The subject: a walking, pointing, four-coin sovereign with a rug's worth of standing docket and no observed vacancy for portraiture. The sessions: eleven minutes gross. The yield: the outfit de-shoed (both, sequential, mid-frame), the bear freighted offstage on urgent business, the blanket's country toured at prestige gait, the lens itself petitioned at point-blank per the customs office's standing warrant, and of the forty frames fired, thirty-eight documenting motion, protest, or the backs of things.

And the two that remained, per the way of these productions: the accidents. Frame nineteen, the subject mid-turn at Papa's off-camera "duck!", the laugh arriving ahead of the face's arrangement, the whole nine months of her in it. Frame thirty-three, the production abandoned, the outfit half-untucked, the subject returned to the rug's own docket with the bear restored, and the camera, lowered, firing once more from the hip at the ordinary gold of it: the blocks, the concentration frown, the one bare foot, the lamplight going long across the year's true portrait.

The mantel got frame thirty-three. The aunts got nineteen. The almanac got the finding, entered per the first month's own doctrine at the year's far end, vindicated entire:

The unposed pictures were always the pictures. The mess, the pile, the chair-sleepers, the first month's page had ordered them taken on faith; the ninth month's mantel ran the proof. The formal record of a childhood, the page counsels every petitioned household, will hold a dozen frames; the true one is shot from the hip, at the rug, in the ordinary gold, per the doctrine, and the production's own failure is the method: stage the portrait if the aunts require it, then lower the camera and wait for the year to look like itself.

It always does, within the minute. It did.

"Duck," the portrait's subject had said, at frame nineteen's own instant, to the whole enterprise.

Correct, per custom. Filed, framed, sent north to the aunts with the household's love.

The rug's gold ran long into the evening, unphotographed thereafter, per the ten-minutes doctrine, watched.

Both records, the page rules as the almanac ruled the box: both. The mantel and the memory. Both, as ever.

The year, looking like itself, ran on toward its candle.
