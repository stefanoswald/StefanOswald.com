Night 245

The Landing Curriculum

Month 9: The Edge of Everything

With the step came the step's constant companion, and the page gives it its own chapter because every walking household lives here for months: the fall.

They came at the new trade's full rate, the lurch that outran the feet, the turn that lost the map, the classic mid-stride sit-down executed dozens of times daily, and the house ran the era on two curricula at once: hers, and theirs.

Hers first, because the body wrote it. The nine-month faller is engineered for the work: the center of gravity low, the diaper the finest crash equipment ever fitted, and the sit-down landing, the drop to the bottom the crib academy taught in the pulling-up era, now running as walking's default failure mode, automatic, padded, and correct. The falls themselves were the gait's tuition per the whole book's oldest law: each one recalibrating the next stride's ledger, the balance learned at the exact edge where it kept being lost.

Theirs, the harder one: the triage glance, and the face.

The glance, drilled until it ran cold: where landed (bottom and hands, the overwhelming standard, carry on) and what hit (the head's rare involvement graded per the refrigerator list's standing clauses: the height, the surface, the cry's arrival, instant crying being, counterintuitively, the reassuring sound, and the after: the vomiting, the wrong drowsiness, the unsoothable hour, each a call; the goose-egg on a busy forehead with a fast cry and a faster recovery being, per every pediatric line ever staffed, the age's ordinary weather).

And the face: the era's deepest craft. The fallen walker's first act, every fall, was the lookback, the social referencing of the whole pointing year, aimed now at the question that matters: how bad was that? and the answer she read off the parental weather became the fall's actual size. The gasp taught falls were emergencies; the level face with the easy voice, oops, up you get, taught falls were punctuation. The house chose punctuation, held it through its own flinches, and watched the curriculum compound: by the month's end the faller supplied her own verdict, sitting down hard mid-kitchen and announcing to the room, cheerfully, the syllabus entire:

"Uh-oh."

Up she got. On she went. The ledger took the entry in its flattest, proudest hand:

Falls today: beyond count. Injuries: zero. Gait: improving. Face: held.
