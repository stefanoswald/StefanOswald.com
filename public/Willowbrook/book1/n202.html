Night 202

The Laughing Game Named Again

Month 7 — Explorers and Guardrails

The cloth went up — over her own face this time, held two-fisted, the world self-subtracted — and the room was given its cue.

"...Where's Olivia?"

The cloth held. Beneath it, audibly, the mischief gathered — the breath quickening, one sock-foot pumping — the pause stretched by its small director for maximum charge.

Drop. "There she is!" Detonation: the full-body laugh, and the cloth already rising for the encore.

The page marks the milestone precisely, because it is easy to miss inside the giggles: the game had changed authorship. Peekaboo, version one, was performed at her — the parent hiding, the parent revealing, the baby the audience. The transfer of the cloth to her own hands (begun as grabbing in the fifth month, compiled now to full production) made her the author: choosing the hide, working the timing, running the reveal. The oldest game had been handed over, and she ran it like repertory theater — the cloth, the couch-back, the high chair tray held absurdly before her face, the crib rail (the morning address delivered now with hide-and-reveal segments), each venue tested for its comic properties.

Beneath the theater, the syllabus, as ever. Authored peekaboo is the object-permanence doctrine performed from the inside: I persist while hidden, and I know it, and I can bank your certainty of it and sell it back to you as surprise. It is timing — the held pause being the first joke structure she would ever own, the same architecture underneath every punchline she'd land for life. And it is the social flowering's capstone: reading the audience, working the audience, the room's laughter conducted from behind a burp cloth by someone eight months old.

The evening's run went nine shows, the pauses lengthening as the artist tested her instrument, the ninth held so long that the audience broke protocol and peeked first — received, on the drop, as the funniest event in recorded history.

Closing night was called by yawn, mid-hide.

The cloth came down on a director already half asleep, the theater dark, the run extended indefinitely by acclaim.
