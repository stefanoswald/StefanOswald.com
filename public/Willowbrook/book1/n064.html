Night 64

Naps, a Field Guide

Month 3 — Rhythm

Naps, Mama decided, deserved a field guide, like birds. They came in species.

The Long Restorative — the crown jewel, an hour or more, usually mid-morning at this age. The Catnap — thirty or forty-five minutes, one sleep cycle exactly, common and normal and slightly heartbreaking when you had plans. The Stroller Doze, the Car Seat Special (transferred to the crib when possible, watched when not), and the rare, majestic Contact Nap, taken entirely on a parent, pinning them beneath a warm and non-negotiable weight.

The field guide's first law: at three months, naps are still wild creatures. Some days four, some days three, lengths assorted. The tidy two-nap schedule of the parenting charts lives months away; chasing it now is chasing autumn in July.

The second law: protect the habitat anyway. A darkened room, the white noise sea, the wind-down verse of song — same cues as night, so sleep smells like sleep wherever it lands.

And the third law, for the pinned parent under the contact nap: you are not trapped. You are exactly where the science and the sweetness agree you might as well be. Breathe. The chores are liars, remember.

Today's specimen: Long Restorative, eighty minutes, crib. A rare beauty.

Mama photographed it for the records, from the doorway, on tiptoe.
