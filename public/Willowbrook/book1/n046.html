Night 46

Grandpa Joe Comes to Town

Month 2 — Smiles and Sleep

Grandpa Joe drove in from Honeywell Farm with eggs, a rocking chair he had fixed for the occasion, and his particular brand of visiting, which was mostly silence with a hat on.

He washed his hands without being asked — forty years of livestock teaches a man what hands carry. Then he sat, received Olivia like a parcel of seed corn, precious and calmly held, and said, "Well now," which was a whole speech, from him.

And then he did the finest thing a visitor can do: he stayed put, contentedly, for two hours, and ordered the parents to be elsewhere. "Sleep or shower," he said. "I've held things that kick harder than this."

This is the visit worth learning from, the kind this page is really about. The best guests hold the baby so you can be a person; the other kind hold the baby so you can make them coffee. Families are allowed to tell the difference out loud.

Mama slept. Papa showered and then also slept.

Grandpa Joe and Olivia discussed the ceiling, crops, and nothing.

"Well now," he said again, as she gripped his enormous finger.

It meant: you'll do, little one. You'll more than do.
