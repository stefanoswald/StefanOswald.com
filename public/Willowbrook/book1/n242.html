Night 242

The Eighth Month, Counted

Month 8 — The Velcro Weeks and the Thaw

The audit convened at the open window, the spring's first soft night attending, the almanac's eighth chapter the thickest yet in either handwriting.

The countables, entered to the sound of the peepers starting down at the brook. Thirteen seconds standing, mid-floor, self-risen, boxed in the record. One word landed — no, at three inches, the year's biggest inches — and one more all but shipped, the crib's dark dog still under review. A three-beat goodbye engineered and holding at two addresses. One night out, unchaperoned, the sitter's thumbs-up framed in both memories. One food strike weathered on doctrine; one Cheerio standard achieved; three meals load-bearing. A push wagon in scheduled service, a big tub graduated to, a fleet reviewed, first shoes benched per doctrine, a festival attended at altitude, a balloon befriended, a picnic convened, an hour moved and crossed in eight days. The helping bureau: open, staffed, terrible, retained. The velcro weeks: peaked, eased, missed already in the leg's own memory, exactly per the bench's oldest warning.

The uncountables, entered as the peepers found their full spring voice. That the separation, hardest weather of the winter, had built the year's best infrastructure: the ceremonies at the doors, the second harbor's lap, the wave that held what grief had held — the whole apparatus of leaving-and-returning now load-bearing, and load coming. That the person, arriving all year per the boat of the fifth month's audit, was now unmistakably in harbor: a citizen with a docket, a diplomat with protocols, a colleague with a caseload — helping, naming, pausing at cords, showing rings to the watchers on the rug. And the eighth month's own finding, said at the window in the season's own terms: the year had turned. The candle end was visible now down the lane of it — the steps in the building, the words at the warehouse doors, the birthday somewhere in the green weeks ahead — and the two of them had stopped, somewhere in the winter, being people this was happening to, and become the people keeping it: the craft owned, the doctrines theirs, the almanac's two hands steady on the wheel of it.

"Any day now," said Papa, meaning the steps, meaning all of it.

"Maybe," said Mama, and the window's soft air moved through the old word's whole meaning: soon, and not yet, and let it come at its own pace, and let it never, and yes.

The peepers ran the night shift. The audit closed on their singing.

Audited. Approved. Carried forward — paused, at three inches, on the very edge of everything.
