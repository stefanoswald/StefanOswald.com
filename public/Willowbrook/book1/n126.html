Night 126

The Circus Sleeps Anyway

Month 5: Nearly Sitting, Nearly Everything

The wedding was lovely. The baby was lovelier, danced with by four aunts in rotation, applauded for existing, asleep on Papa's shoulder by the cake.

Then came the guest room, and the circus matinee.

The travel crib stood in a strange corner smelling of elsewhere. The window leaked unfamiliar streetlight. The white-noise sea shushed loyally, but the room around it was the wrong room, and Olivia, laid down at the usual hour by the usual liturgy, surfaced at once and filed a formal exception to everything.

It took ninety minutes, three encores of the song, one emergency feed, and a hallway walk in wedding clothes. This page exists to certify: that is the standard rate. First nights away run on circus rules, the routine performed faithfully and discounted heavily, the wake-ups doubled, the parents doing 2 a.m. shift changes in party shoes. It signifies nothing about the sleeper, the parenting, or the future. Elsewhere is simply loud, in a frequency only babies hear.

And then, this is the other half of the certification, she slept. In the strange corner, in the wrong room, in the portable sea: slept. Babies do land, even abroad. The skills bank travels; the routine is home; the second night away, whenever it came, would run at half the circus and the tenth at none.

Morning arrived on schedule, sunny, amnesiac.

Olivia woke delighted with the new ceiling. The aunts had breakfast waiting. The coffee plan, packed with rigor, held.

Journey one: survived by all hands. The suitcase would remember what the heart would forgive.
