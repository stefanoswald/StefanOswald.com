Night 50

The Weather Inside

Month 2 — Smiles and Sleep

This page is quieter than the others, and more important. It is about the weather inside.

Most new mothers get the baby blues in the first weeks — teary at nothing, up and down like a kite, tender everywhere. It rises with the great hormone tide going out, and like a tide, it goes: usually within two weeks, on its own, with sleep and food and kind company.

But sometimes the weather stays. Weeks pass and the fog does not lift — or it thickens. No joy where joy should be. Dread, or numbness, or anger that frightens you. Not feeling like the baby is yours; not feeling like you are yours. That is postpartum depression, and here is everything this page truly wants to say about it: it is an illness, not a failing. It is common — one mother in seven or so, and fathers get their version too. And it is treatable, very. Asking for help is not the moment you failed; it is the moment you got better.

Mama's weather, that gray afternoon, was mostly tired with patches of sun. But she and Papa had made the pact anyway, early, out loud: we say the true number when asked. We call the doctor at two weeks of fog. No hero acts.

The rain eased. The kettle sang. Small mercies, taken daily, as prescribed.

If it is heavy where you are tonight, reader: say it to someone tomorrow. That is the whole page. That is the whole point.
