Night 67

The Baby in the Mirror

Month 3 — Rhythm

There was another baby in the house, and she lived in the mirror by the play mat.

Olivia had noticed her before, vaguely. This month, the noticing became a friendship. Tummy-time flew by when the mirror baby attended — every head-lift rewarded with a face rising to meet hers, every smile answered instantly, in kind, by the friendliest baby in Willowbrook.

She had no idea it was her. That knowledge — the strange, sky-opening discovery of the self — waits until well past the first birthday. For now the mirror baby was simply excellent company: reliable, expressive, always exactly as pleased to visit as Olivia was.

Which is why an unbreakable low mirror is quiet gold in a play space. It doubles the faces (babies' favorite scenery), sweetens the tummy-time economy, and lays the first planks of a bridge she will cross later, when the sky opens and the friend turns out to have been home all along.

"Who's that?" Mama asked, the ancient question.

Olivia smiled. The baby smiled back, flawlessly timed, as always.

Best friends. Same nap schedule, even. Uncanny.
