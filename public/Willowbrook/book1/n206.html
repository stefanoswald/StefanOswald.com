Night 206

What the Year Turned

Month 7: Explorers and Guardrails

The year turned mid-month, per the calendar's habit, and the house marked it in the only currency the era deals: an ordinary bedtime, kept holy, followed by two people at a dark window doing arithmetic they'd been saving up.

She was asleep by seven-fifteen, per the liturgy, sovereign through the neighbor's early sparklers. The vigil she would never remember was therefore theirs alone, and the page keeps it with them.

Last New Year's, the arithmetic ran, she had been a rumor, a flutter Mama carried up these same stairs, a name still in committee, a nursery half-painted. The people at last year's window were expecting; the people at this one had arrived somewhere themselves and knew it: seven months of nights survived and craft acquired, a person raised from rumor to a name that answered, to a wave at this very glass, to a laugh that ran the house's whole economy.

The year ahead they could see the shape of now, the way the halfway hill had promised: the walking, somewhere out there in it. The words, loading nightly. The birthday with its single candle, absurd and enormous, already visible on the spring side. And past the visible: the first everything, on and on, the almanac's blank pages standing by in ranks.

They made the era's version of resolutions, brief, over cocoa, half in eyebrow dialect. To keep the shifts and the soup. To sit on more benches and say the password sooner. To take the unposed pictures, per the first month's standing order, and be in some of them. To hold the Maybe posture through whatever the year threw: the setbacks that would turn out to be renovations, the strikes that would end in treaties, the whole curriculum of things that seem bad and are becoming something else entirely.

Midnight found one of them asleep on the couch (the almanac declines to say which) and was rung in by the other in a whisper, solo, toasting the window with the cold half of the cocoa.

Upstairs, the whole reason slept through her first year's turning, starfished, one fist around the sleep sack's seam.

Happy New Year, Willowbrook. The page turned. The shape held.
