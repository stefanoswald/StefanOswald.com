Night 82

One Book Every Day

Month 3 — Rhythm

The habit was one book long, and it began before it made any sense.

Every evening after bath, Papa read Olivia one board book. She could not follow a plot. She occasionally tried to eat the protagonist. By any strict accounting, the audience was unqualified — and the reading went on anyway, nightly, because the accounting is wrong, and this page exists to say so.

What a three-month-old takes from a lap and a book is nearly everything but the story. The voice, pitched into its reading music, going up the hills and down. The page as an object: turnable, patterned, bright — hers to slap, which is early literacy wearing a bib. The rhythm of ritual: bath, book, song, sleep, the hallway of open doors. And underneath it all, laid brick by nightly brick, an equation that will hold for the rest of her life: book equals lap, book equals voice, book equals loved.

Read anything. Read the board book with one word per page; read the sports section in the reading voice, some tired night — she will bank it identically. The habit is the content, at this age.

One book. Every day. Started absurdly early, continued absurdly long.

Tonight's selection was consumed twice, once by ear and once, briefly, by mouth.

A rave review, in this house's press.
