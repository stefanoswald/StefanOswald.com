Night 93

The Regression That Is Progress

Month 4 — Rolling and Laughing

It struck in the fourth month like weather off the sea: the sleeper who had found her long stretch was suddenly up at eleven, at one-thirty, at three, at four-forty, blinking in the dark, fully staffed and ready for the day at moonrise.

The famous four-month sleep regression — and this page brings the news that reframes it: it is not a regression at all. It is a renovation. Around now, a baby's sleep rebuilds itself permanently into grown-up architecture — cycles with light floors and deep basements, and brief surfacings between cycles, all night, forever after. Adults surface too; they check the pillow is a pillow and dive again, never remembering. A baby surfacing asks the only question she has: is everything how it was when I fell asleep?

Which is why the falling-asleep conditions suddenly matter so much. Sail off in arms, surface in a crib — the pillow is not a pillow; sound the alarm. Sail off in the crib, drowsy but aware, and the 2 a.m. surfacing finds the world in order: dive resumed, case closed.

So the house leaned on the old lottery ticket harder — down drowsy when it worked, comfort without drama when it did not, the boring-night rules kept holy. And they endured, in shifts, knowing the renovation finishes; it always finishes.

Three weeks, this one took.

On the far side, a sleeper with grown-up architecture and the same cheeks.

Renovations end. Write it on the 3 a.m. wall.
