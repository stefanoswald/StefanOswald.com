Night 148

The Season Pass Falters

Month 5: Nearly Sitting, Nearly Everything

The bedtime that had run silken for a month hit gravel this week, and the page takes its notes in real time.

Same bath, same zip, same book, same wrong-noted song, and at the crib, revolt. The arch, the wail, the full parliamentary objection from a body that had signed the drowsy-but-awake accords weeks ago and now, at the chamber door, tore them up nightly.

The suspects were assembled and interviewed. The almost-crawl, prime among them: new motion rehearses at night, and a body mid-launch resents all horizontal proposals. The teething foothills, ever eligible. The lengthening evening light of high summer, bedtime arguing with a bright window. And the simplest suspect, often guiltiest at five months: the day's last wake window grown too long or too short by a shifting nap, delivering an overtired or under-tired citizen to the chamber.

The house's response held to doctrine, and the doctrine held the house. The routine unchanged, the gravel is weather; the road stays the road. The window audited and trimmed (fifteen minutes earlier, this week's fix, the catnap having quietly shortened). The window itself darkened against the summer sun. And at the crib, the patient middle way: comfort present, drama absent, the settling assisted at the minimum dose and re-attempted, some nights twice, some thrice, without anyone, this is the doctrine's core, deciding at 8 p.m. that all past progress was illusion and all future sleep is lost.

Twelve days, this gravel patch ran. Then the crawl launched, the window settled, and the accords, unannounced, resumed.

Silken again. Until the next patch, pulling to stand, the almanac forecast, would be along by autumn.

The road stays the road. Write it small and keep it: the road stays the road.
