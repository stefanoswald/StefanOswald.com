Night 163

The Dropped Toy Doctrine

Month 6 — The Feast Begins

The Dropping Game had run for months on its original charter — release, clatter, assistant retrieves. This month the game's own scientist rewrote the founding theory, and the revision deserved its page.

It showed in the gaze. The ring went over the tray's edge as ever — but the eyes that followed it down stayed down, fixed on the landing site, waiting. Not the old blank blink of a thing subtracted from the universe; a stare with a theory in it: the ring is not gone. The ring is on the floor. The floor is where the ring continues to be.

Object permanence, arriving on its six-to-eight-month schedule: the great truth that hidden and vanished are different words, that the world persists unwatched, that things — and, soon, people — continue offstage. The peekaboo cloth had been teaching it all spring; the high chair's edge was where she published.

The house ran the confirming trials that week, delighted lab partners all. The toy under the cloth, half-showing: retrieved, instantly. Fully covered: a pause, a considering frown — then the cloth yanked, the toy seized, the case closed with a shriek of vindication. The bear behind Papa's back: sought around the flank, located, prosecuted.

The page flags the doctrine's shadow side, kindly: the same truth that finds the hidden toy finds the departed parent. Offstage no longer means dissolved; it means elsewhere, and elsewhere can be minded. The separation feelings of the coming months are not regression but graduation — she will cry at the door precisely because she now knows there is a beyond-the-door with a mother in it. The goodbye rituals, built small and kept sure, would earn their wages soon.

Tonight the ring went over the edge once, formally, at meal's close.

She leaned. She looked. She pointed the whole arm — the gavel's new gesture.

"Correct," said Papa, retrieving. "It was down there the entire time."

The scientist accepted the ring, and the confirmation, as her due. Theories that hold get to be the world, after that. This one would hold.
