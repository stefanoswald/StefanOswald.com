Night 51

Papa's Hour

Month 2: Smiles and Sleep

There was an hour that belonged to Papa alone, and it started when Mama left the house.

That was the trick of it, discovered by accident: as long as Mama was in the building, Olivia held out for headquarters, and Papa played assistant. The moment the door closed, walk, errand, haircut, anything, headquarters transferred. And in the transfer, something grew.

He had his own methods now, unsupervised and patent-pending. His burp technique, unorthodox, effective. His bouncing walk with the half-turn at the window. His radio station, wrong for babies according to no one who was home. Olivia learned him, his rumble, his rhythm, the different way his arms decided things, and he stopped checking over his shoulder for the real parent, having quietly become one.

This page's advice runs two directions at once. To the Papas: take the solo hours; competence only grows unsupervised. To the Mamas: leave the building. Truly out, not hovering, not texting how is she from the driveway. He will do it differently. Different is not wrong. Different is a second whole parent, which is the entire prize.

Mama came home to both of them asleep on the couch, one big hand covering one small back like a roof.

She took the picture. She did not fix anything. There was nothing to fix.
