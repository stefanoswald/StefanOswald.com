Night 232

Sunscreen Season

Month 8 — The Velcro Weeks and the Thaw

The spring sun climbed toward its first real authority, and the household updated its solar treaty: the six-month embargo — the first summer's iron rule of shade, cloth, and timing only — had lapsed with the age line months back, and the season's new instrument came in a tube.

The page files the updated doctrine entire, for every household entering its second sun.

Sunscreen, now licensed: the mineral kind for preference at this age — zinc, titanium, the physical blockers that sit atop the skin rather than soaking in — broad-spectrum, the strength the doctors name, applied in the quantity nobody believes (more; the universal error is thrift) and renewed on the clock, every two hours and after every water engagement, the morning application being a contract, not a coating.

The old regiment, the page insists, retains command: sunscreen joined the defenses; it did not relieve them. Shade remains the senior officer — the stroller's canopy at full sail, the oak's moving chess, the outings steered around the fierce middle hours per the first summer's calendar. The hat treaty, renewed for its second season (the negotiation briefer this year; the precedent held, the duck's escort clause invoked once). The cloth: light long sleeves for the long exposures, the swim shirt already purchased against the summer's brook ambitions. And the watch on the cheeks, the collar-check's solar cousin, for the pink that means the system missed.

The first full application was conducted before the Saturday park run and logged for its comedy: the face dabbed in small strategic deposits and blended while the subject attempted to eat the process; the legs done during a distraction (the window; a dog; providence); the hands — the hands that live in the mouth — done last and least per the practical counsel, the ears and neck-back and foot-tops done first and most, those being the spots the audits of every summer clinic name as the forgotten provinces.

Eleven minutes, first sortie to full coverage. (By July, the almanac would record, the drill ran ninety seconds and the subject held out her own arms for it, the routine having joined the body's list of things that simply happen before outside — the sunscreen years beginning, as all the long routines begin, in one season's patient installments.)

The park received them, dabbed and hatted and treaty-compliant, at ten sharp — ahead of the fierce hours, under the oak's opening canopy, the whole solar architecture holding.

The sun, second season, found its negotiations in order.

It settled for making the morning gold, which had been the arrangement all along.
