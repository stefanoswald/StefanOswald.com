Night 257

The Sleep of the Walking

Month 9 — The Edge of Everything

The steps, three weeks old and compiling daily, sent the year's final skill storm through the nights on schedule, and the page logs it as the era's last full drill of a doctrine now old enough to run itself.

The disturbance's signature, familiar from every predecessor storm: the 2 a.m. surfacing — no hunger's rhythm, no pain's alarm — and the monitor's screen delivering the cause in grainy silver: the citizen up, rail-gripped, and walking. Laps of the crib's short coastline, hand over hand and then — the almanac certifies the footage — hands-free strides down the mattress's whole length, the new gait drilling itself at the hour skills have always chosen, the body's own night classes running per the pattern the book first named at the rolling storm of month five: new motion rehearses at night, loudly, and a body mid-breakthrough resents all horizontal proposals.

The doctrine, applied at its fourth edition with the ease of long practice. The pause, first, always: the surfacings self-resolving at rates the first-year parent never believes and the fourth-storm parent banks on — the monitor watched, the lap run to its own conclusion, the practiser dropping mid-drill to sleep where she landed at rates approaching half the nights. The minimum-dose visit for the remainder: the lay-back-down administered once, twice, boring and level per the strike-era craft (the mechanical gap of the standing era long closed; she could sit herself down from the rail now, and the storm's protests were rehearsal's, not stranding's), the room's boring-night scripture kept whole. The windows audited per storm procedure — trimmed a quarter hour against the drilling's sleep tax. And the forecast held against the 2 a.m. arithmetic per the doctrine's founding rule: the storm is a renovation; renovations end; this one, the charts and the almanac's own precedents concurred, was the year's last major weather — the walking, once cheap, having nothing left to drill at scale.

Eleven nights, the storm ran — the almanac's shortest on record, the doctrine's compounding dividends visible in the very ledger.

And the page marks, at the storm's close, what the fourth edition proved about the household rather than the sleeper: the parents had run it entire without one 2 a.m. catastrophizing session, one doctrine dispute, one forecast of all future sleep's ruin — the craft's deepest measure being not the settled baby but the settled keepers, the storms received now as the book's own weather: named, known, waited out, filed.

The last drill's footage, saved to both memories: the practiser mid-mattress at 2:14, six strides, a sit, a considering pause — and sleep, mid-thought, where she sat, folded over her own feet like a closed book.

Papa stood the doorway watch a minute past the need, per the oldest custom in the book.

Rise and fall. Rise and fall. The tide, walking now, still the tide.

The storm passed. The nights, renovated one last time, held.
