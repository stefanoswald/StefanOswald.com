The Nine-Month Conference

Month 9 — The Edge of Everything

The nine-month conference convened at the pediatrician's per the calendar's spring session, and the patient — this being the visit's own headline — attended on foot: the exam room toured upright at the two-gait economy's prestige rates, the scale mounted standing (briefly; the weighing renegotiated per tradition), the stethoscope confiscated per precedent by a pincer now certified at clinical grade.

The conference's ledger, entered per custom. The curves: climbing their own hills at their own held speeds — the weight's velocity easing per the first year's natural late-season arithmetic (the food strike's old lesson co-signed at clinical authority: the slowing is the schedule, not the strike), the length up its stately quarters, the head's marvelous circumference on its steady road. The milestone review, conducted per the doctor's craft as conversation rather than examination: the steps (demonstrated, unsolicited, at center floor — the audience effect suspending itself for medical personnel, a corollary the almanac logged with interest), the pincer, the two names and four coins of the spoken ledger, the signs' small treasury, the pointing's full triangle — the doctor's own beam completed at the tour's every station per a professional fluent in the age's whole grammar. No shots on the spring docket — the visit's mercy, the next round holding for the twelve-month conference — and the questions run from the almanac's kept list per nine months of the office's own training: the molars' forecast (the parade's next floats, due in the year's second act), the milk arithmetic at the coming transition (the whole-milk changeover staged for the birthday per the standing counsel, the bottle's own retirement planning opened), the summer's brook-and-sunscreen protocols ratified.

And the conference's closing exchange, entered in the almanac at the visit's true rank. The doctor, at the door, watching the patient tour the hallway's new country at full prestige gait: "You know what I get to watch, in this job? The whole town, learning to walk. Same hallway. Thirty years." And, at the family's own register, the professional benediction the nine-month conference exists to issue: "She's exactly herself, and exactly on her own schedule, and that's the whole report. See you at one year."

At one year. The phrase rode home in the car's own quiet, doing its arithmetic on both front seats.

At one year: the candle's conference. The birthday's own docket. The book's last pages, visible now from the appointment calendar itself.

"Duck," offered the patient from the back seat, at a passing crow, the borders still trading wide.

"Crow," corrected the market, both voices, in chorus, laughing.

Exactly herself. Exactly on schedule. The whole report, co-signed at every window.

Filed, at one year minus three months. The road home ran green the whole way.
