Night 108

Three Naps, More or Less

Month 4 — Rolling and Laughing

Out of the fourth month's renovations, a shape emerged, and the shape had three rooms.

The morning nap, first and most faithful: an hour or so after waking, deep and dependable, the one you could schedule plumbers around. The midday nap, the long anchor when it held. And the late-afternoon catnap — brief, moody, essential — the little bridge that carried her to bedtime without the dusk collapse.

Three naps, more or less, is the fourth-and-fifth-month standard issue, arriving as the renovation dust settles. The chalkboard in the nursery held the rhythm in pencil-loose chalk: times as neighborhoods, not addresses. Wake windows still governed — stretching toward two hours and a bit — and the signs still outranked the clock on any disputed call.

The parental arts at this stage were two. Protecting the anchor: the midday nap got the full treatment, dark room, white sea, wind-down verse, because the anchor holds the day. And forgiving the catnap: thirty minutes in the stroller, twenty on a shoulder — bridge naps count however crossed, and chasing their perfection is chasing fog.

Days with a shape, nights with architecture: the sleep estate, four months in, was almost governable.

"Almost," said Mama to the chalkboard, and wisely wrote nothing in ink anywhere.

The first room filled at nine sharp. The house exhaled, and planned its plumbing.
