Night 228

The Big Tub Graduation

Month 8 — The Velcro Weeks and the Thaw

The sloped baby tub, faithful vessel since the sponge era's end, had been shipping water over its rails for weeks — its passenger sitting solid now, splashing at industrial scale, the little harbor outgrown from the inside.

Graduation was conducted on a Tuesday evening: the real tub, the big white ocean itself, three inches of elbow-tested water, and the graduate installed at its center — dwarfed, delighted, and instantly at work on the new physics, the splash's range and report both upgraded by the porcelain acoustics to concert dimensions.

The transfer's protocols, entered for every graduating household. The non-slip mat, laid before the first voyage — porcelain and wet sitting-balance being a partnership requiring insurance. The three inches held as the standard depth for the sitting era, the old rule restated at the new address: water to the hips, no higher, the tub's grandeur being no license. The faucet's discipline: cold finished last so the spout runs cool, the hot tap's handle beyond her reach or guarded, the spout itself padded per the audit's bathroom annex against the standing era's inevitable rendezvous. And the constitution's oldest bathing law, unamended since the sponge days and unamendable ever: the hand's reach, the unbroken presence, no exception the width of a ringing phone — the big tub changing the scenery and not one syllable of the scripture.

The faculty transferred with the graduate: the duck, Esquire, bobbing now on open water, his jurisdiction vastly expanded and his expression unchanged; the pouring cup, its seminars resumed at the new campus with deeper reserves; and a graduation gift, the era's classic — the stacking cups, perforated at their bases, converting the bath to a rain-engineering laboratory the curriculum would run for years.

The washing, per tradition, occupied its ninety seconds inside a session the customer stretched to twenty minutes by filibuster and charm.

The little tub went to the basement with the swaddles and the bassinet — the keepsake shelf of outgrown harbors, growing crowded now, each vessel outlived by its sailor per the book's oldest arithmetic.

The big tub's first dispatch, filed in the almanac in the graduate's own style: splash range, one to four feet. Duck, promoted. Ocean, mine.

The steam settled. The hooded towel received its small pink prophet, as ever.

New waters. Same hand, within reach. That part graduates never.
