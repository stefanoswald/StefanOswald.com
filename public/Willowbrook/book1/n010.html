Night 10

The Five S's

Month 1: Arrival

Grandpa Joe had farmed forty years of springs, so he knew a thing or two about new creatures. "Babies aren't ready for the big quiet world," he said on the phone. "Give 'em the noisy snug one they came from."

Mama already had the list, five S's long. Swaddle, snug around the arms. Side or stomach hold, in arms only, for soothing; the crib is always for backs. Shush, louder than you think, like a faucet running, the womb was never quiet. Swing, small quick sways, her head supported, never shaken, never ever. And suck, a clean finger or a pacifier once feeding is going well.

One S alone did a little. Stacked together, they worked like a spell: swaddled, held, shushed, swayed, and somewhere mid-sway, Olivia's eyelids surrendered.

"Old tricks," Grandpa Joe said. "New baby."

Both were right. Back she went into her crib, on her back, fast asleep.
