Night 20

Advice From Everywhere

Month 1: Arrival

Everyone had a book, and every book disagreed.

Feed on a schedule, said one. Feed on demand, said another. Never rock her to sleep, warned a third, while a fourth promised that rocking was the whole point of arms.

The neighbor swore by cabbage leaves. The internet, consulted at two a.m., swore the sky was falling, as the internet does. Even strangers in the checkout line arrived with instructions, delivered over the eggs.

Mama read, nodded, thanked, and slowly discovered the secret underneath all the books: they were written about babies in general, and she was raising one baby in particular.

So the rule of the house became this. Safety advice, sleep position, car seats, doctor's orders, followed to the letter. Everything else, run through the family test: does it work for Olivia, and does it keep us mostly sane? Keep what fits. Wave kindly at the rest.

"You are the expert on her now," the doctor had said. It was becoming true, feeding by feeding.

Olivia yawned at the whole library. Then she slept, unscheduled, magnificent.
