Night 236

The Spring Festival

Month 8 — The Velcro Weeks and the Thaw

Willowbrook keeps one festival at the spring's true opening — the square in bunting, the brass band on the library steps, the market stalls doubled and the whole town's winter of indoors turned out into the sun at once — and the household attended its citizen's first, per the calendar's oldest civic liturgy.

The strategy, drafted on the veteran doctrines. The timing: arrival at the opening hour, the crowd's gentlest edition, the exit planned per the fuse rules before the afternoon's full roar — festivals being the market outing's final exam, richest sensory fare on the town's whole menu, dosed per the sixth-month counsel in sensible portions. The rig: the carrier for the crowd's thick passages (close enough to kiss, the old spec, now worn on the back per the age's upgrade), the shoulders for the vistas — and the shoulders, the page records, were the day's great discovery: the festival received from Papa's altitude, above the crowd's weather entire, the band and the bunting and the balloon-seller's bouquet all pointed at from the reviewing stand of him, the lookback conducted by leaning over his forehead upside down, an innovation both parties endorsed.

The exhibits, per the correspondent's own filing. The band: the dancing era's first live orchestra, the knees bouncing on the shoulders at the big drums, the trombone tracked slide by slide with the tractor's own devotion. The balloon-seller: a red one petitioned by point and full committee, granted, tied to the wrist per the stall's own wisdom, and regarded thereafter with an expression the parents could not quite place until Mama named it on the walk home — proprietary, she said; she looked like it was a friend of hers. (It was, the town's discreet records would note, a reasonable association.) The bakery stall: Miss Rosa's, in festival glory, one festival bun's corner gummed per tradition, the colleague encountered mid-square in her own carrier and the two of them holding, above the crowd, a summit of pointed balloons and parallel wonder.

And the roar itself, the day's real syllabus: the town, entire, at once — every fence-line and bench and library Thursday of her eight months assembled into one loud gold civic fact, received at altitude, banked whole.

The fuse announced at ninety minutes per forecast; the exit executed per doctrine, waving, the band's last march trailing them down Maple Lane.

The balloon rode home on the wrist, was pointed at through the entire bath, and spent the night tied to the crib-distant dresser at the bear's own altitude — the two of them, plush and buoyant, holding the watch together.

By morning it had settled to the floor, per the way of balloons.

It was pointed at anyway. Filed. Kept three more days. Some friends, the almanac noted, you keep till they're all the way done.

The town said the same, always had, about everybody in it.
