Night 30

One Month Old Tonight

Month 1: Arrival

One month. Thirty days, or was it three hundred? The first month is the longest short time there is.

They celebrated the way you celebrate at this altitude: a candle in a muffin after dinner, blown out by Papa on Olivia's behalf, since her only party trick was hiccups.

Look what had happened, though. Look. A baby who could find a face now and hold it with her eyes. Who quieted, sometimes, at the sound of her own name in Mama's voice. Who had gained a pound of pure future.

And look harder: two people who a month ago could not fold a swaddle now read cries like weather maps, ran night shifts like a small railroad, and knew, not hoped, knew, they could be wrecked by four a.m. and still be kind at five.

Nobody tells you the first month grows the parents more than the baby. It is supposed to be that way.

"Happy one month, little one," Mama whispered at the window.

Outside, the dusk went gold, then blue, and the first star took its post.
