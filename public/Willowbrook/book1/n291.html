The Tractor's Passenger

Month 10 — High Summer

The Sunday liturgy's tractor station — the ritual pat administered since the first summer's carrying tours, the warm red flank saluted weekly like a large parked animal — was upgraded at the month's end by the farm's own senior authority, per a ceremony the page suspects had been scheduled in his private almanac since the christening: the boarding.

The throne's protocols, as the diplomat conducted them. The engine: off — the page stipulates the ceremony's whole constitution, the tractor's boarding running per the farm's iron statutes at parked-and-silent grade only, the working machine's radius being the fence lines' most absolute border (the farm safety canon, the page notes at its gravest margin, earning its every clause annually per the rural registries' own accounting; the diplomat's forty years enforcing it in the ankles). The ascent: in the arms, per the passenger's rank — hoisted to the seat's cracked kingdom, received at the wheel's great circle, the grip achieved at both hands per the rail's whole certified curriculum. The audience: conducted at length — the wheel turned at its parked liberty (the steering's theater received per the imitation bureaus at full enrollment: the arm-over-arm copied from forty years' observed craft, rendered at the era's fidelity), the levers regarded and embargoed per the one-finger treaty's gentle extension, the seat bounced per the lap spring's eternal offices. And the horn — the ceremony's crowning sanction: one toot, administered at the small palm's own press under the great scarred guidance, received by the barnyard at the whole species' protest (Henrietta's constituency achieving lift; the Duchess's situation, the record notes, further complicated) and by the passenger at the year's own documented summit of civic power: the face running triumph's full parliament, the lookback firing at the diplomat per the witness protocols — see what we did — the coin spent at the horn's own echo:

"Guck!" (Truck, per the household phonology; the farm's taxonomies, the page notes, trading wide per the era's custom; the diplomat declining the correction at his own market's rates: "Close enough. She's got the spirit of the thing.")

The page files the throne's true offices per the farm's whole standing syllabus. The machine's introduction at ceremony's pace — the tractor met parked, sanctioned, and grandfathered years before the working radius's curriculum would need its teeth — the farm children's oldest safety pedagogy running per the diplomat's own craft: the fascination fed at the fence the fear never has to guard. And the ceremony's deeper freight, entered at the golden hour's own light: the seat's cracked kingdom had held, per the farm's succession, the diplomat's own father, the diplomat at four, the passenger's father at four — and now, at ten months and in the arms, the fourth generation, the wheel's great circle turning under the smallest certified hands in the line's whole registry.

"Well now," said the diplomat, at the register the almanac reserved its deepest files for.

Sundays, said the whole ceremony, per the farm's one eternal clause.

The pat, administered at the dismount per the original liturgy: the flank still warm, the animal still large, still red, still — per the passenger's whole revised taxonomy — "Guck."

Close enough, ruled the farm, entire. The spirit of the thing, four generations certified, rode home asleep.
