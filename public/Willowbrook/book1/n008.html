Night 8

Enough Visitors for Today

Month 1 — Arrival

Everyone in Willowbrook wanted to meet Olivia. The doorbell had opinions all day long.

Visitors brought casseroles and kindness, and also noise, and germs on unwashed hands, and one aunt who woke the baby on purpose "just to see her eyes."

By evening, Papa became a gate. A polite gate, but a gate. "Thank you for coming," he said warmly. "We're going to rest now."

Here is a rule new families are allowed to carve over their door: visitors who help — who wash their hands, hold the baby so you can shower, leave before you wilt — are medicine. Visits that leave you more tired than they found you can wait a month. Or three.

"No" is a complete sentence, though "not today, thank you" wears nicer shoes.

The house went quiet. Mama exhaled all the way out for the first time since Tuesday.

Olivia slept through the whole diplomatic triumph, which was exactly the point.
