Night 300

Three Hundred Nights

Month 10: High Summer

The third stone. Three hundred nights.

The first, at one hundred, marked a habit become a place. The second, at two hundred, marked the place acquiring citizens. This one, the page rules at the stone's own setting, marks the citizens gone fluent, the ritual no longer performed nor even co-operated but conversed, its whole architecture running at three hundred nights' compounded ease in a dialect two-thirds of a year deep.

The evidence, gathered at the week of the stone per the almanac's custom. The liturgy's stations, self-administering: the bath answered at its word per the receptive ledger's oldest entries (the commute bathroom-ward now running ahead of the announcement, the clock, the page notes, hers too now); the sleep sack's arms offered before the zip's request; the book basket consulted at the reader's own franchise, the night's selection hauled lap-ward per a bibliography the shuffle treaty had long ceded to its junior partner. The book itself, traded: the bear book's three hundredth-odd running now at the era's full two-voiced edition, the pauses hers to fill ("Where's the button? There it is, " "Buh!"), the gasp pre-delivered per the prediction's whole joyful economy, the final spread's goodnight roll-call conducted at the recital's own rates (the moon jabbed: "Moo..." corrected mid-coin per the taxonomies' trading: "Nigh-nigh"), the last page turned by the junior partner at a timing the senior one had stopped assisting weeks back. The song, harmonized: the wrong notes' heirloom edition carrying now its faithful under-hum, the settling verse a duet at the dialect's deepest register. And the closing exchange, minted at the stone's own week and chalked at the ledger's tenderest line: the lamp out, the rail's last look, the state's three-hundredth "Goodnight, Olivia"...

"Nigh-nigh."

Aimed. Returned. The liturgy, speaking both directions at last.

The page sets the stone per the road's own survey. Three hundred nights had built what the first hundred promised and the second banked: not a routine but a country, the lamp's small nation, its borders four steps wide, its citizenship now conversant, its constitution (the sequence, the sameness, the shape) holding through every documented weather of the year's whole file. The stones ahead, the page notes at the milestone's arithmetic: the three-sixty-fifth, sixty-five nights out, arriving with the candle's own season, and past it, per the series' whole standing forecast, the next book's stones entire: the country's borders holding, its dialect compounding, its citizens returning nightly per the oldest law in the whole shelf's canon, book equals lap, book equals voice, book equals loved.

Stella, at the window per her round-number policy, flared the stone's own registry.

Three hundred down. The candle's season, sixty-five nights wide.

"Nigh-nigh," said the country's newest fluent citizen, to the lamp, to the bear, to the whole small nation, per the dialect.

Nigh-nigh, held the nation, entire.

The stone set. The road ran on, gold, toward its first year's own last marker, visible now, the page certifies, from here.
