The Year in One Walk

Month 12 — The Candle's Country

On the year's last Sunday — five nights out, the weather watch's "clear before" holding per the knee's calibration — the household took the walk the almanac had been quietly drafting all month: the year in one walk — the whole map's own circuit, at the slowest documented pace of the entire book.

The circuit, logged station by station at both voices' term. The lane's own length, first: the porches' waves returned at the walker's certified altitude, the gold dog's fence-line salute conducted at the whole year's compiled diplomacy ("Fuff." — the metronome's answer, per tradition), the Hendersons' sprinkler corner at its winter sleep, named at the summer's own file ("wawa," at the dry arc's remembered address — the memory, the page certifies, running the season entire). Dandelion Park at the map's green heart: the bench's parliament saluted at its empty winter term ("Rosa's bench; the password lives here"), the swings' bucket pushed once at the cold chains' own ceremony, the oak's blanket country walked at the acorn-crunching archaeology, the pond's station held at the founding coin's own address — the ducks, per the season's paperwork, wintering elsewhere, and named anyway, at the water's empty gold, by the whole family, in chorus, per the liturgy the year had built. The Lantern Library at the Thursday map's own light: the lantern saluted, the vault's step conducted at one "hi!" per the acoustics' standing blessing. And the brook's bridge at the circuit's turning: the water's winter voice attended at the rail's held term, the stepping stones consulted at the point's deferred franchise ("next summer," honored the escort, per the statutes; "wading rights come with the next book"), one pebble tossed at the paternal license's eternal seminar — the splash received, per the whole enrolled tradition, as the finest physics on the town's entire syllabus.

And the circuit's close, per the page's own deepest station: the hill's path regarded at the willow's distant gold — the climb, per the light's arithmetic, banked for the next book's own Sundays — and the turn for home taken at the dusk's first lamps: the lane's lights arriving porch by porch at the year's whole compiled applause, the walk's last hundred yards run at the shoulders' term, the sleeper's weight at its whole documented gold, and the door — the same door, the page marks the circuit's entire arithmetic, that had opened for three at the book's first page — opened, at its last Sunday, for the same three: heavier by one walking talking year, lighter by every carried thing the year had taught them to set down.

"Home," spent the circuit's own conductor at the porch's gold, half-asleep, aimed.

Home, held the door, entire.

Five nights. The map: walked whole. The year: circuited, named, carried over its own threshold — and the town's gold, at every documented station: complete, and theirs, and kept.
