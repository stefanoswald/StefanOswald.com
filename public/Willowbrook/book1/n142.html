Night 142

The Babyproofing Expedition

Month 5: Nearly Sitting, Nearly Everything

Motion having arrived, the house convened its most undignified and important meeting: the crawl-through.

Papa went down on hands and knees at the living room border and traveled the house at hull height, seeing what the ship would see. Mama followed with the checklist. This page files their route notes, for every household whose rocket is counting down.

The electric: outlets capped, every one, at knee-discovery they multiply. Cords, lamp, blind, charger, vanished upward or boxed; the blind cord especially, looped killers, retired entirely. The topple hazards: bookshelf anchored to the stud (done that hour, the drill still warm), the dresser next, the television strapped, furniture tips being the quiet leader of the year's real dangers, and the anchor kits costing less than lunch.

The swallowables: the floor swept at pebble resolution, then swept again lower, coins, buttons, the pen cap under the couch since spring, all of it toilet-tube-tested and confiscated. The chemical country: cabinet locks on the under-sink nations, the medicines promoted to high cabinets with childproof caps as second walls, never first. The stairs, gated top and bottom, top gate screwed to the wall, pressure gates being for doorways, not summits. The pet bowl relocated, the plants audited by name against the poison list, the trash can converted to lidded government.

Two hours, one sore back, one house translated into baby.

The expedition's motto, taped inside the cupboard with the others: the crawl-through repeats. Every month, new altitude, new reach, new audit. Proofing is a verb for two years, not a weekend.

The rocket, napping through all of it, would find a safer planet on waking.

She would spend the next year testing every rivet anyway. That was her job. Now it could be survived. That was theirs.
