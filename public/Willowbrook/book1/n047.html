Night 47

The Library Lantern

Month 2 — Smiles and Sleep

The first real outing with a destination was three blocks long, and the destination was made of books.

The Lantern Library sat round and warm at the center of town, its rooftop light coming on just as Mama arrived — dusk being when libraries are most themselves. Inside: the good hush, the paper smell, and a librarian who did not coo loudly or reach for the baby, but smiled the measured smile of a professional welcoming a very young patron.

Olivia received her first library card that evening. It had to be signed on her behalf, her current signature being a fist.

They came home with three board books, thick-paged and chewable-cornered, which is exactly right: at this age a book is a thing to hear, stare at, and eventually gum. All three uses are reading.

The tip folded into this page is small and mighty: start the library habit absurdly early. It is free, it is warm, it gets a housebound parent out the door with a purpose, and it quietly files, in the baby's growing mind, one of the great facts of life — that there is a building in town where the stories live, and the light is always on.

Molly Mouse watched from the wainscoting as they left, and approved. New reader. Excellent.
