Night 106

Filmed Before a Live Audience

Month 4: Rolling and Laughing

The camera roll passed four thousand that month, and this page is about the two economies of remembering.

The first economy was booming. Every laugh: filmed. The rolls, the ba-ba-ba, the foot-catch, the duck research: filmed, filmed, filmed. And rightly, the page carries no scold. These months evaporate; the videos are the dew collected, and some February night years off, three seconds of this exact laugh would be worth any sum.

But the second economy ran quieter and paid better, and it traded in unfilmed minutes. Papa noticed it by accident, the evening the phone died mid-laugh: how different the same moment felt with both hands empty and nothing between his face and hers. Present-tense, uncropped, unrepeatable, and somehow more kept, not less.

Babies notice the lens age too, the researchers murmur; a parent behind a phone is a parent slightly elsewhere, face half-eclipsed, the serve-and-return running at half speed.

So the house struck its treaty. Film the moment, thirty seconds, generous, then the phone goes down and the encore plays live, unrecorded, to its intended audience of two. The best of both economies: the dew collected, the evening still owned.

Some laughs for the archive. Most laughs for the room.

Tonight's ratio ran about one to nine, which the auditors, both of them, watching her sleep later, scrolling nothing, declared exactly right.
