Night 104

An Elephant, Briefly

Month 4 — Rolling and Laughing

Sunday at Honeywell Farm ran its usual program — chickens viewed, tractor patted, Grandpa Joe's hat borrowed for one photograph — until, at the far fence, the program departed from all known formats.

Beyond the back pasture, where the willows started, something large and gentle moved in the golden light. Grey-blue. Unhurried. Ears like patient flags, and — Papa squinted — a red balloon, tied to its tail, riding the evening air.

An elephant. Behind Honeywell Farm. Nobody at the farm found this remarkable; Grandpa Joe, asked, said only, "That'd be Ellie," in the tone he used for weather and second cousins.

Olivia, from the carrier, stared with every circuit she had. The great head swung toward her across the distance. Something passed between them — the old freemasonry of the very young, who have not yet been told what is unusual — and Ellie dipped her trunk once, courtly, the balloon bobbing, before the willows folded her back in.

Babies this age cannot say what they see, which is how towns like Willowbrook keep their arrangements. The page only notes what the research and the grandmothers agree on: that new sights are food, that animals hold babies' attention like nothing else with a face, and that a Sunday with one astonishment in it feeds a week of growing.

"You saw that," Papa said, not quite a question.

"Ba," Olivia replied firmly, which the record will show means yes.

The balloon was visible over the willows for a while, going home. Somewhere a star came out early to watch it.
