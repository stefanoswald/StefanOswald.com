Night 158

The Sweet Potato Landslide

Month 6: The Feast Begins

Every feast finds its first love, and in the third week the ballots came in orange.

Sweet potato, roasted soft and mashed with a ripe pear, the kitchen's Tuesday offering, met a reception unprecedented in the young administration's history. The first spoonful silenced the table. The second was met halfway with the whole torso. By the third, negotiations collapsed entirely: both fists entered the bowl, the spoon was disarmed and dropped overboard, and the electorate communed with the landslide directly, palm to mouth to hairline, a democracy of one voting orange with every limb.

"Mmmmm," said Olivia, not babble, the parents would swear to the almanac: commentary.

The page rides the landslide to its counsel. First loves in food are anchors, not ruts, the loved food becomes the safe harbor from which the strange ones get tried, the sweet potato lending its credibility to the neighboring lentil by shared spoon. The kitchen's play: pair the beloved with the debutante, and let the vote drift. And the sweetness itself, no scandal, the milk was sweet all along; the bridge from sweet toward earthy is walked in months of small crossings, not forced in a day.

The opposition benches were noted for balance: broccoli's numbers had softened since its first week, zucchini polled poorly, and green beans had been formally worn rather than consumed. Standard mid-term results, the almanac recorded. All parties would stand again; tastes at this age are polls, not constitutions, the same food winning on its eighth ballot that lost its first seven.

The landslide was cleaned from the hairline with a warm cloth and mild awe.

The bowl was refilled once, by acclamation.

Orange, the record states, governed all that autumn. The record is faintly orange itself, at the corner. The almanac forgives it.
