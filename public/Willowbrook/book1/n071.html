Night 71

Date Night, Population Three

Month 3 — Rhythm

The reservation was for eight o'clock, the table was their own kitchen table, and the third guest was asleep down the hall with the monitor as her ambassador.

Date night at home: the third month's most sensible romance. Somewhere out in Willowbrook were restaurants, and someday they would sit in one again, ordering appetizers like reckless royalty. But tonight the sitter question had no easy answer, the evening stretch was newly reliable, and the couple in question was tired in a way restaurants cannot fix.

So they made the cheap magic instead. Real cooking, the meal with too many pans. Candles, because candles cost nothing and change everything. Phones exiled to the counter, face down, demoted. And one rule, instituted by Mama and honored imperfectly: the first twenty minutes, no baby talk. Not because Olivia wasn't the best subject — because they had been colleagues for three months and needed reminding they were also this.

It creaked at first, the old conversation. Then it caught, the way it always catches: some story from before, some plan from after, laughing with their actual laughs.

The ambassador slept through the entire summit.

Relations between the two nations were declared excellent, and the treaty was sealed with the good dessert, eaten slowly, all the way to the end.
