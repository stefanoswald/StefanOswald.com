Night 90

Stella Counts to Three

Month 3 — Rhythm

Three months is a season, and stars keep excellent seasonal records.

Stella drifted down through the summer dark to the window on Maple Lane and ran the quarter's audit, as promised in her contract, which is written in light and notarized by the moon.

Item: one laugh, first of edition, heard through the glass on a Tuesday and logged at full value. Item: locomotion by rolling, in both directions, distances increasing weekly; the shelf-clearing recommendation, she noted, had been actioned. Item: two parents, formerly cliff-walkers, now craftspeople of the night shift; their lamp burned briefer and calmer than in any prior quarter.

Item, filed under miscellaneous wonders: one bear, dresser division, keeping counsel; one bench friendship, struck in the park, with a matching baby eleven days younger two streets over — Stella checked that window too, these being package deals in her line of work.

"Quarter's closed," she reported up to the Sleepy Moon. "Findings: ordinary. Ordinary as in miracle after miracle, on schedule, witnessed by nobody but everybody."

"Mmmmm," hummed the Moon, which is Moon for that is the only kind of quarter there has ever been.

Stella twinkled once, for yes, and took her post for the season ahead — the laughing season, the sitting season, the season of the world reached for and caught.

Three months down in the town of Willowbrook. The light held. The hum went on.

Goodnight, Willowbrook.
