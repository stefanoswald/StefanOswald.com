Night 224

The Push Wagon Debut

Month 8 — The Velcro Weeks and the Thaw

The cruising fleet, coastal since the seventh month, took delivery this month of its first blue-water vessel: the push wagon — wooden, handle at her collarbone height, weighted per the veteran counsel with two bags of rice in the hold until its speed spoke toddler rather than luge.

The launch, conducted on the living room's open interior — that unclaimed center the coastal towns had eyed all winter. She pulled to stand at the handle per the crib academy's transferable credits. Gripped. Leaned — and the wagon, traitor and liberator at once, moved. One step chased it. Another. And then the pattern compiled and the room held its breath for the second time that season: step-step-step, upright, hands on the handle, the wagon rolling slow and true across the entire uncharted interior — locomotion, standing up, at last, the unthinkable's dress rehearsal in full costume.

The face at the far wall, when the voyage docked against it, ran the summit expression at a wattage the almanac declined to estimate.

The page certifies the vessel class while the crossing echoes. The push wagon — the walker-wagon, the weighted cart — is the era's one endorsed machine: the child powers it, steers it, and balances behind it, every system live, the wagon lending only the hands' anchor while the legs learn the blue-water trade. (Its notorious cousin, the seated baby-walker with the wheels and the tray, stays retired per the safety boards' long-standing verdict — the page inters it once more without honors: stairs, speed, and idle legs, the trifecta that ended its era.) The rig tuned per the fleet's manual: weighted against runaway, socks gripped or feet bare per the barefoot doctrine, rugs flattened at the shipping lanes, and the docking taught early — the turn at the wall being the skill that converts crossings into commerce.

By month's end the wagon ran scheduled service: cargo (blocks, the bear, one mitten in perpetual transit) loaded and hauled between provinces, the putting-in industries acquiring a freight division, the interior — the page marks it formally — no longer unclaimed. Charted. Crossed daily. Hers.

Four steps of open water remained between the fleet and the true unthinkable: the crossing with no handle at all.

The wagon, wise vessel, said nothing. Rice shifting gently in the hold, it rolled on, ferrying the future back and forth across the living room at one small stride per second, letting the legs believe it was the one doing the carrying.

It had never once been the one doing the carrying.

Spring, said the light at the windows, lengthening. Soon, said the wagon's wheels, saying nothing. Step-step-step, said the record, nightly.
