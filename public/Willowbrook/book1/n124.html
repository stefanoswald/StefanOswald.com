Night 124

The Bedtime Dividend

Month 5 — Nearly Sitting, Nearly Everything

Five months of the same four steps — bath, pajamas, book, song — and the fifth month paid the dividend.

It looked like this. The bathwater running, and Olivia's body slowing at the sound. The sleep sack's zip, and the yawn arriving on the zipper's cue. The book, one story longer now that her attention could carry it, her hand resting on the page like a paperweight with opinions. The song, wrong in its beloved places. And then the crib, received awake and calm — awake and calm — with a squirm, a sigh, and sleep found unassisted, most nights now, the drowsy-but-awake lottery ticket having quietly become a season pass.

Parents who kept the four steps through the unrewarding months sometimes ask when the routine starts working. The honest answer: it was working the whole time, underground, the way bulbs work. The fifth month is merely when it blooms — the sequence so deeply banked that the routine itself now does the soothing, each step handing her gently down to the next, the hallway of open doors walkable in the dark by her own small feet.

The dividend compounds from here, the page notes, through toddler bedtimes and beyond: the child who fights sleep least is most often the one whose evenings have had the same shape since before memory.

Tonight, the whole liturgy ran eleven minutes, unhurried.

The window was still faintly light when she found sleep on her own, mid-first-verse of nothing.

Five months of deposits. The interest, arriving nightly now, in silence.
