Night 174

The Page Turner

Month 6 — The Feast Begins

The nightly book acquired, this month, a co-operator, and the operation changed hands mid-story.

It began with the grab — the page, mid-turn, seized and held, the reading stalled while small fingers appraised the cardboard's edge. Then the wrestle: both hands on one thick page, the full-body heave, the page going over — backward as often as forward, three at a time by preference — and the co-operator looking up for the applause that such labor plainly commanded.

By week's end she took the book itself: hauled it from the basket, hoisted it across her knees right-side-up by luck and turning odds, and worked the pages solo in long sessions of what the observing state could only call reading — the gaze traveling the spreads, the commentary babbled at the pictures, one corner sampled by mouth (criticism, the page assumes) and the whole volume finally worn as a hat.

The page beneath the comedy files the milestone properly. Book handling is literacy's true first chapter — before letters, before words, the object itself must be learned: books open, pages turn, turning changes the picture, the pictures hold still while the world doesn't. The chewed corner and the backward reading are the syllabus, not violations of it, which is the entire case for board books' armored construction and against rescuing the volume from its student.

The nightly reading adapted its liturgy: Papa read whatever spread her turning landed on, continuity surrendered to co-operation, the bear book's plot experienced henceforth in shuffle mode and improved, arguably, thereby.

Her own shelf — the lowest one, audit-cleared — took its first three residents that week, hers to haul at will.

She hauled them nightly. Wore them occasionally. Read them, by every measure that would matter later, constantly.

The library's future, as a mouse two blocks east had already minuted, was secure.
