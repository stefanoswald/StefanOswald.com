Night 149

The Inventory of Sounds

Month 5 — Nearly Sitting, Nearly Everything

The house had learned to pause outside the nursery door at wake-up time, because the morning address had become the day's first appointment.

She woke talking now. Alone, unprompted, to the ceiling and the general assembly: the full fifth-month inventory, delivered from the crib like a senator warming up. Ba-ba-ba, the founding consonant, now in chains of six and eight. Ma-ma — sound, not summons, the page reminds the tender-hearted, though the sound alone stopped Mama's feet in the hall every time. Da-da-da, likewise, the lamp's old title now in general circulation. The new arrivals: a guttural g from somewhere in the back country, a wet raspberry perfected over two devoted days, the squeal register (discovered at the market, deployed since at capacity), and the growl — determination's own note, minted during the almost-crawl and now applied to all serious business.

The morning speech ran minutes at a stretch, self-sustaining, no audience required — which was precisely its importance. This is practice in the pure state: the mouth-ear loop running drills, sounds tried, heard, adjusted, retried, the babble feeding on its own echo. The wiring of speech, laid solo, in the crib's own laboratory, before breakfast.

The parental arts: the pause at the door (solo practice left solo a while — rushing in ends the seminar), and then, on entering, the answer. The inventory met, matched, served back with interest: her chain, then a turn, then hers. The morning's first conversation, in the true mother tongue: turns.

"Ba-da-ba. GRRR. Ma-ma-ba," concluded the senator, sighting Mama at the door.

"Strong agenda," said Mama, lifting her. "Let's take it to breakfast."

The assembly adjourned to the high chair, in full voice, the day's first bill already passed: morning, carried unanimously.
