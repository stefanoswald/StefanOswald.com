Night 14

Mama's Cup

Month 1 — Arrival

There is a rule they say on airplanes, and it is really a rule about parents: secure your own mask first.

Mama was healing. Her body had done an enormous thing, and enormous things are not tidied up in a weekend. Yet somehow her own lunch kept not happening, and her water glass lived forever in the other room.

It was Miss Rosa who set it straight, arriving with soup and a level look. "You feed her," she said, nodding at Olivia, "so who feeds you?"

So the house got new rules, small as vitamins. A full glass of water at every feeding — the baby eats, you drink; the pair of you, a tiny assembly line. Real food before noon. A nap outranking any chore. And a walk to the mailbox counting, in the first weeks, as a marathon, because it is one.

An empty cup pours nothing. Mama drank the whole glass, looking out the window.

Olivia, against her chest, rose and fell like a small boat on a kind sea.
