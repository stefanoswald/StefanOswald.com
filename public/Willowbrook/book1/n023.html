Night 23

The First Walk Outside

Month 1 — Arrival

On the ninth day, the front door opened outward for a change, and the family went to meet the weather.

Not far. To the corner and back, the stroller wheels ticking over the sidewalk seams like a slow clock. But oh, it counted. Fresh air on new cheeks. Real daylight, which tunes a baby's small clock better than any lamp — and lifts a cooped-up parent like bread rising.

The dressing had taken longer than the walk, as it always does at first. The rule of thumb rode along: one more layer than you would wear yourself, a hat for the wind, and a hand slipped down the collar now and then — a warm chest means all is well, never mind cool little hands.

Neighbors waved from porches without crowding in. Willowbrook knew how to greet a new citizen: gently, and from a respectful distance.

Olivia slept through her entire debut.

The town noted her arrival anyway, and the wind was careful going past.
