Night 85

The Off Day

Month 3 — Rhythm

Some days come with no headline. This was one, and it belongs in the book precisely because it was one.

Olivia woke fussy and stayed fussy. Not sick — no fever, feeding fine, the list consulted twice. Not teething, probably. Not any solvable thing. Just off: clingy, whimpery, refusing the mat, the bouncer, the world, wanting only up, only arms, only the window and the slow bounce, on repeat, for hours.

The plans died quietly, one by one. Walk: canceled. Errands: canceled. The parent Mama had intended to be that day — productive, showered by ten — canceled too.

Here is what this page knows. Babies get off days exactly as their owners do: growth gathering invisibly, a dream gone wrong at the last nap, some small weather in the nervous system with no name and no fix but company. The task on such days is not to solve, because there is nothing to solve. It is to lower the bar until it rests on the floor, and keep the baby company where she is.

So: the window. The bounce. An old song hummed forty times. Toast for lunch, eaten standing, in shifts of one hand.

By evening bath, whatever it was had passed through, unexplained. Olivia beamed from the water like a returning holiday.

No lesson. No milestone. One day, weathered together — which, on the true syllabus, is the oldest subject there is.
