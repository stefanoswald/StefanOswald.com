Night 117

Sleeping Through the Heat

Month 4: Rolling and Laughing

The heat wave leaned on Willowbrook for a week, and the nursery required diplomacy.

Babies sleep best in the same weather adults do, the cool side of comfortable, the famous range around which all the books circle being roughly the high sixties to seventy, but summer holds no hearings. So the house worked the old levers instead of worrying the thermometer.

Curtains drawn through the fierce hours, the nursery banking its morning coolness like a root cellar. The fan in the corner turning the air without aiming at the crib, moving air, not blasting it, the breeze for the room and not the baby. Bedtime wardrobe stripped to summer kit: a diaper, the thinnest cotton sack or none at all when the night refused to drop, because a baby's night outfit should always lose a layer before the parents' own would.

And the parent's hand as the only thermometer that mattered: the chest and the back of the neck, checked at the late look-in. Warm and dry, carry on; sweat-damp at the neck, subtract a layer, no debate. Cool hands and feet, as ever, testifying to nothing.

Extra milk stood ready for the thirst the heat runs up. The white-noise sea shushed on, indifferent to weather.

Olivia slept starfished to her maximum span, occupying the crib's entire cool geography, a connoisseur of the fitted sheet's fresher provinces.

Outside, the heat pressed on the town like a hand. Inside, the small climate held: sixty-nine degrees of policy, and a fan taking slow attendance in the corner.
