Night 151

The Oak Tells the Sky

Month 5: Nearly Sitting, Nearly Everything

Trees file their reports upward, on the night wind, and the oak of Dandelion Park had a season's worth to send.

Stella caught the message at her post, one leaf, spiraling up on the warm air, which is how oaks write, and read it to the Sleepy Moon, who hummed to show attendance.

That the blanket babies had become sitting babies, the oak reported, all across the park, the summer's crop coming upright on schedule like a field finding vertical. That the small one from Maple Lane, the reaching one, the oak's particular study since her first dandelion seed, now sat beneath the branches like a person at a desk, working leaves through her hands, conducting the shade. That her ship had launched backward, per the oldest specifications, and her voice had gone to chains and growls, and her parents had audited their joy at golden hour on the oak's own blanket ground, which the oak counted, in its ledgers, as rent fully paid.

"Five months," said Stella. "The busy season."

"Mmmmm," hummed the Moon, which is Moon for they are all busy seasons, but this is the one where you can watch the person arriving, like a boat coming in from far off, a little clearer every week.

The oak sent one last leaf up: a postscript. The fireflies concur. The brook also. See attached burbling.

Stella filed the season complete and settled to her watch, low over Maple Lane, where the nursery window held its small correct darkness and the bear kept the dresser and the person, arrived a little clearer, slept in her republic with both busy hands at rest.

Stella twinkled once, for yes.

Five months down in the town of Willowbrook. The summer held. The watch held. All of it held.

Goodnight, Willowbrook.
