Night 26

Hiccups and Other Small Weather

Month 1 — Arrival

Hic. The whole of Olivia jumped, once, like a sneeze had knocked at the wrong door.

Hic. Again. Mama used to worry; now she just watched the show. Newborns are full of small weather. Hiccups after meals — she had practiced those before she was even born, tiny rhythmic taps from the inside. Sneezes that clear dust, not colds. Startles where both arms fling wide, as if applauding a dream. Trembly chins, milk-drunk smiles, breathing that hurries and rests and hurries again while she sleeps.

All of it ordinary. All of it, the first time you see it, alarming at two a.m. — which is why the doctor's phone number lived on the refrigerator, and why no question was ever too small for the visit list.

The hiccups needed nothing: no startling, no tricks, just time or a little feed. They left the way weather leaves.

Hic... and then no hic. The front passed. Clear skies.

Olivia looked mildly betrayed, then deeply asleep, in that order.
