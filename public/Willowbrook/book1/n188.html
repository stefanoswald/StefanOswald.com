Night 188

The Babble Turns to Point

Month 7 — Explorers and Guardrails

The gesture had been assembling for weeks — the whole-arm aim at the stairs, at the potatoes, at the ring gone overboard — and this month, at the park, it completed itself with the piece that changes everything.

A dog crossed the far lawn. The arm went up and out, the point at full extension — and then the head turned back. Eyes found Mama's eyes. Checked them. Followed her gaze out to the dog and back. The face said, as plainly as any sentence the house would ever hear: are you seeing this? We are seeing this.

The lookback. Joint attention, the developmental literature's quiet crown jewel, and the page pauses the stroller to say why. The point-plus-lookback is the first triangle: me, you, and the thing — two minds aimed at one object on purpose, each tracking the other's aim. It is the platform every word will land on (the naming game runs entirely on it: she points, the parent names, the label soldering to the object inside the shared beam), the infrastructure of every future show-and-tell, bedtime story, and inside joke. Language is social before it is verbal, and this was the social half, shipping.

The parental art, gloriously simple: complete the triangle, every time the arm invites it. Look where she points. Name what you find there — "Dog! A brown dog, running" — and point back at the world in turn, building the game's two-way traffic. The pointing months are the naming months; the beam is open now, and everything named inside it sticks at double rates.

The dog was named. Then the ducks, the kite, the leaf, the man with the hat, the hat itself, the moon — the moon! — arriving pale and early over the kite hill, pointed at with the full arm and filed, lookback confirmed, as shared property.

"Moon," said Mama, inside the beam.

The arm held. The triangle held. Somewhere in the small vaulted files, the word went in beside the sight of it.

First bill of a long, long ledger — the whole world, itemized together, starting with dog and moon.
