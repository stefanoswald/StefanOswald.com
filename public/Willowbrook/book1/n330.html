Night 330

Oliver Owl Asks the Almost-Question

Month 11 — Lantern Season

The winter watch convened at the willow's oldest branch per the season's own rotation — Stella down from the long post, the Sleepy Moon at its cold clear angle, the town's lights holding their early-dark parliament below — and Oliver Owl, per his office at the weighings, had been waiting with the question since the family's own Sunday climb.

"They came up the hill," he opened, per the order's one method. "Did you see? They carried the whole year up and set it down at the roots. The small one left a word in the rings. The tree is still holding it — you can see the gold hasn't settled."

"I saw," said Stella. "I see everything on that lane. It's been my whole post since June-before-last."

"Then here is the almost-question," said the owl, the spectacles catching the moon's hum at the angle his order reserves. "Thirty-five nights from now, they light the candle. And the parents below think — they all think, every June-before-last's family in every town I've kept — that the candle closes something. The first year, finished. The book, done. So: does it?"

The branch held the question per the liturgy's term. The moon hummed the considering hum. Below, the crickets' successors — the winter's own quiet — kept the ledger at the season's rates, and one window on Maple Lane held its small correct darkness at thirty-five nights' term.

"No," said Stella, at the watch's own certainty. "It opens. I've kept enough first years to know the arithmetic. The candle's not a door closing — it's the first light of the next room. She'll wake up one-and-a-day and want breakfast and the bear will be in the crib at last and the whole second book will just — begin. They never end, these books. They only ever hand off."

"Mmmmm," hummed the Sleepy Moon — which is Moon, the whole branch could translate, for correct, and for the deeper file: and the parents will grieve the closing anyway, a little, at the candle's own light, and the grieving will be right too — every arrival, per the town's oldest arithmetic, being also a departure, and the wave owed at both.

"Then that's the blessing," said Oliver Owl, settling per his office. "Not the question's answer — the both of it. Light the candle; grieve the year; open the room. Tell the watch to hold all three."

"The watch," said Stella, rising to the post, "has held all three since the door on that lane first opened for three of them. It's the whole job, if we're being exact."

She took the winter station low over Maple Lane, per the whole year's custom.

The willow let its gold down over the branch's closed session, the word in its rings still warm.

Thirty-five nights down to the candle in the town of Willowbrook. The almost-question: asked, held, both.

Goodnight, Willowbrook — the next room's first light, already at the shelf.
