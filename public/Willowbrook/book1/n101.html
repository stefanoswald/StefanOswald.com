Night 101

The Kick Factory

Month 4: Rolling and Laughing

The lower half of Olivia had opened a factory, and the factory made kicks.

Morning shift: on the mat beneath the play gym, both legs pistoning, the dangling felt star and wooden ring swinging like storm rigging under her strikes. She had learned the physics weeks ago, kick, and the world above jingles, and the factory had scaled production ever since. Kick. Jingle. Kick kick. JINGLE. The quarterly reports were excellent.

Bath shift: the evening's two inches of water converted to weather, spray on the walls, a mother's shirt soaked on schedule, the splash zone expanding weekly like a small wet empire.

This is the leg era arriving, and every kick is on the payroll of the future: the same alternating piston pattern rehearsing now on the mat is the exact motor score that crawling will perform later, and walking after that. The strengthening runs down the whole chain, hips, thighs, the small heroic core, and the best factory conditions cost nothing: floor time, bare legs, room to fire, things worth kicking suspended in range.

The bouncer and its cousins, the page notes gently, idle the factory, containers hold the legs that floors employ. Short stints, then back to the shop floor.

Final shift of the day: the crib, lights low, two last slow kicks under the sleep sack like machines powering down.

Production would resume at six sharp. It always did.
