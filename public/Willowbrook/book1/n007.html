Night 7

Sleep When She Sleeps (Everyone Says So)

Month 1: Arrival

"Sleep when the baby sleeps," said the neighbor, the doctor, the aunt on the phone, and a stranger at the mailbox.

Mama wanted to laugh. When Olivia slept, that was the only time to shower, or eat with two hands, or simply stare at a wall being nobody's anything.

But that afternoon, the couch made its case. Olivia was down; the sun was warm; the dishes glared from the sink.

"Maybe," Mama told the dishes, and lay down anyway.

Here is the truth inside the tired old advice: the chores are not wrong, but they are liars. They say they must be done now. They never must. A twenty-minute nap does more for a worn-out parent than a clean counter ever did for anyone.

Mama slept. The dishes sulked and survived.

When Olivia stirred, Mama rose half-new, which in the first month is as good as brand-new gets, and plenty.
