Night 249

The Maps Redraw Again

Month 9 — The Edge of Everything

The walking aged the winter's audit a season in a fortnight, and the household ran the constitution's next scheduled amendment: the standing-reach survey — the maps redrawn, as the page forecast at the first crawl, for a citizen now operating at a whole new altitude.

The survey's method, unchanged since the first expedition and re-commissioned now: the parental crawl-through, conducted this time at a crouch — the auditor's eyes at her standing eye-line, the hands sweeping her standing reach, the house re-read room by room at thirty-one inches of ambition plus arm.

The findings, filed for every household mid-amendment. The table's edge country, annexed overnight: the runner's hang (a yank from cascading the fruit bowl — the runner retired per the tablecloth's old precedent), the placemats' overhang trimmed, the counters' first three inches now hers and audited hourly by the resident: the mug's new inland parking law, the knife block's relocation to the backcountry, the standing rule — hot behind, sharp behind, always — drilled into both adult reflexes at the stove's own margins. The drawers and doors at the new latitude: the second wave of latches (the shallow drawers the crawl-era audit had graded safe by unreachability, reachable now), the oven door's handle reclassified as rigging, the dishwasher's mid-cycle lock ordinance re-ratified. The bathroom's standing annex: the toilet lid's latch (the wet hazard boards' old counsel, due now), the counter's cosmetics migrated north. And the anchor doctrine's final review: every surface she could now reach was also a surface she would now board — the audit walked with the climbing clause's mathematics running throughout, launchpads policed at the new elevation, the fleet review's floor-clutter ordinance enforced as elevation control per the winter's amendment.

And the survey's standing counsel, restated at its third edition because the raising years never retire it: the audit is a verb, per the first expedition's motto — the map redrawn at every mobility upgrade, the house never finished, only current. The crawl-through of month seven, the climbing clause of month eight, the standing-reach of month nine — and ahead, the page notes without alarm, the chair-dragging era of the toddler country, when the citizen learns that furniture moves and every map acquires a ladder.

(The almanac scheduled that survey in advance, on the veterans' counsel, for the first witnessed drag.)

The amendment closed at dusk, the house current again: the fruit bowl inland, the runner retired, the mugs parked deep, the whole geography holding one move ahead of its surveyor —

— which is, the page's oldest law runs, the only place a family's maps can ever hope to live: one move ahead, redrawn without resentment, current until Tuesday.

The surveyor slept. The auditors compared maps over the cold half of the tea, and kept both drafts.

The house, one shelf higher everywhere, held.
