Night 270

Bella Comes Back

Month 9: The Edge of Everything

The garden bureau's morning rounds, the seedling's row inspected per the summer's standing docket, were interrupted, on the month's last day, by the season's own signature: wings.

She came over the fence line at the morning's gold hour, riding the warm air's first true thermals, rainbow-sheened, unhurried, trailing the pollen-light the town's records have always declined to explain, and made her landing, per a scheduling the almanacs of Willowbrook have never once caught in error, on the stake of the bureau's own tomato row: Bella. Back, as the spring's whole file had promised, changed and beautiful, on schedule.

The audience, per the page's records the encounter deserves: total stillness. The walker, mid-round, one hand on the watering can's co-carry, stopped entire, the year's whole wardrobe of response suspended: no point fired, no coin spent, no lookback even, the wings at arm's length holding her in the one register the almanac had never yet documented at full term: wonder, unbrokered. Thirty seconds, the senior partner's watch ran it, unmoving, the wings opening and closing at their own slow liturgy, the pollen-light doing its work in the morning gold, the smallest citizen and the town's own symbol of growing regarding one another across two feet of June air.

Then the wings took the thermal, cleared the fence line, and were gone toward the park's country, and the point fired at last, at the empty gold air, and the lookback, and the coin, spent in the softest documented edition of its whole career:

"Duh."

"Butterfly," supplied the market, at matching register. "Bella," it added, on the town's own instinct, the way names arrive there. "She comes back every spring. She always comes back."

The page holds the visit lightly, per its custom with the town's arrangements, and files only what belongs to the book. That the butterfly's return is the season's plainest lesson in the year's deepest subject, the going and the coming back, the door problem's whole curriculum written in wings: what leaves, returns, changed and the same, on its own almanac, every spring of the raising years ahead. That the wonder-register, arriving unbrokered at nine months, is the faculty the whole growing enterprise runs on, and its first documented term deserved its own page. And that somewhere in the town's discreet records, wainscoting, willow bark, starlight, the visit was logged from the other side as well: the small one from Maple Lane, met at last. Walking now. Gentle-certified. Watches whole.

The tomato row got its watering, eventually, at the round's restored docket.

The stake, the almanac notes, was inspected daily thereafter with a new second purpose, the fence line watched at the morning gold hour per a hope the household entirely shared.

She would be back. She always came back. The whole town, the book has it in writing, in every file the year had opened, was built on exactly that.
