Night 226

Mud Season

Month 8 — The Velcro Weeks and the Thaw

The thaw's rumor, filed by the icicle in the seventh month, became in the eighth the thaw itself — and the thaw, as northern towns know and calendars decline to admit, is not spring. It is mud season, spring's unglamorous customs hall, and the household walked it daily with the devotion the page has urged since the weather-walk doctrine of month two.

The tour's exhibits, taken at stroller pace on the chased-light route, extended weekly now with its lengthening asset. The snow line, retreating up the lawns in dirty scallops, pointed at daily as it went — the yard's geography redrawn each morning, the winter's white nation shrinking to embassies under the north-facing eaves. The puddles: the season's capital works, the sidewalk's every low spot a new great lake, regarded from the stroller with the leaning, pointing, lookback-firing fascination of a hydrologist at a new dam. (The wading rights, the page notes, belonged to a future season and better boots; this year's franchise was observational, plus one supervised glove-dip that ended in the customs office as all things end.) The brook, swollen with the melt to twice its voice, thundering its small thunder under the bridge — the white-noise sea's wild cousin, listened to from Papa's arms with eyes gone round at the volume the world could produce when it tried.

And the mud itself, the season's namesake and its editorial: the world, between snow and green, being simply brown, wet, loud, and utterly interesting — the page's whole old thesis about weather and babies restated by the landscape at full saturation.

The era's kit adapted per the driveway doctrine's spring annex: the snowsuit retired mid-month with honors (the small star restored to mobility, celebrated by all limbs), the mud budgeted for at every door, the towel bin instituted at the threshold, the stroller's wheels hosed weekly like a working vehicle, which they were.

And the season's finding, entered as the walks lengthened: the town, re-emerging, was being received by its youngest correspondent as entirely new — the bench, the library lantern, the market's Saturday roar, all of it pointed at, named again, refiled — the winter's long interior having rendered the whole map fresh, the world twice-discovered before her first birthday.

The first green arrived at the month's very edge: crocus spears at the Hendersons' walk, pointed at, named, beamed.

Coming, said everything now, at full mud-season volume.

The almanac's fresh page, started at the icicle, was filling fast.
