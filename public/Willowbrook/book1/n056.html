Night 56

The Unpacked Bag

Month 2 — Smiles and Sleep

This is a story about a bag, and like most stories about bags, it is really about peace.

The diaper bag had begun life as chaos with a zipper. Every outing, a excavation: wipes under the spare shirt under the mystery sock, the good pacifier discovered at home, afterward, in the fruit bowl.

Then one Sunday evening Papa emptied the whole thing onto the table and founded a civilization. Diapers, a day's worth plus two, because the day you pack exactly enough is the day of the famous triple. Wipes, and then more wipes; wipes solve things that are not even diapers. Full change of baby clothes — and the veteran move, learned the hard way at the market: a spare shirt for the adult. Burp cloths. Sacks for the unspeakable. The backup pacifier, sworn to its pocket. And taped in the lid, the little card: doctor's number, all clear.

The civilization had one law: the bag reloads at night, the way firefighters rack their boots. Not in the morning scramble — at night, while the day's lessons are fresh.

After that, leaving the house stopped being a raid and became a stroll. Grab, sling, gone.

The bag sat by the door in the lamplight, packed, patient, faintly smug.

Peace, with a zipper. The bag slept. The house slept over it.
