Night 41

Bathtime Changes Its Mind

Month 2 — Smiles and Sleep

The belly button had healed, the sponge era had ended, and tonight Olivia sat — reclined, rather, in her little sloped tub — in two inches of actual water.

Two inches was plenty. Elbow-warm, tested twice; a wrist works, an elbow works better, and a bath thermometer works for parents who like numbers: comfortably warm, never hot.

For three baths she had been against it. Tonight, mid-protest, her own hand slapped the water — splat — and everything changed. Splat. SPLAT. A weapon! A toy! A whole physics laboratory from the waist down!

Mama kept one hand supporting the small back at all times, which is the one bath rule with no exceptions: a hand on the baby, always, and never a step away, not for the phone, not for the towel, not for three seconds. Baths are lovely and water is serious, and both things are true at once.

The washing took ninety seconds. The splashing took considerably longer, by popular demand.

Warm towel, hood up: a small pink prophet emerged, fists full of victory.

Bathtime had officially switched sides. It was fun now. Everyone got a little wet agreeing.
