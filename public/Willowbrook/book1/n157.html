Night 157

Water, Two Ounces, Neat

Month 6: The Feast Begins

With the feast came the feast's companion, poured to the depth of one thumb: water.

Two ounces stood in the training cup, open-topped, small enough for small hands, weighted against ambition, and the six-month clause was read into the record: water enters with solids, in sips, as apprentice. Before this line it had no seat at all, the milk carrying all hydration and the small kidneys preferring it so. And even now, ounces not bottles: a few swallows with meals, the milk still sovereign, water learning its future trade at the margins.

The cup itself carried the page's second counsel. The open cup, hardest first, teaches truest, lip, tilt, sip, the full adult mechanics, learned wet. The straw cup, a worthy second chair. The old spouted sippy, beloved of diaper bags everywhere, demoted by the dentists and the speech folk alike to travel duty: convenient, spill-proof, and teaching mostly the bottle's old lesson over again.

The apprenticeship ran as apprenticeships run. Grip achieved, both-handed, white-knuckled. Tilt achieved, over-achieved, corrected by a guiding maternal finger. Sip achieved, one true swallow, witnessed, followed by the traditional chin cascade, the droplets sitting on her bib like campaign medals, which by any fair reckoning they were.

She pounded the cup on the tray: the gavel, now applied to all governance.

Refill petitioned. Refill granted, two ounces, neat.

The sovereign milk watched from the counter, unthreatened, its long reign secure for months yet, but the apprentice was in the building now, learning the trade one wet chin at a time.
