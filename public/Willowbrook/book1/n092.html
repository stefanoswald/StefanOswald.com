Night 92

Both Directions Now

Month 4 — Rolling and Laughing

The rolling had gone professional.

Tummy to back, back to tummy, chained together now in twos and threes: Olivia moved across the cleared living room floor like a small delighted log, arriving at destinations and claiming them. The lower shelf: reached and raided. The dog-eared magazine basket: reached, tipped, thoroughly edited.

Both directions is the fourth month's motor headline, and it changes the safety math for good. The floor is her country now, all of it, and the audit walks on knees: down at her level, crawling the rooms she rolls, seeing what she sees. Cords that dangle. Bags with straps. The one small button, escaped from a coat, invisible from standing height and perfectly sized for the laboratory of the mouth — found on the second pass, pocketed with a shudder.

The changing table completed its retirement from solo use; the floor changed her now on wild days, scripture on the wilder ones. And the play yard earned its keep at last, not as a cage but as a harbor — somewhere safe to anchor the roller while a kettle boiled or a door was answered.

She rolled to Papa's feet and gnawed his slipper, conquering it.

"Welcome to the mainland," he said.

She was already rolling elsewhere. Empires do not rest.
