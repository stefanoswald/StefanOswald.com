Night 169

Soup Weather

Month 6: The Feast Begins

The cold snap settled in, and the kitchen answered the oldest way: the pot went on in the afternoon and stayed on, and the windows blushed with steam, and the house smelled like the inside of comfort.

Soup weather, arrived, and the feast's newest member found the season built for her.

From the pot, tribute flowed trayward all evening in feast-legal form. The carrot coins, simmered past all resistance, pinched at (rake-assisted) and gummed to nothing. The potato, mashed at tableside with one fork and no ceremony. The soft-shredded chicken, first of its line, received with the frown of a critic and the seconds of a convert. The broth itself, spooned in tastes, the kitchen's whole day distilled to a warm salt-sweet fact.

The page stirs its counsel in. Family food, adapted, beats baby food, purchased, on every count that matters by this stage: the pot's carrot outranks the pouch's purée on texture (the chew practice pouches never provide), on variety (tonight's soup was eleven foods wearing one name), and on the deep syllabus, she was eating the family's dinner, at the family's table, in the family's steam, enrollment in the household cuisine beginning night one. The adaptations, brief: salt held back till her portion ships (the small kidneys' one standing request), the choke chart consulted on shapes, the spice introduced like the allergens, early, gently, often. Bland is a habit, not a nature; the babies of the spiced world eat the spiced world's suppers.

Seconds were petitioned by gavel and granted by ladle.

Outside, the first hard frost took the garden. Inside, the republic ate soup with its whole face, and the windows wept warm, and winter, wherever it was assembling, could take its time.

The pot would be back tomorrow. Pots know their season. So, already, one carrot coin at a time, did she.
