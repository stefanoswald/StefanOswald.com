Night 36

The Four Steps of Bedtime

Month 2 — Smiles and Sleep

The routine was four steps long and never changed, which was the whole point of it.

Bath or a warm washcloth wipe-down, first. Fresh diaper and pajamas, second, with the sleep sack zipped like an envelope sealed. Third, one small book in the lap-lamp glow — Olivia could not follow a word and followed everything: the voice going up and down the hills of the sentences, the pages turning like slow doors.

Fourth, the song. The same song, every night, slightly wrong in the same places, because Papa learned it wrong in 1990-something and refused all corrections.

Then crib, on her back, one hand resting a moment on her chest like a signature at the end of a letter. Same words each night: "Goodnight, little one. We are just outside."

Babies cannot read clocks, but they read order beautifully. Bath means jammies mean book means song means sleep — a hallway of open doors, each leading gently to the next, every single night.

By the third week, Olivia began yawning at the sound of the bathwater.

The hallway was working. They walked it together, and it always ended in the same soft room.
