Night 285

The Backyard Tent

Month 10: High Summer

The far aunts' visit had left, among its cargo, one item the household had eyed all month: the tent, the aunts' own venerable two-sleeper, bequeathed at the driveway wave with instructions to "give her the stars early." The month's last warm Saturday honored the bequest: the backyard tent night, the year's gentlest expedition, one lawn from home.

The pitch, conducted at the afternoon's gold per the helping bureau's full enrollment: the poles threaded at four hands (two of them ceremonially), the stakes hammered per the percussion catalog's happiest commission, the flap's zipper, the page notes the whole evening's finest toy arrived free with the venue, run at the customs office's rates until further notice.

And the night's own program, logged at the almanac's tender best. The dinner picnicked at the flap per the expedition's charter. The dusk received from the tent's own harbor, the lawn's fireflies rising per the fourth month's oldest pages (reached for again, missed again, laughed at again: the whole first summer's gesture, rerun by a reacher who could now chase), the lane's porch lights arriving one by one per the liturgy the stroller had watched all year. The liturgy itself, ported per the travel treaty's proven law, the routine is home, and run entire at the tent's own floor: the pajamas, the book (the bear book, naturally, its meadow spread read one lawn from the meadow's own crickets), the song at the flap with the wrong notes carrying across the actual dark.

And the stars, the bequest's own cargo. The flap held open at the settling's edge, the first arrivals pointed at over the willow's hill: the summer's whole ceiling, live, at an altitude no nursery window had ever offered, and among them, low and gold and (the town's records would note, per their custom, without explanation) rather nearer than the others, one particular star, received by the smallest camper with the point's slowest, fullest lock and one soft coin, spent at the sky itself:

"Duh."

Star, supplied the market, in the settling register. That one, the household's own dialect added, unspoken, per a year's worth of window liturgies: that one's ours.

(The sleeper, the page records honestly per the circus doctrine's terms, lasted in the tent until the true dark's first deep hour, then transferred per the contingency's standing plan to the crib's own architecture, the parents following at midnight with the tent left standing per treaty for the morning's rediscovery. First camps, the veterans' law runs, are measured in dusks, not dawns. The dusk had been total.)

The tent stood the week, rediscovered daily, the flap's zipper amortizing the whole bequest.

The stars, the page certifies, took. The pointing at them never stopped, not that summer, not the ones after.

"Give her the stars early." Honored, at one lawn's range, entire. The aunts were sent frame one of the flap at dusk.

They framed it. Of course they framed it. Some bequests pay both directions.
