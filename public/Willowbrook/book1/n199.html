Night 199

The Putting-In Era

Month 7 — Explorers and Guardrails

For six months the traffic had run one way: everything out. Out of the cupboard, out of the basket, out of the high chair overboard — extraction the era's whole economy, the emptied container its own reward.

This month the current reversed, and the house woke to a new sound: clink. Clink. Clink. Blocks, entering a bowl. On purpose. One at a time, each drop supervised by its own architect, each arrival in the steel confirmed by its ring.

Then — the cycle's other stroke — the dump: bowl seized, inverted, holdings scattered, and the filling begun again, immediately, contentedly, a tide with hands.

The putting-in era, the charts' quiet marker for the turn of the first year's third quarter, and the page gives it its engineering due. Insertion is extraction's harder sibling: the release (the Dropping Game's old degree) now aimed — object positioned above a target, let go at coordinates, the miss corrected next round. Container and contained, the first physics of in, the concept every tidied toy and packed suitcase and posted letter runs on for life. And the cycle itself — fill, dump, fill — is not indecision but experiment at the age's own pace: both directions of the great truth tested until owned.

The house restocked for the era per the standing counsel — problems, not products. The steel bowl and its block currency. The muffin tin, one tenant per berth. The oatmeal canister with the slot cut in its lid, blocks posted through like mail (the era's deluxe model, minted from trash). The shape sorter joined from the rotation basket and was respected for its round port exclusively, squares and stars being propaganda for months yet.

Clink, went the winter mornings now. Clink. Scatter. Clink.

The extraction lobby retained the cupboard, of course — some industries are eternal.

But the current had turned. In went the blocks; in went the mitten to the boot; in went one puff, ceremonially, to Papa's coat pocket, discovered there at work like a small soggy postcard.

In: achieved. The suitcases of her whole life stood quietly in the future, waiting to be packed and unpacked and packed. It starts at a steel bowl. It started this month.
