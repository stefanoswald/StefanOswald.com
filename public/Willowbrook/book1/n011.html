Night 11

A Bath Before Swimming

Month 1 — Arrival

Until her little belly button healed and its stump waved goodbye, Olivia could not soak in a proper bath. So bathtime was a sponge affair: a warm room, a basin, a washcloth, and two parents moving like careful jewelers.

Face first, with just clean water. Then the folds and creases — under the chin where milk hides, the tiny fists that never open, behind the ears where mysteries collect. Warm, wet, wrapped again quickly, because small bodies lose heat fast.

Olivia was against the entire project at first, then confusingly in favor of it.

Two or three baths a week is plenty at this age; more dries the paper-thin skin that still smells like the beginning of the world.

Afterward she lay in her hooded towel like a small astonished monk, warm and furious and clean.

"Same time Thursday," Papa told her. She could not agree, but she did fall asleep, which everyone took as a yes.
