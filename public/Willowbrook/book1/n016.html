Night 16

What the Doctor Said

Month 1: Arrival

Olivia went to be counted. Ounces, inches, the circumference of her marvelous head, at the checkup, everything about her turned out to be a number, and every number lived on a curve.

"Don't marry the percentiles," the doctor said kindly. "A fifteenth-percentile baby growing along her own curve is doing every bit as well as a ninetieth. The curve is the story; the number is one word of it."

Mama unfolded her list of questions, she had learned to write them down at home, because they evaporate in offices. How many wet diapers? Six or more a day meant plenty of milk. That rattly newborn sneeze? Normal. The flaky skin, the crossed eyes, the hiccups that shook her like a tiny engine? Normal, normal, normal.

Never feel foolish for asking, they learned. Pediatricians have heard everything, twice, today.

Back home, Olivia slept off the indignity of the scale.

She was growing exactly at the speed of herself. It was the right speed.
