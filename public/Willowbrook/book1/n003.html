Night 3

The Swaddle Song

Month 1: Arrival

Fold, fold, tuck. Mama laid the blanket out like a diamond and folded the top corner down.

Olivia's arms waved about like little windmills. For nine whole months she had lived in the snuggest home there is. The world felt terribly roomy now. A swaddle, snug around the arms but always loose around the hips, is a letter from that old home: you are held, you are held, you are held.

Fold, fold, tuck. Not too tight, two of Papa's fingers could still fit at her chest. Snug is a hug; tight is not.

And when, some week soon, Olivia showed the first sign of rolling, the swaddle would be retired with honors, because rolling babies need their arms free. But that was a story for another night.

Tonight there was only the fold, the tuck, the sigh, and a burrito-shaped baby drifting off, held, held, held.
