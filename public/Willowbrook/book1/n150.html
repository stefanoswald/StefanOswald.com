Night 150

Five Months, Counted

Month 5: Nearly Sitting, Nearly Everything

The audit moved outdoors for the summer, to the oak's blanket at golden hour, the accountants of joy having learned to hold meetings where the light was best.

The countables, entered into the evening's minutes. A sitter, tripod certified, hands-free in gusts. A traveler: one wedding survived, one circus night amortized, one suitcase wiser. A speaker of chains and growls, answerer to her own name, deliverer of morning addresses. A ship launched, sternward, gloriously, with the house proofed to its hull line and the breakables in the high country. One hen audience granted. One economy of Tuesdays, running at surplus. One bedtime gravel patch, twelve days, weathered on doctrine.

The uncountables, as ever, took the longer entry. The stranger file opening, her smiles becoming currency instead of weather, spent now on the known, which made each one, the parents privately agreed, worth more. The long stares at the dusk feed, unbudgeted wealth. The way the house had stopped being a place they kept a baby and become the place she lived, her republic in the corner, her orchestra in the cupboard, her almanac on the shelf filling with two handwritings.

And the fifth month's particular finding, said aloud this time, by Papa, watching her work the ring in the last gold light: "She's not a baby who does things anymore. She's a person who's busy."

The person, busy, looked up at her name in the sentence. Smiled the currency at both of them. Returned to work.

The oak moved the shade to keep them, the way it had all summer.

Audited. Approved. Carried forward, sitting up.
