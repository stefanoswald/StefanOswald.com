Night 4

Day Moon, Night Sun

Month 1: Arrival

Olivia had her days and nights on backwards, the way new babies often do. At noon she slept like a stone. At two in the morning she was ready to discuss everything.

"You are nocturnal, my love," Mama whispered, "and I am not."

So the family began teaching the oldest lesson: day is bright, night is boring. Daytime feedings came with open curtains, chatter, and songs. Nighttime feedings became the dullest party in Willowbrook, lights low, voice a murmur, no games, no fun, straight back to bed.

Babies are clever. Give them a week or three of bright-loud days and dim-quiet nights, and their tiny inner clocks slowly turn right-side up.

Not tonight, though. Tonight Olivia stared at the ceiling, fascinated by absolutely nothing, while Mama half-dreamed beside her.

That is normal. That is temporary. Say it twice, because both parts are true.

Outside, the real moon kept the light low, doing its part.
