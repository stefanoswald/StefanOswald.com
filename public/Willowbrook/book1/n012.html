Night 12

The Sound of the Sea

Month 1 — Arrival

The nursery was silent, and that was the problem.

For nine months Olivia had slept inside a rushing, whooshing, drumming world — blood and breath and voice, louder than a vacuum cleaner, day and night. Then she was born into rooms where the loudest thing was a clock.

So Mama gave her back the sea. A small machine on the dresser breathed a steady shhhhh into the room — not across the crib, but from a polite distance, and no louder than a soft shower running.

Steady noise does two jobs. It sounds like home. And it swallows the creaks and door-thumps and dog-barks that yank a light sleeper awake.

Olivia's eyes drifted, closed, stayed closed. The sea rolled on, going nowhere, all night.

Somewhere above the roof the real stars listened to it too, and found it very relaxing, and one of them yawned.
