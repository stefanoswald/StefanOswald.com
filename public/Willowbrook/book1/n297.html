Night 297

The Leaf Pile Returns

Month 10: High Summer

The oak's high country, first to file per its custom, shipped its early consignments through the month's last week, and Saturday raised at the yard's old address the season's founding institution, second annual edition: the leaf pile.

The page notes the anniversary at the record's own symmetry: the first edition's audience had supervised from a blanket's anchored border, all points and commentary; the second's arrived on foot, the pile's whole commerce conducted this year at first person, per the upright franchise the twelve months between had built.

The commerce, logged at the rake's own pace. The handfuls: hoisted two-fisted at the pile's margins and released at arm's full altitude, the wind taking its franchise per the season's contract, the leaves' descent tracked per the tracking curriculum's oldest drills, the transaction repeated at rates the rake could not match and did not try (the pile's whole civic purpose, Papa had long since ruled per the raking fathers' universal treaty, being its unmaking). The first sit: administered at the pile's own crest per the ceremony's requirements, the crunch received at full committee, the leaves worn per the funnel's entire millinery tradition. And the listening, the page's true station: the pile's base consulted at a stillness the commerce twice suspended, the small head cocked at the rustle's own frequencies, the file, a year old, wainscoting-grade, visibly running: the fence gap regarded, the point rising at the yard's old crossing, the coin spent at the autumn itself, interrogative:

"Fuff?"

Not the dog's address, the market ruled at the translation's own committee. The other file. The trundling one. The leaf-backed nation's own.

"Maybe," honored the state, at the household's deepest currency. "He came last year, right out of that pile. Hedgehogs remember their inns."

The pile, per the year-old vote's standing renewal, was left standing at dusk, the season's first civic works, resident presumed, the fence gap's watch reassumed at the window per the winter-long precedent.

(He came, the record notes, at the month's own edge: the rustle at dusk, the caramel trundle at the yard's crossing, one leaf aboard per the knapsack's eternal negotiations, received this year not by a blanket-anchored baby but by a walker at the window's full altitude, the point firing, the lookback firing, the whole year-old file confirming at last at first person: the returning things return. Harry, per the town's discreet registries, logged the small one's growth at his own unhurried rates and trundled on, winter quarters' portfolio in hand, per his office. Sundays, said the farm; autumns, said the fence gap. The town kept all its appointments. It was, the page certifies again, built on exactly that.)

"Fuff," ruled the window's watch, satisfied, at the crossing's empty dusk.

Close enough, per the whole taxonomy's trading. The spirit of the thing, as ever, exact.
