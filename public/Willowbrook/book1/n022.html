Night 22

Spit Happens

Month 1 — Arrival

The third outfit of the day went into the basket, and it was not yet noon.

Olivia was what the doctor cheerfully called a happy spitter. Milk went down; some scenic percentage came back up, usually onto whichever shoulder had just been declared clean. The washing machine had never known such purpose.

"Is it too much?" Mama had asked, the way all parents ask.

"Is she growing? Is she content? Plenty of wet diapers?" the doctor asked back. Yes, yes, and heavens yes. "Then it's a laundry problem, not a medical one. It peaks around four months and fades when she sits and stands."

So the house adapted, the way houses do. Burp cloths colonized every chair arm. Feeds got a touch smaller and calmer, with quiet upright time after instead of bouncing. And white shirts learned to live dangerously.

It was, Mama decided, a fair tax on a working milk operation.

Olivia, spotless and satisfied, slept in outfit number four like royalty between wardrobe changes.
