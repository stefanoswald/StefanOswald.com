Night 184

The Pull to Stand

Month 7 — Explorers and Guardrails

The lap spring's forecast filled early: on an ordinary Thursday, the monitor showed the morning address being delivered, unprecedentedly, from altitude.

She had pulled to stand. In the crib, gripping the rail — the mattress long since lowered per the standing advice, the foresight now cashing its check — hauling herself up hand over hand like a sailor taking rigging, to sway at the summit on legs that had never held the whole job before. The face at the rail, when Mama arrived: triumph at full parliamentary volume. The morning address, resumed from the new podium, gained a register.

The pull to stand, filed in the eight-to-ten-month country by the charts and arriving here at seven-and-change, this baby running her own almanac as all babies do. The mechanics, for households watching their own sailors: the grip leads, the arms haul, the legs learn the finish — furniture, rails, and parental legs all serving as rigging, the world reassessed overnight as a climbing surface.

The security review, same day, per doctrine. The crib to its lowest setting, done. The mobile, that hanging temptation, retired with honors. The freestanding rigging audited: the bookshelf's anchor re-torqued, the floor lamp deported, the tablecloth — that classic silent hazard, a yank away from cascading dinnerware — stripped from the kitchen table for the duration. And the fall math accepted with the era's required calm: new standers drop, plentifully, onto bottoms built for it, the crumples part of the curriculum, the guardrail work being landing zones, not prevention.

By the weekend she pulled to stand on everything: the couch, the gate, the dishwasher door (closed, henceforth locked mid-cycle), Grandpa Joe's shin at Sunday dinner — received by that veteran without spilling his coffee, forty years of livestock telling in the ankles.

Up. The whole project of her, visibly, from here on: up.

The floor's long era of sovereignty had been given notice. The floor, wise old country, took it graciously — it would receive her ten thousand more times yet, and knew it.
