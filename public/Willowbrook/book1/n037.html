Night 37

The Evening Buffet

Month 2: Smiles and Sleep

From five o'clock onward, Olivia wanted dinner. Then second dinner. Then a light third dinner, a snack, an aperitif, and dinner again.

Cluster feeding, the books called it. Stocking the cellar, Grandpa Joe called it, which made more sense: little meals packed close together all evening, the way you lay in wood before a cold night. Babies who cellar-up at dusk often pay it back with their longest sleep stretch after.

Knowing that changed the whole feeling of the marathon. Not a milk shortage. Not fussiness. Just a small mammal preparing for the long dark, the way small mammals have forever.

So the family stopped fighting five o'clock and furnished it instead. Mama took the corner chair with the good pillow, feet up, water in reach. Papa ran the kitchen and delivered dinner to her elbow in installments, matching Olivia course for course.

The whole operation looked less like a struggle and more like a tiny strange restaurant, table for two, one guest extremely regular.

The tip, paid at nine o'clock, was four hours of silence.

Worth every course.
