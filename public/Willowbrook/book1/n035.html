Night 35

Drowsy but Awake

Month 2 — Smiles and Sleep

The advice sounded like a riddle: put her down drowsy but awake. Asleep enough to go, awake enough to know she went.

The idea underneath is kind. A baby who drifts off in her own bed, even once in a while, is slowly learning the greatest bedtime skill there is: falling asleep where you wake up, so the small surprises of the night do not require a search party.

Some nights it worked like magic — eyes at half-mast, down into the crib, a squirm, a sigh, sleep. Mama stood in the dark feeling like she had landed a plane.

Many nights it did not. Down drowsy, instantly furious, up again, rocked fully asleep, transferred like a bomb-disposal operation. That is not failure; that is a baby being two months old.

Rocking and nursing to sleep are not bad habits now. They are the job. Drowsy-but-awake is a lottery ticket worth buying most nights, never a test to pass.

Tonight the plane landed. Mama tiptoed out on soft socks, triumphant.

Tomorrow, who knew. That was tomorrow's page.
