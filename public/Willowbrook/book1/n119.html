Night 119

The Fourth Month, Counted

Month 4: Rolling and Laughing

They took the audit to the porch swing, it being summer, the accountants of joy preferring fieldwork in the warm months.

The countables: laughter, gone from event to repertoire, four reliable bits and rising. Rolling weaponized into transport, the floor annexed entire. Feet discovered, gripped, and partially eaten. One consonant, ba, minted and spent lavishly on lamps, bears, and elephants. A high chair seated at headquarters, an audience installed at dinner, a renovation survived at 3 a.m. on schedule and on shifts. One duck, titled. One elephant, briefly, gloriously, and Grandpa Joe not even looking up.

The uncountables took the second half of the swing's slow arc. How the laughing had changed the house's weather, a residence with a comedy season keeps different books entirely. How the regression had taught them the deepest lesson yet, deeper than any technique: that the hard stretches are renovations, that they end, that the two of them could be shift workers and sweethearts in the same fortnight and hold.

And this, said neither aloud: that she had begun, unmistakably, to be a person. Preferences. Comic taste. A way of greeting the duck. Four months old, and already nobody else.

The fireflies came up out of the grass like slow sparks. Olivia reached for every single one, missing magnificently, undiscouraged, which was, the parents agreed, the whole quarter in one gesture.

The swing creaked its old summer creak.

Audited. Approved. Carried forward, laughing.
