Night 223

Pat-a-Cake and the Body Map

Month 8 — The Velcro Weeks and the Thaw

The gesture wardrobe's winter additions ran to choreography, and the month's two hits shared a syllabus the page pauses to admire.

Pat-a-cake landed first: the old rhythm played hand to hand, her palms meeting Mama's on the beat — the clapping of the seventh month now synchronized to another person's timing, which is to say: dancing, seated, in miniature. The rolling verse rolled the forearms over each other (achieved at roughly the fidelity of a small cement mixer, and improved weekly); the marking with a B was marked with conviction upon the nearest surface; and the throwing in the oven was flung with both arms wide in the so-big grammar, the finale's applause self-supplied.

And where's-your-nose arrived on its heels: the question posed, the small hand flying — to the ear, at first, per the standard early routing errors, then compiling week by week to the true map: nose found, head found, belly found and drummed, toes found (the old southern relatives of the fourth month, saluted anew), and the advanced placement item the family drilled purely for its comedy — where's Papa's nose? — answered by seizure of the target with the full certified pincer.

The page files the science beneath the parlor games. The body map — the self's own geography, surveyed since the hands' discovery in month two — was going verbal: the labels soldering to the territories inside the naming beam, the first vocabulary of self assembling in the region where all self-knowledge starts, which is to say: the nose. The synchronized games ran the deeper syllabus: rhythm shared is timing learned is conversation's meter drilled — the pat-a-cake beat and the dialogue's turn-taking being, the researchers keep finding, the same machinery at different speeds. And the whole curriculum ran on the only fuel it ever runs on: the games played for their own sake, laughing, nightly, the learning smuggled per this book's constant custom inside the fun.

The month's recital, performed for the video-call grandfather on his standing Tuesday: pat-a-cake entire, nose located on the first routing, belly drummed at length.

"Well now," said the screen, which the family could translate at sight these days: the whole map's coming in. Keep drawing it.

They kept drawing it. The territories, labeled nightly, lit up one by one, all the way to the toes.
