Night 115

The Very Large Dog

Month 4 — Rolling and Laughing

Down the lane lived a dog of great age and volume, gold going white at the muzzle, whose evening patrol brought him past the porch as reliably as dusk.

Tonight, patrols intersected, and Olivia met her first dog.

The meeting ran on protocol, because babies and dogs deserve protocol. Distance first: the dog seated at his neighbor's heel a respectful lawn-width off, Olivia aloft in Mama's arms, both parties surveying. The dog's owner ran her half — a calm dog, a held leash, no lunging love. Mama ran hers: no small hands grabbing at fur or ears or tail, not this year; the reaching fist redirected to a wave. Watching, at this age, is the whole visit.

Olivia watched with her entire administration. The size of it. The tail's metronome. The huff of dog-breath, audible across the grass, and the vast pink yawn that finished the inspection. Her arms pumped once — the fourth month's standing ovation.

Every animal met calmly now was a deposit in a long account: creatures are interesting, creatures have rules, the rules keep everyone's ears comfortable. The account would mature in toddlerhood, when the reaching years arrived with their own curriculum.

The patrol moved on, nails ticking the sidewalk, tail still governing time.

"Dog," said Mama, planting the word by the experience, seed to soil.

"Ba," said Olivia, watching him go — which was, all present agreed, remarkably close.
