Night 123

The Stranger File

Month 5 — Nearly Sitting, Nearly Everything

The visitor was friendly, vouched for, and bearing a gift, and Olivia regarded her the way a border official regards a smudged passport.

The easy smiles of the earlier months — issued to anyone with a face, that golden age of universal diplomacy — were being reviewed. From the harbor of Papa's arms, she studied the newcomer: the long look, the check back over her shoulder to Papa's face (reading his weather about this person, the fifth month's newest instrument), then the slow, provisional, not-yet of her expression.

This is discernment arriving, not rudeness — the file system coming online. Known faces one drawer, new faces another, and the new drawer handled with proper procedure. It would sharpen further in the months ahead, into the full stranger wariness of the eight-and-nine-month country, and it deserved, the page insists, congratulation rather than apology.

So the house adopted the protocols worth teaching relatives early. No swooping — the visitor who waits, chats with the parents, lets the baby audit from safe harbor, gets cleared in minutes; the one who dives in cheek-first gets filed under alarm. No passing the baby like a parcel over her objection. And no "she's just shy," said in that tone — the border takes its time because the border is working.

Twenty minutes in, from the harbor, Olivia extended one starfish hand toward the visitor's bracelet.

Visa granted. Provisional. Renewable.

The visitor, well-briefed, let the bracelet be inspected and made no sudden moves. Some passports just need a proper reading.
