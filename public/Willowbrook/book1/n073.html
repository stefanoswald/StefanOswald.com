Night 73

The Drool Era Dawns

Month 3 — Rhythm

The springs turned on in the third month, and they did not turn off.

Drool — abundant, sudden, gleaming. Fists went into the mouth and stayed there like tenants. The laundry gained a new department: bibs, worn now as daily fashion, changed like tides.

"Teething!" announced the aunts, thrilled. Possibly — but here is the calmer truth this page carries: the drool era usually opens months before any tooth appears. Around now, the saliva works simply come online at full pressure while swallowing hasn't caught up, and the fists-in-mouth business is as much laboratory as toothache. Actual first teeth keep their own calendar — six months, eight, sometimes a birthday-late surprise — and arrive when they arrive regardless of forecasts.

Meanwhile, the era asks small housekeeping. Soft bibs to spare the chest from constant damp. The chin patted dry, not rubbed, and a whisper of balm if it chaps — drool rash being the era's one real nuisance. Cold things to gum when the gums seem to itch: the chilled (never frozen) teether, the cold washcloth corner, all of it inspected by the toilet-tube rule.

Olivia removed her fists to smile — a bright, streaming, four-fountain smile.

"Very becoming," said Mama, deploying bib number six.

The era had dawned. It would sparkle, literally, for months.
