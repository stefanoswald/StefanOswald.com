Night 269

The Ninth Month, Counted

Month 9 — The Edge of Everything

The audit took the porch swing's first summer session, the evening warm enough at last, the almanac's ninth chapter between them and the season's early crickets tuning under the lane's lights.

The countables, entered to the swing's old creak. The step — the step — boxed, double-boxed, signed, three weeks compiled now into a walker touring her world at converted routes. Four coins minted and spending: duck of the pond's own Tuesday, ball, dog certified at last, more in the sign's spoken edition — plus the hah of the hot treaty and the boom of the sky's drums, the working ledger past counting when the gestures' treasury consolidated in. One worm met gently; eleven chicks stroked at one finger's weight; one brook enrolled at family rate; one garden planted with its own gardener; one tower, two high, standing at the head of the whole line of made things. Boots exercised, storms filed under magnificent, one tulip half-crushed into the administration's first state flower. The falls: beyond count, injuries zero, face held. The last skill storm: eleven nights, doctrine's record. The two-gait economy: converted, the freight line retired unmarked, mourned in arrears per the year's own custom.

The uncountables, run long per the chapter's depth. That the walking had changed the household's very tense — the year's whole grammar gone from what she'll do to what she's doing, the future arriving daily now on its own two feet down the hallway's converted line. That the words had ended the year's beautiful one-way commentary: the documentary had gained a second narrator, and the house's oldest institution — the narrated world, running since the refrigerator's cold apple of month two — ran two-voiced now at every window, the beam speaking back. And the ninth month's own finding, said at the swing in the season's plain gold: they were raising a person now in the person's own company — consulted, answering, showing her rings to the watchers, spending her coins at the market she'd been banking all year — and the company, both auditors entered under the same date, was the year's whole dividend, compounding.

"Ten weeks," said Papa, the calendar's arithmetic arriving per the heat's own page.

"Maybe," said Mama — the old word restored to service after its one retirement, because the birthday's country ran on exactly its economy: soon, and not yet, and let it come at its own pace, and let it never, and yes.

The crickets took the night shift formally somewhere in the second cocoa.

The audit closed on their singing, the chapter's last entry in the junior narrator's own contributed hand: one scribbled line, crayon, unattributed, filed by the auditors as the ninth month's true signature.

Audited. Approved. Carried forward — walking, talking, ten weeks out, at company rates.
