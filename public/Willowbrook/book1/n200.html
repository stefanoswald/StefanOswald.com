Night 200

Two Hundred Nights

Month 7: Explorers and Guardrails

The second stone. Two hundred nights.

The first stone, set at one hundred, marked a habit becoming a place. This one marks the place acquiring citizens, because the ritual, somewhere in its second hundred, had stopped being performed for her and started being performed with her.

The evidence, gathered nightly now. At the word bath she crawled bathroomward, the liturgy's first responder. The sleep sack, once an imposition, was entered like a uniform, arms offered, familiar zip received with the calm of an old arrangement. The book was co-operated per the shuffle-mode treaty, pages wrestled, spreads appointed by her turning. At lamp-out she performed her own verse: one long exhale-babble at the ceiling, a settling declaration the parents could not translate and would not have traded for any sentence in any language. And the song, the wrong-noted heirloom, was attended now with something the almanac had recorded, cautiously, as humming: a tuneless underneath-sound, riding along, her first harmony.

Two hundred nights of the same shape, and the shape had done what shapes do: become load-bearing. Through the strikes, the fevers, the renovations and regressions and travel circuses, the four steps had held, moved earlier, trimmed shorter, sung through gravel, but held, and the holding was the finding, the one this page sets in the road for every family checking their own stones: the ritual's power was never any single night. It was the two-hundredth night knowing the shape of the hundred-and-ninety-ninth. Continuity is the lullaby under the lullaby.

The page looks briefly up the road: stone three would fall past the birthday, in the toddler country, where the liturgy would grow negotiations, stalling tactics, one more book pleaded in actual words. The shape would hold there too. Shapes like this hold for years, and then, the oldest parents in Willowbrook confirm it, they hold in memory for the rest of the life they held.

Tonight, night two hundred ran eleven minutes, unremarkable, which was the entire miracle.

The exhale-babble was delivered at the ceiling. The hum rode the song.

Stella, outside, flared once for the stone, as is her policy on round numbers.

Two hundred down. All the rest to go. The shape held.
