Night 72

Thumb or Silicone

Month 3: Rhythm

The great debate reached the little house on Maple Lane, as it reaches every house: the thumb or the pacifier, and the loud aunts of the world had opinions on both.

Olivia, uninterested in the literature, had lately discovered her thumb, while remaining a loyal client of the pacifier at sleep times. The house takes, this page reports, the calm position.

Sucking is not a vice; it is one of the first tools babies own for settling themselves, standard equipment since before birth. The pacifier at sleep times carries a quiet bonus in the first months, a small extra safety margin the sleep doctors nod at, with the usual manners: offered once feeding runs smooth, never tethered to strings or clips in bed, never dipped in anything, retired without drama if it starts costing more wake-ups than it saves, since paci-reinsertion at 3 a.m. is a chauffeur job that ends careers.

The thumb needs no reinserting and cannot fall on the floor; its bill, if any, comes years later and is payable at the dentist. Fair terms either way.

The debate, in short, has no trophy. Comfort is the point, and this too shall be outgrown, as nearly all of it is, by the time anyone is old enough to be embarrassed.

Olivia slept with the pacifier and kept the thumb in reserve, a woman with a spare.

The aunts slept unconsulted. All parties woke happy.
