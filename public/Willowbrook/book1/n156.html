Night 156

Six Handfuls of Broccoli

Month 6 — The Feast Begins

The feast's second week opened the self-service wing, and the kitchen was never clean again, and the page will defend the mess to its last line.

Alongside the spoon courses came the graspables: soft-steamed broccoli florets, stick-cut and fist-sized, the stem a natural handle engineered by nature for exactly this clientele. Banana in its half-peel holster. Avocado spears, rolled in crumb for grip. The rule of the cut in this era: bigger is safer — pieces the size of her own fist, held and gnawed, outranking the diced hazards; the food graspable by palm, this being months before the pincer's fine delivery.

Olivia received her first floret like a scepter and governed with it. Gnawed the crown. Wore the crown. Painted with the crown in a broad green civic mural, tray to eyebrows. Some measurable fraction arrived internally; the tarp below the chair — the era's wisest furniture — received the remainder with dignity.

The defense of the mess, entered formally: the mess is the meal. Self-feeding runs the whole curriculum at once — grip, aim, pressure, texture, the hand-eye syllabus with lunch as the lab fee — and the smeared face is a face running experiments. The wiped-clean, spoon-only path is tidier tonight and poorer all year: eaters allowed the mess come to the table sooner, wider, and gladder, the research and the grandmothers concurring again.

Portion of floret consumed: perhaps a sixth. Portion of purpose served: entire.

The mural was documented, then washed. The muralist required a full bath and glowed throughout it.

Tomorrow: carrots, said the menu. The tarp braced itself. The kitchen had chosen its side, and it was hers.
