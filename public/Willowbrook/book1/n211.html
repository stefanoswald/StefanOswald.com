Night 211

The Seventh Month, Counted

Month 7 — Explorers and Guardrails

The audit convened in deep winter, cocoa standing in for the porch's fireflies, the almanac fat now with its seventh chapter, the subject asleep upstairs with her mouth open and her second tooth just showing — the parade's next float having arrived quietly during the sick week, discovered at a spoon's click like the first.

The countables, entered by lamplight. A crawl at velocity, a pull to stand, a cruising route fully charted, one unassisted stand of three seconds (witnessed, timed, wept over discreetly). A name landed — ma-ma, aimed, certified — and its companion da-da a fortnight behind. A gesture wardrobe nine words deep. One gift given (pre-owned, eaten per protocol). Peekaboo's authorship transferred. Two hundred nights stoned and passed. One snow touched, one year turned, one sick week held from a mattress on the floor, one icicle reported melting by the house's own correspondent.

The uncountables ran longer than any prior audit, the way they do as the person assembles. That the house now contained somebody — not a baby who did things but a colleague with a docket: her cupboard, her routes, her bear book, her standing appointments at the window. That she summoned them by name now, and the summons still rang both of them somewhere behind the sternum, every time, without wear. That the doors and the goodbyes had built their bridge exactly as the doctrine promised, the wave now holding what the grief had held. That the winter — the first deep one, the real test of the craft — had been not survived but kept: anchored, rhythmic, soup-fed, the two of them running the season like people who knew the trade now, because they did.

"Seven months," said Papa, closing the chapter.

"Maybe," said Mama, in the old dialect — meaning the spring side of the year was visible now, the candle end of it, the steps somewhere in it, and she was in no hurry and infinite hurry at once, both speeds, as ever, at once.

The cocoa was finished. The lamp clicked down.

Upstairs the second tooth gleamed in the open-mouthed dark, newest countable, sleeping among the uncountables.

Audited. Approved. Carried forward, standing — briefly, magnificently — free.
