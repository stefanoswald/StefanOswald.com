Night 69

The Practice Goodbye

Month 3 — Rhythm

The first practice morning was one hour long, and Mama spent it in the coffee shop across the street not drinking her coffee.

The goodbye itself had followed the good-goodbye rules, which she had read twice and Papa had taped inside the cupboard. Short. Warm. Honest. "Mama goes, Mama comes back." A kiss, a wave at the door — the same wave every time, so the wave itself becomes a promise — and then actually leaving, no doubling back for one more look, which teaches only that goodbyes are negotiable and long.

No sneaking off, either, though every fiber suggests it. A baby who looks up to find her person vanished learns to watch the exits. A baby whose person waves and goes and comes back — a hundred times, a thousand — learns the deepest math there is: leaving is half of a shape, and the other half is return.

Olivia, for the record, cried ninety seconds and then discovered the plant-house's wooden spoons.

Mama, for the record, cried slightly longer, into her untouched coffee.

Both were fully recovered by pickup, and the reunion was a festival.

Practice makes promises. The wave was becoming one already.
