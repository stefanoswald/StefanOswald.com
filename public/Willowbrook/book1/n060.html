Night 60

The First Laugh

Month 3 — Rhythm

It arrived on an ordinary morning, because milestones have no sense of occasion.

Papa was performing the belly kiss, a classic from his almost-laugh repertoire, when Olivia — instead of the usual delighted cough — laughed. Rolled-out, bubbling, unmistakable laughter, up from somewhere deeper than she was tall.

The room stopped. Papa did it again. She laughed again, harder, and then Mama was laughing, and then Papa, and for a minute the little house on Maple Lane was nothing but laughing, three people running on one baby's brand-new joy.

The first laugh usually lands around three or four months, and here is its secret: it is social from the very first note. She was not laughing at the kiss. She was laughing with him — her first joke shared, first joy paid forward on purpose.

They spent the day bankrupting themselves for encores, as all parents do, and learned the second secret: laughs cannot be farmed. They grow wild or not at all.

That night Papa wrote the date inside a book cover, hand not quite steady.

Some sounds you keep forever. This one had a date now, and a witness, and a home.
