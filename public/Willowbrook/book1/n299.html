Month 10: High Summer

The amber turned crisp at the month's own edge, the morning walks acquiring their first visible breath, the lane's gold going high and cold-rinsed, and the household ran the season's quiet domestic liturgy at the dresser's summit: the changeover, the summer kit struck, the knits come ashore.

The ceremony, per the drawer's own turning. The summer fleet, mustered and sorted at the fleet review's standing method: the sun hats to the keepsake harbor (the treaty's third-season veteran, the page notes, retired at honors, the fourth season's edition already sized and waiting), the wading kit dried and stowed at the basement's own registry, the rompers' whole light administration folded down, and the sizes audited at the changeover's true business: the summer's own growth read garment by garment per the almanac's tenderest arithmetic, the newborn-marked editions long harbored, the six-month fleet joining them now at the changeover's rates, the drawer's whole inventory, the page certifies, running two sizes north of the year's first edition.

"Baby," minted the ledger at the romper's own exhibit, the coin's arrival timed per the market's poetry, as ever, to its address.

"Baby," honored the market, at the register's own softness. "That was yours. When you were little."

When you were little. The phrase's first documented spend, chalked at the column with the date: the past tense, arriving at the drawer's own summit, sixty-six nights out.

The romper joined the harbor. The cardigan took the season's watch.

The drawer, turned, held the year's whole arithmetic folded: the little, kept; the growing, worn; both, per the book's eternal ruling, both.
