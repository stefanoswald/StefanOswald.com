Night 152

The Readiness Board Convenes

Month 6 — The Feast Begins

The six-month line arrived, and with it the question the high chair had been asking since assembly: now?

The readiness board convened over coffee, checklist between them, candidate in the chair displaying, unprompted, most of the evidence.

Sitting steady with minimal support: certified, the tripod long since graduated. Head control: months certified, the lighthouse fully operational. The tongue-thrust reflex — that ancient guardian that pushes all solids back out — faded: tested gently with an empty spoon, which was mouthed, not ejected. And the intent, the great unmistakable sign: food tracked like courtroom evidence for two months, the reach across the table now arriving with real aim, the watching gone predatory in the most endearing available way.

The board reviewed the calendar clause: around six months, the milk-alone era closes — the iron shipped from before birth runs low about now, and the gut and the swallow stand ready where four months ago they did not. Not a birthday rule; a readiness rule, the signs outranking the date in either direction, the pediatrician holding the tiebreaker for early movers and late bloomers alike.

Motion carried, unanimous, one abstention (the candidate, conflicted by a passing dog outside).

The feast was scheduled for tomorrow's lunch — mid-day, the books' counsel: alert hours, not the tired dusk, and days ahead for watching how the first course settles.

The tray was wiped that night with ceremony. The tiny spoon stood by like a christening ship.

Six months of watching the potatoes. The border was opening at noon.
