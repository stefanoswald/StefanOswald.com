Night 164

The Republic Expands

Month 6 — The Feast Begins

The launch completed itself in the sixth month's third week: the backward era ended not with ceremony but with a single recalibrated push — and she went forward. Toward the ring. On purpose. Arriving.

The steering committee had convened at last, and the republic required new maps within the day.

Forward crawling converts a baby from resident to citizen — destinations reachable, desires prosecutable, the gap between want and get collapsed to mere effort. She crossed the yes space in a rolling four-beat gait (the classic cross-crawl, opposite hand and knee, the pattern the physical therapists cherish for the brain-bridging it drills). She crossed it back. She spent one entire morning simply commuting, wealth beyond counting.

The house answered as houses must: the gate moved wide, the republic annexing the hallway's near province; the crawl-through audit rerun at the new borders (one philodendron deported to a hanging basket, one power strip walled behind the couch); and the floor's daily state upgraded from tidy-optional to swept-mandatory, pebble resolution, the mouth still serving as the republic's customs office.

And the page enters the era's golden rule as law: the best crawling terrain is boring, open, and hers. Not the walker contraptions (retired by the safety boards for good cause), not the seated containers idling the factory — floor, floor, floor, with things worth traveling toward placed at province distance.

The counsel for the watching heart rode in the margin: the crawl's arrival redistributes the baby, and some parents grieve the lap era's first thinning even while cheering the settler on. Both feelings are canon. The book has said it before and will again: every arrival is also a departure, and the parents' job is to wave at both.

By Friday she had found the kitchen, the promised land, tile cool under conquering palms.

The pot orchestra was down there. Somewhere a spoon waited.

The republic, fully mobile, played itself to sleep.
