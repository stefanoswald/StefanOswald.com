Night 183

The Door Problem

Month 7 — Explorers and Guardrails

The dropped-toy doctrine collected, this month, its foretold tax.

The truth published from the high chair — gone things persist — had generalized, as the page had warned it would, from rings to mothers. Elsewhere now existed. And elsewhere could be minded: Mama stepping into the bathroom was no longer a subtraction from the universe but a mother, continuing, behind wood, withheld. The door closed on a scream of pure jurisprudence: I know you're in there.

Separation feelings, arriving on the object-permanence schedule, right on time and — the page insists, against the era's 6 p.m. evidence — right on curriculum. She cried at departures now precisely because she had learned enough world to know departures were real. Graduation, wearing grief's costume.

The household doctrine, assembled from the practice-goodbye pages and ratified by a month of doors. Narrate the offstage: the voice through the door — "Mama's washing hands, back in a minute" — teaching that elsewhere has a soundtrack and a return policy. Keep the goodbyes short and sure per the standing rules: the wave, the words, the going — no doubling back, the double-back teaching only that grief works. Play the curriculum's own game hourly: peekaboo at the doorframe, the hide behind the couch, the thousand comic rehearsals of gone-then-back that build the bridge the real goodbyes walk on. And receive the reunions at their true size — the crawl-sprint to the returning parent, the boarding, the summit — because the reunion is the lesson landing: leaving is half of a shape.

The bathroom runs got easier by the month's end. Not silent — easier. The shape was building.

The page's last word goes to the parents' half of the ache, acknowledged: being grieved at every doorway is heavy freight, and also — hold both — the plainest possible evidence of a bond doing exactly what bonds are for.

She minded because it mattered. It would mind less as the proof accrued.

The proof was accruing. Nightly. Doorway by doorway.
