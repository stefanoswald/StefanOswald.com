Night 21

The Burp Symphony

Month 1: Arrival

Somewhere inside Olivia, after every meal, a small bubble of air looked for the exit and could not find the map.

So Papa became a musician. Up over the shoulder, cloth in place, and then the patting, firm as a friendly drum, gentle as everything else. Pat, pat, pat. Sometimes the sitting version instead: Olivia perched on his knee, chin cradled in his hand like a tiny thoughtful judge, while his other hand circled her back.

Some nights the burp came quick and enormous, a sound so large from someone so small that both parents applauded. Some nights it never came at all, and that was fine too, not every meal traps a bubble, and a comfortable baby need not be patted forever.

But the habit of the pause, meal, then quiet upright minutes, then bed, that stayed. It kept the milk down and the spit-up off the third shirt of the day. Mostly.

Tonight's burp arrived in the key of B flat, Papa decided.

"Bravo," he whispered. The audience was already asleep.
