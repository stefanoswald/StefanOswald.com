Night 171

Her Own Cupboard

Month 6 — The Feast Begins

The kitchen annexation had created a governance problem: dinner hour found the republic's citizen at ankle level, prosecuting every cabinet, while the state attempted soup above.

The house solved it the old way, the way this page recommends to every kitchen with a crawler: cede one cupboard entirely.

The low one by the pantry was emptied of its breakables and refitted for its new administration: two pots, the wooden spoons, a nest of steel bowls, the funnel of mysterious charisma, one dead whisk. The latch was removed from this door alone — every neighboring cabinet locked per the audit, but this one swinging free, hers, discoverable fresh each evening.

The effect ran better than the theory, and the theory was good. Each dinner hour she commuted to her office, flung the door, and governed: unpacked the holdings entire, banged the quarterly reports, nested the bowls wrongly with total conviction, wore the funnel briefly as a hat. Twenty minutes of sovereign business at Mama's ankle — present, occupied, safe, and (the deep clause) participating: she was doing kitchen, as far as her administration knew, exactly like the tall staff. The pots above and the pots below rang in rough ensemble, and dinner got cooked with both hands for the first time since the crawl.

The page's counsel, generalized: the yes space travels. One low drawer in the office-corner, one basket by the laundry, one shelf of hers in every province the family actually lives in — sovereignty distributed where the household happens, beating any toy corner exiled from the action. Babies do not want the toys. They want the verbs — the family's own doing, scaled to reach.

Her cupboard was repacked nightly by the janitorial state, wrongly, per her filing system, which had begun to have opinions.

Reopened at six sharp daily. Flung, unpacked, governed.

The soup upstairs never suffered again. The funnel never recovered its dignity. Fair trade, ruled all parties.
