Night 5

Skin and Song

Month 1 — Arrival

The evening was fussy and so was everyone in it. So Mama tried the oldest remedy on the shelf: she unwrapped Olivia to her diaper and laid her on her own heart, skin to skin, blanket over them both.

And there it was. Olivia's breathing found Mama's breathing. Her scribbly heartbeat smoothed out to match the steady drum below it. Warm chest, familiar thump — to a newborn, that is the sound of home, the first lullaby she ever heard from the inside.

Skin to skin steadies a baby's temperature, calms her heart, and settles her whole small weather system. It does the same for the person holding her, which is a secret nobody tells you.

Papa took a turn later, Olivia curled on his chest like a comma in a long sentence.

No skill required. No song required either, though he hummed one anyway, terribly, and she liked it very much.
