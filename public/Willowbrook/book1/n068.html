Night 68

Choosing the Village

Month 3 — Rhythm

The calendar said what calendars say: the leave was ending, the world wanted its parents back, and someone kind would need to hold Olivia for some hours of some days.

So began the choosing of the village — and this page holds the lantern for every family walking it.

They toured the sunny center in town where the babies napped in a row like loaves. They met the woman who kept four children in a house full of plants and patience. They did the arithmetic of grandparents, of shifted schedules, of Miss Rosa's offer to trade days. Every option had a price; only some prices were money.

The list that mattered, they found, was short. Warm hands and few babies per pair of them. Safe sleep done right — backs, bare cribs, no exceptions, no negotiation. People who talked to babies, not near them. And the feeling in the doorway: the one the checklists cannot hold, the yes or the no that arrives in the chest.

The guilt came too, uninvited, as it comes to all. The page says plainly: being cared for by more loving people is not less love. The village is not a substitute for the family. It is the family, wearing more faces.

They chose the plant-filled house. The chest said yes.

Two weeks of practice mornings would come first. The calendar, satisfied, turned its page. So did they.
