Night 140

The Almost-Crawl

Month 5 — Nearly Sitting, Nearly Everything

The position appeared this week, unannounced, in the middle of ordinary floor time: hands planted, knees under, belly — for the first time in her life — lifted clear of the earth.

And then: rocking. Forward, back. Forward, back. An engine revving with the brake still on, a rocket running its countdown and declining, every time, to launch.

The almost-crawl had arrived, and the fifth month is a common season for its debut — the strength assembled (five months of tummy-time bench presses, paying out at last), the wiring nearly so, the whole system rehearsing the motion it cannot yet ship. Some babies rock for days before first movement; many for weeks; and the movement, when it comes, ships in any direction — backward first as often as not, the launch reversed, the rocket arriving at the wrong wall astonished.

The parental arts of the almost: bait and patience. The ring placed just past the fingertips — close enough to burn, far enough to require the impossible — then the watching, without rescue, while frustration did its work. Because the frustration is the fuel; the page says so plainly. The baby annoyed at the unreachable toy is the baby building the reach. Rescue every reach and you rescue away the crawling.

(Within reason. The fourth failed lunge with tears attached gets the toy delivered and the campaign adjourned. Fuel, not despair.)

Tonight she rocked through half the toy rotation, growled once at the ring — a new sound, filed under determination — and collapsed grandly, spent.

The countdown continued. Launch expected within weeks.

The house, forewarned, began quietly moving the breakables to higher ground.
