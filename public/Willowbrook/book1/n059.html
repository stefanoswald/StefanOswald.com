Night 59

The Moon Hums Month Two Closed

Month 2 — Smiles and Sleep

High over Willowbrook, the Sleepy Moon was humming, which meant the month was nearly folded and put away.

Stella drifted down through the dark to the window on Maple Lane, as had become her habit and her honor. Second months, she had learned across many towns and many babies, are when the lights below steady themselves — fewer three a.m. lamps burning confused, more of them burning calm, doing the quiet work: a feed, a sway, a song flat in all the right places.

Inside this window: the crib, bare and correct. The bear on the dresser, one ear flopped, watch duly kept. The baby, bigger than last month by a full moon's worth, asleep with both hands — her new hands, the found ones — resting on her chest like a job well done.

"They learned the smile," Stella reported upward. "And the long sleep came once, and they didn't chase it, and it came again."

"Mmmmm," hummed the Moon, which is Moon for so it goes, month by month, for every family that ever was: badly, then barely, then beautifully, then all three again by Thursday.

Stella twinkled once, for yes.

Two months down in the town of Willowbrook. The watch continued. The hum went on, softer, softer, softest.

Goodnight, Willowbrook.
