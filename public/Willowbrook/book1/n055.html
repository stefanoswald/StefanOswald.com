Night 55

The Almost Laugh

Month 2: Smiles and Sleep

It lived somewhere south of a coo and north of a squeal, and it came out when the world was exactly funny enough.

The almost-laugh. A cough of joy. A hiccup with an opinion. Papa first mined one out with the puffed-cheek face, and instantly reorganized his entire life around getting another.

The kisses on the belly, almost-laugh. The slow loom in, nose landing on nose, almost-laugh, with kicking. The sock puppet's tragic monologue, nothing, a tough crowd, noted for the record.

Real laughter was coming; it arrives for most babies around three or four months, and when it lands the almost versions are instantly forgotten, the way sketches are forgotten when the painting is done. But this page votes to remember the sketches. The parent-weeks spent absurd and hopeful, performing for one, face after face, voice after voice, learning by pure trial what this particular person finds funny, that is not silliness. That is the first draft of her sense of humor, co-written nightly.

"She nearly laughed at the eyebrow thing," Papa reported at dinner, a man describing a miracle.

"Maybe," said Mama, hiding hers.

The eyebrow thing entered the permanent repertoire. Opening night was expected within the month.
