Night 28

A Letter From Your Bear

Month 1: Arrival

On the dresser, out of the crib and across the room, sat a honey-brown bear with one flopped ear, keeping the night watch. If bears could write, his letter would say:

Dear Olivia,

I am not allowed in your bed yet. This is correct. Your bed is for you alone, bare and boring and perfectly safe, and I am a professional, so I guard from the dresser. When you are big, past your first birthday and then some, I will move in, and you will chew my ear, and it will be an honor.

For now, my job is watching, and yours is growing, and your parents' job is everything else. Between us, they are doing better than they think. Tell them, when you learn how. Babies know these things first.

The moon comes by at eleven. The star checks in at three. You are, at all hours, guarded by an entire administration of soft and shining things.

Sleep, little one. I have the watch.

B. Bear, Dresser Division

Downstairs, nobody knew the nursery ran on such careful paperwork. But it did. All nurseries do.
