Night 284

The Echo Season

Month 10: High Summer

The month's language weather shifted, in its final week, from minting to echoing, and the household woke to find itself living with a parrot of considerable charm and improving aim: the echo season, opened.

The phenomenon, per the almanac's delighted field notes. The sentence's tail, caught and returned: "Time for your bath" "Ba!" "Look at the big truck" "Guck." "Say goodnight to the bear" "Beh." The catches running at the utterance's final stress per the era's universal acoustics (the last word of every sentence being, to the apprentice ear, the loudest lit window in it), the returns rendered in the household phonology's own editions and improving, the page certifies, at rates the ledger's chalk could no longer match: the echo season's coinage running not a word a week but a dozen attempts a day, most of them single-spend prototypes, the mint running hot.

The page files the season's linguistics at their working depth. The echo is speech's apprenticeship proper, the ear-to-mouth loop of the babble's old drills now running on live ammunition: each caught tail a full production cycle (heard, parsed, motor-planned, shipped) run at conversation's own speed, the apprentice manufacturing her pronunciation against the master copies at every exchange. The imitation department's whole year, the brushes and the phones, the cousin's studied jumps, arriving at its true headquarters: the mouth. And the season's standing counsel, per the market's oldest laws upgraded to the era's volume: the echoes received as speech entire (the "Guck" confirmed, "Truck! Yes, a big truck", the expansion craft of the holophrase pages now running at every catch), the household's own speech worth echoing (the narration's old documentary running cleaner takes now, the page notes, its narrators newly conscious of syndication), and the season's one caution chalked where the whole family read it: the parrot's range included everything, the sentence's tails caught at the market, the bench, the traffic's own commentary, the household's saltier registers, the page counsels with the veterans' knowing flatness, now broadcasting to an affiliate with no editorial standards and perfect timing.

(The almanac logs one such syndication, at the month's edge, at the dropped casserole: the tail caught, returned at full fidelity, and re-aired thereafter at intervals the household could neither predict nor survive with straight faces. The page declines to print the coin. The page notes only: the affiliate's first laugh-line, and the house's whole comedy season, renewed.)

"Goodnight, Olivia," ran the liturgy's last line at the season's own close.

"Nigh-nigh," returned the echo, at the rail, in the settling register, the catch of catches, the coin the whole ledger had been waiting on, chalked at dawn with the date and both hands' initials.

Nigh-nigh. The apprenticeship, speaking. The season, the page rules, the year's most voluble gift, every word the household had ever poured, coming back up the well.
