Night 359

Papa's Letter

Month 12 — The Candle's Country

The following night's latest hour held the table's second session — the lamp at the same low gold, the house at the same documented sleep — and the almanac's commission ran its twin: Papa's letter, at the tradition's own founding pair.

Entered, per the same permissions, entire.

"Dear Olivia —

Your mother wrote hers last night. I watched her fold it and I thought: I'm not going to be able to say it like she does. Then I thought: she'd tell me that's not the assignment. So here's mine, in my own registers.

I read you a seed catalog once, at two in the morning, in the cello voice, because the books said low sounds settle babies and the only thing in reach was turnips. You slept by page four. I have never in my life been prouder of anything, and I have met presidents of two companies.

Here is what I want you to know about your father, for the record, from the source: I was scared of you. Eight pounds, and I was scared. Not of dropping you — of not being enough of whatever fathers are supposed to be made of. And you fixed it, and you don't even know you did it, and here's how: you fell asleep on my chest, week two, hand around my thumb, and I understood all at once that 'enough' isn't a thing fathers are made of. It's a thing babies decide. You decided. You kept deciding, every day — the burp symphony, the airplane arms, the zoomies down the hall, da-da at the window while I stood outside pretending the car needed checking because my eyes were doing something unscheduled.

You walked into your mother's arms on a Wednesday and I missed it by one room and I have never once minded, because I've seen every step since, and there have been thousands, and I intend to see the rest — the school ones, the stage ones, the aisle one someday if you want one, the ones away from us that are the whole point. I'll be the one at the window. It's my post. I hold it.

The bear moves in next week. He sent letters all year — you'll find them someday, dresser division, good soldier. Be gentle with his ear. He's been practicing being soft. So have I, little one. So have I. That was the whole year, for me.

Happy first birthday, walker.

— Papa (first chair, your section, permanent)"

Folded. Sealed. Berthed at its sister's shelf: the pair, per the harbor's deepest registry, together.

Six nights. The tradition: paired, founded, permanent.

The letters would compound, per the page's whole certified forecast, at every candle of the shelf — and be read, per the asking years' own far paperwork, at exactly the right documented hour, twice.
