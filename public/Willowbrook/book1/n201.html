Night 201

The First Snow Touch

Month 7 — Explorers and Guardrails

The snow that had performed at the windows all month was, this bright Saturday, finally met in person.

The expedition suited up per the driveway doctrine's winter annex: the snowsuit (a garment that converted her to a small immobile star, tolerated under protest), the hat war's seasonal treaty honored, the boots that had never touched ground and mostly wouldn't. Papa carried the explorer out into a yard gone blank and blazing, the oak sketched in white, the whole familiar province translated overnight.

The first contact ran mittened: held horizontal, she was lowered until the mitten pressed the crust — the give of it, the creak, tracked with full committee focus. Then the page's small ceremony, recommended to every first winter: one mitten off, thirty seconds, the bare palm to the snow itself. The shock arrived on schedule — the cold's bite, the eyes going wide, the hand retracted and studied (wet! the mitten's whole purpose, published in one trial) — followed, on the second, voluntary, press, by the era's characteristic verdict: again.

A pinch of snow was offered for the customs office and processed with the lemon face's winter cousin. The snowball, demonstrated by Papa at gentle velocity against the fence, was received as the finest cannon fire in history and demanded — point, grunt, point — into repetition until his aim gave out.

Twelve minutes, all told, per the winter clause: outings brief, cheeks monitored, the shiver line never approached. The return indoors ran the classic sequence — the peeling of layers, the pink face emerging, the cocoa steam (hers a warmed milk in solidarity) — winter's full liturgy, first edition.

At the window afterward, pointing: snow, named, beam confirmed, refiled from window-theater to known country. Somewhere under the fence line, a hedgehog slept through the entire state visit, leaf-roofed, dreaming whatever the cautious dream.

The mitten dried on the radiator like a small flag.

First snow: touched. The winter, henceforth, was hers too.
