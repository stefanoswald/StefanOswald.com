Night 110

Peekaboo, Version One

Month 4: Rolling and Laughing

The oldest game in the world came to Maple Lane in its earliest edition, and the reviews were ecstatic.

Version one ran simply. Mama behind the burp cloth: gone. The world, briefly, minus its center. Then, "peekaboo!" restored, entire, smiling, exactly where the world ought to be. Olivia's reaction climbed the same ladder every round: the suspended stare, the held breath, and at the reveal, the full-body startle of delight, joy with a jump in it.

Here is what the cloth was actually teaching, under the giggles. At four months, gone is gone, a face vanished is a face subtracted from the universe, and its return is genuine news each time. The great truth called object permanence, the knowing that hidden things persist, is under construction all this half of the year, and peekaboo is its scaffolding: disappearance, suspense, return, a thousand gentle repetitions of the pattern that will someday hold at daycare doors, leaving is half of a shape.

Version one's rules: short hides, big reveals, quit while they're laughing. The advanced editions, her pulling the cloth, hiding herself, the endgame where she hides a toy and finds it herself, would ship over the coming months, each one marking new machinery come online.

Six rounds tonight. At round four she reached and yanked the cloth early, a beta tester ahead of the release schedule.

"Peekaboo," conceded Mama, found.

The center of the world laughed, restored to herself, twice over.
