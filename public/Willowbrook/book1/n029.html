Night 29

The Six-Diaper Salute

Month 1 — Arrival

In the first month, love is measured in strange units, and one of them is diapers.

Six or more wet ones a day — that was the golden number taped inside the changing table, the daily proof that milk was going in and the small machinery was running. Papa counted them like a lighthouse keeper logging ships. "Number four, right on schedule. Carry on."

The changing table had its own safety liturgy, recited in motion: one hand on the baby always, supplies within reach before you start, never a second's step away — babies choose exactly that second to learn rolling. Wipe front to back, air out the little bottom when it reddened, cream standing by like a small white fire brigade.

And somewhere along the way, changings stopped being chores. Eye to eye, narrating nonsense — now the LEFT sock, madam — Papa found they were conversations. Some of her first and favorite ones.

Diaper number six arrived at bedtime, punctual as a train.

Papa saluted. Olivia kicked him gently in the wrist, which he chose to take as a salute returned.
