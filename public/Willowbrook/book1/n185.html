Night 185

Cruising Season

Month 7: Explorers and Guardrails

Standing, once achieved, revealed its limitation within the week: the summit had no roads.

Then the discovery, one sideways step along the couch edge, hand over hand, the feet shuffling under the rigging, and the roads existed. Cruising season opened.

The couch became the coastal highway: end to end, hand over hand, a sock lost in transit and left where it fell, commerce refusing delay. The coffee table (padded at its corners since the audit's second pass) served as the roundabout. The gap between couch and armchair, one open foot of uncharted water, was studied for days like a strait on an admiral's map, and finally crossed: one hand releasing, the body tilting across the void, the far shore caught. The house heard about it at volume.

Cruising, the charts' bridge between standing and the unthinkable, and the page walks its engineering. The hands still hold the balance while the feet drill the steps, weight shifting, sideways first (all cruisers open sideways, the forward gear compiling later), each transit strengthening the exact hips and ankles that solo walking will lease. The furniture is the physical therapist, and the parental art is furnishing the practice: routes at rail height, gaps in graduating widths, the cushioned landing zones maintained, and the hands off, the cruiser hovered and steadied learns the hover, not the balance. Spotting distance, yes; scaffolding, no.

The shoe question surfaced on schedule and the page retires it as it retired the leg-bowing rumor: barefoot, indoors, the whole season, the feet reading the floor through their own soles being the balance curriculum entire, shoes waiting for pavement and weather, not for walking practice. Socks with grips for cold floors, and the one sock perpetually lost in transit accepted as the season's uniform.

By month's end the living room was fully charted: every rail-height edge a road, every gap a strait with a personal history.

The center of the room, open floor, no handholds, four steps of pure unthinkable, remained unclaimed, eyed daily from the coastal towns.

Spring, said the almanac's forecast, quietly. Perhaps sooner. Cruisers keep their own calendars too.
