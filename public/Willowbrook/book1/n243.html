Night 243

The Peepers Take the Night Shift

Month 8 — The Velcro Weeks and the Thaw

The spring nights in Willowbrook are staffed differently, and the eighth month's close found the new shift fully posted: the peepers in chorus from the brook's whole length, the swollen water running its melt-song underneath, and on the bank, conducting both with a reed baton and no mandate whatsoever, Freddy Frog in his lily-pad hat.

Stella came down low over the water for the season's handover, per the watch's rotation. The Sleepy Moon leaned in above, humming the receiving hum against the chorus, which obligingly hummed back.

"The winter file's closed," Stella reported, over the singing. "The ice's last dispatch went through in February. Since then it's been the walks — they never missed the light, that family; I've watched a lot of winters and they never missed the light — and now:" she ticked the spring's items on her points, star-fashion: "Standing free, thirteen seconds, self-risen. A no that lands. A balloon renewed its acquaintance out at the farm — the big one's back for the season, tell your waters. And the steps are close. Days, maybe. The willow wants word the moment there's word."

"The BROOK will know FIRST," announced Freddy from the bank, at the volume of his entire species and office. "The brook knows everything first. Water gets around."

"Mmmmm," hummed the Moon, which is Moon for he's not wrong, and also for it doesn't matter who knows first, small loud friend; it matters that the whole town leans toward that window; it has all year; listen to it lean.

The chorus rolled up the banks and over the lanes with the night's soft air — through the open window where two auditors slept on a closed eighth chapter; past the dresser where a bear held the watch and a settling balloon kept him company; over the crib where the citizen slept starfished at thirteen seconds' worth of new altitude, her whole spring loading around her: the steps, the words, the candle, the green weeks coming in on the melt-water like freight.

Stella took her post low over Maple Lane, the peepers singing under her like the town's own white-noise sea, spring edition.

"Any day now," she said, to the night in general.

"MAYBE!" conducted Freddy, magnificently, who had learned the word at some window or other. The chorus took it up. The brook carried it. The willow, up on its hill, let down its first green of the year and listened to the whole town sing the family's oldest word back to it.

Stella twinkled once, for yes.

Eight months down in the town of Willowbrook. The night shift singing. The steps inbound.

Goodnight, Willowbrook.
