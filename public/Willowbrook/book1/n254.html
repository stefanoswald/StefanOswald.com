Night 254

The Garden Bureau

Month 9 — The Edge of Everything

The spring's garden went in on the month's second Saturday, and the helping bureau — open since the coffee table's founding shift — took its muddiest posting yet: the earth itself.

The staffing plan, drafted per the bureau's charter: the citizen installed in the turned bed's corner, dressed for write-off per the mural-era doctrine, jurisdiction one square yard of warm crumbled earth — and the garden planted around her at the pace her participation set, which the charter had long since priced honestly: slower is the tuition; together is the degree.

The shift's log, filed by the senior partner between rows. The dirt, processed by full committee: gripped (both fists, released, regripped — the texture tour's richest exhibit to date), sifted (the pincer running trials on individual crumbs; customs sampling one, issuing the Betrayal Wing's earthiest review, resampling anyway per scientific rigor), and drummed (the flat-palm percussion of the bath's old kick factory, land edition). The tools, apprenticed: her own trowel issued per the second-cloth doctrine (deployed as drumstick, as scepter, once — the bureau's timesheet stars the entry — as a trowel, one scoop moved mostly toward its trench). The planting itself, the shift's ceremonial center: one tomato seedling installed by four hands — the hole hers-and-Mama's, the seedling steadied together, the earth firmed by two palms pressing under two guiding ones — the bureau's first living project, flagged in the row with its own stake, hers to inspect per the standing rounds all summer.

And the watering, the closing rite: the small can co-carried, tipped, the water's arrival at the seedling's foot received with the pond's own voltage — "duh!" (the vocabulary stretching, the category of wet things trading wide per the era's borders).

The page banks the bureau's horticultural finding for every household with a yard's worth of syllabus: the garden is the year's whole curriculum in one bed — texture and cause-and-effect, patience at the season's own pace, the helping bureau's realest work, and, waiting down the summer per the veterans' testimony, the harvest lesson no market teaches: the tomato, warm off the vine she watered, eaten in the row, the food chain walked at first-person scale.

(The almanac took the appointment: August, the stake's row, the customs office's verdict pending.)

The shift closed at nap's demand, one citizen carried in wearing the jurisdiction entire — knees, palms, one cheek, the write-off complete and the bath's afternoon session convened early.

The seedling stood its row, staked and firmed, first project of the bureau's first gardener.

Both, the page notes, planted this month. Both expected, per the season's whole arithmetic, to grow.
