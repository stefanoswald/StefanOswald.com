Night 113

Toes in the Brook

Month 4 — Rolling and Laughing

The hot afternoon walked them down to the brook, where Willowbrook keeps its coolness in public trust.

At the shallow pebbled edge, where the water runs two inches deep and has never once hurried, Papa crouched with Olivia and performed the season's sacrament: toes, dipped.

The first dip produced the full fourth-month gasp — eyes enormous, limbs starfished, the whole system pausing to classify the sensation. Cold. Moving. Alive somehow, tugging gently at the toes with a thousand small transparent fingers. The second dip was met with kicks; the third, with the pumping legs of a professional at her sport. The brook took the splashing kindly, as brooks do, having raised generations of Willowbrook toes.

Water outside the bath is a whole new subject — temperature and current and sparkle, the sound tuned so exactly to the shush of every white-noise machine that Olivia twice looked around for her bedroom. And the safety print, small but permanent: hands on the baby, always, in any water, of any depth; sun managed by the willow's shade; the outing ended while it was still a delight.

Ten minutes, then home by the shaded path, one baby heat-cured and half asleep, toes still curling around the memory.

Freddy Frog, from a lily pad downstream, had watched the entire ceremony without being observed himself.

"New swimmer," he noted, to no one official. The brook burbled agreement, and kept it on file.
