Night 220

The Food Strike

Month 8: The Velcro Weeks and the Thaw

The government, fed, governed, until the third week, when the government shut down.

It began with the sweet potato. The landslide food, the anchor of anchors, the orange administration itself: refused. Head turned, lips sealed, the plate regarded with the cold eye of a stranger. The next dinner extended the shutdown to the scramble. By the weekend the approved list had contracted to bread, cheese, blueberries, and, the page records it with the era's own bafflement, green beans, the Betrayal Wing's founding exhibit, now inexplicably in government.

The food strike, arriving on the eight-to-ten-month schedule like its nap-strike cousin, and the page runs the reframe before the panic: the strike is development, not disorder. The suspects, per the standing lineup: the autonomy project, first and largest, the whether and the how much being the only powers the high chair grants, and the eighth-month self exercising them precisely because they are hers (the spoon rebellion's politics, extended to the menu). The teething parade's next floats, gums preferring cold and soft on the swollen weeks. The novelty engine's strange reversals, loving and leaving foods on cycles no science has mapped. And the growth curve's own arithmetic: the first year's furious velocity easing now on schedule, the appetite easing honestly with it.

The doctrine, held through the shutdown with white knuckles and long views. The division of labor, unbroken: the plate kept arriving with the family's food, the strikers' list included but never the whole offer, and the whether left entirely to her, the page's hardest and most-repeated counsel: no coaxing, no airplanes, no dessert treaties, no short-order second kitchen. (Every negotiation, the literature is ruthless on this, teaches that refusal is currency; the calm plate teaches that food is just food, and returns to it are free.) The portions honest, a tablespoon per year of age being a serving, the parental eye being calibrated to adult plates and reliably double-counting the crisis. And the ledger kept weekly, not nightly: the strike-week's intake, totaled across seven days, came out, as the doctrine promised it would, roughly, mysteriously, sufficient.

The strike broke, as they do, without press release: the sweet potato accepted on an ordinary Thursday as if the shutdown had never occurred, the government's orange wing reopened without explanation or apology.

The gallery hung the era's finding where the family could see it at every future strike, and there would be future strikes, well into the toddler country and beyond:

The appetite is a weekly weather system. The plate is a standing offer. Hold the offer; skip the siege; trust the child.

Fourteen ballots for green beans, the exhibit added, in smaller print. Nobody knows anything. Keep hanging the show.
