Night 65

The Reach

Month 3 — Rhythm

Above the play mat hung a small felt star, and below it lay Olivia, plotting.

For weeks her arms had waved at the world on instinct, close but random, weather more than intention. Now something new gathered in her face. She looked at the star. She looked at her hand. Back at the star. And then — slowly, wobbling, mightily — the hand rose, steered, and swatted it.

The star swung wildly. The astronomer shrieked with power.

This is the reach, the third month's headline act: the first time seeing and wanting and doing line up in one deliberate arrow. It looks like swatting a felt star. It is actually the opening move of every skill she will ever own — the spoon, the pencil, the doorknob, the world.

Helping is easy and mostly means not helping. Hang things just barely in range. Let the misses happen; the misses are the curriculum. Cheer the swats.

She hit it four more times, each swing wilder with confidence, then held her own hand up and studied it — the tool, reconsidered, promoted.

The star swung. The baby gloated. Mission entirely accomplished.
