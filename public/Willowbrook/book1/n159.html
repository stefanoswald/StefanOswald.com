Night 159

The Six-Month Reckoning

Month 6 — The Feast Begins

The six-month visit convened, and the delegate attended sitting up — her own doing, her own tripod, her first appointment conducted from the vertical.

The numbers came in and were entered with ceremony: weight roughly doubled from the birth certificate, the classic six-month arithmetic; length up its stately inches; the marvelous head still on its own curve, climbing its own hill at its own correct speed, percentile unmarried as ever. The checklist ran its friendly gauntlet — the sit, certified live on the table; the reach and transfer, demonstrated upon the doctor's own stethoscope, confiscated twice; the babble chains, performed at volume for a captive clinician; the name-turn, the stranger-study (the doctor herself filed, inspected, provisionally cleared).

The conference held the feast report: solids landed, iron leading, allergens campaigning on calendar, gag drills passed, water apprenticed. Continue all fronts, said the doctor, and issued the era's advisories: the crawl imminent, proof onward and downward; the first tooth on approach — the drool consulted, the gums read like weather maps — with the fluoride counsel attached and the first dental visit chalked for the far side of the first crown or the first birthday, whichever led.

Then the shots — the six-month round, the third verse of the old song. The wail, sixty seconds, operatic. The nursing, the winding down, the parking-lot sleep of the twice-protected.

The page's note, six months deep: these visits had become something the first one wasn't. Not an exam survived — a conference attended, between people who had learned her curves and the professional who kept them, questions arriving on paper as always but fewer now needing asking. Half a year of expertise, accrued in the night shifts, recognized in daylight.

"Halfway to one," said the doctor at the door, the way doctors mark what parents cannot quite say aloud.

The delegate slept the whole drive home, medaled at the thigh, twice her original self in every way the scale knew — and several ways it didn't.
