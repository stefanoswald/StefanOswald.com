Night 84

The Saturday Adventure

Month 3 — Rhythm

Saturday morning belonged to the market, and this Saturday, the market got a new critic.

Papa wore Olivia through the stalls — close enough to kiss, face out now for stretches, her head steady enough this month to swivel at will. And swivel it did. The pyramid of oranges: reviewed. The flower stall's riot: reviewed at length. The fishmonger's bell, the bread smell, the general roar of neighborly commerce: absorbed by a brain doing its favorite thing, which is everything at once.

For a baby, an outing like this is not errands; it is the opera. New light, new faces at every range, a hundred sounds layered — rich sensory fare, and like rich fare, best in sensible portions. The rule Papa carried: watch for the doneness signs. The stare that goes glassy, the fuss climbing, the head burying into his chest — the critic announcing intermission. Then a quiet street, a swaying walk, the show dialed down to one rumbling voice and a heartbeat.

Forty minutes was the number today. Enough opera, then home by the hushed route, one orange richer.

Olivia slept before the corner, a critic overcome by the arts.

Her review, filed as one long exhale: five stars, too stimulating, will absolutely return.
