Night 210

The Thaw's First Rumor

Month 7 — Explorers and Guardrails

The rumor arrived on a bright cold Tuesday at the kitchen window, and its first witness was, fittingly, on point duty.

The eave's great icicle — all winter a fixed citizen, pointed at daily, filed and known — was dripping. Drop. Gathering, lensing the sun, letting go. Drop. The point shot up; the lookback fired — this is different, confirm — and the beam held on the icicle through six consecutive drops while the state provided the words: melting. The sun's warmer. Winter's getting tired.

The thaw's first rumor, and the house received it the way northern houses do: gratefully, skeptically, and with doctrine. (February's rumors retract, the almanac warned in the margin; two more snows came before the true melt, per tradition, each received by the window's monitor with proprietary interest.)

But the light itself had turned and the household rode it. The chased-light walks stretched with their asset — perceptibly longer each week now, the blue dusk pushed back past the anchor nap's wake-up, the streetlamp liturgy running later. The first bare-pavement stroller run in a month rang like a holiday, the wheels' old song remembered. At the park, the bench hosted its first thaw summit — Rosa, the strollers, the season's survivors' census taken in cocoa steam — and the two colleagues, eight months and eight months minus eleven days, sat propped face to face in their winter suits and ran their parallel studies at close range, the visiting-faculty effect now fully domestic.

And under it all, in the household's own weather, the same turn: the winter had been long, and had held, and the holding showed — a family coming out the far side of its first deep season with the craft proven and the cabin fever survived and something in both parents standing a little more unassisted too.

The icicle lasted the week, diminishing daily, reported on at every breakfast.

Its final drop went unwitnessed, per the way of such things, discovered gone one noon — pointed at anyway, the empty eave, filed.

Spring's whole rumor, in one wet spot on a windowsill. The monitor held the point a long time.

Coming, said the light, lengthening. Coming, said the eave. The almanac started a fresh page.
