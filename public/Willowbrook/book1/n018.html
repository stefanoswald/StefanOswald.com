Night 18

The Night Nest

Month 1 — Arrival

Olivia's bed was almost empty, and that was the finest thing about it.

One firm mattress. One fitted sheet, snug as a drum. And one baby, laid down on her back — always her back — in a sleep sack instead of loose blankets. No pillows, no bumpers, no charming stuffed bear; Bobby Bear kept his post on the dresser, on watch across the room, until she was much older. Beautiful cribs in catalogs are full of soft things. Safe cribs are boringly, wonderfully bare.

The crib stood in Mama and Papa's room for now, close enough to hear her breathe — same room, own bed, the way the doctors advise for these first months. The room sat cool and dim, warm-pajamas cool, not stuffy.

It looked plain as a summer field, that little bed. It was actually a fortress, built entirely out of things left out of it.

Olivia slept starfished like a tiny queen of nothing and everything.

The moon checked on her twice anyway. That is simply the moon's policy.
