Night 198

Clap, and So Big

Month 7 — Explorers and Guardrails

The wave, as forecast, was only the first delivery. The gesture wardrobe filled all month, garment by garment, and the house dressed for every occasion.

The clap arrived second: palms together, first in the slow ceremonial style (contact achieved roughly every other attempt, the misses swishing past each other like polite strangers), then compiling to true applause within the fortnight. It was deployed immediately as universal punctuation — for finished meals, for completed towers, for the dog through the window, for applause itself, the feedback loop discovered and run at capacity: she clapped, they clapped, she clapped at the clapping, civilization in miniature.

So big came third, taught at the farm per family tradition: "How big is Olivia?" — the arms flung wide overhead, the full wingspan — "Sooo big!" It was performed thereafter on request and frequently without one, a party trick that was also, the page notes, the era's whole developmental syllabus in one move: language comprehended (the question triggering the pose), ritual mastered, body commanded, audience worked.

The page files the science beneath the vaudeville. Gesture is language's advance guard — the research draws straight lines from the size of the first gesture wardrobe to the size of the coming word one — and each learned move is a conversation piece that works before the mouth can: the clap says I approve, the so-big says watch me, the wave says the shape of goodbye, the point says that one, the sign says more. She held, by the month's end, a working vocabulary of eight or nine hand-spoken words. The mouth, drilling its ba-ba chains in the wings, was months from matching it.

The parental art, as with the babble: answer everything. The clap returned, the so-big measured with awe, the applause loop run generously — every answered gesture confirming the great discovery underneath them all: I signal, and the world signals back.

The evening's finale ran the full wardrobe in sequence, unprompted: clap, so-big, wave, point at the moon through the frost-edged glass.

Standing ovation. Both shows. The wardrobe took its bow.
