Night 57

Eight Weeks of Hands

Month 2: Smiles and Sleep

The hands had been there all along, of course. Waving at the edges of her world like friendly strangers, swiping at faces, gripping fingers by old reflex.

But this week, Olivia found them. Truly found them, held one before her face and studied it, turning it slowly, an astronomer with a brand-new moon. Then the other. Then, in the discovery of the century, brought them together, and they held each other, and her whole body went still with the news: these are mine. Both of them. Mine.

Hand-finding is the second month's quiet milestone, the hinge everything later swings on: the reach, the grasp, the pincer, the crayon, the shoelace, the everything. It needs no equipment and no lessons, just stretches of unbundled time, arms free, on the mat or across a lap, for the astronomer to work.

The old newborn grip, the automatic one, was loosening now, right on schedule, making room for the deliberate version, the one where she would see a thing, want a thing, and send a hand out on purpose to fetch it. Weeks away. In training tonight.

Mama watched the two small hands negotiate like old diplomats.

"Introduce yourselves properly," she advised. "You're going to be working together for a very long time."
