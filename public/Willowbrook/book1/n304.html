Night 304

The Harvest Moon Convenes the Watch

Month 10: High Summer

The harvest moon rose at the month's last night per the season's own great amber liturgy, the year's largest rising, the willow's hill silhouetted entire, and the town's night watch, per a custom older than the town's own records, convened at the summons: the summer's closing session, called to order over Willowbrook at the moon's full documented gold.

The attendance, per the watch's whole registry. Stella, down from the long summer post at the session's own low altitude. The Sleepy Moon, presiding at the harvest edition's rank, the humming, the page notes, running tonight at the register the town reserved for its weighings. Oliver Owl, from the willow's oldest branch per his office, the half-moon spectacles catching the rising's amber at the questions' own angle. And below, per the summons' whole reach: the crickets at the ledger's closing recitation, the brook running the summer's file at its septembering volumes, the fireflies' late cohort rising at their season's last civic duty, and, the record certifies, at the far pasture's own dark, one red balloon at anchor above the willows' line, in attendance per the arrangement nobody at the farm had ever found remarkable.

The session's business: the summer's ledger, read and closed. The crickets ran the recitation per their office, the words minted (the column recited coin by coin, the nigh-nigh's entry receiving the assembly's own hum), the stones set, the tomato's yield, the goose egg's rainbow, the bear's first meal of nothing-and-everything (the item, the minutes note, receiving from the willow's own branches the gold letting-down reserved for the year's weighings), and the ledger, read entire, was closed at the moon's own seal: the summer of the walking, the talking, the sharing's first economies. Held, per the watch's verdict, entire.

And the session's second docket, per the harvest rising's whole point: the calling of the candle's season. Oliver Owl, at the office the willow's oldest branch existed for, asked it per his order's one liturgy, the question, which is always the blessing:

"And is the town ready? The first of her birthdays, the one they keep for themselves, since she'll keep none of it, the candle they light for their own first year. Is the town ready to hold it?"

The assembly's answer ran per each office's own instrument: the crickets' ledger-roll, the brook's downstream yes (which had known first, per its charter, and had been carrying the date all summer), the fireflies' late cohort spelling what fireflies spell, the balloon's far bob at the pasture's dark, and Stella, at the watch's own final word, low over Maple Lane where the small correct darkness held its sixty-one-nights-wide file:

"The town's been ready since the June it happened. The town, if we're being exact, was built for it."

"Mmmmm," hummed the harvest moon, at the presiding register, which is Moon, the whole watch could translate, for session closed; season called; light the amber and hold the watch: the candle's country is open, and the first citizen of it is asleep at her window, and all of it, all of it, held.

Stella twinkled once, for yes.

Ten months down in the town of Willowbrook. The harvest gold rising. The candle's season: convened.

Goodnight, Willowbrook, sixty-one nights wide, and counting, and held.
