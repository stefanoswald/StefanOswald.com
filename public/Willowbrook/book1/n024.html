Night 24

When the Crying Wins

Month 1 — Arrival

There came a night when nothing worked. Fed, dry, burped, swaddled, shushed, swayed — and Olivia cried on, and on, and the walls leaned in, and Papa felt something hot and helpless rise in his chest.

This is the page every parent lives, and here is what it says.

A baby is never hurt by being laid down safe. She can be hurt by arms that have run out of calm. So Papa did the strongest thing in the book: he laid Olivia in her bare crib, on her back, crying and completely safe, and he walked to the hallway and breathed for five slow minutes.

Never, ever shake a baby — no matter the hour, no matter the noise. Step away instead. Call someone. Tag in a partner, a neighbor, a grandmother, a friend.

When he came back, he was Papa again, not a storm wearing his shirt. He picked her up and swayed, and eventually — babies always land eventually — she slept.

Getting help is not failing. It is the assignment.

He whispered it to the dark, to remember: strong enough to step away.
