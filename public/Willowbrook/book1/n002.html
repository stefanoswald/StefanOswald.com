Night 2

The Language of Crying

Month 1 — Arrival

That night, Olivia cried. She had only one word, and this was it.

Papa walked the floor and listened, the way you listen to a song in a language you are just beginning to learn. A short, rhythmic cry — that one often means hungry. A whimpery, rubbing-eyes cry — tired, probably very. A fussy, squirmy cry — perhaps a bubble of air with nowhere to go.

He did not know yet which was which. That was all right. Nobody speaks Baby on the first night. You learn it the only way it can be learned: by showing up, again and again, until the sounds become sentences.

"I'm here," Papa said, which is the correct answer to every cry ever cried.

Olivia settled against his shoulder like a small warm loaf of bread. The moon leaned on the windowsill and listened too.

Fluent takes a while. Present, you can be tonight.
