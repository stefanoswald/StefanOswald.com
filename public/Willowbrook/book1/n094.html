Night 94

Ninety Minutes Becomes Two Hours

Month 4 — Rolling and Laughing

Quietly, without a press release, the window stretched.

Ninety minutes of good awake time had been the third month's number. Now Olivia ran a solid two hours between sleeps, sometimes a whisker more — and the day, given that extra half hour a stretch, reorganized itself like a room with a wall pushed back.

Suddenly there was time for the whole errand, not the half. The park visit and the bath. Play that developed chapters: mat, then mirror, then lap, then the grand finale of airplane rides, all inside one window with runway to spare.

The stretching is standard issue — wake windows grow all the first years, from the newborn's forty-five minutes to the toddler's afternoon-long marches — but the fourth month's stretch is the first one parents feel, because it hands back something they had forgotten owning: slack. The margin between activities. Tea drunk warm. The ambition, cautiously rediscovered, of doing two things in one morning.

The signs still governed, clock be advised: the stare, the ear-rub, the yawn drifting through like a leaf. Windows are weather, not law, shrinking on off days and after adventures.

But today the window held wide open to the last minute, and closed on schedule like a well-run shop.

Tea: finished. Warm. Both cups.

Civilization, returning in increments of thirty minutes.
