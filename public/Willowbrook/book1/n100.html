Night 100

One Hundred Nights

Month 4 — Rolling and Laughing

The hundredth night deserved a page of its own, so here it is — a small stone set in the road.

One hundred nights of stories read to someone who began by understanding none of the words and now, already, turned toward the reading voice like a plant toward a window. One hundred bedtimes: some silken, some sandpaper, a dozen abandoned mid-song for emergencies of digestion, every single one of them counted anyway, because the streak was never the point. The showing up was.

Parents wonder, in the early months, whether the nightly book is doing anything at all. The hundredth night is a good place to answer plainly: it is doing everything, invisibly. The data people have measured it — the language, the later reading, all of it downstream of laps and voices — but the realer proof sat in the crib tonight: a baby for whom the sequence bath-book-song had become the shape of safety itself, the hallway of open doors, walked nightly, ending always in the same soft room.

A ritual one hundred nights old is no longer a habit. It is a place. She would live in it for years and remember it, bodily, forever — the lamp, the lap, the voice going up the hills and down.

"One hundred," said Papa, closing tonight's book.

"Maybe," said Mama, smiling, meaning: and thousands more.

Outside, Stella flared briefly brighter, marking the stone. Stars respect round numbers; they are made of them.
