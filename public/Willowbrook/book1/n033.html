Night 33

The Conversation

Month 2: Smiles and Sleep

"Ah-goo," said Olivia.

"You don't say," said Papa, and waited.

Waiting was the trick, and he had just discovered it. When he answered and then paused, really paused, eyebrows up, floor open. Olivia gathered her whole small self and produced another sound. Then he spoke; then she spoke. Serve and return, back and forth, the true tennis of the second month.

Scientists have big words for this. Baby brains grow on turns the way gardens grow on rain: not on words poured over them, but on being answered. Every returned coo whispers you said something, and it mattered, and someone is on the other end of the line.

So the house filled with the strangest talk. Narrated laundry. Discussed weather. Long vowel arguments about nothing, conducted with enormous respect on both sides.

"Ah-gooooo," Olivia concluded, with a small fist for emphasis.

"Well argued," Papa admitted. "Motion carried."

The motion was bedtime. It passed, eventually, by one yawn to none.
