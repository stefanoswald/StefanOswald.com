Night 17

The Carrier

Month 1 — Arrival

Papa's arms were full-time employed, and his coffee had been cold since Tuesday. Then the carrier came out of the closet.

He read the checklist twice, out loud, like a pilot. Close enough to kiss — her head right below his chin. Face visible, never pressed against fabric. Chin off her chest, airway straight, back supported, knees tucked up like a little frog.

Then he stood up, and there it was: a warm, breathing backpack worn frontwards, and two free hands. He made a sandwich. He answered the door. He was, briefly, a wizard.

But the real discovery was hers. Held upright against his chest, Olivia rode the sway of his walking like a boat at anchor. His heartbeat under her ear, his voice rumbling through his ribs into her back. Carried babies often cry less; motion and closeness are their native country.

They walked to the corner and back as the streetlights woke up.

Both of them thought they were the one being carried.
