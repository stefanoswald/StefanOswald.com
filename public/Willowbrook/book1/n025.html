Night 25

The Laundry Mountain

Month 1: Arrival

On the couch there arose a mountain, and the mountain was made of laundry, and the laundry was unbelievably small.

Papa folded socks shorter than his thumb. How could someone eight pounds strong generate this? Six outfits a day, cloths beyond counting, and a sleep sack with mysterious opinions of its own.

He was learning the physics of the first month: the house will not be what it was, for a while, and the trick is to stop paying rent on the old standards. Good enough became the family motto. Clean enough. Fed, held, loved, the real checklist had only three boxes, and all were ticked.

The rest was triage, and triage had rules. Anything a visitor offers to do, say yes. Anything that can be done one-handed, done while swaying. Anything else, the mountain, mostly, can simply be moved to the chair.

He folded exactly eleven items, declared victory, and moved the mountain to the chair.

Beside him, the reason for it all made a small sound like a door creaking in a dollhouse, and slept on.
