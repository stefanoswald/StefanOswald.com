Night 263

Mama's Morning

Month 9 — The Edge of Everything

The spring's second Sunday arrived per the calendar's tenderest ordinance, and the household staged its first full observance: Mother's Day, inaugural administration edition.

The conspiracy, logged by its junior member's own almanac. The waking: managed — the dawn shift taken whole by the senior conspirator, the lark's five-forty summons intercepted, the bedroom's door held against all governance until the appointed hour. The breakfast: assembled at the conspirators' combined competence (the eggs his, the toast's char negotiable, the coffee correct — the page notes the menu mattered at the margin the gesture mattered at the center). The delivery: processional — the tray borne ahead, the citizen carried alongside in ceremonial dress (the festival outfit, third deployment) bearing the gift her office had spent the week's shifts preparing: one tulip from the garden bureau's own borders, held in the certified grip at half-crush, presented at the bedside per the offering economy's fullest protocols — extended, aimed, and released into her mother's hands with the "muh!" the vocabulary's fourth coin had been minted, as if on purpose, to spend.

(The card, in the senior conspirator's hand, held the junior's contribution per the era's custom: one page of authentic scribble — the crayon's first commissioned work — annotated: she says it's a duck. The almanac disputes nothing.)

And the day itself, run per the honoree's own petition, which the page records for its whole cohort of first-Mother's-Day households: not away from the family but in it, at zero logistics — the park's long morning, the picnic blanket's full session, the nap negotiated to overlap (the era's supreme luxury, engineered by the senior conspirator's schedule-craft), and one hour, mid-afternoon, of the quiet-ten-minutes doctrine at extended term: the honoree on the rug, the citizen at her rings, the being-with running at the year's own speed, unphotographed, entire.

The page holds the day's deeper file open a moment, because the first Mother's Day audits more than breakfast. A year ago — the honoree said it at the window, the evening's own entry — there had been no mother in this house; there had been a woman carrying a rumor up the stairs. The office had been assumed without ceremony, at 3 a.m., in a hundred unwitnessed inaugurations — the fog's feeds, the fevers' vigils, the guilt's hallway hearings, the craft compiled shift by shift — and the day existed, per the page's reading, to hold the one ceremony the office never staged for itself: the seeing. The tray, the tulip, the scribbled duck — the whole conspiracy's cargo was one sentence, delivered at last in the language the year had built:

We see you. The office is the house's whole ground. Happy first of many.

"Muh," concurred the junior conspirator, at the bedside, extending the tulip's second presentation (retrieved, re-gifted, the economy's finest custom).

Received, whole, again.

The tulip stood the week in the kitchen window's glass, half-crushed and sovereign, the administration's first state flower.

The office ran on, per its way, unceremonious by Monday.

The seeing, banked, ran on underneath. It always had. Now it had a Sunday.
