Night 75

The Rolling Commute

Month 3 — Rhythm

The first roll had been an accident. The tenth was a hobby. By this week, it was a commute.

Olivia had connected the dots: roll, and the world moves. The bear by the mat's edge, formerly decorative, was now a destination. Back to side, a mighty heave, side to tummy — pause, regroup, sphinx up, scout — then the return trip, tummy to back, which she had learned in the other order like most babies and performed now with a showman's flop.

Two rolls in sequence and she had traveled her own length. Three, and the mat had borders no more.

This is locomotion's first draft, and it rewrites the house rules on the spot. The mat needs open runway now — floor time in safe, cleared country, the best gift for a new roller. And the changing table enters its dangerous age: one hand on the baby always was the rule before; now it is scripture, and the floor becomes the wiser changing station on squirmy days. Sofas and beds, never solo, not for a moment — the moment is exactly what rollers are made of.

She reached the bear in four rolls, seized it, and bit it in triumph.

The commute home could wait. Traffic was terrible anyway, said her yawn.
