Night 125

Packing for the First Journey

Month 5: Nearly Sitting, Nearly Everything

The invitation had sat on the counter for a month, a cousin's wedding, two towns over, one night away, and the fifth month finally said yes to it.

The packing began three days early, which was the first lesson: travel with a baby is ninety percent logistics and ten percent travel, and the logistics reward respect. The list, handwritten and amended twice nightly, organized itself into the traveler's true categories.

The Absolutes: diapers in double ration, the medicine kit with the thermometer, the sleep sack that smelled correctly of home, the white-noise machine, portable sea, non-negotiable, and the bear, though he rode as cargo, cribs abroad being governed by the same bare rules as cribs at home.

The Feeding Department, packed to survive delay and spillage both. The Wardrobe, computed at outfits-per-day times two plus one, an equation written in spit-up long ago. And the Parent Provisions, the category first-time packers forget: the spare adult shirt, the snacks, the phone chargers, grace.

The wisdom gathered from veterans ran simpler than the list. Keep the bedtime liturgy portable and perform it wherever the crib stands, the routine is home, more than the address is. Expect the first night away to be a circus, and grade it as a circus, generously. And pack the morning-after coffee plan with the same rigor as the diapers.

The suitcase closed on the second try.

Down the hall, the unwitting traveler slept her regular hours, passport-free, her whole country coming with her.
