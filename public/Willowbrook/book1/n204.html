Night 204

Reading the Same Book Forever

Month 7: Explorers and Guardrails

The book basket held eleven volumes in good standing. The month, nevertheless, belonged to one.

The bear book. (The one where the bear loses his button and finds it. That is the entire plot. The page records it for posterity because posterity would otherwise not believe the numbers.) Selected nightly now, hauled from the basket, delivered to the lap, opened by force of will, and at its final page, raised again in the up-gesture that had come to mean the single most exhausting word in the parental lexicon: again.

Again. And again. Four consecutive readings the house record, set on a Tuesday, the record-holder's appetite visibly unslaked at curtain.

The page's mission tonight is the reframe every wearied reader deserves. Repetition is not a rut; it is the age's research method. The fourth reading is not the first reading repeated, it is a different study: round one for the shape of the thing, round twelve for the page-turn timing, round thirty for the pleasure of prediction itself (the gasp rehearsed before the button is found, she knows it's coming, and the knowing is the joy), round fifty for single words, lifted now from the stream because the stream finally stands still enough to fish. The vocabulary research is blunt on the matter: the same book seventeen times beats seventeen books once, the fixed text turning language into landmark. Fluent readers are built on worn-soft favorites, not broad fresh catalogs.

So the house adopted the standing rules of the again era, offered here to all its citizens. The again honored within reason, endurance budgeted at three per sitting, the fourth deferred to morning with full ceremony (the book itself tucked in beside the crib's exterior, a promise in cardboard). The performance kept honest, the pauses held, the gasp delivered, reading forty being reading one as far as the customer's rights extend. And the tattering embraced: the soft covers, the gummed corner, the spine's white creases, a book worn to velvet being, on any nursery's shelf, the visible shape of love and literacy in one object.

Reading four ran complete at full production values.

The button was found. The gasp preceded it by a full page, on schedule.

Again, said the up-gesture, at the dark.

Morning, said the tuck-in ceremony. The bear kept the watch beside the rail, buttoned, believed in.
