Night 70

The Pumping Chapter

Month 3: Rhythm

Some pages are less poetry, more plumbing. This is the pumping page, and it earns its place.

Mama was building the freezer stash for the working weeks, and learning the trade's unglamorous truths. That the pump is a colleague, not a judge: what it draws says little about what Olivia gets, babies being better engineers than machines. That output follows calm, a photo of Olivia, a warm drink, shoulders down from the ears, more than it follows effort. That morning is the generous hour, and adding one pump then builds a stash faster than heroics at midnight.

The logistics ran on labels and love. Date on every bottle. Freshest to the front... no, oldest to the front, first in, first out, like Grandpa Joe's hay. Small portions frozen, two and three ounces, because thawed milk keeps no secrets and wastes no forgiveness.

And Papa owned the operation's second half entirely: the washing, the drying rack like a small plastic forest, the freezer inventory kept in his head with the seriousness of a harbor master.

Not poetry, any of it. But open the freezer door: rows of labeled ounces, days of independence, banked by two people as a team.

That is its own kind of poem, actually. It rhymes with ready.
