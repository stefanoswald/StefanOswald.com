Night 138

The Rainy Day Orchestra

Month 5 — Nearly Sitting, Nearly Everything

The rain canceled the park, the walk, and the Tuesday summit, and by ten a.m. the house had the caged energy of a small zoo.

So Mama opened the low cupboard, and the orchestra was founded.

Three pots, inverted: the drum section. One wooden spoon, issued to the percussionist, who tested it first on the floor, then the pot, then — discovery of discoveries — the second pot, which rang differently. A metal bowl, tone bright; a plastic one, tone dull; the whisk, held by the conductor herself, mostly ceremonial. The rain kept exterior time on the windows.

The concert ran twenty-two minutes, which in fifth-month attention is a Wagner cycle.

The page behind the racket: the kitchen outperforms the toy catalog, and the fifth month is when the free instruments come into range. Cause and effect at full volume — I strike, therefore it clangs — plus tone, texture, weight, and the delicious governance of banging sanctioned by the state. The curation is the only work: nothing sharp, nothing glass, nothing small-parted, lids matched to pots for the coming era of putting-things-in-things.

And rainy days themselves earned their line in the almanac: the canceled day is not the lost day. The house, mined at floor level, holds a season of programming — the pot orchestra, the cushion mountains, the laundry-basket boat. Weather is a curriculum change, not a closure.

The finale was fortissimo, then facedown asleep on the rug at 11:15, spoon still in hand.

The rain played on solo through the nap, adequately, said the reviews.
