Night 161

The First Tooth

Month 6: The Feast Begins

It was found, as first teeth are, by accident, a click against the spoon at breakfast, a hard little edge where no edge had been.

Mama's finger went in for confirmation. There: the bottom front, right of center by a whisker, one serrated pearl breaching the gum like the first crocus through March ground. The tooth. The tooth! The house was informed at volume. Papa examined it twice and declared it the finest incisor in the county. The almanac took the date with a box drawn around it.

The bottom centrals lead the parade for most babies, this page notes for households consulting it mid-drool: commonly somewhere in the six-to-ten-month country, though the parade keeps its own schedule entirely, some arrive at four months, some march in past the first birthday, all of them fully punctual by their own almanac, and the late paraders lose nothing but waiting.

With the crowning came the new offices. The wiping: a soft cloth or the tiny brush, twice daily now, water and a rice-grain of fluoride paste, the habit installed while the habit is easy and the teeth are one. The vigilance: no bottles taken to bed, milk pooling on new enamel being the one true villain of the age. And the retrospective pardon: the previous fortnight's gravel nights and gnawed cribs rail, all charges now explained, all parties forgiven.

The teether corps was rechilled in celebration. The washcloth resumed its icebox shifts, needed on the far side now, the neighboring gum already swelling with the parade's next float.

Olivia bit the spoon again, on purpose, for the sound of it.

Click. Applause. Click. Applause.

Some instruments you're simply born to play, a little at a time.
