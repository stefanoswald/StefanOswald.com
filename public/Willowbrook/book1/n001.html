Night 1

Welcome Home, Little One

Month 1 — Arrival

The door of the little house on Maple Lane opened slowly, slowly, and Mama carried Olivia inside for the very first time. Papa set the bag down and forgot where he put it. Nobody minded.

The house did not know what to do either. The kettle stood very still. The lamp glowed a little softer than usual, the way lamps do when they are meeting someone important.

Mama did not tidy anything. Papa did not fix anything. They simply sat on the couch, the three of them, and breathed. In, and out. In, and out. That was the whole plan for the day, and it was exactly enough.

If the dishes wait, let them wait. Houses are patient. These first days are only for landing, like a leaf coming down to a quiet pond.

Olivia slept in the crook of an arm that was already learning her shape. Outside, the first star found the window.

Welcome home, little one. Welcome home, everyone. The whole family was brand new tonight.
