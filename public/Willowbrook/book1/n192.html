Night 192

The Mama Word Lands

Month 7 — Explorers and Guardrails

The sound had been in circulation since the fifth month — ma-ma-ma in the general chains, spent on lamps and bears and ceilings, the almanac declining certification every time the tender-hearted petitioned.

This month, the certification came through.

The evidence assembled the way the texts said it would: the sound narrowing its targets. Ma-ma at the bathroom door (the offstage address). Ma-ma from the crib at morning, ceasing when Papa entered, resuming — pointed, repeated, upgraded in volume — until the correct party arrived. And finally the morning that closed the case: both arms up from the crib rail, eyes locked on their target across the room, past Papa, through him: "Ma-ma." The reach and the word and the aim, all one arrow.

A name. Her first — the sound soldered at last to its person, spent now like a key instead of a song.

(Da-da landed within the fortnight, per the usual sequence's mercy, aimed and certified, and was received by its holder with a composure that fooled no one and required a minute at the window.)

The page holds the science lightly tonight: the first words land somewhere in this half-year's spread, ma-ma and da-da leading nearly everywhere on earth (the lip-consonants babies drill first happening to be the ones every language long ago assigned to its two most-summoned people — no accident, the linguists note; the babies chose those words, generations back, and the languages agreed). More words would trickle, then pour, across the coming year — the naming beam loading them nightly.

But the science was not what the house did with the morning. What the house did was stand at the crib rail getting summoned by name, twice, three times, the summoned party complying at each summons like the mechanism of a bell, both of them delighted by the machinery, ringing it and ringing it.

First the smile aimed. Then the laugh shared. Then the point. Now the name.

Person, calling person. The whole project of language, and possibly of everything, arriving where it always arrives: at somebody in particular.

The almanac's entry ran one line, boxed, in the hand of the named:

Ma-ma. Aimed. Me.
