Night 336

The Remember Game, Part One

Month 12 — The Candle's Country

The remember game was founded — per the way of the household's best institutions — by accident, at the liturgy's book station, when the album (shelved per its doctrine at the basket's reach) was hauled lap-ward at the reader's own franchise in the bear book's standing berth.

"Dis?" at the opening leaf: the hospital band's impossible circumference.

And Mama, per the market's eternal law, answered — and the answering, the page certifies at the institution's founding, became the month's own liturgy: the year, told back, as a bedtime story, to its own subject.

Part one, as the founding session ran it: the fog months, translated at the lamp's gold. "Once upon a time — this is a true one; the true ones are the best ones — you came home to this house so small the bear was bigger than you. You cried in the only word you had, and we learned it. We folded you like a little letter — fold, fold, tuck — and you slept on our hearts, skin to skin, and your heartbeat learned its manners from ours." The album's leaves turned at the teller's pace: the swaddled editions, the three a.m. kitchen's grainy survivor ("Papa lived in this picture; he was very tired and very rich"), the first bath's astonished monk, week nine's underlined SHE SMILED — "and one Tuesday, over a diaper, you smiled at me on purpose, and I made a sound I had never made before, and we never got over it. We still haven't."

The audience's own offices, logged at the session's term: the leaves consulted at the "dis?" economy's full rates, the "baby" coin spent at every early edition per the changeover's founding poetry, and the telling received — the page marks the institution's whole deep mechanism — at the register children keep for exactly this: the story of themselves, told by the people who were there, at the hour the day lies down.

The page charters the game for the whole shelf ahead, per the veterans' testimony and the far books' own standing repertoires: the remember game — the family's origin liturgy, founded at the album's December lap and run at every age's own editions for decades: the tell-me-about-when-I-was-a-baby of the toddler volumes, the birthday-eve retellings, the someday-far editions told across kitchen tables to listeners taller than the tellers — the year's whole documented country converting, at the lamp's gold, into the thing it had always been becoming: the family's own first story, hers, told and retold at the only rates stories keep: together, at bedtime, true.

"More," petitioned the subject at part one's close, per the phrase's own founding office.

Tomorrow, honored the tellers, per the serial's whole ancient craft. Part two: the laughing season.

Twenty-nine nights. The game: founded. The story: hers, telling.
