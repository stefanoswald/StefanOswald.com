Night 209

The Sick Week

Month 7: Explorers and Guardrails

The season's second illness came in heavier than the first, the winter's proper virus, fever and cough and misery in full kit, and ran the house for six days. The page keeps this one not for its doctrine, already banked, but for what the week itself was.

The doctrine performed as built. The tripwire list consulted nightly and never crossed, though twice approached (the breathing watched at two a.m. with the old rise-and-fall vigilance of the first month, the count taken, the line held). The pediatrician's nurse, called twice, patient both times per the standing assurance. The fluids pressed in milliliters, the fever managed for comfort, the humidifier running its faithful mist. The shifts, rotated. The soup, made and mostly unmanaged.

But the week's true record is the other one. The mattress dragged to the nursery floor on night two, because the monitor was not enough and the chair was not sustainable, and a parent slept, the word used loosely, beside the crib like a harbor pilot bunked by the channel. The showers skipped and the work called off, the world's other claims discovering, again, their actual rank. The particular weight of a sick baby asleep on a chest, heavier than her healthy weight by some physics no scale explains, and the particular stillness of the parent underneath, breathing shallow so as not to disturb the harder breathing above.

And the sixth morning. Every parent knows the sixth morning: the waking to suspicious silence, the padding down the hall braced for anything, and the finding, at the rail, of a citizen standing, hollering the morning address at full restored volume, thin and bright-eyed and ravenous, demanding the world resume.

The world resumed. The mattress went back upstairs. The almanac took the week in one line and the line held everything: Sick week. Long one. She's up. We're old. All fine.

The page adds only its blessing, for every family reading this from a mattress on a nursery floor: the watch you are keeping has been kept by everyone before you and will hold like theirs did. Six mornings are survivable. The sixth one is coming.

It comes.
