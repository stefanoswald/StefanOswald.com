Night 34

The Sleepy Signs

Month 2: Smiles and Sleep

Olivia sent letters before every sleep, and Mama was learning to read the mail.

First letter: staring away, into the middle distance, done with faces for now. Second: the slow ear-rub, the eye-rub, fists like little polishing cloths. Third: the yawn. Fourth letter, always in red ink: overtired, wired, arch-backed, crying at the very sleep she needed.

The art was catching the mail early. A baby tucked in at the first rubs drifts off like a boat slipping a dock. Miss the window, and the same baby fights bedtime like it insulted her family, tired bodies brew a second wind that is all storm.

At this age the windows came quick: an hour or so of happy awake time, sometimes less, then the letters began.

So evenings turned into gentle espionage. Mama watched for the stare, folded the day up early, dimmed the room before the red-ink letter could be written.

Tonight she caught the very first yawn, mid-arrival.

"Message received," she whispered, and the mail went to bed on time.
