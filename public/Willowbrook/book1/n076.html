Night 76

First Trip to Dandelion Park

Month 3: Rhythm

The park had been three blocks away all along, waiting the way parks do.

Mama spread the blanket under the big oak at Dandelion Park, laid Olivia on her back upon it, and prepared to entertain. No entertainment was required. Overhead, the leaves were doing the thing leaves do, ten thousand green flags in a slow wind, light spilling through in coins and patches, and Olivia watched the show with her whole face, arms stilled, utterly employed.

Twenty minutes. The wind section, the leaf ballet, one bird crossing stage left. The best baby television ever aired, in continuous production since forests began.

This page's advice is nearly this simple: go outside and lie down. The park asks nothing, no gear beyond a blanket and a hat, no skills, no schedule. And it gives lavishly: new light for new eyes, air that improves naps like a spice, and for the parent, sky instead of ceiling, which the inside of a third month badly needs.

Other blankets dotted the grass, other parents upon them, a nod passing between them like members of a quiet union.

A dandelion seed drifted down and landed on Olivia's blanket, first of thousands.

The oak approved of her. Trees can tell who is going to climb them someday.
