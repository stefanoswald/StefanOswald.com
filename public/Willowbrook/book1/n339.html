The Remember Game, Part Four

Month 12 — The Candle's Country

Part four — the serial's closing session, per the contract's own last billing — took the lamp at the album's final filled leaves: the talking months, told to their own present.

The telling, at the closing's gold. "And then came the summer you started talking back. Duck, first — at the pond, on a Tuesday, and eleven ducks confirmed it in chorus; we heard them. Then ball, and dog, and more — you signed it first, with your little hands, and then one day you just said it, and the whole kitchen stopped. You named the bear Baba and he took it very well; he takes everything very well; he's moving in soon, he sent letters. You learned no — " the tellers' pause, per the honest registers — "and we celebrated, which confused the aunts. You learned hot and gentle and home. You said home at the table, and at the hill, at the town's own tree, and the tree kept it; trees keep the important ones. And now —" the leaf at the album's last filled berth: the bee's October wings, the gratitude table's four-generation gold — "now you say one. Because that's what you're about to be."

And the closing's own duet, run at the game's whole compiled mechanism: the coins supplied at every retold station per the cast's certified inventory — the "duck" at the pond's leaf, the "Baba" aimed dresser-ward at the letters' own mention (the watch, the page notes, receiving the citation at his standing composure, one flopped ear at the lamp's gold), the "home" at the willow's photographed hill — the year's telling arriving at its own present tense: the story caught up to its subject, the serial's whole country walked to the very leaf on which the tellers and the told now sat, telling it.

The last page, held at the session's close per the album's own pending doctrine: the candle's blank leaf, the advance scribble at its caption, the translation beneath — one, it says; she says it's a duck; it's one — shown at the lamp's deepest gold and told at the contract's final term: "And this page is empty, because it hasn't happened yet. It happens in twenty-six nights. And then we'll tell it too. We'll tell all of it, forever. That's the whole game."

"More," petitioned the cast, at the blank leaf, per the founding office.

"Yes," honored the tellers, at the dialect's entire register. "Exactly. More. That's the other book. There are six more books, little one. There's a whole shelf."

Twenty-six nights. The serial: complete to its present. The game: permanent.

The blank leaf: waiting, scribble-captioned, at the candle's own term — and the telling, per the whole institution's charter, only ever beginning.
