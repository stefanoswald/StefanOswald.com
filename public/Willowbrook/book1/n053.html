Night 53

The Long Stretch

Month 2: Smiles and Sleep

Mama woke with a start, because nothing had woken her.

Light, real, yellow, morning light, lay across the bed. The clock said 5:40. The last feed had ended at eleven. She did the arithmetic twice, disbelieving it politely, then leaned over the crib rail with her heart tapping.

Starfish position. Chest rising, falling. Face of someone extremely committed to her current project. Olivia had slept six and a half hours, and the world had simply... permitted it.

Around the second and third month, this begins: the night organizing itself into one long anchor stretch, usually the first shift of the night. It is not owed, some babies file the paperwork months later, and growth spurts repossess it without notice, but when it comes, it comes like this, unannounced, leaving a parent standing in morning light feeling smuggled into a new country.

Two notes ride along with the miracle. Take the early bedtime seriously, because the long stretch spends itself in the first hours, parents who go down soon after the baby collect the inheritance. And expect the encore to be shorter; one long night is a preview, not a contract.

Olivia woke at six, ravenous and radiant, having done nothing unusual at all in her own opinion.

Mama had coffee in the sunshine. Actual sunshine. It tasted like the future.
