Night 6

The Kitchen at Three A.M.

Month 1 — Arrival

There is a country only parents know. It is called Three in the Morning, and Papa was its newest citizen.

He stood in the kitchen swaying — parents sway even when the baby is in the other room — and fed Olivia while the refrigerator hummed its one long note.

Here is what he learned in that quiet country: night feeds go easier when everything is ready before bed. Water where you can reach it. Burp cloth on the chair arm. Lights that can whisper instead of shout.

And he learned the stranger thing too: that these bleary hours have a secret sweetness. The world entirely asleep, one small person entirely awake in your arms, trusting you completely. There will come a night — you will not know it is the last one — when nobody needs you at three a.m. anymore.

Papa yawned an enormous yawn. Olivia finished, burped like a tiny sailor, and slept.

Citizens of that country are very tired, and very rich.
