Night 251

Duck

Month 9: The Edge of Everything

The warehouse doors, rattling since the crib's dark "dog" of the naming-market era, opened on a Tuesday at the pond.

The scene, entered in the almanac at the step's own rank. The morning walk's pond station, the stroller docked, the resident pointing per custom at the water's traffic, and then, aimed down the point's own line, in the clear carrying register of her morning addresses:

"Duh. Duck."

A beat of parental stillness, per the miracle-weather rules. Then the confirmation protocol, run with a steadiness Mama did not feel: "Duck. Yes. Duck!" And the speaker, satisfied, re-aimed and re-issued, "duck", at the next duck. And the next. The pond held eleven ducks that morning and every one of them was named, individually, the word spent like new-minted coin at a market that could not stop honoring it.

The first true noun. The page marks its papers precisely, because the era files them fast now: mama and dada had landed in the winter, names for her two fixed stars; uh-oh had labeled events; the "hah" of the hot treaty ran a warning's office. Duck was new business entire, a word for a thing in the world, aimed at the category (all eleven, plus the bathtub's Esquire at that evening's session, plus the bear book's mallard spread, located and prosecuted at bedtime), portable, spendable, hers. The naming market's year of stocked warehouse, the dozens of understood words banked behind the pointing finger, had shipped its first outbound freight.

The forecast, posted for the households at their own ponds: the first words trickle, duck was followed at day-intervals by ball, then the long-suspected dog certified at last, then more (the sign's spoken edition, arriving to double service), the trickle running one-a-week-ish through the birthday country, the pace unremarkable at any speed per the charts' widest windows of the whole raising enterprise. The explosion, the warehouse's full shipping season, the naming of everything, waits in the toddler book's own pages, months out, on her almanac as ever.

And the counsel, unchanged since the babble's first serve: answer everything. Every duck confirmed, every aimed syllable banked and returned with interest, the market's oldest law, you said something, it mattered, someone is on the other end, now trading in actual words.

The pond was renamed that morning, as far as the household's maps run: Duck. The whole park, in time, would go the same way, the swings, the oak, the bench all awaiting their own Tuesdays.

Eleven ducks, confirmed in chorus from the water.

The first coin, spent eleven times, devalued zero.

The warehouse doors stood open. Everything in the world, the page notes, was in there, waiting, labeled, hers.

Duck. So it begins.
