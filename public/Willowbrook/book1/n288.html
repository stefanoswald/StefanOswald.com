Night 288

The Stroller Negotiations

Month 10 — High Summer

The stroller — the household's oldest vehicle, the lane's daily ferry since the ninth day of its passenger's life — faced at the tenth month the constitutional challenge the walking had made inevitable: the passenger, per the sovereignty project's whole summer platform, no longer wished to be one.

The negotiations, as the week's walks conducted them. The boarding refused per the feet's established grammar — the back arched at the harness per the era's parliamentary style, the petition's terms plain: the walker walks. The counter-terms, per the state's own non-negotiables: the lane's distances running past any ten-month range, the errands' clocks real, the street's borders absolute per the coming curriculum's oldest cliff. And the settlement, drafted at the lane's own pace per the two-yeses craft the shoe standoff had chalked: the walk's new constitution, in three articles.

Article one, the walker's franchise: the route's safe stretches — the block's long middles, the park's paths, the driveway's whole nation — walked, hand-escorted or free per the terrain's grading, at the pace the new gait set, which was, the page certifies, not a pace at all but a progress: the pedestrian era's true speed being the archaeology the pavement pages forecast — every pebble petitioned, every fence slat consulted, every gate's hinge tested at the point's full lock, the block's one-time five minutes running twenty at the surveyor's own rates. (The page's counsel, chalked for every hurrying household: budget for it. The slow walk is the walk now — the errand's clock and the child's curriculum running at incompatible speeds, and the wise calendar buying the twenty minutes rather than spending the whole walk at extraction's rates. The destination, the veterans' law runs, was always the smallest thing on the route.)

Article two, the vehicle's retained offices: the distances, the tired legs' honest ends (the walk's last third boarding at rates the pride declined to log), the street crossings' absolute boardings per the cliff statutes — the harness run at the acknowledge-and-proceed craft where the parliament objected, the minutes read aloud, the business conducted.

And article three, the settlement's masterstroke, drafted by the citizen herself at the week's third session: the pushing — the stroller, empty, propelled by its former passenger at the handle's own altitude (the grip achieved at tiptoe's full extension, the state's finger providing covert rudder), the vehicle converted per the push-wagon's whole physics into the walk's own engine: half a block of triumphant freight, the lane's porches applauding per their year-long custom, the vehicle's constitution ratified by the one amendment nobody had drafted — that the ferry's next era, per the sovereignty project's deepest terms, was hers to drive.

"Puh!" minted the driver at the handle, mid-block — push, the ledger ruled, chalking it.

Push, honored the lane, at the summer's own gold rates.

The negotiations closed at the season's end with all parties' dignities intact and the walks, per the new constitution, twice as long and four times as slow and — both signatories concurred at the almanac's entry — better.

The archaeology ran for years. The pebbles, the page certifies, never once ran out.
