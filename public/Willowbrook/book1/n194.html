Night 194

The Winter Fortress of Cozy

Month 7: Explorers and Guardrails

The first true snow shut the town for a Tuesday, and the house, unable to chase the light, built against the dark instead.

The fortress rose mid-morning: couch cushions ramparted, the good blanket roofed across to the armchair, the lamp relocated to cast its gold through one gap, a cave of the kind fathers have engineered since couches were invented, scaled this year for a citizen of seven months. She was admitted at the flap and underwent, visibly, the ancient effect: the world reduced to one warm pocket, sounds softened, light honeyed, everything reachable and everything hers. The republic, indoors, in exile, in glory.

Camp ran all day on winter programming. The book basket, relocated wholesale into the cave, worked through twice in shuffle mode. The pot orchestra, guest-performing in the annex. The crackle paper's ceremonial remnant. Peekaboo at the flap, at the era's advanced difficulty settings. And stretches, this the page underlines, of nothing scheduled at all: the baby working her blocks in the lamp gold, the father horizontal at the flap on perimeter guard, half-reading, the snow doing its slow work at the glass. Boredom, the winter's rumored menace, never filed a single report.

The page's finding, for the season's many shut-in Tuesdays: the indoor day is not the outdoor day's poor substitute but its own country, and small children are its native citizens. The house at floor level holds programming for months of Tuesdays, the fort, the cupboard, the stairs academy, the window's own theater (snow: pointed at, named, beam confirmed, filed with dog and moon). The cabin fever the parents rightly fear is chased by structure, not square footage: two anchors a day, a fort raised, a bath brought forward, a video call with a grandfather placed from inside a blanket cave, and the shut-in day acquires a shape, and shapes are what days are for.

The fortress stood till bath time, dismantled under protest, one wall left standing by treaty for tomorrow.

Snow continued at the windows, unpointed-at for whole minutes, the pointer asleep.

Winter, the house concluded, would be survivable at this rate. Cozy, the old northern nations insist, is a verb. The house conjugated it all season.
