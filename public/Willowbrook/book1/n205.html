Night 205

The Cousins' Visit

Month 7 — Explorers and Guardrails

The holidays shipped their traditional cargo mid-month: the far aunts, the pie, and — the visit's main event as far as the resident scholar was concerned — a cousin. Aged two and a half. Mobile beyond all local precedent.

The scholar's week was made and reorganized on contact.

From the blanket base she tracked him like coursework: the running (running!), the jumping-adjacent activities, the block tower built at heights her administration had never budgeted, the vocabulary deployed in bursts (whole words! aimed! working!). The point ran hot all week; the lookback wore a groove — are you seeing this — and the imitation department, that quiet engine the texts rank above every toy in the catalog, ran double shifts: the almanac logged a first attempted jump (both knees flexed at once from sitting, achieving nothing, repeated with conviction), a new syllable suspiciously matching the cousin's favorite, and the block tower ambitions revised permanently upward.

The page files the finding under the season's heading: older children are the best faculty. The research is settled and the grandmothers concur again — the just-older child, doing just-reachable things, pulls development like a tide: close enough to copy, advanced enough to stretch toward. Willowbrook's arrangements would supply the faculty in permanent posts soon enough (a certain colleague eleven days younger two streets over would grow into co-professor; the far cousin held the visiting chair).

The week's other syllabus ran for the parents: the household of two children, previewed. The pace, the referee work (the cousin's generous-but-sovereign relationship to the block supply requiring occasional diplomacy), the bedtime liturgy performed in stereo down two hallways. They took notes of their own, filed under someday, maybe, exchanged over the dishes with the eyebrow dialect.

Departure day ran the full ceremony: the wave deployed at length in the driveway, the horn answered, the house echoing strangely large all afternoon.

The tower record stood at six blocks by New Year's — cousin's coaching, scholar's execution.

Faculty visit: concluded. Curriculum: permanently revised. The blanket base was never fully believed in again.
