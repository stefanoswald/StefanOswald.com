Night 83

Bobby Bear's Second Letter

Month 3 — Rhythm

Dear Olivia,

Three months into my watch, and the paperwork has piled up. Herewith, the quarterly report of B. Bear, Dresser Division.

You laughed. I was there; it was a Tuesday; the belly kiss did it. I have heard many first laughs from many dressers, and I rank yours in the top one.

You rolled. Twice the first day, forty times since. I logged each one. The mat's corners, formerly the edge of your nation, have fallen. I recommend the authorities clear the lower shelves, as expansion appears imminent.

You found your hands, and then found that they obey you. This is bigger news than the newspapers ran. Everything you will ever hold — every ball, every crayon, every hand — was signed for this month, in advance, by those fists.

Your parents have steadied, if you had not noticed from your altitude. They laugh more at midnight and check the breathing slightly less, which is how healing looks on grown people.

As for me: the dresser is drafty and the honor is total. They say when you are one, I move in with you. I am practicing being soft.

Grow on, little one. I have the watch.

— B. Bear

The moon countersigned, as required. All quarterly reports require the moon.
