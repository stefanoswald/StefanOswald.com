Night 87

Three Months of Nights

Month 3 — Rhythm

Somewhere around now, the nights turned into craft.

Not easy — craft. The difference showed in the hands. Watch Mama run the late shift tonight: the sleep sack zipped in one pass, the lights already low because they were never raised, the feed taken in the quiet chair with the practiced pillow, the burp drawn out with two minutes of patient jazz, the lowering into the crib performed in the old smooth motion — bottom first, then the head, hands sliding free like a magician finishing.

Three months ago, every step of that was a cliff. Tonight it was a path, walked in the dark, known by the feet.

This page marks the milestone no chart lists: parental competence, the quiet kind, assembled entirely from repetitions nobody applauded. A thousand small motions, each learned badly, then less badly, then so well they disappeared into the hands.

The nights themselves had organized too — the long first stretch mostly holding, one honest feed in the small hours, dawn arriving more politely than it used to. Not every night. Enough nights.

Olivia stirred once, found the world correctly arranged, and went back down without opening a case.

Mama stood a moment in the doorway, master of a trade she had never wanted to need, now quietly proud of.

The moon, an old journeyman itself, tipped its light. Craft recognizes craft.
