Night 237

The Hour That Moved

Month 8 — The Velcro Weeks and the Thaw

The spring delivered, alongside its festivals and thaws, its one piece of pure administrative weather: the clocks sprang forward, and the household's smallest citizen — who had never met a clock and ran entirely on the sun and her own machinery — woke into a world that had moved breakfast.

The page files the survival doctrine, tested this month and re-tested every spring and fall of the raising years.

The problem, stated honestly: the five a.m. lark's dawn shift became, overnight and by decree, a six a.m. shift — the transition's one genuine gift, gratefully banked — but bedtime's far end tore: seven p.m. arriving at what every cell of her insisted was six, the sleep pressure an hour short of the liturgy, the crib met with the fresh outrage of the genuinely not-tired. The first sprung night ran forty minutes of protest and the second thirty-five, the body's clock conceding nothing to legislation.

The doctrine, applied from the third night per the veteran counsel the house had banked at the bench: the slide. Fifteen-minute installments — bedtime at 7:15's-worth-of-old-time, held two nights; then 7:30's; the naps and meals sliding on the same gentle schedule, the whole architecture walked across the gap in quarter-hour steps rather than thrown. The light enlisted as ally rather than saboteur: the blackout curtains (the early-waking era's investment, compounding again) holding the evening's new brightness out of the argument entirely, and the mornings opened bright and early per the day-is-bright doctrine of the very first month — the small clock winding, as it has wound since the fourth trimester, on light and dark and the household's steady order.

Eight days, the crossing took, per the almanac's log — the standard rate, the page notes, being roughly a week per hour moved, in either direction, at every age including the parents'.

And the doctrine's deeper line, entered while the household straddled its two times: the clock is the newest and least of the family's instruments. The liturgy's sequence — bath, book, song, the hallway of doors — carried the crossing entire, portable across time zones and legislatures alike per the travel pages' old finding: the routine is home; the hour is furniture.

The eighth night ran on the new time, unprotested, the lark reporting at six sharp per the season's improved contract.

The kitchen clock and the crib, reconciled, resumed relations.

Until October, noted the almanac, which had already started that page.
