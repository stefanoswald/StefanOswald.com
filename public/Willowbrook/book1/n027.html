Night 27

The Photo Nobody Posed For

Month 1: Arrival

There were photos already, hundreds, somehow. Olivia in the hat. Olivia meeting her aunt. Olivia unimpressed by a professional pumpkin.

But the true picture of the first month was one nobody took.

Ten o'clock at night. Mama asleep mid-page, the book rising and falling on her chest. Papa upright in the chair, chin dropped, one hand still resting on the crib rail where the last pat landed. And between them, the small tyrant of the house, sleeping the innocent sleep of someone who intended to wake them both at one, and again at four.

No one remembers the first month in order. It blurs, feed and fold, sway and shush, like a landscape seen from a train. That is all right. You are not meant to keep the hours. You are meant to keep the feeling.

So here is the advice smuggled into this page: take the unposed pictures too. The mess, the pile, the chair-sleepers. Someday they will be the ones you cannot stop looking at.

The moon took tonight's photo through the window, and kept a copy.
