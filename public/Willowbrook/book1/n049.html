Night 49

All-Weather Baby

Month 2 — Smiles and Sleep

The sky was doing something between mist and rain, and the old Mama would have stayed in. The new Mama looked at the ceiling she had memorized, and dressed the baby.

There is a saying from the cold countries, where babies famously nap outdoors in all seasons: no bad weather, only bad clothes. It holds for parents even more than babies. A house can shrink around a person by the second month — same walls, same laundry, same loop of thoughts. Weather, any weather, is the antidote. Daylight resets the baby's clock and the parent's mood, even the gray kind of daylight; especially, somehow, the gray kind.

So: one more layer than Mama wore herself, rain cover clicked on, umbrella up, and out.

The world, it turned out, was in excellent form. Rain-silvered blossoms clung to the sidewalk. The brook running loud and important. Rain drumming the umbrella in the exact rhythm, Mama realized, of every shush she had performed at two a.m. — nature having invented white noise first.

Olivia stared out from her dry cave, drunk on the ceiling being sky.

They came home twenty minutes later, damp at the edges and entirely repaired.

The ceiling looked better too. Ceilings do, after.
