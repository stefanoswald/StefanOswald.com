Night 98

The Audience Eats

Month 4 — Rolling and Laughing

Dinner acquired an audience, and the audience took notes.

From her new chair at headquarters, Olivia had discovered the second-best show in the house (the first being faces): food, traveling. The fork's rise from Papa's plate — tracked. The arc, the arrival, the chewing — tracked, tracked, tracked, her whole head following like a spectator at the smallest tennis match ever played. When Mama passed the bread, Olivia's eyes went with it, person to person, an auditor confirming the transfer.

Lately, boldly: the reach. A starfish hand, extended toward the passing potatoes with clear editorial intent.

This is the watching phase, and it is not idle. Interest in food — real, tracking, reaching interest — is one of the readiness signs the sixth month will ask about, and it ripens exactly like this, at the family table, weeks ahead of any bite. She was studying the technology: things go in mouths; the big ones do it constantly; the tools flash and travel. Anthropology, from a high chair.

The parents' assignment was purely theatrical: eat together, visibly, narrating the menu. "Papa is eating soup. Warm soup. Mmm." Product placement, the pediatric kind.

No tastes yet — the milk months held the border. But the border had a crowd at it now, standing on tiptoe.

"Soon," Papa told the reaching hand, moving his plate a strategic inch.

The hand withdrew, unoffended, and filed its report: potatoes exist, acquisition pending.
