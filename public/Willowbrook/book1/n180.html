Night 180

The Sixth Month, Counted

Month 6 — The Feast Begins

The audit convened indoors this quarter, autumn at the glass, one candle lit for no occasion beyond arithmetic, the almanac open to its two-handwriting ledger.

The countables ran long this month and were savored item by item. One tooth, crowned, right of center, finest in the county. One feast, launched: iron led, allergens on calendar, sweet potato in landslide government, water apprenticed at two ounces neat. One forward crawl, achieved mid-month, the republic mobile and the kitchen annexed. One transfer, hand to hand; one pincer, previewed; one doctrine of dropped toys, published from the high chair. One library address, resonant. One sport, founded in a hallway. Twice her birth weight, certified by the county scale, half her first year, certified by the calendar itself.

The uncountables took the candle's whole quiet.

That the feast had changed the table's very shape — three eaters now, one of them painting. That the crawl had redistributed the household's entire gravity: doors watched, floors read at pebble depth, the lap era's first thinning grieved and cheered in the same breath, as the book had promised it would be. That her sounds had begun to lean toward meaning — the mmm at the sweet potato, the ma-ma landing more often, suspiciously often, in Mama's actual direction, the almanac declining to certify what both auditors privately believed.

And the halfway finding itself, entered formally now: the fog era's two survivors had become, somewhere between the swaddle and the soup, a family that knew what it was doing — not always, not smoothly, but knowing, the way hands know. The evidence was asleep on Papa's shoulder, twice her original size, one tooth to her name, smelling of bathtime and tomorrow.

"Half a year," said Papa.

"Maybe," said Mama, meaning: all of it, again, forever, yes.

The candle was blown out by the only party licensed for it, by proxy, asleep.

Audited. Approved. Carried forward, at a crawl, at speed.
