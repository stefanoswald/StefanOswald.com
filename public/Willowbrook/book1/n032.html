Night 32

The First Real Smile

Month 2: Smiles and Sleep

It happened on a Tuesday, over a diaper, which is how the best things happen.

Mama leaned in, said the usual good morning nonsense, and Olivia's whole face opened. Not gas. Not a passing weather-smile in her sleep. A real one, aimed, with her eyes in it: the first social smile, arriving right on time in the second month.

Mama made a sound she had never made before and called Papa, who ran sock-footed and nearly passed the door.

Here is what that smile was: proof of wiring. Weeks of faces held eight inches away, feeding distance, exactly how far new eyes can focus, had been quietly building a bridge. Today the bridge opened for traffic.

Smiles feed on answers, so answer every one. Smile back big, say her name, let her feel the astonishing power of it: I beam, and the world beams back.

They spent the day being smiled at like two lottery winners.

And the diaper? Finished eventually. Nobody remembered by whom.
