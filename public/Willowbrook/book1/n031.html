Night 31

Stella Takes the Watch

Month 1 — Arrival

High over Willowbrook, where the wind goes to slow down, a small gold star noticed a new light on Maple Lane — the low, warm, three-a.m. kind of light.

"A new one?" Stella asked.

"A new one," hummed the Sleepy Moon, who does not say much, but says it kindly.

So Stella dipped lower, as stars do for babies, until her glow rested on one particular window ledge. Inside: a crib, plain and safe as a good promise. A bear on watch upon the dresser. Two grown people asleep in improbable positions, and one very small person asleep in the only proper position, flat on her back like a starfish who owns the place.

"They're doing it right," Stella reported.

"Mm," agreed the Moon, which is Moon for they always worry, and they are always doing better than they think.

Stella settled in for the long watch, the one that lasts all the years of growing up. First month: complete. Only everything left to go.

And the Sleepy Moon hummed.
