Night 40

Bicycle, Bicycle

Month 2 — Smiles and Sleep

Somewhere in Olivia's middle, a bubble had taken a wrong turn, and everyone in the house knew about it.

So Papa laid her on the mat for the morning commute. "Bicycle, bicycle," he announced, taking one small foot in each hand, and pedaled her legs in slow, gentle circles — round and round, like the world's smallest spin class.

Then knees up together, folded soft against her belly, held a breath, released. Again. A clockwise rub around her tummy, warm palm, easy pressure, following the same direction the plumbing runs.

Gas is the second month's most reliable villain — new gut, big appetite, swallowed air, learning digestion out loud. The counterattacks are old and unpatented: the pedaling, the knee-folds, the warm bath, good burps after meals, and upright snuggle time while dinner settles.

Halfway through the third lap, the bubble surrendered — audibly, from the southern route — and Olivia's whole face unclenched like a fist becoming a hand.

"And the crowd goes wild," whispered Papa.

The cyclist, instantly cheerful, demanded no further stages. Race complete.
