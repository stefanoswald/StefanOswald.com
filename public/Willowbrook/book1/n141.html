Night 141

Backward Ho

Month 5: Nearly Sitting, Nearly Everything

Launch occurred on a Wednesday. In reverse.

The rocking had built all week, the growls deepening, and then, motion. Genuine, self-generated, historic motion, first traveled distance of a lifetime... directly backward, away from the ring she wanted, at a stately pace, under the coffee table, where she came to rest bewildered as a rowboat blown offshore.

The face that peered out from under the table held the fifth month's most complicated expression: triumph and outrage in equal parts, at the same event.

Backward first is the standard opening, this page assures every family who witnesses it. The arms outpower the legs for now, each push of the mighty hands sends the ship sternward, and the steering committee has not yet met. For a week or three, the house would work like this: Olivia advancing on every desire and receding from it, a small determined tide, fetched out from under furniture at intervals, relaunched, received back under the table.

The temptation is to turn her around, aim her, fix it. Resist, mostly: the backward era is the system calibrating, and it corrects itself with mileage. The better help is the terrain, floor time doubled now that it had somewhere to go, the yes space earning its charter daily, pathways cleared at hull level.

By Friday she had reversed fully across the living room, a personal best, wrong way entirely.

"Magnificent," said Papa, retrieving her from beneath the armchair.

It was. Motion is motion. The steering would come; the sailing had begun.

Forward, the last frontier, waited smugly by the bookshelf. She glared at it, and shipped backward again, growling.
