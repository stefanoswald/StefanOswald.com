Night 97

A Chair at Headquarters

Month 4 — Rolling and Laughing

The high chair arrived in a box with forty steps of assembly and one purpose: promotion.

Olivia was months from meals — the milk administration remained in full power, and the tray would stay bare a while yet. But her back had firmed, her head rode high and steady, and propped snug in the new chair's recline, strapped and supervised, she achieved for the first time in her life the thing she had campaigned for since the bouncer: altitude. Table height. Eye level with the society of dinner.

The effect was instantaneous. The bouncer had made her a spectator; the chair made her a member. Faces across, not above. The conversation at its own latitude, her name sailing past at close range. She pounded the tray once, flatly, like a chairwoman gaveling herself into the minutes.

The practical page here is short. A high chair earns its floor space before solids ever start — five, ten supervised minutes at family meals, building the sitting muscles and the table habits in the same sitting. Straps always, the recline until she sits solid, and the tray's grand opening held for the sixth-month feast when the readiness signs align: sitting steady, reaching well, watching food travel with unconcealed intent.

She was already watching the potatoes with unconcealed intent, two months early. Ambitious, this one, the family agreed.

Member seated. Minutes approved. Meeting adjourned to bath.
