Night 212

The Icicle Tells the Willow

Month 7 — Explorers and Guardrails

News moves through Willowbrook by its own weather: the fireflies carry summer's, the oak files autumn's, and winter's — winter's belongs to the ice, which holds everything it hears in cold clear trust and releases it, drop by drop, at the thaw.

The eave icicle on Maple Lane, melting all week over the kitchen window, had therefore been publishing. And the Great Willow on its hill, bare-branched and patient in the snow-quilt, received the winter's file on the bright cold wind, and passed it up, as the willow does, to the watch.

Stella came low to read it. The Sleepy Moon leaned close and hummed the receiving hum.

The file, in the ice's spare hand: One stood free. Three seconds. The room held its breath correctly. Item: one name aimed and landed; the mother answers to it now like a bell. Item: one sick week, kept from a floor mattress; the watch below matched the watch above, night for night. Item: the game of appearing has changed hands; the small one runs it; the laughing is authored now. Item: two hundred nights of the same shape. The shape holds. Final item: the light is turning. They walked in it daily. They always walked in it daily. Recommend: spring.

"Recommend spring," Stella repeated, to the Moon, in the manner of signing off on a season.

"Mmmmm," hummed the Moon — which is Moon for so recommended, and so ordered, in the fullness of the light's own paperwork; tell the bulbs; tell the hedgehog; tell the balloon-tailed one behind the far pasture, who has been asking.

The willow let the news down into its roots, where the town's springs are kept. Below in the lanes, the snow lay considering its position. In one window on Maple Lane, the small correct darkness held: the crib, the bear on watch, the second tooth, the sleeper who had stood — briefly, magnificently — free, and would soon, said every file in the season's cabinet, do the unthinkable thinkable thing.

Stella twinkled once, for yes, and took the long watch.

Seven months down in the town of Willowbrook. The ice speaking. The light turning. The steps somewhere out there in the spring side, waiting to be counted.

Goodnight, Willowbrook.
