Night 181

Oliver Owl Asks the Half-Year Question

Month 6 — The Feast Begins

On the Great Willow's highest branch, where the tree keeps its oldest wood, Oliver Owl took the autumn air and the half-year reports, as was his office.

Stella came down through the first cold starlight with the season's file, and the Sleepy Moon leaned close over the hill to hum attendance, and the willow's long branches let down their faint gold in the dark, the way they do when the year is being weighed.

"The Maple Lane one," said Stella, opening without preamble, stars being brief. "Half a year tonight. One tooth. Crawling since Tuesday week. Eating the family's soup. Said mmm to a sweet potato and meant it."

"Mm," hummed the Moon, professionally moved.

Oliver Owl heard the file entire — the feast and the launch, the transfer and the doctrine, the library address (he approved of libraries; owls and librarians share an order) — and blinked his slow blink, and asked, as was his office, the question. Oliver Owl speaks only in questions; it is how the wisest listening is done.

"And do they know," he asked the branch, the star, the humming dark, "that the halfway mark is the only one they'll see from the middle? That every other milestone shows itself going or gone — but this one they can stand on, tonight, and look both ways?"

Stella considered the small correct darkness of the nursery window, far below, where the parents had lit one candle for no occasion beyond arithmetic, and counted, and looked — she had watched them do it — both ways.

"They know," she said. "Tonight, they know."

"Then what," asked Oliver Owl, settling his feathers toward sleep, "could an old bird possibly add?"

Nothing, said the willow's gold hush. Nothing, hummed the Moon, in Moon. The question was the blessing; it is always the blessing, from that branch.

Stella twinkled once, for yes, and rose to her post.

Six months down in the town of Willowbrook. Both ways visible from the middle. The watch went on.

Goodnight, Willowbrook.
