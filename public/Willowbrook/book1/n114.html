Night 114

The Tuesday Blanket Summit

Month 4 — Rolling and Laughing

Tuesdays had become the walking date, and this Tuesday the walk pitched camp: one blanket, two mothers, two babies, laid side by side under the oak like loaves set out to prove.

Olivia and Lily regarded each other.

This was not friendship — the manuals are firm that real peer play lives years away, and that babies this age treat other babies chiefly as fascinating weather. But it was something, and the mothers watched it happen: the long mutual stare, the slow reach of one starfish hand toward another, the moment Lily grasped Olivia's sleeve and Olivia consulted the sky about it. Two laboratories, sampling each other.

Babies notice babies. The small face at matching altitude, the familiar-sized sounds — infants stare longer at other infants than at almost anything, filing the earliest data under a folder that will someday be labeled people like me. The blanket summit was banking capital for a friendship neither would remember starting, which is how the best old friendships begin: before memory, in the shade, by the accident of mothers.

The mothers, meanwhile, ran their own summit — sleep totals traded, a teething rumor examined, Rosa's cinnamon rolls distributed and diplomatically praised beyond their considerable merits.

Lily fell asleep first, mid-grip. Olivia held the session open another minute, sentinel over her sleeping colleague, then followed.

The oak minuted the meeting. Attendance: four. Resolutions: none. Success: total.

Same time next Tuesday, agreed all parties, two of them unconscious.
