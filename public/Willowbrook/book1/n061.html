Night 61

The Swaddle Retires

Month 3: Rhythm

It happened during a diaper change: a heave, a determined twist, and Olivia was suddenly looking at the other wall. Rolled. First time, no witnesses but Mama, who gasped as if the coffee table had done it.

And with that twist, an era ended. The swaddle, faithful friend of the fourth trimester, the fold-fold-tuck of a hundred nights, was finished, effective immediately. The rule is plain and unbendable: the first sign of rolling, even attempting, and the arms come out for good. A roller needs her hands free to push, prop, and protect.

Mama expected mutiny at bedtime. The mutiny lasted four minutes. The sleep sack, sleeveless, zippered, a wearable blanket, took the swaddle's place, warm without wrapping, safe without strings.

Olivia slept her new sleep with both arms flung overhead like a tiny weightlifter mid-cheer. Apparently that had been the plan all along.

The swaddle blanket, washed and folded, went to the keepsake drawer, honorably discharged.

Fold, fold, tuck, one last time, empty, just for the drawer.

Eras end. Usually mid-diaper-change. Watch for the twist.
