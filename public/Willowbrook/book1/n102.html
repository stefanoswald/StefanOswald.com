Night 102

Rubber Duck, Esquire

Month 4 — Rolling and Laughing

Bathtime gained its first employee: a yellow duck of no experience and immense presence.

It had arrived in the fruit bowl weeks ago, a gift from Grandpa Joe, who claimed all babies were owed one duck by ancient law. Tonight, with Olivia's grip gone professional and her sitting propped steady in the sloped tub, the duck was formally deployed.

Contact. Grip. Retrieval to the mouth for standard analysis — rubber, aquatic, faintly squeaky under pressure: findings favorable. Then the discovery that would fund a month of baths: pushed under, the duck came back. Every time. Released, it bobbed up, undrownable, punctual, delighted-looking. Olivia stared at this violation of everything gravity had taught her, and pushed it under again. Science.

Bath toys earn their keep exactly here. The tub is a laboratory with the best variables — water that pours, splashes that answer kicks, objects that float where all other objects famously drop — and one simple duck converts washing time into a standing physics seminar. The housekeeping is the only fine print: toys that trap water inside grow dark secrets; squeeze them dry, or buy the sealed kind, and the seminar stays clean.

The washing itself took its usual ninety seconds, performed around the research.

The duck was still bobbing when the towel came. It would be waiting tomorrow, wearing its one expression, ready for further peer review.

Esquire, the family called it thereafter. Nobody remembered deciding this. Ducks acquire titles; it is known.
