Night 43

Follow the Bear

Month 2: Smiles and Sleep

Bobby Bear had a new job: moving target.

Papa held him a bright arm's length from Olivia's face and waited for her eyes to catch. They caught. Then, slow as honey pouring, he sailed the bear to the left.

The eyes followed, jerky, a hop and a catch-up, like a reader losing the line and finding it. Back to the middle. Now right. The whole small face went along this time, chin and all, committed.

Tracking, the books call it, and the second month is its season. Following a slow-moving thing side to side is how the eye muscles learn teamwork; a few passes here and there, during happy awake time, is exercise enough, always stopping while it is still a game, never pushing to the fussing point.

"And now, the finale," Papa announced. The bear rose, hovered, and descended, wobbling, to land nose-to-nose. The audience made her new sound, the almost-laugh, the cooing hiccup of delight.

Bobby Bear returned to the dresser, professionally satisfied.

Some jobs are better than guarding. Not many. This was one.
