Night 48

Five Points of the Harness

Month 2: Smiles and Sleep

Before the engine, always, came the ritual.

Rear-facing, reclined at the angle the little level-sticker approved. Straps threading at or below the shoulders, this being the rear-facing rule. Chest clip riding level with her armpits, armpit height, every time, and the harness snug enough that Papa's pinching fingers slid off the webbing instead of gathering a fold of it. No puffy coat between her and the straps; warm car, or a blanket tucked over the buckled harness, never under.

It took ninety seconds when done right, and Papa had decided early to be the sort of tedious that grows old bones. He did the pinch test even for the four-block drive to the market. Especially for it, short trips being where corners love to get cut.

One more habit rode along, learned from a story too sad to tell here: shoe in the back seat, phone in the diaper bag, something always back there that had to be fetched. A rhythm, so that looking in the back was not a decision but a reflex.

Click, pinch, level, go.

Olivia fell asleep before the second stop sign, safe as arithmetic.
