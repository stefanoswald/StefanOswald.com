Night 213

The Velcro Weeks

Month 8 — The Velcro Weeks and the Thaw

The eighth month opened with an adhesion event.

The separation feelings, building since the door problem's first jurisprudence, reached their forecast peak — and the peak had hands. She clung now: to the leg at the stove, to the shoulder at the bench, to the collar at every attempted put-down, a barnacle of principle attached to the household's moving parts. The yes space, that proud republic, was boycotted unless a parent sat within it. The cupboard governed only with company. Even Papa, first-chair certified, was some evenings ruled insufficient: only the leg would do, and the leg had dinner to make.

The velcro weeks, the bench mothers called them, and the page ratifies both the name and the reframe that gets families through: this is the attachment system working at specification. The eight-month mind has completed its great arithmetic — one particular person, irreplaceable, currently at the stove, could conceivably be elsewhere — and responds with the only policy it has: maintain contact. The clinging is not regression, not spoiling's harvest, not a verdict on the daycare choice or the traded Tuesdays. It is love, newly aware of its own stakes, holding on with both fists.

The craft, assembled at the stove one-legged. The carrier's return to service — the old close-enough-to-kiss rig, dusted off, converting the barnacle to a passenger and restoring both hands to the dinner. The narrated departures kept holy, even across a room: "Mama's going to the sink. Watch me go. Here I am." The peekaboo curriculum run at double sessions, the gone-then-back drilled in comedy so it held in earnest. And the put-downs, when they had to happen, made boring and brief per the goodbye doctrine — the wail acknowledged ("You'd rather come along. I know.") without the counter-cling of guilt.

The weeks ran their course as the charts promised they would: peaking, plateauing, easing as the proof of return accrued its compound interest.

The barnacle years, the oldest bench veterans note, are brief in the ledger and long in the leg — and missed, absurdly, forever after.

The leg held. The dinner happened. Both facts, nightly, all month: the whole doctrine in one kitchen.
