Night 38

The Breathing Check

Month 2: Smiles and Sleep

Every parent does it, and none of them talk about it, so this page will.

Papa woke at 3:40 for no reason, listened, heard nothing, and was up and across the room before he knew he had voted on it. Hand on the crib rail. Eyes adjusting. And there: rise, and fall. Rise, and fall. The small tide, right on schedule.

Newborn breathing is jazz, not marching. Quick runs, then a rest, a pause of five, even ten seconds, then quick again. It has a name, periodic breathing, and it is normal, and it retires on its own as the months go by. What earns a call to the doctor is different: long pauses beyond that, color changes, working hard to breathe. The refrigerator list said so, and the refrigerator list brought peace.

He stood a minute longer than the check required. That part was not worry. That part was just looking at her, which has no medical name yet.

Rise, and fall. Rise, and fall.

He went back to bed on the tide of it, and slept.
