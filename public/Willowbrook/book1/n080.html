Night 80

The First Cold

Month 3 — Rhythm

It arrived, as they do, on a Friday night: the snuffle, the warm forehead, the cry with a rasp on it. Olivia's first cold, and nobody in the house had ever been sicker, judging by the parental heart rate.

The refrigerator list came down and earned its magnets. Under three months, any true fever is a call, no debating. Past that corner, the watchwords steadied things: how she looks matters more than the number — still feeding, still making wet diapers, consolable between the misery? Steady on. Working hard to breathe, refusing feeds, dry diapers, a limp or unconsolable baby, or that instinct in the parental chest that says wrong? Call, always call; pediatricians expect first-cold calls the way harbors expect boats.

The treatment, humbling as ever, was mostly patience with accessories. Saline drops, then the bulb — a brief indignity, a large payoff. The humidifier breathing its mist. Extra feeds in smaller sittings, upright snuggles for the drainage, and sleep taken in shifts again like the newborn weeks.

Three days of pitiful. One night, the worst one, spent chest to chest in the rocking chair, both parties dozing in fifteen-minute installments.

Then, Tuesday morning: clear eyes, ferocious appetite, a smile like weather breaking.

First cold, survived by all three patients. The parents' recovery took longest. It always does.
