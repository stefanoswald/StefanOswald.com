Night 58

The Second Month, Counted

Month 2: Smiles and Sleep

They counted it out on the porch, the way accountants of joy do.

One real smile, now spent daily and never devalued. One conversation ongoing, in vowels, with turns taken. Two eyes that followed a bear across a room. Two hands, newly introduced to their owner. One long sleep stretch, arrived like an early spring; one bottle, mannerly; one library card, signed by proxy; one vaccine day survived by all parties, one of whom cried longest and was not the baby.

And underneath the countables, the other ledger, harder to say aloud. They were not the two people from the hospital photo anymore. Those two had been holding a stranger, beautifully terrified. These two were holding Olivia, a known person, with jokes almost, with preferences certainly, with a way of settling into the crook of an arm as if the arm had been ordered to her measurements.

"We know her now," Mama said, surprised by it.

"She knows us back," said Papa, more surprised by that.

This is the second month's real report card, for every family: the strangeness leaves. The people arrive. The rest is laundry.

The string lights came on down Maple Lane, one porch at a time, like slow applause.
