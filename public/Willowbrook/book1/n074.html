Night 74

Strong Enough to Look Around

Month 3: Rhythm

The wobble was leaving the tower.

Three months of tummy-time bench presses had built something visible now: a head that rose and stayed risen, a neck that steered it like a lighthouse turning. Held sitting on Papa's lap, Olivia rode tall, swiveling to the kettle's whistle, the door's creak, Mama's voice from the hall, each sound collected with a deliberate turn, spotted, filed.

The milestone underneath is head control, the unglamorous foundation stone. Nobody frames photos of it, but everything sits on it: sitting itself, a month or three away; the safe start of solid food, which waits for exactly this steadiness; every future gymnastic from rolling to running.

It changed the days in practical ways. Carrying could go upright and world-facing now for stretches, her back to his chest, the town scrolling by like the world's slowest, best television. The play gym gained a sitting-supported wing, pillows standing by. And tummy time, the old gym itself, went advanced: chest high, forearms planted, a small sphinx surveying her rug.

"Anything to report?" Papa asked the lighthouse.

The lighthouse turned, found his face, and beamed its beam.

All clear on all coasts. Steady as she goes.
