Night 95

Feet, Discovered

Month 4: Rolling and Laughing

The morning Olivia found her feet, she greeted them like relatives arriving from a long journey, which, in a sense, they were. They had been down there the whole time, waving from the far south, uncatchable.

Now the hips had loosened, the tummy had strengthened, and the whole arrangement folded up like a deck chair: hands north, feet lifted south, and the historic meeting in the middle. Grip. Both feet caught, one hand each. She held on and rocked, a small round boat on a calm sea, immensely pleased with her catch.

Then, inevitably, the laboratory being the laboratory, a foot went toward the mouth. It reached. Analysis commenced.

The books call the pose happy baby, and yogis borrowed it fair and square. Underneath the comedy runs real engineering: folding up strengthens the exact middle muscles that sitting will lease next month, and finding the feet extends the body's map southward, the self, surveyed to its very borders, all territories now known to their owner.

No equipment, no instruction. Bare feet help, socks are fog on the southern provinces, and diaper-free minutes on a towel are the deluxe expedition package.

She released the feet. They stayed down there, loyal.

Caught them again tomorrow, and every day that month, the commute between hands and feet becoming, for a season, the best show in the south.
