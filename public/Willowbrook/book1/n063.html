Night 63

The Window Watcher

Month 3 — Rhythm

Between waking and the next sleep, every baby has a window — a stretch of good awake time before the gears grind. In the third month, Olivia's window ran about ninety minutes, give or take a mood.

Mama had stopped watching the clock, mostly, and started watching the baby, but the clock made a useful accomplice. Wake at seven? Somewhere around eight-thirty, the window would begin to close: the stare, the ear-rub, the first yawn drifting by like a leaf on the brook.

Miss the window and everyone paid the toll — the overtired baby who fights sleep hardest exactly when she needs it most, the second wind that is all storm. Catch it, and sleep came easy as a door swinging shut on good hinges.

So the days ran on window-time now. Big outings early in the window, when she was brightest. Quiet play as it narrowed. The walk to the crib while it was still open a crack.

Ninety minutes is not the rule — it is her current number, and it will stretch as she grows, month by month, into afternoons.

The window closed at eight-twenty-nine. Olivia was in bed at eight-thirty.

Through the actual window, the first star clocked in, punctual too.
