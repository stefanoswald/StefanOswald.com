Night 151

Stella Counts Four

Month 5: Theo Arrives

The nightly census. Revised.

Stella, at the old window first:

one girl, big bed, bear in arms.

Sleeping the sleep of

the hardworking.

(Big-sistering. The legs alone.)

Status: counted. Kept.

Then, new on the rounds...

the second window.

The little room.

The crib, second generation.

And in it: the new account.

Theo. Swaddled. Starfished

already, the family sleep,

inherited.

Status: counted. Kept.

"Two now," Stella reports upward.

"Two windows. Two sleepers.

The watch doubles."

"Mmm," hums the Moon...

which is Moon for

the watch doesn't double, old friend.

The watch just widens.

It's held every child

this town ever grew.

Two in one house is Tuesday.

Stella settles between

the windows, the geometry

worked out generations ago...

where one star can hold

two sleepers in one gold.

Below, the house breathes

its new four-part rhythm.

Down the hall, a mother

surfaces, listens, hears

two kinds of quiet,

and sleeps again.

The town rests.

The count is four.

The gold reaches all of it.

Goodnight, big sister.

Goodnight, little brother.

Goodnight, Willowbrook...

one light richer,

all accounts full.
