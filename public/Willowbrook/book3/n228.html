Night 228

The Storm That Turned Into a Nap

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

For the record...

because this book keeps

honest records...

some storms end like this:

The trigger: the sock.

(Wrong sock. Both socks

were wrong. All socks

in the house: wrong.)

The storm: instant.

Full volume. The floor.

The pounding. The

"NO SOCKS EVER" platform,

announced at length.

Mama, running the four

checkers from the doorway:

hungry? No, just ate.

Angry? At socks. Unclear.

Lonely? Attended all day.

Tired...?

The nap had been short.

The morning had been long.

The verdict was still

forming when...

mid-thunder...

mid-POUND, even...

the storm...

stopped.

Face down on the rug.

One sock on. One sock

flung.

Asleep.

ASLEEP. Mid-tantrum.

The transition took

four seconds. Thunder

to snore. The fastest

weather change in the

recorded history of

the house.

Mama stood a while,

beholding it.

Then covered her with

the throw blanket...

right there, rug, one

sock, as found...

and let the storm

sleep itself off.

Forty minutes later:

a girl woke sweet as

morning, fully reset,

no memory of the sock

platform whatsoever.

"Hi, Mama! ...why am I

on da floor?"

Weather, love.

You napped where it

dropped you.

Tired storms are just

naps, arriving loudly.

The four checkers strike

again.

Case: closed.

Sock: recovered.

Nobody tell her about

the platform.
