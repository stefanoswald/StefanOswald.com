Night 146

The Fog, Weathered Together

Month 5: Theo Arrives

Let's tell the truth

about these weeks,

because this book tells true:

everyone is tired.

The wall still talks at three.

The shifts still rotate.

Mama's eyes have the fog in them.

Papa reheats the same coffee

four times before noon.

The house runs on casseroles

and lists and love.

And in the middle of the fog:

one small anchor.

Her routines, kept.

The breakfast at the same chair.

The stories at the same lamp.

The song, nightly, wrong.

Thursdays, sacred. Kept.

(Rosa carries half of them.

The village doing its verse.)

And it turns out...

the veterans knew,

the books knew...

the anchor holds BOTH ways.

Her routines steady HER,

yes. But watch:

they steady the house too.

The bath at six-thirty

means the day has a shape.

The story at seven means

the fog has a floor.

The littlest citizen's schedule

becomes the family's handrail.

"Same as always," she demands,

nightly, of the routine.

Same as always, agrees the house,

gratefully, holding the rail.

The fog thins by weeks.

It always thins.

And when it lifts,

the family finds itself

still in formation:

four people, one handrail,

every routine a rope

they held together.

Weathered. Together.

The only way through

any fog there is.
