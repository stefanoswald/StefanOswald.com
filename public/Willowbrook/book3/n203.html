Night 203

The Standing-Up Question

Month 7: Potty Power

It was bound to come up.

The bathroom has no

privacy. (One inch,

sometimes. That's all.)

And Papa... stands.

"PAPA. WHY YOU STANDING??"

The question. At volume.

Mid-stream, as it were.

Papa, veteran of two

kids' worth of questions...

answered plain:

"Boys' bodies and girls'

bodies are built a little

different. Boys can stand

for this one. You sit.

Both work great."

"...Fee-oh will STAND?"

"When he's big. Probably.

You can teach him

everything else, but

that lesson's mine."

(The one lesson she

subcontracts. Noted

with visible relief

by all parties.)

Simple. Done. No giggling

from the grown-up...

the questions answered

plain grow up asking

plainly, which is the

entire goal.

And then, of course...

the market. The checkout.

The clerk.

"MY PAPA GOES POTTY

STANDING UP."

(The clerk. Deadpan.

Saintly. Eternal:

"Thank you for the

update.")

Papa examined the

ceiling at length.

The town absorbed the

news as the town absorbs

everything: warmly,

and forever.

(The clerk still asks

after "the standing

gentleman." Years on.

Willowbrook keeps

its treasures.)
