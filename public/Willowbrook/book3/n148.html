Night 148

The Cow Takes the Watch

Month 5: Theo Arrives

A quiet story tonight.

About the night shift.

In Theo's room, on the dresser,

stands the plush cow.

(The fair cow. The ring-toss

triumph. Guard of the crib corner

since the waiting months.)

And if cows could write...

the way a certain bear

famously could...

his first letter would say:

Dear Theo,

I have the watch.

I learned the job from

the best there is...

the bear across the hall.

He held this exact post

for a girl once.

Dresser Division. 365 nights.

His paperwork is legendary.

He briefed me fully:

Watch the breathing.

Guard the dark.

Catch the bad dreams;

let the good ones through.

Be findable.

And when the time comes...

past your first birthday,

per the safe-sleep statutes

this family keeps holy...

I move in, and the real

work begins: the being-held.

I hear your sister is teaching

you things. Learn them.

She taught the bear to drink

from a cup once. He speaks

highly of her methods.

Sleep, small loud one.

The house is full of guards.

The dresser sees everything.

The Cow, Dresser Division,

Second Generation

(Across the hall, the bear

approved the letter.

Bears review all correspondence.

The tradition holds.

The watch holds.

Every child in this family

gets a guard and a law:

somebody soft is always

watching over you.

It's the oldest law

on the books.)
