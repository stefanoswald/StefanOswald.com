Night 265

The Domino Line, Counted

Month 9: Counting to Five

Sunday. The workshop.

And on the bench: offcuts.

Small wooden blocks,

chair-leftovers, smooth

as duck's backs.

(She sanded some herself.

Quality confirmed at

the source.)

Grandpa stands them up.

In a line. Counting:

"One... two... three...

four... five."

Five soldiers of pine.

"Now. Touch the first

one. Gentle."

(She knows this game!

Papa's blocks! But these

are WORKSHOP dominoes.

Everything at the farm

is the original edition.)

Touch...

click-click-click-click-

CLICK.

Five falls. One finger.

"AGAIN!"

Again. And again.

And then the lesson,

at rocking speed, while

they stand the line

back up:

"See how it only works

if they're close enough

to reach each other?

Stand 'em too far apart..."

(he does; the push;

one falls; the line

survives)

"...nothing passes along.

Numbers are like that.

Families are like that.

Close enough to touch,

and one small push

moves the whole line."

She stood the five

back up. Close. Touching-

close.

Push.

Click-click-click-click-

click.

The whole line, moved

by one finger.

"Our family is close-

enough," she said.

"Reckon so," said

Grandpa Joe.

"Built it that way

on purpose."

Click. Click. Click.

The oldest engineering

there is.
