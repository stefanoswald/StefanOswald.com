Night 230

Grandpa on Weather

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

Sunday. The porch.

And a question for the

oldest weatherman

in the family:

"Gampa. Do you get

storms? Inside ones?"

Grandpa Joe rocked

a while.

(He answers big questions

at rocking speed.

It's the right speed.

Big answers need it.)

"Had a temper on me

like a August thunder-

head, when I was young.

Ask anybody. Ask the

barn, there's still

a dent in the door

where I put a bucket

through it, nineteen-

and-something."

(A DENT. In the BARN.

From GRANDPA.

The revelation of the

century. She made him

show her. He showed her.

It's a good dent.

Honest-looking.)

"What happened to your

storms?"

"Same thing that happens

to all weather, if you

let it teach you."

Rock. Rock.

"You learn you can't

argue with a storm.

Not the sky's, not

your own. Arguing feeds

'em. What you do is...

you shelter. You wait.

You let it rain itself

out. And you remember

the one thing weather

never changes."

"What?"

"There's always sky

behind it. Always was.

Storm's just passing

through the sky.

Never once been the

other way around."

Rock. Rock.

"Your mama teach you

that yet? The sky part?"

"...I'M da sky," said

Olivia.

"There it is," said

Grandpa Joe.

"Forty years it took me.

You got it by three.

This family's improving."

The porch rocked on.

The dent stayed in

the barn...

kept, like everything

here, as a lesson

in oak.
