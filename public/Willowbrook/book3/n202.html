Night 202

The Wiping Apprenticeship

Month 7: Potty Power

Nobody writes poems

about this part.

This book will anyway.

(It tells true. It said so.)

The wiping.

The last frontier of

throne independence.

The arrangement, struck:

she tries first.

("Front to back. Always.

Dat's da rule." recited

now BY her, TO Duck,

in the doll's ongoing

program.)

Then Mama checks.

Finishes the job as needed.

No shame. No sighs.

Just apprenticeship...

the way every skill

in this whole shelf

of books gets built:

try, help, try, less help,

try, done.

"When do I do it ALL

myself?"

"When your arms grow

a little more. They're

still building the reach.

Like the shoelaces."

(The someday file.

She knows the someday

file. Laces live there.

Whistling lives there.

The reach joins them.

The file always pays out.

She's seen it pay.)

Weeks of apprenticeship.

Then months.

Then, one ordinary day,

per the file's custom...

"I did it ALL. Checked

MYSELF."

Certified. Graduated.

Unglamorous. Enormous.

The books that skip

this part are lying

about parenthood.

This one doesn't.

Poem complete.
