Night 324

The Interrupting Fix

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

A subtle rep this week.

The hardest small one:

NOT INTERRUPTING.

Because Olivia, full

of words now, full of

NOW, full of things

that must be said the

instant they arrive...

interrupts.

Everyone. Constantly.

Lovingly. Like a

delighted rooster.

And this week she

learned what it costs:

June, new June, shy

June, talks slowly.

Finds her words like

the ring finger finds

standing: with effort,

needing room.

And every time June

got halfway to a

thought, "I like...

um... I like da..."

Olivia finished it.

Wrong, usually.

And June stopped

talking. For the whole

visit. Just... stopped.

The lesson, after:

"When you grab someone's

sentence, it's like

grabbing their shovel.

They stop digging."

The tool, taught:

THE SQUEEZE.

When the words want

to burst out and it's

not your turn: squeeze

your own hands together.

Hard. Put the burst

in the squeeze.

Wait for the period.

Next visit: June,

mid-search, "I like...

um..."

(The squeeze. Visible.

White-knuckled. Heroic.)

"...da yellow flowers.

Da puffy ones."

"DANDELIONS!" (Released!

At the period! Legally!)

"ME TOO! Dey're da

BEST..."

June talked all visit.

Slow. Complete.

Listened-to.

Listening is kindness

wearing its quietest

clothes, little one.

Give people their

whole sentences.

They're theirs.
