Night 163

When the Loud Is Too Loud

Month 6: Big Sister School

Some days, Theo cries LONG.

The teething kind. The hours kind.

And today, his sister's ears

got full.

All the way full.

Hands up. Over the ears.

Face crumpling, not mad,

not sad...

TOO MUCH.

(The overwhelmed bubble.

She knows this one.

The loud party taught her.)

And she knew what to do.

She took herself to the fort.

HERSELF. Her own porch trick.

Flap down. World smaller.

Bear held. Breathing done.

Flower in. Dandelion out.

Mama found her there,

mid-repair.

"Too loud out there?"

"Too loud. I fixing my ears."

"Good fixing. Want company?"

Company: accepted.

Here's the wisdom, plain:

loving the baby doesn't mean

loving the noise.

Big sisters get full too.

Big sisters get forts too.

Take the quiet when you

need it, little one.

The loud will still be there.

(It was. It's fine.

Everyone survived.

The fort did its office.

Forts always do.)
