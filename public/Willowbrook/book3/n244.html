Night 244

The Counting Caterpillar Returns

Month 9: Counting to Five

Look who's back in the garden.

On the big leaf. Striped

and scrunching.

The Counting Caterpillar!

(A caterpillar, anyway.

Possibly a descendant.

Professors of his line

have taught this garden

for generations.)

Last year, a whole book

ago. Olivia counted his

scrunches to three,

wobbling.

Now watch:

Scrunch, "one."

Scrunch, "two."

Scrunch, "three."

Scrunch, "FOUR."

Scrunch, "FIVE!"

Five. Clean. Certain.

No wobble anywhere.

The professor pauses

at five, as if checking

her work.

Correct, says the pause.

Onward.

"He's slow," she observes,

"but he never skips one."

That's the whole secret

of counting, little one...

and of most things:

slow, and skip nothing.

The caterpillar has taught

it here forever.

One leaf, one scrunch,

one number at a time.

This month, the numbers

go everywhere.

The professor's course

is open.

Class: enrolled.

Pace: caterpillar.

Perfect.
