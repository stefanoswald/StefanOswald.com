Night 101

The Ultrasound Picture

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

The grown-ups came home

with a photograph.

"Look! The baby's first picture!"

Olivia looks.

She looks closer.

She turns it sideways.

This is not a baby.

This is... static.

Grey fuzz. A blur. Maybe

weather.

"Dat's not a baby."

"It is! Look, that's the head.

That's a little hand."

(The grown-ups point at the fuzz

with total confidence.

The fuzz does not improve.)

Olivia renders her verdict:

"Da baby looks like OATMEAL."

(...Fair. Honestly fair.)

So Mama gets HER baby book.

The first pages.

"Look. Here's YOUR

first picture."

Olivia looks at her own

grey oatmeal blur.

Then at her current arms.

Legs. Excellent working hands.

"...I was OATMEAL?"

You were beautiful oatmeal.

Everyone starts as oatmeal.

The oatmeal becomes a whole person.

It just needs time and love

and a mama building it.

She studies both blurs,

side by side. Sisters, maybe.

"Okay, baby," she tells the photo,

patting it once.

"Grow out of da oatmeal.

I wait for you."

The photo goes on the fridge.

Between her drawings.

Family gallery. First entry,

newest member.

Oatmeal and all.
