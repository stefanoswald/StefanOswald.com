Night 103

The Big Sister Shirt

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

A package! From the north!

The aunts strike again.

Inside, wrapped in tissue:

a shirt.

Small. Soft. Her size.

And on the front,

in careful embroidered letters

(read aloud by Mama,

tracked by a small finger):

BIG SISTER.

And one small gold star,

stitched at the shoulder.

(The aunts know

the family branding.)

It goes on IMMEDIATELY.

Over the pajamas. No matter.

And the lane must be told.

The tour, at once:

The Hendersons' fence: shown.

The gold dog: shown.

(Wag. He can't read.

He approves anyway.)

The mailbox: shown.

(Formally. It has seen

all the family news.

It expects to be kept informed.)

"What does your shirt say?"

neighbors ask, playing along.

And she announces it...

chin up, star shoulder first:

"BIG! SISTER!"

The shirt will be worn

approximately four hundred times

before spring.

Wash cycles: nightly.

It will be worn INTO

the hospital, per her plan,

"so da baby knows who I am."

(The baby will know.

The whole county knows.)

Best shirt ever stitched.

The aunts get a thank-you drawing.

A duck. Wearing a shirt.
