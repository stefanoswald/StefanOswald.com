Night 284

Theo's Hundred Falls

Month 10: Try Again

A homework assignment

from Mama this week:

"Watch your brother.

Just watch him walk."

And she watched.

Theo, walking. Which is

to say: Theo, FALLING.

Fall.

Up.

Walk-walk-walk. Fall.

Up.

Fall.

Up, laughing this time.

Fall.

Up.

By snack: dozens of

falls.

By dinner: a hundred,

easy. Nobody counts.

HE doesn't count.

And that's the lesson,

delivered by its most

oblivious teacher:

"Mama. He falls ALL

DA TIMES. And he never

gets da hot chest.

He just... gets UP."

"Why do you think?"

She watched another

fall. Another up.

Thinking hard.

"...Because he doesn't

know falling is bad?"

CLOSE. So close.

Because falling ISN'T

bad, and nobody's

told him otherwise yet.

To Theo, falling is

just part of walking.

Not failure. Not

embarrassment. A step

that goes down instead

of forward. The floor

is part of the path.

Somewhere between his

age and hers, kids

learn to feel bad

about falling.

Somewhere after that,

grown-ups learn to

not even TRY, in case

of falls.

He hasn't learned it.

She's watching him

not-learn it...

and un-learning a

little herself:

"Da trike falls were

just... part of da

trike."

Part of the trike.

Part of the puzzle.

Part of everything.

The best try-again

coach in the house

is nineteen months old

and doesn't know

he's teaching.

Those are always

the best ones.
