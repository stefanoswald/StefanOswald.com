Night 181

The Watch Reviews the Term

Month 6: Big Sister School

The willow. Month's end.

The watch, in session.

"Report," says Oliver Owl,

"on the school below."

Stella has been waiting

all month to give this one.

"The laugh landed. Hers,

as forecast. The prophecy

holds, the porch-step one,

the laughing part.

The baby rolled, sat, ate,

made the family face.

And the girl..."

(Stella pauses. Stars pause

when the file is this good.)

"The girl builds towers

for the knocking now.

Answers the babble in turns.

Drew the line at the bear

and shared the doll...

the exact right line,

first try.

They pinned a badge on her

tonight. Felt and gold star.

She's wearing it to sleep."

"Whoo," says the owl...

and then, per his office,

the question:

"And do they see it,

the parents? What's actually

being built down there?

Not a helper. Not a minder.

The other thing.

The lifelong thing."

The Moon hums low.

Stella answers slow:

"They see it.

You can tell, they write

it all down. Two handwritings.

Three, counting the ducks

she draws in the margins."

"Then the term is complete,"

rules the owl, settling.

"Log it: the town has

a new keeper in training.

Youngest on record.

First class."

Logged. Sealed. Willow-gold.

Below, two windows,

two sleepers,

one badge over one heart.

The watch holds them both

in one wide gold.

Goodnight, keeper.

Goodnight, kept.

Goodnight, Willowbrook.
