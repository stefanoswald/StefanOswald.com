Night 171

The Two Chairs

Month 6: Big Sister School

Sunday. The farm.

And on the porch, waiting,

under one sheet:

the unveiling.

Grandpa Joe pulls it back.

TWO chairs.

Hers, brought out, polished,

the star bright as ever.

And beside it, new oak,

new joinery, same template:

Theo's.

Two small chairs.

Two carved stars.

Side by side in the porch gold.

"You sanded his, remember,"

says Grandpa. "Before he

was even here. Big sisters

sand first. It's how

it's always gone."

She runs her thumb

over the rail she smoothed...

the way she watched

him do it. Smooth as

a duck's back. Smoother.

Theo is set in his chair...

propped, sturdy-ish,

gripping the armrest

with the move...

and she takes hers,

and the two of them sit,

side by side,

in the family's oldest law,

now in duplicate:

you'll always have a seat

at this farm.

Two seats now.

"Well now," says Grandpa,

looking at his porch...

and the record notes

he looked a long time,

and the record notes why:

somewhere behind his eyes,

a whole row of chairs,

stretching both directions,

every star carved

by somebody

for somebody smaller.

The row grew by one today.

The law held.

It always holds.

It's in oak.
