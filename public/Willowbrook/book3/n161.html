Night 161

The Babble Answer

Month 6: Big Sister School

Theo talks now.

Not words. The before-words.

"Ba-ba-ba. Da-da-ma."

The babble chains.

The exact ones his sister

ran at the same age,

in the same room,

at the same ceiling.

And here's what the house

noticed this week:

she ANSWERS him.

Properly. In turns.

"Ba-ba-ba," says Theo.

"Really?" says Olivia.

"Den what happened?"

"Da-da-ba-ma."

"NO. He did NOT."

"Ba!"

"I KNOW. Dat's what I said."

Serve and return.

The oldest game in the books...

the one that built her

whole voice, turn by turn,

at every counter and crib...

running now, second generation,

staffed by its own graduate.

Nobody assigned her this.

Nobody ever has to.

The answering is in her

the way the song is,

the way the gentle is:

poured in so long

it pours out on its own.

The books say babble answered

becomes language sooner.

The books say the turns

are the whole engine.

The books never imagined

the engine could be

two and three-quarters

years old,

lying on a rug,

scandalized by "ba."

His first word is loading.

(The far pages know it.

It's not mama.

It's not dada.

Take one guess.

One.)
