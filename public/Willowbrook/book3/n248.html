Night 248

Five Ducks Exactly

Month 9: Counting to Five

The pond. The old census

problem.

(Last year's attempt:

ducks refuse to hold

still. Count dissolved.

Final answer: "ALL da

ducks." True but

unnumbered.)

This year, the pond

cooperated.

Five ducks.

EXACTLY five.

Paddling in a row,

slow and orderly,

as if the pond had

heard about the course

and prepared materials.

"One! Two! Free!

Four! FIVE!"

The count: clean.

First try.

Recounted immediately:

five.

Third count, for science:

FIVE.

"MAMA. DERE ARE FIVE

DUCKS. I COUNTED DEM

FREE TIMES."

The census closes.

The books balance.

Somewhere, a caterpillar

professor nods.

And then, because ponds

are teachers too...

one duck dove.

Underwater. Gone.

"...FOUR!"

(Subtraction! Live!

The pond runs an

advanced curriculum!)

The duck resurfaced.

"FIVE!"

(Addition! The full

syllabus!)

Ducks: five, then four,

then five.

Mathematician: one,

delighted beyond all

measure.

The pond, as ever:

the best classroom

in town, tuition free,

lessons swimming.
