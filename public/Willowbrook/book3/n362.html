Night 362

The Willow Walk at Three

Month 12: Almost Three

The party ended at

the blessed early hour.

The guests waved off.

And then, the

tradition, second

annual, headed for

permanent:

THE WILLOW WALK.

Up the hill at golden

hour. The family of

four. The birthday

pilgrimage.

The path: the brook's

stepping stones...

CROSSED this year,

for the first time,

on her own feet, hand

in Papa's, the wading

rights finally paying

their toll. ("Da

stones held me! Dey

was waiting till I

was free!") Theo:

carried, studying

the crossing for his

own someday.

The meadow. The climb.

The reveal, the whole

town below, gold

going blue: the lanes,

the lantern, the farm,

Lily's new roof,

June's street, every

single place the

year happened, one

hill wide.

And the tree.

The long gold branches.

The old trunk, flat-

palmed hello.

The roots' furniture,

seated.

"Da willow keeps our

words," she told Theo,

the tour guide now.

"I gave it HOME last

year. It's in da

rings. Gampa says

trees keep everyfing."

And this year's word,

considered carefully,

delivered at the

trunk with her palm

flat on the bark:

"FAMILY."

(The tree took it

into the rings, per

the town's records,

beside the last one.

HOME, then FAMILY.

The willow is building

a sentence, year by

year. The town's

records decline to

predict it. The town's

records are lying.

Everyone knows the

sentence. It just

needs more birthdays.)

Down the hill at dusk,

one sleeper per

shoulder by the bottom.

Three years old.

The whole town,

witnessed.

The word, planted.

Home. Family.

The sentence, growing.
