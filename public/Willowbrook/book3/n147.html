Night 147

The Fort Gets Its Resident

Month 5: Theo Arrives

The fort. Thursday.

And a motion on the floor:

"Fee-oh is CALM today.

And AWAKE.

I fink... it's TIME."

The induction.

The spot, saved since

before he existed,

upgraded twice,

guarded by the plush cow,

duck-drawing on the wall...

receives, at last,

its resident.

Carried in, carefully,

Mama-supervised at the flap...

and laid on the softest cushion,

in the lamp-gold,

under the blanket roof.

Theo blinks at the ceiling.

The gold light through the weave.

The two faces leaning over.

His fist windmills.

"He LIKES it," rules Lily.

"He LOVES it," amends Olivia.

(The fist is enthusiasm.

The science holds.)

The ceremony, conducted:

one story, read to the resident.

One song, hummed in duet.

One formal declaration:

"Fee-oh. Dis fort is YOURS too.

Forever. Us free."

(Us three. The fort's charter,

amended. Founders: two.

Members: three. Growing.)

He stayed twenty minutes...

a fort record for anyone

under six months...

and left asleep,

carried out like a tiny king

from a canvas palace.

The spot stays his now.

Every fort they ever build...

and the far books hold

NAVIES of them...

keeps a Theo spot.

It's in the charter.

The charter is three kids

under one blanket.

The charter is the whole point.
