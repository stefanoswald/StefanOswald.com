Night 72

The Sleepover Attempt

Month 3: A Friend Called Lily

The boldest Thursday yet:

a SLEEPOVER.

Lily's jammies came in a bag.

Her toothbrush. Her own bear...

a floppy rabbit, actually.

(Bobby Bear received him

with dignity. Colleagues.)

The plan: dinner, bath, stories,

two girls, one big bed, sleep.

The reality:

dinner, perfect.

Bath, a double splash contest.

(The floor towel earned overtime.)

Stories, four. (Sleepover rates.)

Lights out and...

giggling.

Whispers. Giggles.

A foot in a face. GIGGLES.

The monitor, at ten o'clock:

two girls, wide awake,

playing peekaboo

under one blanket.

At ten-thirty: Rosa's car.

The experiment: concluded early.

The walk of shame: giggled

the whole way to the door.

"Next year," the mamas agreed.

Maybe the year after.

But here's the secret nobody

regretted:

it wasn't a failed sleepover.

It was a PERFECT evening

with a bonus ending.

Some things you try early

just to start the counting.

First attempt: logged.

The giggles echo still.
