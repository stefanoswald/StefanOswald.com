Night 212

The Willow Hears About It

Month 7: Potty Power

The willow. Month's close.

The watch, assembled.

"This month's report,"

says Stella, "is about

plumbing."

"Whoo?" says the owl.

(A genuine question,

for once.)

"The small one below.

She learned her own signals.

Answered her body's phone.

Weathered the rain weeks.

Trusts herself now,

at the plainest scale

there is."

The owl blinks slowly.

The Moon hums, considering.

"You find it small,"

says Oliver, and here

comes the question,

per his office...

"but tell me: what is

any trust built on,

if not this? The body

says a thing; the self

listens; the thing is

honored. Scale it up.

What is courage but

signals, answered?

What is honesty but

the inner phone, picked up?

The child who learns

at two that her own

signals are TRUE...

what won't she trust

herself to know at

twenty?"

The watch sits with it.

The willow lets down

a little gold.

"Then log it big,"

says Stella at last.

Logged big:

one small citizen,

fluent now in her own

first language...

the body's, with all

the other languages

of self-trust

queued behind it,

loading in order,

for life.

"Mmm," hums the Moon.

Which is Moon for:

the ordinary miracles

are the load-bearing ones.

They always were.

Goodnight, champion.

Goodnight, Willowbrook.
