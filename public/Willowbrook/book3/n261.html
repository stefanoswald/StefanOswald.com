Night 261

The High Five

Month 9: Counting to Five

A month of fives deserves

its own gesture.

This week, it arrived:

THE HIGH FIVE.

(She'd seen it forever...

the big kids, the papas,

the ballgame on the

market's radio. Palm

meets palm. The sound

of done-well.)

Taught properly, at last:

"Five fingers up. Flat.

Now, eyes on MY hand..."

(the secret; watch the

target; every high-five

master knows)

"...and SMACK."

SMACK.

Clean contact. The good

sound.

"HIGH FIVE!"

And then, immediately...

because everything flows

downhill in this house

within the hour...

the teaching, re-taught:

"Fee-oh. Hand up. Flat.

Like dis."

(The baby palm, raised.

Wobbling. Willing.)

"Eyes on MY hand..."

smack.

(A soft one. Calibrated.

Sister-grade contact.)

"HIGH FIVE, FEE-OH!"

His whole face: the

wattage. A new game.

A new touch. A new

thing his hands can

say.

The house now runs on

high fives:

Tower built? High five.

Potty ordinary? High five.

Steps counted? High five...

one big palm, one middle

palm, one tiny palm,

descending like stairs.

The family handshake:

installed. All three

sizes.

Five fingers, flat,

eyes on the hand,

smack.

The sound of done-well,

echoing down the hall,

several times an hour,

for the next fifteen

years minimum.

Well done, everyone.

SMACK.
