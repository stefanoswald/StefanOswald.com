Night 100

One Hundred Nights of Book Three

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

One hundred nights.

Book three's first stone.

Look what these hundred held:

A girl who names her weather now...

and everyone else's.

(A chicken's, once. Famously.)

A girl who does it herself...

jacket, sandwich, big bed,

crooked buttons worn like medals.

A girl who crashed into

her best friend at full speed

and learned the family word

for what happens next:

maybe.

And now, a girl standing

at the edge of the biggest

change of her whole small life...

tearing a paper chain

toward a brother or sister...

with a bear certified

in baby care

and a duck packed

where it can't be refused.

One hundred nights of becoming

exactly who that baby needs:

somebody kind.

Somebody weathered.

Somebody who knows

that scared-plus-anyway is brave

and that love multiplies.

The chain on the wall

is getting short.

The stone is set.

The spring is coming.

Sleep, almost-big-sister.

You're a hundred nights readier

than you were.

You're exactly ready enough.

You'll see.
