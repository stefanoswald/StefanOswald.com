Night 124

The Green Pinch Arrives on Schedule

Month 5: Theo Arrives

Day four.

It arrived on schedule.

The way Mama said it might.

Theo was feeding. Again.

(Newborns feed ALWAYS.

The cellar-stocking hours.

She doesn't remember hers.

This book does. Book one.

Chapter: all of them.)

And Olivia stood

across the room,

holding the bear,

watching the arms be full

of somebody else...

and the pinch came.

The green one.

Right on time.

Her arms crossed.

Her lip went out.

And then...

here's the part

that makes this whole book

proud...

she didn't kick the couch.

She didn't pinch the baby.

She used the tool.

The month-one tool.

The telling.

"Mama. Da green pinch.

It's here."

Mama looked up.

Everything soft.

"Thank you for TELLING me.

Come here. There's room."

(There was room.

There's always room.

Lap architecture expands.

It's the whole design.)

One arm around Theo.

One arm around her.

The pinch, held between them

until it let go.

It came back other days.

Pinches do. That's their way.

But it never once

had to hide.

Named things are tamer things.

Told things are held things.

The toolbox works.

Even on this.

Especially on this.
