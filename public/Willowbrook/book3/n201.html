Night 201

The Body Is Yours Talk

Month 7: Potty Power

Bath time. Calm water.

And the talk, the one

wise houses give early,

plain, and often:

"Your body belongs to YOU.

All of it. Did you know?"

"...MY body?"

"Yours. Your arms, your

tummy, your private parts...

the underpants parts...

all yours.

Nobody touches your body

without asking. Not kids.

Not grown-ups. Not even

family. Doctor visits...

Mama's there. Bath help...

you can say 'I do it.'

And if ANYBODY ever

makes your body feel

weird or secret-y...

you tell me. Or Papa.

Right away. Even if

somebody said not to.

ESPECIALLY then.

You will never, ever

be in trouble for telling."

"...Even if it's a

GROWN-UP?"

"Especially then, love."

The bath went on.

The talk took two minutes.

It gets repeated...

different words, growing

words, every year,

at bath times and bedtimes,

plain as brushing teeth.

Little talks. Often.

That's the armor.

Her body: hers.

Her voice: loud.

Her tellers: always,

always safe to tell.

The water's warm.

The foundation's poured.

It holds for life.
