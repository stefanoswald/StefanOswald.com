Night 297

The Sandcastle's Five Falls

Month 10: Try Again

The sandbox. The ambition:

a CASTLE.

(A real one. Bucket-

towered. Stick-flagged.

The full vision.)

The record of the falls:

Fall one: dry sand.

The tower poured out

of the bucket as a

pile, not a tower.

(Lesson, via Lily,

via Mateo, via the

lane's whole engineering

lineage: "You need

WET sand. Packed.

Packing is da secret."

The packing doctrine!

It reaches everything!)

Fall two: wet sand,

un-packed. Slumped.

Fall three: packed,

BEAUTIFUL, un-bucketed

too fast. Sheared.

("Slow," counseled the

site foreman, herself...

to herself. "Caterpillar

speed.")

Fall four: perfect

tower... for four

seconds... until the

demolition contractor...

who has a UNION now,

who has RIGHTS, who

truly believed this

was a sacrifice tower...

SWAT.

("FEE-OH. Dis one was

NOT for knocking!"

The union negotiated:

one corner of the

sandbox designated

his, one perpetual

sacrifice pile

maintained. Labor

peace, restored.)

And build five:

wet. Packed. Slow.

Un-bucketed with the

two-hand wiggle-lift.

Guarded.

STANDING.

Towered, moated,

stick-flagged.

A castle.

Five falls in the

building of it...

each one, the builder

could tell you now,

a TEACHER:

"Dry taught wet.

Slumped taught packed.

Fast taught slow.

Fee-oh taught... walls."

The falls taught the

castle.

The castle stood till

the rain took it...

which is the deal

all castles sign,

and all builders learn,

and none of it,

NONE of it,

made building it

less worth it.

Build again tomorrow?

Build again tomorrow.

Obviously.
