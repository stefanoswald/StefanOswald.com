Night 156

The Hard Share

Month 6: Big Sister School

It was bound to happen.

Today it happened.

Theo, rolling now,

reaching now, world-grabbing...

got hold of BOBBY BEAR.

THE bear.

Her bear.

The ancient-law,

hers-forever,

patchwork-heart bear.

And gummed his ear.

The floppy one.

The sacred one.

"NO! MINE! DAT'S MINE!"

The grab, the rescue...

the bear extracted...

and Theo, startled, wailing...

and the storm and the guilt

arriving together,

which is the worst weather

there is.

Mama held the whole mess.

"He didn't know. He's little.

Everything is chewable to him.

AND, the bear is yours.

Both true."

(Both true. The family's

oldest arithmetic.)

And then, the crossroads.

Nobody pushed her to it.

She stood a long minute,

bear in arms,

brother sniffling...

and went to her shelf.

Came back with the knit doll.

Duck.

"Here, Fee-oh.

You can chew DUCK.

She's soft. I share her.

But Baba is MINE.

Baba is my forever one."

Theo received the doll.

Gummed it. Delighted.

Peace, restored, on TERMS.

Her terms. True ones.

Sharing isn't everything-goes.

Sharing is knowing

what's shareable...

and what's soul.

The bear is soul.

The doll is love, loaned.

She drew the line perfectly.

The books couldn't have

drawn it better.

(The bear, for the record,

suffered no lasting damage.

His ear has survived worse.

His ear has survived HER.)
