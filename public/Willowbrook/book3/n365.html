Night 365

Goodnight, Two

Month 12: Almost Three

The last night of the

third book.

She's asleep now...

three years and one

day old, cake-fed,

willow-walked, barrow-

rich, one firefly

blessed, with the

bear in her arms

and the whole year

folded down around

her like the good

quilt.

And above the town,

the watch assembles

for the turning of

the book:

Stella, low over the

window, at her post

of three years and

counting: "The file's

complete. She came

into this book a baby

who had feelings.

She leaves it a girl

who KEEPS them...

hers, her brother's,

her friends', a whole

town's worth. Youngest

keeper on record."

"Whoo," says Oliver

Owl, from the willow's

oldest branch, and

asks, per his office,

the turning question:

"And what will they

read tomorrow?"

"The next one," says

Stella. "From Three

to Four. I've read

ahead. The pretend

grows wings. The bear..."

(she says it softly;

it's still processing)

"...the bear gets up

and WALKS in that one.

Dreams first. Then

more. It's the book

where the whole world

of Willowbrook opens

its doors."

"Mmmmm," hums the

Sleepy Moon, the

deep hum, the book-

turning hum, the one

she's hummed at the

end of every volume

since volumes began:

then it isn't an ending.

It never was. Blow out

the candles and the

light just moves to

the next page. Oldest

arrangement there is.

The willow lets down

its gold over the whole

town, the farm, the

bakery, the library,

the new houses and

the old ones, every

window with a child's

breathing behind it.

The brook carries the

day downstream to

wherever the days go.

Goodnight, three-year-old.

Goodnight, little brother,

dreaming his sister's

name.

Goodnight, bear...

rest well; big year

ahead; the paperwork

is nearly through.

Goodnight, Mama.

Goodnight, Papa.

You did the whole

year beautifully.

The book gives its

word, same as always,

at its own last line:

you did it beautifully.

Goodnight, Willowbrook.

From Three to Four

begins tomorrow.
