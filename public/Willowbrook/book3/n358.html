Night 358

Waking Up Three

Month 12: Almost Three

Eyes open.

The light: different?

(The light is always

different on birthday

mornings. Science

can't explain it.

The books don't try.)

And the room:

BALLOONS. Red ones.

At the foot of the

big bed, bobbing.

IT'S THE DAY.

Down the stairs at

full zoom, past the

banner (THREE, duck-

authenticated), into

the kitchen where:

"HAPPY BIRTHDAY!!"

The family. Assembled.

Papa's terrible

wonderful singing.

Theo banging the

tray: "LIVY! LIVY!"

(He knows something's

up. Whatever it is,

he's for it. His

sister's day. His

favorite kind.)

The pancakes: with

berry stars. Three

of them, in a

constellation.

(Rosa's design brief,

leaked to the kitchen.

Stars in bunches.

The sky does it

that way.)

"How old are you

today, Olivia?"

The moment. The

certified answer.

The fingers: pointer,

tall one, ring one...

thumb pinning the

loyal pinky...

"FREE!"

THREE.

Official. Fingered.

True.

"Do I FEEL free?"

she wondered aloud,

mid-pancake, checking

her arms, her legs,

her whole new self.

"...I feel like ME.

But wif more birfday

in it."

Like herself, with

more birthday in it.

(The threshold

question, answered

best of any year.

Same you. Plus

candle. Plus light.)

The party: at noon.

The morning: all

theirs.

Three, so far:

delicious.
