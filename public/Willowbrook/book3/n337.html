Night 337

Three Fingers, Certified

Month 12: Almost Three

The answer needs to

be ready.

The question is coming...

from every clerk and

neighbor and aunt,

all month, then all

year:

"How old are you?"

The old answer: two

fingers. Retired with

honors.

The new one, drilled

since the counting

month, now in final

certification:

THREE.

Pointer, tall one,

ring one: UP.

Thumb: pinning the

pinky. (The pinky

still tries to rise.

Pinkies are loyal

to their neighbors.

The thumb holds firm.)

"FREE!"

Certification runs:

The mirror: three

shown, three returned.

(The colleague, synced

as ever.)

The market clerk:

"Free! Almost!"

("ALMOST free," she

corrects herself...

precision matters

in the last month...

"free at da CAKE.")

The farm: "Well now.

Three." Rock. Rock.

"Big number. Fits

you already."

And Theo, studying

the trick with his

whole face, attempting

it: a fist with

intentions.

"You'll be TWO soon,"

his sister told him.

"Two fingers. I'll

teach you. It's easier

dan free. Da fingers

cooperate at two."

The curriculum, pre-

loaded, as ever, one

year down the line.

Three fingers.

Certified. Holstered.

Awaiting the cake.
