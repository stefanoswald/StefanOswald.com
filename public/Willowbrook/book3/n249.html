Night 249

The Five-Block Certification

Month 9: Counting to Five

The construction industry

and the counting course

merged operations today.

The commission: a tower

of EXACTLY five.

Not four. Not six.

Five, certified.

The build, counted aloud

at every level:

Block one: seated. "ONE."

Block two: "TWO."

Block free, three:

"FREE."

Block four: the hover,

the care, the tongue

out. "FOUR."

And block five...

the summit, the pinky

block, placed at full

stretch...

"FIVE."

A five-tower.

Counted into existence.

Each block wearing

its number.

The certifying authority

(herself) walked a

circle around it.

Approved.

And then the demolition

contractor arrived.

(On foot now. FEET.

The commute is faster.

Nothing on the rug

is safe. Everything

on the rug is thrilled

about it.)

"Fee-oh! Count first,

DEN knock!"

He counted, in his way:

"Ba! Ba! Ba!"

(Three ba's. Close

enough. Generous grading.

The family standard.)

SWAT.

Five, unbuilt, in one.

"...ZERO!" ruled the

certifier, staring at

the wreckage.

"Zero blocks! Dat's

da fist number!"

Zero: discovered in

the field.

The industry rebuilds

at dawn.

Mathematics: fully

load-bearing.

The count goes on.
