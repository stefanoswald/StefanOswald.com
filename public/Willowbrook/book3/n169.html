Night 169

The First Chase

Month 6: Big Sister School

LAUNCH DAY.

It came (per forecast)

on an ordinary afternoon.

Theo, rocking...

rocking...

and then, hands, knees,

hands, knees...

MOVING.

Forward. (Forward! First try!

The forecast is generously

amended.)

And here's the part

the almanac boxed:

he wasn't crawling

toward a toy.

The toys were RIGHT there.

The ring. The blocks.

He went past all of it.

Hands, knees, hands, knees,

across the whole rug,

straight at

his sister.

The destination was HER.

Was always going to be her.

"HE'S COMING! MAMA!

HE'S COMING TO ME!"

She held out her arms...

the catch position,

the Wednesday-before-lunch

position, inherited...

and he arrived,

and was caught,

and was swung,

and the room did what

this family's rooms do

at first arrivals:

came running, cheered,

teared up, wrote it down.

First crawl: aimed.

First destination: sister.

The chase begins here...

him after her,

room to room, year to year,

the whole rest of the books.

"Forever?" she asked once,

about the following.

Forever, said the schedule.

It started today.

Right on time.

Straight at her.
