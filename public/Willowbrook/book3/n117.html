Night 117

The Real Night

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

A hand on her shoulder.

Gentle. In the dark.

"Olivia. Sweetheart. Wake up."

Mama's voice. Calm as the pond.

"It's baby time. The real one."

THE REAL ONE.

She sits up, instantly,

completely awake, the way

only the biggest nights allow.

The house is moving

like a quiet machine:

Papa, bag in hand.

(The duck inside. She checks.

Confirmed. Deep in the corner.)

Mama, breathing slow...

flower in, dandelion out...

between the packing.

And headlights in the drive:

Rosa. Forty seconds flat.

(The drill. It's running

like butter. Just like

the trick promised.)

At the door, the goodbye...

big, but not scared-big.

Ready-big.

"Next time you see us,"

says Mama, hands on her cheeks,

"you'll be somebody's

big sister."

"I ALREADY somebody's

big sister," says Olivia.

"Da baby just hasn't

MET me yet."

(Frame it. Frame the whole

sentence. Rosa did, verbally,

retelling it for years.)

Kiss. Kiss. The wave...

three beats, the old ceremony...

and the car pulls out

into the dark,

carrying the moon

toward morning.

Go get our baby, Mama.

We'll hold the fort.

(The fort has a spot saved.

It's been saved for months.)
