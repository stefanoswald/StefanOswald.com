Night 354

The Town Delivers, Third Edition

Month 12: Almost Three

The party's final week.

And the town, per its

constitution, per two

prior editions, per

the way of this place...

began to deliver.

The parade, logged:

The farm truck, Monday:

eggs for the baking.

("Henrietta's finest.

The Duchess contributed

WILLINGLY this year.

Whole new bird since

the feather. You've

ruined her reputation

entirely." Grandpa,

delighted.)

Rosa's crates, Wednesday:

the noodle supplies.

(Spaghetti at a birthday.

The catering order of

her career, she claims.

The constellation cake:

in progress. Off-limits.

Under cloth. Like the

workshop.)

The Hendersons: the

walk swept, the hedge

trimmed party-neat.

(Unasked. Per the

economy. The detectives

noted it and left

a thank-you drawing

in the mailbox.

A duck. Sweeping.)

June's family: a bag

of balloons. Red.

("She said red is

your color." The new

family, fluent already.

The lane teaches fast.)

And the library: one

parcel at the desk.

A book. Wrapped.

"For after the party.

Birthdays need a

wind-down story.

Librarians know."

The town, assembled

at the door in

casseroles and

crates and kindness,

three years running.

"Everybody keeps

BRINGING fings,"

observed the birthday

girl at the window.

Everybody keeps

bringing things.

That's what you built,

little one, you and

your family and every

wave and casserole

of three whole books:

a town that shows up.

It shows up

every single time.

Two sleeps.
