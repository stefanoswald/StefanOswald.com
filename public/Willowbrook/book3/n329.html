Night 329

The Gentle Inspection

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

An afternoon that

summarized itself.

Nobody staged it.

The book just watched:

2:10, a worm on the

wet walk. Crouched at.

One gentle finger.

"Go home safe, little

traveler." (The summit

protocols. Third year

running.)

2:30, the gold dog's

fence. Slow company.

One soft treat. His

speed, kept.

3:00: Theo, falling

hard off the porch

step. The kit deployed:

the level face, the

"up you get," the

kiss on the actual

owie, the "you're

okay, I've got you."

(Received. Rewrapped.

Running again in

ninety seconds.)

3:40: June, at the

fence, worried-quiet

about becoming a

big sister. ("Da baby

gets everyfing?")

And the candle theology,

passed on entire:

"Love doesn't get

littler. It gets MORE.

Like candles. My mama

showed me wif real

ones. Tell your mama

to show you."

4:15, one bubble,

for Harry, blown

toward the hedge.

(He wasn't out. It

didn't matter. Some

kindnesses are just

left, like dandelions

at a fence.)

One afternoon.

Five gentlenesses.

Zero of them assigned.

The muscle, running

its own reps now...

unsupervised,

unannounced,

habitual.

Which is the month's

whole finish line:

kindness that stopped

being an exercise

and became a gait.

She walks kind now.

It's just how

she moves.
