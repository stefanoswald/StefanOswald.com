Night 153

Gentle Hands, Advanced Course

Month 6: Big Sister School

Big Sister School, term two.

Today's course: gentle hands,

advanced.

Because Theo is grabbier now...

and grabbier babies invite

grabbier games...

and the line needs teaching.

Mama teaches it the old way.

The way of the worm summit.

The chick brigade.

The whole one-finger dynasty:

"His head, here, the soft part...

that's ONE-finger country.

Barely-touch country.

Like the flower petal.

Show me."

One finger. Feather-light.

Perfect.

"His hands, those can take

real pats. He's sturdy there.

Show me."

Pat, pat. Certified.

"And when he grabs YOUR hair..."

(he does; the grip spares no one)

"...we don't yank away.

We open his fist gentle,

finger by finger. Like this."

The curriculum, absorbed.

And then, the graduate moment...

she adds her own course unit,

unprompted:

"And NO bonking. Even when

he bonks ME. He's little.

He doesn't know bonking yet.

I teach him LATER."

(Correct on all counts.

The bonking curriculum

comes with toddlerhood.

She'll teach it by surviving it.

The far pages confirm.)

Gentle: three creatures deep now.

Worm. Chick. Brother.

The hands that learned it

smallest teach it best.

The dynasty continues.
