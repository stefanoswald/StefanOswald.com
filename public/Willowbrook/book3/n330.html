Night 330

The Kindness Jar Fills

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

All month, on the

counter: A JAR.

And beside it: paper

slips, a crayon cup.

The rule: any kindness

anyone sees, write

it (or dictate it),

slip it in.

No names required.

Small ones count.

The jar filled slowly,

then all at once...

the way jars and

seeds and children do.

And tonight: the

reading. The whole

jar, aloud, at the

table:

"Held da market door.

Six people."

"Someone fixed the

gate squeak." (Still

officially unsolved.

The detective smiled

into her milk.)

"Theo gave his cracker."

"O. sat with L. on

the bench and stayed."

"M. carried R.'s chair

into the sun."

"A dandelion got a

hen to nod."

"Someone's best rock

is in someone's

treasure box now."

"P. hoped the phone

man's day got easier."

"A crooked square

went into a blanket."

Forty-some slips.

One month. One family,

one lane, one town.

"Dat's LOTS of kind,"

said Olivia, at the

empty jar.

Lots-lots.

"Now what? Is it done?"

Mama put the lid

back on. Set the jar

back on the counter.

Fresh slips beside it.

"Now we fill it again."

Now we fill it again.

That's the whole

instruction.

The jar never finishes.

Neither does the town.

Neither, now, will

she.
