Night 122

The Finger Grab, Part Two

Month 5: Theo Arrives

Remember the field trip?

The baby cousin? The finger?

"It LIKES me"?

That was practice.

This is the real one.

Theo, home now, in his crib...

HER old crib, the good one...

and Olivia at the rail,

on her step stool

(the star one; it migrated

to the baby's room day one),

conducting her morning visit.

She offers the finger.

The way you do.

The way the cousin taught her.

And the tiny hand...

smaller than the cousin's,

newer, pinker...

closes around it.

GRIPS it.

The famous grip. The newborn vice.

The hold-on-for-dear-life

that babies ship with.

"Mama. MAMA. He GOT me."

He got you.

"He's STRONG."

He's strong.

"...I'm not going anywhere,"

she tells him. Informationally.

"You don't have to squeeze."

(He squeezes anyway.

It's his whole move.

He has one move.)

So she stays.

Ten minutes. Fifteen.

Finger captured, arm going sleepy,

step stool creaking.

Because here's the thing

about that grip...

the thing every big sibling

learns at some rail:

it works both directions.

He grabbed her finger.

Something grabbed her back.

Neither one of them

ever fully let go.

Check any far page

of any far book.

Still holding.
