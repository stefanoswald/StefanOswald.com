Night 186

Accidents Are Just Rain

Month 7: Potty Power

Two days after the parade:

the accident.

Mid-play. Too absorbed.

The signal came too late.

Wet pants. Wet sock.

And the face, crumpling,

the champion's title

suddenly in doubt...

"I... I didn't..."

Here's where houses divide.

Here's where this one stood:

"Whoops! Rain happened.

Let's get dry."

That's it. Full text.

No sigh. (Sighs are scoldings

in soft clothing. She'd hear it.)

No "you should have."

Just: dry clothes,

matter-of-fact hands,

and the doctrine, delivered

while the fresh socks

went on:

"Learning potty is like

learning weather.

Some days: sunshine.

Some days: rain.

Rain doesn't mean

you're not a champion.

Rain means you're still

learning your own sky.

Everybody rains.

EVERYBODY. Ask Papa."

(She asked Papa.

Papa confirmed. Historically.

With a story his mother

told at his wedding.

Solidarity is the doctrine's

secret ingredient.)

"Still a champion?"

"Still a champion.

Champions have laundry.

It's in the rules."

The play resumed.

The title held.

The rain dried.

It always dries.

Sunshine came back

by afternoon...

literal and otherwise...

the way it always,

always does.
