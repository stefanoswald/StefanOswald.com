Night 278

The Circle Won't Circle

Month 10: Try Again

Day three of the trike.

The honest day. The

grinding-middle day.

Every mountain has one...

usually several...

and this book doesn't

skip them.

The problem, precisely:

legs know STOMP.

Legs know RUN.

Legs know JUMP (five

in a row, counted).

Legs do not know CIRCLE.

The circle is new

machinery. Push-down-

forward-around-up...

a shape no leg has

ever needed before.

And so: lurch. Stall.

Lurch. Stall.

Half a driveway in

twenty minutes.

The hot chest. The

handlebar smacked.

"DA TRIKE IS BROKEN."

(It's not broken.)

"MY LEGS ARE BROKEN."

(They're not broken.

They're LEARNING...

but that sentence

never lands mid-

frustration, so Papa

didn't say it.)

What he said:

"Want to stop for today?"

"...NO."

(Mark that no.

That no is the month.

That no is the whole

person she's becoming.

Offered the exit,

mid-grind, day three:

no.)

Two more driveways.

One accidental half-

circle, the pedals

caught, one full

rotation, ten inches

of TRUE pedaling...

"DID YOU SEE DAT?"

Saw it.

Ten inches.

Day three's whole

harvest: ten inches

and a no.

Some days that's

the entire crop.

Plant it anyway.

It's enough.

It's always been

enough.
