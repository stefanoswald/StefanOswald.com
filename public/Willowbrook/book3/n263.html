Night 263

Five in the Family

Month 9: Counting to Five

Dinner. The counting

fever, applied to the

ultimate set:

"How many in our

FAMILY?"

The census, conducted

around the table:

"Mama: one.

Papa: two.

Me: free.

Fee-oh: four.

And..."

(The grown-ups, expecting

the count to close at

four)

"...BABA. FIVE.

Five in da family."

A pause at the table.

The bear, present, as

ever, at the fifth

chair's corner, one

ear flopped, patchwork

heart at attention...

awaiting the ruling.

And the ruling came

from the head of the

table, Papa, with

appropriate gravity:

"Five. Correct count.

He's done more nights

of service than any

of us."

FIVE. Ratified.

The family: officially

a hand's worth.

One per finger:

Mama the thumb (sturdy;

the starter; everything

opposable runs through

her).

Papa the pointer (finds

things; points at ducks;

navigates).

Livy the tall one

(obviously).

Theo the ring one (the

one that needs a little

help standing; give

him time).

And Baba the pinky...

small, essential,

the one you'd miss

most if it were gone.

The census closed at

five.

(It reopens in far

books. Families do.

The hand has a sequel,

remember.

But that's other pages.)

Tonight: five.

Counted. Complete.

High fives all around...

which now, mathematically,

this family was built

to give.
