Night 227

The Body Map of Mad

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

A rainy-day project,

straight from the manual:

THE BODY MAP.

Big paper. Olivia lies

down. Mama traces...

the whole girl, outlined,

crayon around every

finger. (Giggling. The

tracing tickles. Science

requires sacrifice.)

Then: up. The outline

regarded. HER, on paper.

And the mapping:

"Where does MAD live?"

She knows immediately.

Red crayon: the chest.

Scribbled hot. "HERE.

It gets hot HERE."

"Where does SAD sit?"

Blue. The shoulders,

drooped. And, unexpected...

the throat. "It gets

stuck HERE when I don't

cry yet."

(The books would pay

for this data. The

throat. She's exactly

right.)

"Worry?"

Yellow. The tummy.

Butterflies drawn...

actual butterflies,

Bella-styled. "It

flutters."

"And happy?"

EVERY crayon. Everywhere.

Feet especially.

"Happy lives in da

WHOLE body. Dat's why

it bounces."

The map went on the

wall by the cozy corner.

And now, mid-weather,

she can REPORT:

"Da hot is starting.

In my chest."

Early warning system:

online.

Storms spotted at the

first hot,

sheltered before the

thunder,

by a girl who mapped

her own weather

one rainy afternoon

in crayon.

Meteorology: mastered.

The wall keeps the map

for years.

The body keeps it

forever.
