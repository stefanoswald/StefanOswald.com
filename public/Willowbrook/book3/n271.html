Night 271

Counting Jumps

Month 9: Counting to Five

Remember the almost-jump?

(The heel's argued margin.

The conviction without

the clearance. A whole

book ago.)

This month, quietly,

somewhere between the

counting and the chasing...

it stopped being almost.

Both feet. Off the ground.

TOGETHER. Airborne.

The real, whole,

certified JUMP.

And a jumper with a

counting fever counts,

obviously, her jumps:

"ONE!" (Off the porch

step. Stuck landing.)

"TWO!" (Flat ground.

Clean.)

"FREE! FOUR!"

(The rhythm arriving...

jump-land-count, jump-

land-count)

"FIVE!!"

Five jumps. In a row.

Counted. Landed.

The gymnastics and the

arithmetic, fused into

one bouncing girl.

And at the fence, the

audience: Theo, gripping

the rail, knees pumping...

the jump-adjacent bounce

of the not-yet-jumper,

the same one she once

did at the sprinkler's

edge, studying the big

kids.

"You'll jump too,"

she told him, mid-air.

"When you're free.

I'll count you."

(She will. The far

pages have it. His

first jump, counted

by the same voice.

"ONE!" The tradition

already has his name

on it.)

Jump five stuck its

landing.

Arms up. The gymnast's

finish. Ta-daa.

The almost era: closed.

The counted-flight era:

open.

Five at a time,

off every step in town,

forever.
