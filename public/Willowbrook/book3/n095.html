Night 95

Will Mama Still Love Me

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

Bedtime. Lights low.

And out of the quiet,

very small:

"Mama?

When da baby comes...

you still love ME?"

Oh.

Oh, little one.

There it is. The big worry.

The one every big-sibling heart

carries up to bed some night.

Mama doesn't rush the answer.

She gets the candle.

(The birthday box. Two of them.)

She lights one.

"This is my love for you.

See how bright?"

Nod.

Then she takes the second candle

and lights it FROM the first.

Two flames now.

"Look at your candle.

Did it get smaller

when it lit the new one?"

...No.

It's the same.

It's exactly as bright.

TWO brights now.

"That's how love works, baby.

It doesn't divide.

It multiplies.

The new baby gets a whole flame...

lit from ours...

and yours never, ever dims.

Not one flicker. Not ever."

Olivia watches the two flames

dance together.

"...Two brights," she whispers.

Two brights.

Soon: three, four, five brights.

A whole family of fire,

every flame full-sized.

Blow them out together now.

Whoo.

The love stays lit.

It doesn't need the candles.

It never did.
