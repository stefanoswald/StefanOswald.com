Night 136

Theo Meets the Bench

Month 5: Theo Arrives

The park. The bench.

THE bench. The founding one.

Where a password started

everything, years ago:

"How's the sleep?"

And today the bench receives

its newest constituent.

Theo, carrier-borne, presented

to the parliament.

The mothers coo in order.

The password is asked of Mama...

tradition, and answered

with the fog-weeks laugh

that IS the answer.

And on the blanket country

just beyond:

the big kids' summit.

Olivia. Lily. The recruit.

And now, laid on the blanket's

center in his bundle:

the newest delegate.

The old ones gather round.

"He can't DO anything yet,"

Olivia explains, chairing.

"He grips. Dat's his move."

(The move is demonstrated.

Three fingers offered.

Three fingers gripped.

The delegates are charmed

into full recess.)

"When he's big, he plays wif us.

I'm teaching him everyfing."

The blanket that held two,

then three,

holds four now.

The parliament grows

the way parliaments should:

one small delegate at a time,

gripped in, welcomed,

argued over gently,

and guarded by everyone.

The bench watches its country

widen in the sun.

The bench has seen

this before.

The bench never gets tired of it.
