Night 287

Papa's Broken Shelf

Month 10: Try Again

The garage. Saturday

afternoon. Papa building

a shelf.

(A real one. For the

garage wall. Wood,

brackets, the level

with the little bubble.)

And the shelf...

fell down.

Fully built. Loaded.

CRASH, the whole thing,

brackets and all, onto

the garage floor.

Papa said a word.

(Not a bad one. A

LOUD one. "GAH." The

hot chest, adult

edition, live and

in person.)

And his audience...

watching from the

doorway, expert now

in exactly this weather...

coached:

"Papa. Do da breaf."

...

He did the breath.

(The flower one. Hers.

Borrowed back again.)

"Now say da yet."

"...I can't build

this shelf..."

"YET."

"...yet."

And then, the real

lesson, the one only

grown-ups can teach

because only grown-ups

can model it at full

scale...

he looked at the wreckage

and NARRATED his try-

again, out loud, tool

by tool:

"Okay. What did the

fall teach me? ...The

brackets were too far

apart. The middle sagged.

Draft one taught me

where draft two's

brackets go."

(Draft one. The duck

folder. The whole

family curriculum,

cross-referenced in

a garage.)

Shelf, version two:

three brackets.

Loaded. Tested.

HOLDING.

"You did it, Papa!

Da fall taught you!"

The fall taught him.

The falls teach everyone

who lets them.

The shelf holds to

this day...

and holds, along with

the paint cans,

one afternoon's worth

of proof that the

tools are for every

size of person,

and the biggest students

learn loudest.
