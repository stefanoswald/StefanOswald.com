Night 323

The Kind True Thing

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

A hard moment at

Thursday's art table:

Lily's painting tore.

Mid-masterpiece. The

wet paper gave, and

a whole corner came

away, the corner

with the sun on it.

"Is it RUINED?" Lily

asked. "Tell me da

troof."

And there stood the

needle every kind

person learns to

thread:

The kind lie: "It's

fine! You can't even

tell!" (You could tell.

Lies rot kindness

from inside. Even

nice ones. Especially

nice ones.)

The plain truth: "Yeah,

it's ruined." (True.

And a hammer.)

She threaded it:

"...Da corner tore

and da sun came off.

DAT part's true.

But da rest is still

beautiful, da whole

garden part.

And we could... glue

da sun back?

Or paint a NEW sun

on da other side?

Torn paintings can

get fixed different."

Truth: kept. (The tear

is real. Named.)

Kindness: kept. (The

beauty is real too.

Named louder.)

And the fix: offered...

because kind truth

always brings its

toolbox.

The sun was glued back.

Slightly crooked.

"Crooked suns," ruled

the artist, "are morning

suns. Coming-up ones."

The painting hangs

in Lily's room still.

Crooked sun. True

corner. Kind seam

right down the middle,

holding.

Tell the truth,

little ones.

Bring the glue.
