Night 267

This Little Piggy, Second Generation

Month 9: Counting to Five

The oldest counting game

in the family vault:

the piggies.

Played on her toes since

before memory. Five little

pigs, one market, one

wee-wee-wee.

And tonight, after his

bath, with his five

impossible toes pink

and available:

the second-generation

performance.

"Fee-oh. Foot. I do

da piggies."

The foot: surrendered.

(He surrenders everything

to her. Standing policy.)

"DIS little piggy went

to MARKET..."

(The big toe. Wiggled.

He watches, suspicious

and thrilled.)

"Dis little piggy stayed

HOME.

Dis little piggy had

roast beef.

Dis little piggy had

NONE..."

(the poor piggy gets

her kiss, per the

inherited liturgy)

"...and DIS little piggy..."

(the pause; she has

the pause; the pause

is the whole art)

"...went WEE WEE WEE..."

(the tickle-run, up

the leg, to the belly)

"...ALL DA WAY HOME!"

SHRIEKING. The good kind.

Both of them.

"ADEN!" says Theo.

(Again. His third word.

Arrived this week.

His vocabulary: "Livy,"

"mama," "aden."

A complete philosophy,

honestly: love, safety,

and MORE.)

Aden. The other foot.

Aden. The first foot,

re-pigged.

Aden, aden, aden.

The game passes down

like everything...

song, kiss, count,

pause and all...

five toes at a time,

wee wee wee,

all the way home.
