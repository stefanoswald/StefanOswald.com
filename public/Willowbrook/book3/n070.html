Night 70

The First Fight

Month 3: A Friend Called Lily

It was about the tower.

(It's never really

about the tower.)

Lily added a block.

Olivia didn't WANT a block added.

Words got loud.

Hands got pushy...

the tower went down...

NOBODY knocked it. BOTH

knocked it...

and now:

two girls.

Two corners of the fort.

Backs turned.

Silence so loud it hums.

The mamas, wise, bench-trained...

don't rush in.

Fights need a minute to cool

before they can mend.

One minute. Two.

Then a small voice

from one corner:

"...Wiwi sad?"

From the other corner:

"...Livy mad?"

The weather reports.

Exchanged. Across the fort.

The vocabulary, month one's

whole toolbox. WORKING.

"I sorry I push."

"I sorry too."

And then, the two-year-old

peace treaty, universal edition:

"...Build again?"

"Build AGAIN."

The tower rises. Both hands. Taller.

First fight: survived.

Write it down, because it matters:

real friends aren't the ones

you never fight with.

They're the ones you build

the tower again with.

Every time. Taller.
