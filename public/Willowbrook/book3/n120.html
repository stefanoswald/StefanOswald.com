Night 120

The Town Hears First-ish

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

Officially, the town found out

through Rosa's kitchen.

(Rosa to the bench,

the bench to the bakery line,

the bakery line to everyone.

By noon: casseroles were moving.)

But UNofficially...

in the town's own channels...

the news ran earlier.

Dawn-early. First-light early.

The brook knew first.

(It always knows first.

Water gets around.

The hospital has pipes;

pipes talk to brooks;

don't ask; it's Willowbrook.)

The brook told the willow.

The willow let down its gold...

its biggest gold in years...

and told the hill.

And up at the watch:

"A boy," reported Stella,

who had personally attended

the arriving, dipping low

over a certain bright window

at the exact gold minute.

"Small and loud and perfect.

Theo."

"Whoo," said Oliver Owl...

which, for once,

was not a question.

"Mmmmm," hummed the Moon...

the receiving hum,

the welcoming one,

the one she hums

exactly once per citizen.

A new account, opened.

A new window to watch.

A new small light

in the town's long ledger.

The watch adjusted itself...

wider now, by one...

the way it has

for every arrival

since the town began.

Welcome, Theo of Willowbrook.

Your sister is on her way,

eating toast at a run.

Your whole life

is about to begin

with her face.

There are worse ways.

There is, the watch agrees,

no better one.

Goodnight, no.

Good MORNING, Willowbrook.

Somebody's here.
