Night 221

Theo's First Storm

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

A milestone this week...

the kind the baby books

don't put on charts:

THEO'S FIRST TANTRUM.

The trigger: the latched

cabinet. (Not his cupboard...

the LOCKED one. He wanted

in. The lock held. The

world betrayed him.)

The storm: tiny. Mighty.

Backward-arching. Fists

pounding tile. The full

nine-month fury.

And the house's response...

watch the layers:

Mama: the calm. The ground.

(Veteran of the veteran's

storms. Steady as ever.)

And the commentary booth,

rug-side, providing expert

analysis:

"Dat's his first REAL one,

Mama. Look at him GO.

He's SO mad. His words

are far away, he doesn't

even HAVE words yet,

his words are SO far...

Fee-oh! FEE-OH. It's

just weather! You're

da sky!"

(The doctrine. Shouted

supportively at a baby

mid-meltdown. He could

not hear it. It didn't

matter. The booth believes

in saturation coverage.)

The storm rained out

in ninety seconds...

baby storms are squalls...

and the repair hug came

from TWO directions.

"He stormed good,"

the booth concluded.

"For his first one."

He stormed good.

The weather runs in

the family.

So does the weathering.

He's got the best

forecaster in town

on his side...

and she works

for hugs.
