Night 235

What the Storm Month Made

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

Look back at this month.

The loud one. The stormy

one. The month the books

warn about.

Now count what it BUILT:

A cozy corner, hers,

chosen, stocked, used

by her own storm-sensing

choice.

A body map on the wall...

the weather charted by

its own sky.

A roar spot by the fence...

the pressure valve,

in daily service.

The four checkers...

half the storms solved

at the snack stage.

The choice umbrella...

the other half never

forming at all.

The repair ritual...

arms first, words short,

no lectures riding in.

The sorry-flower...

her own invention,

family canon now.

A grandfather's dent

in a barn door...

proof the weather

runs in the blood

and the sky wins anyway.

A papa who kneels

and repairs his own

thunder.

And one first word,

landed in the middle

of it all like sun

through a storm gap:

Livy.

The month the books

warn about.

And here's what the

books don't say loudly

enough, so this one will:

the storm months are

the building months.

Every tool on that list

got built BECAUSE the

weather came.

Calm seasons build

nothing.

Weather builds weatherers.

She's weathered now.

Corner-stocked, map-drawn,

roar-equipped, repair-

fluent.

Bring what you've got,

three.

She's ready.

Her whole family's ready.

They built it in the rain.
