Night 226

Lily's Moving Day

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

The storm's true center,

faced in daylight:

Lily IS moving.

Two streets over.

The bigger house. Rosa's

bakery is thriving, and

the new place has a real

kitchen for it, and a

yard, and a room for

Lily with a window seat.

Good news, wearing

scary clothes.

So the family did what

this family does with

scary clothes:

walked straight at them.

Moving day: attended.

Boxes: "carried."

(One box. A pillow box.

Carried the whole way.

The stagger-hug method.

Heroic.)

The new room: explored.

The window seat: tested,

approved, claimed for

Thursdays.

And then, the important

part, the WALK.

Old house to new house.

Counted in Olivia-steps.

"How far is it REALLY?"

Answer: four hundred

and something steps.

One gold-dog fence.

One good puddle pothole.

Seven minutes, at

archaeology pace.

"Dat's... CLOSE."

That's close.

Thursday-close. Spaghetti-

close. Run-there-when-

you're-six-close.

The geography, re-proven

by feet.

The storm's center,

dissolved by walking

through it.

"Bye, old house," said

Lily at the end, at her

empty old window.

"Hi, new house," said

Olivia at the new one.

Both girls. Both houses.

Both sentences.

Moving is a storm.

Friendship is the ground.

The ground, as established:

weather-proof.
