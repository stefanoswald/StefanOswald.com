Night 257

The Five Senses Walk

Month 9: Counting to Five

A new walk game,

five fingers long:

THE SENSES WALK.

One lane. Five ways

to know it.

Finger one. SEE:

"Find something to see."

The gold dog, mid-patrol.

(Waved at. Logged.)

Finger two. HEAR:

Eyes closed, holding

Papa's hand. "...Da

brook! I hear da brook

from HERE!"

(Two streets off. True.

The brook carries.

It likes being heard.)

Finger three. SMELL:

The bakery corner.

Obviously. Deliberately

routed. "CINNAMON.

Rosa's making da rolls."

(Correct. Thursday

batch. The nose knows

the schedule.)

Finger four. TOUCH:

The oak's bark. The

old rough friend.

Palm flat. "Rough.

Same as always."

(Same as always. Trees

are reliable that way.

It's most of their

charm.)

And finger five. TASTE:

saved for the corner

market. One sample

strawberry, per the

grocer's standing

generosity.

"SWEET."

Five fingers. Five doors.

One ordinary lane,

entered through all

of them...

and revealed, the way

it's revealed to anyone

who walks it slow enough:

as completely,

inexhaustibly

extraordinary.

The walk took an hour.

The lane is three

hundred yards.

Perfect math.

The best distances

are measured in

noticing.
