Night 343

The Party List

Month 12: Almost Three

The planning summit.

Third annual.

And this year, the

subject sits AT the

table, with opinions,

as a full member.

"Okay, birthday girl.

What kind of party?"

And she recited...

unprompted, verbatim,

the family constitution

she's absorbed through

two birthdays and

every family

celebration since:

"Small and ours."

(The doctrine. From

the mouth of its

third beneficiary.

The parents exchanged

the eyebrow. Some

things you hope are

soaking in. Everything

soaks in. That's the

entire shelf's thesis.)

The list, hers:

"Wiwi. Obviously."

(Obviously.)

"June. She's new but

she's OURS now."

(The circle, widened

and kept.)

"Mateo. He's my pit

crew boss."

"Gampa. Da aunts.

Rosa, she MAKES da

cake, she has to

COME to da cake."

"Da plant-house teacher.

Da recruit.

And Fee-oh."

("Theo lives here,

love." "He still gets

INVITED. Everybody

should get invited.

Even da ones who

live here."

...Noted. He got a

card. He ate part

of it. He treasured

it, in his way.)

Games: "hide and seek.

I count now."

Menu: "spa-ghetti?"

(Spaghetti at a

birthday? The summit

considered... and

approved. Her party.

Her institution.

Rosa, consulted,

wept briefly and

agreed to cater.)

Small, and ours,

and noodles.

The best constitution

in town,

amended by its

newest citizen,

perfectly.
