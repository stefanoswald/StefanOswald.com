Night 220

Papa's Storm

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

True-story book.

Here's the truest one

this month:

Papa stormed.

The paint spilled...

the big jar, the new rug,

the third spill of a

long day at the end

of a long week...

and Papa yelled.

"OLIVIA! NO! I SAID

CAREFUL!"

Loud. Sharp. Grown-up

thunder.

And she went still...

the frozen kind of still...

eyes wide, chin wobbling.

And Papa caught himself.

Mid-storm. Caught it.

Breathed. (The flower one.

Her breath. Borrowed back.)

And then he did the

bravest thing in this

whole month of storms:

he knelt down and

repaired it.

"I yelled. That was

my storm. You spilled

paint, that's just

an accident. Accidents

need help, not thunder.

I'm sorry, Olivia.

I'll try better."

She studied him a

long moment.

"...You got weather too?"

"Everybody's got weather.

Papas too. Papas

especially, sometimes."

"Did you do da breaf?"

"I did the breath."

"...Good job, Papa."

(Good job, Papa.

Forgiveness, at two-

almost-three, arrives

faster than anywhere

else on earth.)

The paint cleaned up.

The rug survived.

And the realest lesson

of the month got taught

by accident:

storms happen to

everyone.

Repair is what makes

a family.

Sorry is a grown-up

word too.

The strongest ones

use it kneeling.
