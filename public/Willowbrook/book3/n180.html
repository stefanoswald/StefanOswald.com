Night 180

The Big Sister Badge

Month 6: Big Sister School

Bedtime. Month's end.

And Mama comes in

with something behind her back.

"Big Sister School

has finished its first term.

The faculty met."

(The faculty is Mama and Papa.

The meeting was ten minutes

on the couch, both of them

a little teary. Standard

academic proceedings.)

"And the faculty decided...

you've earned this."

From behind the back:

a badge.

Homemade. Felt. A gold star

stitched on it, needlework

begged off the aunts by wire,

rushed south for tonight.

It says. Mama reads it,

her finger under the words...

BIG SISTER.

FIRST CLASS.

Pinned on. Right over the heart.

"For teaching him peekaboo.

For the towers you build

just to let him knock.

For answering his ba-bas.

For sharing Duck

and keeping Baba...

both, correctly.

For every fetch and coach

and gentle finger.

For the first smile

and the first laugh...

which chose you,

because you showed up

the most."

The badge-wearer stands

very tall.

Statue-of-herself tall.

Proud, the month-one word.

Earned, the whole way.

"Term two?" she asks.

Term two runs forever, love.

The school never closes.

The badge never comes off.

(It came off in the bath.

It went back on after.

FOREVER, she said.

Forever it is.)
