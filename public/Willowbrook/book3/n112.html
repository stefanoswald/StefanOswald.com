Night 112

The Waiting Games

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

The chain says: any day.

The baby says: not yet.

And in between stretches

the hardest kind of time...

the ANY-DAY days.

Impatient's whole home turf.

So the family plays

the waiting games.

(Busy hands. Steady heart.

The cookie-timer wisdom,

scaled up to a season.)

Extra park mornings...

long ones, unhurried,

Papa and Olivia while

Mama rests her moon.

The swings ridden to the sky.

The best stick: visited.

Double stories at night.

(Waiting rates.)

And the great project:

the fort renovation.

Because the fort's saved spot...

the baby's spot...

needed upgrading.

New cushion. Softest one.

The plush cow, transferred

to guard duty.

A drawing on the wall...

(a duck; the theme continues)...

"so it's not boring in dere

for da baby."

The days fill. The days pass.

That's the whole secret

of any-day days:

you can't hurry the baby,

but you can love the waiting

until it's not waiting anymore...

just living,

with something wonderful

leaning closer

every single night.
