Night 133

The Bath Audience

Month 5: Theo Arrives

Theo's bath tonight.

The little basin. The sponge era.

(His cord stump just waved

goodbye. She was told.

She has QUESTIONS. Later.)

And in attendance,

on the star stool,

broadcasting live:

the audience.

"Now Mama washes his FACE.

Just water. No soap on faces.

Dat's da rule."

(It is the rule.

She grew up under it.

Now she announces it.

The rituals witness their

own graduates. It's the way.)

"Now da creases. Babies got

CHEESE in dere creases."

("Milk," corrects Mama, laughing.

"Milk in the creases."

"Cheese," maintains the broadcast.

The record shows: sometimes cheese.)

"Now quick-quick wrap!

Babies get cold FAST.

Dere. Burrito."

The burrito is achieved.

The audience approves.

And then her one request,

same as every bath night:

"Can I do da toes?"

She can do the toes.

One small towel corner,

ten impossibly small toes,

dried one by one

by hands that were

this small once,

in this same basin,

under this same lamp.

"All clean, Fee-oh.

You smell like da beginning."

(Like the beginning.

Nobody taught her that.

Some things you just know

from having been there

recently yourself.)
