Night 154

The Rolling Race

Month 6: Big Sister School

Gym hours. The mat.

The coach at her post.

And today, the student...

five months strong,

inch-economy compounding...

has been rocking sideways

all week.

Loaded. Everyone can see it.

"You can DO it, Fee-oh.

Roll! Like dis!"

(The coach demonstrates.

Full roll, mat's length.

Teaching by body.

The oldest pedagogy.)

And Theo...

watching her...

rocks...

rocks...

HEAVES...

and ROLLS.

Tummy to back. All the way.

One mighty baby-quake

of a roll.

He looks stunned.

The world just SPUN.

And before he can decide

how to feel about it,

his coach decides for him:

"YOU ROLLED! YOU ROLLED!

MAMA! HE ROLLED!

I TAUGHT HIM! I SHOWED HIM

AND HE DID IT!"

The stunned face catches

her joy, joy is catching,

it's the most catching thing

alive, and breaks

into the new laugh.

Rolled AND laughing.

A banner day at the gym.

(The record, for fairness:

babies roll on their own

schedule. The coaching

didn't make the roll.

But the coach's face was

the first thing he found

when the world stopped

spinning, and THAT,

the books agree,

is worth every hour

of the mat.)

"Tomorrow: da OTHER way,"

announces the coach.

Term two proceeds.

The student is thriving.

The staff is insufferable.

The gym has never

been better run.
