Night 34

Pouring Her Own Milk

Month 2: Me Do It

Big morning.

The little pitcher debuts.

(Small. Half full. Chosen

for exactly this moment.)

"Me pour it."

Me pours it.

Two hands. Deep focus.

The tongue comes out...

the concentration flag.

Tip... tip...

glug...

Milk in the cup!

Also: milk NEXT to the cup.

(A moderate lake.)

Also: one drip on the sock.

(The defeated sock. Bad week for it.)

But look at the cup:

MOSTLY FULL.

Poured by Olivia.

"The cloth is right there,"

says Mama. Calm as ever.

Wipe, wipe. Lake: managed.

Here's the house rule now:

spills aren't failures.

Spills are tuition.

You pay a few lakes

to learn a river.

She'll pour for herself forever.

The lake dries.

The skill stays.

Best trade there is.
