Night 277

The Tricycle

Month 10: Try Again

A delivery from two

streets over:

Mateo, wheeling something.

Red. Three-wheeled.

A BELL on it.

"My old trike. I got

a big-kid bike now.

Mom says it's yours

if you want it."

(The lane's economy:

everything flows downhill,

scooter to trike to

wagon, house to house,

generation of kids to

generation. The trike

has carried three

children already.

It's barely broken in.)

IF SHE WANTS IT.

The mounting: immediate.

The bell: rung eleven

times.

The pedaling:

...

The pedaling.

Feet ON pedals: yes.

Pedals moving: no.

Push forward: the pedals

resist. Push HARD:

one lurch, then stuck.

The trike: unmoved.

The rider: increasingly

of the hot chest.

Mateo, from his bike,

with the wisdom of

five-and-a-half:

"Yeah, pedals are dumb

at first. Your legs

gotta learn the circle.

Took me a WEEK."

A week?!

"Maybe less for you.

You're pretty strong."

(The faculty's blessing.

It landed. It always

lands, from the big

kids.)

The circle. The legs.

The week.

The month's next

mountain, red and

belled, parked by

the porch.

"Da trike wins today,"

she ruled, dismounting.

Today.

The word doing its

quiet work already.

Round one: trike.

The rematch: daily.
