Night 326

The Blanket for June's Baby

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

News from the new

family: June's mama

is expecting.

A baby. Coming in

the fall.

And Olivia knows...

knows in her bones,

knows from her own

book, what happens

now:

"DA BASKET. Mama.

Dey need DA BASKET."

The basket. The

blessingway. The meals

and the wool and the

folded wisdom, the

village welcome that

provisioned Theo's

arrival, assembling

now for a family

too new to know

it was coming.

The bench organized.

(The bench always

organizes. June's mama

will learn.)

And Olivia's contribution,

worked on for two

weeks with Mama's

hands over hers:

one knitted square.

Small. Holey. Lumpy.

(Braid-soil grade.

It bakes up the same.)

One square of a

blanket the whole

bench was making...

every family one

square, all joined:

the town, literally

stitched together,

for one new baby.

Her square went in

crooked.

It stayed crooked.

(The blanket-joiner...

the elder aunt, by

mail, left it crooked

ON PURPOSE. "The

crooked square is

how you find the

love in a blanket.

Every good one has

one.")

The basket delivered.

The new mama cried.

June showed everyone

which square was

her friend's.

The economy, paid

forward.

The circle, widened.

The town, doing the

thing the town does...

now with one more

pair of small hands

in the doing.

That's how it keeps

going, this place.

That's exactly how.
