Night 256

Five Kisses, Distributed

Month 9: Counting to Five

Bedtime. The kiss count...

the old uncountable

custom, got NUMBERED

tonight.

By its recipient.

Who is in charge of

all counts now.

"FIVE kisses. I say

where."

The distribution, as

directed:

"One: forehead."

(Delivered.)

"Two: dis cheek."

"Free: DIS cheek."

"Four: da nose."

(The boop-kiss. Formal.)

"...And five..."

A pause. The planner

planning.

"...five is for FEE-OH.

You carry it. He's

sleeping. Put it on

his head. Tell him

it's from me."

And so the fifth kiss

traveled.

Down the hall. Into

the little room. Onto

a sleeping sandy head...

"from your sister"...

where it soaked in

per the old kiss

physics, keeping the

spot warm all night,

per regulations.

It became the standing

order.

Every night from this

one: four kisses kept,

one kiss forwarded.

The courier never

missed a delivery.

And on the nights...

years of them ahead...

when the two of them

would quarrel by day,

tower and knock, storm

and slam,

the fifth kiss still

went down the hall.

Every night. Unbroken.

Some arithmetic is

really architecture.

Four plus one,

holding two rooms

together,

in the dark,

forever.
