Night 292

The Try-Again Museum

Month 10: Try Again

The month's end deserved

a museum.

So they built one.

The low shelf, cleared.

The exhibits, installed

by the curator herself:

THE PUZZLE BOX.

(Placard, dictated:

"It was too hard.

Den I grew at it.

19 days.")

THE TRIKE BELL.

(Unscrewed for display,

re-screwed after.

"Da hill taught my

legs. Bring-bring.")

THE KITE, folded.

("It couldn't fly and

DEN it could. Da sky

took it. Da string

hums.")

THE DUCK FOLDER.

("How da duck got good.

We don't crumple

teachers.")

ONE BAND-AID, unused.

(From the pool. "Brave

is scared plus anyway.

I'm coming back anyway."

Nobody's sure why the

band-aid represents

this. The curator was

firm. Curators are.)

And one exhibit on

loan from the garage:

A BRACKET.

(Papa's. From shelf

draft one. Placard:

"Da fall taught him.

Even papas. Even

shelfs.")

The museum ran a week.

Visitors: everyone.

Theo (twice daily,

supervised, exhibits

defended). Lily

(Thursday; left her

own loan: a whistle

diagram, "for when

Livy's whistle comes."

It's coming. It's a

yet.) Grandpa Joe

(Sunday; studied every

placard at rocking

speed; said "well now"

at the bracket;

donated, before leaving,

one small item for

the permanent collection:

a smooth pine block.

Placard, his own words:

"Sanded by the curator,

age 2. First thing

she ever made smooth.

Everything since:

same method.")

Everything since:

same method.

The museum closed

Sunday night.

The method never did.
