Night 94

The Baby's Room

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

The little room...

the one that held boxes...

is becoming something.

Papa rebuilt the crib in it.

(HER old crib! The old country!

"Da baby gets my crib?"

The baby gets your crib.

"...Good," she rules, finally.

"It's a good crib.

I sleeped GREAT in it.")

The dresser is filling

with impossibly small clothes...

her old ones, down from the attic,

washed soft again.

The tiny socks! LOOK at them!

(She puts one on her thumb.

It fits her THUMB.

Was she ever this little?

She was. There are photos.

She still doesn't believe them.)

Her jobs, self-assigned:

folding the small things.

(Wads. Style. Approved.)

Placing the stuffed guard...

the plush cow from the fair...

in the crib corner.

("He watches till da baby comes.

Like Baba watched me.")

The room fills, week by week.

Small and warm and waiting.

A whole country,

prepared for a citizen

who hasn't landed yet.

She stands in the doorway

some evenings, surveying.

"Almost ready, baby,"

she reports to the tummy

at dinner.

Almost ready.

Everything here almost is.
