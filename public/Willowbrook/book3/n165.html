Night 165

Nurse Olivia's Rounds

Month 6: Big Sister School

Theo caught his first cold.

(The plant-house economy.

Everyone pays the tax.

She paid it at his age too.)

Snuffly. Rattly. Pitiful.

And the house's smallest

medical staff reported

for duty at once.

The rounds, as performed:

Tissues: fetched at speed.

(The service is famous for it.)

The humidifier: watched.

("Da mist is his white sea,

but WET," she explained.

Not wrong, honestly.)

The extra milk runs: escorted.

And the treatment only

she can administer:

the song. Crib-side.

In doses. Wrong notes entire.

He snuffled. He settled.

He slept, congested,

guarded, sung-to.

"Will he be better?" she asked,

at her own bedtime,

the worry showing.

"Three days," said Mama.

"Colds are three-day visitors.

You had a hundred of them.

Look how you turned out."

Three days it was.

On the third morning:

the restored shriek,

the mat demanded,

the face requested.

"MY PATIENT IS BETTER,"

announced the staff.

Her patient was better.

First case: closed.

The nurse kept the tissues

stocked for years.

Somebody always

catches something.

Somebody always

fetches at speed.
