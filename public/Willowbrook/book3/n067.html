Night 67

The Standing Playdate

Month 3: A Friend Called Lily

After spaghetti night,

the mamas made it official.

Thursdays.

Every Thursday. Alternating houses.

The standing playdate.

(It stood before, sort of...

the traded Tuesdays, the benches.

But that was the mamas' trade.

This one belongs to the GIRLS.

Big difference. Huge.)

Thursday comes.

The doorbell.

And the greeting...

you'd think it had been years.

"WIWI!!"

"LIVY!!"

Running hug. Spinning. Shrieks.

(It has been two days.

Two. Days.

Friendship at two doesn't

do math. It just does joy,

at full volume, every time.)

In they go, to the business:

the fort. The blocks. The cupboard.

Two bosses. One agenda. Chaos.

Perfect.

At pickup: the clinging protest.

"Five more minutes", granted.

The long goodbye. The wave

from the window. Both windows.

Thursday: the week's new sun.

Everything else orbits it now.

"How many sleeps till Wiwi?"

Two sleeps.

Count them down, little one.

Fridays were made for missing.

Thursdays were made for this.
