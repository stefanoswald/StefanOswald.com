Night 102

Grandpa Builds Again

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

Sunday. The workshop.

And on the bench:

new oak. Cut fresh.

Small pieces. Chair-shaped pieces.

"Anodder chair, Gampa?"

"Another chair."

He doesn't say more.

He doesn't have to.

She knows whose.

The star template comes down

off its nail...

the same one. HER star's mother.

Traced onto the new back rail.

"Same star?"

"Same star. Every child

in this family gets one chair

and one law. You remember

the law?"

She remembers.

"Always a seat at da farm."

"That's the one."

And then he hands her

the sanding block.

HER sanding block. From before.

"This chair needs smoothing.

Big sisters sand

little ones' chairs.

That's how it's always gone."

So she sands.

Shff... shff...

Her brother-or-sister's chair,

smoothed by her own hands,

weeks before they exist.

An hour of it. Gold dust light.

"Smooth as a duck's back?"

He runs his thumb along it.

"Smoother. You're getting good."

The two chairs will sit

side by side someday.

Two stars. One law.

The workshop hums on,

building the family

one seat at a time.
