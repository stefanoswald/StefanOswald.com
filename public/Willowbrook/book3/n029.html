Night 29

Henrietta Has a Bad Day

Month 1: Big Feelings Have Names

Even chickens have days.

Henrietta is having one.

Her feathers are ruffled wrong.

Somebody, the record shows

the Duchess, took her nest spot.

Her bok has gone flat.

Bok. Bok. (Sad trombone.)

Olivia knows this weather.

She's studied it all month.

"Hen-etta... sad?"

"Reckon so," says Grandpa Joe.

"Or thereabouts. Chicken-sad."

Olivia kneels at the fence.

Applies the kit, through the wire:

Soft voice: "It's okay."

Slow blinks. (Cat-learned. Universal.)

And one dandelion,

pushed gently through the fence.

Henrietta regards the flower.

Bok?

She pecks it. Keeps it.

Struts off, a little straighter.

"Well now," says Grandpa.

"You speak chicken feelings."

She speaks ALL feelings now.

That's the month's whole diploma.

The kit works on everybody.

Hens included.

Especially hens, maybe.

Nobody ever asks about theirs.
