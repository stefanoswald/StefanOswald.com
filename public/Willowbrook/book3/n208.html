Night 208

The Sunday Suit Question

Month 7: Potty Power

Sunday morning. The farm day.

And a wardrobe standoff:

she wants THE DRESS.

The fancy one. The twirly

one. The aunts' needlework

masterpiece.

For the FARM.

Where the mud lives.

Where Henrietta lives.

Where the berry rows

stain everything they touch.

The old way: "No. Play

clothes." (Storm guaranteed.)

This house's way:

the two-yeses craft,

now in its master era:

"The dress OVER play

clothes? Or the dress

FOR dinner after?"

She considers.

Counter-offers:

"Da dress. Wif my BOOTS.

And da old shirt UNDER.

If mud comes, da dress

goes in da truck."

...

The negotiated outfit:

twirly dress, red boots,

undershirt insurance,

truck-evacuation clause.

Legendary. Photographed.

(The aunts got a print.

The aunts framed it.

The aunts frame

everything. It's their

whole love language,

in wood and glass.)

The mud came, of course.

The clause executed.

The dress rode home

clean in the truck

while its owner berry-

stained the undershirt

to glory.

Everybody won.

That's what the craft

is FOR.

Sovereignty, weather-proofed.

Style, farm-proofed.

The twirl happened

at dinner,

per the treaty,

spotless.
