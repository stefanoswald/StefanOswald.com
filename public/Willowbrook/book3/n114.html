Night 114

The Just-Us Day

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

Before the baby comes,

Mama declared a holiday.

Not on any calendar.

The JUST-US day.

One whole day. The two of them.

The old routes. The good ones.

The bakery, first.

(One celebration bun, split.

Rosa, knowing everything

as always: "Take your time

today, you two." They did.)

The library. The lantern desk.

One story read in the vault,

her lap, the old acoustics.

The pond. The ducks...

all of them, counted, greeted,

named by the founding coin.

Lunch at the corner place.

(The crust. The booth. The works.)

And home for the nap...

taken TOGETHER, in the big bed,

the moon-bump between them,

three hearts in one blanket.

No hurry anywhere. All day.

Mama memorizing her.

This exact her. The last-chapter

her: two years old, one bow,

questions and sauce stains

and the whole world coming.

"Good day?" at bedtime.

"BEST day."

Then, sleepy, the wisdom

she didn't know she had:

"We do just-us days AFTER too?"

Yes. Oh, yes.

That's the whole point, little one.

Just-us survives everything.

It just has to get scheduled

on purpose now.

First one: already promised.

The calendar of two:

continued, forever,

inside the calendar of four.
