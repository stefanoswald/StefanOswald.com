Night 361

The Wheelbarrow

Month 12: Almost Three

The garage. The sheet.

The moment.

Grandpa Joe, hat off,

per the register...

pulled it away:

A WHEELBARROW.

Child-sized. Oak.

One wheel, true and

smooth-turning. Handles

at exactly her grip

height (measured, as

ever, when nobody

was looking).

And on the side,

carved deep:

the star.

(The signature. The

family's whole visual

history: chairs, stools,

now this. Everything

he builds says the

same thing in the

same carve: you

belong here. Always

a seat. Always a

star.)

"Every gardener,"

he said, "needs her

own barrow. You've

been hauling that

watering can two-

handed for a year.

Time your equipment

grew with you."

The reception:

IMMEDIATE OPERATIONAL

PLANNING.

"I can carry da

weeds! And da

carrots! And..."

(the vision expanding

in real time)

"...FEE-OH. Fee-oh

can RIDE in it.

To da garden. I'M

DA TRACTOR NOW."

(Theo, consulted,

climbed in on the

spot. The maiden

haul: one lap of

the yard, passenger

shrieking joy,

driver bell-less

but improvising:

"BRING-BRING!")

"Well now," said the

builder, watching

his work enter

service, watching

the third generation

haul the fourth

around a lawn...

and the record notes

his eyes did the

thing, and the record

notes nobody minded,

and the record notes

the aunts got it

on camera.

A barrow. A star.

A tractor-girl.

The farm extends

its fleet.

The family extends

its everything.

Best gift: the usual

verdict.

He's never once

missed.
