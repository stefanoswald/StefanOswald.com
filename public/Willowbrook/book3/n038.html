Night 38

I Carry It

Month 2: Me Do It

Groceries. The unloading.

Papa hands out bags...

"ME CARRY IT."

She has selected...

the milk.

The whole jug.

It is one-third her body weight.

"How about the bread..."

"MILK."

The milk, then.

The journey: car to kitchen.

Distance: thirty feet.

Method: the stagger-hug.

Jug clutched to chest,

back arched, steps tiny,

the whole girl a wobbling

tower of determination.

Papa walks beside. Spotter distance.

Hands ready. Hands NOT helping.

Ten feet... the grip slips...

regripped!

Twenty feet... a lean...

corrected!

The step up, the big test...

UP. Made it.

Kitchen. Counter, too high...

floor delivery accepted.

THUD.

Delivered. Intact. Herself.

She looks up, flushed with the work:

"HEAVY."

It was. And she carried it.

That's the point of heavy things.

Somebody offers you the bread...

and you take the milk.
