Night 127

The Town Meets Theo

Month 5: Theo Arrives

The town came in waves.

(Gentle waves. The bench knows

the visitor rules cold...

this town wrote them.)

Rosa, first: bread and a peek.

Hands washed without asking.

"Oh, he's a GOOD one,"

she ruled, professionally.

(She rules on all the babies.

She's never once been wrong.)

The bench mothers: the basket's

second load. Casseroles moving

per the ancient economy.

The aunts, by wire, nightly.

(In person: two weeks. Booked.)

And Sunday, the truck...

Grandpa Joe.

Hat off at the door.

Hands washed at the sink.

And the parcel received

into arms with forty harvests

of exactly this:

"Well now," he said.

And that was all he said

for a long, long minute.

(The record notes his eyes.

The record always notes

his eyes. The family knows

what the register means.)

And through it all,

running point on every visit:

the big sister.

"DIS IS FEE-OH.

He's MY brudder.

He's loud but he's nice.

You have to wash hands.

You can hold him SITTING.

Support da head.

Da head is sleepy."

(The rules. Her rules now.

Enforced at the door.

The town complied, charmed

beyond all resistance.)

Willowbrook: introduced.

Theo: welcomed, ruled good,

casseroled, guarded.

Population of the heart: plus one.

The town ledger: updated.

It's getting to be

a full book, that ledger.

The best kind of full.
