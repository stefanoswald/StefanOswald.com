Night 185

The Something

Month 7: Potty Power

Four days of trying.

Four celebrated nothings.

And then. Tuesday

(of course Tuesday;

check this family's records;

Tuesday owns the milestones)...

morning, post-breakfast,

her own idea, per doctrine:

"I try."

Seated. Book. Waiting.

And then...

her face.

The face of somebody

whose body just did

the thing.

She looked down.

She looked up.

And the announcement

rattled the windows:

"MAMA! PAPA!

SOMEFING CAME!

DA SOMEFING CAME!!"

The house arrived at a run.

(You always arrive at a run.)

Confirmation: SOMETHING,

verified, historic.

The parade was immediate

and total: the high-fives,

the phone call to the farm

("Well NOW," at full register),

the dance Papa invented

on the spot, the Potty

Champion Strut, performed

by the entire family

around the kitchen,

twice.

"I DID it. My body

LISTENED to me."

That's it exactly, little one.

That's the entire miracle

of it: the body and the self,

shaking hands.

First of thousands

of agreements you'll make

with yourself.

Flush ceremony: conducted.

(She pulled the handle.

"BYE, somefing!"

The pipes received it

per the research.)

The throne earned its name

today.

Long live the champion.

The strut is canon now.

Ask anyone.
