Night 104

When the Baby Comes, What Stays

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

Change is coming.

Big change. Good change.

But little hearts need to know

what STAYS.

So tonight: the staying list.

Said slow, at the lamp:

"When the baby comes...

your bed is still your bed.

Your bear is still your bear...

he's yours forever;

that's ancient law.

Your Thursdays are still

your Thursdays. Lily's not

going anywhere.

The song still gets sung...

same wrong notes, nightly.

The stories still get read.

YOUR stories. On MY lap.

That lap seat is reserved.

Baby or no baby.

Grandpa's farm is still

your farm. Your chair

still has your star.

And my love..."

(the candle truth, restated)

"...is still your love.

Whole. Bright. Never dimmer."

Olivia listens to the whole list.

Counting it like stars.

"What DOES change?" she asks.

Honest question. Honest answer:

"The house gets one more person

to love. And louder. And busier.

And some days I'll be

very busy with the baby...

and you might feel the pinch.

The green one. Remember?

When it pinches, TELL me.

We'll fix it. Every time."

Deal.

The staying list is long.

The love is longest.

Sleep on it, big sister.

It holds.
