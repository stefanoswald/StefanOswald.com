Night 119

The Phone Call

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

Dawn. Rosa's kitchen.

The phone, on speaker,

in the middle of the table

like a small warm sun.

And out of it: Papa's voice.

Tired. Thick. Shining.

"Olivia? You there, big sister?"

"PAPA! IS DA BABY DERE?!"

A laugh, half laugh,

half something wetter...

"He's here, baby. HE.

A little brother.

Born this morning,

just as the sun came up.

Small and loud and perfect.

Mama did so great.

Everybody's great."

A BROTHER.

A BRUDDER.

(Lily, beside her, is bouncing.

Rosa is crying into a dish towel.

The kitchen is a festival.)

"What's his NAME?!"

And down the line,

from the room where morning

started early,

Papa says it...

the small green-star name

that kept circling back

all those months:

"Theo.

His name is Theo."

Theo.

Theo. Theo. Theo.

She tries it out loud...

three times, like new shoes.

It fits. It already fits.

"When do I MEET him?!"

"Today. This morning. First.

Before anybody.

Big sisters meet first...

that's the law. Come soon.

He's waiting to find out

who's been reading to him

all this time."

Today.

This morning.

FIRST.

Eat fast, big sister.

The best introduction

of your whole life

is one car ride away.
