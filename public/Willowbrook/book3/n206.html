Night 206

Paying the Potty Forward

Month 7: Potty Power

The little potty's sunset

came this week.

The big throne: fully

scaled. The insert: routine.

The little one: unused

a whole month.

So: the question.

"What happens to my

little potty?"

Options: the basement

harbor. (Theo's turn

comes eventually...

but the farm workshop,

it turns out, is already

building him a NEW one.

Oak. You can guess

the carving.)

Or...

"The plant-house has

a new little one starting.

Littler than the recruit.

Her family could use it."

And the champion...

who once guarded a bear

with ancient law,

who knows exactly what's

soul and what's shareable...

ruled without a pause:

"Give it to da new kid.

Clean it FIRST." (Cleaned.

Thoroughly. Ceremonially.)

"And tell her da rules:

no hurry. Rain is okay.

Everybody's clock is

different."

The potty rode to the

plant-house with its

doctrine attached...

taped to the lid,

in Mama's hand,

dictated by the founder:

THE RULES.

1. No hurry

2. Rain happens

3. You're still a champion

4. Wash hands (sing a song)

The equipment flows

downstream.

The doctrine flows

with it.

That's this whole town,

in one small throne:

everything passed on,

everything taught forward,

nothing hoarded but

the soul things.

The new kid learned

in record time.

The rules were good.

The rules were hers.
