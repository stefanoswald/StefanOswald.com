Night 219

The Repair

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

Every storm has an after.

This page is about

the after...

because the after is

where the actual

parenting lives.

The storm ends. The rain

stops. And there's a

moment, every time...

where a small person

stands in the wreckage

of her own weather,

wrung out, a little

scared of what came

out of her,

and looks up.

THAT moment.

Here's what it needs:

The comeback hug.

Arms open first. Always

first. Before any words.

(The lesson of the open

arms: you cannot storm

yourself out of this

family. Nobody ever has.

Nobody ever will.)

Then the short kind words.

SHORT. "That was a big

one. You're okay. We're

okay."

And then, this is the

discipline. NO LECTURE.

No riding the moral in

on the reconciliation.

The teaching happens

later, at calm, at

bedtime, in stories.

The repair is just

repair.

"We're okay?" she asked,

into the shoulder.

"We're always okay.

Storms don't break us.

We're weather-proof."

Weather-proof.

She said it herself

at the next repair.

And the one after.

It's load-bearing now.

Some houses crack a

little at every storm.

This one seals stronger

at every repair.

That's not luck.

That's the after,

done right,

every single time.
