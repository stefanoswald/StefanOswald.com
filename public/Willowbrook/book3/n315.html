Night 315

The Gold Dog Slows Down

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

The gold dog is old.

He was old when this

book started. He was

old when SHE started...

he sniffed her stroller

on the ninth day of

her life, and waved

his tail, and filed

her under his.

And this season, the

old is showing.

The patrol: shorter now.

Half the lane, most

days. The trot: a walk.

The walk: unhurried

even by his standards.

He lies in the sun

more than he patrols.

And the lane, watch

the lane, adjusts

around him:

The kids bike SLOWER

past his gate.

The grocer saves him

ends of things.

And at the fence,

daily, his smallest

oldest friend has

changed her greeting:

no more racing him

to the corner. (He

can't. She noticed

before anyone said it.)

Now: she walks to HIM.

Sits at the fence.

One soft treat, brought

from home, offered

flat-palmed per the

ancient protocol.

And stays a while.

Slow company. His speed.

"He's OLD," she said

to Mama, gently, the

way you say important

things.

"He is. And what do

old friends need?"

"...Kindness dat goes

DEIR speed."

Kindness that goes

their speed.

(That's the whole

gerontology textbook,

little one. You just

wrote it at a fence.)

The tail still wags

for her. Slower.

Meaning the same.

Meaning more, maybe.

Old wags are all

meaning.

Sit with the slow ones,

reader.

Bring something soft.

Stay a while.

The fence teaches it

better than anywhere:

love keeps pace.

That's what makes

it love.
