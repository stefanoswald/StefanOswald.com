Night 71

Introducing the Gold Dog

Month 3: A Friend Called Lily

Remember the friend-job?

Filed at the Same Game?

"I show you gentle."

Today: the showing.

The fence. The evening patrol.

The gold dog, arriving on schedule...

and Lily, hanging back.

Way back. Behind-the-legs back.

(Big dogs are a lot,

if a big dog once barked

your whole self over.

Lily has reasons. Reasons

are allowed.)

Olivia takes the post.

"Watch me, Wiwi."

The demonstration:

Flat hand. Slow. Low.

The dog sniffs. The tail swings.

"See? He gentle.

He OLD. He nice."

Lily watches. The whole method.

Then, a half step out.

Another.

Her hand... flattens...

(Olivia's hand under it,

steadying. A friend-boost.)

Sniff.

The big warm nose, gentle

as the blanket.

The tail: swinging for TWO now.

"...He nice," whispers Lily.

He's nice.

Fear doesn't always need

a grown-up to fix it.

Sometimes it needs a friend

who goes first

and holds your hand flat.

Courage is borrowable.

That's a best-friend service.

No charge. Ever.
