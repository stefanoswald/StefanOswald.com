Night 36

Climbing Into the Car Seat

Month 2: Me Do It

New policy at the car:

"ME climb in."

So: the ascent.

Foot on the door frame...

knee on the seat edge...

haul... pivot... turn...

plop.

Seated! Self-seated!

(Time elapsed: three minutes.

Time saved by lifting her: three minutes.

Cost of lifting her: one revolution.

Papa does the math. He waits.)

But now, the buckles.

"ME do..."

No, love. Not the buckles.

Here's the line, drawn kindly:

climbing in: yours.

Buckles: mine. Always mine.

Some jobs are me-do-it jobs.

Some jobs are safety jobs.

Buckles keep you safe at speed.

Safety jobs stay with the big people.

Not forever. But for years yet.

She frowns. She accepts.

(The frown is allowed.

The buckle clicks anyway.)

Click, click. Pinch test. Snug.

"YOU climbed in," says Papa.

"I buckled. Good team."

Good team.

That's the whole arrangement,

age two:

her mountain, his knots.
