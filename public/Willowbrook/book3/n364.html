Night 364

Everything Three Holds

Month 12: Almost Three

The birthday's last

hour. The porch swing.

All four, in the dusk

gold, the new three-

year-old between her

parents, the almost-

two-year-old commanding

a lap, the swing

creaking its old

summer song.

And the survey, taken

gently, of everything

three holds:

Preschool, in the fall.

(The cubbies. Lily's

class. The whole new

country of it.)

The trike to the corner.

The big swing. The

solo farm sleepover.

(Grandpa's been ready

for three years.

"Anytime" finally

means next month.)

Chapter stories, with

cliffhangers.

A brother turning two...

her year to coach the

whole curriculum she

just lived.

The barrow. The garden.

The whistle-code with

Lily, developing.

June's baby coming,

and a crooked square

already in its blanket.

And underneath the

list, the things three

holds that don't

list...

the tools, the words,

the muscle, the lens

that finds the gold

in every gray,

the "I like dis me,"

the sky that she is,

the town that shows up,

the family word working

in its third keeper:

Maybe.

"Big year coming,"

said Papa, to the dusk.

"Da biggest," she

agreed. Then, per the

family's oldest

correction:

"So far."

So far.

(Every year: the

biggest. So far.

Forever. That's the

shelf's whole promise,

and it keeps it,

book after book

after book.)

The fireflies came

up out of the lawn.

She reached for one...

three years old now,

still reaching,

never stopping...

and this year,

for the first time,

one landed.

On her finger. Blinking.

"...Mama. Look."

We see it, little one.

The whole porch sees it.

Some years, the light

comes to you.

This is going to be

one of those.
