Night 293

The Seed Is a Yet

Month 10: Try Again

The windowsill got a

new resident this week:

one paper cup. Dirt.

And, buried, invisible...

one bean seed.

"Now what?"

"Now: water. And wait."

Day one: dirt.

Day two: dirt.

(Watered. Inspected

at length. Dirt.)

Day three: "Is it

BROKEN?"

Not broken. Working.

"But I can't SEE it

working."

And there it is...

the garden's whole

hard lesson, the one

the trike and the kite

never had to teach:

some try-agains happen

where you can't watch.

Underground. In the

dark. In the quiet.

The seed is down there

RIGHT NOW, swelling,

cracking, pushing a

root down and a shoot

up, trying again

every single hour...

and showing you

nothing.

"Da seed is a yet,"

she said slowly,

working it out at

the sill.

"A buried yet."

A buried yet.

(Write it down. The

poets work whole

careers for less.)

Day six: dirt.

Day seven: dirt.

Day eight...

a green hook.

Barely up. Bent over,

still pulling its

head out of the dark.

"IT CAME! DA YET

CAME UP!"

The yet came up.

They always do...

the watered ones,

the waited-for ones.

Not on your schedule.

On theirs.

Same as words.

Same as whistles.

Same as everything

growing quietly

in every dark

everywhere...

including, always,

the growers themselves.
