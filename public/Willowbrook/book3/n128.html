Night 128

The Hard Afternoon

Month 5: Theo Arrives

This book tells true stories.

So here's a true one:

some afternoons are hard.

Four p.m. The fog hour.

Theo: crying. The long kind.

(The witching hour. He found it.

They all find it. Book one

has a whole chapter.)

Mama: bone-tired. Rocking.

Doing the sway. The shush.

Running on the two hours

of sleep the wall allowed.

And Olivia: at the rug,

where her tower...

SIX HIGH, a record,

a masterpiece...

just got finished.

"MAMA! LOOK! LOOK MY TOWER!"

"In a minute, baby..."

The minute didn't come.

The crying ate it.

The tower stood, unseen.

And her face did the thing.

Both feelings at once...

proud with nowhere to land,

and something lonelier under it.

Here's what happened next.

Not magic. Just the family

doing its slow true thing:

Mama saw. Late, but saw.

And instead of pretending,

she said the honest words:

"Your tower is six high

and I MISSED it and

that wasn't fair.

Theo's crying is loud

and my eyes are tired.

Show me now?

I'm really looking now."

And she really looked.

Some days the house

runs out of hands.

It never runs out of true.

The tower got its witness.

Late counts.

Late, said out loud, counts double.
