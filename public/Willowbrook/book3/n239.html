Night 239

Theo Stands Alone

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

Meanwhile, because the

house never runs just

one story at a time...

THEO STOOD ALONE.

The couch's end. The

cruise's last station.

The reach for the toy...

the hand letting go...

and:

standing.

Free. Wobbling. Arms

out in the tightrope

pose his sister wore

at the same spot, a

whole book ago.

And counting him...

out loud, the official

timekeeper, the coach

of coaches:

"ONE! TWO!..."

(The room holding its

breath per the miracle-

weather statutes.)

"...FREE!!"

Three seconds. Down to

the bottom, per regulation.

The applause: seismic.

The counter: prouder

than the stander.

"FREE SECONDS! Dat's

more dan I did my

first time! (Is it,

Mama?) (It's not.)

(I'm saying it is.)

FEE-OH! You're gonna

WALK soon! And den

you're gonna RUN and

I will run WIF you

and we will run

EVERYWHERE..."

The forecast, issued

at full joy:

walking, incoming.

The schedule. HER

schedule, the one Mama

gave her in the waiting

months, is running

early. ("Second kids

run early. Dey got

a pace car." She IS

the pace car. She

knows she's the pace

car.)

The steps come soon.

(Next month's pages

know the day.)

And when they come,

the first three will

be counted out loud

by the same voice

that counted these...

the voice that is,

per the record,

his whole first word.

One. Two. Free.

The house is rising

again.

Both kids. All altitudes.

The floor, veteran of

everything, braces

happily.
