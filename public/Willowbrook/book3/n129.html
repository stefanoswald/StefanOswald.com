Night 129

The First Smile Goes To...

Month 5: Theo Arrives

Six weeks in.

And you remember

the porch-step prophecy.

Papa's story. His brother.

"The laughing part, maybe,

belongs to YOU."

Well.

The morning visit. The rail.

The step stool. The routine:

"HI FEE-OH. It's me.

Da big sister. Again. Always."

And she made the face...

the puffed-cheek one,

Papa's old classic,

inherited, drilled on the bear...

and Theo...

Theo's whole face...

OPENED.

The corners. The eyes. All of it.

A smile.

A real one. The social kind.

Aimed. At HER.

"MAMA! PAPA! COME! COME NOW!

HE SMILED! AT ME! HIS FIRST ONE!

I GOT DA FIRST ONE!"

They came running.

(You always come running.)

And Theo, showman already,

brother already...

did it AGAIN.

At her. Only at her.

The face got it a third time.

The parents got theirs

by dinnertime, but the record,

the family record, the one

that matters at every

holiday table forever:

First smile: hers.

The laughing part: claimed.

Just like the prophecy said.

"He's MINE now," she announced.

(He was hers before.

But now it's ratified.

Smiles are signatures.

He signed.)

The almanac took the entry

in three handwritings...

two big, one scribbled:

a duck, drawn next to the date.

Witness mark of the claimant.

Case closed. Brother: hers.
