Night 242

The Storm Month, Counted

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

Month's end. The audit.

The loud month, counted:

Storms weathered: per

the diary, twenty-some.

Storms self-sheltered:

four. (The trend line

is the story.)

Tools built: the corner,

the map, the roar stone,

the checkers, the diary

itself.

Repairs: every single

one delivered. Zero

storms unresolved.

Zero. The record stands.

One grandfather's dent:

discovered, honored,

instructive.

One papa's thunder:

caught, knelt, repaired...

the month's quietest

biggest lesson.

One best friend: moved

two streets, geography

re-proven by feet,

window seat certified.

One brother: standing

free, three counted

seconds, walking

incoming.

And one word.

THE word.

"Livy", first out of

the whole language,

aimed through crib bars,

the happiest tears in

the book, the bets

dissolved, the cookie

split four ways.

"Dat was a BIG month,"

ruled its survivor,

at the lamp, at the

tally's end.

The biggest yet, maybe.

The loudest, certainly.

And look what the loud

built:

a girl who forecasts

her own weather,

a family that repairs

everything it cracks,

and a baby whose first

word was his sister.

The books warn about

this month.

The books should also

promise it.

Audited. Approved.

Carried forward:

weathered, worded,

and gentler every week.
