Night 301

Spuh-Get-Ee

Month 10: Try Again

First Friday.

Spaghetti night. The

institution. Both

families, the long

table, the eternal menu.

And mid-dinner...

mid-SLURP, in fact...

the request came around,

as it always does:

"What are we eating,

Olivia?"

(The old joke. The

table asks weekly.

"Puh-sketti" is the

beloved answer. Half

the family secretly

hopes it never

graduates.)

But she'd been training.

The mirror. The bath.

The bear. Syllable

by syllable, weeks

of it: spuh. get. ee.

She put down her fork.

(The pause. She HAS

the pause.)

And summited:

"Spa-getti."

.

Clean. Whole. Both

G's where G's belong.

The table ERUPTED.

Rosa's dish towel:

deployed. Papa: on

his feet. Lily: "SHE

SAID IT! SHE PRACTICED

AND SHE SAID IT!"

(The best friend knew

about the training.

Best friends know

about all the training.)

And Theo, fork aloft,

joining the eruption

with his own version,

brand-new, instantly

canonical:

"GETTI!"

(The family lexicon,

compounding forever:

she spent a year at

puh-sketti; he opens

at getti; somewhere

in the far books their

children say both,

and nobody remembers

which came first,

and the dish is just

called that, in this

family, at this table,

in ink.)

"Spaghetti," she said

again, showing off now,

sauce to the eyebrows

per regulation.

The mouth's mountain:

flagged.

The word: hers.

The table: hers.

The whole golden

noodle-slurping

institution of it:

hers, hers, hers.

Some summits you eat.

Those are the best

ones.
