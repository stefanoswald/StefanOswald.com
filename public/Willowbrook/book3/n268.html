Night 268

The Stairs Go Past Five

Month 9: Counting to Five

The stairs. Twelve of them.

And a counter with ten

fingers and a mission.

The census:

"One, two, free, four,

five..." (hand one, spent)

"...six, seven..." (the

sequel hand, deploying;

seven now fully real,

Freddy vindicated at

last) "...eight, nine,

TEN."

Ten. Both hands. All

fingers standing.

And two stairs left.

TWO STAIRS. ZERO FINGERS.

The crisis of the age.

"PAPA. I'm OUT OF HANDS."

And Papa knelt on step

ten and offered the

great secret:

"Borrow mine."

His hand: presented.

His fingers: hers to

count on.

"...Eleven." (His thumb.)

"...TWELVE." (His pointer.)

Twelve stairs.

Ten of hers, two of his.

Counted to the top.

And at the top, the

bigger discovery,

dawning down her whole

face:

"Papa. Da numbers...

don't STOP?"

No, little one.

The numbers don't stop.

Not at five. Not at ten.

Not at any hand anyone

is holding.

They go up past the

stairs, past the stars,

past everything...

forever, borrowing

fingers all the way.

She sat down on step

twelve to absorb the

size of it.

"...Dat's LOTS-LOTS."

The lots-lots-est thing

there is.

Welcome to infinity,

little counter.

It starts at stair

eleven,

on somebody else's

thumb.

It always does.
