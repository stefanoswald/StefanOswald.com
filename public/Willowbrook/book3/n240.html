Night 240

The First Window-Seat Thursday

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

The first Thursday at

the NEW house.

(Four hundred and some

steps. One gold-dog

fence. Seven minutes.

The geography, holding

exactly as walked.)

And the window seat...

claimed on moving day,

reserved for exactly

this...

received its purpose:

two girls. Two books.

One seat built for

precisely this cargo.

The new room, toured:

bigger. The bakery smell,

already moved in and

living in the walls.

(Rosa's kitchen runs

downstairs now. The

whole house smells like

rising. Lily lives inside

a cinnamon roll.

The envy is real and

openly discussed.)

And the together-quiet...

the rare gold kind,

the couch kind from

the old house...

found its new address:

the window seat, the

street below, two books,

ten minutes of shared

silence in the new light.

"Same," said Lily,

finally, looking up.

"Different house. Same us."

Same us.

The only geography

that ever mattered,

confirmed at the new

coordinates.

Thursday: transplanted.

Rooting nicely.

The spaghetti institution

relocates next month...

first Friday, new kitchen,

same eternal menu...

and the new house,

the girls agreed,

"is broked in now."

Broken in. Blessed.

Thursday-certified.

Home is where the

window seat holds

two.
