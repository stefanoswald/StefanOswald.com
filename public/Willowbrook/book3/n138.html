Night 138

The Cry Decoder

Month 5: Theo Arrives

A month of the one-word

language, and somebody

in this house has gone fluent.

Not the parents.

(They're good. Veterans.

Book-one graduates.)

Somebody FASTER.

The crying starts, the monitor

crackles, and before

either grown-up moves:

"Dat's da hungry one."

...

It was the hungry one.

Next day: "Dat's da tired one.

He rubbed his face at lunch."

(He had. It was.)

Next: "Dat's da UP cry.

He wants up. He's bored

of da bouncy chair."

(Correct. Again.)

The parents stare at her.

"How do you KNOW?"

She shrugs. The professional

shrug of the bilingual.

"He TELLS you.

Hungry is short-short-short.

Tired is whiny.

Up is mad-but-not-really."

(Book one, chapter two,

reinvented from scratch

by a two-and-a-half-year-old

with a month of listening.)

Here's why she's faster:

she remembers, somewhere

under memory, what it was

to have one word

for everything...

and she LISTENS like it matters,

because he's hers.

The house appoints her

official translator.

Duties: ongoing.

Accuracy rate: humbling.

The baby has a voice.

The voice has a sister.

Nothing he says

goes unheard in this house.

Nothing ever will.
