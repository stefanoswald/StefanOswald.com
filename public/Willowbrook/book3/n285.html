Night 285

Spaghetti, the Word

Month 10: Try Again

There is a word she

cannot say.

THE word. The family

word. The first-Friday,

sauce-on-eyebrows,

founding-story word:

Spaghetti.

Current edition:

"puh-SKETTI."

(Adorable. Beloved.

Rosa has threatened

to rename the dish

officially. But its

speaker has standards...

her EYE outran her

HAND at the ducks;

now her EAR outruns

her MOUTH. She can

hear that puh-sketti

is wrong. She wants

the real one.)

So: training. Her idea.

At the mirror. (The old

colleague, drafted as

speech coach.)

The word, broken into

mountains:

"Spuh."

"Spuh." Got it.

"GET."

"Get." Easy.

"EE."

"Ee." Baby stuff.

Now assemble:

"Spuh-get-ee."

"...puh-SKETTI."

(The pieces hold alone.

The assembly collapses.

Like the early towers.

Exactly like them.)

Again. Slow. Caterpillar

pace:

"Spuh... get... ee."

"Spuh... get... ee..."

"SpuhGETee."

CLOSE. SO CLOSE.

Daily drills, self-

assigned. In the mirror.

In the bath. To the

bear. ("Spuh-get-ee,

Baba. You try." He

declined verbally

but supported morally.)

The word is coming.

(First Friday is coming

too. The far pages

have circled the date.

Some payoffs deserve

a full table.)

"I can't say it..."

she reported at bedtime,

and caught herself,

and deployed the tool:

"...YET."

Yet.

The mouth's mountain,

half-climbed,

flagged for the summit.
