Night 140

When Will He PLAY?

Month 5: Theo Arrives

An honest complaint today.

Filed at the block bin:

"Mama. When will Fee-oh PLAY?"

Because look at him.

The blocks are RIGHT THERE.

The fort has his SPOT.

The curriculum is PREPARED.

And he just...

sleeps. Grips. Eats. Repeats.

"He's small, love.

Playing comes in pieces.

Want the schedule?"

YES. She wants the schedule.

Mama gives it true:

"This summer: he'll laugh.

You can make him laugh...

that's a game. The first one.

By fall: he'll grab toys.

You hand, he grabs. A game.

By winter: peekaboo. Real games.

He'll chase you crawling.

By his birthday: he'll stack

a block on your tower.

Badly. You'll let him.

And by NEXT summer...

when you're three and big...

he'll run behind you

everywhere you go,

forever, calling your name."

Olivia absorbs the schedule.

The long arc of it.

"...Forever?"

Forever. That's the deal

with little brothers.

You wait a year for a playmate

and get one for life.

She looks at the sleeping

gripping eating repeating

future-playmate.

"Okay," she rules at last.

"I wait. But I'm keeping

da fort spot ready."

Keep it ready, big sister.

He's coming.

Every week, a piece closer.

The waiting is the fort's

whole first game...

and you're already winning it.
