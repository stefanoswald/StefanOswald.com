Night 264

Three Fingers for the Birthday

Month 9: Counting to Five

The counting month's

last assignment...

issued by the calendar

itself:

the birthday is coming.

(Three-ish months out.

The season after next.

But rehearsals start

early in this family.

The two-finger trick

took weeks. The three

needs lead time.)

The old answer: "TWO."

Two fingers. Mastered.

Public-certified.

The upgrade:

"You'll be THREE.

Show me three."

The attempt: four

fingers.

"...Free?"

Close. The engineering

review: the pinky

won't stay down alone.

The ring finger is

loyal to it. They

travel as a pair.

The fix, the old fix,

the two-trick's own

method:

THE THUMB.

The thumb holds the

pinky down. Pointer,

tall one, ring one:

UP.

"FREE!"

Three. Certified.

Wobbling but willing.

Drilled thereafter at

every counter per

tradition:

the mirror ("How old

will Livy be?" the

mirror girl shows

three back, always

in sync, the old

colleague reliable

to the last),

the market clerk

(deadpan, saintly:

"Three! A big year."),

the farm ("Well now.

Three." Rock. Rock.

"The chair'll still

fit. I built it

roomy.")

The answer: loaded.

The birthday: incoming.

The hand: ready,

one thumb-pinned

pinky at a time.

Three.

The biggest number

she's ever been.

So far.

(So far. The hand

knows. The hand has

two more fingers

and a sequel.

Everything in its

season.

The caterpillar

paces these things.)
