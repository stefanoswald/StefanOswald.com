Night 275

Freddy's Famous Miss

Month 10: Try Again

The brook. The famous jump.

The one Freddy performs

daily. The whole-brook

crossing. The ta-daa.

"OBSERVE!" he announced,

per the liturgy. "THE

LEGENDARY LEAP!"

Backed up. Wiggled.

JUMPED...

and missed.

SPLASH.

Not the ta-daa splash.

The OTHER splash. The

short one. The middle-

of-the-brook, nowhere-

near-the-far-bank splash.

He surfaced in the reeds,

lily-pad hat askew,

stunned silent...

(Freddy. Silent. The

brook itself went quiet

at the novelty.)

in front of EVERYONE.

Olivia. Tessa. Two

dragonflies. The whole

afternoon audience.

And here's why this

page matters:

she had never seen

Freddy fail.

Nobody had. The showman

shows only the ta-daas.

That's what showmen do...

and what watching children

learn wrong from them:

that the great ones

never miss.

"...Freddy?" she called.

"You okay?"

A long pause in the reeds.

Then, quieter than

anyone had ever heard him:

"...I've never missed

before."

"Everybody misses,"

said Tessa, arriving

at her speed. "You've

just never done it

in daylight.

Now, the interesting

question:"

(the turtle's version

of the owl's office)

"...what does the

legendary Freddy do

NEXT?"

Tomorrow's page knows.

The reeds held their

breath.

So did the audience.
