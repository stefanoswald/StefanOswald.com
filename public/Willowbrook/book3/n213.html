Night 213

The Season of Storms

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

A weather report tonight,

before the season starts:

Storm season is coming.

(It's already knocking.

Three approaches, and three

brings the big weather.

Every book about every

child says so. This book

says it kindest.)

Here's why, told plain:

Olivia is bigger now.

Her wants are bigger.

Her plans are bigger.

Her ideas are ENORMOUS.

But her words, growing

fast as they are...

still can't carry

everything she means.

And when a big want

meets a small can't...

thunder.

That's all a tantrum is:

a feeling too big

for its bag.

The bag rips. The weather

gets out. Loud.

"Will you be mad at

my storms?" she asked,

hearing the forecast.

"Never at the storm.

Storms aren't naughty.

Storms are weather.

We might not like the

thunder, but we'll

never once stop loving

the sky."

The season is coming.

The house is ready.

It stocked up on patience

and named everything

in advance...

which is, as always,

half the taming.

Let it rain, little sky.

We built this house

for weather.
