Night 159

First Foods, Second Audience

Month 6: Big Sister School

A big day at the table.

Theo's first FOOD.

(Six months. Sitting steady.

Watching the forks like

a hawk in a bib.

All the signs. It's time.)

The audience: assembled.

Front row: his sister,

who has been WAITING for this.

("He eats wif us now?

He's a TABLE GUY now?")

The first spoonful:

iron-rich mash, per the books.

Approaching...

contact...

and THE FACE.

The historic face.

Outrage and intrigue,

in full parliament,

over one gram of breakfast.

The audience LOSES IT.

"HIS FACE! MAMA! HIS FACE!

Dat's da face I made!

Show him MY face!"

(The photo, the fridge one,

the sweet-potato landslide,

eyebrows to hairline...

is fetched and displayed

beside the sputtering brother.

The resemblance: legally binding.

The table: weeping with laughter.)

Spoonful two: met halfway.

Spoonful three: demanded.

A table guy. Confirmed.

And his sister, already

planning the curriculum:

"Wait till you get NANA.

And da sweet potato.

And SPAGHETTI, Fee-oh.

First Friday. You'll see.

Da sauce goes on your EYEBROWS.

It's da best night we have."

It is, little one.

And now it seats six eaters.

Welcome to the table, Theo.

Your sister saved you

everything good...

including the face.

ESPECIALLY the face.

It's the family crest,

worn once per eater,

at first mash,

forever.
