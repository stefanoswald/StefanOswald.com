Night 321

The Thank-You Round

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

The dinner table has

the feeling check.

(One feeling each,

nightly, since the

storm months.)

This month it gained

a sibling custom:

THE THANK-YOU ROUND.

One thank-you each.

But, the rule that

makes it work...

aimed. At a person.

By name.

Not "thanks for dinner."

(Loose gratitude.

Floats away.)

Instead:

Papa: "Thank you, Olivia,

for setting my spoon

backwards. It's very

continental."

Mama: "Thank you, Theo,

for the dandelion you

put in my shoe. It was

a surprise."

Olivia: "Fank you, Mama,

for da crust part.

You always give me

da crust part and

you LIKE da crust part."

(...The table went

quiet a beat at that

one. She'd noticed.

They always notice.

The givers think the

giving is invisible.

It never was.)

And Theo: "Fank oo...

LIVY."

(For nothing specific.

For everything. His

standing position.)

Gratitude with an

address arrives.

Gratitude without one

just circles the

airport.

Name your thanks,

little ones.

Send them where

they live.
