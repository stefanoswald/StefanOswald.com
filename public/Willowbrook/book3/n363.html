Night 363

Bobby Bear's Letter at Three

Month 12: Almost Three

Dear Olivia,

Three. THREE. I've

now held you longer

than the dresser held

me, and I want it

known: crib-side was

always the better post.

The year in review,

from your left arm:

You cried into my

fur four times. (The

whistle year was long.

I absorbed what I

could. It's the job.

It's the honor.)

You told me every

secret. They're safe.

Bears are vaults.

Even the one about

who REALLY ate the

last star cookie.

(Especially that one.)

You taught your

brother to be gentle

with my ear, and he

is, mostly, and when

he isn't, you defend

me like ancient law.

I am the most protected

bear in the county.

Possibly the state.

And now, the part

I've been practicing

how to say:

Little one, something

is happening to me.

In your dreams lately...

have you noticed?...

I've been... walking.

Talking. Coming along

on things.

The older bears say

it happens, when a

bear is loved hard

enough, long enough:

we start showing up

INSIDE the stories,

not just beside them.

Next year. I have

it on good authority

(the moon; she

countersigns everything)...

next year, when you're

three-and-more and

your pretend grows

its full wings...

watch for me.

I might do more

than watch.

That's all I can say.

The paperwork is

still processing.

Happy birthday, my girl.

Three years of nights,

and I have loved

every single shift.

Yours in fur and

ancient law,

B. Bear

(Crib Division. Heart:

patchwork. Ear: yours.

Next posting: pending.

Wonderfully pending.)

(Across the hall, the

plush cow read his

copy and stood a

little straighter.

Changes were coming

to the whole soft

division.

The best kind.

Book four kind.)
