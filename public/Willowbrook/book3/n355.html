Night 355

Mama's Letter at Three

Month 12: Almost Three

The kitchen. The latest

hour. The lamp low.

The annual commission:

Mama's letter. Third

edition. For the

someday-asking.

"Dear Olivia. Livy,

Livy-loo, the whistle

of this house...

You are almost three,

and this year you

became the thing I

always suspected you'd

be: somebody's safe

place. Your brother's,

mostly. But I watch

you now, at fences,

at benches, at the

sandbox edge where

new kids hover...

and I see it: people

feel better where

you are. That is the

rarest thing, baby.

Smarter than smart.

Better than tall.

This year you taught

me things. That's

not a figure of

speech. The bench

with Lily, sitting,

staying, fixing

nothing. I have

started doing that.

At hard visits. In

hard rooms. It works.

My almost-three-year-

old taught me the

deepest kind of

company and I use

it now, in the world,

as a grown woman.

Thank you.

You gave away your

best rock this year.

You got a feather

back. You didn't

know I saw the math

of that land on your

face, the discovery

that the books balance

strangely and always.

Keep giving the rocks,

my love. The feathers

find you. They found

me: I gave this year

to you and your

brother, every day

of it, and I have

never once been

richer.

You said 'I like

dis me' at the gate

on Tuesday.

So do I. Oh, so do I.

Happy third, my sky.

Mama"

Folded. Sealed.

Harbored, beside its

two sisters.

The tradition: three

deep now.

The drawer where the

someday lives:

filling, year by

golden year.
