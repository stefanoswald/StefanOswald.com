Night 300

Three Hundred Nights of Book Three

Month 10: Try Again

Three hundred nights.

The third stone of

the third book.

Stand on it a moment.

Look back:

Night one: a girl

learning that the hot

feeling had a name.

Night three hundred:

a girl who, this very

week, blew a feather

with her nose, braided

lumpy bread, rebuilt

a castle five times,

bounced a sorry and

tried again deeper,

and taught her brother

the thunder count

at the same window

where she learned it.

In between, the whole

country of this book:

the feelings named,

the self declared,

the friend crashed-into

and kept, the brother

waited-for and won,

the storms weathered,

the counting mastered,

the yets planted

in every wall.

Sixty-five nights left

in this book.

(She can't count that

high, YET, but she

knows what's at the

end of it. Everyone

knows. It has three

candles on it.)

"Free hundred stories,"

she said at the number,

working it. "Dat's...

lots-lots."

Lots-lots.

"Read da next one."

The next one, then.

And the next, and the

next, down the last

stretch of the third

book, toward the

candles...

each night one stone,

each stone set the

same way:

lamp, lap, love,

"again tomorrow."

Again tomorrow.

Three hundred kept

promises.

Sixty-five to go.

The road runs gold

from here.
