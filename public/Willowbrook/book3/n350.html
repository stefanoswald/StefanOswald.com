Night 350

The Almost-Three Checkup

Month 12: Almost Three

The birthday checkup.

Third annual conference.

And this year, the

patient RUNS half

the meeting:

"I hafta check YOUR

ears too. Dat's fair."

(The doctor, who has

run this office long

enough to know a

fair deal, surrendered

her otoscope-adjacent

penlight and submitted

to inspection. Ears:

"Clean. Good job.")

The exam, conducted

as conversation:

The curves: climbing

their hills, own

speeds, all well.

The heart, listened

to: "strong as a

drum." ("It's got

da kindness muscle

in dere," the patient

explained. The doctor

wrote something down.

The record suspects

it wasn't medical.)

The questions, from

the almanac's list:

sleep (good), eating

(the gallery rotates;

ballots proceed),

the whistle (demonstrated,

unrequested, at close

range).

And the milestone

review, but the

doctor barely needed

the checklist:

"She just negotiated

my penlight away,

asked why the scale

has numbers and

where they stop,

and told me about

her brother's coming

year in clinical

detail.

She's not almost-three.

She's three-and-

change with a late

cake."

Three-and-change with

a late cake.

"See you at four,"

said the doctor at

the door. "Or sooner,

knowing the trike

treaty's expanding."

(A band-aid was

issued preemptively.

For the collection.

The museum has a

wing now.)

Conference: adjourned.

Patient: thriving.

Cake: cleared for

landing, seven

sleeps out.
