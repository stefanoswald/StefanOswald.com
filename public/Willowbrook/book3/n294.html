Night 294

Tessa and the Big Rock

Month 10: Try Again

At the brook lives

a rock.

The big flat one.

The sunning rock.

The best real estate

on the whole bank...

warm all afternoon,

view of everything.

Freddy gets there in

one jump. ("OBSERVE!")

The dragonflies, in

zero.

And Tessa...

Tessa has been not-

climbing that rock

for as long as anyone

remembers. Too steep

for a turtle. Everyone

knows it.

Today, Tessa decided

otherwise.

The climb, witnessed

from the bank, in

its entirety:

One foot up. Rest.

Another foot. Rest.

A slide back, half

her progress, gone.

(The bank gasped.

Tessa didn't.)

Feet again. Rest.

The long middle,

where nothing seemed

to move but everything

was moving...

(caterpillar-certified

pacing; the professor

would weep)...

one entire afternoon.

And at the gold hour:

a turtle. On the

sunning rock.

Warm. Victorious.

Unhurried even in

triumph.

"YOU DID IT!" hollered

the bank, girl, frog,

dragonflies, all of it.

"TOOK YOU ALL DAY!"

added Freddy, who

cannot help himself.

And Tessa, settling

into the warmth she'd

earned inch by inch:

"It took me all day...

and now I'm here.

How long it takes

is not part of

whether you did it."

How long it takes

is not part of

whether you did it.

Carve THAT on the rock.

(The brook already

has. It carves

everything true.

Give it a few

hundred years.)

Slow is also a speed,

little one.

Sometimes it's the

only one that

gets there.
