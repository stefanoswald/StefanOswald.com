Night 245

The Whole Hand

Month 9: Counting to Five

The counting tool she

carries everywhere:

THE HAND.

Five fingers. Five numbers.

The match, made official

at breakfast:

The thumb: "ONE."

(The sturdy one. The

starter.)

The pointer: "TWO."

(The busy one. The

duck-finder. It's pointed

at half the town.)

The tall one: "FREE."

The ring one: "FOUR."

(The shy one. Needs help

standing alone. That's

fine. Fours are like that.)

And the pinky: "FIVE!"

(The little one. The

Theo of fingers.)

Count up: open them

one by one. One, two,

free, four, five!

Count down: fold them.

Five, four, free, two,

one... FIST.

Zero is a fist.

(Zero is a fist! The

best math discovery

of the month. She

opens and closes it

like the counting has

a door.)

The hand goes everywhere.

The abacus is installed.

Five crackers, five

steps, five ducks...

all countable now,

on equipment she was

born holding.

"What comes after five?"

The OTHER hand.

(Her eyes went wide.

There's a SEQUEL.

Next month's course.

The caterpillar paces

these things.)
