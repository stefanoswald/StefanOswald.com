Night 150

The Month of Theo, Counted

Month 5: Theo Arrives

Month's end. The audit.

Taken on the big bed...

all FOUR. The new arithmetic.

The countables:

One brother: arrived, named,

loud, perfect.

One meeting: the doorway,

the shirt, the voice he knew.

One grip: mutual, permanent.

One green pinch: on schedule,

named, told, held.

One first smile: HERS,

by prophecy and puffed cheek,

ratified forever.

One curriculum: begun.

(Signs. Months early.

Faith-based. Underway.)

One best friend: introduced,

pledged, gripped.

One thousand fetches, roughly,

by the house's fastest legs.

The uncountables:

That the house is louder now,

and fuller, and runs on fog

and casseroles and four...

and that not one flame dimmed.

(She checked. She watches

the candle math closely.

Two brights. Now the third,

lit fresh, full-sized.

All burning. None smaller.

The theology holds.)

That she was afraid, once,

of being less loved...

and became, instead,

MORE herself:

the teacher, the broadcaster,

the legs, the first smile's

owner, the voice

he turns toward.

"Good month?" asks Papa.

She looks at the bundle

between them.

Her bundle. Her month.

"Best one," she says.

"So far," adds Mama, smiling.

So far.

The books are long.

The best-ones keep coming.

That's the whole shelf's promise.

Audited. Approved.

Carried forward: four.
