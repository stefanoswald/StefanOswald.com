Night 39

The Help That Isn't Helping

Month 2: Me Do It

A visitor today. A kind one.

Watching Olivia work the puzzle.

The star piece. The tricky one.

Turn... doesn't fit...

turn... doesn't fit...

And the visitor, meaning well...

reaches over and pops it in.

"There you go, sweetie!"

Silence.

Storm flags rising.

"ME. DO. IT."

The piece comes back OUT.

The puzzle is now un-solved,

on principle.

The visitor looks at Mama.

Mama smiles the diplomat smile.

"She likes the working part."

And that's the whole lesson,

for every kind visitor everywhere:

the struggle IS the game.

Popping the piece in

is like eating her cookie

to help her with dessert.

The turning, the not-fitting,

the almost, the AT LAST...

that's what the puzzle is FOR.

Help, at two, means waiting.

Means hovering hands that don't land.

The star piece goes in, eventually...

turned by its rightful turner.

"ME DID IT."

The only signature that counts.
