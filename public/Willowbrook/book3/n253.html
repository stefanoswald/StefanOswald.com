Night 253

The Snack Subtraction

Month 9: Counting to Five

Snack time. Five crackers.

Counted onto the plate:

"One two free four five."

Standard census. Clean.

Then: the bite.

And the discovery:

"...FOUR."

Wait.

There WERE five.

She ate one.

Now there are FOUR.

The plate, she checks

it twice, confirms.

EATING IS COUNTING.

Eating counts DOWN.

"Mama! Da crackers go

DOWN when I eat dem!

Five... den four!"

(Subtraction. Discovered.

At a snack table. The

way it's been discovered

by every child since

crackers began. The

pond's diving duck

hinted at it. The plate

proved it.)

The experiment continues,

bite by bite, announced:

"Free!"

Munch.

"Two!"

Munch.

"ONE."

(A pause at one.

The last cracker,

regarded. The gravity

of one. The endangered

number.)

Munch.

"...Zero. Da fist number.

ALL GONE."

All gone: now a number.

The emptiness: countable.

The plate: mathematically

concluded.

"MORE five?" she petitioned.

More five: granted.

(The refill: addition!

Zero to five in one

pour! The plate runs

BOTH directions!)

Math, it turns out,

is edible.

It always was.

The best classrooms

have crumbs.
