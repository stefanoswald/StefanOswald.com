Night 255

Freddy Counts to Five (Loudly, Wrongly)

Month 9: Counting to Five

The brook. The rock.

And an announcement:

"I HEAR THERE'S A

COUNTING COURSE!"

yelled Freddy. "I WILL

NOW COUNT TO FIVE.

I AM EXCELLENT AT THIS."

(Nobody asked. As ever.

The seminar convened

itself.)

"ONE!"

(Good start.)

"TWO!"

(Solid.)

"SEVEN!"

"...No," said Olivia.

"FIVE!" concluded Freddy.

"DONE! APPLAUSE!"

"NO," said Olivia,

delighted. "You skipped

FREE and FOUR. And

seven isn't real yet."

(Seven isn't real yet.

The finest sentence

in the month. Seven

exists; it simply

hasn't been unlocked.

The hand only goes

to five. Facts.)

"SEVEN IS EXTREMELY

REAL," countered Freddy.

"I'VE SEEN IT."

"Excuse me," said Tessa,

arriving at her speed,

which gave everyone

time to settle.

"Count with me, both

of you. Slowly."

And the turtle counted...

one scrunch of progress

per number, caterpillar-

certified pacing:

"One... two... three...

four... five."

Together, the second

round: girl, frog,

turtle. "One... two...

FREE... four... FIVE!"

Correct. All parties.

Even the showman.

"I HAVE MASTERED

COUNTING," announced

Freddy.

"You had help," said

Tessa.

"MASTERED IT."

The brook burbled its

honest minutes: two

students, one teacher,

five numbers, in order,

eventually.

Slow, and skip nothing.

Even frogs get there.

Especially with turtles.
