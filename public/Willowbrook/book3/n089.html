Night 89

The Month of Friends, Counted

Month 3: A Friend Called Lily

Bedtime. Month's end.

Look at the friend-harvest:

One grocery crash

(the famous one, lived

and learned and told).

One spaghetti night...

now an institution, in ink.

One first fight, survived,

repaired, built taller.

One dog-fear, halved,

hand under hand.

One defense, the blue jacket,

planted. "She my friend."

One laughing fit, cause: pickle.

Cure: none. Status: chronic.

One quiet-together, ten minutes,

rarer than gold, banked.

Turns: invented.

Ducks: mailed. Both ways.

And one more friend...

the forever kind...

growing, right now,

smaller than a lettuce,

down the hall of this

very house.

"Good month?" asks Mama.

"BEST month," rules Olivia.

Then, drowsy, the real summary:

"Friends is my favorite."

Friends is everyone's favorite,

little one.

It's the whole reason

for towns.

Sleep now. Thursday's coming.

Spring's coming.

Everything good is coming,

and you'll meet all of it

the way you meet everything:

running, at full speed,

arms open.
