Night 211

The Whole Month Dry-ish

Month 7: Potty Power

Month's end. The audit.

The potty ledger, read:

Somethings: beyond counting.

(Counting stopped weeks ago.

That's the real milestone...

when the wins go ordinary.)

Rain days: a handful.

All dried. All rained-out

weather, none of it verdict.

Bathrooms reviewed: eleven.

(The gas station holding

firm at two stars.

The library's quiet flush

still the critics' choice.)

Ducks: victorious.

Six pairs, in rotation,

laundered with honor.

The strut: performed

now only on request...

another ordinary-marker;

champions don't strut

their commute.

And the biggest entry,

the one under all of it:

one girl who trusts

her own signals now.

Who answers her body's

phone. Who rains sometimes

and knows it's rain.

Who has two clocks

and honors both.

That's not potty learning.

That's SELF learning,

wearing its plainest clothes.

The skill folds into

ordinary life now,

where skills belong...

unremarked, load-bearing,

hers.

"Are we done wif potty

stories?" she asked

at the audit's end.

Mostly, champion.

The book moves on.

The ducks ride with you.

The throne stands ready.

Ordinary now.

Which was, from the very

first no-pressure day,

the entire plan.
