Night 205

Not Everybody's Ready

Month 7: Potty Power

The blanket. The delegates.

And the recruit, walking

now, talking some, still

littler, still in diapers.

And one of the newer

bench kids, a bigger one,

four-ish, mayor-adjacent...

pointed:

"He's still in DIAPERS?

That's for BABIES."

The blanket went still.

And the champion...

duck underpants, eleven

bathrooms reviewed,

every credential in town...

stood up.

(The blue-jacket move.

Learned at a sandbox.

Deployed now downstream.)

"Diapers aren't for babies.

Diapers are for people

whose CLOCKS aren't done.

Everybody's clock is

different. Mine finished

in da spring. His finishes

when it finishes.

My brudder's hasn't

even STARTED.

Nobody's racing."

(The two-clocks doctrine.

The no-racing law.

The whole curriculum,

delivered standing,

at a playground,

to a four-year-old,

verbatim.)

The bigger kid blinked.

"...Okay."

Okay.

The blanket resumed.

The recruit, who heard

all of it, leaned

on her shoulder awhile.

Grace, defended, is

grace doubled.

The bench mothers,

who missed none of it,

reported it down the

lane by supper.

The town filed it

where the town files

such things:

under how it's done.

Under her name.

Again.
