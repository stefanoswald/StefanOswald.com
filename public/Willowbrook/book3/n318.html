Night 318

The Unkind Day

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

True-story book.

So here's the month's

true low:

Olivia was unkind today.

Really unkind. Witnessed.

The recruit, little

June's shadow now,

the two smallest always

underfoot, reached

for the good shovel.

And Olivia, tired,

whistle-sore, having

one of the wrong-shaped

days...

snatched it.

Hard. And shoved.

A real shove. The

recruit sat down

backwards in the sand

and wailed.

The playground went

quiet the way

playgrounds do.

And there she stood...

the doorkeeper, the

pit crew, the secret-

rock giver, the girl

with the felt badge...

having just been,

publicly, small and

mean.

Here's what matters...

not the shove.

What came after:

The repair. Real one.

Second-sorry grade,

unprompted:

"I snatched and I

pushed you. Dat was

unkind. You can have

da good shovel da

whole time. I'm gonna

sit over dere till

my weather passes."

(Self-sentenced. To

her own corner-equivalent.

The tools, self-applied,

at the bottom of her

own failure.)

And at bedtime, the

question, small:

"Am I not kind anymore?"

Oh, little one.

"The kindness muscle

isn't a crown you

lose. It's a muscle.

Muscles fail sometimes...

tired days, heavy lifts.

Failing doesn't unmake

the muscle.

QUITTING does.

Did you quit?"

"...I repaired."

"Then you trained.

Hardest rep of the

month."

The unkind day, entered

in the record...

next to its repair,

which is the only way

this family enters

anything.

Kind people aren't

people who never fail.

They're people who

always mend.

She mends.

She's kind.

Both facts. Same girl.

Sleep now.
