Night 145

Papa's Double Shift

Month 5: Theo Arrives

Saturday. Mama's morning off.

(Ordered by the household

per the oldest doctrine:

the cup pours nothing empty.

She protested. She went.

Rosa's, then nowhere,

gloriously nowhere.)

Which leaves:

Papa. Two children. One morning.

The double solo.

The configuration:

Theo in the carrier.

Olivia at hand-height.

The market, attempted.

The log, honest:

One diaper event, managed

on a bench with dignity

mostly intact.

One toddler negotiation

at the pastry stall, resolved

per the two-yeses craft.

(This bun or that bun.

That bun. Obviously.)

One feeding, timed by luck

to the coffee stop.

One stranger: "Both of them?

By yourself? Brave man."

And Papa, bouncing one,

holding hands with the other,

crumbs on everybody...

"Not brave. Just Saturday."

(The record notes: it was

a little brave. The record

also notes: he loved it.

The market saw a father

mid-craft, two-handed,

fluent, HAPPY...

and the town filed it

where towns file such things:

under how it's done.)

Home by noon. Everyone fed.

Everyone intact.

Mama returned to a napping

baby, a fort in progress,

and a kitchen only

moderately historic.

"How was it?" she asked.

Papa, from inside the fort:

"We're getting bagels

every Saturday now.

It's a thing. We voted."

The double solo: certified.

The Saturday thing: founded.

Fathers don't babysit

their own children.

They just have Saturdays.

Great ones.
