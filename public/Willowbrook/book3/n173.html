Night 173

Art for His Wall

Month 6: Big Sister School

A project this week:

art for Theo's wall.

Because his room, per

its curator's inspection,

"needs more DUCKS."

(All rooms do. Her position

is consistent and lifelong.)

The commission, executed

at the kitchen table:

paper, the fat crayons,

deep focus, tongue out.

The result:

a duck. THE duck.

Her finest work.

(Yellow. Roundish.

Three legs, "he's RUNNING"...

one enormous eye.

The gallery's best-reviewed

piece of the season.)

Framed. (A real frame.

Mama insisted. Art is art.)

And hung...

here's the wisdom part...

not at grown-up height.

At CRAWLER height.

Low on the wall,

right where a sitting baby's

eyes actually live.

"So he can SEE it.

Art is for da looker."

(Curatorial genius,

frankly. Galleries charge

consultants for less.)

The installation ceremony:

Theo, seated before the work.

The artist, presenting:

"Dat's a duck, Fee-oh.

Ducks are da best fing.

Dis one is running because

he's happy. Say duck.

DUCK. It's a good first word.

Just saying."

(The campaign begins.

Note the date.

The far pages are smiling.)

The audience gummed

the frame corner.

Highest praise in his

critical vocabulary.

The artist accepted it

as such.

Five stars. Would gum again.
