Night 260

The Running-After Era Opens

Month 9: Counting to Five

Two weeks of walking,

and Theo has revealed

his life's purpose:

FOLLOWING HER.

Everywhere.

At top toddle.

She goes to the kitchen:

toddle-toddle-toddle.

"Livy!"

She goes to the fort:

toddle-toddle. "Livy!"

She goes BEHIND THE

COUCH to test it:

a pause... then the

round head appearing

at the corner, delighted,

relentless: "LIVY."

The running-after era.

The one from the schedule...

"he'll run behind you

everywhere you go,

forever, calling your

name"...

arrived exactly as

promised, syllable

for syllable.

And here's what she

does with it...

watch, because it's

the whole future of

these two:

she slows down.

Just enough.

Runs at almost-catchable

speed. Lets the gap

close... close... then

squeals ahead, then

slows again.

The chase, calibrated

by its target to be

WINNABLE.

(Nobody taught her

that. The coaching

gift again. The watch-

my-face-not-the-floor

instinct: joy is best

when the little one

can almost reach it.

She sets every game

to almost.)

He catches her four

times an hour.

The catch is a hug,

every time, both of

them down, laughing.

"He follows me ALL DAYS,"

she reported at dinner.

Complaint format.

Glowing content.

Forever, per the schedule.

Forever, per every

book on this shelf.

Run on, little ones.

The chase is the

whole story now.

The story is very,

very good.
