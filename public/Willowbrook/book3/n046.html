Night 46

The Bath Runs Deep (Feelings, Not Water)

Month 2: Me Do It

Bathtime. And trouble.

"ME turn the water."

Ah.

The taps.

The taps are a safety job.

(Hot lives in those taps.

Hot is nobody's me-do-it at two.)

"The taps are my job, love."

Storm warning...

Storm ARRIVING...

The full thunder. The arch.

"ME! DO! IT!"

Papa stays the ground.

(Month one's training. It holds.)

"You're mad. You want the taps.

The taps stay mine."

Boom. Boom. ...boom.

Rain. Hiccups. Quiet.

And THEN, the craft:

"But YOU are in charge of...

the plug."

The plug?

THE PLUG.

Pulling it at bath's end...

the great gurgle, the goodbye spiral...

that's HERS now. Official.

Also: pouring the cup for rinsing.

Also: choosing tonight's boat.

Three real jobs.

Zero scalding risk.

The storm clears completely.

Every no can carry a yes inside it.

Not the SAME yes.

But a true one.

That's the compromise craft.

The plug is pulled at bath's end

with enormous ceremony.

Gurgle. Bye, water.

Her job. Done well.
