Night 286

The Pool Lessons Begin

Month 10: Try Again

A new mountain this

week, a WET one:

SWIM LESSONS.

The town pool. Saturday

mornings. The toddler

class: six kids, one

teacher, a bin of

noodles, the shallow

steps.

Day one, honestly told:

The pool is BIG.

(Bigger than the brook.

Bigger than the tub

by a thousand tubs.

The blue goes DOWN

at the far end. She

saw it. She has

opinions about it.)

The face at the steps:

brave.

(Which, the formula,

running since the ear-

light, means: scared,

plus anyway. Her grip

on Mama's hand said

the scared. Her feet,

stepping in anyway,

said the plus.)

The lesson itself:

Kicking, holding the

wall. (The kick factory!

Reopened! The oldest

machinery in her whole

body, splashing at

industrial scale.

THIS part: loved.)

Blowing bubbles in

the water. (Suspicious.

Attempted. Snorted.

Retried. The pinwheel

breath, wet edition...

she made the connection

herself: "It's da

dandelion blow but

IN da water!")

The float, held by

teacher, back on the

water, sky above:

survived. Barely. Eyes

locked on Mama the

whole float.

Verdict, in the towel

after:

"Da pool is big.

I'm coming back

anyway."

Brave, defined again,

dripping.

Lesson two: Saturday.

The water will wait.

Water's good at that.

It's been waiting for

every swimmer ever,

since swimming began.
