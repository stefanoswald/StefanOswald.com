Night 126

Reading to Theo, In Person

Month 5: Theo Arrives

The librarian's commission,

issued months ago:

"Big sisters read to babies."

Practiced on the bump.

Gasp supplied. Pictures shown

to a tummy.

And now...

the audience has a FACE.

The nursery. The afternoon lull.

Theo, swaddled, propped

in the boppy on the rug.

Awake. Blinking. On duty.

And the reader takes her place.

The bear book. What else.

"Da bear. He lost his button."

(Book: shown. Held at

the eight-inch range. She knows

the range. Nobody taught her.

Some things transfer

in the blood.)

"He look and look. SO sad."

Theo blinks at the page.

His fist windmills.

"Dat means he likes it,"

she narrates to the room.

(Correct, probably.

The fist is enthusiasm.

The science backs her.)

"Den. DERE'S da button!

GASP!"

(The gasp: supplied.

His job someday. Hers for now.)

"He finded it. Happy.

Da end. Nigh-nigh, bear."

The book closes.

The listener, day-eleven old,

milk-drunk, entirely hers...

has fallen asleep

at her voice.

At HER voice.

The first of ten thousand pages

she will read this boy.

The library two blocks east

felt it, probably.

Somewhere in a wall,

a small librarian

updated her files:

new patron. Pre-enrolled.

Read to by the best.
