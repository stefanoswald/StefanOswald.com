Night 344

Invitations by Trike

Month 12: Almost Three

The invitations: made.

(Crayon. Ducks on

every one. The duck

attends everything.)

And the delivery

method, insisted upon:

THE TRIKE.

"I deliver dem MYSELF.

I'm da postmaster."

The route, ridden...

bell announcing at

every stop:

BRING-BRING.

Lily's new house.

(Four hundred steps.

Seven minutes. Two

by trike.) Card:

delivered. RSVP:

a hug at the door.

BRING-BRING.

June's. Card handed

up. June's mama,

hand on heart:

"Her first party

invitation in the

new town." (First

of hundreds. The

circle keeps what

it opens.)

BRING-BRING.

Mateo's. "Pit crew

boss is INVITED."

("Wouldn't miss it,

little Oswald.")

BRING-BRING. Rosa's.

BRING-BRING. The

plant-house.

And the farm's card:

mailed. (The trike's

range has limits.

The flag went up.

The postmaster

saluted it.)

Every card: ducked,

delivered, bell-rung.

The lane tracked the

postal service porch

to porch, waving.

Fourteen waves.

(She counted. The

economy circulates.)

The party assembles

itself now, card by

card, the whole

year's people,

gathered by trike

and crayon toward

one cake.

Bring-bring.

Coming, said every

door.

Of course.

Always.
