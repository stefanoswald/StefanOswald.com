Night 276

Freddy Tries Again

Month 10: Try Again

What the legendary

Freddy did next:

he came back.

The next day. And the

next. And here's the

part that made it

legend for REAL...

he came back SMALLER.

"Today," he announced...

volume at half power,

which for Freddy is

a whisper, "the SHORT

jump. Rock to rock."

Rock to rock: landed.

"THE MEDIUM JUMP."

Landed.

Day two: medium-plus.

Day three: the three-

quarter. (Missed once.

Landed twice. The miss,

publicly shrugged off:

"THAT ONE WAS PRACTICE."

They're all practice,

Freddy. That's the

secret you're

accidentally teaching.)

Tessa coached from

the bank, mostly by

existing slowly and

saying "again" at

the right moments.

Olivia cheered every

attempt, the attempts,

not just the landings.

(The audience statutes,

brook edition. She

knows how practice

likes to be watched.)

And day five:

"THE LEGENDARY LEAP.

ATTEMPT TWO."

Back up. Wiggle.

The whole brook holding

its burble...

JUMP...

and the far bank,

CAUGHT. Both feet.

Ta-daa restored.

The audience went wild.

The dragonflies did

a lap.

And Freddy, soaked

in glory, hat straight...

said the truest thing

of his entire loud career:

"MISSING IT MADE

LANDING IT BIGGER."

Missing it made landing

it bigger.

Carve it on the rock.

The brook already has,

in its way.

It tells the story

downstream to this day.
