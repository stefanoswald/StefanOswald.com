Night 331

Kindness to the House

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

One recipient got

missed all month.

The quietest one.

The one that holds

all the others:

THE HOUSE.

It came up at tidy

time, when the tidying

was going grudgingly:

"Why do we hafta put

da toys home?"

And Mama, instead of

the usual answers

(because-mess, because-

bedtime, because-I-

said), tried the

month's answer:

"Because it's kind.

To the house.

The house holds us

all day. Every day.

It keeps the rain off

your bed and the warm

in your bath and the

window where your

star comes.

It never asks for

anything.

Tidying is how we

say thank you to it."

...

The tidying changed

speed.

Toys: homed. ("Fank

you for holding my

blocks, shelf.")

The spill: wiped.

("Sorry, floor.")

The door: closed

gently. GENTLY,

for the first time

in recorded history...

("Da house doesn't

like slams. Slams

are unkind to its

bones.")

Kindness to things

sounds silly until

you watch it work:

the child who thanks

the shelf

becomes the person

who maintains what

holds her...

houses, tools,

friendships, towns,

her own body.

Care is care.

It practices on

anything.

The house, that night,

seemed to settle

warmer than usual.

(Houses can't feel.

Probably.

But the family that

lives like they can...

lives better.

That's just true.)

Goodnight, house.

Thank you for holding

everybody.
