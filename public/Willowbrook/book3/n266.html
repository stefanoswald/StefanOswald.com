Night 266

Teaching Theo the Thunder Count

Month 9: Counting to Five

A storm tonight. A real

one. Sky drums, rolling in.

And a baby at the window

who has never met thunder

awake before.

The first BOOM: his lip

wobbled. The arms went up...

"Livy!" the harbor

requested.

And the harbor, who

learned this exact fear

at this exact window,

from her Papa, a book

ago, ran the old

curriculum:

"Fee-oh. Watch. Da flash

comes..."

FLASH.

"...now we COUNT. One...

two... free... four..."

BOOM.

"Four! Da storm is FOUR

away! Dat's far!

Count wif me."

FLASH.

"One... two... free...

four... FIVE..."

BOOM.

"FIVE! It's LEAVING!

Da counting says so!"

And the baby, held,

counted-for, his hand

squeezed through each

number...

stopped wobbling.

Watched for the flash.

And at the next boom,

said his own version

of the count:

"Fee!"

(Three-ish. Close enough.

Generous grading. Family

standard.)

"FEE!" she agreed.

"Da storm is fee away

and going home!"

Boom... boom... gone.

The sky's drums, made

friendly, secondhand...

the flash-boom count

passed down entire,

window to window,

big hand to small.

"Storms are just loud,"

she told him, at the last

far rumble. "Big is not

bad. I learned it here."

He learned it there too.

Same window. Same count.

Same steady bigger hand.

That's how courage

travels in a family:

downhill, counted,

holding on.
