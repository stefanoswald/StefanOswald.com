Night 262

Molly's Five-Book Rule

Month 9: Counting to Five

Thursday. The library.

And a discovery at the

lantern desk:

"How many books can

I TAKE?"

The librarian, gravely:

"The children's card

allows five."

FIVE.

The number of the month.

The hand's whole span.

The library speaks HER

language.

The selection, conducted

with the weight of

five-only:

(This is the rule's

secret gift, the

librarian knows...

limits make choosers.

Unlimited books get

grabbed. Five books

get CHOSEN.)

One: the bear book's

cousin. (Loyalty.)

Two: a tractor one.

(For reading to Theo.

"He's a tractor guy.")

Three: one about storms.

(Research. The expert

stays current.)

Four: chosen by smell.

(A real method. The

old paper kind. The

librarian nodded:

"A classic approach.")

Five, the fifth choice,

agonized, three finalists,

one selected:

a counting book.

(Naturally. Count books

about counting. The

loop closes.)

Five books. Counted

onto the desk. Stamped...

thunk, thunk, thunk,

thunk, thunk, five

stamps, the library's

own percussion.

And in the wainscoting,

per the town's discretion,

a small acorn-satchel

presence updated her

files:

patron now borrowing

at maximum capacity.

Reads to a sibling.

Chooses by smell.

The lantern's future:

never more secure.

Five books a week,

fifty-two weeks,

for years...

you can build a whole

person out of that

arithmetic.

The library has.

The library does.

It's the longest

count in town.
