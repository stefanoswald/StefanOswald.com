Night 158

The Two-Kid Bath

Month 6: Big Sister School

Tonight: the experiment.

The TWO-kid bath.

(Theo sits sturdy-ish now.

The baby seat is in.

The logistics finally allow it.

The parents finally dare it.)

The configuration:

Theo: seat end. Anchored.

Olivia: faucet end. Sovereign.

Two washcloths. One duck.

(Esquire, presiding over

his largest jurisdiction ever.

His expression: unchanged.

His commitment: total.)

The proceedings:

She washes herself...

big girl, own cloth...

while narrating HIS wash:

"Now Mama does your creases.

You got cheese in dere, Fee-oh.

I had cheese too once.

Everybody starts wif cheese."

The splash diplomacy:

his kicks, mighty now...

soak her. She splashes back.

GENTLY. (Graded splash.

Sister-calibrated.)

He shrieks joy. She shrieks joy.

The floor towel earns

its overtime. Again. Forever.

And at the end: two burritos.

One big. One small.

Side by side on the mat,

hooded, pink, laughing

at each other...

the first of ten thousand

shared baths, beach days,

puddle seasons, pool summers.

The water years, begun

together.

"Same time tomorrow?"

asks the big burrito.

Same time always,

say the books.

The tub just doubled

its congregation.

The duck has never

been prouder.

You can tell.

(You can't tell.

He has one expression.

But you can TELL.)
