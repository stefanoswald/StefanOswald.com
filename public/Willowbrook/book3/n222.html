Night 222

The Roar Spot

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

Some storm energy

doesn't want breathing.

Some storm energy

wants VOLUME.

And volume, indoors,

rattles babies and

grown-ups and windows.

So Papa built the valve:

THE ROAR SPOT.

The back corner of the

yard. By the fence.

Marked with a flat stone.

The rules, inaugurated:

"This is the roar spot.

When the mad is too big

for inside, you come

here, and you ROAR.

As loud as you want.

As long as you want.

The fence can take it.

The sky can take it."

The test roar, conducted

immediately:

"RAAAAAAAH!"

(Impressive. The gold dog,

two yards over, stood up.

Then, understanding...

dogs understand everything...

lay back down.)

And it WORKS.

Mid-storm, offered the

choice, corner or roar

spot? she takes the

yard some days, marches

to the stone, and lets

the thunder OUT.

Roar. Roar. Roar.

Then: quiet. Emptied.

Back inside, wrung out,

ready for the repair.

The mad needs somewhere

to GO, little one.

Better a fence than

a brother.

Better a stone than

a swallowed storm.

(Swallowed storms leak.

Everyone learns it

eventually. She's learning

it at a flat stone,

early, loud, and free.)

The spot serves for years.

The fence never once

complains.

Neither, notably,

does the sky.
