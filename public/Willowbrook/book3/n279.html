Night 279

The Downhill Secret

Month 10: Try Again

Day five. New venue.

Papa wheeled the trike

to the park...

to the path by the

kite hill...

where the ground does

something useful:

it slopes.

Gently. Barely.

Just enough.

"New plan. You don't

pedal. You just put

your feet ON the pedals

and let the hill do it."

The launch: a push.

And the hill, the

patient old teacher...

turned the wheels,

and the wheels turned

the pedals,

and the pedals turned

HER FEET...

around. And around.

The circle, performed

BY her legs instead

of asked OF them.

"DEY'RE CIRCLING!

MY FEET ARE CIRCLING!"

Ride after ride down

the gentle slope,

the legs memorizing

the shape from the

inside...

(this is the secret

of teaching anything:

when the learner can't

make the shape,

let the world make

the shape THROUGH them

until they own it)...

and on ride nine,

at the slope's flat

bottom, where the hill's

help ran out...

the feet kept going.

On their own.

Three full circles

on flat ground.

THE LEGS LEARNED IT.

"I'M PEDALING!

DA CIRCLE IS IN MY

LEGS NOW!"

The circle is in

her legs now.

It never leaves.

(That's the miracle

of body-learning,

the bicycle promise:

once in, never out.

Sixty years from now,

these exact circles.)

Round five to the trike?

No.

Round five, and match:

HER.
