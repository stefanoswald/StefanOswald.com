Night 177

The Morning After the Wish

Month 6: Big Sister School

The morning after

the hard wish,

she went to his rail

a little quietly.

(Wishes leave a residue.

Even named ones.

Even understood ones.)

The step stool. The visit.

"...Hi, Fee-oh. It's me."

And Theo...

who slept through

the whole hard afternoon,

who holds no ledgers,

who knows exactly one thing

about his sister and knows it

with his entire being...

lit up.

The whole face.

The reserved-for-her wattage.

Arms up. Bouncing.

The morning shriek of joy

that is her name

in his one-word language.

The residue dissolved.

Just like that.

Because here's what

little brothers know

that nobody teaches them:

you are not your worst

afternoon.

You are the face

he waits for.

Every morning. No votes

counted but this one.

"Hi," she said again,

whole now.

And gave him the finger

to grip, the old contract...

and the day began

the way their days begin:

held on, both directions,

neither letting go.

The wish was weather.

This is the sky.

The sky held.

It always holds.
