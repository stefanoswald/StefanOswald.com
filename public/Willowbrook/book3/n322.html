Night 322

Grandpa's Invisible List

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

The kindness detectives

took their game to

the farm Sunday.

And cracked the town's

biggest case.

The clues, collected

over one afternoon:

The neighbor's gate...

squeaking last week...

silent now. Oiled.

By whom?

The bird feeder by

the church, always

full. Always. Filled

by whom?

Mrs. Aldana's garden

tools, old, dull...

returned to her porch

last month sharpened

like new. By WHOM?

The detective followed

the evidence. The oil

can in the truck.

The seed sack in the

barn. The whetstone

on the workshop bench,

recently used.

"GAMPA. It's YOU.

You're da secret

kindness man.

Da WHOLE TOWN one."

Grandpa Joe rocked.

Neither confirmed

nor denied.

(The secret-rep code.

He wrote it, probably.

Before it had a name.)

"Why don't you TELL

people?"

Rock. Rock.

"Telling turns a gift

into a bill.

Somebody fixed your

gate, that's the

town being good.

JOE fixed your gate...

now you owe Joe.

I'd rather the town

be good."

I'd rather the town

be good.

Forty years of

anonymous oil and

seed and sharpening,

so the town could

believe in itself.

The detective closed

the case and told

no one.

(Except this book.

This book keeps

everything.

Somebody should know,

someday, where the

town's goodness

came from.

Now somebody does.

It came from under

a straw hat,

quietly,

for decades.)
