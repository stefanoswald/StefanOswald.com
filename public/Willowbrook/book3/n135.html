Night 135

The Double Outing

Month 5: Theo Arrives

The first big outing. All four.

The new configuration, solved:

Theo: in the carrier, on Mama.

(Close enough to kiss.

The old spec. Second edition.)

And the stroller. HER old one...

repurposed:

"I push da fings."

Freight officer.

The diaper bag rides. The blanket.

Duck the doll, buckled in,

per regulations.

The lane, received at

four-person pace.

(Slower than two-person pace.

The math of families:

each member halves the speed

and doubles the noticing.)

The stations, all observed:

The gold dog, introduced

to the carrier's cargo

through the fence, formally.

(Sniff. Wag. The patrol

adds a name to its list.

It's understood.)

The Hendersons' porch: waved.

The mailbox: patted.

(Theo shown the mailbox.

"Dis is da mailbox.

Mail is love wif a stamp.

You'll get some.")

The corner: touched. Turned.

Home by the gold hour,

one baby lane-blessed,

one freight officer promoted

for flawless service,

two parents quietly amazed

that the whole circus

made it to the corner and back.

It gets easier, say the veterans.

Then it gets louder.

Then you miss the loud.

Today it was just right:

one lane, four people,

every step of it theirs.
