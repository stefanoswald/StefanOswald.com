Night 347

The Whistle

Month 12: Almost Three

It was a Tuesday.

(You know it was

a Tuesday.)

An ordinary afternoon.

She wasn't even

practicing.

(That's how the

longest yets pay out...

off-duty, mid-life,

when the trying has

sunk so deep it

works without you.)

She was just... blowing

air. Absently. At a

dandelion puff on

the porch step, lips

pursed from the blow...

and out came

a WHISTLE.

A real one. Thin,

wavering, one-note...

but round and true

and unmistakably

NOT SPIT.

She froze.

Tried again.

...whistle.

AGAIN:

WHISTLE.

"MAMA!!! MAMA COME

NOW!! MY WHISTLE

CAME!! DA YET PAID!!"

(DA YET PAID. The

family's whole

philosophy of effort,

cashing its biggest

check, in exactly

those words.)

The demonstration

ran all afternoon.

For Mama. For Papa

by phone. For Theo

(who attempted it

immediately: pure

spit; "dat's how

mine started too,

buddy; yours is

coming").

For Lily, next

Thursday, who'd

whistled first,

months ago, and who

celebrated this one

like her own:

"WE BOAF WHISTLE

NOW. We can do

SIGNALS."

(Signals. The whistle-

code era, opening.

The far books are

full of it. Two-note

means come over.

Three means hurry.

It lasts into their

teens.)

One year, that yet

took. A whole year

of spit and faith.

"Was it worf da wait?"

asked Mama.

The answer came back

in one thin, true,

wavering note...

held as long as her

breath could hold it,

which was exactly

as long as the smile

allowed,

which wasn't very

long at all.
