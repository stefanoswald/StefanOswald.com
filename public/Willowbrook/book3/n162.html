Night 162

Six Months of Him

Month 6: Big Sister School

Six months of Theo.

The half-birthday.

(No candle, candles wait

for the whole year...

but a muffin happened.

Muffins mark things

in this family.

It's the old way.)

And at dinner, the accounting...

delivered, of course,

by the house historian:

"Fee-oh is HALF now.

He can do SO MUCH fings.

He rolls BOF ways.

He sits like a big guy.

He eats MASH. He made da face.

He laughs at my face

DA MOST of anybody's.

He plays peekaboo...

I taught him da pull.

He knocks my towers.

Dat's his job. I gave it to him.

He talks baby talk

and I answer it because

I SPEAK it still.

And he grabs my finger

every morning and I say

hi Fee-oh it's me

and he smiles because

he knows it's me.

He always knows it's me."

The table sat quiet a moment

after the report.

(The record notes both

parental faces did the thing.)

Six months.

Half a year of brother.

And the historian's summary,

delivered with the last

of the muffin:

"He was worf da wait.

Da WHOLE chain of days."

The whole chain.

Every torn link.

Worth it, said everyone,

in the old dialect,

without a word.

Half down, little Theo.

The best halves keep coming.

Your historian has

the records ready.
