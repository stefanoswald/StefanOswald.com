Night 353

The Maybe Night

Month 12: Almost Three

Night 353.

(Every book, this night.

The family doesn't

plan it. The books

just keep landing

it here. Some rituals

choose their own

calendar.)

THE MAYBE NIGHT.

The year's weather,

retold through the

old lens:

"Lily moved away...

that seemed bad.

Remember the storm?"

"But da new house

got da window seat.

And da bakery smell

LIVES dere now.

Maybe."

"The sparkly rock...

you gave away your

best treasure. That

cost you."

"But Wiwi got a

MYSTERY. And den

DA DUCHESS gave me

a fevver. Da books

balanced. Maybe."

"You shoved the

recruit. Your worst

day."

"...And den I learned

da second sorry.

Da deep one. I

needed da bad day

to learn it.

...Maybe."

(Even the worst day.

Even that one. The

lens holds.)

"And the whistle

took a WHOLE YEAR..."

"...and da long yets

pay da BIGGEST.

Da wait made it

worf more.

MAYBE."

The whole liturgy,

run by its inheritor

now. Mama barely

prompting, the lens

fully installed,

grinding its gold

from every gray.

And the closing words,

hers this year,

unprompted, at the

lamp's last gold:

"Life doesn't happen

TO us, Mama."

"No, baby. It doesn't."

"It happens FOR us.

...I'm gonna teach

Fee-oh."

She's going to teach

Theo.

(She does. Book four.

Book five. All of them.

And further than that...

but that's the last

book's last page,

and we don't skip

ahead.

Tonight is enough.

Tonight the philosophy

turned three years old

in its newest keeper,

and said itself

back to its source,

word for word,

at the lamp where

it lives.)

Maybe.

Always maybe.

Goodnight.
