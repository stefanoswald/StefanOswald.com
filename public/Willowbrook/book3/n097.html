Night 97

The Names Game

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

Dinner. Big agenda item:

what do we CALL the baby?

The grown-ups float names.

Soft ones. Family ones.

Each one tried out loud.

And the resident expert

has candidates too:

"DUCK."

"...Duck?"

"Duck is a GOOD name."

(It is her oldest word.

Her first love. High honor,

actually.)

Noted, says Papa. Straight-faced.

The list continues.

"PANCAKE."

(Also noted. Also a

compelling argument, breakfast-wise.)

"WIWI-TWO."

(Lily will be so proud.

Also vetoed. One Wiwi

per town. It's an ordinance.)

The grown-ups keep floating.

And one name, a small one,

a green-star of a name...

keeps coming back around,

landing softer each time.

They don't tell her yet.

(It's a spring secret.

Some presents keep

till the unwrapping.)

"Duck is still available,"

she reminds the table.

On file, sweetheart.

Middle-name shortlist.

(It was not on the shortlist.

But somewhere in the far books,

a big sister will call

her little brother "Duck"

when he's being silly,

and only the two of them

will know it almost

was official.)
