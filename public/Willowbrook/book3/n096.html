Night 96

Practicing on Baba

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

Big Sister School: open.

First student: Olivia.

Practice baby: guess who.

(The bear accepts the posting.

He has held every job

this family ever needed.

"Baby" is just the next one.)

Lesson one: the holding.

"Babies' heads are sleepy.

We hold under the head. Always."

The bear is cradled...

head supported...

(his ear flops anyway;

ears are exempt)...

"Like dis?"

Exactly like that.

Lesson two: the gentle voice.

"Babies like small sounds."

"Hi baby," she whispers

to the bear. "I da big sister.

Dis is my house. You safe here."

(Perfect. Frame it.)

Lesson three: the shush-sway.

Sway... sway... "shhhh..."

(The bear's eyes, glass as ever...

somehow look sleepier.

He commits to every role.)

Lesson four: diapering.

(A washcloth. Applied to a bear.

Approximately. We'll call it

conceptual.)

School runs daily now.

The bear naps in the doll stroller,

buckled, per regulations.

The skills are stacking up.

Come spring, there'll be

a big sister on staff

who's already done

the training hours.

The bear will write

the recommendation letter.

Glowing, obviously.
