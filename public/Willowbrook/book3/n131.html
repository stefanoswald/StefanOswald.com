Night 131

The Nap Trade

Month 5: Theo Arrives

There is a thing that happens

in houses with two children.

A rare thing. A holy thing.

Veterans speak of it in whispers.

THE OVERLAP.

Both children.

Asleep.

At once.

Today, for the first time,

it happened.

Theo: down in the crib,

milk-drunk, swaddle-tight.

And Olivia, mid-play,

mid-BLOCK, actually...

folded sideways on the rug

like a puppet with cut strings.

(Big-sistering is exhausting.

Nobody tells you.

The legs alone.)

And Mama stood in the doorway

with a coffee gone cold

three hours ago,

doing the math:

Shower? Sleep? Eat with two hands?

Stare at a wall, gloriously?

(Book one, chapter seven:

the dishes are liars.

She knows the doctrine.

She wrote half of it.)

She chose:

none of them.

She stood in the doorway

and looked. Just looked.

Two sleepers. Two children.

HER two children...

the sentence still brand-new,

still catching her sideways.

The tower six high on the rug.

The crib holding its second

generation.

The house, breathing.

Ten minutes she stood there.

The quiet-ten doctrine,

double edition.

Then the coffee, reheated.

Then the wall, stared at.

Then, of course...

one of them woke up.

The overlap is brief.

That's what makes it holy.

Stand in the doorway

while it lasts.

It counts as everything.
