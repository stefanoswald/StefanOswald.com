Night 327

Papa on the Phone

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

A small scene. A big

lesson. Nobody planned

it.

Papa on the phone.

The delivery: wrong.

(The shelf brackets...

again with the brackets...

the wrong size, the

second time, the

reorder lost.)

And Olivia, playing

nearby, ears on...

(ears are always on;

that's the whole point

of this page)...

heard her Papa, who

had every right to

be thunder, say:

"No, I know it's not

your fault... yeah,

it's frustrating for

you too, I bet...

no, take your time...

thanks for sorting

it, hey, I hope

your day gets easier."

I hope your day gets

easier.

To a stranger. A

voice. Someone he'd

never see, who'd

gotten it wrong twice,

who couldn't do

anything for him

that kindness would

speed up.

He hung up. Went back

to his coffee. Thought

nothing of it.

And at dinner, at the

thank-you round:

"Fank you Papa for

being kind to da

phone man. He got

it wrong two times

and you still hoped

his day gets easier."

Papa put his fork

down.

"...You heard that?"

"I hear everyfing.

I'm free."

(Almost three. And

yes. They hear

everything. THAT'S

the lesson, not the

phone call. The ears.

The always-on,

taking-notes,

becoming-you ears.)

Be kind where it's

invisible, readers.

It's never invisible.

Somebody small is

always doing the

audit.

The audit came out

lovely today.

It usually does,

in that house.

That's not luck.
