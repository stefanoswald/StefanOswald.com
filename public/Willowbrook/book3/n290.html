Night 290

The Puzzle Summit

Month 10: Try Again

Day nineteen.

The puzzle. The daily

visit. The mountain.

Eleven pieces placed.

(The corner cluster,

long solid. The duck's

head, the reeds, the

water, assembled

across weeks, piece

by stubborn piece,

each one a small

summit of its own.)

And one piece left.

The last one. Sky-

colored. Curly-edged.

She held it a moment...

(the pause; she has

the pause; the pause

is the art)...

and placed it.

Click.

TWELVE.

The duck pond: whole.

The picture: complete.

The mountain: summited.

She sat back in the

full builder's gravity...

the tower-finisher's

pose, the statue-of-

herself, and regarded

the thing that was

too hard.

"It was too hard,"

she said, wondering

at it.

"It was too hard

TODAY," Mama corrected

gently. "Remember?

Day one. And then?"

"...And den I grew

at it."

She grew at it.

Nineteen days of visits.

Four hot chests. Two

walk-aways. One yet,

installed mid-month

and load-bearing ever

since.

And the applause...

per the statutes, at

completion, full house:

Mama. And the demolition

contractor, toddling

in, surveying the

finished pond...

"FEE-OH. NO. Dis one

we don't knock.

Dis one we SHOW."

He was shown. He patted

the duck. ("Duh!"...

his fourth word arriving

THIS EXACT WEEK, a

whole other page's

story, but the timing...

the timing was perfect.)

The puzzle stayed

assembled three days...

museum-grade, visited,

shown to Thursday's

guests...

then was unmade,

BY her,

back into its box.

"Why?"

"So I can climb it

again."

So she can climb it

again.

The summit isn't the

prize, little one.

The climbing is.

You just proved you

know it.

Box: shelved.

Mountain: renewable.

Climber: certified.
