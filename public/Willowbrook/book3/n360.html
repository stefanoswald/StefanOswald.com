Night 360

The Constellation Cake

Month 12: Almost Three

The cake.

Rosa's masterwork:

one gold star, center...

the tradition, third

year crowned...

and around it, new

this year, per the

client's own brief:

the constellation.

Little stars, a whole

bunch, one for each

person in the room,

Rosa admitted later;

she'd counted the

guest list and starred

them all...

because stars are

better in bunches.

The sky does it that

way.

And three candles,

lit and dancing.

The song rose, the

whole room's worth,

wrong notes defended

by the aunts' harmony,

Theo conducting with

a breadstick...

"...happy birthday,

dear O-liv-ia..."

(She looked around

the room during her

own song. The record

notes it. Slowly.

Every face. The same

slow look as the

wish-gathering of

every year, the

look that IS the

wish, everybody

knows by now.)

"...happy birthday

to you!"

The breath: gathered.

The biggest one yet.

Three candles is

real lungwork.

WHOOOO...

All three. One blow.

The room detonated.

And the wish?

(Unrecorded, per the

almanac's oldest

discretion. But she

told the bear that

night, and the bear...

per a records

arrangement this

book has with the

dresser division...

allows this much:

it had Theo in it.

And Thursdays.

And the word "always."

The rest is theirs.)

Cake: cut. The gold

star slice: hers.

The constellation:

distributed, one

star per person,

exactly enough...

"See?" said Rosa.

"Stars in bunches.

Never runs short."

Never runs short.

Not in this room.

Not ever.
