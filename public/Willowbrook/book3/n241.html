Night 241

The Gentle Week

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

And then, at the month's

edge, quietly, the way

seasons turn...

a gentle week.

Seven days.

The diary tells it:

"Clear."

"Clear."

"One squall, corner,

self-sent, 3 min."

"Clear."

"Grumble at bath.

Checked checkers.

Hungry. Solved."

"Clear."

"Clear."

Seven days, one shower.

A month ago: seven days

meant seven fronts.

What changed?

Everything the month

built, the corner,

the map, the checkers,

the choices, the roar

stone...

but under all of it,

the deepest current:

THE WORDS ARE WINNING.

Every week, more words.

Every word, a feeling

that doesn't have to

thunder to be heard.

"I'm frustrated at dis

puzzle", said, at calm,

where a month ago the

puzzle would have flown.

The storm season isn't

over. (Three is long.

The diary has entries

yet to come. The books

are honest about it.)

But the first thinning

has arrived, exactly

as promised on the

season's first page:

the weather grows

gentler as the words

grow stronger.

She's talking her

weather now.

It's the whole arc

of the whole book,

visible in one quiet

week:

feelings, named,

becoming feelings, said,

becoming a girl

who can tell you

about her rain

instead of raining.

The gentle weeks

multiply from here.

The diary thins.

The sky, as established

from the very first

month:

holds.
