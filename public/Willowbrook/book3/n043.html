Night 43

The Out-of-Bed Discovery

Month 2: Me Do It

Night three of the big bed.

The parents are on the couch.

The house is quiet.

Too quiet.

Then, small footsteps.

And at the doorway:

Olivia.

Casual. Bear under arm.

As if passing through

on unrelated business.

"...Hi."

She has discovered

the big bed's biggest feature:

IT HAS NO WALLS.

She can get OUT.

Anytime! The whole night

is technically accessible!

Freedom!!

And so begins the oldest

dance of the big-bed era:

the walk-back.

Calm. Boring. Kind.

"Back to bed, love."

Walked back. Tucked. Kiss.

Ten minutes: doorway. "Hi."

Walked back. Tucked. (No kiss this time.

Kisses are for round one.)

Round three: walked back. Silent.

Round four: ...

No round four.

Sleep won round four.

The walk-backs will run a while.

Weeks, maybe.

Boring wins. It always wins.

Freedom is thrilling.

But the bed is warm,

and the hallway has no bear.
