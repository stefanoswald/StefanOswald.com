Night 351

The Aunts Arrive with the Sweater

Month 12: Almost Three

Six sleeps out: the

northern car.

THE AUNTS.

(The guest room aired.

The routine eternal.

Some chapters of every

birthday are pre-

written. The best

ones.)

The reunion: full

ceremony. "NAN-NANS!"

The run. The knee-

greeting, doubled...

Theo runs now too;

the aunts absorbed

the double impact

like veterans.

And the needlework,

presented:

THE BIRTHDAY SWEATER.

Soft as everything

they make. And across

the front: three stars.

Gold ones. In a row.

"One star for each

year," said the elder

aunt, fitting it.

"We'll add one every

birthday. By the time

you're grown it'll

be a whole sky."

A sweater that becomes

a sky.

(They mean it. The

far books confirm:

the star sweaters

arrive annually,

each with its new

star, until a sixteen-

year-old owns a

constellation and

wears it, voluntarily,

which for sixteen

says everything...

every Christmas.)

Theo's parcel: a

matching one. One

star. ("You start

your sky now too,

little man.")

Two children. Two

skies. Beginning.

The aunts installed

themselves in the

kitchen within the

hour, party prep,

casserole logistics,

the needles already

out for some next

secret.

The house, fully

staffed for joy.

Five sleeps.

The stars, accumulating.

On sweaters.

On cakes.

On everything this

family touches,

apparently.

(The town's records

note: it was always

that kind of family.

The stars just

make it visible.)
