Night 339

The Remember Game: The Crash and the Brother

Month 12: Almost Three

Part two. The big one.

"Once, in the grocery

store, a girl with a

cookie's worth of zoom

ran away from her mama..."

"DAT'S ME."

"...and crashed into

another runner in the

cereal aisle..."

"WIWI!"

"...and the carts got

swapped, and the

spaghetti happened,

and your mama said

the word..."

"MAYBE!"

"...and it became the

best story we have.

Because life doesn't

happen TO us..."

"IT HAPPENS FOR US."

(Letter perfect. The

philosophy, fully

installed. Third

generation pending.)

"And THEN, the chain

on the wall. Remember

tearing the links?

And the kicks you

felt with your hand?

And then one morning,

before the sun..."

(quieter now; this

part always goes

quieter)

"...Rosa woke you up,

and there was a phone

call, and a name."

"FEE-OH."

"And you walked into

a hospital room in

your BIG SISTER shirt,

and a brand-new person

turned toward your

voice, because he

already knew it.

You were his first

familiar thing,

Olivia.

Before the whole world:

your voice."

Silence at the lamp.

(Even the teller's

voice did the thing.

It does the thing

every year at this

part. It's supposed

to.)

"...And den he said

my name first of

all da words."

And then he said

your name first

of all the words.

"Best year part,"

she ruled, half-asleep.

Best year part.

Of any year.

Of any book on

this shelf,

maybe.

Goodnight, Livy.

That's still the

best word he's got.
