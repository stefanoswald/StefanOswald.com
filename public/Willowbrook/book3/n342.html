Night 342

Bella Lands on Theo

Month 12: Almost Three

The morning gold.

The garden. The watch.

(She's kept it all week...

the fence line, the

warm-air hours. The

appointment approaches.

She can feel it the

way Grandpa feels rain.)

And then, over the

fence, riding the

thermals, trailing

the light the town

declines to explain:

BELLA.

"FEE-OH. FEE-OH COME

HERE. STILL. BE STILL.

Butterfly-still.

Like I taught you."

(She has been TRAINING

him for this. Stillness

drills. For weeks.

The boy vibrates at

rest, but for his

sister, he tries.)

Bella circled the

garden once. The survey.

Twice.

And landed...

not on the fence post.

Not on the stake.

On THEO.

On his outstretched,

trembling-with-effort,

almost-still arm.

Wings opening. Closing.

The rainbow, at rest,

on twenty-two months

of barely-managed

wonder.

His eyes found his

sister's, the lookback,

HIS lookback now...

are you seeing this...

"I see it," she

whispered. "She picked

you dis year.

She picks somebody

every year.

It means da spring

knows you now."

The spring knows

him now.

Wings up. Gone over

the fence, gold-trailed.

And Theo, arm still

out, turned to his

sister and said his

newest word...

learned this exact

moment, minted at

its own address:

"Fly."

Fly.

Word eleven.

The garden filed it.

The book files

everything.
