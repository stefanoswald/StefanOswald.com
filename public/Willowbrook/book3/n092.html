Night 92

Talking to the Tummy

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

Mama's tummy is rounder now.

And Olivia has learned

the most amazing thing:

THE BABY CAN HEAR.

In there! It hears!

So obviously...

obviously...

the baby needs the news.

Every day. All of it.

She presses her mouth close:

"HI BABY. It's me.

Da BIG SISTER."

(Volume: adjusted downward

after Mama jumped.)

Today's report:

"I had toast. Wif jam.

Wiwi comed over.

We builded a fort.

You can come in da fort

when you get here.

I saved you a spot."

(She did. There's a spot.

The bear guards it.)

Then, quieter, the sign-off

she invented herself:

"Grow good, baby.

Nigh-nigh in dere."

Inside, the baby floats

in the warm dark,

and the muffled little voice

comes through the walls...

and here's the true science,

the real, documented magic:

the baby is learning that voice.

Filing it. Before birth.

When they finally meet,

the baby will know her sound

already.

Big sister: the first voice

on the welcome committee.

Already on the job.
