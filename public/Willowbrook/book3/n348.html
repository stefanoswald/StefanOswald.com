Night 348

What Three Can Do

Month 12: Almost Three

A bedtime question,

eight sleeps out:

"What can FREE do?

Dat two can't?"

The catalog, offered:

"Three can pedal to

the CORNER. (The trike

range extends. New

treaty. Papa walks

behind.)

Three can have a

REAL chore. (The table-

setting goes official.

A checklist. Possibly

a small allowance

of berries.)

Three gets the BIG

swing sometimes. (The

belt one. With help.

The bucket's days

are numbered.)

Three can sleep-over

at the farm ALONE-

alone. (The overnight,

solo edition. Grandpa's

been asking since

she was four months

old. The answer's

about to change.)

Three starts PRESCHOOL

in the fall."

(The big one. The

plant-house's graduation.

The room with the

cubbies she's peeked

at. Lily's going too.

Same class. The

universe occasionally

just gets it right.)

"...And three can

hear longer stories.

Chapter ones. Where

you have to WAIT

for tomorrow to

find out."

Waiting for tomorrow

to find out.

(The cliffhanger.

The reader's whole

life, about to open.)

She lay there absorbing

the arsenal.

"Free is BIG."

Three is big.

"...I'm ready."

You are.

That's the strangest

part of every birthday,

little one:

by the time it comes,

you already are.

The cake just makes

it official.
