Night 110

The Night Everything Was Quiet

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

A strange, soft evening.

Nobody planned anything.

Nobody did anything.

The house just... paused.

Mama on the couch,

the bump enormous now,

a whole moon under her sweater.

Papa's hand resting on it.

Olivia tucked in the crook,

her hand there too.

THUMP, says the moon,

unhurried.

The lamp gold. The rain soft.

Nobody talking much.

"Soon," says Mama. To the room.

To the bump. To everyone.

Soon.

The house feels it too...

you can almost hear it

making its own staying list:

the rooms ready,

the small country down the hall,

the crib re-strung with love,

one duck standing by.

The whole family, all four...

(four! the word lands new)...

breathing together

in the lamp light,

on the edge of the biggest page

any of their books

has ever turned.

"Baby," says Olivia softly,

to the bump, at the evening's end.

"You can come now.

We all ready.

I saved you a spot

in da fort."

THUMP.

(That means soon.

It absolutely means soon.)

Goodnight, family of almost-four.

Sleep while you can.

Joy is coming

at its own pace,

any night now,

down the end of the chain.
