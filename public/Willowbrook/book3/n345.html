Night 345

The Cake Consult

Month 12: Almost Three

The bakery. The consult.

Rosa with her sketch

pad, the real one,

the professional one...

taking the client

meeting seriously,

because the client

takes it seriously:

"Alright, miss. Third

cake. Talk to me."

The requirements,

presented:

"Free candles.

Da gold star on top...

dat's TRADITION,

Rosa."

("Tradition," Rosa

agreed, sketching.

Third star. She'd

have quit baking

before skipping it.)

"And dis year...

can da star have...

FRIENDS?"

"Friends?"

"Little stars. Around

it. Because..."

(and here the client

explained her design

brief, and the baker

put down her pencil

to listen)

"...because da big star

is me, and dis year

I got MORE people.

Fee-oh's a star now.

And June's new.

And da big star

should share da cake

top. Stars are better

in bunches. Da sky

does it dat way."

...

Rosa did the dish

towel thing.

Then sketched it:

one gold star, center.

A constellation

around it.

"Best design brief

I've had in thirty

years of cakes,"

she said, and meant

it.

The cake: commissioned.

The constellation:

confirmed.

The client, satisfied,

accepted the traditional

consult fee:

one warm crust end,

eaten at the counter,

feet swinging.

Some things change...

the candles count up,

the star gets friends.

Some things never do.

The crust end is

eternal.

So is the baker.

So is the swinging

of the feet.
