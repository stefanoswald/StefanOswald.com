Night 223

Two Storms at Once

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

It was going to happen

eventually.

Tonight it happened:

TWO storms. At once.

Theo: teething, tired,

denied the electrical

cord. Full squall.

Olivia: hungry, tired,

her tower knocked by

the squall's own author.

Full front.

Both. Simultaneously.

Dinner burning gently

in the background.

The parents' eyes met

across the weather...

the old dialect, one

glance carrying the

whole plan:

divide and ground.

Papa took the squall.

(Sway, shush, teether

from the icebox, the

old kit, still loaded.)

Mama took the front.

(Low voice, near presence,

the corner offered...

declined, the ground

held anyway.)

Nobody fixed dinner.

Dinner waited. (Slightly

carbonized. Fine.)

And here's the note

this page exists for,

addressed to every

two-child house mid-

double-storm:

you can't calm two

at once alone,

and calming one at

a time IS the system,

and the burning dinner

is the receipt of a

job done right...

priorities, correctly

ordered: people, then

pasta.

Both storms rained out

inside fifteen minutes.

Both repairs delivered.

Dinner: served, dark

at the edges, eaten

by candlelight because

Mama declared it

"fancy"...

and the whole wrecked

evening ended, somehow,

in laughing.

"Fancy," agreed Olivia,

eating burnt pasta

by candlelight.

Weather-proof, this family.

All four corners of it.
