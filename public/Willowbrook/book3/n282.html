Night 282

The Drawing That Wouldn't Duck

Month 10: Try Again

An art crisis at the

kitchen table.

The commission: a duck.

(For Theo's wall. The

gallery expands.)

The problem:

"It doesn't LOOK like

a duck."

Draft one: regarded.

Crumpled.

Draft two: "Da head

is WRONG." Crumpled.

Draft three: crumpled

BEFORE completion...

the artist's eye now

outrunning the artist's

hand, which is the

exact moment art gets

hard, at every age,

forever.

(Her eye knows ducks.

Her eye has studied

ducks her whole life.

Her hand is two-going-

on-three. The gap

between them: the

hot chest.)

Mama intervened...

not at the drawing.

At the crumpling.

She smoothed the drafts

back out. All three.

Laid them in a row.

"Look. Duck one. Duck

two. Duck free.

See what's happening?"

...Duck two's head is

better than duck one's.

Duck three's, even

crumpled, has FEET.

"Dey're getting... MORE

duck."

"They're getting more

duck. That's what drafts

ARE. Not wrong ducks...

PRACTICE ducks. Every

one teaches your hand

something. We don't

crumple teachers."

We don't crumple teachers.

Draft four, drawn on

the smoothed-out wisdom

of one through three:

a duck.

An actual, by the

artist's own exacting

eye. DUCK.

Framed. Hung. Gummed

by its recipient, per

the gallery's rating

system. Five stars.

The three drafts?

Kept. In a folder

Mama started that day,

labeled in her hand:

"How the duck got good."

The folder grew for

years.

Everything good has

that folder.

Most people just

throw theirs away

and think the good

came from nowhere.

Hers is kept.

Hers will always know

better.
