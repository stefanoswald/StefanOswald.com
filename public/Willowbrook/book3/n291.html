Night 291

Theo Says Duck

Month 10: Try Again

The campaign began

almost a year ago.

The wall art. The daily

lobbying. "Say duck,

Fee-oh. DUCK. It's

da best first word."

The first word came:

Livy. (Better. Even

the lobbyist ruled it

better. The bets

dissolved in happy

tears.)

Then: mama. Then: aden.

Then this week...

at the gallery wall,

at the framed running

duck, his morning

inspection...

the point.

The syllable:

"Duh."

The lobbyist froze

mid-rug.

"...Say it aden, Fee-oh."

"Duh. DUCK."

DUCK.

Word four: DUCK.

The campaign, the

longest-running lobbying

effort in the family's

history, spanning two

books, one gallery

installation, and

approximately four

hundred crib-side

appeals...

VICTORIOUS.

"HE SAID DUCK!

MAMA! DA CAMPAIGN

WORKED! IT TOOK A

YEAR BUT IT WORKED!"

It took a year but

it worked.

(Write that on the

month's flag. It's

the whole doctrine

in seven words.

Some yets are nineteen

days. Some yets are

a year. The word

holds for all of them.)

Theo, delighted with

the commotion, worked

his new machine at

everything:

the wall duck: "Duck!"

Esquire, bath-side:

"Duck!"

The knit doll: "Duck!"

(Correct, technically!

Her NAME is Duck!

The taxonomy holds!)

The pond, Saturday:

"DUCK DUCK DUCK..."

(All five. Counted

by his sister.

Confirmed by the

flotilla, in chorus,

per tradition.)

Two kids. One word.

One year apart.

The family lexicon,

compounding.

The ducks of Willowbrook,

twice-named,

eternally pointed at,

swam on.
