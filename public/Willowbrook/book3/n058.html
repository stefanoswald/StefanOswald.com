Night 58

What Me-Do-It Is Really Building

Month 2: Me Do It

Watch her leave the house now.

Just watch.

The jacket: floor, arms, FLIP. On.

The boots: right feet. (Both right

feet, eventually. Checked. Swapped.

By her.)

The doorknob: reached, turned...

(new this week; the world

just got one door bigger)...

open.

"Ready!"

Six weeks ago,

every step of that was a war

or a waiting.

Now it's a sequence. Hers.

Here's what this month

was really building...

not the skills.

The skills are the visible part.

Underneath, brick by brick:

I am someone who can.

That's the sentence.

That's the whole foundation.

Every flipped jacket lays it.

Every honored no-thank-you.

Every do-over. Every crooked button

worn proudly to the market.

I am someone who can.

She'll stand on that sentence

her entire life...

at every new school,

every hard job,

every closed door.

It's being poured NOW.

This ordinary month. This door.

"Ready!" she says.

She is.

She so completely is.
