Night 141

The Photo of Four

Month 5: Theo Arrives

The aunts require it.

The mantel requires it.

History requires it:

the photo of four.

The production, mounted

on the porch steps.

(The family's step. The gold hour.

Tradition sets the venue.)

The attempts, logged:

Frame one: Theo yawning.

Frame two: Olivia waving

at the camera. Mid-frame.

Both arms.

Frame three: Papa blinking.

Frame four: everyone perfect

except the baby has vanished

below the blanket line.

Frames five through thirty:

variations on chaos.

And frame thirty-one:

Theo mid-yawn AGAIN,

Olivia mid-giggle AT the yawn,

Mama laughing at them both,

Papa looking at Mama...

nobody at the lens,

everybody at each other...

THE ONE.

(It's always the one.

Frame nineteen taught them.

The unposed doctrine holds

at every family size.)

Printed. Framed. Mantel'd...

beside the rug-gold portrait

of the family of three,

beside the hospital-band frame

of the family becoming.

The wall grows its timeline.

"Dat's US," says Olivia,

at the new frame, nightly.

Pointing each face:

"Mama. Papa. Me. MY Fee-oh."

That's you.

All four. Mid-laugh. Forever.

The only way this family

has ever photographed true.
