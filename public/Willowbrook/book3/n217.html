Night 217

The Storm at the Market

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

It happened at the market.

(It always eventually

happens at the market.

The market is where

storms go public.)

The trigger: the cereal

with the cartoon duck.

Denied. (Sugar-cereal.

The line held kindly.)

The storm: TOTAL.

Floor-grade. Full volume.

In aisle three, where

she once, long ago,

crashed into her best

friend.

(The aisle has seen

her whole range now.)

And here's what Mama did,

for every parent who'll

ever stand in that aisle:

She did not shush for

the audience.

She did not give in for

the audience.

She did not perform

ANYTHING for the audience.

She got low. Stayed ground.

Guarded the flailing

from the shelf corners.

And waited out the weather

exactly as she would

at home, because the

doctrine doesn't change

venues. That's what makes

it doctrine.

And the audience?

The town's audience?

One old farmer nodded...

the veteran nod, the

we've-all-stood-there.

The clerk, the saintly

one, mouthed "you're

doing great."

Rosa's cousin pushed

her cart AROUND, gently,

like traffic past

roadwork.

Willowbrook knows storms.

Willowbrook raised

everybody's.

The rain passed. The cart

resumed. The duck cereal

stayed shelved.

And at checkout, the clerk

slipped her one sticker...

"for after the weather"...

which is the only correct

sticker there is.
