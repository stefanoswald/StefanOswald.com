Night 52

The Both-Ways Day

Month 2: Me Do It

A strange and important day.

Morning:

"ME do it!" everything.

The jacket flip. The pouring.

The sandwich, solo. The bed, climbed.

Big girl. All systems go.

But then, at dusk...

tired, day-worn, small again...

she lifted both arms up:

"Carry you?"

(She means carry ME.

The pronouns swap when

the heart runs low. Always.)

And Mama carried her.

Like a baby. Cradled, even.

Extra swaying on the stairs.

Was that a step backward?

No.

Write this one down big:

Growing up isn't a door

that locks behind you.

It's a tide.

In... and out.

Big girl at noon,

little girl at dusk,

same girl all day.

The ME-DO-IT needs the carry-you

to come home to.

That's what makes the doing brave...

knowing the arms stay open.

They stay open, little one.

At two, at ten, at forty.

Do everything.

Then come be carried.

Both. Always both.
