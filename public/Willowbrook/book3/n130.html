Night 130

Teaching Him the Signs

Month 5: Theo Arrives

Remember the diagnosis?

"He has all the feelings

and only one word."

Remember the prescription?

"Somebody should teach him

some signs."

School is in session.

Teacher: Olivia.

Student: Theo. Age: seven weeks.

(Months too early. Years of faith

required. She has both.

Faith is her whole faculty.)

The curriculum, as delivered

at every feeding, every day:

"FEE-OH. Look. Dis means MORE."

(Fingertips bunched, tapped.

Demonstrated at close range.

Repeated. Repeated.)

"When you want more milk,

you do DIS. Den no crying.

Crying is hard for you."

(The student windmills a fist.

"CLOSE!" rules the teacher.

Generous grading. The best

teachers grade generous early.)

"And DIS means all done.

And DIS means milk.

You got a lot to learn, buddy.

Good fing I know everyfing."

Mama watches from the chair,

saying nothing, filing all of it.

Because here's what's actually

being taught, months early,

at the wrong age,

with perfect results:

not the signs.

The showing up.

The patient repeat.

The faith that small ones learn

if you just keep serving.

He'll sign "more" someday...

months from now, at some

high chair, and the whole

family will erupt,

and the teacher will say,

absolutely insufferable

and absolutely right:

"I TAUGHT him dat."

You did, little one.

You started today.
