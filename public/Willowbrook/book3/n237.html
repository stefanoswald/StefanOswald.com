Night 237

The Storm Diary

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

The almanac, the family's

old two-handwriting ledger,

keeper of first laughs

and boxed Tuesdays...

gained a new column

this month:

THE STORM DIARY.

Each storm, logged. Brief.

No judgment. Just weather

records:

"Tues, sock storm...

turned out: nap. Slept

on rug, 40 min."

"Thurs, banana (halved)...

rained 8 min, repair

good."

"Sat, market, duck

cereal, line held,

sticker after."

"Sun, no storms.

Farm day. NOTE: farm

days = clear skies."

And the patterns...

that's the diary's whole

point, began to SHOW:

Farm days: clear.

Skipped-nap days: heavy

weather, evening front,

every time.

Hungry-plus-transition:

squall risk severe.

Thursday pickups from

Lily's: brief showers.

(Goodbyes. Forever

goodbyes. Known cause.)

The diary turned weather

into DATA, and data

into mercy:

"She's not being difficult,"

the entries said, plainer

every week.

"She's being PREDICTABLE.

Feed her before transitions.

Guard the nap like treasure.

Storms have causes.

Causes have fixes."

Old parenting wisdom,

rediscovered by ledger:

you can't stop the rain,

but you can learn your

own valley's weather...

and build the picnics

around it.

The diary ran two years.

By the end, the entries

were mostly:

"Clear."

"Clear."

"One squall, self-

sheltered, corner, 3 min."

"Clear."

Valleys learn.

Skies grow.

Keep the ledger.

It ends up being

a book of getting

better...

which is the only

weather report

worth binding.
