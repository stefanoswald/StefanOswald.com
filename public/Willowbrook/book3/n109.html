Night 109

The Chain Gets Short

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

Look at the wall.

The chain, the great chain,

that stretched the whole kitchen...

is down to THIS:

a dozen links.

Maybe fewer.

(Babies keep their own

almanacs. The chain is

a guess with scissors.

But it's CLOSE now.

Everyone can feel the close.)

The nightly tearing has become

high ceremony:

The chair, climbed.

The link, selected.

The countdown, chanted:

"Free... two... one..."

RRRIP.

The link, added to the pile...

the pile of finished waiting,

kept in a bowl like receipts.

"How many sleeps now?"

They count the survivors.

"Ten? MAYBE ten?"

Maybe ten.

(The family word, working

overtime these weeks.)

The hospital bag stands ready.

The duck waits in its corner.

Rosa's sleepover bag is packed.

The big sister shirt is clean.

Everything is ready

except the calendar,

which only the baby can read.

Rrrip.

Nine, maybe.

Sleep fast, little one.

The end of the chain

is the beginning

of everything.
