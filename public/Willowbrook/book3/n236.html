Night 236

When Mama's Weather Comes

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

A hard afternoon for Mama.

(Grown-ups have them.

The bank called twice.

The washing machine made

a new noise. Theo's nap

broke early. The usual

weather, adult edition.)

And Olivia, the house's

best forecaster, read

the signs:

"Mama. Your chest is

getting hot."

(The body map. Applied

OUTWARD. She reads

everyone now.)

And Mama, here's the

lesson, done right...

didn't say "I'm fine."

(Fine is a fib children

smell instantly. Fibs

about feelings teach

fibbing about feelings.)

She said:

"You're right. My storm

is building. Thank you

for the forecast.

I'm going to use my tools."

And she DID. Visibly.

Narrated, even:

"Breath first." (The

flower one. At the sink.

Three of them.)

"And I need one minute

on the porch. Alone-

minute. Not a mad-at-you

minute, a weather minute.

I'll be right back."

One porch minute.

(Her roar spot is quieter.

Grown-up roar spots

usually are.)

Back in: leveled. Repaired

before anything broke.

"Better?" asked the

forecaster.

"Better. Good tools."

Good tools.

The same ones. The same

shelf. Every size of

person in this house

uses the same weather kit...

which is exactly why

the smallest person

trusts it.

The tools aren't kid stuff.

The tools are LIFE stuff.

Mama proved it at the

sink today,

three breaths at a time.
