Night 312

Fixing Mateo's Chain

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

An unusual sight on

the lane:

Mateo. Stopped.

The big-kid bike, upside

down at the curb. The

chain: off. Hanging.

Greasy and wrong.

And the big kid, the

builder, the faculty,

the one who fixes and

teaches and knows the

packing secret...

stuck.

(Big kids get stuck too.

This is news at almost-

three. Enormous news.

The faculty is mortal.)

The response, immediate:

"I'll HELP!"

What can an almost-

three-year-old do for

a bike chain?

Watch:

She fetched his tool

belt. (From his porch.

She knows where it

lives. Everyone knows

everything on this

lane.)

She fetched his mom.

(The actual fix. Chains

need big hands. Knowing

WHO to fetch is half

of helping, maybe

the biggest half.)

And she stood by.

The whole repair.

Handing things.

Narrating support:

"You almost got it.

Da chain is learning."

(The chain is learning.

Mateo grinned through

the grease.)

Chain: on. Hands: black.

Bike: rolling.

"Thanks, little Oswald,"

said Mateo, the lane's

name for her, hers

since the sprinkler

days.

"You're a good pit crew."

PIT CREW.

(A new title. Collected.

Worn all week.)

The finding, filed:

kindness isn't only

for smaller people.

It flows uphill too.

The big ones need

fetchers and standers-by

and somebody to say

the chain is learning.

Everybody's stuck

sometimes.

Every size.

Cross the grass anyway.

Fetch what you can

carry.

Stand by for the rest.
