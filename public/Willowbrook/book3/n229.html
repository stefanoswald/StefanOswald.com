Night 229

The Choice Umbrella

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

The best storm gear,

it turns out, isn't

for weathering storms.

It's for preventing them.

And the best prevention

this house owns is old

equipment:

THE CHOICES.

(The two-yeses craft.

Running since the shoe

standoff, a whole book

ago. Now storm-rated.)

Watch it work, hour

by hour:

Getting dressed...

storm risk: high...

"The stripes or the

flowers?" Storm risk:

zero. She's choosing,

not being dressed.

Leaving the park...

storm risk: EXTREME...

"Should we hop to the

gate or tiptoe to the

gate?" The leaving,

unchosen. The HOW, all

hers. Hopped. No storm.

Dinner vegetables...

"Big fork or little

fork?" (The broccoli

was never optional.

The fork was. The fork

is enough.)

Bath, teeth, bed...

each fork of the evening

offered as a fork:

this way or that way,

both ways arriving at

the same non-negotiable

place, the traveler

sovereign the whole

route.

Storms this week: two.

Storms last week: six.

The umbrella works.

Here's why, said plain:

most toddler storms

aren't about the thing.

They're about the

powerlessness.

Hand back the power...

in safe, small, real

pieces...

and the weather has

nothing to feed on.

Choice is the umbrella.

Carry it everywhere.

It never once wears out.
