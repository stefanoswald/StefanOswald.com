Night 199

The Handwashing Anthem

Month 7: Potty Power

The rule, welded on

from the first something:

potty, THEN hands.

Always. No exceptions.

Rain or shine or hurry.

And to weld it deeper,

the anthem.

(Every habit sticks better

with a song. The family

knows this. The family

runs on wrong notes

and rituals.)

The washing anthem...

composed by committee,

Papa on melody

(wrong, beloved),

lyrics by the artist

herself:

"Wash da fingers,

wash da fumbs,

wash da duck-butt..."

(nobody knows why

the duck-butt is in

the handwashing song;

it scans; it stays;

tradition is not

a democracy)

"...all da bubbles,

all da germs,

down da drain,

BYE!"

One full verse: exactly

the right scrub length.

(The doctors say a song's

worth. This song is

a song's worth. Engineering.)

Fingers, thumbs, between,

the backs. Rinse on "BYE."

Towel. Done.

Performed now, unprompted,

every single time...

the anthem drifting

from the bathroom

down the hall,

duck-butt verse and all,

the sound of a habit

setting for life.

Sixty years from now,

at some far sink,

she'll catch herself

humming it.

So will Theo.

So will children

not yet dreamed of.

Anthems outlive

everything.

Especially the ones

with duck-butts in them.
