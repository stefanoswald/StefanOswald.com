Night 166

He's MY Brother

Month 6: Big Sister School

The market. The stroller.

And a stranger, kind,

but LOOMING, swooping

at the baby:

"Oh what a DARLING..."

And suddenly, at the wheel:

a bodyguard.

Two and three-quarters,

arms crossed,

planted like the blue jacket

taught her.

"Dat's MY brudder.

You have to ask ME."

The stranger blinked.

Straightened. And, being

a Willowbrook stranger,

which is the best kind...

played it exactly right:

"May I say hello

to your brother?"

The bodyguard considered.

Consulted the file.

(Kind eyes. Proper distance.

Asked permission. Cleared.)

"Yes. But NO swooping.

He startles. Wave from DERE."

The wave, performed

at regulation distance.

Theo: waved at. Delighted.

The bodyguard: satisfied.

"Well," said the stranger

to Mama, "he's well guarded."

He is.

He will be at every park,

every school hallway,

every bus stop of his life.

It's not a job she was given.

It's an office she assumed...

the day the doorway happened,

the shirt, the voice he knew.

MY brother.

You have to ask her.

The town learned fast.

The town approved completely.
