Night 210

The Skill Goes Invisible

Month 7: Potty Power

Watch her now, month's end.

Mid-morning. Mid-play.

The tower at six blocks

and rising.

And then, no announcement,

no negotiation, barely

a pause...

she stands, goes,

handles the entire

business herself:

throne, wipe-try, flush

("bye, good hurrying"),

anthem, hands, towel...

and returns to the tower.

Block seven placed.

Nobody clapped.

Nobody was ASKED to clap.

The skill has gone

invisible...

the final stage of every

learned thing, the stage

past the parades and

the struts and the ducks

announced at checkouts:

just... life.

Running itself.

Hers.

That's how you know

it's finished. Not the

first success, the first

UNREMARKED one.

The month opened with

a small chair appearing

quietly.

It closes with a girl

disappearing quietly

into her own competence

and coming back to

block seven.

The whole arc, potty

to person:

announced, celebrated,

rained on, defended,

taught forward, and

finally, the highest

honor any skill receives...

forgotten.

Ordinary.

Which was, from the

first no-pressure day,

the entire plan.

Block eight, little one.

The tower's waiting.

So is everything else.
