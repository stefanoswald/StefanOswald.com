Night 346

Grandpa's Secret Project

Month 12: Almost Three

Something is happening

in the workshop.

Sunday's evidence:

The door: CLOSED.

(The workshop door

is never closed.)

The sounds: sawing.

Sanding. The good

hum of building.

And Grandpa Joe:

whistling. (He whistles

when he builds

birthday things.

He doesn't know he

does it. The whole

family knows he

does it.)

The detective went

to work:

"Gampa. What's in da

workshop?"

"Sawdust."

"What ELSE?"

"More sawdust."

"GAMPA."

"Some wood that used

to be bigger."

(The farm's finest

stonewalling. Forty

years of practice.)

The clue-gathering,

frustrated at every

angle:

Too big for a step

stool. (She has one.)

Too small for a bed.

Wheels? she heard

a wheel sound. WHEELS.

A wagon? She HAS the

wagon...

"Is it got a STAR

on it?"

Rock. Rock.

"Everything I build's

got a star on it.

That's not a clue.

That's a signature."

That's a signature.

The detective retired,

defeated, delighted.

(Being un-figure-

outable is half a

present's magic.

He knows it. He

closed the door

ON PURPOSE. The

mystery is gift-

wrapping.)

The workshop hummed

on.

The whistling continued.

Twelve sleeps to

the reveal.

(It has wheels.

It has a star.

It's exactly what

a three-year-old

gardener needs.

No peeking, reader.

Twelve sleeps.)
