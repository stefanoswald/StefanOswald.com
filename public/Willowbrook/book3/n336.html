Night 336

The Door Frame

Month 12: Almost Three

The door frame. The

family's tallness diary.

Birthday-month tradition:

the measuring.

"Back straight. Heels

DOWN..."

("Dey ARE down."

They were, this year.

The tiptoe era of

measurement fraud:

outgrown. Mostly.)

The pencil. The mark.

Step away. Look:

The new line.

And below it, the

ladder of old ones:

the two-mark.

The one-mark. (So low!

Was she ever THERE?)

And the first mark

of all, the baby

one, taken lying down

and transferred,

barely off the floor.

"Dat whole SPACE is

me?"

That whole space

is you.

A year of soup and

sleep and stubbornness,

stacked up in inches.

A year of climbing

and yets and growing-

at-things, written

on the house itself.

(The house keeps it

forever. Door frames

are never painted

over in homes like

this. Decades on,

someone will run a

thumb up these lines

and feel the whole

childhood under it.

Houses hold what

they're given.

This one was given

everything.)

Theo got his first

standing mark too...

wobbling, thrilled,

"me! me!"...

the ladder starting

its second climber.

Two marks. One frame.

The diary, growing

the way it likes best:

upward, in company.
