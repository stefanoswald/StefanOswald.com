Night 357

The Party Eve

Month 12: Almost Three

The eve. The readiness:

The banner: up. THREE,

it says, with a

scribbled duck beneath.

(The authenticating

mark. Standard since

edition one.)

The cake: DELIVERED.

Under cloth. On the

high counter.

("No peeking." "One

peek?" "It's better

tomorrow. Cakes are

like yets." ...That

landed. No peeking

occurred. Mostly.)

The spaghetti supplies:

staged. The balloons:

red, June-supplied,

blown by Papa till

he saw spots.

The workshop mystery:

arrived by truck,

sheeted, in the garage.

(Wheels confirmed

by silhouette. Nothing

else. The wrapping

holds.)

And the last night

of two, kept, per

the family's oldest

eve-doctrine...

completely ordinary:

Dinner at the table.

(The feeling check:

"excited-nervous.

Dey share a room

tonight." Correct.

They always do,

on eves.)

The double bedtime.

Theo first, his

song, his kiss, the

courier kiss delivered

to his head: "from

me. Last one from

a two-year-old me.

Tomorrow dey come

from a FREE me.

Dey'll be bigger."

Then hers. The whole

liturgy, unhurried.

The bear, tucked

close.

"Last sleep of two,"

she whispered at

the lamp.

Last sleep of two.

"...Two was good,

Mama."

Two was so good,

little one.

Two was the whole

wide world.

Sleep now.

Three is waiting

at the window

with the sun.
