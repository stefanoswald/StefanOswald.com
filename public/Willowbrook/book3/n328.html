Night 328

The Wave Economy

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

The detectives' last

case this month:

THE WAVE.

The lane's oldest

currency. So common

nobody sees it.

Today: counted.

One walk. Every wave

logged:

The Hendersons' porch:

wave given, returned.

The mail carrier:

returned, plus a

"morning!"

The gold dog: tail-

edition, exchanged.

Mateo, passing on the

bike: the cool-kid

chin-lift wave. (Learned

immediately. Deployed

for weeks after.)

The bus driver, a

STRANGER, passing

through: waved anyway.

Waved BACK anyway.

Two strangers, richer.

The market clerk,

through glass.

Rosa, flour hand,

through steam.

June, at her new

window, the biggest

wave, the whole-arm

kind, both directions.

Total: fourteen waves,

one walk.

Cost: zero.

And the finding...

the month's economics,

concluded:

"Mama. Da waves are

FREE. And everybody

gets richer."

Everybody gets richer.

The wave is kindness

at its absolute minimum

denomination, the

penny of the muscle...

and the lane runs

its whole warmth on

the circulation.

A street where waves

circulate is a town.

A street where they

don't is just houses.

This one's a town.

Fourteen waves deep,

every single walk.

Keep the currency

moving, little one.

Mint freely.

The bank never

closes.
