Night 30

The Month of Names, Kept

Month 1: Big Feelings Have Names

Bedtime. The month's last review.

"What feelings do you know now?"

Olivia counts them off...

her way, on fingers:

"Happy!" (The whole-body one.)

"Sad." (Heavy. Carryable.)

"Mad!" (The thunderstorm.)

"Scared." (Big ears. Pops when you look.)

"Cited!" (Can't sit down.)

"Shy..." (Behind the legs. Allowed.)

"Fusstrated." (The doorbell of learning.)

"Sorry." (The fixing one.)

"Brave!" (Scared plus anyway.)

"Home." (The one with no name

that she named anyway.)

Ten feelings. Two years old.

Some grown men know four.

"And what are they all?"

She knows this. The big one.

"Wevver."

Weather. Yes.

"And what are you?"

A pause. A grin.

Arms spread wide as the crib allows:

"THE SKY."

The sky. Forever and always.

Sleep, big sky.

Your weather is all quiet now.

Stars only, tonight.

Every one of them proud of you.
