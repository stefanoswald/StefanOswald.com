Night 121

The Meeting

Month 5: Theo Arrives

The hallway. The door. The room.

Morning gold through the blinds.

Mama, tired, shining,

the moon gone from under

her sweater and into her arms.

And in the arms:

a bundle.

Small. So small.

Blanket-wrapped. A knit hat.

One tiny fist escaped, waving

at nothing, the old windmill way.

Olivia stops in the doorway.

(The BIG SISTER shirt, worn

as planned. So he knows.)

Suddenly shy. Suddenly two.

"Come meet him," says Mama.

The careful climb. The scoot.

And then, the lean-in...

and there he is.

THEO.

A squished little face.

Dark lashes. A nose like a button.

The oatmeal, become a whole person.

He is the smallest thing

she has ever been this close to.

"Hi, Fee-oh," she whispers.

"It's me.

Da voice. From outside."

And Theo...

day-old, brand-new,

eyes barely working...

turns.

Toward the sound.

Toward the voice he's been

filing for months.

The voice he already knows.

The room goes very quiet.

"He KNOWS me," she breathes.

He knows you.

He knew you first of anybody,

after Mama.

You were the welcome committee

the whole time.

"Hi, Fee-oh," she says again.

And one finger, gentle,

certified, summit-trained...

strokes his impossible cheek.

Big sister: met.

Little brother: home free.

The whole rest of both their lives:

begun, right here,

in the morning gold.
