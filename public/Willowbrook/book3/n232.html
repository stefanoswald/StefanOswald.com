Night 232

The Word Gets Closer

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

Something is happening

in Theo's babble.

The house pretends

not to notice.

(The house is TERRIBLE

at pretending.)

The chains are narrowing.

The ba-ba-da-da of the

early months has developed

a... favorite.

A shape it keeps circling:

"Lee-lee."

"Vee-vee."

"Yee-yee."

Aimed, this is the part

nobody says out loud...

at HER.

At his sister. When she

enters. When she leaves.

When she's across the

room and he wants her

closer.

"Lee-lee!"

The bets on the fridge

tremble in the draft.

Papa's "Dada": nervous.

Mama's "Mama": resigned.

(Mamas usually win these.

Mamas know when they're

beaten. She took it

beautifully.)

And "DUCK", the campaign,

the wall art, the daily

crib-side lobbying...

"Duck's still in it,"

insists the lobbyist.

"Lee-lee could be duck.

Ducks say... no. Okay.

Ducks don't say lee-lee."

(The concession, gracious.)

"...But if it's ME..."

She doesn't finish the

sentence.

She's been finishing it

in her head all week.

If it's me.

If his first word,

of all the words,

out of the whole language

she's been pouring into

him since before he

was born...

if it's her...

Soon, little one.

The shape is circling.

The word is choosing

its runway.

Sleep tight.

Tuesday's coming.

(It's always Tuesday.

You know it's Tuesday.)
