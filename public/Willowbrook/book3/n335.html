Night 335

The Last Month of Two

Month 12: Almost Three

The calendar turned

its last leaf.

And there, at the far

end of the month,

drawn in crayon

(by its subject,

commissioned):

a cake. Three candles.

A duck beside it.

(The duck attends

everything. Standing

policy.)

"Da last month of two,"

she said at the wall,

trying the size of it.

The last month of two.

Two, which held:

the feelings and their

names. The big bed.

The me-do-its. Lily's

crash retold and Theo

entire, waited for,

met, taught, first-

worded. The storms

and their tools.

The counting. The yets.

The kindness jar.

Two was ENORMOUS.

Two was the biggest

year anyone ever had.

(They all are. That's

the shelf's whole

secret. Every year:

the biggest yet.

Forever.)

"Will I still be ME

at free?"

The old question. The

threshold one. Every

birthday asks it.

"More you than ever.

That's all growing is:

more you."

More you.

Thirty-one sleeps of

two remaining.

Spend them well,

little one.

(She did. Watch.)
