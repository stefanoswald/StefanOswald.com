Night 259

Counting the Uncountable

Month 9: Counting to Five

A warm night. The yard

blanket. The old

impossible homework:

COUNT THE STARS.

(Attempted once before,

a book ago, pre-five.

Result: "one, two, free,

fo..." dissolve. Beautiful

failure. The sky kept

its number.)

Tonight, armed with

the whole hand:

"One! Two! Free! Four!

FIVE!"

(Five stars. Counted

clean. The pinky pointing

at the fifth, the low

gold one, the family

one, who twinkled at

being census'd.)

"...and MORE five."

The other hand: deployed.

"Six? SEVEN?" (Seven

is real now. Freddy

will be vindicated.)

"...Lots. LOTS-lots."

And then the sky did

what the sky does to

every counter who ever

lay on every blanket:

it spilled.

Stars behind stars.

Stars between stars.

The count drowned,

happily, at ten fingers

deep in a sky ten

thousand deep.

"Papa. Da sky doesn't

FIT on my hands."

No, little one.

And here's the secret

that makes it good news:

some things count,

and some things you

just lie back and

let be many.

The crackers: count them.

The minutes: count them.

The stars, the fireflies,

the kisses, the loves:

let them spill.

The uncountable things

are the wealth.

"Lots-lots," she said

again, to the whole

sky, satisfied.

Lots-lots.

The truest census

of the best things.

Filed under: everything

that matters.
