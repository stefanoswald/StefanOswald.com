Night 224

The HALT Detective

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

A detective lesson today.

Because some storms...

the wise houses know...

can be solved BEFORE

they land.

The clues, taught like

a rhyme:

"When the grumbles start,

we check four things.

Hungry?

Angry?

Lonely?

Tired?

One of those is usually

the culprit."

(The grown-ups call it

HALT. She calls it

"da four checkers."

Hers is better.)

Today's case:

grumbles building since

lunch. Whining at toys

she loves. The front

assembling on the radar.

The detective work,

done TOGETHER:

"Hungry?" ...When DID

lunch happen? Three

hours ago. AH.

Cheese: deployed.

Apple: deployed.

And the front...

watch it on the radar...

dissolved.

Never landed. Solved

at the clue stage.

"I was HUNGRY-grumpy!"

she announced, amazed,

mid-apple.

The revelation of the age:

feelings have CAUSES.

The weather comes from

somewhere. Check the

four checkers, and half

the storms turn out

to be a snack wearing

a costume.

She runs the checkers

herself now, sometimes...

out loud, at grumble-

onset:

"Hungry? ...maybe.

Tired? ...MAYBE."

Self-forecasting.

At two-almost-three.

Half the grown-ups

in any given town

can't do it.

She learned it in

an afternoon,

from an apple,

solved.
