Night 125

The Helper Economy Booms

Month 5: Theo Arrives

The house has new jobs.

LOTS of new jobs.

And the helping bureau...

open since the coffee table,

staffed by one,

promoted yearly...

just hit its boom years.

The duties, as performed:

"Olivia! Diaper, please!"

GONE. Back in four seconds.

Diaper: delivered.

(The changing station knows

her route by heart now.)

"Burp cloth!"

INCOMING. At speed.

(Speed is part of the service.

The service is famous for it.)

The tiny sock, escaped,

per tiny sock tradition,

to the under-couch country...

located, retrieved, returned.

(Her old skills. The finding.

Socks never stood a chance.)

The pacifier run.

The blanket fetch.

The singing, requested

by Mama at the fussy hour:

"Sing him the wrong-note one."

(She knows it wrong perfectly.

He settles for her.

HE SETTLES FOR HER.

The house notes it quietly.

The house notes everything.)

"What would we DO without you?"

says Papa, at the day's end.

And she stands there...

tired, useful, lit up...

in the answer:

they'd manage.

But it wouldn't be THIS.

This house runs on four people now.

One of them is small and loud.

One of them is the legs.

Best staff she's ever been.
