Night 196

I Need Privacy

Month 7: Potty Power

A new sentence this week.

Arrived from nowhere.

(From the watching, really.

Everything arrives from

the watching.)

At the bathroom door,

mid-routine, a hand up:

"I need PRIVACY."

...Privacy.

The word, deployed.

The door, pushed...

not closed; nearly closed;

a governing inch left...

by its small owner.

And the house, mark this...

honored it.

Completely. Instantly.

"Of course. I'll be

right here."

Guard posted outside.

Door at its inch.

The champion, solo,

sovereign, PRIVATE.

(Ninety seconds later:

"MAMA I NEED HELP

WIF DA BUTTON."

Privacy has terms.

Privacy is scalable.

The guard responded

at speed.)

Here's why the honoring

matters more than the

comedy:

her body is HERS.

The learning starts

exactly here, at a door,

at an inch, at a request

taken seriously the first

time it's ever made.

Bodies get privacy.

Bodies get asked.

Bodies get to say

who and when and whether.

The whole curriculum

of consent begins

with one door,

one inch,

one "of course."

This house said of course.

Say of course.

The inch is everything.
