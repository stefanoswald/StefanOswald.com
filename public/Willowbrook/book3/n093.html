Night 93

The Kick

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

Evening. The couch.

Mama suddenly:

"Quick! Hand here! NOW!"

The small hand, placed

on the round tummy...

waiting...

waiting...

THUMP.

Something MOVED.

Something in there

PUSHED HER HAND.

Olivia snatches back...

stares at the tummy...

stares at her hand...

"DA BABY KICKED ME!"

The baby kicked.

Well, kicked NEAR you.

(Probably kicked AT you,

honestly. Siblings start early.)

Hand back on. Waiting...

THUMP.

"IT DID IT AGAIN!"

She laughs. She keeps the hand there

half the evening.

Every thump: a shriek. A wonder.

This is their first touch.

Do you understand, little one?

Someday you'll tell this baby:

"You kicked my hand

before I ever saw your face."

The first hello,

delivered through the wall,

foot to palm,

sibling to sibling.

"Night, baby," she says,

at the last thump.

Pat, pat, on the tummy.

THUMP.

(That means goodnight.

It absolutely means goodnight.)
