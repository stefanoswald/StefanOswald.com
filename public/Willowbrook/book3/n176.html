Night 176

The Hard Wish

Month 6: Big Sister School

This is a true-story book.

So here's the truest one

this month.

A worn-out afternoon.

The crying long. The nap short.

Thursday cancelled. Lily sick.

The tower knocked

(NOT the sacrifice one...

the TABLE one; he's pulling

up now; nowhere is safe)...

and Olivia, at the end

of her whole small rope,

said the thing:

"I wish da baby

would GO BACK."

Silence.

The kind that comes after

the forbidden thing.

Her own eyes went wide...

hearing it, fearing it...

And Mama...

here's the part to keep...

didn't gasp. Didn't scold.

Sat down on the floor

and said:

"That was a big true feeling.

Tell me about it."

And it came out, the rope's

whole end: the noise

and the knocked tower

and the missing Thursday

and the tired.

"You don't wish THEO away,"

said Mama, when it emptied.

"You wish the HARD away.

Those are different wishes."

...They were.

They were different wishes.

"I love Fee-oh," she said,

small.

"I know. Loving people

and being worn out by them

live in the same house.

Every family's house.

Mine too."

The wish, spoken, shrank.

(Named things. Tamer things.

The oldest tool in the box.)

By dinner she was building

him a sacrifice tower.

By bath they were laughing.

Say the hard thing here,

little one. Always.

This house can hold

every wish you've got...

and love you through

the whole weather of them.
