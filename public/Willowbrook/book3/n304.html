Night 304

Oliver Owl on the Word Yet

Month 10: Try Again

The willow. The session.

And Stella's report ran

one word long:

"Yet."

"Whoo?"

"That's the month.

The whole month, below.

They taught her the

word yet. Everything

else grew out of it.

The trike, the kite,

the puzzle, the pool.

One syllable. I watched

it change every sentence

in that house."

Oliver Owl was quiet

a long time.

(Owls measure words

by weight, not length.

He was weighing.)

"I have asked questions

from this branch,"

he said at last,

"for longer than that

town has had lanterns.

And I will tell you

what the little word

is, because I have

watched it work for

generations:

Yet is a question

wearing an answer's

clothes.

'I can't', that's a

closed door.

'I can't YET', listen

to it, that's really

asking: 'when? how?

what will it take?'

It turns every wall

into a door with a

handle.

The child who owns

that word will spend

her whole life finding

handles."

"Mmm," hummed the Moon...

which is Moon for

and the ones who never

learn it spend their

whole lives describing

walls.

"Log the month," said

Oliver, settling.

"Log it under the

small mighty things:

one girl, one syllable,

every wall in the

house rehung as a

door."

Logged. Willow-sealed.

Below, the town slept...

full of walls,

full of handles,

full of small people

growing at things

in the dark.

Goodnight, climbers.

Goodnight, Willowbrook.

Yet, and yet, and yet.
