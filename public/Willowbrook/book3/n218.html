Night 218

Holding the Line, Kindly

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

Tonight's storm had

a simple center:

a cookie. Before dinner.

"No, love. After."

THUNDER.

And here is the hardest

craft in this whole

month, told plain:

the line held.

Through the storm.

The WHOLE storm.

Because here's what

the books know and

tired parents forget

at 5 p.m.:

a no that melts under

thunder teaches thunder.

Not cookies, thunder.

It teaches: storms work.

Storm harder.

And a no that holds...

kindly, sadly even,

but HOLDS...

teaches the other thing:

storms are safe to have

and they don't drive

the house.

The house drives the house.

Papa held it like this:

"You want the cookie NOW.

I hear you. It's after

dinner. I know that's

hard."

(Empathy: full.

Line: immovable.

Both. Same breath.

That's the whole recipe.

Both is always the

whole recipe.)

The storm raged eleven

minutes.

Dinner happened. Teary,

then less teary, then

fine.

The cookie happened.

After. As stated.

Delicious. Uncontested.

"You said after and

it WAS after," she

noted, mid-cookie.

Yes.

The words mean things

here.

That's not strictness,

little one.

That's the ground

being solid...

which is the only

thing that makes it

safe to storm on.
