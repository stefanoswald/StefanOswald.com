Night 238

The Self-Forecast

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

It happened on an

ordinary Thursday-eve.

(Lily-missing hour.

Known squall window.

The diary has it charted.)

The blocks weren't

stacking right.

The chest, she'd tell

you herself, was

"getting hot."

And then, the moment

this whole month built

toward:

Olivia stood up.

Mid-frustration. PRE-

thunder.

And announced, to the

room, like a small

weather service:

"I'm getting a storm.

I'm going to my corner."

And WENT.

Herself. Un-sent.

Before the storm landed.

The corner received her:

squeeze pillow, squeezed.

Pinwheel, blown, the

spinner spinning her

own front down.

Four minutes.

And out she came...

rained out BEFORE raining,

the storm processed

in shelter, the thunder

never loosed on a single

tower or brother...

"All done. Da storm

came and I was already

inside."

The storm came and

I was already inside.

Frame it. Teach it.

Put it on the wall

next to the body map.

That's the whole skill,

the one the month was

for:

not never-storming.

(Nobody never-storms.

Grandpa has a dent.)

FORECASTING. Sheltering.

Meeting your own weather

at the door with your

own tools in hand.

Two years, ten months old.

Self-regulating.

Imperfectly, occasionally,

gloriously.

Every future hard day...

every test, every

heartbreak, every bank

calling twice...

just got a trained

meteorologist assigned

to it.

She's in there for life.

She started at a pinwheel.
