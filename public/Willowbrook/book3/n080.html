Night 80

The Defending

Month 3: A Friend Called Lily

At the park, a bigger kid.

Four, maybe. Confident.

Running the sandbox

like a small mayor.

And when Olivia's dig

got too close to his roadworks:

"SHE's too little.

She can't play this."

Oh.

Olivia's face, falling...

And then:

a blue jacket, arriving.

Planting itself. Squarely.

Between.

"She CAN play."

Lily. Chin up. Feet set.

Eleven days of seniority

deployed like a knight.

"She dig GOOD.

She my friend."

The mayor blinked.

Mayors aren't used to knights.

"...She can do the roads then,"

he ruled. Face-savingly.

And the dig continued...

three workers now,

roads and rivers both.

Nobody made a speech about it.

But down at blanket level,

something big just happened:

the first defense.

A friend who stands up.

Who plants the jacket.

Who says CAN when somebody

says can't.

Olivia will do it too...

next month, next year,

her whole life,

for Lily, for Theo, for strangers.

It starts here. Sandbox. Age two.

"She my friend."

The whole speech.

It's the only speech.
