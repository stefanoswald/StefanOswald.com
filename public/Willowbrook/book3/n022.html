Night 22

Silly Is Medicine

Month 1: Big Feelings Have Names

Today was a hard day.

A scraped knee. A lost mitten.

A nap that didn't take.

By dinner, everyone was flat.

So Papa made a decision.

He put the fruit bowl lid

on his head.

"I am ready for dinner," he said.

Formally.

...

Olivia stared.

Then: the giggle.

Then: the BIG laugh.

Then Mama put socks on her hands

and ate with sock-puppets.

Then the wrong-word game:

"Please pass the ELEPHANT."

"More SNOWPLOW, please!"

Total collapse. All three.

This is silly.

Silly is medicine.

It costs nothing.

It has no side effects

except hiccups.

It cannot fix the scraped knee...

but it fixes the flatness

the day left behind.

Every house needs a lid-hat.

Every hard day needs

one ELEPHANT passed politely.

Doctor's orders. Papa's, anyway.

He has a hat now.

He must know things.
