Night 49

The Choosing Power

Month 2: Me Do It

New morning policy:

Olivia dresses Olivia.

Chooses AND puts on.

Today's selection:

The striped shirt.

The polka-dot pants.

One red sock. One duck sock.

And the rain boots.

(Forecast: cloudless.)

Together: a lot.

Together: completely hers.

Mama looks at the ensemble.

The old instinct twitches...

match it, fix it, swap the boots...

And then the rule holds:

clothes are a hers-country.

(Weather gets a vote...

coats in cold, no negotiations.

Style gets NO vote. Style is free.)

"You chose all that yourself!"

"ME did."

Off to the market, in the outfit.

And here's what the town does...

the whole town, bless it:

Rosa: "NICE boots."

The grocer: solemn nod.

The librarian: "Excellent socks."

Willowbrook knows the rules.

Confidence is built at two,

one wild outfit at a time,

by every grown-up

who salutes the boots

instead of checking the sky.
