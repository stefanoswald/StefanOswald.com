Night 174

The Just-Us Promise, Kept

Month 6: Big Sister School

Remember the promise?

The just-us day's ending?

"We do just-us AFTER too."

First one: already promised.

Today: kept.

Papa took Theo.

(The Saturday thing. Bagels.

The double-solo craft.

He's fluent now.)

And Mama took Olivia...

just Olivia...

on the old route.

The bakery. ("Just US?"

asked Rosa, reading it

instantly. "Then it's

the just-us bun." A new

pastry, invented on the spot,

which was the regular bun

with extra ceremony.

Perfect.)

The library. The vault.

One story, her lap,

no stroller parked anywhere.

The pond. All the ducks.

Counted. Just the two of them

counting.

And here's what Mama noticed,

and told her at the pond:

"You know what's different

about just-us now?"

"What?"

"Nothing.

That's the whole point.

This, us, didn't change.

It just got a brother

to come home to."

The calendar of two,

alive and well

inside the calendar of four.

Kept. Scheduled. Sacred.

"Same time next monf?"

Same time every month.

In ink.

Some appointments

outrank everything.

This one always will.
