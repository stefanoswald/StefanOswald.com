Night 178

The First Word Bets

Month 6: Big Sister School

Theo's babble is thickening.

Ma-ma-ma. Da-da-da.

The real words are loading.

Any month now.

So dinner convened

the official pool:

FIRST WORD BETS.

The predictions, logged

on the fridge list:

Papa: "Dada." (Hope

springs eternal. The lamp

got it first last time,

he notes, still not over it.)

Mama: "Mama." (Statistics.

Also hope. Also valid.)

And Olivia:

"DUCK."

The table pauses.

"Duck?"

"Duck. I'm teaching him.

Da art is on his wall.

I say it every day.

Duck was MY first word.

It's da BEST first word.

He'll see."

(The campaign, running

since the gallery installation.

Daily. Relentless.

"Say duck, Fee-oh. DUCK."

The lobbyist works

crib-side hours.)

The bets: sealed.

The pool: one cookie,

winner takes all.

And the far pages...

which know the answer,

which have always known...

are keeping the secret

beautifully.

(One hint. Just one:

the first word wasn't duck.

The first word wasn't

mama or dada either.

The first word of Theo's

whole life was two

syllables long,

and it made one person

cry the happiest tears

in the entire third book.

Guess. You have months.

You'll never quite guess.

You'll be so close.)
