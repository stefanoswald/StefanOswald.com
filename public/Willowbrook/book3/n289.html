Night 289

The Kite Day

Month 10: Try Again

And then, a Tuesday,

naturally, the wind

came right.

You could feel it from

the porch: steady, kind,

pulling at jackets.

Kite wind.

"NOW?"

Now.

The hill. The red diamond.

The launch:

Papa held the kite high.

She ran, the string

in both fists, the

running her department...

"GO GO GO..."

release...

and the kite

CLIMBED.

Up. And UP.

No nosedive. No spin.

The tail streaming its

little bows, the diamond

leaning into the wind

like it had always

known how...

(it had; kites always

know; they just wait

for the day the sky

cooperates)...

higher than the oak.

Higher than the hill.

Small and red and

DANCING against the

whole blue.

"IT'S FLYING! PAPA!

DA SKY TOOK IT!"

The sky took it.

And the string...

this is the part she

never forgot, the part

nobody forgets...

the string HUMMED.

Alive in her hands.

The sky, tugging,

saying more, saying

higher, saying I've

got it, you hold on.

They flew it till

the light went gold.

Two sets of hands on

one humming string.

"Da kite couldn't fly,"

she said at supper,

"and DEN it could.

Because we kept

showing up."

Because you kept

showing up.

That's the whole

month.

That's most of life,

honestly.

The wind comes.

Be on the hill.
