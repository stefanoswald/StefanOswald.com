Night 298

The Speed Run

Month 10: Try Again

A morning worth

recording, exactly

because nobody would

have recorded it:

Watch her get ready.

The jacket: floor,

arms, FLIP. On.

(Once: a war.)

The zipper: gripped,

set, ZZZIP.

(Once: the doorbell

of frustration itself.)

The boots: checked,

swapped, right-footed,

ON.

(Once: forty minutes

and a storm.)

The buttons, the

sweater's five: done,

and, check them...

RIGHT-HOLED. All five.

(Once: the crooked

badge of pride.

Now: just buttons.)

Total elapsed time:

about a minute.

One fluent, unremarkable,

completely miraculous

minute.

"Ready!"

Every skill in that

minute was a mountain

once.

Every mountain got

climbed by the same

route: badly, then

less badly, then well,

then, the summit

past the summit...

invisibly.

This is where all

the yets go, little

one. This is the

afterlife of effort:

it becomes ordinary.

It becomes a minute

you don't even notice,

built out of a hundred

hours you'll never

remember...

and underneath every

easy thing you'll

ever do, forever,

this same invisible

architecture:

old walls,

turned doors,

turned hallways

you walk without

looking.

"Ready," she said.

She was.

She had no idea

how much.

That's mastery.

That's the whole

point of it.
