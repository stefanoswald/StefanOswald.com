Night 167

The Quiet Practice

Month 6: Big Sister School

Theo naps twice a day.

And here's the hard truth:

his naps happen

during HER awake.

Her LOUD awake.

The zoomies hour.

The pot-drum hour.

And the crying-when-woken baby

taught everyone quickly:

nap hours are soft hours.

So: the quiet practice.

The skills, drilled like the gym:

The tiptoe. (Past his door.

Exaggerated. Cartoon-grade.

Effective.)

The whisper voice.

("Like da library. Da inside

of da inside voice.")

The pots: embargoed till wake-up.

(Hard. The pots CALL to her.

The pots understand. They wait.)

The zoomies: relocated.

(The yard. The far hallway.

Approved circuits posted.)

Is she perfect at it?

No. She's two-almost-three.

The tiptoe sometimes ends

in a crash. The whisper

sometimes forgets itself

mid-sentence, "and den da duck...

oops." (Whispered: "oops.")

But watch the trying.

The trying is enormous.

Quiet, for a loud person,

is the biggest gift there is.

She gives it twice a day,

imperfectly, on purpose,

for him.

That's not volume control.

That's love,

learning its indoor voice.
