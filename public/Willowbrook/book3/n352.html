Night 352

The Just-Us Morning

Month 12: Almost Three

The tradition, standing:

before every birthday,

one JUST-US morning.

The last edition of

the age.

Two's final tour,

taken at two's own

pace:

The bakery. ("Da

almost-free bun,"

invented on the spot

by Rosa, who invents

one every year now.

It was a regular bun

with three sugar

stars. It was perfect.)

The library. One story

in the vault, her

lap, the acoustics.

(The librarian, at

the stamp: "Last

checkout as a two-

year-old. Historic."

The books were chosen

accordingly: one

about birthdays,

one about big sisters,

one, by smell...

about the sea.)

The pond. All ducks

greeted. ("Dey knew

me when I was ONE,"

she told June's mama,

passing. "Dese are

LONG friends.")

The bench. A sit.

A quiet one.

And the fence, coming

home: the gold dog,

sunning. The slow

company. One soft

treat.

"Same me tomorrow?"

she asked, at the

gate. (The threshold

question again. It

visits every year.)

"Same you. Plus one

candle. That's the

whole change."

Same you, plus one

candle.

"...Good," she said.

"I like dis me."

I like this me.

(Of every sentence

in three books,

the parents agreed

that night, that

one might be the

whole prize.

She likes this her.

Keep that, little

one. Guard it like

the bear.

The world will

have opinions

someday.

Come back to this

gate when it does:

I like this me.

You said it first

at almost-three.

It was always true.)
