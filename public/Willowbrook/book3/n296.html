Night 296

The Braid Lesson

Month 10: Try Again

Thursday. Rosa's kitchen.

Bread day. And a new

lesson at the flour table:

THE BRAID.

Rosa's hands do it like

water: three dough ropes,

over, under, over...

a braid falling out of

her fingers the way

songs fall out of singers.

"Now you two."

Three ropes each.

And the mystery, met:

Over... which one goes

over? THIS one? Now

under, wait, the

MIDDLE one? now

there's four somehow?...

how are there FOUR...

The first braids:

knots. Honest knots.

Lumpy dough pretzels

with ambitions.

"Good," said Rosa...

and meant it, the way

professionals mean it:

"Every braider alive

made THAT first.

That's braid soil.

Braids grow out of it.

Again."

Ropes: re-rolled.

Again: over... under...

over... a pattern!

A wobbly, thick-thin,

unmistakable PATTERN...

"DAT'S A BRAID.

A LUMPY BRAID.

I MADE A LUMPY BRAID!"

"Lumpy braids," ruled

Rosa, "bake up the

same as pretty ones.

Butter doesn't check."

Into the oven, beside

the professional loaves:

two lumpy braids,

one per girl.

Out: golden. Lumpy.

Delicious beyond all

justice.

"Braid soil," Olivia

told Papa that night,

delivering his slice.

"You grow braids

out of it. Rosa said."

Rosa said.

Rosa knows.

Everything rises at

her table...

dough, and girls,

and the patience

they're both

made with.
