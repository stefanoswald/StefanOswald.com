Night 309

The Give-Away

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

The plant-house put out

a call: the toy bin

is thin. Donations

welcome.

And the house held

a summit at the shelf.

"You could give one

of yours. A real give...

not a loan. It goes

and stays gone."

The sorting, conducted

under the old law...

the high-shelf law,

the soul-versus-

shareable line she

drew herself, a year

ago:

Baba: soul. (Never.

Not discussed. The

law of laws.)

The sparkly rock: soul.

The knit Duck: soul,

plus staff.

But the middle shelf...

the stacking cups.

(Good ones. Loved ones...

loved-PAST ones. Theo

has his own set now.

Hers sit.)

She held them a long

minute.

(Giving-for-keeps has

a cost. That's what

makes it giving. Loans

are kindness with a

string; gives cut

the string. The cut

is felt. It should be.)

"...Da cups. Dey should

go where da playing is."

Delivered. Personally.

Into the plant-house

bin, where, within

the minute, a small

new kid had them out,

stacking, delighted.

She watched from the

door.

The cost, felt.

And then, the other

thing, arriving right

behind it:

"Mama. Look how happy.

MY cups did dat."

Her cups did that.

The string, cut,

turned into this:

joy, traveling loose

in the world,

wearing her old

fingerprints.

"Giving is weird,"

she reported. "It

takes away AND it

gives you somefing."

It's the whole

arithmetic of the

month, little one.

Minus one toy.

Plus one lighter,

larger heart.

The math never

balances.

It always comes

out ahead.
