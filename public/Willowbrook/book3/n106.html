Night 106

The Worry Sneaks Back

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

A hard afternoon.

Tired. Rainy. Nap refused.

And when the storm came...

over a broken cracker,

which is never about the cracker...

it rained out something real:

"DA BABY GETS EVERYFING!

Da baby gets my CRIB

and my CLOTHES

and my MAMA!"

There it is.

The worry, back again,

wearing storm clothes.

(Worries do that. One candle

ceremony doesn't retire them.

They circle back on tired days.

That's normal. That's allowed.)

Mama holds the whole storm.

Doesn't argue with thunder.

Waits for the rain part.

Then, in the after-quiet:

"The baby gets your OLD crib.

You have the big bed. Yours.

The baby gets your OLD clothes.

The ones that fit your THUMB.

And the baby gets..."

(this is the important part;

she lifts the wet chin)

"...SOME of my hands.

Some of my hours.

But NONE of your share of me.

Your share is yours.

Bolted down. Forever.

Nobody can get it.

Not even somebody

very small and very cute."

Sniffle. Hiccup.

"...Bolted?"

"Bolted. Grandpa-built.

Star on it."

The smallest smile.

Worries come back.

So do answers.

As many times as it takes.

That's the deal. That's love

with a wrench in its hand.
