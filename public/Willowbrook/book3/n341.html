Night 341

Spring Comes Back Around

Month 12: Almost Three

The spring came back.

(It does that. It's

the most reliable

character in the

whole series.)

And the garden called

the family out for

the annual planting...

the third one of her

life, the first one

she MOSTLY remembers:

"Da tomato row goes

DERE. It always goes

dere. I water it.

Dat's da rules."

(The rules. Written

in seasons now.

She holds the garden's

constitution in her

head like the address:

lived-in knowledge.)

Her lettuce row:

re-planted. Her stake:

re-staked.

And this year, new...

a THIRD row.

Small. Low. Marked

with a stake bearing

one green star:

THEO'S ROW.

(Radishes. Fast crop.

Twenty-two-month

gardeners need fast

results. The garden

bureau's onboarding

wisdom, applied.)

His planting: four

seeds in, three seeds

eaten ("FEE-OH.

Seeds are not food

YET. Dey become food.

Dat's da whole point.")...

one seed re-planted

under four-hand

supervision.

The watering round,

now a two-can operation:

her can, his tiny one.

Glug. Glug.

"Grow good," she told

the beds. The old

blessing.

"Gow goo," echoed

the apprentice.

The wheel, turning.

The garden, generational.

The spring, keeping

every appointment...

including the one

it keeps next week,

on rainbow wings.

(You know who's coming.

She's never once

been late.)
