Night 234

The Word Tour

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

A first word must be

toured.

(Town law. Her "duck"

toured. His "Livy" tours.

The circuit stands ready.)

The stations:

The bakery. Jingle.

"Rosa! Say who I am,

Fee-oh! SAY IT!"

"...Livy!"

(Rosa's dish towel came

out. The dish towel of

emotions. "His first

word is his SISTER,

I cannot..." The celebration

bun: doubled. One each.)

The bench. The parliament.

"Livy!" performed for

the assembled mothers,

who produced between

them exactly the sound

you'd expect. (The recruit's

mother cried. It's that

kind of word.)

The library desk.

"Livy!" at cathedral

gain, per the acoustics,

per tradition.

(The librarian, gravely:

"An excellent first word.

Points to a well-read

household." The wink.

The whole desk knows

who reads to whom.)

And Sunday. The farm.

The fence line. The

porch.

"Livy!"

"Well NOW," said Grandpa

Joe, at THE register,

the deepest one, the one

the family measures

occasions by.

"First word's his sister."

Rock. Rock.

"That boy's got his

priorities straight.

Farm approves."

The farm approved.

The town approved.

The word toured for

a week and never once

got old.

It never got old, period.

Ask her at any age.

Ask her decades from

this Tuesday.

The best word anyone

ever said, ever,

was her own name,

said first,

through crib bars,

by somebody reaching.
