Night 192

The Nighttime Question

Month 7: Potty Power

A bedtime question,

asked at the diaper shelf:

"Why do I still got

diapers at NIGHT?

I'm a CHAMPION."

Fair question. Honest answer:

"Because you have two clocks,

love. A day clock and

a night clock.

Your day clock learned

the potty. YOU taught it.

But your night clock is

still little. It sleeps

so deep it can't hear

the tummy phone yet.

That's not a behind thing.

That's a body thing.

Night clocks finish on

their own time, some

this year, some in years.

All normal. All fine."

"...I can't teach it?"

"Nobody can. It grows.

Like your legs. You didn't

teach your legs long...

they just grew. The night

clock grows in the dark,

all by itself, while

you dream."

She considered her body...

this strange, patient

committee of clocks

and legs and signals,

each part on its own

schedule, all of it hers.

"Okay. Night diaper.

For my little clock."

For the little clock.

No shame at this shelf.

No shame anywhere in

this book.

Bodies bloom in order.

Every body. Every order.

The champion sleeps

protected, undefeated,

her second clock growing

quietly in the dark...

right on time,

whatever time that

turns out to be.
