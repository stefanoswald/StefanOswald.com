Night 281

Harry and the Bubble

Month 10: Try Again

A backyard afternoon.

Bubbles on the breeze.

(Mama's wand. The good

soap. The yard full

of floating glass.)

And out from under

the hedge, drawn by

the shimmer: Harry.

The hedgehog. The

prickly one. The one

whose whole life is

a lesson in wanting

soft things with a

spiky self.

He remembers bubbles.

(A book ago. Every

one: popped. Every

try: pop. Bella's

mercy, the one on

the nose, a gift,

not a skill.)

Today he tries again.

Bubble one: chased. POP.

Bubble two: leapt at. POP.

Bubble three: approached

slowly... POP. (Spikes.

Always the spikes.)

And Olivia, watching

the small prickly

persistence, coached:

"Harry! You gotta be

STILL! Da soft spot!

Bella showed you!

Not chase. WAIT!"

Wait.

The hardest instruction

in any language.

Harry sat. Trembling

with the effort of

not-chasing.

Nose up. Still. Still...

And a bubble, one

bubble, drifting on

its own schedule...

came down...

down...

and rested.

On his nose.

The soft spot.

Not popped. RESTING.

Harry went cross-eyed

with achievement and

held stiller than any

hedgehog in recorded

history.

Some things can't be

chased, little one.

Some things, bubbles,

butterflies, sleep,

friends, can only

be waited-for, with

your soft spot up.

Harry learned it in

one afternoon.

It takes most people

decades.

The bubble stayed

four whole seconds.

Best four seconds

of his prickly year.
