Night 115

Papa's Version

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

Porch steps. Dusk. The two of them.

(Mama inside, resting her moon.)

And Papa tells a story

she's never heard:

"You know, I was a big brother.

Am one. Your uncle, the far one,

the one from the wedding.

He was MY baby, once."

PAPA was a big sibling?!

PAPA?!

"What was it like??"

He looks out at the lane.

The old smile.

"Loud. He cried a LOT.

And I was jealous, green pinch,

just like yours...

because he got all the arms.

And then one day...

he was maybe six months...

he laughed. First laugh.

And it was because of ME.

I made the face,

and he laughed at MY face,

before anyone else's."

(A pause. The dusk holds it.)

"And that was it.

He was mine after that.

That's the big-sibling secret,

Olivia. The baby doesn't

just belong to Mama and Papa.

Some part of it...

the laughing part, maybe...

belongs to YOU."

She sits with that.

The lane lights come on.

"Da laughing part," she says.

"I want da laughing part."

It's yours, little one.

It's traditionally yours.

The craft passes down:

big sibling to big sibling,

porch to porch,

laugh to laugh.
