Night 254

Grandpa's Egg Count

Month 9: Counting to Five

Sunday. The coop. The

morning gather...

and this Sunday, the

gatherer-in-training

runs the count.

"How many eggs today,

boss?"

The nesting boxes,

checked in order:

Box one: an egg.

Warm. Brown. "ONE."

Box two: "TWO."

(Henrietta's. Announced

by Henrietta. At length.)

Box three: empty.

("Da Duchess is

finking about it,"

ruled the counter.

Correct. The Duchess

produces on her own

schedule. Like all

artists.)

Box four: "FREE!"

Box five: "FOUR!"

And the floor, the

renegade spot, where

one hen of anarchist

principles always lays:

"FIVE!"

Five eggs. One basket.

Counted into it, one

warm weight at a time,

each one a number you

can HOLD.

"Good count," said

Grandpa Joe. "Five

eggs, five fingers.

The hand and the

henhouse agree."

And then the old farm

math, delivered at

rocking speed on the

walk back:

"Numbers started in

places like this, you

know. Somebody had to

count sheep. Count

eggs. Count the days

to harvest. All the

arithmetic in all the

books, it walked out

of barnyards first."

She looked at her

basket. Her five.

Her barnyard arithmetic,

warm and brown and

countable.

"I'm doing da OLD maf,"

she said.

The oldest, little one.

The original.

Egg by egg by egg,

same as forever.
