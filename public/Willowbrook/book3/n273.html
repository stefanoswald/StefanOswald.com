Night 273

Stella Counts to Five

Month 9: Counting to Five

The watch, at month's

close, runs its own

arithmetic.

Stella counts:

"One:

the girl. Big bed.

Bear in arms. Dreaming,

by the look of it,

in numbers."

"Two:

the boy. The crib.

Walking now, even

asleep his feet practice;

watch the blanket twitch.

His first word rides

his breathing out:

you can almost hear it."

"Three:

the mother. Finally

down. The diary filled

in, the day held,

the candle math still

true, every flame

full-sized."

"Four:

the father. The porch

checked, the doors

done, one last look

down the hall the way

he's looked for a

thousand nights."

"And five..."

(the count pauses;

the gold dips lower)

"...the house itself.

Count it. It holds

the other four.

It learned every game,

kept every routine,

absorbed every storm.

Houses count.

This one counts double."

"Mmm," hums the Moon...

which is Moon for

a good census, colleague;

and what of the

uncountables?

Stella looks down at

the whole of it...

the lane, the lit

lanterns of the town,

the ten thousand stars

she herself is one of...

"Lots-lots," she says,

in the town's own

newest arithmetic.

Lots-lots.

The Moon hums the

agreeing hum.

Five counted.

Everything else: spilling,

beautifully, past all

hands.

Goodnight, counters.

Goodnight, countless.

Goodnight, Willowbrook.
