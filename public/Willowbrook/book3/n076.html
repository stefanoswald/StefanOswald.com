Night 76

What Lily Knows

Month 3: A Friend Called Lily

Lily is older.

Eleven days older.

Eleven ENTIRE days.

And with great age

comes great knowledge,

which she shares freely:

"You can whistle like DIS."

(A demonstration: mostly spit.

Olivia tries: entirely spit.

The knowledge is transferred.

The whistle is coming. Someday.)

"Da best stick lives DERE."

(Behind the oak. It's true.

It's a magnificent stick.

It's the park's Excalibur.

They visit it. They don't move it.

Best sticks live where they live.)

"Da moon FOLLOWS your car."

(...What.)

"It DOES. Watch tonight."

(Olivia watched.

IT DOES.

The moon followed the whole

way home from the farm.

Lily knows THINGS.)

Eleven days doesn't sound

like much.

But somebody a little ahead...

the cousin principle,

the faculty next door...

pulls you forward like a tide.

And here's the beautiful part:

Olivia knows things TOO.

(The gentle-hand. The pond breath.

Where the fountain button is.)

The knowledge flows both ways.

That's not teaching.

That's friendship...

school with no walls,

enrollment: two,

tuition: free, forever.
