Night 317

The Listening Bench

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

Thursday. The park.

And Lily was sad.

Real sad. Deep-weather

sad.

(Her grandmother...

the faraway one, the

one from her mama's

old country, is sick.

Rosa's been on the

phone in the other

language, quiet-voiced,

for days. Kids hear

everything. Kids

always hear everything.)

And Olivia, the fixer,

the kit-carrier, the

girl with a tool for

every weather...

reached for the tools:

A joke? (No. Wrong

weather.)

The pinwheel? (Not

that kind of storm.)

A sorry-flower? (Nobody

did anything wrong.)

The kit... didn't fit.

Some sadnesses have

no tool.

So she did the only

thing left...

which turned out to be

the biggest tool of all,

the one at the bottom

of every kit:

she sat down.

Right next to her.

Close. Shoulder to

shoulder on the bench.

And said nothing.

For a long time.

(The together-quiet.

The couch kind. The

rare gold kind...

deployed now not for

comfort's coziness

but for comfort's WORK.)

After a while, Lily

leaned her head over.

After another while,

she talked. A little.

About her abuela.

The lemon tree in

her yard, far away.

Olivia listened.

That's all. Listened.

"Fanks for sitting,"

said Lily, at the end.

For sitting.

Not for fixing.

Nothing got fixed.

Somebody just wasn't

alone inside it...

which, for the

unfixable weathers,

is the entire

medicine.

Learn this one deepest,

little reader.

Half of love

is just sitting close

and staying.
