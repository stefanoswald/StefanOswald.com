Night 198

Meanwhile, Theo Stands

Month 7: Potty Power

Mid-potty-month,

the OTHER milestone

went off like a firework:

THEO PULLED TO STAND.

The couch rail. The haul.

The wobbling triumphant

vertical of him...

eight months old, upright,

gripping, GRINNING...

"MAMA! LOOK! FEE-OH'S

TALL! HE MADE HIMSELF

TALL!"

Everything abandoned.

The cheer section: instant.

And the coach, mid-cheer...

already coaching:

"Dat's da pulling up!

I did dat! Now you do

da CRUISING, dat's

walking wif hands...

and DEN da real walking!

You're on da SCHEDULE,

Fee-oh!"

(The schedule. Mama's

old timeline, memorized,

administered downstream.

He IS on the schedule.

Slightly ahead of it.

Second kids run ahead...

they have a pace car.)

The house audit moved

at once: crib mattress

lowered, "I KNOW dis

one!" she announced,

consulting, couch corners

re-checked, her HIGH SHELF

inspected and ruled

"still safe. For now.

He's coming for it

someday, Baba."

(He was. He did.

Book four. The shelf

war of the far pages.

Legendary. Survivable.

Not today's problem.)

Two milestones, one month,

one house:

she climbs thrones,

he climbs couches.

Everybody rising.

The house holds all

altitudes.

It's getting good at it.
