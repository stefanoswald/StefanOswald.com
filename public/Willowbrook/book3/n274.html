Night 274

The Twelve-Piece Mountain

Month 10: Try Again

A new puzzle arrived.

Not the wooden three-piecer.

(Mastered. Ancient history.

Theo's now.)

This one: TWELVE pieces.

A picture puzzle. A duck

pond scene, chosen by

its subject matter,

obviously, that comes

apart into twelve

curly-edged mysteries.

The first attempt:

two pieces joined.

(By luck. The luck was

not repeatable.)

Ten pieces refused all

proposals.

The box was consulted.

The pieces were stirred.

The hot feeling visited

the chest.

"Dis puzzle is too HARD."

And Mama, instead of

solving it, instead of

the visitor's mistake,

the pop-it-in mercy...

said the month's whole

motto, first edition:

"It's too hard TODAY.

It won't be too hard

forever. That's what

puzzles are for...

they wait while you

grow at them."

The box closed. Placed

on the shelf. Not away...

VISIBLE. Waiting.

"We'll visit it every

day. A little. Like

watering the lettuce."

The mountain, introduced.

The month, begun.

(Twelve pieces. Twenty-

some days. The far

pages know the finish

date. It's a good one.

Mountains usually are...

from the top.)
