Night 118

The Longest Sleepover

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

Lily's room. The spare mattress.

The real sleepover, at last...

on the biggest night possible.

Neither girl sleeps much.

(Of course not.

Somewhere across town,

a baby is arriving.

You don't sleep through

the arrival of a person.)

They whisper under the blanket:

"Is da baby here yet?"

"Not yet. Mamas take a while.

My cousin took ALL NIGHT."

(Expertise. Comforting.)

"What if it's a brudder?"

"Den you teach him fings."

"What if it's a sister?"

"Den you teach HER fings."

(Either way: teaching.

The curriculum is ready.)

Rosa checks on them at two...

finds them finally asleep,

tangled like the grocery-store

heap of long ago,

one bear and one rabbit

standing watch.

And downstairs, near dawn,

the phone...

the phone lights up...

Rosa reads it.

Reads it again.

Puts her hand over her mouth

in the kitchen's small light.

Then climbs the stairs,

sits gently on the mattress edge,

and wakes the big sister

the way big news should

always arrive:

softly, smiling, first.

"Olivia. Honey. Wake up.

There's somebody

who wants to meet you."
