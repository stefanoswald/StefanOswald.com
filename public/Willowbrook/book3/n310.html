Night 310

Kind to Theo When He's Terrible

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

Let the record show:

today, Theo was TERRIBLE.

(Twenty months of

terrible. The best kind.

But terrible.)

The morning: her tower...

the SHOW one, not the

sacrifice one, knocked.

Union rules violated.

The afternoon: her

drawing, a good duck,

draft-folder quality...

grabbed. Crumpled.

Partially EATEN.

And the kindness muscle

faced its heaviest

lift of the month:

being kind

when the other person

was just terrible

and small

and sorry-proof.

The storm rose. (Fair.

The provocation was

real. The book doesn't

pretend otherwise.)

The breath happened.

(Five fingers. The

fist opened empty.)

And then the choice...

watched from the kitchen

by a mother holding

her own breath:

Olivia looked at her

crumpled duck.

Looked at her brother...

who was holding the

evidence, beaming at

her, entirely unaware,

saying "Livy!" like

she was the sun.

And chose.

"...You can't eat

ducks, Fee-oh."

(Flat. Older-sister

flat. But not thunder.)

"Paper is not food.

Here. DIS one's for

eating."

A cracker: supplied.

The duck: recovered,

smoothed, filed in

the folder. ("Draft

five: partially eaten.

Still teaching.")

And the seminar, small

and tired and real:

"Being kind to Fee-oh

is HARD some days."

"The hardest reps,"

said Mama, "grow the

most muscle."

The hardest reps grow

the most muscle.

He'll test that muscle

for twenty more years.

It'll hold.

It got built on days

exactly like this one...

one eaten duck

at a time.
