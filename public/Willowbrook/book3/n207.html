Night 207

Theo Cruises the Coastline

Month 7: Potty Power

Meanwhile, at the couch:

the coastline has a

new sailor.

Theo CRUISES now.

Hand over hand, sidestep

by sidestep, the couch's

whole length, the same

coastal highway his sister

charted years ago,

reopened for its second

captain.

And the harbor pilot

walks the route with him.

BACKWARD. In front.

Cheering every shuffle:

"Dat's it! Hand, den foot!

Hand, den foot!

Dis couch is EASY.

I learned on dis couch.

Da gap is coming up...

da armchair gap...

it's scary but you can

do it. I did it...

watch my face, not da

floor..."

(Watch my face,

not the floor.

Nobody taught her that.

THE COACHING GIFT.

The far books' every

teacher, every captain,

every steady-voiced friend

of hers says some version

of it forever.

It starts here.

At a couch.

Walking backward.)

The gap: crossed.

(One hand released,

the lean, the catch...

the whole historic

maneuver, second edition.)

The cheer: seismic.

Two mobile children now.

The house adjusts its

whole physics again.

The floor keeps score:

one walker, one cruiser,

zero stationary citizens

remaining.

The running-after era:

loading.

The coach: ready.

She's been ready

her whole life.
