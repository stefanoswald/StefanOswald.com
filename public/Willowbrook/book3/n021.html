Night 21

The Worry About the Dark Corner

Month 1: Big Feelings Have Names

A new feeling moved in this week.

It comes at bedtime.

It lives. Olivia points...

THERE.

The corner. By the dresser.

Where the shadow is.

"What's in the corner, love?"

She doesn't have a word.

Just... something. Maybe.

A worry.

Worry is scared's quieter cousin.

It doesn't jump out.

It whispers what-ifs.

Papa doesn't say "nothing's there."

(Worry never believes that.)

He says: "Let's go SEE."

The lamp comes over.

The corner lights up:

Dresser. Wall. One sock. (Ah. There it is.)

No something. Just corner.

They check behind. Under. All of it.

Worry watches the whole inspection.

Worry is thorough. It appreciates thorough.

"Corner's empty," Olivia reports.

Empty corner. Certified.

The lamp goes back.

The shadow returns, but now

it's just the dresser's shadow.

Named. Known. Boring.

Worries shrink when you inspect them.

Every age. Every corner.

Bring the lamp. Look together.
