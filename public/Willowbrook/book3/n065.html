Night 65

The Spaghetti Night

Month 3: A Friend Called Lily

They came at six.

Rosa with her half of dinner.

Lily with her appetite.

And the kitchen filled up

with the good noise...

sauce bubbling, bread toasting,

mamas laughing, papas...

(Lily's papa was working late,

so OUR Papa did double

noodle-slurping demonstrations.)

The table, when it was ready:

spaghetti, mountains of it.

The accidental groceries,

become a feast.

Two girls, side by side,

slurping noodles at length.

(Slurping is legal

on spaghetti night. House rule.

Sauce to the eyebrows. Legal.

All of it legal.)

The grown-ups talked till late.

The girls played till they dropped...

actually dropped, mid-fort,

carried to the couch in a heap.

And at the door, at goodnight,

Rosa said to Mama:

"Some Tuesday, huh."

Some Tuesday.

It started with a scare.

It ended with THIS.

One more page tomorrow, little one.

The page where Mama

explains the whole thing.

The page this book was named for.
