Night 214

The Wrong Banana

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

The season opened over

a banana.

(It's always a banana.

Or a cup. Or a sock.

The books confirm: storms

pick the smallest doors.)

The crime: Mama broke

the banana in half.

To SHARE it.

But Olivia wanted it

WHOLE.

"FIX IT! PUT IT BACK!"

And here is the terrible

truth of bananas:

they don't go back.

The unfixable, discovered,

at snack, at two-almost-three.

The storm was immediate

and total.

Now, the grown-up view,

for the record:

it's just a banana.

And the kid view, equally

true:

the world just broke

and nobody can fix it

and nobody even seems

SORRY.

Both views. Same kitchen.

Mama held the second one:

"You wanted it whole.

It can't go back.

That's a hard, true thing."

No fixing. No laughing.

(Save the laughing for

years later. It keeps.)

Just the weather, witnessed,

until it rained out.

Then: the half banana,

eaten. Fine, actually.

The storm never was

about the banana.

It was about a world

that breaks things

and a girl learning...

one snack at a time...

that broken and okay

can live in the same

peel.
