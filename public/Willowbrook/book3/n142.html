Night 142

Spaghetti Night, Party of Six

Month 5: Theo Arrives

First Friday.

Spaghetti night. In ink.

And tonight, the institution

seats its sixth.

Theo, three weeks old,

asleep in the carrier...

parked at the table's head

like a small guest of honor

who slept through his own party.

(He did. Newborns do.

The party proceeded around him

at one notch quieter,

which for spaghetti night

is still considerable.)

The menu: eternal.

The noodles: slurped in tandem

by the founding two.

The sauce: eyebrow-deep

per regulations.

The retelling: performed...

"remember? Da carts?"...

with a new coda now:

"...and DEN we got a FEO!"

(The story grows.

Good founding stories do.

The crash begat the dinner,

the dinner begat the tradition,

the tradition now holds

a sleeping baby at its head.

Maybe runs long.

Maybe runs generations.)

At the door, at goodnight,

Rosa held the carrier handle

a moment:

"Six now."

Six now.

"Table's gonna need a leaf

soon enough."

It would. It got one.

(The far books confirm:

the table grew twice more.

The tradition never once

missed a first Friday.

Some things you plant

by accident

and harvest forever.)
