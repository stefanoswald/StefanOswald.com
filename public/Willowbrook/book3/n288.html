Night 288

The Kite That Wouldn't

Month 10: Try Again

A new thing from the

market: a KITE.

Red. Diamond. A tail

of little bows.

(She's watched the big

kids fly them off the

kite hill her whole

life. The dancing

diamonds. The shape

that flies. Today:

hers.)

And here is the honest

record of Kite Day One:

Run one: no wind.

The kite dragged the

grass like a tired pet.

Run two: wind! gust...

the kite up! six

feet! nosedive.

Crash.

Run three: up! spinning...

the TAIL tangled...

down.

Run four through nine:

variations on the theme.

Grass. Gust. Spin. Crash.

One tree, narrowly

negotiated.

The kite flew, cumulatively,

maybe forty seconds

all afternoon.

The hot chest visited.

The yet was deployed...

through teeth, but

deployed:

"Da kite can't fly...

YET."

And Papa, winding the

string at day's end:

"Kites are the honest

mountain, kiddo.

You can't pretend a

kite is flying.

It flies or it doesn't.

And it doesn't, until

the day the wind and

the string and your

running all agree.

Then it just... does.

And you never forget it."

"When do dey agree?"

"Some windy day.

We'll keep showing up

till we're there for it."

Keep showing up till

you're there for it.

The kite went home

folded.

The hill kept the

appointment open.

(A far book, the sixth

one, has a whole month

about a kite. This

red diamond is its

grandmother. The

showing-up starts here.)
