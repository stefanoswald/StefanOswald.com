Night 56

The Do-Over

Month 2: Me Do It

The pour went wrong today.

Not a lake. An OCEAN.

The pitcher slipped...

the whole thing...

milk EVERYWHERE.

Floor. Chair. Sock. (Poor sock.)

And Olivia froze.

Face crumpling toward the storm...

This is the moment.

Right here.

This is where a house decides

what mistakes mean.

Mama took a breath...

(the flower one)...

and said the family words:

"Whoops. Big spill.

Want a do-over?"

A do-over.

Not a scolding. Not a sigh.

A fresh start, offered like a napkin.

They cleaned it together.

(Cloths for two. The bureau's way.)

Then: the pitcher, refilled.

Halfway. Her hands. Steadier now...

the wet lesson still in them.

Pour...

IN the cup. All of it.

"ME DID IT."

Second try. Do-over. Nailed.

Mistakes aren't verdicts here.

They're first drafts.

The do-over is the family's

oldest piece of paper:

blank, fresh, always in supply.

Use it lavishly.

Everyone gets do-overs.

Everyone. Every day.
