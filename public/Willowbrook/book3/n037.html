Night 37

The Toothbrush Turn

Month 2: Me Do It

At the sink, a treaty.

The toothbrush treaty.

Because "ME brush it" is here...

and the molars are also here,

and molars need real brushing.

So: two turns.

Turn one: HERS.

She brushes. Her way.

(Her way is mostly

chewing the brush

while walking in a circle.

Some bristle contact occurs.)

Turn two: MAMA'S.

"My turn now, open big..."

The real scrub. Fronts, backs,

the new molar country.

Quick. Thorough. Done.

Her turn made it hers.

Mama's turn made it clean.

The treaty holds because

both turns are honored.

Skip hers: revolution.

Skip Mama's: cavities.

Keep both: peace, and teeth.

Someday, years yet...

turn two retires,

and her turn will be the whole job,

done right, because

it was always partly hers.

That's the long game at the sink.

Spit! (Mostly dribble. Progress.)

Rinse. Done.

Treaty renewed nightly.
