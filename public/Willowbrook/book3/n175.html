Night 175

The Double Bedtime

Month 6: Big Sister School

Bedtime runs double now.

The logistics, nightly:

Theo first. He's littlest.

His bath, his milk, his song...

(the wrong notes; he's a

full subscriber now)...

down by seven.

And Olivia WAITS.

Waiting at almost-three

is a whole skill.

The hardest one.

And here's how she does it:

she HELPS with his...

(the towel handed, the song

under-hummed at the door)...

and then...

her turn comes.

And her turn is FULL.

Nothing rushed. Nothing

shortened. The whole four steps,

unhurried:

HER bath. (Solo again.

The duck's full attention.)

HER stories. (Two. Her lap

rights, undiminished.)

HER song. (The original

subscriber. Front row.)

HER tuck, her kiss, her

"nigh-nigh" aimed and returned.

"Why does Fee-oh go first?"

she asked once.

"Littlest first. You went

first for two years.

Now you get something better."

"What's better?"

"Last. Last means

nobody's waiting.

Last means all the time

in the world."

She considered this.

Last: the unhurried seat.

The whole-lamp seat.

"...Last is good," she ruled.

Last is very good.

The double bedtime holds:

first the little,

then the long.

Everybody down by eight.

Everybody full.
