Night 144

The Song Works on Him

Month 5: Theo Arrives

The fussy hour. The long one.

Papa walking the floor

with Theo, sway, shush,

the whole book-one kit...

and the fuss holding out.

And from the doorway,

in pajamas, uninvited,

essential:

"Papa. Sing da song.

DA song. Our one."

The wrong-note one.

The heirloom.

So he sings it...

and she climbs onto the chair

and sings it WITH him,

her under-hum from the old

duet nights, the wrong notes

doubled now, perfectly wrong

in two voices...

and Theo...

Theo stops.

Mid-fuss. Listening.

The song his sister marinated in

for two years of bedtimes.

The song that leaked through

the wall of him for nine months.

The family's own frequency,

sung in stereo.

The fuss dissolves.

The eyes go heavy.

Done. Down. Out.

Papa and Olivia look at

each other over the crib rail...

co-performers, victorious.

"Da song works on him,"

she whispers.

Of course it works on him.

It's not a song, little one.

Not really.

It's the house, hummable.

It worked on you.

It'll work on him.

It'll work on children

neither of you have met yet,

in houses not built yet,

sung wrong in exactly

this way,

because this is the way

your family sings it.

Heirloom: transferred.

Second listener: acquired.

Wrong notes: eternal.
