Night 143

Theo's First Sunday

Month 5: Theo Arrives

Sunday. The truck. The farm.

Theo's first.

The introductions, conducted

at the fence line

per four generations

of exactly this liturgy...

and narrated, of course,

by the officer:

"FEE-OH. Dis is da FARM.

Dat's Henrietta. She's loud

like you. You'll be friends."

(Henrietta: BOK. Agreed.)

"Dat's da Duchess.

She says no to everyfing.

Don't take it personal."

(The Duchess: turned her back.

Then, the record certifies...

peeked. Even Duchesses peek

at babies. It's law.)

"Dat's da horse. He's big

but he's easy. Big fings

are mostly gentle."

(The great head, lowered

over the fence. One vast

soft breath, taken of the bundle.

Filed, in whatever way

horses file the new ones:

herd, small, ours.)

And the willow line, last.

The long look. And. Sunday

providing per its custom...

far off over the green:

a red bob. Rising once.

"...and DAT'S Ellie,"

whispered the officer.

"She's da secret. You'll see."

"Well now," said Grandpa Joe,

holding his newest parcel

in the gold,

the chair-in-progress waiting

in the workshop behind them.

"That's everybody, then."

That's everybody.

The whole constituency, met.

The farm took him in

the way the farm takes

everything:

slow, warm, permanent.

Sundays: now seating four.

The truck rode home heavy

with eggs and belonging.
