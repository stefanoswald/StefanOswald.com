Night 319

The Kindness Detectives

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

A new walk game this

week, invented on the

spot, destined (though

nobody knows it yet)

to become a whole

month of a whole

future book:

KINDNESS DETECTIVES.

The rules: walk the

town. Spot kindnesses

in the wild. Call them

like bird sightings.

The first patrol's log:

"DERE! Da grocer!"

(Saving soft ends for

the gold dog. Spotted

through the window.

A daily kindness,

running for years,

never once announced.)

"DERE! Da walk!"

(The Hendersons' path:

shoveled. By whom?

Unknown. The secret-rep

kind. The detectives

noted the mystery

with professional

respect: "Da best ones

hide.")

"Da bench!" (A forgotten

mitten, placed VISIBLE

on the armrest by

some passing stranger,

where its owner could

find it. The town's

oldest tiny custom.)

"ROSA!" (The celebration

bun economy itself...

spotted in the act,

through the bakery

glass, slipping an

extra roll into a

tired-looking man's

bag. The detectives

pressed their noses

to the window. Rosa

winked.)

Seven kindnesses,

one walk.

"Mama. Da town is FULL

of dem. Dey're

EVERYWHERE.

Why didn't I see dem

before?"

Because you weren't

looking, little one...

and here's the

detectives' real secret,

the reason the game

matters:

what you look for,

you find.

And what you find,

you learn to do.

And what you do...

somebody smaller

is always watching.

The patrol continues

all month.

The town never once

runs short of sightings.

It never has.

That's the whole

case file on

Willowbrook.
