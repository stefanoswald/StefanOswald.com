Night 307

The Casserole Run

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

News from down the lane:

the Hendersons are sick.

Both of them. The whole

house, down with the

season's crud.

And in this town, you

know what that means.

You've read three books

of what that means:

CASSEROLES ARE MOVING.

Mama made the family's...

the good one, the

noodly one, and the

delivery run was

staffed, obviously,

by the wagon and its

officer.

The casserole rode

like treasure. (It IS

treasure. She knows

the economy now. She's

watched it her whole

life: the baskets,

the fog-week meals,

the food that shows

up exactly when a

family can't cook.

The village runs on

casseroles the way

lanes run on waves.)

Up the walk. The knock.

Mrs. Henderson, robe

and tissues, at the door.

"We heard you got da

crud," announced the

officer. "Dis is da

noodle one. You don't

cook till you're better.

Dat's da rules."

That's the rules.

(It is. It's the town's

oldest rule. Delivered

verbatim by its newest

enforcer.)

Mrs. Henderson laughed

herself into a cough

and took the treasure.

"Your family's been

feeding this lane

since before you were

born, sweetheart."

"I know," said the

officer. "Now I'M da

family too."

Now she's the family

too.

The economy has a new

courier.

The wagon rolled home

empty and heavier

somehow...

the way giving always

weighs,

which is to say:

just right.
