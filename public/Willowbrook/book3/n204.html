Night 204

The Farm Sleepover, Potty Edition

Month 7: Potty Power

The big test of any

new skill: TRAVEL.

Tonight: the farm overnight.

(Her third ever. First

since the ducks era.)

The kit, packed by

the champion herself:

Underpants: four pairs.

("Two is not enough

if rain happens twice."

Veteran math. Approved.)

The seat insert: packed.

The step stool: the farm

HAS one. (Star-carved.

The workshop provides

for all altitudes.

Grandpa thinks ahead

by generations.)

And the routine, ported:

the try before bed,

conducted at the old

commode, "still old,

Gampa" ("still older

than your father")...

the anthem sung, duck-butt

verse echoing off farmhouse

tile that has heard

four generations of

washing songs.

The night: DRY.

(Day-dry. The night diaper

rode along per the two-

clocks treaty. No shame

at any latitude.)

The morning report,

by phone, at volume:

"MAMA. I POTTIED AT

DA FARM. DA SKILLS

CAME WIF ME."

The skills came with her.

That's what makes them

skills.

"Well now," said the farm.

The heritage commode

notched its fourth

generation.

Five stars, revised.

Heritage plus.
