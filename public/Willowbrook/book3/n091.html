Night 91

How Long Is Spring

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

The first question of waiting:

"Is da baby coming TODAY?"

Not today.

"TOMORROW?"

Not tomorrow, love.

"...AFTER tomorrow??"

Oh dear. Time is hard at two.

Spring is a word.

Words don't tell you

how many sleeps.

So Papa built a thing.

A paper chain!

Link after link after link,

one for every sleep,

stretching across the kitchen wall

all the way to a drawing:

a little baby, with a star on it.

"Every night, we tear one link.

When the links run out..."

"DA BABY!"

The baby.

So every night now,

after teeth, before stories:

the tearing.

One link. RRRIP.

(The best chore ever invented.)

The chain gets shorter.

The spring gets closer.

Waiting, made visible.

Made tearable.

Made hers.

Rrrip.

One sleep closer, baby.

See you at the end

of the chain.
