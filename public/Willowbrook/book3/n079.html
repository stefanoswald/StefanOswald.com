Night 79

The Quiet Kind of Together

Month 3: A Friend Called Lily

A strange Thursday hour.

Listen...

nothing.

No shrieks. No crashes.

No negotiations.

The mamas peek in, worried...

and find:

the couch.

Two girls.

Two books.

Total silence.

Olivia: the bear book.

(Reading it her way.

Lips moving. Pages turning.)

Lily: the tractor one.

(Deep in it. One finger

tracking something important.)

Not playing together.

Not playing separately.

Something else. Something new.

Together-quiet.

Alone, side by side, on purpose.

Comfortable as socks.

This is an advanced friendship

skill. Most grown-ups

are still working on it...

being with someone

without performing anything.

Just... shared quiet.

Two books. Four knees. One couch.

The mamas back away slowly.

(You don't interrupt

a together-quiet.

It's rarer than gold.)

Ten whole minutes it held.

Then: "Wiwi! Look! Da button!"

And the noise resumed,

right on schedule.

But it happened.

The quiet kind of together.

The keeper kind.

First of thousands.
