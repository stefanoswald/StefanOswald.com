Night 77

The Third Friend Question

Month 3: A Friend Called Lily

A Thursday development:

the little one came.

The bench recruit. The scooter.

(Walking now. Talking a little.

Growing the way they do.)

And three...

three is a NEW SHAPE.

Two is easy. Two is a pair.

Two is you-pull-me-I-pull-you.

Three wobbles.

Watch it wobble:

Two build the tower.

One knocks it. (Not meanly.

One-year-olds knock. It's physics.)

STORM WARNING...

"NO! We BUILDING!"

The little one's lip: wobbling.

And then Lily...

eleven days of wisdom...

finds the third-friend key:

"You knock DIS one."

A sacrifice tower.

Built quick, two blocks,

JUST for knocking.

The little one knocks it. Bliss.

Builds-and-knocks. Repeat.

Three jobs found:

two builders, one demolisher.

The shape: stabilized.

Three takes practice.

(It takes practice at every age.

Ask any grown-up.)

But the blanket held three today.

The circle widened

and didn't break.

That's the whole trick

of every circle ever:

widen, hold, widen, hold.

Room for the next one.

Always room.
