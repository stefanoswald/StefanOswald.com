Night 333

The Kindness Month, Counted

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

Month's end. The audit.

The reps, counted:

Doors held: many.

(Doorkeeper: a standing

title now. The market

knows her.)

Casseroles run: one,

with rules enforced.

Dandelions to difficult

hens: twenty-some.

Nods received: three.

(THREE. The Duchess

is practically effusive

now. By Duchess

standards.)

Toys given for keeps:

one set of cups,

traveling loose in

the world.

Rocks secretly gifted:

one. (Soul-grade.

Wages paid inside.)

New kids passworded:

one. (June. Roads

department. Thriving.)

Uphill kindnesses: one

pit crew.

Slow-speed fence

sittings: daily.

Soggy crackers received

as treasure: one,

unforgettable.

Benches sat, fixing

nothing, staying anyway:

one. (The winner.)

And one unkind day...

one snatch, one shove...

entered honestly,

next to its repair,

per the family's

whole method.

The finding, at the

tally's end:

the muscle is real.

It grew all month...

you could watch it

grow, and here's

the strangest thing

about its growing:

"Mama. When I do

da kind fings...

I feel strong AFTER.

Like da reps go

in ME."

The reps go in you.

That's the secret

the whole month was

keeping:

kindness is the only

exercise where

every rep

strengthens both

people.

Audited. Approved.

Carried forward:

stronger, softer,

both, the way

only this muscle

ever manages.
