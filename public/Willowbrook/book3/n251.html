Night 251

The Calm-Down Five

Month 9: Counting to Five

The cozy corner got

an upgrade this week.

The counting kind.

The old tool: the breath.

Flower in, dandelion out.

Good tool. Faithful.

The new edition:

THE CALM-DOWN FIVE.

Five breaths. Counted

on five fingers.

Fold one finger per

breath.

Watch it work, mid-

shelter, during Tuesday's

squall:

Breath one, flower,

dandelion, thumb folds.

Breath two, pointer

folds.

(The storm, measurably

smaller already. Counting

occupies the front of

the mind; the weather

loses its stage.)

Free, four...

and five: pinky down.

FIST.

"Zero storm," she

announced, opening

the fist like releasing

a butterfly.

Zero storm.

The fist opens empty.

The weather, breathed

out one finger at a

time.

Here's why it works,

for the grown-ups

taking notes (take

notes; this tool is

yours too):

you cannot count and

panic at full strength

simultaneously. The

brain shares the road.

Numbers take up lanes.

Five fingers of lanes

is usually enough.

The corner's shelf

now holds: pillow,

pinwheel, books, and

one invisible tool

that lives in her

own hand,

five breaths deep,

installed for life.

Storm math.

It adds up to calm.

Every time.
