Night 314

Kind to Herself

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

The milk spilled today.

(It happens. It'll

always happen. The

lakes are eternal.)

But this time, something

new came out with it...

something Mama had

never heard before:

"I'm SO dumb.

I ALWAYS spill.

I'm bad at cups."

...

Where did THAT come

from?

(Nobody knows. It leaks

in. From bigger kids,

from overheard corners,

from the general air.

The mean inner voice

finds every child

eventually. The only

question is whether

anybody's home to

argue with it.)

Somebody was home.

"Hey. Hold on.

If LILY spilled her

milk, what would

you say to her?"

"...I'd say 'whoops,

rain happened.

Everybody spills.'"

"And if Lily said

'I'm so dumb'?"

"I'd say NO you're

NOT, you're my best

friend, it's just

MILK..."

She stopped.

Hearing it.

The gap between the

two voices, the one

for Lily and the one

for herself, standing

wide open in the

kitchen.

"Talk to yourself,"

said Mama, "like you

talk to Lily.

You're in there too.

Be YOUR best friend

too."

The retraining, begun

that day, continued

for years (it continues

for everyone, for

always, this muscle

never fully finishes):

Spill: "...Whoops.

Rain happened."

Fall: "Everybody falls.

Ask Fee-oh."

Bad drawing: "Draft.

We don't crumple

teachers. Even me."

Even me.

The hardest rep in

the book.

The one that holds

all the others up.

Be kind in there,

little one.

You live in there.
