Night 356

Papa's Letter at Three

Month 12: Almost Three

The next night. Same

lamp. The twin

commission:

"Dear Olivia...

Your mama writes hers

beautiful. I write

mine true. Here's

this year's true:

I dented my own barn

this year. Metaphorically.

The shelf collapsed

and I said the loud

GAH and you, age two...

stood in my garage

door and told me to

do the breath.

And I did the breath.

Your breath. The one

your mama taught you

that you taught me.

This family passes

its tools in every

direction, and this

year I learned I'm

not always the teacher.

Best demotion of my

life.

You whistle now. It

took you a year. I

watched you fail at

it, quietly, in

mirrors, to the bear...

for twelve months,

and I want you to

know: watching you

NOT give up taught

me more about you

than the whistle did.

The whistle is one

note. The year of

spit was the whole

song.

Your brother's first

word was your name.

I don't have anything

to add to that.

Some facts are

complete.

When you were born

I promised to hold

a post at the window.

I'm still there. The

post got bigger...

two of you now, and

you at trike speed...

but I want it on

record at three,

same as at one, same

as at every age

you'll ever read this:

holding the post is

the best thing I do.

Happy birthday, whistler.

Papa

(first chair, your

section, permanent...

and these days,

sometimes, gratefully,

your student)"

Sealed. Shelved.

The pair, complete.

Three years. Six

letters.

The someday-drawer,

gaining weight...

the good kind,

the kind that

waits.
