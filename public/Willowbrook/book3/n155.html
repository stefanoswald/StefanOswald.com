Night 155

My Baby, My Show-and-Tell

Month 6: Big Sister School

Thursday. The blanket.

And today's programming,

announced by its host:

"FEE-OH SHOW."

The delegates assemble.

(Lily, front row. The recruit.

Two newer bench babies,

attending in carriers.

Full house.)

The presentation:

"DIS is my brudder Fee-oh.

He is five months.

He can do FINGS now."

Exhibit one: the grip.

(Fingers offered around

the circle. All captured.

The move never misses.)

Exhibit two: THE ROLL.

"Fee-oh. Roll."

(A pause. The audience holds.

The performer considers...

rocks... ROLLS.

The blanket ERUPTS.

He rolls the other way...

new this week. ENCORE.)

Exhibit three: the laugh.

"Watch dis."

The puffed-cheek face...

and the laugh, on cue,

belly-deep, contagious...

and the whole blanket

goes down with it,

delegates and babies

and one passing dog.

"DA END," bows the host.

Standing ovation.

(Sitting ovation.

The audience is two.)

And Lily, loyal forever:

"Dat's da BEST baby show

I ever saw."

It was the only baby show

she ever saw.

It was also the best.

Both true. Both filed.

The show runs weekly now.

New material monthly.

The performer works cheap:

one face, any face, hers.
