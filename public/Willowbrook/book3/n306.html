Night 306

The Door Hold

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

Today's rep: found at

the market door.

An old man with a cane,

approaching. And Mama,

about to hold the door...

beaten to it.

By somebody faster.

Somebody door-height.

Somebody who planted

herself against the

heavy glass with her

WHOLE body, feet

braced, both hands,

the full cushion-

carrying commitment...

and held it.

"I got it!"

The old man came

through. Slow. Smiling.

"Well, thank you,

young lady."

"You're WELCOME."

(Volume: full. Pride:

fuller.)

And then, because

held doors gather

traffic, a mother

with a stroller. Through.

"Thank you!"

Two kids with a wagon.

Through. "Thanks!"

The grocer himself,

carrying crates.

Through. "Much obliged,

doorkeeper."

DOORKEEPER.

A title. Earned. Worn

visibly for the rest

of the errand.

Six people, one door,

one girl holding it...

and here's the month's

first finding, collected

at the glass:

kindness this small

costs nearly nothing...

thirty seconds, one

braced body...

and pays EVERYONE.

The passers, passed.

The keeper, thanked

six times, glowing

for an hour.

Cheapest rep in the

gym.

Biggest return in town.

Hold the doors,

little one.

All of them.

Forever.
