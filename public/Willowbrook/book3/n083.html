Night 83

Two Families, One Table

Month 3: A Friend Called Lily

Remember spaghetti night?

The accidental one?

The swapped-cart one?

It happened again.

On purpose this time.

And again.

And now, look at the calendar...

it has a DAY.

First Friday. Every month.

Alternating houses.

SPAGHETTI NIGHT.

(In ink. Institutions get ink.)

The recipe never changes:

the long noodles, the red sauce,

the good cheese, the garlic bread...

the exact menu two little girls

once assembled by larceny.

The table gets louder each month.

Both families, all the news,

the girls slurping in tandem,

sauce achieving eyebrows,

the papas trading terrible jokes,

the mamas trading real ones.

And every single time,

at some point in the noise,

somebody tells the story.

"Remember? The carts?"

They remember. Everyone remembers.

The retelling is on the menu.

This is how family traditions

are actually born:

not planned. CAUGHT.

From an accident that turned gold

and refused to stop glowing.

Set the table, girls.

First Friday, forever.
