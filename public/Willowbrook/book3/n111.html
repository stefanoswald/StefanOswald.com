Night 111

The False Alarm

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

In the night: commotion!

Lights on. Voices.

The BAG, grabbed!

Rosa, called!

Olivia, bundled in blankets,

delivered to a sleepy Lily's house

in PAJAMAS...

IS IT BABY TIME?!

...

It was not baby time.

It was practice baby time.

(The baby knocked, sort of,

then changed its mind.

Babies do that. Doctors checked.

All is well. Just a drill.)

By lunch, everyone was home.

The bag went back to its post.

And Olivia, who had been

promised a baby, filed

a formal complaint:

"Da baby TRICKED us."

It did, a little.

"Dat's BAD."

And Mama, of course,

you know what Mama said...

"Maybe."

Because look what the trick did:

Now everyone knows the drill.

Rosa's door: forty seconds.

The bag: flawless. The road: clear.

When the real night comes,

it will run like butter...

BECAUSE of the trick.

"Da trick was... practice?"

The trick was practice.

Maybe strikes again.

"Okay, baby," she told the bump

that evening. "Good drill.

But NEXT time, for REAL."

THUMP.

(Agreed. Allegedly.)
