Night 108

The Blessingway Basket

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

Something is happening

at Rosa's kitchen table.

The bench mothers are here.

All of them. And a BASKET...

a big one, filling up:

Little knitted things.

(The aunts sent north's share.)

Freezer meals, labeled,

stacking like soft bricks...

"for the fog weeks,"

say the veterans, knowing.

A jar of Rosa's starter,

with feeding instructions...

"bread for the first month.

It rises while you don't."

Tiny socks. A soft blanket.

And one envelope of

handwritten pages...

each mother's best

first-weeks wisdom,

folded like recipes.

(They ARE recipes.)

Olivia contributes too.

Her item, placed on top:

one drawing. A duck. Of course.

Labeled by Mama, dictated:

"For da baby. From da big sister.

Ducks are da best fing.

You will see."

This is how the village

meets a baby:

before it arrives,

in a basket,

in casseroles and wool

and folded wisdom.

Nobody organized it.

The bench just knows.

The bench always knows.

The basket goes home

in the wagon.

Heavy as love.

Spring is provisioned.
