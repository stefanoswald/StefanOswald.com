Night 48

When Me-Do-It Can't

Month 2: Me Do It

The lace-up shoes.

Papa's old pair, found in a box,

tried on for fun,

enormous, wonderful.

And the laces...

"ME tie it."

Oh, sweetheart.

She tries. The laces flop.

Tries. Tangle.

Tries. Knot, the wrong kind...

the kind that means nothing.

The hot feeling rises...

And here's a hard truth

this month has to hold:

some things aren't yours yet.

Not because you're not enough.

Because hands grow in order.

Pouring came. Buttons came.

Laces come around five or six.

That's YEARS of hands from now.

"Laces are a someday skill,"

says Papa. Honest and warm.

"Your hands are still building it.

Want to see the bunny-ears trick

anyway? Just to visit it?"

He ties. Slow. Loop, loop, pull.

She watches like the puzzle box.

Filing it. For someday.

"Someday me?"

Someday you. Guaranteed.

Not every wall falls today.

Some you just wave at,

and grow toward.
