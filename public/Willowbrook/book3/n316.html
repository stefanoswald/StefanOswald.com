Night 316

Theo's First Kindness

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

A hard moment for Olivia

today. A small one...

the whistle, again.

Lily whistled a real

whistle at Thursday's

goodbye, and hers is

still all spit, and

the yet held her over

mostly but a little

sad leaked through

at snack.

She sat with it. Quietly.

(Weathering. Sky-style.

No storm, just grey.)

And across the rug,

somebody noticed.

Somebody twenty-one

months old, mid-cracker,

who has never been

taught a kindness lesson

in his life...

only SHOWN them.

Ten thousand of them.

Since the day he was

born.

Theo stood up.

Toddled over.

And held out his cracker.

His OWN cracker. The

one he was eating.

Half-gummed. Fully

offered.

"Livy. Cacka."

For her.

Because she was sad.

Because that's what

you do, his whole

short life had shown

him, when somebody's

sad: you bring them

something, and you

stay.

He brought something.

He stayed. He patted

her knee, pat, pat,

the family's oldest

gesture, learned at

the source.

And his sister took

the soggy cracker

like treasure...

(it WAS treasure;

you know the exchange

rate by now; this book

has taught it since

the soggy puff of

the very first year)...

and ate it,

and cried a little

more, the other kind

now, the good kind,

and hugged him until

he squirmed.

The tide, flowing back.

Everything poured in,

pouring out.

His first documented

kindness.

Aimed, like his first

steps, like his first

word, like everything...

exactly where you'd

guess.

The muscle runs in

the family.

It's her best

inheritance to him.

It's already working.
