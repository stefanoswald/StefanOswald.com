Night 231

The Apology Flower

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

Today's storm did damage.

Real damage. The worst

kind:

it knocked THEO'S tower.

(His first tower. Two

blocks. Stacked by his

own nine-month hands

that very hour...

his first-ever construction,

and her storm's flailing

foot caught it.)

He wailed. She stormed on.

The weather passed.

And in the after...

the wreckage-survey

moment, she saw it:

the two blocks, scattered.

The brother, tear-streaked.

Her storm did that.

The sorry feeling...

the old one, the heavy

squirmy one from the

bucket-and-Lily days...

arrived at full weight.

And she invented,

on the spot, the ritual

the family kept forever:

She went to the garden.

Picked one flower.

(A dandelion. Gold.

The best kind for the

purpose, as it turns out...

sturdy, abundant,

child-pickable.)

Brought it back.

Knelt at rug level.

"Fee-oh. My storm

knocked your tower.

I sorry. Dis is your

sorry-flower.

And now, watch...

I build it BACK."

Flower: presented.

(Gummed immediately.

Received, in his way.)

Tower: rebuilt, two

blocks, his hands

guided to place the

top one so the rebuild

was HIS.

Sorry, in this house,

from this day:

a flower and a fix.

Words plus hands.

The apology that

repairs something.

The only kind that

ever fully counts.

The garden served

the ritual for decades.

The dandelions never

once ran short.

They grow, conveniently,

exactly at the rate

children need

forgiving.
