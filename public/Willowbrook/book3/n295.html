Night 295

The Nose Blow

Month 10: Try Again

Not every mountain

is a kite.

Some mountains are...

the nose.

Specifically: blowing it.

The problem, universal

to all small noses:

when you say "blow,"

they SNIFF.

In, instead of out.

Every time. The nose's

factory setting is

backwards.

The training, invented

at the kitchen table:

THE FEATHER GAME.

One feather (the sill

museum's, on loan).

Placed on the table.

"Make it move, with

your NOSE wind.

Mouth closed."

Attempt one: sniff.

The feather, unmoved.

(Slightly inhaled-at.

The feather has seen

things now.)

Attempt two: a blow!

through the MOUTH.

(Cheating. Flagrant.

The feather flew;

the judges disallowed.)

Attempts three through

lots: the slow rewiring.

Mouth closed. Air DOWN

the nose. Out.

The feather... trembled.

Then SKITTERED.

NOSE WIND. ACHIEVED.

"I HAVE NOSE WIND!"

And from there: the

tissue. Applied. Blown...

OUT, properly, both

barrels...

the real, whole,

certified nose-blow.

A skill so small nobody

puts it in books.

(This book puts everything

in books.)

So small, and so

mighty: every cold

of her life just got

easier. Every stuffy

night, self-serviceable.

The feather returned

to the museum with

a new placard:

"Taught da nose wind."

Small mountains count.

Climb them all.

The nose is very proud.

You can tell.

(You can't tell.

Noses have one look.

But you can TELL.)
