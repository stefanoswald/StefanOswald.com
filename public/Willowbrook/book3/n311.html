Night 311

The New Kid at the Blanket

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

A new family at the park

this week. New to town.

And one small girl...

Olivia-sized, maybe less...

at the sandbox edge.

Not digging.

Hovering. The hover

of the new kid, close

enough to want in,

far enough to not

know how.

(Every playground on

earth has that exact

spot. Every new kid

finds it. It's the

loneliest three feet

in childhood.)

The blanket delegation

noticed.

A conference, brief,

whispered, two heads:

"She's new."

"She's got no friend

yet."

YET. (The word, working

everywhere now.)

And the delegation

rose, crossed the

grass, and deployed

the ancient protocol...

the one their mothers

used, the bench's own

founding technology,

adapted for sandbox

use:

Lily: "Hi. Wanna dig?"

(The password. It has

a hundred forms. They

all mean the same:

the circle opens here.)

And Olivia, extending

the second shovel,

the good one, HER one:

"You can do da roads.

Roads are da best job.

I'll show you da

packing. Packing is

da secret."

The new kid took the

shovel.

The hover, three feet

of loneliness...

closed.

Three diggers. One

kingdom. Roads

everywhere by lunch.

Her name was June.

(She's in the far books.

Of course she's in

the far books. Circles

that open keep what

they let in.)

The password never

wears out, little ones.

Somebody's always

hovering.

Be the ones who

cross the grass.
