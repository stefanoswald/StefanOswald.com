Night 225

The Words Come Back

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

Remember the expert's

manual?

"My words go far away.

I can see dem but I

can't reach dem.

Just stay."

Tonight, Papa followed

it to the letter.

The storm: mid-sized.

The cause: unclear.

(Some storms never file

their paperwork.)

And the old instinct...

every parent's...

was to ASK:

"What's wrong? Use your

words! Tell me what

happened! Can you just

SAY it?"

He didn't.

(The manual. The words

are far away. Asking

for them mid-storm is

asking someone to swim

with their arms tied.

It adds a storm ON

the storm.)

Instead: he stayed.

Sat on the floor, near,

not touching, not talking.

The ground, being ground.

Five minutes. Eight.

The thunder slowing...

the rain part...

the hiccups...

And then, on their own,

from wherever they'd

been...

the words came back:

"...Wiwi's moving houses."

Oh.

OH.

(Not moving TOWNS.

Moving STREETS. Two

streets over. But at

almost-three, a moved

house is a moved world.

The storm had a real

center all along.

They usually do.)

NOW the talking. Now

the questions. Now the

holding and the "two

streets is still Thursday-

close" and the whole

repair.

The words come back,

little one.

They always come back.

The ones who wait for

them, quietly, on the

floor...

those are the ones

the words come back TO.
