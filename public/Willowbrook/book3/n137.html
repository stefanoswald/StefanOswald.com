Night 137

Acting Small

Month 5: Theo Arrives

An evening, three weeks in.

Theo: fed, down, quiet.

And Olivia, big girl,

big bed, big month...

comes to Mama small.

Smaller than usual.

Thumb near the mouth.

(Retired months ago.

Visiting tonight.)

"Mama... rock ME like da baby?"

Some houses would say:

"You're a BIG girl now."

This house knows better.

This house read the books

and wrote a few.

"Come here, my baby."

And she gathers her up...

all the long legs of her,

knees hanging everywhere...

and wraps the blanket round,

and rocks.

The old sway. The old shush.

The nursery chair, creaking

its two-generation song.

Ten minutes of being

the baby again.

Drinking the old country.

Filling the tank.

Here's the truth of it,

for every house with a new one:

acting small isn't going backward.

It's checking.

Checking that the old country

still exists. That the arms

still make the shape.

That big-girl is a choice,

not an eviction.

The tank fills.

The thumb retires again,

on its own.

And off she climbs...

big again, restored...

to her own bed,

by her own legs,

per her own choice.

"Nigh-nigh, Mama."

The check cleared.

It always will.

Rock them backward

whenever they ask.

It's how they go forward.
