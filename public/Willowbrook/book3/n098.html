Night 98

Mama Is Tired Differently

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

Something about Mama

is different these weeks.

She sits down more.

She naps when Olivia naps.

(SHE naps?! Grown-ups CAN?)

She says "oof" getting up.

She rubs her back at the sink.

"Mama tired?"

"A little, love.

Growing a person is big work.

The biggest. Even sitting still,

I'm building somebody."

Olivia considers this.

Building. While SITTING.

(Impressive, honestly.

Towers require her whole body.)

And then, watch her...

the kit deploys. Backward.

The pillow, dragged over.

Placed behind Mama's back.

"Dere."

The blanket, hauled onto the lap.

The bear, contributed.

(The ultimate loaner.

Emergency comfort only.)

And her own self, climbing up...

gently, new-gently,

big-sister gently...

to rest alongside.

"I build wif you," she says.

And they sit, the three of them...

Mama, Olivia,

and the somebody in progress...

building together

in the afternoon light.

Some care flows down.

The best houses teach it

to flow every direction.

This one did.

Look at it flowing.
