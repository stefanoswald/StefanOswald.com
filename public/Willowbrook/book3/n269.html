Night 269

The Turn-Taking Count

Month 9: Counting to Five

A walking brother means

a REACHING brother.

Everything she holds:

petitioned. "Aden! ADEN!"

And grabbing back...

the old law, teaches

grabbing.

So the count went to

work on its hardest

assignment yet:

TURNS. By fives.

The system, built with

Mama, run by the senior

partner:

"Fee-oh's turn: five."

The toy: his. The count:

hers, out loud, fingers

folding:

"One... two... free...

four... five. MY turn!"

The toy: transferred.

(He protests the first

dozen rounds. Then...

the miracle of the

audible clock, he

starts to WAIT. The

count tells him the

turn is coming. Visible

time again. It works

at every size.)

"My turn: five.

One... two..."

And by week's end,

the sound in the rug

country, unprompted,

running the peace all

by itself:

one small voice counting

her own turn honestly,

one smaller voice

attempting the count

along, "wa... doo...

FEE!" waiting, mostly,

for the handoff.

Five seconds of having.

Five seconds of waiting.

The whole machinery

of sharing, running

on the month's own

arithmetic.

Turns are just counting

with love in it.

The rug knows it now.

Both citizens certified.

Peace: numbered,

and holding.
