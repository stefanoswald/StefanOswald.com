Night 190

The Long Car Ride Test

Month 7: Potty Power

The true test of every

potty-learner:

THE CAR.

Today: the drive to the

far orchard. Forty minutes.

An eternity, bladder-wise.

The protocols, veteran-grade:

The pre-departure try.

("Nofing came." "Doesn't

matter. We tried." Doctrine.)

The spare clothes, packed.

(Two sets. The veteran number.)

The towel on the car seat.

(Insurance. Unmentioned.

Dignity preserved.)

And the mid-drive moment...

twenty minutes in,

the voice from the back:

"...I hafta go.

FOR REAL dis time."

The gas station. The sprint.

(Every parent knows

the sprint. The carry-run.

The door. The "we're almost

there, hold on, hold on...")

MADE IT.

Made it entirely.

The gas station bathroom...

reviewed, naturally:

"Kind of stinky. Two stars."...

served its noble purpose.

Back on the road,

champion intact, spare

clothes unneeded,

parents exchanging the

eyebrow of victory.

"I held it," she reported,

"like holding da handle

on da swing."

(The best description

of continence ever spoken.

The books should use it.)

The orchard was reached.

The apples were picked.

The bladder was trusted.

Road status: conquered.

Two stars for the facilities.

Five stars for the girl.
