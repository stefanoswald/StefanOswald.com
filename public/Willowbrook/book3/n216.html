Night 216

The Cozy Corner

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

A construction project

this week:

the cozy corner.

(Some houses call it

calm-down. Some call it

time-out. This house

built something else...

and the difference is

the whole point.)

Built TOGETHER. That's

rule one. She chose

the corner. She chose

the cushion. She stocked it:

The squeeze pillow.

(For arms that want

to throw. Squeeze instead.

It's throwable energy,

harmlessly spent.)

The pinwheel.

(Blow the storm INTO it.

The flower-dandelion breath,

now with a spinning toy.

Genius. Rosa's idea.)

Two books. Soft ones.

And the light, a little

lamp, warm.

Rule two, said plain:

"Nobody SENDS you here.

This isn't a punishment

place. It's a weather

shelter. You GO here...

when you feel the storm

coming, or I might ask

'want your corner?'...

and you can say no."

A shelter, not a sentence.

Hers, not the house's.

She tested it that

afternoon, no storm,

just moving in. Approved.

And when the real weather

came, days later...

she looked at the corner

mid-thunder...

and WENT.

Herself. Storming. Sheltering.

Three minutes of squeeze

pillow and pinwheel.

Out she came: rained out,

proud beyond measure.

"I weathered myself."

She weathered herself.

Some corners hold

a whole childhood up.

This one did.
