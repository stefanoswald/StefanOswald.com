Night 252

Hide and Seek Unlocked

Month 9: Counting to Five

Counting to five unlocked

a door nobody mentioned:

HIDE AND SEEK.

(The real one. The one

that NEEDS counting.

You can't seek without

the count. The count

is the game's whole

engine. She has the

engine now.)

The inaugural game,

Thursday, the park:

Olivia counts. Eyes

covered at the oak.

"One! Two! Free! Four!

FIVE! Ready or not!"

And Lily hides.

Behind the same bush.

(The same bush. Every

round. All afternoon.

Hiders at almost-three

have one spot and

total faith in it.

The giggling from the

bush does not help

the bush's case.)

"...WHERE could WIWI be?"

(The seeking, performed

at full theater. Checking

the slide. The bench.

The completely wrong

places. The bush GIGGLES.)

"FOUND YOU!"

SHRIEKING. Collapse.

Again. Again. AGAIN.

Roles swapped: Olivia

hides. (Behind the oak.

Her one spot. Her total

faith. Her audible

giggle.)

Lily counts, "one two

free four five", the

count now community

property, passing

between them like

everything does.

Fourteen rounds.

Two bushes' worth of

faith.

Zero successful hides.

One hundred percent

successful GAME...

because finding was

always the point.

Hide and seek is just

peekaboo, grown up:

gone-then-found,

the oldest happy shape,

now self-service,

now counted,

now theirs for twenty

years of parks.

Ready or not.

Here they come.
