Night 12

The Missing Word for Missing

Month 1: Big Feelings Have Names

The call ended.

Grandpa waved. The screen went dark.

And Olivia... stayed.

Holding the phone.

Touching the dark glass

where the straw hat was.

"More Gampa," she says. Quiet.

There's a feeling for this.

A tender one. An achy one.

It's called missing.

Missing is love

with the person far away.

It aches because the love

is still right here,

reaching.

"You miss him," says Mama.

"I miss him too."

They miss together a minute.

Then Mama says the fixing words:

"Sunday. We'll see him Sunday.

Missing always ends in seeing."

Sunday. The word helps.

Missing needs a Sunday

to lean toward.

"Sunday," says Olivia to the phone.

The phone keeps the message.

The heart keeps the ache,

gently, till the truck

turns up the farm road.
