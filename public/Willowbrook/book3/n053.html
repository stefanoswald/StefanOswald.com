Night 53

The Table Setter

Month 2: Me Do It

A new job posting at dinner.

Position: table setter.

Applicant: one.

Hired: immediately.

The duties:

Napkins, one per seat.

(Placed with ceremony.

Sometimes folded. Into wads.

Wads are a style.)

Spoons, distributed.

(Her arrangement. Papa's spoon

faces backward. He never fixes it.

He says it's continental.)

The little cup, hers, placed

at her spot. Just so.

And the finale:

"Dinner ready!" called down the hall.

(The call is the best part.

It gets to be LOUD. Officially.)

The table, set by Olivia.

Every night now. Her job.

And here's what a job does

that no toy ever did:

it makes you part of the machine.

The family runs on jobs.

Now one of the gears is hers.

Small gear. Real gear.

The dinner arrives

on a table she made ready.

Taste that, in the soup.

That's contribution.

Best seasoning there is.
