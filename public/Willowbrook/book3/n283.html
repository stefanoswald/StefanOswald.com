Night 283

The Yet Word

Month 10: Try Again

The twelve-piece puzzle.

Daily visit. Day nine.

Four pieces joined now...

the corner cluster,

the duck's whole head...

and eight still refusing.

And the old sentence

came out, tired:

"I can't DO it."

And Mama taught the

word.

THE word. The one this

month was always

building toward:

"Say it again, but

put YET on the end."

"...I can't do it...

yet?"

"Feel the difference?"

She said them both,

testing:

"I can't do it."

(A wall. A closed box.

The end.)

"I can't do it YET."

(A door. A not-yet-open

door. A door with her

name on the list.)

"...YET means it's

COMING."

Yet means it's coming.

One syllable. Three

letters. And every

sentence it touches

changes species...

from verdict to forecast.

The house adopted it

at every wall that week:

"I can't whistle... YET."

"I can't reach da shelf...

YET."

"Fee-oh can't jump...

YET." (Administered

to the brother's file

too. The word covers

everyone.)

"I can't tie shoes...

YET." (The someday file,

renamed. Every entry

in it was always a

yet. Now it says so.)

Small word. Whole

worldview.

The growth-minded people

write books about it.

She got it at a puzzle

table, day nine,

eight pieces from

the summit.

Yet.

It's coming.

Everything on the list

is coming.
