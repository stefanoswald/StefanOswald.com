Night 123

Loud Is His Only Word

Month 5: Theo Arrives

Fact one about brothers,

learned the first night home:

LOUD.

The field trip warned her.

The cousin demonstrated.

But this is

through-the-wall loud.

Three-in-the-morning loud.

Every-three-hours loud.

"WAAAAH," says the wall.

Olivia puts the pillow

over her head.

"WAAAAH," says the wall,

through the pillow.

In the morning, at breakfast...

everyone wearing the fog...

she files her review:

"Da baby is VERY loud."

"He is," agrees Mama.

(No argument. Solidarity.

The fog respects honesty.)

"Why he cry so much?"

And here's where two years

of feeling-school pays off:

"Remember your feelings?

How you learned all the names?

Theo has all the feelings...

and only ONE word.

Crying is his whole language.

Hungry-cry. Tired-cry.

Hold-me-cry.

He'll learn the other words...

YOU did, but for now..."

For now: WAAAH.

Olivia considers her brother

with new professional respect.

One word. For everything.

"...Dat's hard," she rules.

It is hard, little one.

Being brand-new is the hardest

job in the house.

Somebody should teach him

some signs.

(Somebody will.

Guess who. Just guess.)
