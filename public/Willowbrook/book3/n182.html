Night 182

The Little Throne Arrives

Month 7: Potty Power

A new thing in the bathroom.

Small. Low. Her-sized.

A potty.

And here's the whole

opening strategy:

nothing.

No speech. No pressure.

No big deal AT ALL.

It just... appeared.

Furniture.

"What's DAT?"

"Your potty. For when

you're ready. No hurry."

That's it. That's the launch.

She sat on it that night...

fully clothed, socks on...

while her teeth got brushed.

Just trying the chair of it.

"Comfy," she ruled.

Comfy. Good. The end.

(Here's the doctrine,

for every house at this door:

the potty wins by patience.

Pushed potties push back.

The child who owns the timing

owns the skill. The books

are unanimous. The veterans

are unanimous. The pants

will follow the readiness,

never the other way.)

So it sits there now.

Low. Patient. Hers.

Getting normal.

Getting boring.

Boring is the plan.

Boring is always

this family's secret weapon.

The throne can wait.

Thrones are good at waiting.

It's the whole job

of thrones.
