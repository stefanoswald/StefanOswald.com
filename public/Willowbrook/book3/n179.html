Night 179

What He Gave Her Back

Month 6: Big Sister School

All month, all six months...

the counting ran one way:

what she gives HIM.

The fetches. The coaching.

The towers built for knocking.

The songs, the signs,

the gentle, the guard duty.

Tonight, at the lamp,

Mama read the other ledger.

"Want to hear what

Theo gives YOU?"

...There's a ledger?

There's a ledger.

"He gave you the first smile

he ever made.

The first laugh.

The first crawl, aimed

at you, past all the toys.

He lights up biggest

for your face. Yours.

He settles fastest

to your voice.

He made you a teacher.

A nurse. A bodyguard.

A translator. An artist

with a gallery.

He made you somebody's

whole sky, little one...

the way I'm yours.

Do you know how big

that is to be given?"

She was quiet a while,

taking the ledger in.

"...He gives good stuff,"

she said finally.

He gives the best stuff.

That's the secret of the

whole big-sibling deal,

the one the badge

doesn't say:

you think you're the giver.

You find out...

some lamp-lit night,

months in...

you were getting

the whole time.

Both ledgers full.

Both still filling.

That's family.

That's the entire math

of it.
