Night 305

The Muscle Nobody Sees

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

A new idea at breakfast:

"You know you have

muscles, right?"

(She knows. She flexes.

The cushion-carrying

muscles. The trike

muscles. Displayed

on request.)

"There's one more.

The biggest one.

Nobody can see it."

"...Where?"

She checks her arms.

Her legs. Behind her

knees. (A reasonable

place for a hidden

muscle.)

Mama taps her chest.

"Here. The kindness

muscle.

And here's the thing

about it:

it grows exactly like

the others.

Not by having it.

By USING it.

Every kind thing you

do, every share,

every gentle, every

help, one rep.

The muscle gets

stronger.

And people with strong

ones can lift things

nobody else can lift:

bad days. Sad friends.

Whole rooms, sometimes."

She sat with that,

mid-toast.

"...I been exercising

it ALREADY."

(The sorry-flowers.

The loaned Duck. The

courier kiss, nightly.

The spare duck-pants,

offered. She has,

in fact, been training

for two books.)

You have, little one.

This month, we train

on purpose.

Kindness reps. Daily.

Watch what gets

liftable by the end.

The muscle flexed...

somewhere invisible,

chest-adjacent...

and reached for

the jam,

and passed it

without being asked.

Rep one.

The month is open.
