Night 57

The Rainy Day Boss

Month 2: Me Do It

Rain. All-day rain.

Plans: cancelled.

So Mama tried something new.

"Okay, boss. YOUR day.

What do we do?"

Boss?

BOSS.

Olivia's eyes went wide

with the weight of office.

The agenda, as dictated:

"Fort."

Fort: built. (She directed.

Cushion placement: hers.)

"Pots."

The orchestra: convened.

(Two encores. Boss's orders.)

"Puddle look."

Twenty minutes at the window

watching the lane's puddles fill.

(Excellent programming, honestly.)

"Nack."

Snack: served. In the fort.

(Boss eats in the fort.

Policy.)

"Baba story."

Story: read. To the bear. By her.

Her whole day. Her whole plan.

Run start to finish

by a two-year-old,

and it was, write it down...

a genuinely great day.

Kids know what they need.

Mostly it's forts, pots,

windows, snacks, and being

the one who decided.

Give them the wheel sometimes.

The route surprises you.

The destination never does:

it's always contentment,

by the scenic road.
