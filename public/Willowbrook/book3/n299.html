Night 299

The Second Sorry

Month 10: Try Again

A Thursday quarrel.

(They happen. The book

tells true.)

The crime: Olivia

laughed when Lily's

braid-bread fell.

(Not mean-laughed.

Surprise-laughed. But

laughs don't come with

labels, and Lily's

face went down like

the bread.)

The first sorry:

quick. Automatic.

"Sorry, Wiwi."...

already half-turned

back to playing.

And Lily... didn't

take it.

Shoulder up. Face away.

The sorry, bounced.

Here's the month's

last lesson, and it's

a hard one:

sometimes sorry needs

a try-again too.

The first one was

a word.

Words are the cheap

draft.

She thought about it...

you could watch her

think, over by the

window seat, the whole

machinery working...

and came back with

the second sorry.

The expensive one:

"Wiwi. I laughed at

your bread and your

face got sad and

I saw it and I still

laughed a little more.

Dat wasn't kind.

Your bread was lumpy

like MY bread.

Lumpy braids bake

up da same.

Rosa said.

...Wanna share mine?"

The whole thing.

Named, owned, mended,

offered.

Lily's shoulder came

down.

The bread was shared.

The braids, both

lumpy, both golden...

were compared and

ruled twins.

First sorries are

drafts, little one.

When one bounces,

don't crumple it...

and don't crumple

YOURSELF.

Try again, deeper.

We don't crumple

teachers.

Even when the teacher

is your own

first miss.
