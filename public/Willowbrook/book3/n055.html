Night 55

The No-Thank-You Try

Month 2: Me Do It

Independence isn't just doing.

Sometimes it's declining.

And there's a big-girl way.

Tonight's plate: the usuals,

plus mushrooms. (New. Suspicious.

Frankly weird-looking. Fair.)

The old way, the one-year-old way:

plate pushed, food flung,

"NO!" at volume.

The new way, taught this week:

"No thank you."

Olivia looks at the mushrooms.

The old machinery revs...

then...

"No fank you."

No fank you!

The mushrooms are declined.

Politely. Officially. HERSELF.

And here's the deal that makes it work:

no-thank-you gets HONORED.

No coaxing after. No siege.

The mushrooms retire without drama.

(They'll audition again.

Ballot two of fifteen.

The gallery is patient.)

Declining is a life skill.

The girl who can say

no thank you at two...

clear, calm, honored...

is building the voice that says it

at ten, at fifteen, at twenty,

when the stakes aren't mushrooms.

Teach it early.

Honor it always.

No fank you.

Noted, sweetheart. Filed. Respected.
