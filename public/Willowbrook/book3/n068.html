Night 68

Sharing Is Hard (A True Story)

Month 3: A Friend Called Lily

Let's tell the truth

about sharing:

it's HARD.

Today's case: the wagon handle.

One handle. Two pullers.

Olivia's wagon. Lily's visit.

Both grips: locked.

"MINE."

"ME TURN!"

The pull. The counter-pull.

The rising storm systems, twin.

Here's what the books say,

and what this house does:

Nobody yanks the handle away.

(Grabbed-from teaches grabbing.)

Instead: the words.

"Two girls. One handle.

What do we do?"

The girls glare. Think. Glare.

And then Lily, eleven days

of extra wisdom, says:

"You pull ME. Den I pull YOU."

...

Turns.

She invented turns.

(Reinvented. Everyone reinvents

turns. It's the wheel

of friendship.)

Olivia pulls Lily: one lap.

Lily pulls Olivia: one lap.

It WORKS.

It's even FUN, the pulling

half is fun too?!

Sharing is hard.

It stays hard for years.

But turns are the door through.

Found today. By them.

That's the only way

it ever really sticks.
