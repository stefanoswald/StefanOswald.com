Night 246

One, Two, FREE. Theo Walks

Month 9: Counting to Five

It was a counting month.

Of course it happened

in counts.

The living room. Morning.

Theo at the couch's end,

standing free, his

three-second trick,

stretched all week toward

something more...

and across the open floor,

kneeling, arms wide,

in the exact spot where

her own first steps once

landed:

his sister.

"Come here, Fee-oh.

Come to Livy."

(The same words. The

same floor. The family's

oldest choreography,

running its second

performance.)

And Theo let go of

the couch...

and stepped.

"ONE!"

Wobble. Recover. Step.

"TWO!"

The lean, the momentum,

the last brave lurch...

"FREE!!"

into her arms.

Caught. Swung. Screamed

over. The house arriving

at a dead run to find

them both on the floor,

laughing, the walker

and the catcher,

the counted and the

counter.

THEO WALKS.

First steps: three.

First destination: Livy.

(The crawl chose her.

The steps chose her.

The pattern is not

subtle. It is also

not breakable. Ask

any far page.)

"HE WALKED TO ME!

I COUNTED DEM!

ONE TWO FREE!"

One, two, free.

The almanac took the

entry in three hands...

two big, one jubilant

scribble (a duck,

walking).

The farm was called.

("Well NOW", the

register, fully deployed.)

The running-after era:

officially open.

She's been ready

her whole life.

Now, finally, gloriously...

so is he.
