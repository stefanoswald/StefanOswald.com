Night 105

The Practice Visit

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

Field trip today.

The bench recruit's family

has a new cousin visiting...

a REAL baby. Two months old.

Research opportunity!

The observations, logged:

Babies are SMALL.

(Smaller than expected.

Smaller than the bear.

"Was I dat small?" Smaller.

"NO." Yes. There are photos.)

Babies are LOUD.

(A demonstration occurred.

Impressive volume from

such small equipment.

"Da baby is MAD?"

Hungry, probably.

Same weather. Milk fixes it.

It did.)

Babies GRIP.

(One finger, offered

per the gentle protocols,

was seized and held

with astonishing strength.

She stared at her captured finger.

The baby stared at her.

Something passed between them.

"...It LIKES me."

It liked her.)

Babies sleep ANYWHERE.

(Mid-visit. Mid-noise.

Just, off. Like a lamp.)

Final field report,

delivered at dinner:

"Babies are small and loud

and dey grab you

and den dey sleep.

I can DO dis."

Certified, field-tested,

spring-ready.

The baby cousin has no idea

what excellent training

it just provided.

The finger-grab, though.

That part she keeps

telling everyone about.

It liked her.
