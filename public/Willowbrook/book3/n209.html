Night 209

The Quiet Check-In

Month 7: Potty Power

So much growing this month.

Thrones scaled. Bodies

sovereign. Brothers cruising.

Skills going invisible

the way mastered things do.

And tonight, no reason,

no trigger, no rain...

Mama held out her arms

at the rocking chair:

"Come here a minute,

big girl."

And the big girl...

who does everything

herself now, who defends

recruits and trains dolls

and reviews plumbing

county-wide...

came.

Climbed up. Folded in.

Knees everywhere,

per the era.

And they rocked.

The old sway. The old

creak. No milestone

in sight. No skill

being built.

(Wrong. One skill.

The oldest one:

the coming-back-to-fill-up.

The both-ways tide.

Do everything;

then come be held.

It needs no occasion.

It IS the occasion.)

"You're getting so big,"

said Mama, into the curls.

"I know," said the curls.

"...I'm still your baby

dough."

(Baby THOUGH. But dough

stands. Dough is canon

now. Rosa would approve

the metaphor entirely.)

Still her baby dough.

Still rising. Still held.

Ten minutes of rocking.

The tank: topped.

The month: soft-closed.

Some check-ins keep

the whole machine running.

This is the one.

Schedule nothing.

Hold often.
