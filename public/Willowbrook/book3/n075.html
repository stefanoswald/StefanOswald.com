Night 75

The Band-Aid Ceremony

Month 3: A Friend Called Lily

Thursday. The park. Full speed.

And then, the trip.

The classic. Root, toe, down.

Lily's knee. Scraped.

Real blood. (A little. Enough.)

Real tears. (Lots. Fair.)

Two medicines came running.

Medicine one: Mama's kit.

The clean. The blow-dry.

The band-aid...

applied to a knee held

still-ish by main courage.

Medicine two:

Olivia.

Who sat down next to the patient,

took the whole leg in her lap

like a professional,

and administered:

one kiss. On the band-aid.

("On the BAND-AID. Not the owie.

I know, Wiwi. I careful.")

One pat-pat.

And the verdict:

"All better soon.

You brave, Wiwi.

Scared plus anyway."

(The formula! Deployed!

On a FRIEND! Correctly!)

Lily sniffled. Nodded.

Brave plus anyway. Yes.

Two medicines.

Both required.

The band-aid fixes the knee.

The friend fixes the rest.

The park run resumed in minutes...

one girl limping grandly,

one girl guarding her flank.

The kit walks on two legs now.

It makes house calls.
