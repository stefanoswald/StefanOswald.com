Night 308

A Flower for the Duchess

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

The advanced course

opened Sunday, at

the coop:

kindness to the

DIFFICULT.

Subject: the Duchess.

(The hen of hmph.

The back-turner. The

no-sayer. Beloved,

in the way of difficult

family, and absolutely

allergic to affection.)

"Why is she like dat,

Gampa?"

"Some are, that's all.

Hen or people. Something

ruffled 'em early and

the ruffle stuck.

Question is..."

(rock, rock)

"...do the difficult

ones get less kindness,

or more?"

Hmm.

The campaign began:

one dandelion. Daily.

Pushed through the

fence. No speech. No

demands. Just left.

Day one: ignored.

Day two: ignored WITH

a hmph. (Progress?

Unclear.)

Day three: pecked.

Kept.

Day four: the Duchess

was WAITING at the

fence spot.

(Waiting. The whole

coop noticed. Henrietta

filed a report at

volume.)

Day five: dandelion

accepted, and the

Duchess, before

strutting off, paused.

Turned. And gave

one small, stiff,

unmistakable nod.

A NOD. From the DUCHESS.

"Well NOW," said Grandpa

Joe, at the register.

"Forty years I've kept

that bird's family.

First nod I ever saw."

The finding, for the

month's advanced file:

the difficult ones

don't need less.

They need more...

and slower, and without

being asked to change

first.

The flower keeps

arriving.

The nod, some days,

arrives back.

That's all. That's

enough.

Kindness doesn't

invoice.
