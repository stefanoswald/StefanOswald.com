Night 215

The Anatomy of a Storm

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

After a storm passed,

at the calm after,

Mama asked a scientist's

question:

"What does it feel like?

In there? In the middle

of one?"

And Olivia, the sky

herself, the only expert

on her own weather...

told it:

"My chest gets HOT.

And my ears get big-loud.

And my words go far away...

I can SEE dem but I

can't REACH dem.

And my arms want to

frow fings.

And everyfing is wrong

and I can't say WHICH fing."

(Write that down.

The books try for chapters

to say what she said

in five lines.

The words go far away...

I can see them but

I can't reach them.

That's the whole

neuroscience, in toddler.)

"Thank you for telling me,"

said Mama. "Now I know

what to do when the

storm comes:

not ask you questions...

your words are far away.

Not explain things...

your ears are big-loud.

Just... stay. Be the ground.

Until your words

come back in reach."

"Yes," said the expert.

"Just stay."

Just stay.

The whole manual.

Dictated by its subject.

Followed by its keepers.

Forever.
