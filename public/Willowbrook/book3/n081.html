Night 81

The Laughing Fit at Nothing

Month 3: A Friend Called Lily

It started with a word.

"Pickle."

Nobody knows why.

Lily said "pickle"...

just, in passing, about lunch...

and Olivia laughed.

So Lily laughed.

So Olivia SHRIEKED...

"PICKLE!"...

and they went DOWN.

Both of them. The floor.

Howling.

It's not funny.

Pickle is not funny.

THEY know it's not funny.

That makes it FUNNIER.

Recovery attempted:

deep breaths... calm...

eye contact...

MISTAKE.

Down again. Worse.

"P..." (can't even finish it)

"...ICKLE!"

GONE.

The mamas watch from the kitchen,

infected now themselves...

(laughing at laughing;

it's the most catching thing

alive)...

Fifteen minutes, the fit ran.

Hiccups. Aching cheeks.

One final whispered "...pickle"

at snack, and one final collapse.

Science cannot explain

the two-year-old laughing fit.

Science should stop trying.

Some joy has no cause.

It's just friendship,

boiling over,

needing a word to pour on.

Any word.

Pickle.

(You laughed. Admit it.)
