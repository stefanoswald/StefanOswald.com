Night 200

Two Hundred Nights of Book Three

Month 7: Potty Power

Two hundred nights.

Book three's second stone.

And what a two hundred.

Look at the span of it:

Night one: a girl learning

that feelings have names.

Night two hundred: a girl

who names them for OTHERS...

brothers, friends, chickens...

and weathers her own

like the sky she is.

In between:

a declaration at a shoe

bench. A big bed climbed.

A grocery crash that

became the family's

founding retelling.

A paper chain, torn

link by link to a brother.

A doorway. A voice

he already knew.

A first smile claimed,

a first laugh landed,

a first crawl aimed

across a rug at HER.

A badge. A shelf. A throne.

Two hundred nights,

and the girl at the lamp

tonight, duck underpants,

felt badge somewhere

in the laundry, brother

asleep down the hall

in her old crib...

is not the girl from

night one.

She's the sky now.

She knows it.

She's somebody's whole

weather system herself.

"Two hundred stories?"

she asks, at the number.

Two hundred.

"Read da next one."

The next one, then.

And the next.

The stones keep setting.

The road keeps going.

One hundred sixty-five

nights to the third candle.

The book gives its word:

they're good ones.

The best ones keep

being next.
