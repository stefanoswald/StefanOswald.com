Night 233

Livy

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

Tuesday.

(Of course. You knew.

The family's milestones

have a standing

reservation.)

The morning visit.

The routine of routines:

step stool, rail,

"Hi Fee-oh, it's me..."

And Theo pulled himself

up the rail,

reached both arms

through the bars

toward her,

and said...

clear as a bell,

aimed like an arrow,

two syllables,

the first true word

of his entire life:

"Livy."

.

.

.

The house heard the

silence and came running.

(You run at silences

like that. They're louder

than any crash.)

And found them:

the boy at the rail,

saying it again...

"Livy! Livy!"...

delighted with his new

machine, firing it

at its target...

and the target,

standing on the step

stool, both hands over

her mouth,

crying.

The happy kind.

The kind this book

promised, chapters ago.

The happiest tears in

the whole third book...

that was the promise.

Here they are.

"He said ME," she managed.

"His first word is ME."

His first word is you,

little one.

Not mama. Not dada.

Not, the record notes,

with love, duck.

You.

The voice from outside.

The face at the rail.

The reader, the coach,

the translator, the

first smile's owner,

the storm-shelter builder,

the sorry-flower inventor.

Of course it's you.

It was always going

to be you.

"Livy," said Theo,

one more time,

and held out his arms.

And Livy, wiping her

face, laughing and

crying in the old

both-at-once...

lifted him out of her

own old crib

and held on.

The bets on the fridge

came down that morning.

Nobody won.

Everybody won.

The cookie was split

four ways.
