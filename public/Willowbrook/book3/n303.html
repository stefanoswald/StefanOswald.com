Night 303

The Month of Yet, Counted

Month 10: Try Again

Month's end. The audit.

Format, this month:

BEFORE and AFTER.

Couldn't pedal.

CAN pedal. (Bring-bring.)

Couldn't fly the kite.

FLEW the kite. (The

string hums. The sky

takes things, if you

keep showing up.)

Couldn't do the puzzle.

DID the puzzle. (Then

unmade it, to climb

again. The summit

renewable.)

Couldn't say spaghetti.

Can't say spaghetti...

YET. (Spuh-get-ee.

The summit flagged.

First Friday circled.)

Couldn't whistle. YET.

(Lily's diagram, on file.)

Couldn't float without

the death-grip. Floated

Saturday with only

a REGULAR grip.

(Progress measured

in grips.)

And the unlisted ones,

the biggest ones:

Couldn't lose without

the hot chest.

Now: mostly can.

Couldn't hear "not yet"

without the storm.

Now: says it herself.

Couldn't watch her own

failure without crumpling

it.

Now: keeps the folder.

"Dat's LOTS of afters,"

ruled the audited,

drowsy at the lamp.

Lots-lots.

And every one of them

was a before, once...

a wall, a crash, a

crumpled draft, a

too-hard-today.

The month didn't teach

her to succeed.

The month taught her

what to DO between

failing and succeeding...

which is the only

part a person controls,

and the only part

that's ever really

taught.

Show up. Breathe.

Say yet. Keep the

drafts. Grow at it.

Audited. Approved.

Carried forward:

trying, trying,

trying again.
