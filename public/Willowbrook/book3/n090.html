Night 90

Oliver Owl on Friendship

Month 3: A Friend Called Lily

The willow. The old branch.

The month's closing session.

"You saw the grocery store,"

says Oliver Owl.

(Not a question yet.

He's warming up.)

"I saw," says Stella.

"I see everything on that lane.

Two runners. One crash.

The whole beautiful accident."

"Then here's the question,"

says the owl,

spectacles catching moonlight.

"They could have scolded it all.

The running. The swapping.

Could have made that Tuesday

a story about being bad.

Instead the mother said maybe,

and waited,

and it became the founding story

of a friendship

and a family philosophy.

So: what turns an accident

into a treasure?"

The Moon hums, considering.

Stella answers slowly:

"...The waiting.

The watching for the gold."

"Mmm," agrees the Moon.

"And below," adds Stella,

"they're waiting again.

Watching for new gold.

Spring gold. The little one."

"Then the town should rest,"

says Oliver, settling.

"Growing takes the whole night.

Every night. All winter."

The willow lets down its glow.

The town sleeps, growing.

One girl. One friendship.

One secret spring.

All of it, growing.

Goodnight, Willowbrook.

Grow well.
