Night 280

Ring the Bell

Month 10: Try Again

And then came the

victory lap.

The whole lane. Flat

ground. Solo.

Pedaling, real pedaling,

the circle smooth in

her legs now, day seven

of a mountain Mateo

budgeted a week for...

(he called it. The

faculty knows its

students)...

down Maple Lane,

past every porch,

RINGING THE BELL.

Bring-bring!

(The Hendersons, waving.)

Bring-bring!

(The gold dog, escorting

at fence-line, matching

her speed, the patrol's

first ever mounted unit.)

BRING-BRING!

(Rosa's corner. The

dish towel, waving back.)

And at the home porch:

the family. Assembled.

Applauding.

Theo bouncing on Mama's

hip, pointing, shrieking

the whole review in

one word:

"LIVY!!"

(His word. Still his

whole headline for

everything good.)

She dismounted like

a knight. Kicked the

little kickstand...

missed, kicked it

again. GOT it.

(Even victory laps

have try-agains.

Especially kickstands.)

"Da trike is mine now,"

she declared.

"Da hill taught my legs

and my legs remembered."

The trike is hers now.

Third child it's carried.

Best bell it's ever had.

Somewhere down the lane,

a five-and-a-half-year-old

on a big-kid bike

heard the bell

and grinned.

The wheels flow downhill.

So does the glory.

Bring-bring.
