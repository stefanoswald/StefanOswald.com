Night 188

The Public Bathroom Tour

Month 7: Potty Power

A side effect nobody

warns you about:

the potty-learner must

inspect EVERY bathroom

in Willowbrook.

Every one.

The hobby, in full bloom:

Mid-bakery: "I hafta GO."

(Did she? Somewhat.

Mostly she wanted to see

Rosa's facilities.

Reviewed: "Smells like bread.

Five stars.")

Mid-library: "I hafta GO."

(The library's: "SO quiet.

Even da flush is quiet.

Library flush." Five stars.)

The corner place: inspected.

The market: inspected.

The FARM: inspected...

"Gampa, your potty is OLD."

("That commode is older

than your father," said

Grandpa, with dignity.

Five stars anyway.

Heritage rating.)

Here's the truth under

the comedy:

every "I hafta go" gets

honored. Every time.

Even the tourist ones.

Because the signal is new,

and trusting it is the

whole game, and the kid

whose signals get honored

keeps sending them.

False alarms are just

the system testing itself.

Ride the tour, parents.

Learn where every bathroom

in town lives.

(You'll know them all

by heart within the month.

This knowledge never

leaves you. Veterans

can map any town

by its toilets, decades

later. It's the parent's

secret geography.)

Tour status: ongoing.

Town: fully reviewed.

Bladder: increasingly

trustworthy.

Five stars.
