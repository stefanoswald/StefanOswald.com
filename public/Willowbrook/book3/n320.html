Night 320

The Month's Kindest Thing

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

Month's end approaching.

And at dinner, the vote:

"What was the kindest

thing all month?"

The candidates, nominated

around the table:

The casserole run.

(Strong. Classic.)

The Duchess campaign.

(The nod! The historic

nod!)

The secret rock.

(Revealed at last,

under vote-night rules.

Lily's mama had guessed

anyway. Mamas guess

everything.)

The give-away cups.

June and the shovel.

The chain pit crew.

Theo's cracker.

(A surge of support.

The candidate, present,

campaigned by banging

his spoon.)

And then Papa said:

"I vote for one nobody's

named."

The table waited.

"The bench. With Lily.

The day you sat and

said nothing."

...

"But I didn't DO

anyfing."

"That's why it wins.

Everything else you

did cost you something

you had extra...

a rock, some cups,

some time.

The bench cost you

the hardest thing:

your fixing.

You wanted to fix her

and you couldn't and

you stayed anyway.

Kindness that can't

fix anything and shows

up anyway...

that's the deepest

kind there is.

That's the kind people

remember at funerals

and weddings.

That's the kind that

holds the world up."

The vote: unanimous.

(Including the spoon.)

Winner: sitting close,

saying nothing,

staying.

The muscle's biggest

lift, it turns out,

looks like no lift

at all.

It looks like a bench,

two girls,

one sad,

one there.

Be there, little one.

It's the whole trophy.
