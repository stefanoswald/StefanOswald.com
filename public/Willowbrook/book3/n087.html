Night 87

Asking Mama

Month 3: A Friend Called Lily

Dinner. The feeling check.

Papa: "Excited."

Mama: "Excited." (Glowing at it.)

Olivia's turn.

She puts down her spoon.

She has been carrying

the fort council's theory

for two days.

It's time.

"Mama.

Is da somefing good...

a BABY?"

Forks: down.

Glances: exchanged.

Smiles: breaking open

like morning.

"Yes, my love.

A baby.

A baby is growing

in Mama's tummy right now.

Your baby brother or sister.

Coming in the spring."

A baby.

In the TUMMY.

(She stares at the tummy.

The tummy looks normal.

Babies start small, apparently.

Like the lettuce row.)

"Am I..." hunting the word...

"...da BIG one now?"

"You're the big sister.

The baby's big sister.

The most important job

in the whole house."

Big. Sister.

She tries it on.

It fits like the flipped jacket:

sudden, magic, hers.

"I be GOOD at it," she declares.

You will, little one.

You have no idea how good.

(We do. We've read ahead.

You're wonderful.)
