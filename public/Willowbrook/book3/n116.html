Night 116

Bella Comes Back Again

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

The garden. The morning gold.

Mama on the bench, moon and all.

Olivia at the lettuce row.

And over the fence line,

riding the first warm thermals...

wings.

"BELLA! MAMA! BELLA'S BACK!"

Back. On schedule.

The town's spring signature,

never once late.

She circles the garden, once...

the old rounds, the survey...

and then, before both

their held breaths,

she lands.

Not on the fence post.

Not on the tomato stake.

On the moon.

The bump. The very crest of it.

Wings opening... closing...

slow as breathing,

rainbow over the unborn.

Nobody moves.

The pollen-light does

whatever it is

the town's records

decline to explain.

"She's saying hi to da baby,"

whispers Olivia.

Certain. Correct, probably.

(The town keeps its arrangements.

The butterfly keeps

its appointments.

Some things get blessed

before they arrive.

It's Willowbrook.

It's how the place works.)

Wings up. Gone over the fence,

trailing gold.

"Da baby got BELLA'D,"

Olivia reports at dinner.

Solemnly. Accurately.

The bump: officially blessed.

Spring: officially here.

Any night now.

The whole town is ready.
