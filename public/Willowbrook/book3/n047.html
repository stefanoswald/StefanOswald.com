Night 47

The Slow Errand

Month 2: Me Do It

Today's errand: bread and milk.

Time budget: doubled.

On purpose.

Because today, Olivia does

every part she can.

The little cart at the market:

she pushes it. (Into one shelf.

Once. Barely. Recovered.)

The bread: she chooses.

(The seedy one. Bold. Approved.)

The coins at the counter:

she hands them over,

one... by one...

to the patient grocer.

(He counts them slowly back

into his till, narrating.

Grocers know things.)

The bread rides home

in her arms. The whole way.

Half an hour, this errand took.

It used to take ten minutes.

But look what came home:

bread, milk,

and a girl who shopped...

really shopped...

chose and paid and carried.

Ten minutes buys groceries.

Thirty minutes buys a person.

The market will see her

for the next sixteen years.

Today it met its customer.
