Night 172

Teaching the Dog About the Baby

Month 6: Big Sister School

The gold dog has known

about Theo for months.

(The carrier introductions.

The patrol updated its list.)

But now Theo CRAWLS.

And crawlers reach fences.

So today: the formal summit,

conducted by the one person

in town fluent in both parties.

To the dog:

"Dis is Fee-oh. He's little.

He grabs, he can't help it,

it's his move. I'm teaching him.

Be patient. You was patient

wif me."

(Wag. The dog remembers.

The dog's whole career

is patience at this fence.)

To the brother:

"Dis is da gold dog.

He's OLD and he's NICE.

We don't grab. We do dis..."

(the flat hand, demonstrated;

his fist, gently unfolded,

guided flat)

"...gentle. Like da worm.

Like da chick.

Like everyfing."

The nose came through.

The flat small hand held.

Sniff.

The tail swung for two...

the way it once swung

for one, for another

crawler, another summer.

Gentle: four creatures deep now.

Taught by the girl

who learned it at a worm,

to a boy who'll teach it

someday to somebody else's

small grabbing hands.

The fence has seen

the whole curriculum twice.

The fence approves.

The dog files the second kid

under: also mine.

The patrol continues.
