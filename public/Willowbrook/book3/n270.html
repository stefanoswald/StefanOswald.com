Night 270

The Number Hunt

Month 9: Counting to Five

New walk game: THE

NUMBER HUNT.

Because here's this

week's revelation:

numbers aren't just

IN hands and crackers.

Numbers are WRITTEN.

On the town. Everywhere.

Hiding in plain sight.

The hunt, as conducted:

The bakery door: "FIVE!"

(House number 5. Rosa's.

"Of course we're five,"

said Rosa. "Best number.

A whole hand of bakery.")

The Hendersons' porch:

"FREE!" (A brass 3.

Polished. Proud.)

The bus stop sign:

"TWO!"

The market's OPEN sign:

hours with numbers in

them, a whole FIELD

of numbers, too many,

gloriously unpicked.

Her own house: "SEVEN!"

(Number 7, Maple Lane.

Her address. HER number,

learned today, patted

like the mailbox.

"I LIVE at seven."

You live at seven.

Remember it forever.

She did. Everyone does.

First addresses are

tattoos.)

The whole lane: a museum

of digits, curated by

whoever nailed them up,

visited now by its

newest reader.

The numbers were always

there.

Reading is just the

world coming into

focus, click, click,

click, one brass

digit at a time.

Letters next, little

hunter.

(Next book. The

caterpillar paces

these things.)

For now: the town

counts itself for her,

door by door,

all the way home

to seven.
