Night 134

The Aunts Arrive for Theo

Month 5: Theo Arrives

Two weeks in, as booked:

the northern car. The aunts.

The guest room's standing air,

filled again.

And the introduction, run

by its usual officer:

"NAN-NANS! DIS IS FEE-OH.

Wash hands first. Da rule."

(The rule. Enforced on aunts.

The aunts, delighted, comply.

They raised the rule-maker's

father's generation.

They know good governance

when it's two feet tall.)

The bundle, received.

The needlework, deployed:

a knit hat, star on it.

Fitted on the spot. Perfect.

(They measured by wire.

The aunts always know sizes.

It's their whole magic.)

And for the big sister...

because aunts NEVER come

one-handed...

her own parcel:

a knit doll. Girl-shaped.

Dark curls. Red bow. Yarn.

"So you have a small one

of your own, while Mama

has hers."

OH.

A baby of her OWN.

The doll is named instantly:

"Duck."

(Of course she is.

The shortlist finally cleared.)

Duck the doll joins the staff...

diapered nightly, taught signs,

read the bear book,

raised exactly as its owner

is being raised: thoroughly.

The aunts stay a week.

Casseroles: reinforced.

Babies: held in shifts.

The house: fuller, warmer,

running on four generations

of knowing how.

Family is the original basket.

It arrives when the baby does.

Every time. North or near.
