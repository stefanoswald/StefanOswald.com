Night 149

Hunting the First Laugh

Month 5: Theo Arrives

The smile is hers. Ratified.

But smiles were only round one.

The REAL prize...

the one from Papa's prophecy,

the laughing part...

is still out there:

the first LAUGH.

And the hunt is ON.

The repertoire, deployed daily:

The puffed-cheek face.

(The smile-getter. Reliable.

But no laugh. Yet.)

The belly kiss.

(Papa's classic. Results:

a squirm, a coo, an almost.

ALMOST.)

The dropped-jaw gasp.

The sock puppet.

(A tough crowd at every age.

The sock persists.)

The peekaboo, version one.

(Suspense achieved!

The reveal: a full-body

wiggle. Adjacent. SO adjacent.)

"He ALMOST did it," she reports,

nightly, at dinner.

"His laugh is IN dere.

I can hear it getting ready."

It is in there, little one.

It's due in the summer weeks...

the books all say so...

and here's what else they say,

though nobody needs to tell you:

first laughs go to the faces

that showed up the most.

The ones that came back

after every almost.

The hunt continues.

The face returns daily.

The laugh is loading.

And when it comes...

(soon; the next month's pages

know exactly when)...

there was never any question

whose face it would choose.

Never any question at all.
