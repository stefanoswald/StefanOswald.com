Night 334

The Watch Votes Too

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

Word of the dinner vote

reached the willow.

(The brook. It carries

everything. Vote results

especially, the water

loves an election.)

And the watch, being

the watch, held its own.

"Nominations," said

Oliver Owl, "for

kindest act observed

from altitude, this

month, town-wide."

Stella went first:

"The girl's, obviously.

The bench. I watched

the whole sit. Eleven

minutes of nothing,

which was everything.

The family voted right."

"The Moon?"

"Mmm." A humming pause.

"Mmm-mm." (The Moon's

nomination, translated:

the grocer's ends.

Years of soft bread,

one old dog. Unannounced,

eternal. The Moon

favors the long

quiet ones. The Moon

IS a long quiet one.)

"And you, old friend?"

asked Stella. "What

did the branch see?"

Oliver Owl was still

a while.

"I saw a hen," he said

at last, "who has said

no to everything for

nine years, say

almost-yes to a

dandelion.

And I ask you...

because asking is my

office...

which is kinder:

the child who gave

flowers to a cold

heart for a week?

Or the cold heart...

who let itself, after

nine years, be

warmed?"

The watch considered.

The willow let down

its gold on the

question, the way

it does on the ones

that don't need

answering.

"Both," hummed the Moon

at last. The family

word. Even the sky

uses it now.

Both.

The vote closed

unanimous, all

categories.

Below, the town slept...

kind, and kinder,

and learning.

Goodnight, muscle-

builders.

Goodnight, Willowbrook.

Both. Always both.
