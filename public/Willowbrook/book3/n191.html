Night 191

The Regression Rain Week

Month 7: Potty Power

Three good weeks.

Then: a rain week.

Three accidents in four days.

After WEEKS of sunshine.

The old house-dividing moment,

again. The doctrine, tested

at its hardest grade:

when it was going SO WELL.

The calm, held. (White-knuckled.

Held.) The dry clothes,

matter-of-fact. The title,

never questioned aloud.

And the detective work,

done quietly on the side:

What changed this week?

Hmm.

Theo started pulling up

on furniture this week.

Theo got a WAVE of attention

this week.

Ah.

There it is.

(The books know this one cold:

potty regressions ride

on change. New sibling

milestones. New rooms.

New anything. The plumbing

is the first thing feelings

borrow when they need

a louder voice.)

The fix wasn't potty-related

at all:

extra just-us time.

Champion-strut revivals.

Her ledger read aloud again.

The sky, per the doctrine:

restored on its own,

inside the week.

Sunshine resumed as if

the rain never was.

Rain weeks come.

They ride on weather

that has nothing to do

with pipes.

Fix the feelings.

The plumbing follows.

It followed.

It always does.
