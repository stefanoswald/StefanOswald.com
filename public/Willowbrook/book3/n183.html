Night 183

Watching Everything

Month 7: Potty Power

Phase one of potty learning

isn't the potty.

It's the RESEARCH.

And the researcher is

THOROUGH.

The bathroom has no privacy

now. (It never really did.

Doors are for other families.)

Every visit: supervised.

Every step: interrogated.

"Where does it GO?"

(Down the pipes. To the

water place. It gets cleaned.)

"Da pipes like da bath pipes?"

(Cousin pipes. Yes.)

"Does EVERYBODY go potty?"

(Everybody.)

"...GRANDPA?"

(Everybody, love.)

"...DA DUCHESS?"

(Chickens have their own

system. Ask Grandpa Sunday.)

(She asked. Sunday. At dinner.

Grandpa answered at length,

professionally, about

chicken systems.

The aunts wept with laughter.

The record preserves it all.)

And the public announcements.

Oh, the public announcements.

The market checkout:

"MY MAMA WENT POTTY TODAY."

(The clerk, deadpan, saintly:

"Wonderful news.")

This is all curriculum.

Every question. Every

mortifying announcement.

The system being studied

before it's joined.

Answer everything, parents.

Flush the embarrassment.

The research phase

means it's working.

The scientist is closing in.
