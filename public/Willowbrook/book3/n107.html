Night 107

Reading to the Bump

Month 4: Waiting for Baby

The librarian said it,

weeks ago, at the desk:

"Big sisters read to babies."

The commission was accepted.

And tonight, it begins.

The reader: Olivia.

The book: the bear one.

(Obviously. Start them

on the classics.)

The audience: the bump.

She settles beside Mama.

Opens the soft-worn covers.

And reads, her way,

which is the true way:

"Da bear. He lost his button.

He look and look.

SO sad."

(She shows the bump the picture.

Holds it right up close.

"See, baby? Dat's da bear.")

"Den. DERE'S da button!

He finded it!

Happy!"

(Picture: shown. Pause: held

for the gasp the baby

cannot yet supply.

She supplies it. "GASP.")

"Da end. Nigh-nigh, bear."

The bump, mid-story: THUMP.

"MAMA. DA BABY CLAPPED."

(It kicked. But you know what?

Let the record show: it clapped.)

Every night now, one story

to the bump. The classics,

read in her voice...

the voice the baby is filing,

the voice it will know

on day one.

First librarian: the round building.

Second librarian: her.

The books flow downhill,

lap to lap to lap,

forever.
