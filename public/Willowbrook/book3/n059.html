Night 59

The Moon Does It Herself

Month 2: Me Do It

From the big bed...

her bed, climbed by her...

Olivia can see the window.

And in the window tonight:

the Moon. Rising.

"Mama. Who puts Moon up?"

What a question.

Who lifts the Moon?

Nobody, little one.

Look at her go.

No hands. No help.

No boost from the hills.

She climbs the whole sky

herself,

every single night,

and has for a very long time.

"Moon do it self?"

Moon does it herself.

The biggest me-do-it there is.

And the stars come out alone.

And the sun gets up alone.

(Early. Nobody makes it.)

The whole sky

runs on me-do-it...

and has never once

missed a night.

"Like ME," says Olivia.

Sinking into the pillow.

Like you.

Exactly like you.

Climb on, little moon.

Rise daily. Shine on your own.

The sky's been doing it forever.

You're in excellent company.

Goodnight, self-risen girl.

Goodnight, Willowbrook.
