Night 247

Counting Everything

Month 9: Counting to Five

The fever has struck.

The counting fever.

(Every child catches it

the month the numbers

click. The town knows

the symptoms well.)

EVERYTHING gets counted:

The stairs: "One two

free four five. MORE

stairs, Mama, da stairs

go past my hand!"

(They do. Twelve of them.

The sequel-hand can't

cover it either. The

mystery deepens

deliciously.)

The crackers at snack:

counted before eating.

Recounted after each

bite. ("Four now.

...Free now.")

The buttons on Papa's

shirt: counted while

he wears it. He stands

still for the census.

Papas do.

The porch lights at

dusk: "One... two...

free..." the lane's

whole evening liturgy,

numbered.

The gold dog's legs:

"FOUR!" (Correct. The

dog was patient about

the audit.)

The Duchess's legs:

"TWO!" (Also correct.

The Duchess was not

patient. The Duchess

is never patient.)

Her own count of

everything countable

in one day, per the

almanac's estimate:

several hundred.

The world, it turns

out, is FULL of

amounts.

It was full of them

all along...

just waiting for

somebody to notice,

one, two, free at

a time.
