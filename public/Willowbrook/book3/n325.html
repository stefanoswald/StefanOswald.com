Night 325

Rosa's Surprise

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

Rosa gives everyone

everything.

The crust ends. The

celebration buns. The

extra rolls slipped

into tired men's bags.

The first cakes and

the flour-dusted hugs

and the whole warm

engine of her.

So this month, the

town conspired.

KINDNESS. BACK. AT ROSA.

The plot (hatched at

the bench, executed

lane-wide):

One Sunday: her ovens,

covered. The baking

handled, badly but

lovingly, by a

committee of neighbors

who'd been secretly

taking notes for weeks.

Her chair: carried

into the square's

best sun.

Her coffee: made.

Her feet: ordered UP.

And a parade of

everyone she's ever

fed, coming by all

morning with the town's

whole gratitude:

flowers, drawings

(one duck, naturally,

from the gallery's

leading artist),

and, the committee's

lumpy triumph...

a braided loaf.

Made FOR the baker.

Lumpy as braid soil.

Rosa took one look

at the lumpy loaf

and did the dish-towel

thing for a full

minute.

"Lumpy braids," she

finally managed,

"bake up the same."

They do.

The town proved it.

The kindness engine

got its kindness back...

which is the only

maintenance engines

like that ever need.

"Even givers need

getting," Mama explained

on the walk home.

Even givers need

getting.

Check on your Rosas,

every town.

Cover their ovens

sometimes.

Carry their chairs

into the sun.
