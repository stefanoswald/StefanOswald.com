Night 164

The High Shelf

Month 6: Big Sister School

Theo reaches everything now.

Sitting-reach. Rug-level reach.

And rug level is where

her things LIVE.

So Papa built the answer:

a shelf.

HER shelf.

Just above baby-reach.

Just at Olivia-reach.

The precious things move up:

Baba. (Obviously. Ancient law.)

The best tower blocks.

The knit doll's tiny brush.

Three books she's not

ready to share.

The sparkly rock. THE rock.

Everything else, the rug

country, the bins, the soft

pile, goes shareable.

Two countries. One border.

Peace, by architecture.

"Up here is MINE.

Down dere is OURS."

Exactly right.

(This is the whole secret

of sharing at every age:

it works best when

some things don't have to be.

A protected mine

makes a generous ours.

Grown-ups relearn this

their whole lives.

She got it at two,

with one shelf

and a papa with a drill.)

The shelf holds for years.

The rock stays sparkly.

The bear surveys the rug

from safe altitude,

one ear flopped,

mildly smug.
