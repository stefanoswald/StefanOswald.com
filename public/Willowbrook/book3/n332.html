Night 332

The Duchess Brings a Feather

Month 11: Kindness Is a Muscle

The last Sunday of

the kindness month.

The fence. The dandelion

spot. The daily flower,

delivered per the

campaign, weeks of

it now, asked-for-

nothing, string-free.

And this Sunday,

at the spot,

BEFORE the flower

went down...

something was waiting.

A feather.

One feather. Speckled.

Brown-gold-cream, with

the green-black shimmer...

the fancy kind, the

Sunday-best kind,

from the wing of

exactly one bird

in that coop.

Placed, not dropped;

PLACED, at the

dandelion spot.

"GAMPA."

He came. He looked.

He took off his hat...

actually took off

his hat...

"Well. NOW," he said,

at a register the

almanac has no

notation for.

"Forty years.

That bird has never

given anything to

anybody but grief

and eggs.

That's a GIFT, that is.

That's the Duchess

saying it back."

Saying it back.

The difficult heart,

flowered at daily

for a month,

had cracked all the

way open...

one feather's worth...

and given.

The feather went home

in careful hands.

It lives on the high

shelf. Soul-grade.

Next to where the

sparkly rock used

to be.

(The economy, noted:

one treasure given

secretly, one treasure

received impossibly.

The books balance

in the strangest,

most reliable ways.)

The dandelion still

went down, of course.

The campaign doesn't

end at success.

That's what makes

it kindness

and not a trade.

Hmph, said the Duchess,

from her roost.

Softly.

It meant thank you.

Everyone knew.

The whole coop knew.
