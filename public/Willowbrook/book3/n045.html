Night 45

Making Her Own Sandwich

Month 2: Me Do It

Today, lunch gets a promotion.

"Want to MAKE it yourself?"

...The whole face answers.

The station, assembled:

two bread slices, waiting flat.

The soft spread, lid off.

The little spreader. (Dull. Perfect.)

Banana coins, pre-sliced.

Begin.

Spread the spread.

(Technique: bold. Coverage: partial.

Some bread. Some table. Some elbow.)

Now the banana coins.

Placed. One... by one...

in a pattern known only to her.

Now the top slice, the flip...

the press...

A SANDWICH.

Made by Olivia. Entirely.

(Entirely-ish. The station counts

as geology, not help.)

She eats it with both hands.

The whole thing.

Even the crust.

(The crust of a ME sandwich

tastes different. Better.

This is documented science.)

"Me made it."

You made it.

Chef of one, table of one,

satisfaction of a whole restaurant.
