Night 243

The Willow in the Wind

Month 8: Tantrum Weather

The month's last night

brought real weather...

sky weather. A windstorm,

riding through Willowbrook

after midnight, loud

in the lanes.

And the watch kept

station through it,

and watched the willow.

The great willow, in

a great wind, does

the thing willows are

built for:

it bends.

The long branches stream

sideways. The trunk

leans and gives. The

whole gold crown of it

lets the storm pour

THROUGH...

and when the wind

passes...

whole. Unbroken.

Upright by morning.

Not one branch lost.

"You see it?" asked

Oliver Owl, feathers

ruffled flat, spectacles

somehow immaculate.

"The oak two hills over

lost an arm tonight.

Oaks argue with wind.

The willow never argues.

The willow bends,

and keeps its roots,

and stands back up.

Now..."

(the question; his

office; even at 2 a.m.

in a gale)

"...which one did the

family below learn

to be, this month?"

Stella, holding station

in the streaming dark,

watched the little house

on Maple Lane, shutters

snug, windows dark,

four sleepers riding

out the noise in the

deep unbothered sleep

of the weather-proofed...

"The willow," she said.

"They bend. They root.

They stand back up.

Every storm this month.

Every single one."

"Mmm," hummed the Moon,

through the wind...

which is Moon for

then name the town

well, old friends.

It was always named

for exactly this.

By morning: calm.

The willow: upright.

The town: unbroken.

One small girl woke

and pointed at the

washed blue sky:

"Da storm went froo.

Da sky stayed."

The storm went through.

The sky stayed.

Goodnight, good morning...

Willowbrook.

Bend well. Root deep.

Stand back up.

Always.
