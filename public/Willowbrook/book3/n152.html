Night 152

The First Laugh Lands

Month 6: Big Sister School

An ordinary Tuesday morning.

(Milestones love Tuesdays.

This family's books prove it.)

The mat. The routine visit.

The face, the puffed-cheek one,

deployed for the hundredth time,

faithful as sunrise...

and Theo...

four months old this week,

loaded, ready, due...

LAUGHED.

Not the almost.

Not the wiggle.

The REAL one.

Rolled up from the belly,

bubbling, unmistakable,

bigger than he is...

"MAMA! PAPA!

IT CAME OUT!

DA LAUGH CAME OUT!

IT PICKED ME!"

They came at a run.

(You always come at a run.)

And she did the face again...

and he laughed AGAIN...

and then SHE laughed,

and then everyone was laughing,

the whole kitchen, the whole

morning, the whole house

running on one baby's

brand-new joy...

exactly the way it once ran

on hers, in this same room,

in another book.

The prophecy, fulfilled:

First smile: hers.

First laugh: HERS.

The laughing part: delivered,

as foretold, on the porch steps,

by a papa who knew.

"He's my whole brudder,"

she declared, breathless.

He was before.

But laughs are contracts.

He signed in giggles.

Witnessed by everyone.

Notarized by the morning sun.

The hunt: complete.

The face: retired undefeated.

(Not retired. Never retired.

Performed daily for years.

Some hits you keep touring.)
