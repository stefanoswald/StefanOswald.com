Night 157

Teaching Him Peekaboo

Month 6: Big Sister School

Today's lesson at the school:

the great game itself.

PEEKABOO.

(She authored it at eight

months, took the cloth,

ran the theater, made the

timing hers. Now: professor.)

The lesson, delivered:

"Fee-oh. Watch.

I go away..."

(Cloth up. Gone.)

"...but I NOT away.

I always come back.

Dat's da whole game.

Dat's da whole EVERYFING."

(Cloth down.)

"PEEKABOO!"

The jump. The stare.

The brand-new laugh.

AGAIN.

Cloth up... the suspense...

(she holds it, the pause,

the authored pause,

her signature move)...

"PEEKABOO!"

LAUGHING. Full laughing.

Ten rounds. Twenty.

And somewhere in the twenties,

the graduate move:

Theo's own hands come up...

swat the cloth...

PULL IT DOWN HIMSELF.

"MAMA! HE PLAYED IT!

HE DID DA PULL!"

He played it.

First game he ever

played BACK.

And his first opponent,

first teacher, first partner

in the oldest game there is:

her.

The game passes down

like the song does.

Player to player.

Gone-then-back,

taught by somebody

who always comes back.

That's the lesson under

the lesson.

He'll know it in his bones

before he knows his name.

Professor's note, filed

at dinner:

"He's smart. Like me."

Like you.

Exactly, wonderfully,

like you.
