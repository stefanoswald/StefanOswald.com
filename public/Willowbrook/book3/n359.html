Night 359

The Party of Three Candles

Month 12: Almost Three

Noon. They came.

All of them. The whole

list. The whole year:

Lily, first through

the door, per always,

the running hug, per

always. June behind

her, first party in

the new town, welcomed

by name at the door

by the birthday girl

herself ("JUNE! You

came to my birfday!

Dis is everybody!

Everybody, dis is

JUNE!" the circle,

opened wide, held.)

Mateo, with the pit

crew's gift: a bike

bell for the trike.

("Upgrade. You earned

it.") Grandpa Joe,

hat in hand, mystery

sheeted in the garage.

The aunts, needles

finally at rest.

Rosa, guarding her

cloth-covered

masterpiece. The

plant-house teacher.

The recruit. The

whole warm crowd

of everyone.

The program:

SPAGHETTI. At a

birthday. Served to

universal delight...

noodles slurped in

a nine-person chorus,

sauce achieving

eyebrows across

three generations.

("Best birthday menu

in thirty years of

catering," ruled Rosa.

"She gets it from

the founding story.")

Hide and seek...

counted by the

birthday girl, per

her request: her

party, her count,

"ONE TWO FREE FOUR

FIVE. READY OR NOT..."

(Everyone hid.

Grandpa Joe hid

badly on purpose,

straw hat visible

above the couch,

and was found to

shrieking triumph.)

And the gift moment

approaching, the

garage door, the

sheet, the wheels...

but first:

the lights dimmed.

The cloth came off

the cake.

And the room went

quiet the way rooms

do...

you know the way...

when the candles

come in.
