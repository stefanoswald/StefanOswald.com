Night 302

Theo's First Yet

Month 10: Try Again

The rug. The blocks.

And Theo, twenty months

now, walking, talking

his six words, building

his first REAL towers...

hit his first REAL wall:

Block three.

Two blocks: he can stack.

Daily. Proudly.

Block three: falls.

Every time. The wobble,

the tip, the collapse.

Again: collapse.

Again: COLLAPSE.

And the small hot chest...

the family weather,

arriving in its second

generation, the fists,

the "AH!", the block

flung...

And before Mama could

move, the doctrine's

whole living archive

was already kneeling

at rug level:

"Fee-oh. Hey. Look at me."

(Watch my face, not

da floor.)

"You can't do free

blocks..."

(the pause; the word;

the inheritance:)

"...YET.

Yet means it's COMING.

I couldn't do free

blocks too. Den my

hands grew at it.

Yours are growing

RIGHT NOW. I can

see dem growing."

And she rebuilt the

two-stack, and guided

his fist to hover...

"slow... slow... DERE"...

and block three,

steadied by four hands,

held.

"YOU DID IT WIF HELP.

Next time: wif LESS

help. Den: no help.

Dat's how it goes.

Dat's how everything

goes."

That's how everything

goes.

The whole month...

the whole BOOK, the

whole method of this

family, delivered

downhill in one rug-

level seminar by a

teacher who's been

two for almost a

whole year now.

"Yet," said Theo.

Word seven.

The most important

one he'll ever own.

Planted by the person

who owns it deepest.

The doctrine is

generational now.

It never stops being

generational.

That was always

the plan.
