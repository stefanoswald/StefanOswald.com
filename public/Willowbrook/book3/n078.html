Night 78

Rosa's Kitchen Rules

Month 3: A Friend Called Lily

Thursday at Lily's house

means one thing above all:

ROSA'S KITCHEN.

The home one. The famous one.

Where the bakery magic sleeps

on its days off.

Kitchen rules, posted by Rosa

(out loud, warm and strict):

"Aprons on. Hands washed.

Nobody touches the oven

but me. Everybody tastes."

EVERYBODY TASTES.

(The best rule in any kitchen

in the entire town.)

Today's project: bread day.

The girls get dough scraps...

real dough, their own lumps.

Knead it! Punch it!

(Punching is BAKING here.

Rosa says so. "Dough likes

a strong argument.")

Roll it. Shape it.

Olivia's loaf: a ball. Sort of.

Lily's loaf: a snake. Committed.

Onto the tray, by the real loaves.

Into the oven. (Rosa's hands only.)

And out: two tiny breads,

golden, lopsided, THEIRS.

Eaten warm. Butter melting.

On the back step. Together.

"Good bakers," rules Rosa.

The highest rank she gives.

The kitchen smells like rising.

So does the whole friendship.

So does everything, at Rosa's.
