Night 139

The Tummy Time Coach

Month 5: Theo Arrives

Tummy time. Theo's gym hours.

He objects. Formally.

(They all object formally.

It's in the rules of the gym.)

And down onto the mat,

face to face at his level,

goes the coach.

"I know, buddy. Da gym is hard.

But you gotta build your neck.

Da neck holds da head.

Da head steers EVERYFING."

(The curriculum. Verbatim.

Two years of it, poured in,

pouring out.)

"Look at me. Livy's here.

Lift up. Just a little.

Little counts."

And Theo, furious,

determined, week-six strong...

lifts.

One wobbling inch.

"DAT'S IT! DAT'S DA INCH!

MAMA! HE DID DA INCH!"

(The first inch of everything.

Book one said so.

The coach didn't read book one.

The coach LIVED book one.

It's better sourcing.)

He drops. He complains.

The session ends per policy...

scooped, praised, done.

"Good gym, Fee-oh.

Tomorrow: MORE inch."

The coaching staff of this house

now runs two generations deep.

Everything she was given...

the face at mat level,

the little-counts,

the scooped-up ending...

delivered onward, entire.

The inch economy compounds.

He'll roll by summer,

crawl by fall, run by the

far pages, and at every

milestone, somewhere at

mat level: the coach.

First hire. Never replaced.
