Night 63

Room for Difference

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

Olivia looked around the park at the friends gathered near the fort.

Wren noticed maps and quiet corners. Noor knew what it meant to cross from one country into another. Dana remembered every rule. Mateo could turn broken wheels into working ones. Lily understood ovens, patience, and the exact moment bread was ready.

None of them approached a problem the way Olivia did.

"That is why the group works," she told Lily. "If everyone thought like me, we would solve only the problems I understand."

She remembered how often children were sorted by sameness, same games, same clothes, same loudness. But her strongest friendships had never been mirrors.

In the notebook she added: A FRIEND DOES NOT HAVE TO BE LIKE YOU. A FRIEND MAKES ROOM FOR WHO YOU ARE.

Lily read it. "Including when you make definitions about everyone?"

"Especially then. You can tell me when I get one wrong."

"Good," Lily said. "That should probably be another rule."

Olivia left space beneath the sentence.
