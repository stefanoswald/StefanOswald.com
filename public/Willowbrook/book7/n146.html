Night 146

The Ordinary Miracle Count

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

This month's family exercise, run at dinner: The ordinary miracle count. (Each member: your most miraculous ordinary. The rules: it must be daily; it must be free; it must have been saved under furniture:) Papa's: "The coffee smell reaching the bedroom before the alarm. Every morning, thirty years, two different houses.

The smell commutes. Nobody carries it. It just... comes for me." Mama's: "The sound of all three of you breathing in one house at 2 a.m. I do rounds sometimes. Don't tell." (Everyone knew. Everyone pretended not to.

The pretending is part of the round.) Theo's: "The socks. They go in the wash angry and come out warm. The pattern forgives them. Every time. The laundry is the house's do-over machine." And hers: "The lamp clicking on.

The exact click. Seven years times free-hundred-sixty-five, that's twenty-five hundred clicks, minimum, and every single one meant: The day's held. You're kept. Stories now. The click is the whole shelf in one sound.

When I'm old..." The table stilled; she said it anyway; this month says things. "when I'm old, somebody could play me that click and I'd be six again, instant. The click is a time machine. Everybody's got one sound like that. Find yours, everybody. That's the count's whole assignment."
