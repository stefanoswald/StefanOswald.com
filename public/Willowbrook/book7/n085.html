Night 85

The Friend at the End of the Route

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

This month checked the route's quietest us: The elder alvarez. (The lane's end; the freshest half-dozen, per route logic; the soup-jar family's matriarch; the widow whose Saturdays begin when the wheelbarrow squeaks her gate.) What grew around the eggs, inventoried: the visit (five minutes at first; forty now; the route reschedules around it). The bean-spacing lore.

The stories (Cuba; the crossing; the recipes her mother carried, the second fat notebook has a whole Alvarez section now; the collecting expanded past parents years ago). And the reciprocals: the soup jars, returned filled. The Spanish, taught porch-wise ("the third language of the web now. Portuguese from the professora, Polish words from Hollis, Spanish from the route; the paper grows every direction").

("The route was eggs, Mama. The eggs were the ticket. Danny's law, from the snack-dog case, years ago: the snack is the ticket; the company is the kindness. Every route in this town is secretly a friendship wearing a delivery costume.

The mail, the bread van, the paper kid. Check the drivers' faces at the doors. The town knew before the pattern did. The town always knows first.")
