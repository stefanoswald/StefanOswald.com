Night 121

The Unanswerables Reopened

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

At the start of the month, Olivia reread the questions no one could answer. "Five years of open windows, Mama. This month's job: not closing them, the windows stay open; the rule's the rule. This month's job is visiting them.

The big questions deserve visits. They get lonely standing open with nobody looking through." The list of windows: "Where does Tuesday go when it's over. Where does the flame go. Who did the Duchess love..."

A pause; the window, visited; the answer, arrived since: a sister, three years gone, and then, at the end, a boot. "That one closed itself. They do that sometimes. The rest: What am I for.

Where do I go to look at yesterday. Who pours the gear-zero foundations. Who asked the first question. And the new ones, this year's crop: What's behind the gate. What's the last word. And the biggest window ever cut: Who's been keeping the story of me?" (the story, in its chair, holds very still.)

"Because somebody has. I've felt it for years. This month's gonna visit that window too."
