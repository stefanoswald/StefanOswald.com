Night 324

Olivia and Bobby Bear

Month 11: Bobby Bear's New Kid

Mama realized that after seven years, there was no proper portrait of Olivia with Bobby Bear.

He appeared in hundreds of photographs, at the edge of birthdays, beneath an arm on Christmas morning, half hidden in the car. None showed the quiet partnership itself.

So one evening Mama brought the camera to the nursery.

Olivia sat beneath the reading lamp with Bobby in his usual place. She opened a chapter book and began to read. Mama did not ask either of them to smile.

The photograph caught what the posed pictures had missed: Olivia relaxed against the pillow, one hand resting on Bobby's worn paw, the old bear listening as he had through thousands of ordinary nights.

Mama printed two copies. One went beside the scribble-hands drawing on Olivia's wall.

The second disappeared into the hiding place with Olivia's secret gift for Theo.

"Why two?" Olivia asked, pretending not to know.

"Some journeys need a picture of where they began," Mama said.

That night the framed photograph watched over the same scene still happening beneath it.
