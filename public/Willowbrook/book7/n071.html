Night 71

Nobody's Perfect Friend

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

A classmate told Olivia that she could not find a best friend.

"I want someone who is never annoying, always free, likes everything I like, and never chooses anybody else," the girl said.

Later, Olivia repeated the list to Lily.

"That does not sound like a person," Lily said.

"It sounds like a reflection that follows instructions."

Olivia was careful when she spoke to the girl again. "Friends disappoint each other sometimes. They need other friends. They disagree. The important part is whether you can be honest, make room, and repair what gets hurt."

The girl frowned. "That sounds harder."

"It is. It is also less lonely."

A week later, she joined Olivia's group at lunch. She and Dana immediately disagreed about the rules of a game. They talked, changed one rule, and kept playing.

No perfect friend appeared. A real one might have begun.
