Night 359

The Last Round of Six

Month 12: The Maybe Year

During her final two days as a six-year-old, Olivia visited the places that had held her growing.

She counted eggs at the farm and read with Grandpa Joe on the porch. She sat on the park bench, crossed the permitted hedge gap, visited the school corner, bakery counter, repair shop, garden, and the old story post.

At Mrs. Hollis's house, they read the newest Friday-log entries aloud. At Number Sixteen, Olivia left the eggs and received a full wave from the porch.

She did not announce that these were farewell visits. She was not leaving Willowbrook. Still, each familiar place seemed to answer the same quiet question: yes, you grew here; yes, you may grow farther.

At sunset she stood at the bottom of Willow Hill.

"Two nights," she told the Great Willow. "Please save my place."

The branches moved in a wind she could not feel below.

Olivia walked home through a town that was neither pushing her forward nor asking her to remain six. Every door stood where it always had, ready when she was.
