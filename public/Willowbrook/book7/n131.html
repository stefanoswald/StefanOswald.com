Night 131

The Wonder Walk

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

This month's field exercise: The wonder walk. (The route, walked slow, both kids, one rule: everything ordinary gets asked for its miracle. Everything delivered:) "The sidewalk: Stone that pours. Somebody figured out how to make rock liquid, shape it, and let it re-rock.

We walk on solved impossible. The streetlight: lightning, tamed, in a glass jar on a stick, coming on by itself at dusk, the town's got pet lightning and nobody says thank you. The mail slot: a hole in a door that connects to every other hole in every door on earf. The bread smell: a billion workers breaving.

The dog at Number Five: a wolf, Theo, an actual descended wolf, that decided ten thousand years ago it would rader love us, the wolves voted and the dogs are the yes-votes..." ("The dogs are the yes-votes..." The deputy repeated, undone, filing it forever.) The walk's finding: "The unanswerables aren't rare, Theo.

They're the pavement. The whole town is miracles that got regular, the always-sounds of wonder; saved under furniture. This month's real gift isn't answers. It's the hearing coming back."
