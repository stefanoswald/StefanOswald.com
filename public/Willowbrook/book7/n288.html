Night 288

The Gift of the Rounds Themselves

Month 10: The Gift Month

The meadow came mid-month, and brought this month its deepest reframe, via the rounds' own keeper, met on the gold path (the meadow hosts colleagues now; the guest rules expanded; the year gathers everyone in): "The gift month..." said Harry, leaf bright in the dream-light. "should log the town's oldest gift format, small keeper: The round itself. The egg route. The Fridays.

The first Saturdays. The rounds I've walked thirty years. The gifts inside them, the eggs, the tea, the rolls, those are the wrapping. The round is the gift: the faithful orbit; the being-counted-on; the somebody-small-and-warm-passing-close, on schedule, so the lonely can set their clocks by the caring.

You can't wrap a round. You can only walk one, for years, till the walking's the gift and the gift's the walking and the wrapped things inside are just... the receipts." ("The rounds are the gift; the eggs are the receipts..." She carried back to waking, and the lamp got the whole reframe: "Mama, this month's been making wrapable things.

The unwrapable ones were already given: the Saturdays. The Fridays. The Wednesdays. The whole calendar of orbits. I've been giving for years and calling it a schedule. The best gifts don't get opened. They get kept, appointment by appointment, orbit by orbit, till somebody sets their clock by you. That's the gift. That was always the whole gift.")
