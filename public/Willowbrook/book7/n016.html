Night 16

The Wobbly Fence Post

Month 1: When No One Is Watching

At the end of Saturday's farm work, Olivia noticed a fence post leaning beside the chicken run. Frost had loosened the soil around it.

Grandpa Joe was asleep in the porch chair. The post was not on Olivia's job list, and no one would know if she left it for Monday.

She fetched the tamping bar, wedged stones around the base, and packed the earth until the post stood straight. It took forty minutes.

In the tally book she wrote: NORTHEAST POST RESET. NOBODY ASKED. I SAW IT.

Joe read the line the next morning. He said nothing about it.

The following Saturday, Olivia found the old tamping bar hanging on the shed's wall of honor. A tag in Joe's block letters read: USED BY THE FOURTH HAND ON A JOB NOBODY ASSIGNED.

"You retired a perfectly good tool," Olivia said.

"Tools wear out," Joe replied. "That kind of work deserves remembering."

The post stayed straight through winter.
