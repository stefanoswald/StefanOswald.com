Night 277

The Gift in Maps

Month 10: The Gift Month

Gift three, drawn in the cartographer's tongue: Noor's map. (The bilingual masterwork, the shared bench-project, years of two hands, copied fair at last: inked, colored, both cartographers' work preserved exactly, the dragons and the waves, and titled in the space they'd always left blank: "The town, as found by two keepers. One came by birth. One came by sea.

The map holds both routes. All maps should.") And the copy's one addition, hers, drawn small at the map's edge, in the old country's direction: an arrow, pointing off-map, labeled: "The first country. Still on the paper. The paper just grew. Your law, made permanent."
