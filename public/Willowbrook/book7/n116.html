Night 116

The Almanac Lesson, Transferred

Month 4: Theo's Big Sister

The farm Saturday ran double this week (his request; the Saturdays are his season's frontier too), and the porch table hosted a succession ceremony nobody announced as one: The fifth hand's first entry. (The almanac, opened. The fourth hand, supervising. The fifth, printing slow and sure: "Saturday.

Fed the chickens with wivy. The duchess's granddaughter sat on my boot. T." the story stops; rereads; confirms: The granddaughter. On the boot. The dynasty of boot-sitting, hen edition, continuing on its own schedule, selecting its own people, explaining nothing, by the eternal policy of hens.)

Joe read the entry twice, tapped it once, and issued the farm's whole ritual: "Well." (And then, because the year loosens even the four-sentence men: "Fifth hand. The book's got room. The book's always got room, that's why we bought volume two."

The two grandchildren, one almanac, one porch, one long gold afternoon. They remembered it under the pages it will keep closest, in the drawer where the true weights live, beside the boot and the letters and everything this family ever handed carefully down.)
