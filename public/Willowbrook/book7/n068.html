Night 68

The Fence Meeting on Leaving

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

This month's hard case stood at the fence, where it always stood: Mateo's orbit. (Middle school now, the buses, the practices, the new bigness; the fence summits thinned to Saturdays, the Saturdays sometimes to nods. The old string, quieting, and this time not from neglect. From physics.)

The summit, called honest: "Mateo. The orbits are doing the thing. Yours got big, it's supposed to get big; the academy graduates; the altitude was the whole point. And this month's machine says attendance is part one, and your attendance is... thirteen-year-old attendance.

So the fence needs a new treaty, honest: not the daily fence. Not the drifted nothing. The standing fence: first Saturday, every month, rolls on me, no matter what orbit. Small enough to keep.

Big enough to matter. The fence don't care about frequency, Mateo. The fence cares about the keeping." (He looked at her a long time over the rail, the kid who taught her the ladder rules, the chalk, the scheduling-not-quitting, half a childhood of fence-lessons, and said: "First Saturday.

Every month. And when you're the one with the big orbit..." He's thirteen; he sees the road. "the treaty holds from your side. Deal? Deal. The fence outlives the orbits. That's what fences are for."

It did. Sure enough across every far page: first Saturday, every month, rolls alternating, decades. The treaty held from both sides. The fence outlived every orbit either of them ever flew.)
