Night 346

The Town Delivers, Seventh Round

Month 12: The Maybe Year

Birthday week, the seventh; the shelf's last, and the town delivered at full depth: Monday: the dandelions (a wagon of them, dried and fresh both; the industry's founder attached a note in his own hand: "From the farm. For the week. T"). Tuesday: the rolls (the basket; and beneath them, flour-dusted, Rosa's own addition: the original bald-babies photograph, reframed, doubled, one for each family; "the founding document goes home with both archives now.

It always belonged to both"). Wednesday: the library. Thursday: the fence (Mateo, first-Saturday off-schedule, "birthday weeks bend treaties": a compass. Real, brass, workshop-mounted. "For after the gate. Whatever's through it, the handles work everywhere.

Find the lantern-equivalent. Navigate"). And Friday: Number Nine's tea, the leather case's colleague. Mrs. Hollis's own brass magnifier's twin, kept fifty years in a drawer: "every keeper needs a backup instrument, colleague. And every retiring teacher needs to see her equipment go back into service.

Take the pair. Take the Fridays too, they were always yours. I just held them till you came."
