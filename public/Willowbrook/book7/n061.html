Night 61

The Showing-Up Check

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

Component one, isolated from the saves: Showing up. The check, run across the web: "Lily: six years of window seats, whistle answers, catching-ends. The fence: Mateo, every season, both sides showing. The Fridays: seventy-plus with Mrs.

Hollis, kept through fevers by notes when the body couldn't. The bench: daily. Wren's mural recesses: two years unbroken. The scared-note answer: There before I decided. And the reverse rows..." The flu week; the soup; the serialized horses; the map of the classroom "So you know the territory". "they showed at my down-days, all of them, uncalled."

The finding: "The friendship's first gear is attendance. Not the fun kind, the faithful kind. The showing-up that runs through boring Tuesdays and fevers and uncalled down-days. Everything else this month finds will sit on this gear. Nothing sits under it."
