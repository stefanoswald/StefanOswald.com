Night 58

The Endings Month, Counted

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

Month's end. At month's end, The easy-to-count part included seven witnesses (the leaves, the Bellas, the box, the old room, the mender, the books, the gloaming). One hard session (the leaf's book; the loving with nowhere to put its hands; the urgency proof). One radio finale.

One last baby tooth. One volume two. One rumor scare. One last leaf (released, not torn; landed on the gate-path; pressed beside true). One drifted chapter. One slide-bottom law. One answer. Some changes belonged outside the count: that the question is always whose book you're reading.

That endings are a skill and towns that practice together get good at them. That the going is the coming-back started. And the bottom of the stack: things end because they're real, and the ending is what real costs, and real is worth it, and the missing is the receipt. "Good endings-month?

The realest one. And Mama, this month wasn't for this month. The year knows what's coming: the shelf, the gate, the last word, the handing of the bear. This month was the training camp. The year sent me to endings school before the big endings come, the year's been doing that my whole life, sending the lessons ahead of the needings.

The year loves me, is basically the finding. The years all did." Remembered. The years all did, little one. The years all do. On we go, trained, receipted, glad.
