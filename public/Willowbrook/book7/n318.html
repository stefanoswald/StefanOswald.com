Night 318

The Ordinary Nights

Month 11: Bobby Bear's New Kid

Olivia had counted the great nights with Bobby Bear: storms, fevers, dream-meadow journeys, first days, and hard goodbyes.

Then she thought about the thousands of nights when nothing unusual happened.

Bath. Pajamas. One chapter. A forecast for tomorrow. Bobby Bear in his place on the pillow. The lamp clicking off. Sleep arriving before the house grew still.

"Those nights were the biggest part," she told him. "The hard nights helped me sometimes. The ordinary nights built me."

Night after night, the same small actions had said: You are safe. Someone will return in the morning. The lamp will be here tomorrow. So will the story.

No notebook could hold every one of those evenings, and Olivia did not try. She simply sat beneath them in memory, as if beneath a sky too full of stars to count.

The extraordinary nights shone brightly. The ordinary ones had made the darkness gentle enough for anything to shine at all.
