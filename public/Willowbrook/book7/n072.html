Night 72

June Finds the Missing Piece

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

June read Olivia's friendship list beside the fort: being there, telling the truth, making room, repairing hurts, and sharing silence.

"It sounds like a barn inspection," June said. "Where is the fun?"

Everyone stared at the page.

June pointed toward the snowbank where Dana had fallen the week before. "We laughed so hard no one could stand up. Does that not count?"

It counted everywhere. The lost first sleepover, the bench crowns, the polite moon, the zucchini pranks, Roosevelt the pig, laughter ran through their whole history.

Olivia had treated it as decoration when it was one of the reasons friendship felt like home.

She added a sixth line: A FRIEND LAUGHS WITH YOU. NEVER AT YOUR HURT, ALWAYS AT THE JOY YOU MAKE TOGETHER.

"Better," June said.

Olivia smiled. "The list proved itself by being wrong in front of friends."

Then Dana slipped into the snowbank again, and the newly added line received immediate practical testing.
