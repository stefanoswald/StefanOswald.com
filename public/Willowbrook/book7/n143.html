Night 143

The Question Month's Weather

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

The thaw arrived mid-month (the year turning toward its final stories; the story feels the calendar tightening like everyone else), and the uncovering ran its annual ritual with a change of preacher: Theo gave the sermon. (The bank; the lid going; the blblblb emerging; the new kids assembled, the crane kid, the little sister, two first-grade recruits, and the six-year-old, delivering the family's oldest text, his edition:) "Listen. The brook ran all winter. Under the lid.

Quiet-but-going. Everybody thinks the winter stops the good things. Wrong. The good things go private. The brook, the beds, the bulbs, the... the everything. Nothing good is stopped. It's all down there. Running.

Waiting for the uncovering. My sister taught me at the same bank and now I'm teaching you and that's also a thing that ran all winter, the teaching. The teaching never stops either. It just changes mouths."

("The teaching changes mouths..." The founder repeated at the lamp, filing the succession complete. "The sermon's his now. The bank's his. I just... attended. Part one of everything. The keeping keeps getting lighter, Mama, that's the strangest part of the trade.

The more you hand on, the more you have. The list grows by giving. The only warehouse that works that way. The kit only keeps what it gives. I wrote that years ago and I'm still finding out how deep it goes.")
