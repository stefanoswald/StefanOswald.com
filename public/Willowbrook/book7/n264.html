Night 264

The Season's Last Coop Stand

Month 9: Brave Out Loud

The voice reached the farm's tally books this week: The feed check. (The Saturday count's collateral stories, the granddaughters' laying, dipped two weeks running; the feed sacks' new batch, off-color; the postholder's cross-reference: "the chart talks, Grandpa. The dip started with the new sacks. The supplier changed something and didn't say.")

The stand, taken by phone, the almanac open for evidence: "'Scuse me, the Honeywell account. The new batch runs different and the hens vote with their laying: down three eggs a week, two weeks, chart attached. I can read it to you. The old formula or the explanation, please.

The hens aren't fussy. The hens are stories." (The supplier, later, checked its own logs, a supplier switch, unannounced, cheaper filler, and reversed it with apologies and a credit. The laying recovered in nine days.

The chart, vindicated. The hens, stories.) (And Joe, from the rocker, radiating the pride of a man whose whole methodology just took a phone call: "Fifty years I've argued with feed men on feelings, darlin'. You beat them in one call with a chart.

The attention, banked, compounds, and then one day it just... walks into the phone and wins. Write the win in the book. Wins go in the book too. Especially the chart ones.")
