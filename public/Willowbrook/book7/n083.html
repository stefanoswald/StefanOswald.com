Night 83

The Friendship Tally book's Zero Column

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

A case from the class: a kid who keeps score. ("I invited her to three parties and she only invited me to one." The tally book-brain, aimed at the one place tally books poison.) The coaching, delivered gently by the town's foremost tally book-keeper, which is what made it land: "I keep more tally books than anybody.

The egg counts, the promise book, the fund checks, tally books are half my whole approach. And here's the rule I run: Tally books are for things, never for friends. The neighborly trust runs on proximity and the friendships run on the parts, and the moment you open a favor-column on a person, you've turned the us into a store. Stores need balanced books.

Usses need... attendance and mending and laughing, none of which balance, they're not supposed to; Lily caught me falling once and I'll never catch her the same weight back; the fort fed kids who never fed it; the given column has no receivable side. That's not bad accounting. That's the whole difference between a store and a home. Close the favor-book. Open the fort."
