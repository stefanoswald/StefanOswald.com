Night 141

The Answer That Came Anyway

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

They remembered this one carefully, because it happened mid-ordinary, the way the big ones do: Tuesday, the office-hours table, the homework half-done, Theo across, the gold hour golding, and the oldest window, the what am I for, saved open since age four... closed itself. Quietly. From inside. ("Mama.

Put down the tea. The what-am-i-for just... Answered. Not with words, with the table. I looked up from the math and saw it whole: Theo reading at my table with my methods in his hands.

The corner running on its third keeper. The garden. The codes. The wall's stitchings. The fund, the bench, the maps. Mama, everything I ever built is a keeping-place. Every single thing. The whole approach, aimed one direction since the crib: I'm for the keeping.

The town keeps its kids, and some kids grow up to be the keeping. The librarian. Mrs. Hollis. Petrov. Joe. The whole second grid, they're all keepers, and I've been apprenticing to all of them, my whole life, without knowing the trade had a name.

The keeping. That's my for. The window can close. The windows close demselves when the answer's done growing. The owl said the reaching was the for, he was right, and the reaching had a direction all along.")
