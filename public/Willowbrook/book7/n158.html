Night 158

The Story of the Almanac's Birth

Month 6: Grandpa Joe's Slow Stories

Slow story five, the origin the fourth hand has waited years for: The book's birth. ("You know the first entry, 1974, the mud planting, the ignored advice. Here's the story under it:") "I was twenty-six and angry, darlin'. The farm was drowning me, my father two years gone, the debts loud, every decision mine and half of them wrong.

And the mud planting. I knew better; my own head told me wait; and I planted anyway because angry men plant angry. Lost the row. And that night I sat where we're sitting and understood the real problem: I had nobody to be accountable to.

My father kept me honest by existing. Gone, the honesty needed a new address." He touched the book's cover, fifty years worn. "So I bought a tally book and gave my honesty a home. The book was never for the weather, darlin'.

The book was my father's eyes, rebuilt in paper. Every entry since is me telling somebody the truth, and the somebody grew: my father's eyes, then Margaret's, then the whole line back and..." The look, level, at the fourth hand. "and forward. You know who reads it now. The book was always a who, not a what. The keeping always is."
