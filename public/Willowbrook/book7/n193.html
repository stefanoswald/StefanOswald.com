Night 193

The Fence Gap Fixed Right

Month 7: Making Things Right

List item two, closed by paperwork: The fence gap. (The rusted snag; one coat-tear a week by the nurse's log; the requisition saved with the log attached, "the facts are the spine; the nurse's numbers did the arguing; I just stapled them to the ask.") The fix: two weeks, district-speed (fast, for districts; the file on her works at district level now too, as it happened; the councilman forwarded with a cover note). The snag: gone.

The nurse's log: closed on that column. And the habit, franchised at the clinic table: "Cross-reference the logs, everybody. The nurse logs the coats. The janitor logs the sticky doors. The librarian logs the families in hard weather.

The town keeps all the stories it needs to fix itself, in fifteen notebooks that never meet. The whole civic craft is introducing the notebooks. I'm gonna spend my whole life introducing notebooks, probably. Somebody has to. The notebooks can't reach each other alone."
