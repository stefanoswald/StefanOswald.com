Night 254

The Stand for the Willow

Month 9: Brave Out Loud

This month's biggest occasion arrived by posted notice: a survey flag on the hill's edge. (A developer's preliminary notice; the parcel adjacent to the hill; the access road option that would cut the hill's east slope, the willow's watershed; the roots' own ground. The town read the notice the way the story read it: cold.) The response, assembled at the speed of a town that practice: the petition (route-logic; three days; six hundred names, "the town's got maybe eight hundred grown-ups; the math is the message").

The council session. And the telling, hers, requested by the council this time, the seat earned years back: "The tree was here first. The town joined the tree, it's in the founding walk; it's in the count; it's in three hundred years of birthdays and words and watchings. The parcel math says the road saves eleven minutes of trucking.

The town math says: the hill is where the town keeps itself. You can't run a road through the town's own keeping and call it eleven minutes saved. The eleven minutes would cost... everything that doesn't fit in minutes. The packet's got the math that fits in minutes.

I'm here for the part that doesn't. The tree can't come to night meetings. The tree sent the town instead. All of it. Count the room." (The room, counted: standing only. The vote: unanimous, the access road re-routed, the hill's slope protected by town rule, permanent, sure enough; the far pages hold the willow safe through every decade it checks.

The tree sent the town instead. The town came. Of course it came. Three hundred years of here, and the tree taught half of them to say it.)
