Night 80

The Introvert's Us

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

The pattern got its quiet-approach tuning this week, because Wren, reading the gear notes at the bench, saved the new rule herself (out loud; clearly; the voice lives at the bench full-time and travels widely now): "the parts run different at low volume. Document it: My attendance is presence, not plans. I don't come to things; I'm just there, near, reliably. My truth-carriage is written or one-sentence, the big truths come out small; the small size isn't the truth's size.

My repairs don't use words at all, after the only fight we ever had, I fixed it by drawing you a map of the classroom. You understood. That was the sorry. My quiet isn't a gear, it's the engine; the talking is my lubrication, rationed.

And my laughing is mostly silent and entirely real. Same machine. Quiet edition. Put it in the notes so the loud ways of working stop grading us by their gears." (Entered verbatim, approved by its author, saved beside her first dispatch from their bedtime world of the quiet, the two documents, four years apart, the voice growing between them like the rings it described.)
