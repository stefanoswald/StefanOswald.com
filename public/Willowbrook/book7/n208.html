Night 208

The Last List Item

Month 7: Making Things Right

This month closed its list where the list's hardest item lived: Number sixteen. (the story forecast it at 188 and the story keeps its promises: the wave, returned. This month, the fifth; the egg route's Saturday arriving to find him on the porch, and the hand coming up, slow, like a thing remembering its hinge.) The route's response, calibrated to the craft: the wave returned. Matched, not escalated ("you don't cheer a quill-coat's first wave; you wave back, same size; the matching says: this costs nothing; there's no bill coming").

The eggs, left per custom. The dandelion, per custom. And nothing else, no knock, no casserole, no performed noticing. The door, still just a door, still just open. ("The hardest mends don't look like mends, Theo.

They look like eggs. Fifty-two Saturdays of eggs. The trade's whole late wisdom: urgency wearing patience's clothes, the urgent part is that the route never misses, and the patient part is everything else. He waved.

That's the whole spring, right there. Some springs are one wave big. They count entire.")
