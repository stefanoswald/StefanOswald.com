Night 113

The Rope Day Rehearsal

Month 4: Theo's Big Sister

Rope-day prep entered its performance phase: The dry runs. The list, drilled to recital grade, living room, towel-caped, the couch as deep end: "I can float. I can kick-swim the whole shallow. I can blow bubbles and grab the wall.

The skills crossed already. I'm just following them." (Word-perfect. Fourteen consecutive rehearsals. The couch, crossed nightly, nine imaginary feet deep.) And the veteran's last coaching note, delivered at rehearsal twelve: "One more thing they don't put in the list, Theo, the thing I found out at the rope, that nobody told me: the deep end is boring.

Once you're swimming, it's just... water. Same water as the shallow, with more under it. The monster was always a re-test in a dragon costume, the whole family of monsters is. So when you cross and it's just water: that's not the victory being small.

That's the victory being true. The true victories are almost always quieter than the fear was loud. Budget for the quiet. The quiet is the whole prize."
