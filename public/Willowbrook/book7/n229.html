Night 229

The Almanac's Town Pages

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

The count's last cross-reference, run at the porch (nine pages left in volume one; they counted everything now): The almanac's town entries. (Fifty years of the valley's weather, and threaded through it, sure enough, fifty years of the other weather: "Bucket line at the library. Not one volume lost. The petersens' Well low.

Ran the hose tuesday and thursday. New family at the corner place. Took eggs. The whole valley at abel's smoke." Entry by entry, the town's second grid, logged, the only continuous written record of it in existence, it seemed, besides the story.)

("Grandpa. The book's got the whole grid in it. Fifty years of the kindness weather, dated, plain, facts-as-spine. The town thinks the grid's invisible and the book's been logging it the whole time...

Darlin', the book logs weather. All the weather. The rain and the kindness run the same way, they fall, they soak, they come up as crops. A farmer'd be a fool to log one and not the other.

The kindness is just weather that people do." The kindness is weather that people do. The story sets it beside the whole month and declares the count complete. The almanac beat everybody to everything. It always did. Attention, banked, fifty years compounding.)
