Night 271

The Month Hands Its Craft to the Wall

Month 9: Brave Out Loud

This month closed at the hoop, fifth stitching, the wall's plan growing: The candidates. ("Talk in the weather" (the fluency's whole finding). "The shrink-check" (the co-authored one; the succession's own law). "Count the room" (the hill's night).

And the winner, because this month kept proving it in every register, thunder to whisper to silent:) "The truth doesn't check your height." (Stitched. Crooked. Gold. Hung fifth in the line: Kindest true thing first.

Endings are the bottoms of slides. A friend is somebody you build a us with. Broken is just the first draft. The truth doesn't check your height. The kitchen wall, five laws deep now, reads like what it is: a constitution, written one year at a time, in wool, by a childhood.

Guests copy it. The copies travel. They had found the wall's laws stitched in kitchens three towns over, attributed to nobody, which is exactly how the best laws travel: unsigned, kid-height, true. The gold season opens tomorrow. The wall is ready. The wall was always ready.)
