Night 106

The Sick Day Service, Returned

Month 4: Theo's Big Sister

This month's reversal test ran itself: She went down, and the student ran the rules upward, unprompted, complete: The bell (the cow's; placed in her sightline, the operation she wasn't supposed to know about, run openly now, its inventor six years deep in the craft). The soup (Mama's hands; his reading of the recipe card, slow, finger under the words, the miles voting: "she means 2. Everybody knows," the card's whole tradition, third generation). The chapters (read aloud at the couch-side: the truck book, his flow's home territory, performed with sound effects, "the lamp travels to the sick; the rule; you said").

And the get-well card, crayon and confidence: "Get big again soon. The walk needs its deputy. The postholder." ("The whole kit came back up the pattern, Mama..." She reported, mending, awed. "Every rule I ever ran on him, run on me, unprompted, his versions.

The coaching seat's real test was never whether he learns the maps. It's whether the maps come back when the coach is the one on the couch. They came back. With sound effects.")
