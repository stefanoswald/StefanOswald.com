Night 352

The Candle Prepared

Month 12: The Maybe Year

The eve of the candle, and the family prepared the founding text for its first-ever full telling (seven years of the snowplow in pieces and polishings; tomorrow: the whole original, complete, the way it actually ran): The parts, assigned at the table: Papa holds the blizzard and the fear ("the real fear this time, the unpolished edition; the founding text gets its founding weight back for the whole telling"). Mama holds the window and the words ("every scared thing I said out loud, the list survives; I kept it; you'll hear your mama scared tomorrow, loves, and that's the point: the Maybe was born in real weather, not story-weather"). Olivia holds the snowplow and the turning. And Theo holds the ending, "Perfect timing" and beyond; the doctor's whole line; the arrival itself, "because the ending of the founding story is a baby, everybody, and the baby part should be held by the person who knows babies-being-hoped-for from the being side.

Tenderhearted holds the arrivals. It's the correct casting. It was always gonna be the correct casting." ("Tomorrow the candle, everybody. The whole original. The founding text, complete, plus seven years of maybes read back after, the biggest harvest the jar's ever held.

Sleep good. The flame's got a long beautiful night ahead of it, and so do we.")
