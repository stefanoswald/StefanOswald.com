Night 73

The Grown-Up Validation

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

The pattern went to peer review this week: Friday tea. (Mrs. Hollis; the six gears presented on one notebook page; forty-plus years of friendship stories on the other side of the table, and her Polish sister seventy years deep on the phone line as standing evidence.) The review, delivered over the pot: "Attendance, truth, difference, repair, quiet, laughter."

The old teacher read the page twice. "Child, I've watched seventy years of friendships from windows and classrooms, and I'll confirm your machine with one new rule: the parts aren't equal at every age. Kids run on laughter first, part six is the doorway gear at your age; you meet in the giggling and build backward. Grown-ups run on attendance first, at my age, part one is nearly the whole machine; the ones who still show up are the whole treasury.

And the middle years..." A look, thirty years long, out the window seat's glass. "the middle years run on repair. The friendships that survive the middle are the mended ones, every time. Same machine, all your life. The load just moves between the parts. Like walking. It's exactly like walking."
