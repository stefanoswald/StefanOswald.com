Night 258

The Playground Rules Meeting

Month 9: Brave Out Loud

This month's gift for everyone, gathered at the fort by general demand: The playground constitution. (Seven years of accumulated case law, the handicap races, the pairs draft, the new-kid check, the castle rule, the loaner rights, the bench game, the quiet corner's five-foot rule, codified at last, one plan, drafted by the governed:) The convention's method, hers: every rule proposed with its origin story attached ("laws without birth certificates become grudges, the inherited-feud lesson; the plan keeps the whys with the whats"). The votes: rotation-order. The ratification: all districts, the little kids' bloc voting first ("the soft-snow part's precedent: the smallest voters know the most about needing rules").

And the preamble, drafted by committee and polished by its founding scribe: "This playground runs on: Everybody plays. The chances get engineered fair. The new ones get checked for safe. The quiet corner is sovereign.

The cruelty is never steady and gets unbolted on sight. The rules keep their whys attached. Amend freely; the plan is a relay. Agreed by everybody, which is the only ratification there is."
