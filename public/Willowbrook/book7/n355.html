Night 355

The Just-Us Morning, Seventh Dawn

Month 12: The Maybe Year

Dawn. Candles. Pancakes. The original three. The letters, seventh annual reading, the shelf's last: Mama's (excerpted; the drawer keeps the whole): "... The shelf ends this month, and here is what the window watched, seven years entire: you arrived attending and you never once stopped.

The stories were our excuse, you found that out this year; you find everything out, but here is what even you haven't found yet, so the letter gets to keep one secret for the someday-drawer: the lamp was my harbor too, love. Every night I lit it for you, it lit me back. The keeping kept the keeper before you ever wrote the words. You were my gold light.

You always..." (The excerpt ends there. The drawer keeps the whole. Somedays exist for a reason.) Papa's: "... Seven things about seven, the last list of the first shelf: One, the wrong notes are yours now, the dynasty is in its best hands ever, and I include my father's.

Two, your brother's ladder wish came true; watch what he does with the altitude. Three, the tree writes in six nights, and for the seventh time we don't know the word, and for the first time I suspect you do. Keep it. The tree says it best.

Four, the gate, whatever it opens, your mama and I ask one thing: come back and tell us at the lamp. All the doors of your whole life, kiddo: come back and tell us at the lamp. That's the family's only standing order. Five, the bear did good.

Six, so did we, I think, watching you. And seven: the shelf ends, the books change shape, and the last thing this letter knows for sure is the first thing it ever knew, you looked, day one, May snow at the window. Keep looking. The looking was always the whole of it..."
