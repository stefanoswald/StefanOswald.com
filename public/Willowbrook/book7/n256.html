Night 256

The Stand She Didn't Take

Month 9: Brave Out Loud

This month's subtlest lesson, saved from a stand not taken: The passed occasion. (A dispute at the exchange, two elders, an old disagreement resurfacing over the mending table's methods; voices rising politely, the way elder voices rise. And the room's resident stander... stood down.) The reasoning, given at the lamp: "The voice wanted to go, Mama, the fixing reflex plus the standing reflex; double pull.

And the discernment held: that fight was theirs. Older than me by decades. Both standing on ground I can't see. Petrov's forty years and Gus-Senior's seventy; the arrows and the marbles of it invisible from kid-height.

The brave voice has a territory, same as the answer shelf: Spend where you can see the ground. I couldn't see the ground. So the voice stayed banked and the ears went in instead, and the ears heard them solve it demselves in twenty minutes, the way fifty-year friends solve things: with shorthand I couldn't even follow. The stand I didn't take was the right stand.

That's the fluency's last chamber: knowing the difference between a room that needs a voice and a room that needs a witness. Most rooms need the witness. The voice is for the other ones."
