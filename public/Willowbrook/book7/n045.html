Night 45

The Friend Moving Scare

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

A scare ran the bench this week: Noor might move. (Overheard fragments; a for-sale sign two doors from hers, mis-attributed by the rumor mill; the bench's whole cartography division suddenly, horribly provisional.) The response, run by the book even scared. Especially scared: source-check before grief ("'everybody says' is not a witness, even when the everybody is your own dread").

The direct ask, at the bench, gentle: "Noor. The rumor mill says maybe you're moving. I'm asking the source. Whatever the answer, the bench holds." The truth: the sign was the neighbor's. Noor's family: staying.

("The paper grew here," Noor said. "It keeps growing here.") And the scare's residue, processed honestly at the lamp: "The dread had her packed and gone in one afternoon, Mama. The whole ending, pre-grieved, over a mis-read sign.

This month's warning label: Don't pre-grieve off rumors. The real endings deserve real grief and the fake ones deserve source-checks, and the dread can't tell them apart, that's not the dread's job. Checking is my job. The dread's just the smoke alarm. You don't evacuate for burnt toast. You check the toaster."
