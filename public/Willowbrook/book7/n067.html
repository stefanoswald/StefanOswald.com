Night 67

Can a Bear Be a Friend?

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

At bedtime Olivia tested her growing definition against Bobby Bear.

He had been present every night for seven years. He had heard every truth she could tell. He was entirely different from her, being stuffed with wool. His torn ear and heart patch had both been mended. He could sit through any silence.

"Bobby passes," Olivia announced.

Then she considered the Great Willow, the brook, the farm, and the nursery lamp. They were not people, yet each had kept her company and helped her become herself.

"Maybe friendship is not only person-shaped," she told Mama. "Maybe we love some places and things because they keep showing up for us, and because we learn to care for them in return."

Mama tucked the quilt beneath Olivia's chin. "That sounds like belonging."

Olivia liked the word. She wrote it beside friend with a line between them, not an equal sign. Some kinds of love touched at the edges without becoming the same.

Bobby Bear sat beneath the lamp, passing every definition that mattered.
