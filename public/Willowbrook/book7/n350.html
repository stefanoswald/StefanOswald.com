Theo's Gift Operation, Final Edition

Month 12: The Maybe Year

The couch office reopened for its fourth annual campaign, and they remembered the operation's scale with professional respect: Three weeks. (The longest hold in office history. The edges technique at full mastery: "Is not a box. Is not a drawing.

Is not a whistle. Is bigger than all the others together. Is the biggest thing I ever made. That's all the edges you get.") The supplies, logged by the quartermaster: paper. Lots. The stapler. String.

The tape reserves, depleted twice. And one request that made the quartermaster's eyes go bright and stay bright for days, relayed to no one, held under the household's highest classification. (the story knows. The story's known since the requisition. The story says only: night 364 approaches, and the operation is equal to it, and the boy has been building toward this particular delivery since a scribble with shared hands five years ago. The tenderhearted go straight to the center. They always did. Fourteen days.)
