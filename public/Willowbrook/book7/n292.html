Night 292

The Solstice Handed Down

Month 10: The Gift Month

The solstice came, the shelf's last one, and the ceremony completed its succession: Theo led. (The candles, lit by his hand, supervised-retired. The cocoa. The teaching, delivered to an audience this year: the little district, invited; Ruthie and the crane kid and four more, assembled at the window for the doorway lesson, taught by the boy who learned it at this exact glass:) "Tonight's the longest dark of the whole year.

The most night there is. And here's the secret the candles are saying, my sister taught me at this window and now I'm teaching you, so pay attention because someday you teach somebody: Tonight's the turning. Tomorrow: a little more light. The darkest night is the one where the light starts coming back.

The dark is the doorway, not the wall. We light the candles to say we know. We remember. Now drink your cocoa and watch the window. The light's already turning while we stand here. That part's not ours to do.

The watching together, that part's ours. That's the whole ceremony. That's basically the whole everything, my sister says. She's right. She's right a annoying amount. You get used to it." (The founder, watching her ceremony run itself from the back of her own kitchen, the visitor's pension, the builder's whole reward: "the routine's his now, Mama.

Fully. He even kept the cocoa timing. And the little district goes home carrying the doorway, six more kids who'll never be scared of the dark's deepest night, because a five-year-old taught them it's a hinge. The tradition franchises at one-year speed now.

The light turns. The teachings turn with it. Everything turns tonight. That's the solstice. That was always the solstice.")
