Night 262

The Grown-Up Who Apologized

Month 9: Brave Out Loud

This month's rarest specimen, witnessed at the counter and saved with honors: a grown-up's public sorry. (The displeased neighbor of the phone call, the updated file, completing her arc in the huddle where the rumor once ran: "I owe this counter a correction of my own. I called a child disrespectful for correcting us here. The child was right, I was slow, and the man at Sixteen..."

The huddle attending; the flour-dust settling; the counter keeping, as it keeps everything. "the man at Sixteen got my casserole Tuesday. He took it. We talked about gutters. That's the whole report. The child runs a better counter than we do.")

(The receipt, issued at kid-height with this month's whole grace: "The correction's received and the file's updated and, for the story, at the venue, the grown-up sorry costs more than the kid one; the pride tax compounds with age; everybody knows. You paid retail in public. The counter keeps that too. The counter keeps everything good that happens at it.

That's why it's the counter." The neighbor, as it happened, became one of the stand's steadiest warm-money customers, and the town's under-conversation gained one more true-groove voice. The arcs complete. They mostly complete. The doors and the time and the eggs do their work.)
