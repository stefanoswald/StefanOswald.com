Night 62

A Friend Can Tell the Truth

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

The first answer in Olivia's friendship notebook was BEING THERE. The second came from a harder memory.

Years earlier, Lily had told her, "Sometimes you try to fix me when I only want you to listen."

The words had stung. Olivia had wanted to defend herself. Instead, they made their just-listening rule, and the friendship grew easier.

They remembered other difficult truths: Lily calling Olivia out by an inch during a game, Olivia admitting when jealousy had made her distant, and both of them apologizing after the argument that nearly spoiled an autumn.

"A friendship that only carries nice words is not very strong," Olivia said.

Lily tightened a loose braid in the yarn. "The hard words have to be carried carefully."

Olivia wrote: A FRIEND TELLS YOU THE TRUTH WITH CARE, AND MAKES IT SAFE FOR YOU TO DO THE SAME.

The sentence was longer than she wanted. Its meaning was exactly the right size.
