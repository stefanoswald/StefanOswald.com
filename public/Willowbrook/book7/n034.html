Night 34

Bella's Return, Counted

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

Witness two: Bella. (The autumn garden; the rainbow wings, which generation now? The book says the Bellas live seasons, not years; this is granddaughter-Bella at least, maybe great-. The kids stopped counting on purpose years ago.

The story respects the policy and keeps the count anyway: fourth-generation. The story can't help itself.) "The Bella-shape ended three times, Theo..." this month's scholar worked it, watching the wings on the fence post. "and the Bella never ended once. The return comes every year, the faiffullest appointment in the garden, and it's never the same wings and it's always the same visit.

So the shape isn't the thing. The pattern is the thing. The shape's the pattern's... clothing. The clothes wear out. The pattern buys new ones. Bella is a pattern the garden does, like the leaf is a thing the tree does, like the whistle is a thing air does..."

And Theo, tenderhearted, cutting to it from the fence: "So when I wave at her, I'm waving at all the Bellas. The whole line. Every year at once." (He waved. The wings, the story swears, dipped. All the Bellas waved back. That's how patterns wave.)
