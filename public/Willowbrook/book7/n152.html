Night 152

The Slow Month Declared

Month 6: Grandpa Joe's Slow Stories

At the start of the month, Olivia said, The saturdays double. (Saturdays and Sundays at the farm this month, because the winter was long, and the joints are loud more days than not now, and the family reads its own weather: the porch hours are the treasury, and treasuries get attended.) this month's format, set by its beneficiary, pace by its teller: "The slow stories, Grandpa. Not the almanac's entries, the stories under the entries. The ones that take a whole porch afternoon.

The ones you've been saving because the fast world never sat still long enough. I'm sitting still. This month sits still. Tell the long ones. The long ones..." said Joe, rocking once. "Nobody's asked for the long ones since Margaret."

A pause, gold and grateful and certain, the practiced ending running forward for once: "All right, darlin'. Sundays too. The long ones. Bring the book, some of them will want writing. And some..." The rock, again. "some will want just the hearing. You know the difference by now. You've known it for years."
