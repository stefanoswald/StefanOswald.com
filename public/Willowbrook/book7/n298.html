Night 298

The Received Column

Month 10: The Gift Month

This month's mirror-discipline, practiced deliberately (the compliment ambush's old lesson, scaled to the season: the givers are in the undercomplimented column; check the givers): The receiving. The practice, run against seven years of deflection instinct: the gifts of the season, taken in whole, the town's deliveries (received without a single "you didn't have to"; "the 'you didn't have to' refuses the gift mid-handing, Mama, they know they didn't have to; the didn't-have-to is the whole gift; you take it with both hands and say the true thing: Thank you, this landed"). The bright-eyed quartermaster services. The counted-back count (banked, not dodged, the whole town's she counted us folder, read three times since, each time all the way in).

And the practice's finding, saved at the lamp: "The receiving is a gift to the giver, the last backwards economics: a gift refused or deflected dies mid-flight; a gift received, whole, with the landing shown, that's the giver's payment; the whole-face smile runs both directions. The good receivers are the gift economy's other half, and nobody trains them, and this month trained me: Take it with both hands. Show the landing. The landing is the thank-you the words can't do."
