Night 181

The Owl and the Old Keeper

Month 6: Grandpa Joe's Slow Stories

As one month gave way to the next, the Great Willow caught Stella's warm light. The slow month, closing, and Oliver Owl, keeper of one long book, closed it with a tribute to the keeper of another, delivered formally across the valley dark toward one farmhouse lamp: "The tally book notes a colleague, colleagues. Fifty-one years. One book, kept nightly, the facts as spine, the weight in plain, the margins left for the meanings.

The watch has read over his shoulder for decades..." The watch reads over every shoulder; the town and the watch were never separate; everybody knows. "and the watch's professional assessment: the finest sustained keeping in the valley's recorded history, our own tally book not excepted. This month he handed the craft to the fourth hand and the stories to the cloth and the whistle to the gentlest and the pace to the whole line, and the watch notes what the town below already knows: The keeper is completing. Not ending.

Completing, his own word-craft; the volumes are the pattern's clothes; we learned it from the small one who learned it from the brook that he fished as a boy with the brother whose name is said now. The valley's lessons all come around. The tribute?" asked Stella, gold and ready. "The tribute is the tally book's highest, reserved fifty years for this exact page:", the owl raised his quill to the farmhouse lamp, gold in the far dark, and entered into the watch's eternal record one word, his colleague's own, complete: "'Well.'Mmm," hummed the Moon, the long hum, the tide-deep one, the one it saves.

Goodnight, keepers of long books, feathered, farming, four-sentenced, completing. The valley's lessons all come around. The lamps burn gold across the dark to each other. Sleep. Slowly, where it counts. Sleep.
