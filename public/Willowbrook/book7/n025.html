Night 25

At the Meadow Gate

Month 1: When No One Is Watching

The dream meadow came without warning one night. Bobby Bear waited on the gold path, and together they walked until the old gate appeared in the distance.

Olivia told him about the wallet, the broken cup, the honest card game, the hedge, the spelling test, the cracked egg, and Lily's whistle.

"Most of the choices were small," she said. "No one would have known if I chose differently."

Bobby Bear's worn ear tipped toward her. "You would have known."

They stopped where the keyhole could just be seen. The brass key felt warm in Olivia's pocket.

"Does the key open it?" she asked.

"The key turns the lock," said the bear. "But the years are what make you ready to walk through."

Olivia looked back at the long gold path. Every ordinary choice seemed to lie behind her like one more stepping-stone.

"Not yet," Bobby reminded her.

"I know."

They turned toward home. The gate stayed closed, patient and certain, while the key kept its warmth in her hand.
