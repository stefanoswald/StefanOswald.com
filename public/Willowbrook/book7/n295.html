Night 295

The Skill as the Gift

Month 10: The Gift Month

This month documented its subtlest format: The skill-gift. "The workshop teaches making things. The check found the deeper wing: the makings that are lessons. The bed trick, given to Marcus years ago: still paying, his letters park straight forever; the gift can't wear out.

The miles method, given to the whole district. The sheep-strategy patent, leaked with love. The whistle-craft, the knot-laws, the quiet-teaching Wren ran, the skill swap's whole economy: Give somebody a thing, they got a thing. Give them a skill, they got a factory." this month's skill-gift, chosen and delivered accordingly: The entry craft, taught to Lily ("the almanac's methods, the facts as spine, the weight in plain, the margins for the meanings, catch the good days live, because the bakery's daughter is gonna keep a counter someday, and counters need their books, and the books need their craft, and the craft was given to me and the given things don't stay given, they move").

(Lily's first entry, made that evening in a new notebook, shown across the window seat: "Saturday. Taught the entry craft by O. The margins are for the meanings. First meaning, margin-logged: The friend who gives you the craft is giving you the way she loves.

I know how she loves now. I always knew. Now it's written. L." they remembered the margin whole. The margins are for the meanings. Hers landed first try.)
