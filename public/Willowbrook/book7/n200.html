Night 200

Night Two Hundred: The Mended List

Month 7: Making Things Right

Night two hundred, the round-number rite, month-themed: The mended list. "The bear's ear and heart-patch. The bear book's spine. The willow kite's spar and leaf-patch. The friendship with Lily. The Vane file.

The mamas' street. The inherited feud. The cup with the gold crack. Rosa's bowl-wif-biography. The whole garden (a forty-year break in the town, mended with a one-page proposal). And the town's oldest mend, still running: the valley itself, the drought pipes, the hail winter, the chalk marks, a hundred years of breaks, gold-seamed by whoever was standing there with the craft.

The list's finding, Mama, Papa: I can't find one unbroken thing on the whole list of things I love. Not one. Everything precious got that way through the breaking-and-mending, the biography rule; the kintsugi count comes back a hundred percent. Which means the breaks were never the enemy.

The breaks were the... the applications. For gold. Everything that ever broke on us was applying to become precious. And this family..." The lamp; the gold light; seven years of it. "this family approves every application. That's the whole shop we run. That's the whole shop everybody should run."
