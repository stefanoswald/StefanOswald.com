Night 157

The Day After Samuel

Month 6: Grandpa Joe's Slow Stories

The lamp took the day after: "Mama. I got a great-uncle. Sixty years quiet and now he's... Arrived. Sam. He laughed at everything. The rod was his. And the strange part, the part the lamp needs: I miss him.

A person I never met, gone fifty years before I was born, and the missing is real, how? Where does missing-wifout-meeting even come from?" And Mama, true-voice, at the glass of the year's deepest window yet: "From the keeping, love. The missing doesn't need the meeting, it needs the mattering.

Grandpa carried Sam sixty years; the carrying shaped him; the shape raised your mama's whole... all of us. You've been loved, your whole life, partly in Sam's honor, the brave-enough-for-two went into the bravery this family taught you. You didn't meet him. You inherited him.

The missing is the inheritance, arriving. Let it in. Set a place for it. That's what the office hours are for, and love, the gone ones' office hours don't expire. Sixty years late is still office hours.

The dead teach the longest courses. Grandpa said it years ago. Sam's course just started. You're enrolled."
