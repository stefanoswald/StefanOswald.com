Night 313

The Account of the Ceremonies

Month 11: Bobby Bear's New Kid

Account five: The ceremonies. "The institutional years, bear: you attended. Every wish big think, seven; every drawing witnessed; every circle kept. Every interview, the fat notebook's sessions; you sat the whole seven. The thank-you-the-year rites.

The chain hangings. The night-before-everyfings: the first days, the recitals, the rope days, the gate-eves pending. The family's whole liturgical calendar, bear, you're on it. Not at it. On it. The ceremonies count you the way they count the candle and the gold light: Furniture of the sacred.

The steady kind. And the settling of this account's easy, because it settles forward: the ceremonies continue. The wish counsels move to his drawings. The interviews, his start next year; Papa's already bought the second fat notebook; the fat notebooks are a dynasty now.

The routines workshop, the furniture transfers, and the sacred... the sacred doesn't even notice the handing, bear. The sacred just keeps running, that's what makes it the sacred. Account five: settled by continuation. The best settlement there is."
