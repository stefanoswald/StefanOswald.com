Night 97

The Bike Upgrade

Month 4: Theo's Big Sister

His hardware upgrade of the season: The bigger bike. (Hers, outgrown, the birthday one, the training-wheels-off one, the block-that-rode-itself one, handed down with the full storied-packing law: "This bike knows the lane, Theo. It did my wobble morning. It knows the mailbox where everybody's alone-riding starts.

The bike's got the maps in it, you're not learning a strange machine; you're inheriting a educated one.") The fitting (Papa; the wrench; the seat raised through three years of doorframe lines in one afternoon). The test ride: wobble-free, the two-wheeler skills, ported clean to the new geometry, and fast, suddenly, the bigger wheels paying out the lane in half the strokes. ("The lane got shorter!

The lane stayed. You got longer. The doorframe's been telling you all year." And the old bike's successor, staged: His old bike, storied and boxed for the crane kid's little sister, the river flowing downstream by the rule.

The bikes of this lane, as it happened, form a lineage now, an educated fleet, maps accumulating frame to frame. Somewhere a historian of small towns would weep. The story just saves it: the fleet teaches. Everything here teaches.)
