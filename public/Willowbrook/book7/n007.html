Night 7

The Shortcut Through the Hedge

Month 1: When No One Is Watching

Olivia was late to meet Lily at the park. The quickest route ran through a gap in the hedge at Number Eleven, a shortcut children had used for years.

She stopped at the opening. The house looked empty. Squeezing through would save four minutes, and no one would know.

But the hedge belonged to someone, and so did the yard beyond it.

Olivia ran the long way and arrived breathless.

"You went around?" Lily asked.

"I wasn't invited through."

The following Saturday, they knocked at Number Eleven. An elderly man answered and listened to their request.

"That gap? Children used it when my Albert was small," he said. "Of course you may pass through. I'll trim the thorns away."

On Monday, the shortcut was both quicker and permitted.

Olivia wrote one sentence in her notebook: SOME FORBIDDEN THINGS ARE ONLY UNASKED THINGS.

The question had cost nothing. It had given a whole neighborhood its path back.
