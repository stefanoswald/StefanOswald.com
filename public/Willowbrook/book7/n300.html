Night 300

Night Three Hundred: The Gift List of a Life

Month 10: The Gift Month

Night three hundred, the shelf's last round-number rite, and the family met it with this month's whole instrument: The lifetime gift check. "Everything ever given to me, the short list: the lamp. The bear. The methods, the maps, the crafts, the posts, the rounds.

The town's whole second grid, drunk from daily. The educated fleet. The letters in the drawer. Twelve, with more coming. The years demselves, seven, gold, each one denser. And everything ever given by me, the true accounting: the same list, Mama.

The exact same list. The lamp I received became the lamp I run at Theo's bedside. The bear I received becomes the bear I hand in, soon. The methods became the clinics. The rounds became his rounds.

The gift check of a whole life comes out to one finding: There's no separate lists. The received and the given are one river, and I'm not the source or the mouth. I'm a bend. A good bend.

A bend that the river carved deep and the river runs through strong. That's all anybody is, the check says. The whole town: bends in one river. The whole everything. Night three hundred, and the books balance perfect for the first time ever, because I finally stopped keeping two."
