Night 293

The Gift of the Last Ordinary Week

Month 10: The Gift Month

This month's last full week ran ordinary, and the year's training received it as what it is (the best days wear ordinary clothes; the catching is the religion; the whole almanac in one week's practice): The week, caught live, day by day: the Monday letters lesson. The Tuesday corner (Milo's now; visited as emerita; the carpet leans for its third keeper true as ever). The Wednesday walk (his program; her deputy seat; the firework, thousands deep now). The Thursday office hours.

The Friday knock (tea; the Friday Log's first alternating entries, "Mrs. H wrote monday's; I wrote today's; the volume two of Fridays is live"). The Saturday farm. The Sunday rest (the part; held; the woolgathering hour deep and sweet).

("The week was the gift, Mama. Nothing happened and everything was present. Grandpa's Tuesday, at week scale. This month made twelve gifts with its hands and the year's last ordinary week out-gave them all just by running, the routines, gold; the orbits, kept; the whole machine of us, humming its work-songs under everything.

I caught it live. All seven days. The religion's whole point, practiced at last at full fluency: The ordinary is the masterpiece. You just have to be trained to see it, and the whole shelf was the training, and the training took.

Thank you, week. You were this month's best gift and you cost nothing and you were priceless. The backwards economics, perfected.")
