Night 133

The Question He Carries

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

The collection round reached the porch: "Yours, Grandpa. The one you've farmed under fifty years." Joe rocked a while. The catalog stayed closed. "Where does the growing come from..." He said at last. "Not the mechanism.

I know the mechanism; fifty years of it; seed, soil, water, sun. The growing. The push. You put a dead-looking speck in dirt and something in it wants. Reaches. Insists. Fifty years, darlin', and I've never once seen the wanting itself, only what it does.

The whole farm runs on a force I've never met." He looked at the fields, the row, the fence line where Scout's line volunteers yet. "Margaret and I argued it our whole marriage, her side: the wanting is God's fingerprint. My side: the wanting is just what matter does, given the chance.

And her winning move, every time, forty years running: 'Either way, joe, either way, the correct response is the same. You tend it. You thank it. You don't waste it.' I never beat that. I stopped trying. I just farm."
