Night 69

More Than a Friend

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

During friendship month, Olivia tried to decide whether Theo counted as one of her friends.

He showed up every day, though he had been born into the arrangement. He told her the truth, repaired fights with sorry-flowers, sat quietly near her when words were difficult, and knew things about her that nobody else noticed.

"He meets every friendship test," Olivia told Mama. "But friend still feels like the wrong word."

Theo looked up from his drawing. "Why?"

"Friends are people you choose. I did not choose to have a brother."

"You choose me now," he said.

Olivia considered the years between them: the bell, the books, the fights, the handprint in the fort, the Wednesday walks he would inherit.

"That is true. We were assigned to each other, and then we kept choosing it."

She had spent a month looking for a better category. At last she understood that the ordinary word was already large enough.

"Brother," she said.

Theo returned to his drawing. "Livy," he answered.

Some names did not need improving.
