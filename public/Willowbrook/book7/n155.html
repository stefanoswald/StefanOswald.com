Night 155

The Story of the Courting Mistakes

Month 6: Grandpa Joe's Slow Stories

Slow story three, told with visible relish (the porch has comedy hours too; this month isn't all weight): The courting disasters. ("Before the willow yes came two years of me being the county's leading fool, darlin'. The story should hold the whole catalog:") The failed picnic (planned three weeks; rained out in minutes; the backup plan was no backup plan; "Margaret sat in the truck cab eating wet sandwiches and I thought: ruined. She told her sister that night it was the best date of her life, 'he didn't pretend it wasn't a disaster.

He just split the disaster fair.' I learned everything from that rain and didn't know it for years"). The wrong flowers (roses, expensive, three towns fetched; "she liked dandelions. Wildflowers. Free ones. I'd spent a week's wages proving I hadn't been looking.

The flowers you notice somebody loving, darlin', that's the whole test. Your brother passed it at three. Took me till twenty-four"). And the letter that saved it after the flower disaster; one page; his hand; "I said the true thing plain: I'd been courting the idea of you.

I'd raver know the actual one. Start over? She kept that letter fifty years. It's in the cedar box. You'll read it someday. It's four sentences. The four-sentence men get one letter like that per lifetime, and it had better be the right four."
