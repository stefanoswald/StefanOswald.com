What Makes a Friend?

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

Olivia and Lily sat on their old park bench while Lily braided three strands of yarn.

"We have known each other since before we can remember," Olivia said. "What actually makes someone a friend?"

"You are asking a fish to explain water," Lily replied.

"A fish who has kept seven years of notes."

They began with memories instead of definitions: the failed sleepover and its happier second try, the whistle code, the winter forts, the arguments repaired at the window seat, the ordinary Thursdays when neither of them had much to say.

"Being alike cannot be the answer," Lily said. "We disagree all the time."

"And never arguing cannot be the answer either."

Olivia opened her notebook. At the top of a clean page she wrote WHAT MAKES A FRIEND?

The answer did not arrive that afternoon. That was all right. The best questions in Olivia's notebooks had always needed room to grow.
