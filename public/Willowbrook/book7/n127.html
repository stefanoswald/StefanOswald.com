Night 127

The Dream Count Question

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

The meadow, mid-month, and the window that lives there, visited at last, toward the gate on the gold path: "Bobby. The meadow. What is it? The other side of the willow. I've said that since four.

The dream country. The unloading dock. The reading room of the night librarians, maybe. But what is it? This month visits the big windows and this is the biggest one I can actually stand in."

The bear walked a while before answering, the long bear walk, the measuring one. "The true answer, Almost, is the gate's answer, and the gate's answer comes at the gate, in its season. But here's the half I can give, and it's a real half: you asked at four if the meadow was yours or the willow's. The answer's this month's whole shape: the meadow is a us.

A third thing. Built by..." The ear flopped; the care was visible. "built by every keeper and every kept, together, since keeping started. The foundation poured before memory, you found that window at the photograph; gear zero; the recognition.

The meadow runs on gear zero, Almost. That's the half. The whole comes at the gate. And the whole is better. Bears don't oversell. You know bears don't oversell."
