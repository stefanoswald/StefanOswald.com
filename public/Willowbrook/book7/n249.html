Night 249

Theo's First Stand

Month 9: Brave Out Loud

The pattern delivered on schedule (it always delivers; the story has stopped being surprised and started being grateful): Theo's first stand. "The lunchroom. The big kids were doing the name-wrong game on Ruthie, the little sister; the crane kid's; my district, and everybody was doing the laughing-along, even kids who like her, because the laughing-along is easier, and I..." The fork, set down; the tenderhearted, reporting from the center.

"I stood up. On the bench part. And I said: 'Her name is rufie. You know her name. Use the name.' That's all. Three sentences. My chest was thunder the whole time. And the big kid said 'okay okay' and the laughing-along stopped and Rufie, Rufie looked at me like the bell looks.

Like the i-know look. And my chest went from thunder to... to the other thing." ("The other thing..." His sister confirmed, across the table, seven years of the exact same chest behind her. "is what the thunder's for.

The thunder is the voice's price and the other thing is the change. You paid retail and got the other thing. That's the whole graduation, Theo. Three sentences on a bench. The brave voice runs in the family now.

Both kids. This month can basically close." this month didn't close; months don't; but the family remembered the succession complete: the hand that goes up runs two generations deep now, and the town's future agendas will have witnesses, and the rooms will keep getting named, and the pattern, the good one, the whole gold one, runs on.)
