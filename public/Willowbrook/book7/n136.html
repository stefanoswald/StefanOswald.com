Night 136

The Answer Shelf Pilgrimage

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

A pilgrimage this week: The answer shelf. (The reference section; discovered at four..." The answers live at the library; they wait for you to come ask. "Revisited at almost-seven, with this month's whole education:) The check, run shelf-wise: "The encyclopedias answered hundreds of my windows over the years, the sky's blue, the yeast, the aquifer, the starlight's age.

The answer shelf is undefeated on the how-questions. And on the big ones, the what-am-i-for, the why-something..." She ran a finger down the big dictionary's spine, fond as family. "the shelf goes quiet. Not failed, quiet.

The books know their territory. That's the shelf's whole wisdom, actually: Knowing which questions are yours. The reference books never once pretended at the unanswerables, no encyclopedia entry for 'The meaning, whole.' The shelf answers what can be answered and stands respectful at the rest.

Same as the town. Same as Mama. Same as everybody good." (And the thank-you, delivered to the shelf directly, by the family law: "Thank you for seven years of territory, books. You knew which questions were yours.

That's the rarest knowing there is." The librarian, shelving nearby, made the list note: "She thanked Reference today. The building has been thanked in all its departments now. The rota should record: we are, as far as the wainscoting knows, the only library in the county that has been comprehensively loved.")
