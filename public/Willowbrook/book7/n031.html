Night 31

The Owl on the Unwitnessed

Month 1: When No One Is Watching

When the final night arrived, Stella watched from over the Great Willow. The integrity month, closing, and Stella posed the recursive one, by the watch's fondness for them: "The small one checked her unwitnessed self all month. So, for the tally book: who watches the watchers? The rounds run unthanked.

The tally book's kept unread. The stars get set with the whole town asleep. Who checks our count?" Oliver Owl, whose own forty-year correction still sat fresh in the tally book, lightening it, took the question like the gift it was: "No one..."

He said. "And that is the design, colleagues, not the flaw. The watch works because no one checks it, because every member does the rounds and keeps the counts and sets the stars on nothing but the inner compass. The moment a watcher watches for the watching-back, the work bends.

Harry's rounds would curve toward the houses that thank. My tally book would flatter. The stars would cluster where the applause is." The spectacles came down. "The small one found the whole of it on a sidewalk with a wallet: the choice made when no one was looking is the actual self.

The watch is simply that vote, taken nightly, by everyone up here, for three hundred years. That's all a watch is. That's all it ever was: the town's inner compass, given wings and a tally book and a hill. Mmm," hummed the Moon, who has never once been thanked by a tide.

Goodnight, unwatched watchers of Willowbrook, and the unwatched watchers below, in kitchens and coops and empty parks, voting their actual names. The count is clean. It was always clean. That's why everything else works. Sleep.
