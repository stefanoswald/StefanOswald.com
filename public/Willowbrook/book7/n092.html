Night 92

The Dark Hallway, Second Generation

Month 4: Theo's Big Sister

His first fear of the season, saved at his lamp: The hallway. (The night walk to the bathroom; the dark between doors; the five-year-old certainty that dark-between is different from dark-in-rooms. It is. The story has always agreed.)

The method, inherited whole and adapted to his order: The weather first ("the scared is allowed. It's a sneeze. You don't obey it, but you don't shame it either. Say hi to it"). Then the furnishing: the hallway walked together, lamp-lit, everything named, "the floor creak by the closet: that's boris.

He creaks at everybody; it's his job. The coat shadow: the coat. Watch, shadows are just things standing in front of light; they got no other powers. The hallway's a room with no furniture, Theo.

So we furnish it: Boris, the coat, the nightlight we're installing this week, and the new rule: The hallway is yours at every hour. You own it. Owners walk their property however they want, fast, slow, or singing." (He walks it singing now.

The story hears him nightly: the hello song, hallway edition, at volume. Ownership, confirmed. Boris, as it happened, creaks along. It's his job.)
