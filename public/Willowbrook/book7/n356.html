Night 356

The Seven Party

Month 12: The Maybe Year

Nearly all of Willowbrook came to Olivia's seventh birthday party.

There were friends from school and the park, elders from the bench, people from the farm and shops, one invited dog, and every person who had helped care for something Olivia would one day hand on.

Each guest brought a story about one thing they were ready to pass down: a skill, a recipe, a book, a tool, or a piece of advice. The exchanges continued all afternoon.

Then Rosa carried out the cake.

Six small night skies circled its edge, one for each birthday before this one. At the center stood a golden willow with five words written along its trunk and one bare branch waiting.

"The seventh sky is the tree," Rosa said. "The years do not disappear. They gather around what has been growing all along."

Seven candles burned in the willow's crown. Olivia blew them out in one breath.

She drew her wish instead of speaking it, as she had every year. Mama glimpsed only a circle with an open gate.

Then the cake was cut, the dog received a star of frosting, and Willowbrook celebrated until gold evening reached the hill.
