Night 337

The Braid: The Farm's Night

Month 12: The Maybe Year

Braid night two, a Saturday, obviously: The farm's thread. (The Saturday of Saturdays, run whole: the coop at dawn, both grandchildren, the count, the granddaughters, one boot sat on at length by the boldest, the dynasty's blessing running generous now. The porch, the catalog, the reading, the arrangement at full fluency. The fence walk.

Sam's posts touched, both kids, the routine established. The spring, the ceremony, Theo presiding: "Thank you for the whole family..." the routine his forever. And the book. Four pages left; the gold-hour reading; the fourth hand's Saturday entry, written with the keeper attending:).

"Saturday. The braid month's farm night. The count: All present. The posts: All standing. The wood ones and the other ones. The arrangement: Continues as long as there's a porch, and the porch isn't going anywhere, and neither are we.

O. Fourth hand. Witnessed: J. T. The hens, the spring, the gold." (And the keeper's addendum, different ink, at the gold hour, both grandchildren watching the pen: "The farm's braid-night entry, for the story: The best crop in fifty-one years drove out this morning and drove home at dusk.

It does that every saturday. The farm counts its blessings in axle-turns. J." The farm counts its blessings in axle-turns. The story adopts the lesson. It's the truest odometer going.)
