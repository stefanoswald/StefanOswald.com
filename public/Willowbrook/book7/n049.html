Night 49

The Question Row's Last Harvest

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

August's last promise paid at the farm this week: The glass gem corn, husked. (The question row's sealed dragon; the catalog's sworn impossibility; the husks drawn back with the whole porch attending) and the kernels caught the light exactly as sworn: stained glass. Rows of it. Ruby, amber, sky, violet, a cathedral window grown from a seed packet and a dare.

"The catalog didn't lie," (Theo, reverent, turning the cob like treasure, which it is; the story rules it so). And this month's reading, delivered over the gemstones: "The corn's the row's whole sermon, Grandpa: the promise stayed sealed all season, husked shut, unpeekable, and the only way to the stained glass was through the whole summer and the ending. The harvest is a ending, the plant's done; the row's done, and look what the ending was holding. Some things only open at the end.

The end is the opening. That's..." (she stopped; the kernels; the light; the gate year, glinting in a corn cob) "...that's the whole year, isn't it. The end is the opening. The tree's got one word left and the gate's got a keyhole and the corn just showed me how it works."
