Night 57

What Endings Cost

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

At the end of the month, Olivia returned to the question she had begun with.

"Why do things end?"

She and Mama sat beneath the reading lamp.

"I found several answers," Olivia said. "A corn plant ends so its seeds can begin. Snow goes away and becomes water. Some endings open a door to the next thing."

She touched the small box where she kept letters, leaves, and pieces of days she did not want to forget.

"But people do not miss a leaf because it has a useful purpose. We miss things because they were real."

Mama waited.

"Maybe missing is part of what real things cost," Olivia continued. "I would rather pay it than have nothing worth missing."

The answer did not make endings fair. It did not need to. It gave Olivia a way to hold grief beside gratitude without asking either one to disappear.

Mama wrapped both arms around her.

"The month can close now," Olivia whispered.

Outside, the last leaf on the maple let go and landed where spring would eventually find it.
