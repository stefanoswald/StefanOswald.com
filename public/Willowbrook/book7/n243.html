Night 243

The Watch Counts the Town Back

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

On the final evening, Stella shone just above the Great Willow. The town month, closing, and the watch, which has heard every count the town ever took, took its own: "The watch's count of the town below," Stella opened, gold banked high, the season's fullness in her. "Entries, colleagues, round the hill:" The owl's ("one library, promise-lit, forty-one initials deep. One almanac, completing.

One kitchen wall, four laws. The town keeps, that's the whole entry. Under every roof, keeping"). Harry's, in from the rounds ("sixty-some rivers, all running. One new wave at Number Sixteen, the route doesn't count the months, but the rounds do: five.

The town mends, that's mine"). The bear's ("two children, one meter, twelve nights. The town hands, everything, always, down and down. That's the bear's whole count, seven years taking it"). And the Moon's, the fullest entry, delivered in its once-a-decade voice, the tide-deep one: "I have watched ten thousand towns.

Most are places. Some, a few, a precious few per century, become what this one became: a practice. Passed hand to hand, porch to porch, kept alive not by the walls but by the walking between them. The small one called it a verb tonight.

She is right, and the watch should log the rest of it: verbs need conjugating, and towns-as-verbs need one thing above all. Somebody in every generation who conjugates on purpose. The town below has had one per generation, unbroken, three hundred years. The wainscoting keeps the list.

The next name on it..." The Moon's glow, warm past all seasonal allowance, over one gold window with a chain of twelve links. "the next name is twelve nights from her gate. The count is complete. The town continues.

It was always going to continue. That is what she made sure of, this year, table by table, without once knowing that was the year's whole work.", banked, brimming, twelve nights high. Goodnight, verb of Willowbrook. The count is complete. The conjugation continues. Sleep. The work turns at dawn.
