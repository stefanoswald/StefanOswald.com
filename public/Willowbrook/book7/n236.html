Night 236

The Bench Faculty Portrait Day

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

The count's art wing: Portrait day. (The exchange's kids painting the bench faculty; the faculty sitting, some stiff, some regal, Gus-Senior with the marble held like a scepter, which it is:) The portraits, hung gallery-style: Mrs. Hollis (seventeen versions; the window seat in most; one, Wren's, just the hands, around a teacup, and the room went quiet at it the way rooms go quiet at the true ones). Petrov (rendered mid-mend by the crane kid, all focus-lines and tool-glint: "the folding kid gets the concentrating..."

By the subject's review). Joe (Theo's, the porch, the rocker, the almanac open, and above the whole scene, carefully lettered: "The book is a who." The subject requested the original. The subject received the original.

The subject, sure enough, hung it in the farm kitchen beside the wedding photo, which is the highest wall in the house). ("The portraits go both ways, Mama, that's the day's whole finding. The kids painted the elders and the elders sat for the kids, and the sitting is a gift too: an hour of holding still while somebody young looks at you hard and tries to get you right. Everybody should get looked at that hard once a year. It's the attention, visible. It's the love, with brushes.")
