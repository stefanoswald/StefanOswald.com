Night 76

The Things We Build Together

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

Once Olivia had the word us, she began seeing it everywhere.

The fort was an us built from blankets, rules, and arguments survived. The community garden was an us built from soil and many hands. The whistle code was an us made of air. The bench group, the kite squadron, the book exchange, and the whole web of Willowbrook were things no one person could own.

"Even the town is an us," Olivia told Lily. "People have been building it for three hundred years, and all of us live inside what they left."

Lily looked toward the Great Willow on the hill. "What about the night watch?"

Olivia had never told Lily everything she suspected about the owl, the star, the moon, and the creatures who gathered there.

"Probably," she said. "The oldest kind."

They sat quietly, looking at the roofs and paths below. Willowbrook did not belong to either of them. It belonged partly to everyone, which meant everyone belonged partly to it.
