Night 52

The Ended Friendship Question

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

This month's honest gap, checked at the window seat: Priya. (Second-Saturday Priya. The hamster-wheel cure. Woven in at five... and drifted since, different class, different orbit, the lunch waves thinning to nods, the nods to nothing.

No fight. No wound. Just... the slow unbraiding nobody chose.) "Did we do something? No. The orbits just... widened. Her new best friend is the chess girl and they fit like crackers matching, and my web grew her directions and.

Lily, is a drifted friendship a failed one?" Lily, braiding, six-years-wise: "The dog book's rule. Some friendships are chapters. Priya was a good chapter, the wheel got cured; the second Saturday happened; the woven-in was real while it wove.

The drift isn't the chapter failing. The drift is the book turning pages. You can love a chapter and not live in it. You can also..." The braid paused; the wisdom aimed. "wave bigger. Nods that used to be waves can be waves again.

Chapters reopen sometimes. Books are like that. You said. You're the one always saying." (The wave, upgraded that week. The nod, surprised into a wave back. Not a re-braiding, orbits are orbits, but a warmth, restored to standing.

"The chapters stay on the shelf..." The notebook ruled. "That's the whole difference between drifted and ended. Shelved isn't gone. Nothing shelved is gone. I got six books that prove it.")
