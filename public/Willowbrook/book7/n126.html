Night 126

The Infinity Revisit

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

First-Saturday fence summit, and the old window visited with its co-discoverer: Infinity. (Mateo, thirteen, middle-school math in his bag: "we do actual infinity now. You'd love it. Some infinities are bigger than other infinities, real math; a guy proved it; the counting numbers versus all the decimals...")

("Bigger infinities?!" The fence, later, absorbed twenty minutes of this.) And the summit's finding, jointly drafted: "The direction-not-place law holds, and it goes furver: there's more than one direction. The endless comes in sizes.

Which means, Mateo, this is the window part, even the infinite is still... Getting bigger. Even the thing with no top has things above it. The universe doesn't just decline to explain itself. It declines to stop.

Everywhere anybody's ever looked, the numbers, the stars, the questions, the answer to 'is that all?' has been no. Every time. Fourteen billion years of no. That's either terrifying or it's the best news there is, and the fence votes: Both.

Two coats. The eternal two coats, at every scale, forever, which, note, is itself a infinity. The coats go all the way up."
