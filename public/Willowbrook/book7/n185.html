Night 185

The Bench Resurrection

Month 7: Making Things Right

List item one, done: The east bench. (The lumber: council surplus, the councilman's file on her now approves supplies by return post. The labor: the apprenticeship plus one first-Saturday Mateo, home from his orbit..." The fence treaty includes bench duty; it's implied.

"The finish: Petrov's shop varnish, the good kind, "benches earn the good kind.") The re-slatting: one Saturday. The result, measured within the week (she measures; the measuring is the love): The east siders returned. (The old ones of the park's east streets, the checkers pair, the pigeon lady, the veteran who likes the morning sun, the whole displaced population, re-benched, their side of the park re-peopled like a spring uncovering.)

("One bench, Mateo. Three slats and varnish, and a whole population came back. The setup of sitting, nobody budgets for it, and it holds up half the town's together. Add it to the knowing: The benches are steady.

All of them. Everywhere. The town runs on places to sit down near each other.")
