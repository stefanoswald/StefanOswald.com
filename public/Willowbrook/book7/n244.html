Night 244

The Loud Month's Plan

Month 9: Brave Out Loud

At the start of the month, "The brave lesson's run seven years, Mama: the scared-plus, the one-step, the commit muscle, the inner compass. The last lesson is the one the whole the path was building to: Brave out loud. The speaking-up kind. The hand that goes up for other people's unfair.

The voice that costs. Because here's the path's whole arc, seen from the top: the early braves were for me, the slide, the deep end, the dark. The middle braves were with people, the duets, the teams, the chains. And the last braves are for people, the witness's job, the umpire's spine, the kindness with a spine that isn't surrender with nice manners.

This month practice the brave voice. The one that spends its comfort on somebody else's dignity. The graduation brave. The town runs on people who got it. Time to get it whole."
