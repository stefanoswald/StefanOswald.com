Night 306

The Account of the Harbors

Month 11: Bobby Bear's New Kid

Account one, read at the lamp: The harbor years. "The needing's first shape, bear: the whole-harbor nights. The baby ones I can't remember, the story keeps those; the crib nights; the first storms. The ones I can: the new-room night at three, when the big bed was a ocean and you were the whole shore.

The fever nights, every one; you ran hot with me; velvet does that; I checked at six and you were warm, and the warm was probably mine reflected but the probably never mattered. The Buttons night. The first-day-of-school eve. The night the carrier was empty at the vet and I cried for a man I never knew and you held the whole confusing weight of crying-for-strangers without once asking me to explain, because harbors don't ask the ships to explain the storms.

They just hold. Seven years of holding, bear. The harbor account: read, and found...found unpayable, obviously. The whole town's tally book law: the unpayable pays downstream. Your downstream's coming, bear. His storms'll be different, tenderhearted storms; they arrive faster and pass louder.

You'll hold them. You were always gonna hold them. That's the job. Account one: settled gold."
