Night 117

The Fear He Beat Alone

Month 4: Theo's Big Sister

This month's graduation exercise, self-administered: The basement. Solo. The keepsake harbor errand. Mama's mention that the spare candles lived down there, and the boy, gone before anyone assigned anything, flashlight from his own kit, eleven steps counted down and up.

His filing, delivered with the casualness of the genuinely arrived: "The basement's just a room. A down one. Wivy furnished it years ago, the furnace is warm-and-loud; the steps are eleven; the harbor's got the candles. I said hi to the furnace.

It said its sound. We're fine." (The sister's face, as it happened, did several things, the furnishing she'd done at four, inherited as setup: the named dark, passed down not as a story but as a working building, rooms she'd made safe still safe a generation later, her flashlight quest now his casual errand. "The furnishing keeps, Mama.

The darks I made friendly stayed friendly, for whoever came next. That's... that's the whole reason to furnish anything. The rooms outlive the scared kid who fixed them. The rooms wait for the next scared kid and they're already fixed.")
