Night 56

The Month's Gift to the Wall

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

This month closed its accounts the family way: The stitching. (The needles; the aunts' trade; the kitchen wall's collection. Kindest true thing first hanging six months strong, gaining its companion:) The candidates, debated at the hoop: "Hold true, let go gold". "The end is the opening".

"Shelved isn't gone". And the winner, chosen because it holds all the others and because Theo voted twice: "Endings are the bottoms of slides." (Stitched. Crooked. Gold thread. Hung beside its elder. The kitchen wall, as it happened, is becoming the family's visible law, the almanac's wisdom, rendered in wool, at kid height, where laws belong.

Guests read it. Guests copy it. The wall franchises like everything here. Two stitchings now. The far pages hold nine. They had seen the whole wall, decades on, and reports only: it runs out of kitchen before it runs out of true.)
