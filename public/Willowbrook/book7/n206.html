Night 206

The Clinic's Succession

Month 7: Making Things Right

The clinic table completed its season the standard way (the standard is hers; the story enjoys that the standard is hers): Succession. The manual, bound for the exchange shelf: "The repair rules: The nine seconds, the interest clock, the i-did and the next-time, the symmetry rule, the triage, the no-discount law, the either-hand part, and the pause before the squeeze. Free for anyone to use. The helpers, retiring from the table, not the trade."

("Retiring from the table, not the trade," the distinction, explained at the lamp: "The trade's in me, the diploma says pre-apprenticed; the trade never retires. But the tables pass. The corner, the whistle, the walk, the clinic, one by one, this whole year, the tables passing to their next hands while my hands... free up. For what, Mama?

The year keeps freeing my hands. For the gate, love..." said Mama, quiet, true-voice, the year's shape said plain at last. "The year's been emptying your hands all along. You don't walk through gates carrying tables.

You walk through carrying what the tables taught." The hands, freeing. The gate, nearing. They counted: forty-some nights.)
