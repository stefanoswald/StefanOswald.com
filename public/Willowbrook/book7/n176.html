Night 176

The Slow Craft Fair

Month 6: Grandpa Joe's Slow Stories

This month's export run: The slow arts table, at the exchange. (Joe presiding, his first town table; the exchange came to him all winter; the spring let him come to it. The wares: the whittled flock, her thin-winged bird, Theo's recumbent herd (the dog joined the cow; a lying-down chicken pends), Joe's fifty years of pocket-birds. The lesson: the knife-law, the grain, the nine-parts-looking.)

The table's run: mobbed. (The town's kids, hungry for the slow arts the way the story has watched them hunger for everything real, the whittling, the entry-craft demo, the waiting built into both. One kid's review, overheard and saved: "it's like the fidget things but it becomes something.") And the table's best trade, struck at closing: Gus-Senior, trading marbles-lore for whittling-lore, two old men and a bench between them, agreeing to co-run the table monthly..."

The slow arts need two chairs anyway. "per Joe. "One to teach. One to say 'well' when the other one's teaching gets long."
