Night 10

The Note on the Somebody Wall

Month 1: When No One Is Watching

The Somebody Wall at school was meant for unsigned kindness. Children posted notes such as SOMEBODY SHARED HER MITTENS and SOMEBODY SAT WITH ME AT LUNCH.

One morning Olivia found a different kind of note:

SOMEBODY THINKS THE NEW KID SMELLS.

She pulled it down before the new student could read it. At lunch, she invited him to sit with her group. Then she carried the crumpled note to Mrs. Vane.

"The wall lets kindness be anonymous," Olivia said. "But it also lets cruelty hide."

Together they added a line beneath the heading: UNSIGNED KINDNESS IS A GIFT. UNSIGNED MEANNESS IS COWARDICE.

Mrs. Vane also spoke privately with the class. No public guessing. No humiliation. The person who had written the note could repair the harm without creating more of it.

The next morning, a new message appeared:

SOMEBODY MADE ROOM AT THE TABLE.

The new student read that one. He smiled.
