Night 358

The Folders and the Frame

Month 12: The Maybe Year

The archive evening (the routine's museum night, run at full depth for the shelf's close): The seven folders, opened entire, floor-spread, the whole drawn record of a childhood toured by its artist and her first curators: The tour's arc, walked folder by folder: the meadow drawn with arms ("I drew what trees felt like before I knew what they looked like; the feeling-size; artists never stop doing that; they just get sneakier"). The green room's inside. The gold squares. The hot thing with its sneeze-lines.

The second grid's rivers. The magnifier of stars. And seven's folder, closed tonight with its last entry, drawn this week: the gate. Gold, patient, keyhole and all, and beside it, not through it, two figures and a bear.

Waiting. Attending. Hands open. ("The folder closes on the waiting, not the walking-through, because the folder's job is the year, and the year's last art is the posture. The walking-through belongs to the next folder.

The first page of eight. The books change shape, everybody, but the folders just keep counting. Folders are like that. Archives are like that. I'm like that.")
