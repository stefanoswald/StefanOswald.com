Night 261

Brave Together

Month 9: Brave Out Loud

At the fort, Olivia taught the children three short lines they could use together:

"That is not funny. Use the right name. New game, everybody in."

One voice could be ignored when a crowd had already begun laughing. Five voices at once could change the direction of the moment.

They practiced the lines when nobody was upset. Theo played the child using a cruel nickname. Lily gave the signal. The group answered together.

"Practicing feels silly," Marcus said.

"That is why we do it now," Olivia replied. "So the words are ready when the real moment does not feel silly."

Twice that season, the children used the chorus on the playground. The first time, the teasing stopped. The second time, several other children joined the final line and started a new game.

Nobody had to decide alone whether to risk speaking first.

Courage was not always one person standing apart. Sometimes it was a promise made on a peaceful day so several voices could arrive together on a hard one.
