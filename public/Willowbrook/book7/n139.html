Night 139

A Question for Theo

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

Olivia had collected questions for years. Some had answers. Some were hers to carry for a lifetime. She wanted to give Theo one that belonged especially to him.

She considered several, but one kept returning: Why can the best things hurt in the middle?

Theo had asked it after a joyful day ended, when being happy about what happened and sad that it was over had seemed terribly unfair.

At his birthday, Olivia planned to write the question on the first link of his new paper chain.

"I will tell him there is no quick answer," she said to Bobby Bear. "He has to keep the question warm and notice what life teaches him. My answer will not be exactly his."

Bobby Bear listened from the pillow.

Olivia folded the paper carefully. Bicycles and sweaters were useful hand-me-downs. A good question was different. It did not arrive used up. It became new in the hands of the next person who carried it.
