Night 125

The God Question

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

This month's largest window, visited with both parents attending, because some windows the whole house stands at: "The God question, Mama. Papa. The web's got three answers minimum: Noor's five appointments, the biggest together; the whole world facing one spot. Ava's whole system, the questions built into the holidays; the youngest asks why is this night different.

Mrs. Hollis's quiet church Sundays. Gus-Senior says the garden's his church. Grandpa says the farm is, 'something keeps the spring running; I keep the farm; we've never been introduced but we work well together.'

And ours is... the lamp? The gloaming? The thanking of everything? What do we believe?" The answer, built by both parents, slow, true-voice: "We believe in the attending, love. You've watched us do it your whole life: the thanking of the carrying things.

The gold hour kept like an appointment. The maybe spoken into the flame. Whether Someone listens, we hold that question open, the way you hold your windows: with respect, without pretending to a certainty we don't have. And here's what we notice, and it's not nothing: every answer in your web, the five prayers, the seder questions, the garden church, the farm's working arrangement, our lamp, every single one is built of attention and gratitude, aimed somewhere bigger than the aimer.

The addresses differ. The mail is the same mail. And the mail, love, seven years of your own saves say it, the mail always matters more than the address."
