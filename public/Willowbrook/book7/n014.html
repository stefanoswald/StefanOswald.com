Night 14

The Overdue Book

Month 1: When No One Is Watching

Olivia kept a library book three days past its due date because she wanted to finish it.

At the desk, the librarian reached toward a little drawer. Everyone in Willowbrook knew that fines sometimes vanished when weather, illness, or money made returning a book difficult.

"The fine machine seems to be working today," Olivia said, placing her nickels on the counter. "The weather was fine. I kept the book because I chose another chapter."

The librarian studied her, then accepted the coins. "A reader after my own heart."

"The drawer should stay available for someone who needs it."

From inside the wainscoting came the faint scratch of Molly Mouse's pencil. Later, a new entry appeared on the keepers' hidden list: OLIVIA. RETURNED A LOVED BOOK, PAID WHAT SHE OWED, UNDERSTOOD WHY MERCY MUST REMAIN MERCY.

Olivia knew nothing of the note. She was already walking home with her next book, determined to finish this one on time.
