Night 354

Seven Years of Maybe

Month 12: The Maybe Year

The morning after the final maybe night, the family spread seven ribboned bundles of old slips across the rug.

They read about preschool, the big slide, moving, school, the flooded gym, the damaged garden, and dozens of worries that had once felt enormous.

Some hopes had come true exactly. Others had changed shape. A few were still waiting.

Theo found his old slip about the big slide.

"I did that years ago," he said.

"It took time when you wrote it," Olivia reminded him.

They did not declare every bad thing secretly good. Some events had remained painful. The maybe had simply stopped fear from writing the ending before the rest of the story arrived.

Olivia tied each year's slips back into its bundle.

"Maybe is not a prediction," she said. "It is a way of leaving the door open."

Seven years of paper covered the rug. None could prove that life always unfolded kindly. Together they proved something gentler and more useful: the first frightened guess was rarely the whole truth.
