Night 36

The Preschool Drive-By

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

Witness four, passed on a Wednesday walk: Miss dana's room. The window. The circle carpet, visible. Strange small people on it, six-year-olds would say tiny people, sitting where she sat, where Theo sat, learning the weather of rooms.

"The preschool-me ended..." She worked it at the fence, Theo alongside. "The room didn't. The room's a pattern too, it does a whole new batch of kids every year, like Bella does wings. And look..."

The window; the carpet; a small hand going up in there, hot-chested probably, brave probably. "the room's running. Right now. Everything it gave me, it's giving them: the jar (still on the shelf. I can see it), the cubbies, the rule of everybody.

My shape ended in that room and the running never stopped. Miss Dana's file probably has new names in it. Good. Saves should fill. Rooms should run. The missing, the missing is real and the missing is the receipt. You only miss the places that ran true while you were in them."
