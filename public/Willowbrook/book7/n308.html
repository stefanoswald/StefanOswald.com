Night 308

The Bear-Craft Lessons Begin

Month 11: Bobby Bear's New Kid

The lesson proper opened this week (the preface given last month; the lessons now, formal, the bear in attendance, "the craft gets taught in front of its subject; bears prefer the transparency; secrets about the bear aren't the bear's way"): Lesson one: The listening. "The bear's first service, Theo: he listens best of everybody. Better than me, better than Mama, because he's got no advice in him. The advice-shaped people always half-listen; they're loading the fix while you talk; you do it, I do it, everybody with hands does it.

The bear's got no hands. The bear is pure ears, in velvet. So the craft's first rule: you give him the whole day, out loud, at the lamp, the good parts and the heavy, and the giving-out-loud is the unloading, and the unloading is the service, and the bear performs it by existing. Try it tonight.

The whole day. He's got room. He's got seven years of my days in him and he's got room. That's the other thing about bears: The room never runs out." (The student tried it that night, his lamp, his day, the bear on loan for the lesson: the whole Tuesday, delivered out loud, good parts and heavy.

And the report, saved at breakfast, wide-eyed: "The day got lighter, wivy. The bear took the heavy half and I didn't even hand it, it just... Went. The unloading dock, Theo. The first service.

He's been running it since before either of us. Welcome to the craft. Lesson two Thursday.")
