Night 53

The Last Leaf Watch

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

This month closed its fieldwork at the source: The last leaf watch. (The willow, gold gone, branches bare, except one. One leaf, high, stubborn, watched for days by two kids with a thermos and the family's whole patience training.) The vigil's talk, leaf-ward: "Some leaves hold longest.

Fourth held longest and flew farfest, the nest rule; maybe leaves run it too. Or maybe it's the tree keeping one. A bookmark. The tree's got one word left to write, maybe the leaf's the placeholder.

Trees do everything for reasons; the reasons are just slow. Theo. Watch. Watch..." And the leaf let go, not torn; released; the difference visible even at distance, and rode the wind's whole length of the hill, gold to the last, and landed... on the gate-path.

The meadow side. (the story states it plainly and offers, per policy, no explanation. The kids looked at each other. Some punctuation the year does itself.) "The tree just showed us..." She said quietly. "The whole month, in one leaf: hold true, let go gold, land where the next thing starts."
