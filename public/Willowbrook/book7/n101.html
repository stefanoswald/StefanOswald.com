Night 101

Theo and the Deep-End Rope

Month 4: Theo's Big Sister

Theo's swimming teacher thought he might cross the deep-end rope in spring.

The idea made his stomach hurt.

At the pool, Olivia sat beside him with their feet in the shallow water. "You do not cross because you feel brave," she said. "You cross after your skills do."

They named them together: float, kick, swim, blow bubbles, find the wall.

"The water does not know how old you are," Olivia said. "It only knows what your body can do."

At home Theo practiced the list while wearing a towel as a cape. He floated on the rug, kicked across the sofa, and grabbed an imaginary wall.

When April came, he stood at the rope and said each skill aloud. Then he followed them into the deep water.

He swam to the wall without touching the bottom. His family's cheer reached the parking lot.

Later Theo admitted, "The deep end was mostly just more water."

Olivia nodded. "That was my secret too."

The rope had looked like a test of courage. It was really a place to bring everything he had practiced.
