Night 20

The Diary's Second Lock

Month 1: When No One Is Watching

A quiet case, witnessed by accident: Theo, dusting the nightstand (his self-assigned Tuesday "helping the room" round, the boy runs rounds now; Harry's trainee has a trainee, arguably), came to the private volume. The lock. The gold-jacketed keeper of the two-a.m. Pages.

Someone saw him look at it. Five years old. Curious as a whole species. The key: Right there, ribboned to the lamp. Nobody watching (the story doesn't count; the story never counts). He dusted around it.

Moved it gently, dusted under, set it back angled exactly as found, and the story heard him tell the cow, later, in couch-office confidence: "The lock means the lid. Wivy says some truths are for saying once, to paper. You don't open other people's lids. Even when the key's right there. Especially then. That's the whole rule of keys."
