Night 77

The Friend Month's Gift

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

This month's machinery went public, by the eternal law (the kit only keeps what it gives): a table at the exchange. "The parts of friendship: Six gears, all ages." The table, packed both directions (kids and elders, friendship being the one lesson with no graduates): the parts, taught ladder-style. The Hollis new rule, included with attribution.

The us-law, delivered with the stitching displayed. And the table's best moment, unscripted: an elder, the mill man's widow, quiet all season, saying, slowly, "I stopped building usses when Harold died. I thought the pattern was... his." And the six-year-old teacher, kneeling by her chair: "The us with Harold is shelved, not gone, shelved isn't gone; I got six books that prove it.

And the pattern was never his or yours. The pattern is everybody's. Part one is just showing up small. The bench does Tuesdays. No big thing. Just Tuesdays." (She came Tuesday. Sure enough: she came every Tuesday after, and taught the mending table by spring.

Harold's trade, her hands, the us rebuilt one gear at a time. The pattern works at every age. That was always its best feature.)
