Night 238

The Town Song Premieres

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

The town song premiered this week (the exchange; the assembly; her at the piano, the Gentle Brook's chords rebuilt; Papa on standby for the hard bridge; the lyric sheets copied at the library, wrong note annotated): The performance: forty-some voices, elders and kids, the verses landing verse by verse (the water's first draft; the bread's always-sound; the gone ones' wing, sung gentle, the room's eyes doing what rooms' eyes do at the gentle verses). And measure twelve: the wrong note, hit by forty voices together, on purpose, gloriously, the whole town flubbing in unison and grinning through it, exactly as scored, and they remembered the sound with its treasures: a community, hitting its crooked tooth together, claiming it together, keeping going together. The dynasty's whole law, sung by a town. ("They asked for the wrong note twice more at the end, Mama.

The encore was just measure twelve, three times, louder each one. The town likes its crooked tooth. The town is its crooked tooth, all the claimed flubs, harmonized. That's the song working. That's the song's whole job.")
