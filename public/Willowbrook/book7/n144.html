Night 144

The Last Window Visit

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

This month's last visit, made at the meadow, toward the gate, the bear alongside: The gate window. ("What's behind it. The year's biggest unanswerable, except it's not unanswerable. It's unanswered-yet. This month should file the difference before it closes:") "The unanswerables are lifetime cargo, the what-is-all-this, the why-something.

They stay open because open is their answer. But the gate's a yet, the rope, the wall, the fifth branch: a door with a season. And this month taught me the posture for both: the unanswerables get carried. The yets get trusted.

I don't need to know what's behind the gate, Bear. I need to keep the key warm and the meter charged and show up at the season, same as every yet since the little slide. The not-knowing isn't a gap in the plan. The not-knowing is the plan.

The corn stayed husked all summer. The stained glass was in there the whole time. Some things only open at the end, and the end is the opening, and the opening is..." She looked at the gate, gold in the dream-light, patient as everything. "eighty-some nights out.

I can wait. The tree taught the waiting. You taught the trusting. The whole shelf taught the showing-up. I'm the readiest not-knower in dream-country, Bear. File it." ("Saved..." said the bear, velvet-solemn.

"The readiest not-knower in dream-country. The gate's read the meter, Almost. The gate concurs.")
