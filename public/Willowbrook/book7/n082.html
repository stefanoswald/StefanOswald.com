Night 82

The Friend You Outgrow the Fight With

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

The saves' conversion case, retold at the court for this month's record: Wes. (The stunt-kite rival of the kite summer; the green feeling that didn't drive; the bridle-check that turned a rival into a squadmate in one afternoon.) The mechanism, extracted: "The rivalry was a us too, Mateo, that's the part nobody sees. A rival's somebody you're building a third thing with: The contest.

It's got attendance, truth (the chalk doesn't lie), even quiet. The rivalry's just a us with the laughing gear missing, and the bridle-check added it. One offered fix, and the whole machine re-shifted from contest-us to squadron-us. Same gears.

Same people. Different... Tune. So rivals are friends with a gear out..." said Mateo, fence-wise, thirteen. "Rivals are friends the contest hasn't introduced yet. The marigolds and the carrots. Half of every deadlock, every rivalry, is two good ways of working awaiting introduction. Offer the bridle-check. The introduction's almost always one fix away."
