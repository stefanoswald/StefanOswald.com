Night 75

The Friendship Sentence

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

At the lamp, Olivia read her six discoveries to Mama.

"A friend shows up, tells the truth with care, makes room for difference, repairs what gets hurt, shares the quiet, and laughs with you."

"That is a good list," Mama said. "Can you make it smaller without losing it?"

Olivia thought of Lily, the fort, the whistle code, the window seat, and all the things that had not belonged to either girl alone.

"A friend is someone you build an us with."

The us was not Olivia plus Lily written side by side. It was the third thing they had made together: a place of habits, memories, trust, and jokes where both of them could live.

That weekend Olivia stitched the sentence in gold thread. The letters leaned and the word friend nearly ran off the cloth, but it held.

Lily signed the back before they hung it on the wall.

The oldest friend helped hang the newest sentence. That felt exactly right.
