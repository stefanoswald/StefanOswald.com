Night 38

The Hard Witness

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

This month's hard session, because the question, honestly asked, has a hard floor, and she went there on purpose: "Mama. The patterns continue. The services get second introductions. The runnings run. But the old man with the empty carrier, his cat's shape ended and the cat was the shape he loved.

The pattern-comfort is true and it's not enough. I knew it at five in that waiting room and I know it now. The Duchess is distributed and I still want the duchess, the boot-sitting one, the exact one. This month can't end on the pattern answer.

The pattern answer is the tree's book. I'm asking about the leaf's." And Mama held it whole, true-voice, the hardest one: "Then here's the leaf's book, love, best as anyone knows it: the shape ends and it isn't enough and the wanting-the-exact-one never fully stops. That's not a flaw in the loving.

That is the loving, continued, with nowhere to put its hands. And what the leaf's book teaches that the tree's book can't: Because shapes end, the shapes matter. The boot's eleven minutes mattered, infinitely, precisely because they weren't infinite. Endless things can wait forever to be loved.

Ending things can't. The ending is what makes the loving urgent. Nobody would kiss anyone goodnight in a world without nights."
