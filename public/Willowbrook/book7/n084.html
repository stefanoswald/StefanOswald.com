Night 84

The Three-Legged Race

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

The winter carnival's three-legged race supplied this month's physics demo: Tied to dana. (The draw's doing, the careful one, the rule-checker, the fort's slowest conversion. The pairing the old saves would have rated: oil, water.) The race, run as friendship-in-a-lab: the first steps.

The fall (mandatory; all three-legged pairs fall; it's the format's whole tuition). And the fix, mid-snow, tied at the ankle: "Dana. The tie means neither approach wins, the race only runs on a third approach we build right now. Count it: Your check on the odd steps, my go on the evens.

Inside-outside-inside..." (The rhythm, found. The third thing, built at speed, out of necessity, in public, laughing, all six gears engaged in forty yards of snow.) (Third place. Nobody cared. "The race was the whole machine in one rope, Mama: you can't run tied, you can only build a us and let the us run.

Every free-legged pair that ever won figured out the same thing. The losers all tried to drag the other leg.")
