Night 330

Goodbye to Bobby Bear

Month 11: Bobby Bear's New Kid

Bobby Bear had belonged to more than Olivia's bed. He had attended sleepovers, fort meetings, window-seat talks, and nearly every hard moment in the web of friends.

Lily sat with him between the two girls and remembered the failed sleepover, the whistle code, and all the treaties he had silently witnessed.

Wren drew him at the center of a web whose threads reached every friend.

Noor drew small waves beneath his paws. "He crossed every difficult place with you," she said. "Bears can be boats."

June dedicated a page of her bug guide to creatures made of wool who never needed identification.

Mateo gave his farewell at the fence. "Seven years, no crashes, one pilot delivered safely. Best flight record in Willowbrook."

Bobby Bear received every goodbye with his usual grave expression.

They were not saying he would vanish. They were thanking him for the shape of the years that were ending, and welcoming the new work waiting down the hall.
