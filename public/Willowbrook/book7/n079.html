Night 79

The Old Photograph

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

A pilgrimage this week, Rosa presiding: The photograph. (The original. Framed at the bakery, back office, between the first dollar and the flour-dusted family: two bald babies, eleven days apart, holding hands in a music class neither remembers. The founding document.

Un-posed, the mothers swear it across two families and seven years: the babies just did that.) The examination, done by its subjects: "We'd known each other eleven days..." said Lily. "You can't build a us in eleven days..." said the pattern's author. "the parts take years, attendance, repairs, the quiet rings... Unless. Unless the pattern's got a gear zero nobody engineers: the recognition. The thing the babies did. The thing the crackers did with Rosa-No-Relation.

The thing the bench did with Noor's first map. Some usses don't get built from scratch, Lily, they get found, already started, like the us was waiting for its people to arrive. And then the six gears build the house, but the foundation..." She looked at the photograph a long time.

"The foundation was poured before the memory started. By who? Saved: The unanswerables. The best window this month ever opened."
