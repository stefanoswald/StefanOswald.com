Night 8

The Word on the Wall

Month 1: When No One Is Watching

On Mrs. Vane's first spelling test, Olivia reached the word neighbor and could not remember whether the vowels went ei or ie.

Then she saw the answer on a classroom chart that Mrs. Vane had forgotten to cover.

Several children glanced up. Olivia lowered her eyes. She tried the rhyme in her head and wrote her best guess.

After the papers were collected, she raised her hand. "The answer to neighbor was on the wall."

Mrs. Vane turned, saw the chart, and sighed. "Then that word will not count for anyone. Thank you for telling me."

Olivia had spelled it correctly, but that was no longer the important part.

At bedtime she explained, "If I copied it, the mark might say I knew the word when I didn't. A test is supposed to tell me what I know."

"And now?" Mama asked.

"Now I know the word and something about myself."

She spelled neighbor aloud once more before the lamp went out.
