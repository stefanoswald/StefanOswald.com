Night 195

The Town Repair Day

Month 7: Making Things Right

This month scaled, per its author's physics (everything scales; the button doesn't care how tall you are): Town repair day. (The list, published at the bakery window: the squeaky gates (eleven, from the old latch count, "the count finally gets its day"), the library's sticking door, the park fence's lean, the wagon fleet's annual maintenance, the mitten tree's post re-set. The turnout: forty-some hands, tools carried, Petrov conducting from a lawn chair, the teacher retired posture; the trade multiplied past him this day and his face, they remembered, was the richest thing at the whole event.) The day's yield: eleven gates, silenced.

One door, planed. One fence, trued. And the intangible, banked: the town, discovering again (it discovers this annually now; the discovering is the tradition) that the fixing is a festival when it's shared, "the barn-raising thing, Lily. The old ones knew: the work is the party if the whole town holds the hammer.

We didn't invent it. We just... Un-forgot it. Half the craft of towns is un-forgetting."
