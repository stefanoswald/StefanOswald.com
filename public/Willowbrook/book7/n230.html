Night 230

The HERE Ceremony

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

The count reached its own foundation this week: The family's here. (The origin, told complete at the family's own porch, the one they chose, the year before her, the young couple with one table for two:) Papa's telling: "We saw eleven houses. Ten were fine. This one, your mama stood on this porch, looked at the hill with the willow, and said...

I said: Oh. Just: oh. The whole decision in one syllable..." said Mama, the four-sentence style, as it happened, marries into this family too. "And the agent said the house needed work, and the inspector said the roof had years of trouble coming, he was right; you were there for the roof's trouble, and we stood on this porch and did the arithmetic every here ever runs on: not 'is it perfect' but 'is it ours.'

It was ours before we signed anything. Some usses get found, already started. You wrote that down once about friendship, kiddo. It's how houses work too. It's how the whole town got built, three hundred years of people standing on unbuilt porches saying oh."

(The ceremony, by the family's forms: the doorframe touched, all four hands, formal. The here, re-said, out loud, together, a renewal of vows the house accepted with its usual creak, which Boris the hallway board seconded, in his office. The count's foundation: laid last, held oldest. Correct order. The foundations always come out last in the digging and first in the holding.)
