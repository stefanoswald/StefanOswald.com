Night 242

The Town Month, Counted

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

Month's end. At month's end, On paper, they could list one count. One founding walk. One bakery century (the trade knows before the tradesman). One bucket line (the treasure was the shelf). One guard's million.

One grid genealogy (every river traces to a rescued winter; the aquifer's bottomless). One gone-ones' wing (the retelling is the rescue). One town song. One newcomer's guide. One reversal (She counted us. So we counted her).

One history coup. One here, re-said. No list could quite hold that the tally book doesn't balance backward and the debt is the engine. That the kindness is just weather that people do. That the best things are for standing under.

That the town made her and got made by whoever it made, circles, gold ones, all the way out. "Good town-month? The fullest tally book ever kept. And Mama, this month's last line, found on the gloaming walk: I spent seven years thinking I was the kid and the town was the place.

The count says it plainer: the town is a thing people do, three hundred years of doing it, and I'm not in the town. I'm a verb of it. So's everybody. The town is everybody, town-ing, together, daily.

And the gate, the word, the whole final stories coming..." Twelve links; the window; the hill out there in the dark, sure as ever. "whatever's next, it happens to a verb, not a noun. Verbs don't stop at gates. Verbs go through.

That's the whole grammar of everything, right there." Remembered. Verbs go through. Twelve nights. On we go, town-ing, all the way.
