Night 211

The Mending Month, Counted

Month 7: Making Things Right

Month's end. At month's end, The month's visible changes were one apprenticeship. One chair. One bench. One inherited feud. Two mamas. One door at Sixteen (a wave, returned, month five, one wave big; counts entire).

One audience-repair. One kintsugi bowl (biography that holds dough). One declined mend (either hand can stay in its pocket; the no is a whole answer). One town repair day. One toothpaste demonstration. One clinic succession.

One fourth law, stitched. Other changes resisted numbers: that the thing tells you. That the windows matter, urgency wearing patience's clothes. That maintenance is love conjugated forward. That the year is emptying her hands, deliberately, table by table, for the gate that doesn't take luggage.

"Good mending-month? The goldest-seamed one. And Mama, this month's last line, from the whole trade: I thought the mending was about the broken things. It's not. The broken things are the occasions. The mending is about the menders, every repair I ran this month built the repairer more than the repaired.

The town doesn't need its things fixed, not really. The town needs its people to be the kind who fix, and the fixing is how the people get made. The benches make the menders make the benches. Circles.

Gold ones. All the way out. Same as everything. Always same as everything." Remembered. The benches make the menders make the benches. Thirty-some nights. On we go, hands free, kit carried, gold all the way out.
