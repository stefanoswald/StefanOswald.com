Night 202

The Treaty Drawer

Month 7: Making Things Right

Every spring, Olivia and Lily emptied the drawer that held seven years of agreements.

They checked the whistle code first. All nine signals still meant what they should, and the one long note for fear remained reserved for real need.

The wave code now belonged mostly to younger children, who used it between bedroom windows. The just-listening agreement had been needed four times that year and worked in both directions. The Sunday rest rule remained untouched.

One old safety plan could retire. Olivia no longer needed to rehearse what to do if she became lost at the market, but Theo did. She taught him the three steps, stand still, call loudly, find a safe helper, until they became boring enough to remember when frightened.

Lily tied the old papers with fresh ribbon.

"Agreements are a little like gardens," Olivia said. "You have to visit them. Some keep growing. Some finish their work. Some make seeds for someone else."

They returned the bundle to the drawer, lighter by one page and ready for another year.
