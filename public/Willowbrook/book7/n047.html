Night 47

The Ending That Was a Door

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

News from Room 6, delivered at the bench with ceremony: Owen handed the corner on. (His year done; the Tuesdays passed to, the story savors this. Milo. The elbow kid. The leaner. The spot-guard. Grown tall enough to hold the chair he once leaned at.)

The chain, drawn in the notebook, visible whole for the first time: "The librarian → me → Milo-as-leaner → me-handing-to-Owen → Owen → milo-as-keeper. The corner's on its third keeper and its first returning one, the leaner became the guard became the keeper, the whole arc in one kid, and Mama, none of it happens if any handing gets hoarded. Every keeper let go on time. That's this month's civics: The endings are the doors.

The post survives you or you hoarded it, and a post that survives free handings isn't a post anymore. It's a institution. The corner will outlive all of us now. That was the whole point of every Tuesday I ever ran it."
