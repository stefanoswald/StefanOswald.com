Night 221

The Gone Ones' Wing

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

One section of Olivia's count was for the ones who were gone.

She wrote Margaret, who had listened to the wind and helped make the family that made Olivia. She wrote Samuel, Grandpa Joe's brother, whose name had taken too long to be spoken aloud. She wrote Wendell and remembered the whistle now carried in a gentler pocket.

The Duchess earned a line of her own: colleague, alarm-raiser, ancestor of hens who still considered boots to be chairs. Buttons the hamster belonged there too. He had been Olivia's first small lesson in grief.

At the bottom she added E. W. Edith Winslow, the child who had drawn Olivia's willow ninety years earlier. Edith had proved that a house could remember its children.

Mama read the page over Olivia's shoulder.

"It isn't a very sad list," Olivia said.

"No," Mama agreed. "It is a very loved list."

The names did not become less gone when Olivia wrote them. They became more present. For that evening, the difference mattered.
