Night 187

The Mama Feud

Month 7: Making Things Right

List item four, approached sideways, because grown-up feuds eat frontal approaches: The two mamas. (The parking thing; fourteen months; two houses, four kids, one street paying the silence-toll.) The sideways: the kids. (The four kids of the two houses, still friends; kids don't honor parking-based borders, recruited into one project: a joint lemonade stand, positioned with cartographic malice exactly between the two driveways.

The mamas, drawn out by kid-logistics, cups from one kitchen, lemons from the other, the stand needing both porches, and stationed, by the geometry of supervision, twenty feet apart, watching their kids run one till together...) (...and they remembered what the street saved: minute forty, one mama laughed at something a kid did. Minute forty-one, the other one did. Minute fifty: "...this is ridiculous, Dee. It's been ridiculous, Marta."

The parking thing, dissolved in lemonade, fourteen months late. The kids collected their job in cookies from both kitchens, separately, which the strategist ruled "correct, the diplomats get paid at both courts.")
