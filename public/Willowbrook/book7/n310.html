Night 310

Lesson Two: The Shapes of Needing

Month 11: Bobby Bear's New Kid

Lesson two, Thursday, by the syllabus: The shapes of needing. ("The craft's second rule, Theo, and it's the one the grown-ups get wrong about bears: The needing changes shape and every shape counts. The catalog, from seven years:") "The harbor shape (the whole-night holding; the fever runs; you'll start here, five-year-olds mostly do). The colleague shape (the pillow presence; the quiet company; needed different, loved identical.

I wrote that at six and it's the craft's whole motto). The witness shape. The ceremony shape (the wish counsels; the interviews; the bear attends the institutions, he's the institution). And the same-as-ever shape, the nights when nothing's wrong and you hold him anyway, because the holding was never about the wrong.

The grown-ups think bears are for babies because they only count the harbor shape. The craft knows: the bear holds every shape for the whole life of the needing, and the needing never ends, Theo. It just changes address. Someday your needing lives mostly in people, the web, the lamp, the porch.

The bear's shape then is the keeping shape: he holds the whole history of every shape that came before. That's why bears get handed and never frown away. They're not toys, Theo. They're archives. With ears. In velvet."
