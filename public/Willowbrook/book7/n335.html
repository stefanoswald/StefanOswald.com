Night 335

The Gold Month Opens

Month 12: The Maybe Year

The gold month opened the way the whole shelf taught it to: At the table, all four, the routine posted. ("The last month of the first shelf, everybody. The braid month, the year's whole design says so: every thread of seven years comes home this month. The people, the places, the watch, the words.

The candle at 353, and this year the story gets told whole; the original; the founding text, complete, for the first time. The word at 361. The gate after. The handing at 364. And the last page at 365, where the bear says the books change shape.

So this month's plan is one line, the only line it ever needed: Attend everything. Open hands. Care. No skipping the end.") (And Papa, from the table's end, adding the family's new rule: "One more part, kiddo, from the grown-ups: this month attends you back.

Seven years you've run the attending. This month the whole apparatus, the family, the town, the hill, whatever keeps the story, runs it at you. Receive it. Both hands. Show the landing. You taught us all how."

Both hands. The landing. This month opened gold and stayed gold, the story attests, every single night. It was always going to. The whole shelf was the loading.)
