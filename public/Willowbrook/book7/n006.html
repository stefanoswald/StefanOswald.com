Night 6

The Honest Reading Log

Month 1: When No One Is Watching

Second grade brought Olivia a weekly reading log. Mrs. Vane did not ask for a parent's signature.

"I trust you to write the true number," she told the class.

Most nights Olivia read far longer than the required twenty minutes. On Thursday, however, the best chapter ended late and bedtime arrived after only twelve minutes of reading.

The next morning, several classmates wrote twenty anyway.

"Nobody checks," one boy whispered.

Olivia's pencil hovered over the box. Writing twenty would not earn her anything she had not usually done. It would also make the log prettier.

She wrote 12.

At home she showed Mama. "The short number looks as if I failed."

"Did you?"

"No. I read for twelve minutes. That is what happened."

The next week held three long numbers and one more short one. Olivia began to like the uneven row. It was not a picture of a perfect reader. It was a true picture of her reading life.
