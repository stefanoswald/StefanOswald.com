Night 234

The Fifty-Year Box

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

Olivia asked the town council to bury a time capsule in the community garden.

"It should be opened in fifty years," she said. "And children should choose what goes inside. We are the people most likely to be here."

The council agreed.

The young committee packed the first Willowbrook guide, the town song, a copy of the garden's first annual book, a dried sorry-flower with its story, photographs from the school exhibition, and one whistle from the old signal code.

Olivia added a letter:

DEAR PEOPLE OF FIFTY YEARS FROM NOW,

IS THE LIBRARY LANTERN STILL LIT? DOES THE PARK BENCH STILL COLLECT STORIES? DOES THE GARDEN STILL FEED PEOPLE? THE DETAILS MAY HAVE CHANGED. PLEASE ADD YOUR OWN, THEN BURY THE BOX FOR THE NEXT FIFTY YEARS.

The capsule went beneath a stone at the garden's corner. Children patted down the soil. None of them could picture fifty years clearly, but they could leave a careful hello there.
