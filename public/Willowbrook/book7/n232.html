Night 232

The Month's Quiet Coda

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

This month's coda, walked alone-ish at gloaming (Mama at the corner, by the old rules; some walks want the alone and the watched-over both; this family engineers for both): The town at gold hour, received whole: the counter's late light. The lantern. The corner. The garden's string lights.

The windows, lighting one by one, the lamps and lighthouses; everybody's light somebody's landmark, and her own house's gold square at the lane's end, with the chain in its window, down to its last dozen links. (No notebook entry was made on the walk. They remembered what she told the lamp instead, after: "I didn't count anything, Mama. First time all month.

I just... Received it. The whole count, walking past me in windows. The counting was this month's job and this month's done, and under the counting, the whole time, was this: the town at gloaming, being exactly what three hundred years of here built.

You don't check a gift while it's being handed to you. Sometimes the gratitude is just... standing in it. The standing-under. The oldest posture I got. The stars taught it. The town confirms it. Some things are for counting. The best things are for standing under.")
