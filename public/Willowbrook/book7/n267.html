Night 267

The Voice and the Vote

Month 9: Brave Out Loud

An institutional defense this week: The rotation under siege. (The class election shape, votes pick the order, not the only; everybody leads; the week-three kid's shoulders, years of them now, challenged by a new fast-loud kid with a platform: "Real elections have winners. The rotation's babyish. Vote it out.")

The defense, made on the floor, against a popular loud: "The rotation's not babyish, it's engineered, and here's its birth certificate, because laws keep their whys attached: the straight-vote system ran here once. Same winners every time. The not-winners stopped voting-caring by November. I watched it; I got saves.

The rotation didn't kill the winning; it killed the giving-up. Every kid in this room has led a line this year, check your own memory of week nine..." The room checked; the week-nine kid, a quiet one, sat taller. "and the leading practice is the whole point of a class job. Real elections have winners, sure.

Real classrooms have twenty-two kids who all need the practice. Different jars. Vote for the jar that grows everybody. That's my whole speech." (The rotation held, 17-5. The fast-loud kid, as it happened, led the line in week fourteen, by the rotation he'd opposed, and led it well, and said nothing, and they remembered the nothing as the way things worked working: the shape converts even its opponents. Good shape always does. That's why you build it instead of winning arguments.)
