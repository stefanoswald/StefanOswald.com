Night 46

The Halloween of Endings

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

Halloween, seventh edition, and the costume, in her tradition of costumes-as-forecasts, came straight from this month: She went as the willow. (Brown and gray layers; gold streamers for the branches, twelve, repurposed from the kite's spare tails; and pinned up the trunk, in gold letters: Home. Family. Growing.

Kind. True. And one blank streamer at the top: "the last word. Coming in spring. The costume's honest, even about what it doesn't know yet.") The night's route (Theo: a mender, tool belt and all, Petrov's own retired apron cut down, the shop outfitted him personally; the trades claim their own): the classics.

Hollis early (costume review: twenty minutes; the willow got the full forty-year assessment: "the blank streamer, child. The blank streamer is the best costume element I have seen since a boy came as his own report card in 1979"). Rosa's full-size. Number Nine, Number Five, the gap at Number Eleven.

And the hill itself, last stop, the willow saluted by the willow: "I wore you. Hope that's okay. The blank one's yours to fill."
