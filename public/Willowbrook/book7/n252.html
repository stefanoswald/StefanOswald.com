Night 252

The Apology Demanded and Declined

Month 9: Brave Out Loud

This month's stress-test arrived by phone (the counter correction traveled; corrections do): a grown-up, displeased. (One of the huddle; a neighbor of standing; the call to Mama: the child was "disrespectful, correcting adults in public; she should apologize...") The family council, gathered at the table; the case reviewed against the whole seven-year law: the correction's content. Its manner.

Its venue (the symmetry rule: the rumor ran at the counter; the correction ran at the counter; matched). The answer, family-unanimous, delivered by the one in trouble herself for the return call: "I'm sorry the correction embarrassed, that's real, and the sorry-for-the-feeling is yours, whole. The correction itself stands. The truth isn't mine to apologize for.

I learned that umpiring, and it holds off the court too. And the 'kids shouldn't correct adults' part, the town's record can't run on a rule where the truth checks the speaker's height first. The chalk doesn't care how tall you are. The button doesn't. The truth doesn't. That's the whole town, everybody. That's the whole everything."
