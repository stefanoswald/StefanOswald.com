Night 347

The Remembering Game

Month 12: The Maybe Year

For the seventh remembering game, the family lit a candle and placed the recorder in the middle of the table.

They passed seven years of stories around like bread.

Mama told the May snow. Papa remembered the spaghetti helmet. Olivia told the first maybe, the garden rising from weeds, the deep-end rope, the difficult Duchess, and the wave from Number Sixteen.

Theo remembered more than anyone expected. He corrected the week when a paper bird had joined the sky puzzle and was proven right by the old notebook.

They spoke the names of Samuel and Margaret. They laughed about first words, failed plans, broken things, and repairs.

When the candle burned low, Olivia looked around the table.

"Whatever is behind the dream gate, this is what it opens onto," she said. "These people. These voices."

Years later, the recording would still sound like chairs scraping, people interrupting, and someone laughing too close to the microphone.

That was why it mattered. It had not preserved a perfect family story. It had preserved the family telling it together.
