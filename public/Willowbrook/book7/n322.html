Night 322

One More Ordinary Bedtime

Month 11: Bobby Bear's New Kid

For the last regular night of Bobby Bear's old place on the pillow, Olivia did nothing special.

She took a bath, put on her pajamas, and chose one chapter. Mama gave tomorrow's forecast: gold all evening. Olivia tucked Bobby Bear into the hollow her arm had made beside him over seven years.

No speech had been planned. No ceremony waited.

At the edge of sleep, Olivia whispered, "Two thousand and something nights. Thank you for every one."

Bobby Bear said nothing, which was the language he had always used best.

The lamp clicked off. Moonlight crossed the floor. Olivia fell asleep easily.

Years later, she would remember some of the grand farewells in perfect detail. She would not remember the chapter from this night or what pajamas she wore. She would remember the feeling: an ordinary bedtime made precious because it was nearly the last of its kind.
