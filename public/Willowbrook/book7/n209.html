Night 209

The Mend-Ahead Discovery

Month 7: Making Things Right

This month's last craft-finding, arrived at the campaign board (the family's whole maintenance calendar visible at once, the fleet checks, the treaty gardening, the body's meetings, the porch Saturdays): The mend-ahead. "Mama. Look at the board with this month's eyes. Half of it isn't repair, it's pre-repair.

The fleet maintenance before the festival. The treaty check before the rust. The Saturdays before the... the whole board is mending things that haven't broken. And the trade's got a boring name for it, maintenance, but this month found its real name: maintenance is the future tense of mending, and mending is the past tense of love, so maintenance is... love, conjugated forward.

The oil change is a love letter. The treaty check is a love letter. The Saturday drive to the farm is the biggest love letter on the whole board, mailed weekly, postmarked in advance. The best menders barely mend at all, Mama. They just love things on schedule."
