Night 93

The Reading Wall

Month 4: Theo's Big Sister

His hill of the season: The reading wall. The map, drawn in his order, weather first: "First, the feelings forecast: the wall makes you feel dumb. That feeling is wrong but it's real, so we name it and don't obey it, everybody's wall makes them feel dumb; mine did; Marcus's did; the wall is a liar about dumbness; it's just a wall. Second, the map: the flow comes from miles, not from trying harder, you read easy books, lots, below your level on purpose, and one day, the dinner test, the words run while you think about trucks.

Dird: the schedule's yours. The dahlia rule. September always comes. Your September might be March. The wall doesn't get a vote on the calendar. Only the miles vote." (The miles accumulated. Easy books by the stack, the corner's whole low shelf, zero shame attached, the coach reads easy books alongside, deliberately..."

Because miles are miles and company is company. "The flow, later, arrived in February, mid-truck-book, mid-thought, exactly as mapped. The yell reached the kitchen. The kitchen was ready.)
