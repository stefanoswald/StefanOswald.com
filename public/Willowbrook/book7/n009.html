Night 9

The Cracked Egg

Month 1: When No One Is Watching

Rain turned the farmyard to mud on Saturday. Olivia collected fourteen eggs, then slipped beside the coop and felt one crack beneath her boot.

She could have written thirteen in the tally book. Grandpa Joe had not watched the count, and no one would miss an egg they never knew existed.

Instead she wrote:

14 LAID. 13 WHOLE. 1 CRACKED. MY BOOT.

At lunch, Joe read the entry and tapped the final line.

"Do you know what makes this old book useful?"

"The egg counts?"

"The bad counts. Any hand can write down a good day. A book earns trust when somebody records the muddy ones too."

He used the cracked egg in a batch of cornbread, so nothing was wasted. Olivia ate her slice on the porch while rain ticked on the roof.

The honest line had cost one egg. It had kept fifty years of farm records worth believing.
