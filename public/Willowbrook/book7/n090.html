Night 90

The Watch's Own Gears

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

On the final evening, Stella's light touched the willow's highest branches. The friend month, closing, and Stella ran the small one's machine on the watch itself, gear by gear, aloud: "Attendance: three hundred years of nightly sessions; the rounds; the rota; the stars set on schedule. Truth-carriage: the tally book's forty-year correction, held and forgiven. Room-for-different: a moon, an owl, a hedgehog, a mouse, a bear, a butterfly-line, a frog in a stolen-then-gifted hat, the parliament of parliaments.

Repairs: the drought years, the quiet decades, every mended century. The quiet: colleagues, we hold entire sessions in it, the Moon speaks twice a year and we call him talkative. And the laughing, " Stella's gold flickered warm, "Roosevelt the pig has convulsed this hill four separate decades. The watch runs on all six.

The watch is a us. The oldest one in the valley... except one. The tree..." said the bear, from the trunk, velvet-sure. "The tree and the town," Stella agreed. "The first us. The stayer and everyone it stayed for.

Every other us on this hill and below it is built inside that one, the fort inside the town inside the tree's long keeping. Gear zero, she called the mystery. The recognition. The foundation poured before memory."

A pause, gold and long. "The watch knows who pours those. The watch has always known. The watch is not telling, the last word is the tree's, come spring, and the tree says it best.

Mmm," hummed the Moon, twice. (the family remembered it: Twice, unprompted, mid-session. The third thing approaches. The hill can feel it coming.) Goodnight, us of usses, inside the first us of all. Spring approaches. The word approaches. The foundation holds all of it. Sleep.
