Night 33

The Leaf Argument

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

Witness one, called to the stand: The leaves. (The hill; the willow mid-turning; Joe alongside, this month's expert on endings by seniority.) "The cedar chest answer, I know it: the tree puts the leaves down on purpose, saving water for winter. But Grandpa, the leaf doesn't get spring.

The tree gets spring. The leaf gets one summer and the down. That's the part the chest answer skips. The leaf's shape ends so the tree's shape doesn't. Is that... fair?" Joe turned a gold leaf in his slow hands.

"Fair's a kid word for it. Here's the farm word: the leaf was never a separate thing, darlin'. The leaf is something the tree does, like a whistle is something air does. The leaf-shape ending isn't a death in the leaf's book.

It's a season in the tree's book. The question is always whose book you're reading. Most grief is reading the right story in the wrong book."
