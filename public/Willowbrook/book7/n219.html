Night 219

The Places That Held Still

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

Count category two, taken in one gold afternoon (Mama driving; the tour her design; the thanking in person, by the family's whole religion): The places. The stops, each thanked at its own ground: the corner. The stepping stones ("stone three's liar edge: still lying; still documented; still crossed, thank you for the consistency, even the treacherous kind; especially the treacherous kind; you taught the checking"). The oak's office (the bucket-pulley, ascended once, ceremonially, "the fifth branch held three years of chapters.

The corner office of my whole childhood"). The pond, the park, the fort's ground (fourth structure now standing; the constitution posted in its fourth hand-me-down printing). The school gates, both. The garden gate.

And the hill, last, at gloaming, the tree gold above: "you get your thanks at the word, old friend. Twenty-some nights. This stop's just... attendance. Part one. You taught it to the whole valley."
