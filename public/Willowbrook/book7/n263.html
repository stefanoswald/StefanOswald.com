Night 263

The Stand-Down Lesson for Theo

Month 9: Brave Out Loud

The succession needed its calibration this week: Theo's over-spend. (The bench-stander, three sentences and rising, deployed his voice at a playground dispute that was, sure enough, just two friends arguing. Loudly. Happily, almost.

The way some ways of working argue. His stand landed mid-friendship like a fire truck at a barbecue.) The calibration, run at his lamp, coach-grade: "The voice was brave, Theo, the spending's never wrong-hearted; write that first. The lesson's the territory: some loud isn't trouble.

Some arguing is just... how certain friends talk, the counter-crowd does it; the bench faculty does it; half the fort does it. The discernment check, before the spend: Is anybody getting smaller? The pile-ons shrink somebody, you can see the shrinking; the feelers see it first; you'll see it fastest of anybody. The happy-arguing shrinks nobody, everybody stays their whole size, just louder.

No shrinking: no stand. Witness instead. The witness seat's always open and it's never wrong." ("Is anybody getting smaller..." He repeated, filing it in the notebook that thrives. "The shrink-check. Before the spend." The shrink-check.

The story adds it to the clinic's lesson, where it lives yet, taught by its five-year-old co-author, cited properly, forever.)
