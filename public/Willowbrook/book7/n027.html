Night 27

The Charity Jar Skim Question

Month 1: When No One Is Watching

The well jar's counting day (the school institution, two years running; she treasurers it still) presented this month's most cynical exam, via a classmate's honest, awful question: "You count it alone? You could take a dollar every week and nobody..." The answer, given to the whole table because the question deserved daylight: "Nobody would know. Correct.

The jar's whole engine is that somebody counts alone and everybody believes the count, that's not a flaw in the jar; that's the jar running on the same fuel as the town. The neighborly trust. The honor board. The unchecked tally books everywhere.

Somebody's alone with every count in this whole town, every day. Rosa with the moment, Petrov with the fees, the librarian with the fine drawer, Joe's whole almanac. The town is a mountain of unwatched counts, and it stands because the counters vote their actual names, alone, daily. That's not naive, by the way..."

The classmate's face had said it. "the counts get published. Checks refrigerate trust. But the check catches the broken counter. The inner compass prevents one. Systems need both. Towns run on both. I count exact and I publish, and the dollar I could take every week is the exact price of everything I am, which makes it..." The grin. "the worst deal in retail."
