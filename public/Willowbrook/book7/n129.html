Night 129

The Question Row's Philosophy Bed

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

The seed order, fourth annual, and the question row's slate, dedicated, this year, to this month: The philosophy bed. ("The dragons this year get chosen for mystery, Grandpa. The bed's a monument to the open windows:") The selections: Moonflowers, returning ("the remembering crop; the old lady's mother's line; some plants are memory, the bed's memorial wing"). A four o'clock ("a flower that keeps time.

Opens at four. How? The botany book says 'circadian' which is science for 'it just knows.' The bed's clock wing"). Resurrection fern, the catalog's dare of dares ("looks dead for months. One rain: green in hours.

The catalog swears. The quiet-but-going crop, the brook's whole sermon, in a plant"). And Theo's pick, deliberated at length, tenderhearted: Sunflowers, again, always, "because they ask the sky the same question every day, all day, their whole life. The sun-question.

They never get the answer. They never stop asking. They're the best askers in the garden and that's why they're the tallest. The asking is the growing, Wivy. The asking was always the growing." (the story sets down the five-year-old's botany and declines to improve it. The asking is the growing. File it on the wall. File it everywhere.)
