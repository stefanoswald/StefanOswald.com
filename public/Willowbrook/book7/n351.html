Night 351

Two Sleeps

Month 12: The Maybe Year

Two sleeps remained before the great sequence began: the final maybe night, the willow's last word, the dream gate, and Bobby Bear's walk down the hall.

Olivia stayed awake beneath the reading lamp.

The brass key was warm in her hand. Her wooden box held the gold words gathered through the years, with one place still empty. The seven-star sweater waited on the chair.

"Are you frightened?" Mama asked.

"Less than I expected. I prepared for so long that the fear has somewhere to sit."

She drew Bobby close.

"I still do not know what the willow will write or what is behind the gate. I do know that whatever happens, I am loved and I can come home and tell you."

That was the readiness she had spent years building, not knowing the ending, but trusting what would hold her when it arrived.

"Goodnight, Mama. Goodnight, bear."

Mama kissed her forehead.

"Two sleeps," Olivia whispered into the gold light.

For once, the final days did not feel like a countdown she needed to hurry. They felt like two more nights worth keeping.
