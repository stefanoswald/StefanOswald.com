Night 183

The Chair That Wanted Shimming

Month 7: Making Things Right

First solo diagnosis: The wobbling chair. (Brought in condemned..." The leg's cracked. "per its owner; replacement leg quoted. The resident's assignment: sit with it till it talks.) The sitting: twenty minutes. The talking: the crack was old, stable, glued generations back, holding fine.

The wobble lived elsewhere: one leg, an eighth-inch short, worn, not broken. The fix: a shim. Cut, glued, invisible. Cost: nothing. The chair: steady as a bench. ("The owner condemned the whole leg because the wobble was loud, Mr.

Petrov. But the loud part wasn't the broke part. The crack was the scar, old, healed, innocent. The real problem was quiet and an eighth-inch big. Now you know rule two, detective: The loud part is almost never the broke part.

The wobble accuses; the shortness is guilty. In chairs and..." The teacher's look, over the glasses. "you already know the rest of that sentence. You've known it since the Duchess." She'd known it since the Duchess.

The mean was the alarm; the sister was the wound. Chairs and hens and everybody. One craft.)
