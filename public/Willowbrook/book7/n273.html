Night 273

The Watch Confirms the Room

Month 9: Brave Out Loud

On the month's last evening, Stella blinked once over the listening tree. The voice month, closing, and the watch's minutes ran, for once, to pure good news: "The count, from above, of the council night..." Read Oliver Owl. "The room: standing only.

The names: six hundred. The telling: 'the tree can't come to night meetings; the tree sent the town instead.'" The spectacles came down; the old bird looked at the tree above them, safe now in perpetuity, five words deep, one word waiting. "Colleagues, the watch has kept this hill three hundred years against every weather there is.

It never once occurred to the tally book that the hill might someday need keeping against surveyors, and it never once needed to occur, because the tree ran a longer game than the tally book did: it spent three centuries teaching the town to say here, and when the flag went up, the town said it. Six hundred times. In writing. The tree kept itself by keeping the town by teaching the town to keep.

The deepest circle the tally book has ever approved. And the readiness?" asked the bear, five nights from his job; the schedule his now; the whole hill runs on his calendar this season. "Ready, entire..." said the owl.

"The child: ready, the meter surplus, the hands empty, the voice fluent, the account paid. The town: ready, it just proved every word the tree ever wrote, unprompted, in a packed room. The tree: ready, it has been ready, the sureness says, for eleven months. And the watch..."

The tally book closed; the gold banked; the whole hill leaned toward the week ahead. "the watch is ready. The Moon's third hum is loaded. The word is chosen. The gold season opens, the gift month, the bear's month, and then the candle, the bark, the gate.

All hands, colleagues. The best season the hill has ever kept is opening now. Mmm," hummed the Moon. Not the third hum; a quick check confirmed; the third hum keeps for the bark, but its nearest neighbor, warm past all allowance.

Banked to the brim. Goodnight, ready Willowbrook. All hands. The gold season opens. Sleep, deep and gold. You'll want the rest.
