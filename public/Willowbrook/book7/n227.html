Night 227

When Willowbrook Counted Olivia

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

At the library exchange, Mrs. Hollis handed Olivia a cloth-bound folder.

"You have spent years noticing what this town gives," she said. "We thought the town should answer."

Inside were letters.

A councilman thanked Olivia for the bench, fountain, garden, and for teaching the council to listen to people shorter than four feet.

The school nurse wrote that Olivia's stapled paper shield had ended eleven years of children tearing their coats on the same hook.

Ms. Alvarez wrote: Some teachers receive a student. I received a colleague.

Gus Senior thanked her for returning his marble, his bench, and his Tuesday place in town.

Wren's page held only one sentence: YOU HEARD ME BEFORE I COULD MAKE EVERYONE ELSE HEAR.

Olivia read slowly. She had remembered each project as a problem that many people solved together. She had not realized how clearly those people remembered her.

The final page, written by Mrs. Hollis and the librarian, said: She counted us, so we counted her. Willowbrook.

Olivia closed the folder against her chest. For once, she allowed the attention to stay.
