Night 17

The Group Project Freeloader

Month 1: When No One Is Watching

The class history poster had four names on it, but only Olivia and one quiet girl were doing the work.

"I could finish it myself," Olivia told Mama. "It would be faster."

"Probably," Mama said. "Would it still be a group project?"

At the next work session, Olivia put down her marker.

"We have four names, so we need four real jobs."

She asked the best letterer to make the title. The boy whose grandfather had worked at the mill took the interview section. Olivia organized the timeline, and the quiet girl drew the old railway bridge.

The room changed. The two boys stopped orbiting and started working. One corrected a date Olivia had copied wrong. The other brought in a photograph no one else had seen.

By Friday, the poster looked better than the one Olivia had planned to make alone. It looked four-handed.

She had thought the problem was laziness. Sometimes, she realized, people were waiting for a job that felt like theirs.

The poster dried overnight beneath the classroom window. Outside, Willowbrook's old mill stood quietly under the stars.
