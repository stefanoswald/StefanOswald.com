Night 115

The Bad Day Service

Month 4: Theo's Big Sister

A heavy Thursday (hers, the gate year has its freight days; the mystery links weigh; even readiest kids carry), and they remembered this month's culminating stories: He read her feet. At the door. Before a word. The service, rendered in his approach, feelings-first, complete: the bell (placed in her sightline, openly now, the operation declassified: "the bell means I know.

The bell always meant I know"). The volume, self-lowered house-wide. The cocoa. And the sitting, near-not-with, the crayon out, ten minutes, no questions. Wren's rule, absorbed through years of ambient, deployed at exactly its moment by a kid who never once heard it named.

"He ran the whole soft landing on me, Mama. Every rule. In his order, the feelings first, the fixing never; he didn't ask what the heavy was; he just... attended it. Part one. The first gear of everything, run by the person I taught it to before he could talk.

This month's over, basically. Whatever the check says. This was this month: the coaching seat looked up and the coach was being coached and the difference stopped existing. That's the seat's real last stage. Not 'the coaching coaches.' The seats dissolve. There's just... us. The us, attending itself. Both directions. Forever, probably."
