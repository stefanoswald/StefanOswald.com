Night 2

The Wallet on Maple Lane

Month 1: When No One Is Watching

At dusk, Olivia found a wallet in the middle of Maple Lane.

She looked up and down the street. No one was calling for it. No one had seen her pick it up. Inside were several bills, more money than her bean jar had ever held, and a driver's license with the new pharmacist's photograph.

For one quick second, Olivia imagined carrying the wallet home. Then she imagined the pharmacist reaching for an empty pocket.

"A choice still counts when nobody sees it," she said to herself.

The pharmacy was four blocks away. Olivia arrived just as the pharmacist was locking the door.

"I think this belongs to you."

His startled face became a relieved one. He offered her a reward, but she shook her head. At last she accepted one stick of licorice so that he could thank her somehow.

She ate it on the walk home. The licorice was sweet, but the best part was knowing that an anxious evening had ended before it began.
