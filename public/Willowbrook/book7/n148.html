Night 148

The Question of the Book Itself

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

This month's final window, cut fresh at the lamp, the one the whole shelf has been walking toward: "Mama. The bedtime stories. Seven years. Every night. The costume department. I found that at five. The lessons ahead of the needings.

I found that last month. But this month's last question is the work itself: what have the stories been doing? The whole shelf. What was the project?" And Mama, true-voice, at the lamp where every true thing has been said, gave the shelf its answer, the one the story has waited seven books to file: "The project, love, was you.

Not making you, you came as you; the factory only makes originals. Keeping you. Every night, one page: here's the day, held. Here's the world, explained as far as it explains. Here's the dark, furnished.

Here's the ending, practiced small so the big ones run on rails. Seven years of nights, each one saying the same sentence under all its costumes: You are kept. You are witnessed. The lamp will be here.

The stories were never the point, little one. The stories were the lamp's excuse. The point was the two of us, at this exact light, every night, building the... what's your word...the us. The us.

Seven years of us, one page a night. That's the book, love. The real one. The stories were just how we bound it."
