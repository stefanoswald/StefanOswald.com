Night 357

The Day After, Kept Slow

Month 12: The Maybe Year

The day after, kept at porch pace (the pace is added; the gold month runs on it): the cake for breakfast (the rule; the willow's crown branch saved for Theo..." The bare one's yours, the waiting one; eat the waiting; it's delicious. "tenderhearted theology, applied to frosting; the family let it stand; the story rules it standing). The thank-yous, specific-true, the year's best hand. And the afternoon given to the quietest tradition of the whole ritual: The looking at everything.

(The room, toured slow: the frame's seven lines. The folders, seven deep. The box. The hinged box. True and the letter and the leaf and the one waiting space, four nights from its word. The chain, down to its last links, each one now a receiving-blank filled: The candle, whole.

The harvest, undefeated. The letters, fourteen. The sky, the tree.) ("Four nights, Mama. The word, then the gate, then the handing, then the page. And the looking-at-everything found this month's quietest true thing: I'm not counting down.

I'm counting up, the chain taught it years ago and it finally went all the way underground: the last nights aren't runway shrinking. They're treasure stacking. Every night from here is another gold one on the pile. Count up, everybody. Always count up. The pile's the point. The pile was always the point.")
