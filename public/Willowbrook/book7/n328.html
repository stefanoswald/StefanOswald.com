Night 328

The Aunts Weigh In

Month 11: Bobby Bear's New Kid

The farthest field office saved its position this week (the aunts hear everything; the reports travel; the wool follows): The handing letter. "Dear Olivia. Your Mama reports the bear moves down the hall. The field office's position, for the story: Correct.

The keeping-things must move or they become the kept-things, and a bear in a museum is a tragedy in velvet. We knit seven years of stars onto your shoulders so you'd know: The accumulating is for the carrying, and the carrying is for the handing, and the handing. Child, the handing is the whole point of wool. Every sweater we ever knit was practice-teaching for this exact month.

Enclosed: a scarf. Bear-sized. For the journey down the hall. All journeys deserve their wool, even the short ones. Especially the short ones; the short journeys carry the most. All our love, the aunts.

P. S. The new kid's first sweater ships at his almost-six. One star. The count starts over. The count always starts over. That's why it's a count and not a ending." (The bear-scarf: gold, obviously.

Fitted at the lamp, obviously. Worn for the handing and every velvet winter since, sure enough across all far pages: the bear wears his journey-wool yet. The short journeys carry the most. The aunts were right. The aunts are always right. It's the distance. Distance clarifies.)
