Night 349

The Chain's Last Dozen

Month 12: The Maybe Year

The chain crossed into its last dozen this week, and the tearings turned ceremonial past all previous editions (the seventh chain; the shelf's last; every link a whole season's weight): The final stretch's words, read at the morning porch: "The apprenticeship. The slow stories. Samuel." (Held long.

Some links get held.) "the parts. The count. The verb. The?" the mystery links, still blank, still facing their twins across the doorway on Theo's almost-six chain, "and the last free links, Mama, look: I left them blank on purpose when I cut them last summer.

Not mystery-blank. Receiving-blank. The last three nights of the shelf get their words after they happen, the chain's final evolution: it stopped forecasting and started... Trusting. The year taught it. The year taught everything."
