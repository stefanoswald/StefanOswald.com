Night 361

The Willow Writes: YOURS

Month 12: The Maybe Year

On Olivia's seventh birthday, her family climbed Willow Hill at golden hour. Half of Willowbrook followed at a respectful distance.

Olivia wore the sweater with seven stars. In her pocket, the wooden box held five gold words and one empty place.

The Great Willow's branches moved although the air was still. Fresh light appeared beneath the words already written in its bark:

HOME. FAMILY. GROWING. KIND. TRUE.

Below them, a sixth word formed.

YOURS.

The hill grew silent. Olivia traced the whole sentence in the air.

"It is an address," she said. "The tree is writing to me, and to Theo, and every child who comes after us."

Mama nodded. "What does yours mean?"

Olivia looked back at the people who had climbed behind her, then across the roofs, brook, farm, and park.

"It means all of this belongs to us. Not to keep away from other people. To care for and hand on."

She wrote YOURS on a small card and placed it in the last space of the box.

The willow's sentence was complete. The town remained on the hill until the gold faded, nobody in a hurry to be the first to leave.
