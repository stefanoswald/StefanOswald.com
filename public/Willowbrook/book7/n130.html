Night 130

Who Keeps the Story?

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

One night Olivia asked a question she had carried for years.

"Who keeps the story of me?"

Mama looked at the notebooks on the shelf. "We keep parts of it. You keep the largest part yourself."

"I mean the parts nobody writes down. The wind arriving when a letter comes. The willow glowing longer than it should. The way I seem to learn a lesson just before I need it."

She looked toward the dark window.

"Maybe Willowbrook has something watching over it. Or maybe being watched over is what love feels like when enough people keep showing up."

"Could it be both?" Mama asked.

Olivia smiled. "Everything important seems to be both."

She could not name the keeper she imagined, and she did not need to. She simply spoke into the quiet.

"Thank you, to the town, the tree, the stories, and everybody whose love I did not notice at the time. I have never been alone."

Mama took her hand. Bobby Bear sat between them, holding his usual silence.

The question remained open, but it no longer felt empty. It felt like a window with warm lights on the other side.
