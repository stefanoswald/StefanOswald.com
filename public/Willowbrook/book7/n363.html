Night 363

The Gate

Month 12: The Maybe Year

The moment Olivia closed her eyes, the dream meadow appeared.

Bobby Bear waited at the beginning of the gold path in his traveling coat. Together they passed the bright flowers and the cow whose bell had guided Theo home in dreams.

At the far end stood the old wooden gate. Its keyhole glowed.

"The key is yours," Bobby said. "It always was."

The brass key, warm from three years in Olivia's hand, turned without effort. The gate opened without a sound.

Beyond it was not another country. It was the meadow again, larger than she had ever seen it. Figures moved through the gold: a woman tending a wild garden row, a laughing boy beside the water, and one magnificently fat hen. Farther on were people Olivia knew, people she knew from stories, and places waiting for children not yet born.

"The gate does not go somewhere else," Olivia whispered. "It goes farther in."

Bobby nodded. "You have belonged here since the crib. Tonight you become one of its keepers. Someday you may warm a key for somebody else."

Then he used the name he had never spoken in full.

"Welcome, Olivia."

For seven years he had called her Almost, because she was always becoming. At the gate, she understood that becoming did not make her unfinished. She could be whole now and still grow tomorrow.

Olivia stepped through. Behind her, the gate remained open.
