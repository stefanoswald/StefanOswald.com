Night 40

The Gloaming Proof

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

Witness seven, called nightly, testifying forever: The gloaming. (The family's oldest institution; the gold hour; the day ending, on schedule, daily, in the most beautiful light the sky owns.) this month's reading of it, delivered at the window with the whole family attending: "The day ends every day, and look what the sky does about it: its best work. The gold, the long shadows, the undersmell, the windows lighting, the whole town's favorite hour is the day's last one. Nobody dreads the gloaming.

Nobody fights it. We put a word-sanctuary word on it and come to the window on purpose. Why? Because the ending's daily, small, practiced, survivable, and followed every time by night-then-morning. The sky's been running ending-practice for the whole world since light started: here's how you end a thing, gold, grateful, and certain of the next one.

The days are the practice endings, Mama. The sky is the costume department's oldest teacher. We've been in the class since birth and never noticed the syllabus."
