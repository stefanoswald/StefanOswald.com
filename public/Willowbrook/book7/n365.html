Night 365

What Will You Read Tomorrow?

Month 12: The Maybe Year

On the final night, Olivia dreamed herself beneath the Great Willow.

The whole night watch waited there: Oliver Owl with his notebook, Stella shining above the branches, the Sleepy Moon, and Bobby Bear looking toward Theo's window.

Oliver read the briefest report he had ever written.

"Seven years. Two thousand five hundred and fifty-five nights. A key turned. A gate opened. A bear was handed on. Every story kept."

Stella placed a seventh star above the roof of Olivia's home. The lights formed a small bright line across the sky.

Olivia thought of the finished words in the willow's bark, the books exchanged in Theo's doorway, and the lamp that would still be waiting when she woke.

"The first shelf is complete," Oliver said.

Then he lowered his spectacles and asked the question he had been saving.

"What will you read tomorrow?"

Olivia looked through the branches at Willowbrook's gold windows. For seven years she had received stories from everyone around her. Beyond the dream gate waited blank pages, new questions, and a life she could begin telling in her own voice.

She smiled.

"Tomorrow," she said, "I start writing."

The moon hummed. Stella twinkled once, for yes. And the first shelf ended exactly where the next story began.
