Night 96

The Jealousy Weather

Month 4: Theo's Big Sister

This month's hardest session, at his lamp, arriving sideways the way the hard ones arrive: "Everybody talks about your year. The gate thing I don't know about. The tree's word. The whole... the everything's yours this year.

And I got a ugly feeling about it and the ugly feeling is green and I hate having it..." The coaching, weather-first, at full dignity, because the green got named to her, about her, which took more brave than any hallway: "Theo. First: the green is a sneeze. It happens to you.

Having it isn't ugly. Obeying it would be, and you didn't; you named it, to my face, which is the bravest weather-report I ever got. Second: the green is information, it says you want a big year too. Correct wanting!

Your big year is coming, the chain proves it; the years take turns; mine's loud now because it's the shelf's last and yours is building because it's the shelf's next..." She stopped; the thing under this month, surfacing early; she let it. "Theo. The things that are mine this year... some of them are getting ready to be yours.

That's all I can say yet. But the green can stand down. Nothing's being taken from you. Some things are being got ready for you. The whole year is secretly half yours and you'll know by the end.

Promise. The biggest promise I got." (The green, later, stood down, not gone; greens don't go; but named, dignified, informed, and holding a promise. The boy slept. The promise held its date: night 364. A quick check confirmed the calendar and said nothing, per policy, forever.)
