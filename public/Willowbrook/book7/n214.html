Night 214

The Founding Walk

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

This month's field opening: The founding walk. (The town's whole origin, walked in one slow route. Joe navigating from the truck at the slow stretches, the cane on the walkable ones; the grandchildren as legs and book:) The route's stations: The hill ("the tree was here first; the town joined the tree; the wagon came over that ridge because a hill with a willow looks like somebody's already home"). The spring-fed brook ("the water wrote the first draft; the mill went where the water narrowed; the town went where the mill went; you live on the water's handwriting").

The mill ruins ("the whistle set every clock in the valley; Gus-Senior's initials at boy-height; the working past, cut in beams"). The four corners ("the bakery's corner was the first store, a bread oven in a shed, 1890-something; Rosa's floor stands on a hundred and thirty years of the same warm smell; the town's oldest always-sound is bread"). And the lane itself, walked home slow: "every house on it was somebody's whole brave once, Theo. Somebody stood where their porch is and said: Here.

The town is three hundred years of people saying here, and meaning it hard enough to plant a doorframe."
