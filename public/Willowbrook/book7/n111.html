Night 111

The Teacher Conversation, His

Month 4: Theo's Big Sister

His first conference, and the hallway bookshelf hosted its second-generation browser (the ears; the tradition; as it happened she taught him the spot beforehand, which is either corruption or lesson, and rules: lesson). The fragments that carried, saved by both browsers: "...the reading arrived mid-year, all at once, the miles method, his sister's, I'm told; tell her the district should license it... a natural repairer, the sandbox treaty, the sorry-flowers; the classroom conflicts route through him now, informally... feels everything first and fully; we're teaching the container, not the flood, his words, incidentally: 'the feelings are weather'..." And the growth note, delivered kind: "...he compares himself to the sister. The teachers know the shape, a big wake to swim in.

We're teaching him it's his ocean." (The debrief, sibling-run, at the lamp, because the browsers debrief: "The wake thing. Is that the green's cousin? The green's whole family. Theo: the wake is real. I go first and the water moves and that's physics.

But here's the other physics: Wakes are free rides. The cyclists know it; the geese know it, the V formation; the front bird breaks the wind for the whole line. You're not swimming in my wake. You're drafting.

And someday, the handicap math; the zero day, you pull ahead and I draft yours. That's not a wake problem. That's a formation. A formation..." He saved. The notebook got it: "Not a wake. A formation."

The teachers, told at the next conference, wrote it on the board. It stayed up all year. Some physics belongs to everybody.)
