Night 24

The Proposal, Translated

Month 1: When No One Is Watching

The resubmission, engineered to its reader (the craft is old now; the craft is the whole kit): The corner proposal, as a list. (Numbered. Contingencied. A maintenance schedule. A fallback plan. A trial period with an exit part..."

Airlocks need exits; that's what makes them enterable. "And at the top, the line that did the work, kind and honest, translated: "This proposal adds no chaos. It adds one system, maintained by me, checked by you, removable at will. The corner will never wobble.

O.") The airlock, cycling: three days of no. Then the quiet, reshaped, better grant: "Tuesdays. Trial basis. My check standards. And Miss, the proposal was properly formatted. Finally, from anyone, in this building, properly formatted."

(The corner opened the following Tuesday. As it happened what the room noticed within a month: Mrs. Vane's checks of the corner became... visits. Then attendance. Then, the story, arriving exactly as forecast, the day she brought her book and read in the corner during silent time, sharp-voiced and settled, while the room pretended not to see.

Some airlocks open all the way. It just takes proper formatting and somebody willing to learn the language.)
