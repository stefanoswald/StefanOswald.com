Night 344

The Seventh Line

Month 12: The Maybe Year

The doorframe. Shoes off. The book. The pencil. The seventh line, and the frame read whole, seven years of ladder, the family gathered at its own oldest archive: "The baby line. The wobbly two. The proud three.

The fourth's whole lesson. The fifth's school year. The sixth, the insides line, the big-gap one. And seven..." The line, drawn; the step back; the whole ladder looked over. "the gate line. The last of the first shelf.

Look at the distance, everybody. The whole climb, in pencil, on one door. The frame kept every me, and the frame's got room above, years of room, and Theo's ladder beside mine climbing its own weather, and someday the next kids' lines start at the bottom while ours run out the top and..." The finding, delivered hand on the frame, the family's whole geology in one sentence: "the doorframe is the town, Mama.

Layers of here, climbing. Everybody measured against the same wood. Nobody's line erased to make room, the room just grows. That's the whole shape of everything this family ever built. The frame keeps. The frame always kept."
