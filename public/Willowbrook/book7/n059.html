Night 59

Bella's Long View

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

On the last night of endings month, Bella Butterfly rested on a branch of the Great Willow.

She was the fourth butterfly in her family to carry the name. Her wings knew the garden route that her great-grandmother had first flown.

"Do you remember the other Bellas?" Oliver Owl asked.

"Not as pictures," Bella said. "I remember them as directions. I know which fence post to visit in May and which kitchen window will be open. Their lives continue in things my body knows."

She opened and closed her rainbow wings.

"One butterfly's life is short. The path is longer."

Oliver wrote this in his notebook. Stella shone above the hill, and the Sleepy Moon hummed.

Below, Olivia dreamed of seeds, snowmelt, old stories, and the gate she would soon open.

Bella looked toward the sleeping house.

"She is learning the same thing," she said. "You may let one shape end without believing the love inside it has ended."

The willow held the night wind in its branches. Bella's wings closed, and the old route waited faithfully for morning.
