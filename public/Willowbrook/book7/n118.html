Night 118

The Question of When

Month 4: Theo's Big Sister

His lamp, late in this month, the question under his whole season: "When's my turn, Wivy? The real turn. The gate-sized one. You got the word-tree and the mystery links and the whole... the whole gold year.

When do I get mine?" The answer, built careful, coach-grade, true: "The calendar of turns, Theo, the whole honest map: your gold year comes when your shelf ends, you're on book... free-ish of you; you got years of building first, and the building years look like waiting but they're the whole factory. My gold year is only gold because six years loaded it. And, the part the green needs to hear, the turns aren't a line.

They're a relay. My gold year hands to yours; that's how the formation works; the front bird doesn't finish the race, the front bird hands the wind-breaking back. Your turn isn't after mine. Your turn is built by mine.

Everything this year loads... lands somewhere. Some of it lands night 364. That's all I can say. The promise holds its date." ("The promise holds its date," he repeated, the phrase his now too; the family's phrases pool like the family's everything.

And the story, checking its own calendar one more time: 249 nights to the landing. The loading continues. The loading is glorious. Onward.)
