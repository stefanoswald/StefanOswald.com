Night 320

The Chain's Mystery Links Named

Month 11: Bobby Bear's New Kid

The doorway ceremony, run mid-month by joint motion (the chains' twin question marks, facing each other since winter; the shapes clear enough now; the filling due): The mystery links, named. Hers, written first, the doorway attending: "The handing." (One word... then, by the year's whole craft, the margin added: "... Which is the gift, the gate, the word, the bear, the shelf's last page, and the whole final stories, all one link.

The year was always one link. I just couldn't see the shape till now.") And his, written second, the lefty letters sure: "The getting-ready's done." (And his margin, five years old, tenderhearted, the doorway's whole poetry: "...

I helped. I didn't know what for. The helping didn't need the knowing. The bell never needed the knowing either. T.") (The two links, hung facing, filled at last, and the doorway, as it happened, holds the year's whole shape now in two paper rings: The handing and the getting-ready's done, face to face, swaying in the house's night breathing, a season's last stretch from the work they name.

The frames keep everything. This one keeps the year's own heart, in two hands' writing, at kid height, where the important things live.)
