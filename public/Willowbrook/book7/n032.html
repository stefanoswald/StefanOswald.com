Night 32

The Question Under the Year

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

The leaves turned, and this month asked its question straight, because this year asks everything straight: "Mama. Why do things end? Not the water-cycle answer. I own the water-cycle answer; the snowman rule, the compost, the distributed Duchess; nothing loved gets lost; the matter goes around.

I mean the real one: why does the shape end? The summer, the preschool, the Duchess-shaped Duchess, the first shelf. The stuff goes around but the shapes stop. Who decided shapes stop? And why does it have to be the rule?"

And Mama, true-voice, the rule, gave this month its terms: "That's the question, love. The real one. Bigger than the lamp, it's a month-sized question at least; people spend lifetimes on it. So let's spend this month. No rushing it. The question deserves the season it came in."
