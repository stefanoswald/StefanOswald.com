Night 132

The Librarian's Question

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

This month's collection round (everybody's carrying one; ask them theirs; the best question at any table) reached the desk: "The unanswerable you've shelved under for forty years. This month collects them. Yours?" The librarian shelved two returns before answering, the pause of the real ones: "Where do the readers go?

Not the people, the readers. The kid who lived in dinosaurs for two years and then... didn't. The dinosaur-reader is gone, the person remains, grown, fine, but that particular reader, that exact hunger, where did it go? Forty years I've watched readers arrive and dissolve and become other readers.

The shelves hold every book they ever were. The people only hold the current one." She looked down the lamplit stacks. "I've decided the library is where the old readers live. Every book you ever loved keeps the reader you were when you loved it.

That's why walking the stacks feels like..." The exact word, chosen by a professional. "like attendance. The building is full of everyone everyone used to be. I'm not a librarian, child. I'm a keeper of former selves. Yours are in here too. All the yous. Shelved. Safe. Visitable."
