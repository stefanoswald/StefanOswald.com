Night 28

The Weekend of the Flu Cover

Month 1: When No One Is Watching

A gray-rain Saturday, a scratchy throat (borderline; Mama's call: "your choice, love, the backup rules exist; Papa can run the count"), and this month's subtlest exam: The optional showing-up. The big think, honest: "The post survives me. I built the survival; that's the whole law. Nobody would blink.

The froat's real-ish. The rain's real. The couch is right there..." And the deeper tally book, consulted: "... But the post isn't the only thing at the farm. The post survives me. The porch hour doesn't, the porch hour only exists if I'm in it.

Joe doesn't need my hands today. The arrangement needs my showing. Duty has backups. Presence doesn't. That's the whole difference and I just found it in a scratchy froat." (She went. Scarfed, medicined, cleared by the health authorities.

Ran a light count, read one catalog page, sat the porch hour under a blanket while the rain did its gray work on the fields. Joe said four words at the truck: "Knew you'd come. How? Same way the gate knows."

The farm and the meadow, as it happened, have always spoken the same language. Everything here does.)
