Night 294

The Gift Wrap Reformation

Month 10: The Gift Month

A small reformation at the workshop table: The wrapping check. ("The paper gets made, shipped, wrapped, admired for four seconds, and torn, the whole supply chain for four seconds. The check objects.") The reformed law, family-agreed: Cloth (the aunts' influence; the wrapping becomes part of the gift, "the furoshiki thing; Noor's mama demonstrated at the workshop; the imports keep arriving; the best ones always do").

Jars (the family's own currency: "a gift in a jar is two gifts; the jar economy expands"). And reused gold (the salvaged paper drawer, smoothed and kept, "the hand-me-down river covers wrapping too; paper that's wrapped free gifts carries free gifts' worth of goodwill; the educated paper law"). (Theo's new rule, carried unanimous: "And the drawings count as wrapping. A gift wrapped in a drawing is wrapped in the three weeks twice."

The drawings count as wrapping. The household's gifts, sure enough across the years, arrive henceforth in cloth, jars, educated gold, and original art. The four-second supply chain: retired. The admiring: extended indefinitely. The reformation: complete, and delicious to behold every December since.)
