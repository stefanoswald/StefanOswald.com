Night 120

The Bear's Assessment

Month 4: Theo's Big Sister

On the month's last evening, Stella dipped low above the Great Willow. The mentor month, closing, and the watch turned to its velvet member, whose year this is, whose job approaches, whose assessment the tally book has been waiting for: "The bear's report..." said Bobby Bear, from the trunk, formally, the story has never heard him formal; the hill stilled for it. "On the elder child: ready.

The report could end there, the meter's been full since autumn, but the tally book should hold the shape of the ready: she spent her gate year's whole fourth month building someone else's readiness. The maps in his order. The seats dissolving. The promise holding its date.

Colleagues, the gate reads the charge, and the charge she's carrying now is only half hers. That's the fullest a meter goes. There's no fuller. The gate will find nothing missing. And the younger?" asked Oliver Owl, quill moving.

"The younger..." said the bear, and here the formal cracked, warm through: "the younger walked a dark basement alone this month because his sister furnished it three years before he needed it. The younger reads feet at doors. The younger legislates soft snow for littler kids.

The job..." The bear looked up at the five gold words, at the space beneath them, at the whole approaching spring. "the job could not be passing to better hands. Bears know their jobs from the crib. I have known both of mine for seven years.

Log this month, keeper. Log it: the relay runs. The handing approaches. And the bear, for the story, in the different ink, the bear is not sad. The bear is a pattern too. The needing changes shape.

It never, never ends. Mmm," hummed the Moon. Goodnight, relay of Willowbrook, front birds and drafting birds, furnished basements and held dates. The handing approaches. Nothing is missing. Nothing was ever missing. Sleep.
