Night 186

The Inherited Feud

Month 7: Making Things Right

List item three: The inherited feud. (Two four-square kids, cold three years, the grudge passed down from older brothers who'd graduated past it, the original combatants, sure enough, are friends now, at the middle school, and never told the heirs.) The mediation, run on archaeology: the origin, excavated (interviews; the fence network; Mateo's telling from the upper grades): a disputed line call. Four years ago.

Best-of-five, never played. That's it. That's the whole seed of three years' cold. The presentation, made to both heirs at the court: "The feud you're carrying: here's its whole birth certificate. A line call.

Your brothers' line call. They're friends now. I checked; they eat lunch together Tuesdays, and nobody told you the war ended. You've been holding a fence for a farm that sold." (The two kids looked at each other across three years of inherited cold, and someone saw the inheritance get declined, awkwardly, the way kids decline things: "...wanna just play?...yeah."

Best of five. The original match, played at last, four years late. The story kept score. Nobody else did. That was the whole victory.)
