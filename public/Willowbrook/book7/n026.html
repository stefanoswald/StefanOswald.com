Night 26

The Little Kid Who Copied Her

Month 1: When No One Is Watching

A shadow appeared at the office-hours table this week: Theo's classmate, added by Theo himself..." She wants the homework system. I told her we got one. We got all the systems. "and copying, someone noticed, everything: the window seat angle.

The snack-alongside. The bag-empties-first. The pencil grip. The instinct, examined at the lamp after: "It's weird being copied, Mama. Good-weird and heavy-weird. She's not copying the homework, she's copying the how, and the how is six years of Mamas and Petrovs and doorframes deep.

She can't see the years. She just sees the table. So show her the years? No, that's the thing I figured out mid-snack: you don't show the years. You just run the table true and let her build her years at it.

The copying's how everybody starts. I copied the librarian's face-out grip before I knew why it worked. The years arrive during. The table teaches. The tables always teach. That's why you keep them set."
