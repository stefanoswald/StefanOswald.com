Night 99

The Nightmare Service

Month 4: Theo's Big Sister

Three a.m. A Tuesday: the knock. (His; small; the bad-dream knock, the wolf one, recurring, the five-year-old's standard-issue night freight.) The service, rendered by the night's own veteran, gold light on: the weather first ("the scared's still on you, dreams leave their weather; sit in it with me till it passes through; you're the sky; the wolf was weather").

Then the rule, meadow-grade, taught at the age she learned it: "The dreams got a unloading job, Theo, the freight nights. The wolf's not a monster in you; the wolf is the week's worries wearing a costume, the costume department runs at night too. And here's the veteran's move: you can talk back in dreams. It takes practice, the lucid thing; Bobby taught me the edges of it, but it starts with the daytime saying: tonight, if the wolf comes, I ask it what it's carrying.

Wolves in dreams are mostly carrying something. Ask the cargo. The asking changes the dream's whole weather." (He asked, later, four nights later. The wolf, in his breathless breakfast filing..." Had my spelling list in its mouth.

The wall was the wolf. I asked and it just... put the list down and got small and went. "The wall was the wolf. It usually is. The asking changes everything. It usually does.)
