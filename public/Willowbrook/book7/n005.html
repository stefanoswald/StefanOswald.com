Night 5

Theo's Paper Chain

Month 1: When No One Is Watching

Theo began his first birthday paper chain that week. He cut the links himself, some wide, some crooked, and wrote one memory on each.

THE BIG ROCK FELL.

I FIXED THE HINGE BOX.

I WON WITH THE SHEEP.

THE MOONFLOWERS OPENED.

The family gathered while he hung the chain beside Olivia's older one.

"What do you want this year to hold?" Mama asked.

"Chapter books," Theo said. "School. And getting ready."

"Ready for what?" Olivia asked.

Theo looked at her, serious beneath the gap where a tooth had been. "I don't know. You're getting ready for something big. I can tell. So I am getting ready to help."

He knew nothing about the gate in the dream meadow or Bobby Bear's final task. Theo simply noticed feelings before people found words for them.

Olivia helped him close the last paper loop. Their two chains stirred together whenever the window was open, one nearly finished and one just beginning.
