Night 233

The Old Photographs Exhibition

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

The count's public wing: The photo exhibition. (The library's archive, mounted with the historical society, the town's faces, century-deep, matched where possible to their living descendants, card by card, her matching:) The matches, made at the long table: the mill crews (Gus-Senior found at boy-height in a 1948 shift photo, grinning exactly his grin, "the grin survives everything; grins are heirlooms too"). The bucket line of '34 itself (Photographed, the whole chain, brook to window, aprons full of books; the town chose its treasure and somebody caught the choosing). The bakery's four generations at one counter, forty years apart, same flour-stance.

And one unlabeled frame that stopped her cold: a girl, 1930s, drawing at an easel in a yard. The yard; the lot's yard; and on the easel, unmistakable: a willow. ("E. W. Everybody. Edith Winslow.

The wall's first kid, caught drawing the drawing. The archive had her the whole time. The archives always got everybody. You just gotta do the matching.")
