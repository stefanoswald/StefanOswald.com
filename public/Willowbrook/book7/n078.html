Night 78

The Winter Us

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

The big snow came, and fort city rose again (third annual; the districts; the treaties; the mitten flag on the founding structure), and this month watched its definition run at city scale: The usses, self-assembling in the drifts: Fort together (the founding; the constitution recited by kids who weren't born when it was drafted, "the us outlives its builders; that's not a bug; that's the whole spec"). The castle-not-war rule. The warming rotation (Rosa's counter; the chalk schedule; the somebody at large, as ever). And the new district, risen unbidden: The little kids' Build, governed by, here is the succession in full.

Mayor Theo, second term, now with a deputy: the origami kid, crane-folder, Tuesday-bench alumnus, friendless three months ago and running a snow district today. ("The ladder works, Mama. Gear by gear, Tuesday by Tuesday, and the lonely kid is deputy mayor of a snow city by January. The pattern doesn't just make friends. It makes citizens. Same thing, maybe. This month thinks it's the same thing.")
