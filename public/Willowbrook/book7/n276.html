Night 276

The Gift in Quiet

Month 10: The Gift Month

Gift two, built in the recipient's native language: Wren's book. (Hand-bound, the library's binding lessons; the apprenticeship's hands. And the pages: Blank. Every one. Except the first, lettered careful:) "This book is quiet. It's for: Drawings that don't owe explanations.

Words that aren't ready out loud. Maps of the classroom weather. The things the volume knob keeps. Nobody reads it. Not me, not anybody. Unless you hand it over yourself, which you never have to.

The lock rule covers books without locks too. For W. The ambassador of their bedtime world I visit best. From the bench. Always from the bench." (The delivery: the mural bench, wordless, by the correct rule.

The receipt: the whole-face smile, third documented instance, and then, they remembered with care, a page turned and a drawing begun, right there, at the bench, in company, the book's whole thesis proven in its first minute: the quiet, given room, sits down beside you and works. It always did. She just built it a room.)
