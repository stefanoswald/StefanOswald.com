Night 362

Seven, Night One

Month 12: The Maybe Year

On her first bedtime as a seven-year-old, Olivia looked around the nursery.

The room was the same. Bobby Bear sat in his old place for two more nights. The seven-star sweater hung over the chair. The brass key was warm, and the wooden box now held the willow's complete sentence:

HOME. FAMILY. GROWING. KIND. TRUE. YOURS.

"I thought seven would feel like six plus one day," Olivia said. "But the willow made it feel whole."

Mama sat beside her. "And tomorrow?"

"Tomorrow Bobby walks me to the gate. Whatever is behind it, I will come back and tell you here."

"Every door?"

"Every door of my whole life."

Mama kissed her forehead. Olivia held Bobby Bear close and felt excitement beside fear, neither one needing to defeat the other.

"Goodnight, Mama. Goodnight, bear. One sleep."

The lamp clicked off. Somewhere beyond dreaming, an old gate waited for the hand that finally fit its key.
