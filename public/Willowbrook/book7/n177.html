Night 177

The Rain Date That Wasn't Wasted

Month 6: Grandpa Joe's Slow Stories

The fishing Saturday met the knees' forecast, and converted, by the family's oldest alchemy: The barn afternoon. The conversion's yield: the tackle box, opened and storied ("every lure's a Saturday, darlin', this one took the big bass of '61, Sam's last summer; this one's Margaret's, she tied flies, did you know; the patience arts ran in her whole family..."). The rods, re-wrapped (the cut-down one re-wound fresh for the next hands. Theo's, next summer, by the fleet schedule; the boy watched the winding like a knighting).

And the rain on the barn roof, doing what barn-roof rain does: making the inside golder. ("The rain didn't take the Saturday, Mama. The rain redirected it. The fishing was the plan; the together was the point; the barn held the point just fine.

The plans are guesses about days, but the point travels. Pack the point, not the plan. The point fits in any weather.")
