Night 94

Theo and the Crane Kid

Month 4: Theo's Big Sister

Theo had his first serious fight with the boy who folded paper cranes. A sandbox tower fell. Theo shouted, "You are not my deputy anymore!" Two days passed without either child speaking.

"I am mad," Theo told Olivia. "And I miss him."

"Both can be true. Which one is bigger?"

"The missing. Folding is no fun alone."

Olivia helped him prepare an apology. It needed two parts: what he had done and what he would do differently.

Theo carried a dried dandelion to school.

"I said you weren't my deputy because I wanted to hurt you," he told the boy. "It hurt me too. Next time I will say I am mad about the tower instead."

The boy accepted the flower. They redrew the sandbox border using a rule Olivia had taught Theo: one person draws the line; the other chooses a side.

By afternoon, two paper cranes guarded the rebuilt tower. One had Theo's name on its wing.
