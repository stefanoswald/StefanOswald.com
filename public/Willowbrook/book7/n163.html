Night 163

Missing Someone Early

Month 6: Grandpa Joe's Slow Stories

After Grandpa Joe told her that his slowing would probably happen over years, Olivia carried both relief and sadness home.

"I am glad we likely have years," she told Mama. "But I am already missing him a little, and he is still here."

Mama knew that feeling. "Sometimes love begins grieving before a loss arrives. It thinks worrying now might protect us later."

"Does it?"

"No. It can steal time from the person who is still beside us. But the ache can remind us to use that time well."

Olivia looked at the calendar. Saturday was farm day.

"So when I feel it, I should go sit on the porch and ask for another story."

"Yes. Let the feeling keep appointments, not vigils."

Olivia wrote that sentence down.

The far sorrow would come whenever it came, and her family would help her carry it then. Tonight Grandpa was alive, the farm was waiting, and there were still questions she had never asked.

She circled Saturday in gold pencil and slept more easily.
