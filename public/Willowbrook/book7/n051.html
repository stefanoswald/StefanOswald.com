Night 51

The Thanksgiving of the Patterns

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

Thanksgiving, seventh edition, and the gratitude round, led by its standing chair, ran on this month's whole engine: "The rule this year: thank the patterns, not the shapes. I'll start. I'm thankful for the return. Bella's kind: the thing that comes back different and same, every year, wearing new clothes.

For the handing, the corners and the whistles and the almanacs, moving keeper to keeper, none of them hoarded. For the arrangement..." A nod porch-ward, to its human half, who studied his plate. "which is a pattern two people do until it's a pattern a family does. For the correspondence that never closes, the letters, the different ink, the answering that outlives the answerers.

And for the table itself, the pattern of patterns; the thing all the other things are for. The shapes at this table will change, and the table goes on. The table always goes on. That's the most thankful thing I know.

Pass the thankful." (They passed the thankful. All the way around. Twice. Theo demanded the second lap; "The thankful was still warm." It was. Sure enough: it stayed warm the whole meal. It stays warm yet.)
