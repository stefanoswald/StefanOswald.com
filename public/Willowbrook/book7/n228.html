The History Homework Coup

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

This month's institutional coda: The history lesson. The week, taught from the count: the founding walk (field trip; the class at the mill beams, finding Gus-Senior's initials. He guest-lectured, marble in pocket). The bucket line of '34.

The grid's genealogy (age-adjusted: "the kindness water-cycle; you're all in it; you got in it when you got here"). And the lesson's final assignment, hers, Vane-approved: Every kid adds one entry to a new class count, "the town that's making you. One maker each. The count never closes.

That's the whole thing about towns. Write your entry like somebody'll read it in ninety years. Somebody will. I found E. W.'s. Somebody finds yours. Say here like you mean it."
