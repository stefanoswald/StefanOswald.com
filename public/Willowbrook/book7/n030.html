Night 30

What the First Month Taught

Month 1: When No One Is Watching

On the last night of the month, Olivia spread her notebook open between Mama and Papa.

There was the wallet returned at dusk, Mama's repaired cup, the solitaire game she refused to cheat, and the reading log with its honest twelve. There was a new path through Number Eleven's hedge, permission asked and freely given. There were fourteen eggs, including the one her own boot cracked. There was the cruel note removed from the Somebody Wall and Lily's whistle answered at a run.

"Which choice was hardest?" Papa asked.

Olivia considered. "The small ones. Big choices announce themselves. Small ones pretend they do not matter."

Mama touched the silver line in her cup. "And do they?"

"They make a person," Olivia said. "One quiet choice at a time."

Outside, rain whispered against the nursery window. No stars applauded. No tally book glowed. The month ended as most honest choices had happened: quietly, with Olivia knowing what she had done.
