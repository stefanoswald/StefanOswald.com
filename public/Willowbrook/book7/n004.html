Night 4

An Honest Game

Month 1: When No One Is Watching

Grandpa Joe had taught Olivia solitaire, which he called "the only card game where your opponent is your own patience."

On a rainy afternoon, Olivia's game became hopelessly stuck. Then she glimpsed the next card reflected in the brass lamp base. If she quietly rearranged two cards, everything would work.

No one was watching. Bobby Bear was nearby, but he could not object.

Olivia reached toward the cards and stopped. A dishonest win would look exactly like an honest one. That was what made it tempting, and what made it useless.

She lost the game, shuffled, and lost again. On the third try, the last card landed cleanly.

At bedtime she told Mama, "The win looked the same as the one I could have cheated. But it didn't feel the same."

"Who knew the difference?" Mama asked.

"I did."

Olivia set Bobby Bear beside the finished rows. Apparently one witness was enough after all.
