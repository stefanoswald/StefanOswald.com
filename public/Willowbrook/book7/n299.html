Night 299

The Gift for the Town Itself

Month 10: The Gift Month

This month's gift for everyone (the town gave a count's worth; the town receives in kind): The town's own gift. The story post. (Petrov-built to her design: a small roofed board at the garden's gate, the town's crossroads now, with a weatherproof box, paper, pencils, and the standing invitation, carved: "Leave a story. Take a story.

The town is made of them. Anonymous is fine. True is better. Both is best.") (The post's first week, by the story's delighted check: eleven stories left, nine taken. A first-kiss-at-the-mill confession, sixty years old.

A recipe with a story baked in. A kid's account of a hand chain. One unsigned entry in handwriting the story recognized instantly, the counter-crowd's convert, the casserole neighbor, beginning: "I was wrong about someone once, and the correction took a child and a year, and here's what I learned..." The post filled weekly for decades.

The town, given a place to hand itself its own stories, handed them. It was always going to. The town is made of them. The carving said so, and the carving was true.)
