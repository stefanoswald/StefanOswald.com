Night 319

Bobby Bear's Turn

Month 11: Bobby Bear's New Kid

The meadow came once more, and this month gave the bear the floor (the accounts settled hers; the settling owed him his; the parts turn both ways or they're not gears, even velvet ones): The job, from inside: "Seven years ago a bear arrived at a crib on the last night of a first book..." He began, on the gold path, walking nowhere, the walking the point. "The job, known from the first night the way bears know: Be the keeping, till the keeper grows. And the bear's side of the years, Almost, since you settled yours: the bear got to watch.

The whole shelf, from the pillow. The words turning on. The braves stacking. The systems planting. The keeper growing, night by night, click by click, into the thing the job promised she'd become. Bears don't get tired, Almost, but they get full, and the bear is full.

Seven years full. The fullest bear in the history of velvet, probably. And the job's last part, known from the crib same as all the rest: The full bear hands on full. The new kid doesn't get a emptied bear, he gets a bear at capacity: seven years of craft and keeping and ordinary nights, pre-loaded, riding along, the educated fleet's finest vessel.

The handing isn't the bear starting over. The handing is the bear arriving, everywhere he goes now, he arrives full. You filled me, Almost. The job says thank you. The bear says it bigger: the bear says you were the best first kid a keeping ever had.

And the bear would know. The bear checked with all the bears. At the... ", the ear flopped; the gold moved; the joke arrived on schedule, velvet-dry, "at the conferences. Bears have conferences. You never asked. Nobody ever asks."
