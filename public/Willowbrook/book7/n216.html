Night 216

The Library's Long Game

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

Count station two: The lantern's century-plus. (The librarian's telling, desk-taught, with the list's cooperation, the wainscoting contributed, sure enough; the building tells its own count through its keepers:) The founding ("a reading room over the feed store, 1902; eleven books and a whale-oil lamp; the lamp was lit the first night and the rota started the second, the promise is older than the building"). The great fire that didn't ("1934; the west wall caught; the bucket line ran from the brook. The whole town, pass by pass; the books came out the windows into aprons and wagons; not one volume lost; the town chose its treasure under fire and the treasure was the shelf").

The wainscoting's oldest entry, shared at last (the librarian read it from memory; the keepers memorize the founding texts): "'The light stays on. Whoever keeps it next: The light stays on. The first keeper, 1902.' Every keeper since has initialed beneath it.

Forty-one initials." (A pause; the desk; the pen-traced spot on the rota.) "Forty-two, someday. The wainscoting is patient. The wainscoting has been patient about you, specifically, since the reader chair. It asked me to mention it. Buildings ask, child. The good ones ask."
