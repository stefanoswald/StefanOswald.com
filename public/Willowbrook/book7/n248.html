Night 248

The Story About Number Sixteen

Month 9: Brave Out Loud

At the bakery counter, Olivia overheard several adults discussing the man at Number Sixteen.

"Strange fellow," one said. "Something wrong there."

Olivia turned around.

"The egg route has visited him for more than fifty Saturdays," she said. "What I know is that he came home from something hard and needed people not to knock for a while. He waves now. He helped close the garden in autumn."

The adults grew quiet.

"Being hurt is not the same as being strange," Olivia continued. "And a rumor can make it harder for someone to come back. Please carry the kinder true story."

Later Rosa found Olivia stacking napkins.

"You corrected grown-ups in my bakery," she said.

Olivia's face warmed. "Was I rude?"

"You were careful, and you knew more than they did. From now on, this counter carries the accurate version."

A town's stories traveled in ordinary conversation. Olivia could not stop every cruel version, but she had changed the one moving through that room.
