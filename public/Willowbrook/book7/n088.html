Night 88

The Month's Last Window Seat

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

This month closed where its stories began: The window seat. (The braid; the cocoa; the two streets' worth of dusk going gold-to-blue outside the glass that has held six years of summits.) The review, run as only the oldest example can run it: "Six gears, one us, gear zero unanswerable," Lily counted, braiding. "You machined our whole friendship, Olivia.

Some people would think that's... cold. Studying the thing you're inside of. And you? I think..." The braid paused; the eleven-days-younger wisdom, aimed one last time for this month. "I think the studying is a gear.

Gear seven, maybe, or part of part two: you pay attention to us on purpose. The pattern's just your attention wearing its counting clothes, and the attention is the love; your grandpa's whole almanac; you said. So this month wasn't cold. This month was you loving us in your own approach.

The way I bake it and Wren quiets it and June bugs it. You notebook it. The us gets loved in six ways of working at once. That's why it holds." (the story sets the braid-pause down gently and confirms: the us gets loved in every approach at once.

That's why it holds. That's why everything here holds. This month rests its case at a window seat, which is where its case always lived.)
