Night 197

The Bench's Own Mending

Month 7: Making Things Right

A small ceremony, month-appropriate: The mural bench, the embassy; Wren's bench; the strong enough silence's home, went loose at one anchor this spring. And its constituents (the original pact's three, plus Noor, the bench seats four now; benches grow) repaired it themselves: the ordered bolt, the borrowed wrench, the re-anchoring done by the hands that wore it loose. ("The bench held us for three years, Lily. Today we held it.

The parts turn both ways, furniture edition. And look..." The plaque idea arrived mid-wrench; the plaques all arrive mid-something. "it should say something. Small. For the next pact that finds it." The plaque, letter-painted by the precisionist of the four: "This bench holds: Quiet, truth, waves, and whoever needs it. Maintained by its friends.")
