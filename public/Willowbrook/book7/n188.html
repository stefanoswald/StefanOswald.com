Night 188

The Door at Number Sixteen

Month 7: Making Things Right

Olivia knew she could not fix the lonely man at Number Sixteen. She could only leave a door open.

The egg route began placing half a dozen eggs on his step each Saturday. For the first month, there was no note. A note could feel like a knock, and he had shown that he did not want knocks.

In the second month Olivia added three words: FROM THE ROUTE.

Theo placed one dandelion beside every carton.

"So he knows the eggs are friendly," he explained.

If the man sat on his porch, they waved from a distance. He did not wave back, and they did not stop.

In the fifth month, his hand lifted.

In the seventh, a note waited beneath the empty carton: THE EGGS ARE GOOD. 16.

By autumn he came to the garden closing and quietly tucked soil around a row of bulbs. Nobody announced his arrival or asked him to explain himself.

Some doors opened through invitation. Others opened because kindness had waited outside without pushing.
