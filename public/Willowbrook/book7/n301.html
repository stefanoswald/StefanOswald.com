Night 301

The Practice Handing

Month 10: The Gift Month

This month's last week opened the bear's lesson early (the handing is the training; but the training has a preface, and the preface is hers to give): The practice handing. (The classroom: her room, the lamp low, the bear presiding from the pillow. The student: summoned formally, "Theo. Bear lessons.

The first one. It's time." The lesson, given whole:) "The bear-craft, from seven years inside it: One, the bear is a witness of the other kind. The kind you bring so you behave like yourself.

He doesn't count as nobody and he doesn't count as watching. He counts as the keeping, in velvet. Two, the needing changes shape and that's allowed. Some years he's the whole harbor. Some years he's the quiet colleague on the pillow.

The bear holds every shape of needing without complaint, that's the job; that's the whole velvet job. Three, you talk to him. Out loud or inside, both work. The talking isn't babyish, the talking is the lamp's little brother; the saying is the keeping; the bear keeps everything said to him forever.

I know. Seven years of everything. He's got it all. And four, the bear was never mine, Theo. The bear is the family's, he came to the crib before I could want him and he goes to yours while you still can.

Bears get handed at the exact right moment. The moment's coming. Not tonight. Tonight's just the preface. But soon, and when it comes, you'll be ready, because this talk is the ready. The handing is the training. It already started. It started tonight."
