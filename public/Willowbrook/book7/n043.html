Night 43

The Radio Show's Final Episode

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

The fort's radio serial (two seasons; the squadron as cast; "The willowhawk rides again" through eleven episodes) faced this month's question in production form: the episodes were thinning. The cast's Saturdays were filling. The old options: let it trail off (the default of everything; the ugliest ending there is) or... "... End it on purpose.

The author's way. The gift-complete way." The finale, written with the whole craft: the Hawk's last flight (the fictional one, "the real Hawks keep flying; the show ends; the things the show's about don't; that's the whole trick of shows"). Every character's thread, landed.

The engine-hum's final note (Theo held it, the story attests, for eleven seconds, a personal and possibly species record). And the sign-off, written for the ages because she writes for the ages now: "This has been the willowhawk rides again. The hawk is yours now. Build your own. Crash glorious. Fly again. Goodnight from the fort."
