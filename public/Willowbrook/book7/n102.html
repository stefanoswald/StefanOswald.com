Night 102

The Money Lessons

Month 4: Theo's Big Sister

His economy launched this week: First allowance, plus the egg route's junior share, formalized (Joe's arithmetic: the Saturday partner earns; the envelope, brown, his name in farmer's capitals, the boy held it like a knighthood, which it is). The tally book lesson, three columns, taught at the office-hours table: "Saved. Spent (now-you's, the rolls, the fun; the fun's allowed; the column exists so the fun's chosen, not leaked). And given, because the columns are how you tell your money what you believe, and this family believes in the mitten jar and the well jar and the whoever's-cold.

You pick the amounts. The picking's the whole lesson." (His first allocation, deliberated with enormous gravity: "Saved: for a real toolbox. Spent: one roll, split with you, the baker's principle; you taught it; the promise-one's yours.

Given: the flower fund. The flower fund? My fund. I'm starting it. For kids whose sorry-flowers don't grow in winter. The dried ones cost drying-time and jars. The apology setup needs funding, Wivy." they remembered the town's newest charity without comment. The comment would just be crying.)
