Night 329

The Almanac Notes the Handing

Month 11: Bobby Bear's New Kid

The Saturday porch took this month's news for the book (four pages left in volume one; the entries chosen like last coins now, and this one chosen instant): The handing, entered. The entry, written by the fourth hand at the keeper's nod: "The bear moves to the boy this month. Night 364 by the family's count. Seven years at the first post.

The craft taught down, four lessons plus one that went backwards. The farm notes for the story: The girl who counts the hens is handing her oldest keeping, and the handing runs on the farm's own law. The cut-down rod, the fifth hand, the land introduced not sold. Everything here hands.

The handing is the holding, continued. O. Fourth hand, witnessed by J." (And Joe's addendum, the different ink, four words, the late style at full compression: "The bear did good." The porch. The gold. And one more line from the rocker, off-book, saved by the story where the porch treasures live: "You know what the bear and the almanac got in common, darlin'?

They're both books somebody loved into being a who. The whole town's just books becoming whos, far as I can tell. Been reading it fifty years. Best library in the county." The best library in the county.

The story, a colleague, tips its whole archive. Four pages left. The gold gathers everywhere now.)
