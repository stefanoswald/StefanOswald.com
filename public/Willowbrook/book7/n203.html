Night 203

The Almost-Fight That Wasn't

Month 7: Making Things Right

This month's prevention exhibit, saved because prevention never gets exhibits and deserves them: The almost-fight. (The bench; a scheduling collision, the trio's fort day versus Lily's bakery shift versus Noor's family day; the irritations stacking; the voices starting their climb; the story has watched a hundred friendships fray on exactly this ordinary Tuesday shape) and the pause landed. (Hers first, the toothpaste law, self-applied mid-climb: the sentence already loaded..." You always do this. "the squeeze... held.

Nine seconds before, not after.) And into the pause, the craft: "Wait. We're not mad at each other. We're mad at the calendar. Three good things, one Saturday, that's not a friendship problem; that's a logistics problem wearing a fight's coat.

The fence rule: don't blame the wind. REschedule, don't re-litigate." (The calendar, fixed in four minutes. The fight, unfought. The toothpaste, untubed. The afternoon, saved whole.)
