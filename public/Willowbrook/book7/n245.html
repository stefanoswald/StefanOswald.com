Night 245

The Meeting She Stood Up In

Month 9: Brave Out Loud

This month's first field exam arrived at council: The skipped item. (The plan listed the school-crossing budget, the guard's corner; the new signal request; the kids' petition attached, and the session, running long, moved to table it. "Next month," the chair said, reaching for the gavel) The hand. The veteran hand, in its biggest room yet.

"'Scuse me. Point of..." She'd studied the forms; of course she'd studied the forms. "point of order, respectful: the item's got forty signatures and the signers can't come to night meetings, they got bedtimes; I'm pushing mine for this. 'Next month' costs forty kids another month of the corner without its signal, and the corner's held for forty years on one man's vest and wave, and the math, the math's in the packet.

Three minutes now beats a month of maybe. The chair's own plan promised us the three minutes. I'm just... holding the plan to its word." (The chair, later, looked at the packet, the clock, and the seven-year-old holding the plan to its word, and took the three minutes.

The signal passed committee that night. The gavel, afterward, privately: "you'll sit up here someday." Her answer, this month's whole plan in one line: "Maybe. Either way, I'll keep coming. The seats matter less than the showing up. The agendas need witnesses more than they need chairs.")
