Night 316

The Last Solo Meadow Walk

Month 11: Bobby Bear's New Kid

The meadow came for its designated night (this month's schedule; the bear keeps it; their bedtime world cooperates): The last solo walk. "The meadow's count grows soon, bear, his dreams reach the fence line already; the cow's been hosting him at the edges for years; the gate's season opens the whole geography. So tonight's the last walk as their bedtime world's only kid-citizen. This month said: take it.

Take it whole." The walk, taken whole: the gold grass, every remembered acre. The unloading dock (empty tonight; the freight all settled; the dock stood down like a harbor at peace). The fence line (the cow, saluted; the bell, rung once, politely, in answer).

The dream flowers' extra colors. And the gate, approached, this once, alone; the bear waiting back at the path's start by her own request ("the last solo walk ends at the door I'll never stand solo at again; the geometry matters; the ceremonies are geometry, mostly"), the keyhole, regarded; the warm shape in her hand, held up to its match, not turned, just... measured. Fitted by eye. Confirmed.

("Sixteen nights..." She told the gate. "The meter's read. The account's paid. The citizen's ready. And gate, for the story, since everything's the story: I'm not coming through you to leave the meadow. Nobody ever leaves the meadow, the rumor stays false forever.

I'm coming through because the meadow grows, and the growing goes through doors, and you're the door. See you with the bear. See you with everybody, eventually. That's what doors are for." The gate, the story attests, glowed at the keyhole.

Briefly. Warmly. The way doors glow when they're told what they're for by somebody who's right.)
