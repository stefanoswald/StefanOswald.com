Night 104

The Deputy Wednesday Inverts

Month 4: Theo's Big Sister

The Wednesday walk inverted this week, fully, formally, by the deputy rule's long arc: Theo ran it. (The route: his narration. The corner: his both-ways, twice, out loud. The guard: his wave, returned with the big-day nod; the guard reads successions; forty years of corners teaches you the handover walks.)

And at his cubby: the firework, initiated from his side, fists, fingers, three steps backward..." See you at the table. "and one addition, his own, added without consultation: a final point at the walker, and: "Good walk, deputy." ("He deputized me, Mama. The post's future tense arrived: someday the walk's his, we saved that two years ago; the half-step rule; official-instead-of-heartbreaking.

Today it went further: he ran the whole program and made me the deputy, and Mama, it wasn't cheeky. It was rehearsal. He's practicing the postholder seat while the postholder can still check the handoff. That's... that's exactly what I'd do.

That's exactly what I did. The pattern doesn't just carry the methods down. It carries the handover methods down. The way things worked teaches its own succession now. I could retire and the whole thing would run.

You're not retiring..." said Mama, amused. "Never. But knowing I could, that's the engineering certificate. The posts survive me. All of them. Even the me-shaped ones.")
