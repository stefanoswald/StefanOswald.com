Night 317

Lesson Four: The Bear at the Hard Times

Month 11: Bobby Bear's New Kid

Lesson four, the craft's deepest, taught plain because the craft teaches plain: The hard times. "The last lesson, Theo, and it's the one you can't practice, you can only pre-know it: someday the hard things come. The big ones. The Buttons-sized and bigger, the losses the whole shelf trained for without naming.

And the craft's last rule is for those nights: The bear holds what can't be said yet. The nights when the words won't come, when the lamp's too bright and the people too people, the bear takes the unsayable at full weight, no translation required. He held mine. The Duchess night.

The carrier night. The nights I couldn't tell even Mama yet, the bear held the yet, till the sayable arrived. That's the job's deepest floor: the bear is where the grief practices before it performs. Same as the truth.

Everything practices on velvet first." ("And the pre-knowing's whole point, Theo: when your hard night comes, and it comes; they come for everybody; the shelf says so and the shelf's never lied, you won't have to figure out where the unsayable goes. You'll know. It goes to the worry side.

It goes to the velvet. It goes to the room that never runs out. The knowing-in-advance is half the holding. The bear and the lamp and the whole kit: pre-added, pre-known, waiting.

Nobody in this family ever carries alone. The craft's last rule. The family's first one. They're the same rule. They were always the same rule.")
