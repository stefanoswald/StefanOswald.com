Night 364

The Handing

Month 12: The Maybe Year

On Night 364, Olivia carried Bobby Bear down the hall.

Mama and Papa stood in Theo's doorway. Above them hung the last two links of the paper chain.

Olivia placed Bobby in her brother's arms.

"He has room for every kind of needing," she said. "You can tell him the first messy version of anything. Take care of him, and let him take care of you. That has always been the arrangement."

Theo held the bear tightly. Then he gave Olivia a small book bound with string and one of Mr. Petrov's tiniest hinges.

THE BOOK OF THE BEAR, the cover said. BY THEO.

Inside, Theo had drawn everything Olivia had taught him about Bobby so he would remember, and so another child could learn after him.

Olivia gave Theo her secret gift: a cloth-bound book titled THE BOOK OF US.

It held seven years of stories, drawings, and pieces from the family recordings. Near the end was one copied page from Olivia's private notebook.

"This is what I wrote the night you said your first word," she explained. "Your first word was Livy. This whole book is me saying your name back."

Theo read the page twice. Then he hugged Olivia with Bobby between them.

That night the bear slept on the worry side of Theo's pillow beneath his welcome drawing. Nothing loved had been lost. The love had traveled down the hall and made another room its home.
