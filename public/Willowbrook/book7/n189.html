Night 189

The Broken Friendship Clinic

Month 7: Making Things Right

The apprenticeship franchised (everything franchises; the story has a stamp for it now): The repair clinic, at the exchange. ("Bring the cold friendships. Diagnosis free. The rules are public domain, they always were.") The clinic's case-flow, sampled: the drifted-versus-broken triage ("drifted needs a bigger wave; broken needs the rule; don't run repair on a drift, you'll make it weird; drifts want warmth, not surgery").

The nine-second window evangelism (taught to eleven kids; the take-back practice run in pairs, giggling, which is correct, part six lubricates even the medicine). The interest-clock arithmetic (day-two silences, priced; whoever's braver pays first; braver means remembered-the-worth). And the clinic's hard case: a friendship broken over something real, triaged honest: "this one's not a shim, it's a re-build. The trust got structural damage.

Rebuilds take seasons, both hands, and the spender goes first and pays retail: the full sorry, the changed behavior, the time. Some repairs cost what they cost. The clinic doesn't discount the real ones. Discounted repairs fail in the first storm."
