Night 162

The Hard Question, Asked at the Porch

Month 6: Grandpa Joe's Slow Stories

This month's hard session, run at the porch because she runs the hard ones direct, the honesty rule; Margaret's law; both ways, always: "Grandpa. The cane. The doubled Saturdays. The volume two bought and the last entry 'thinking on it two years.'

I'm almost seven, not almost four. Honestly: how many porch years we got?" And Joe, who wrote the rule's first edition with his own wife; who has never once broken it with this kid, gave it level: "Honestly, darlin': nobody's told me a number, and the doctor's word is 'years, likely, the slowing kind, not the cliff kind.' The joints will keep loudening.

The fields are handed off. The porch and the coop and the book, those I keep till the keeping's done, and the keeping's got no schedule I've been shown. So: years. Likely. Enough for the gate story you'll bring me this spring, and Theo's rope stories, and volume two's opening, enough for the next stretch, is the honest answer, and nobody, at any age, has ever had more than that.

The next stretch. That's the whole human ration, darlin'. I'm not short-changed. I'm rationed, same as everybody, and my ration's been..." the fields; the fences; the whole gold spread of it, "...my ration's been fifty years of Tuesdays I mostly caught live. Run At month's end, I come out rich."
