Night 281

The Gift for the Farm

Month 10: The Gift Month

The farm's gift, made in the book's own language (six pages left in volume one; the arithmetic gentle now, and unafraid, the porch taught the unafraid): The page-one draft. (Volume two's opening, drafted fair, framed for the porch wall, to hang until its day, then be copied in by the fourth hand at the gold-hour ceremony, exactly as planned:) "Volume two. The honeywell almanac, continued. Carried forward from volume one: The spring never failed yet.

The land with water keeps its promises. Mourn quick, replant quicker, read the loss. The fence don't care; the barns do. The row anyway. The best days wear ordinary clothes. Catch them live. The margins are for the meanings.

The book is a who. Fifth hand pending. Sixth hand expected. The book's always got room. Drafted by the fourth hand, in gratitude, for the day it's needed. Not yet. But drafted. The seed stock, pre-sown."

(Joe read it at the porch, twice, and hung it himself, beside the boy's "The book is a who" portrait, the wall assembling its own succession documents now, as it happened, the way this family's walls always do. His whole review, at the gold hour, one word, the highest: "Well." And then, this once, a second word, which they remembered in the drawer where the true weights live: "...darlin'.")
