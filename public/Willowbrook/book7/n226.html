Night 226

The Gratitude Delivery Week

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

The count's payment run (downstream and direct; this month does both; gratitude runs all directions at once): Delivery week. The route's stops, sampled: the counter (the town song's bakery verse, framed. Rosa hung it by the moment; it's there yet). The desk (a bookmark, sewn, gold thread: The light stays on, the librarian used it daily, sure enough, for years).

The corner (a thermos, the good kind; forty years of cold mornings deserved hardware; the guard's face did the barn-dust thing at a bench he breaks on now). The shop (a drawing for the window: the whole faculty shelf, portrait-style, the clock and cup and teachers rendered with respect, hung between the wrench-valentine and the first dollar). Number Nine, Number Five, Sixteen (eggs-plus, the dandelion doubled; no note needed anymore; the mark reads itself). And the farm, Saturday, the week's anchor: nothing hand-made..."

The farm's thank-you is the showing up, Grandpa; you taught me; the attendance is the gift. "just the whole Saturday, porch to coop to gold, attended entire.
