Night 194

The Apology to the Body

Month 7: Making Things Right

This month's oddest list item, self-saved after a dizzy Tuesday (the busy season's tax: the rushed breakfasts, the loophole minutes stretched too late, the body's logs unread): The body's repair. The diagnosis, run by the bench rules: the thing tells you, and the body'd been telling (the 2 p.m. Fogs; the crank Thursdays; the loud parts are never the broke parts, the crank was the wobble; the shortness was sleep). The repair, run in practice, not promises ("the sorry has a next-time or it's a report"): breakfast, un-rushed, ten minutes minimum ("the body's a colleague, not a vehicle, colleagues get meetings").

The loophole, re-fenced (fifteen minutes means fifteen; the union accepted the rollback with dignity). The water, carried (the coop rule, self-applied: winter's first job is water; so is every season's). ("The body kept every log, Mama, it always keeps them; it's the faifullest notebook I own and the last one I cross-referenced. This month's rule applies inward too: the thing tells you.

Sit with it till it talks. It talks in fogs and cranks and Thursdays. I'm fluent now. Another language for the collection, the first language, probably. The one everybody's born speaking and taught to ignore.")
