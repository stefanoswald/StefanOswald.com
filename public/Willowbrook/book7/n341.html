Night 341

The Braid: The Gone Ones' Night

Month 12: The Maybe Year

Braid night six, kept at the lamp with both parents, because the gone ones' thread braids at the family's own fire: The distributed, gathered. The office hours, held for all of them, one gold evening: Margaret (the honesty rule, recited; the wild row's fourth season ordered; the petrichor entry read from the almanac's photograph, "the correspondence never closed; it never closes; hi, Grandma; the okra stands"). Samuel (the pools; the rod, re-wound for its fourth hands; "he laughed at everything, so tonight's his part of the braid: the funny ones," and the funny ones ran. Roosevelt entire, the flush, the spaghetti helmet, because the braver-of-two gets kept in laughing; he'd have had it no other way, per every account).

Wendell (the whistle, produced from the pocket where it lives; one soft note, blown in the lamp-light; "the gentlest hand gets the gentlest instrument; the hurt ones' chalk mark glows tonight"). The duchess (the boot, saluted; the granddaughters' count, reported to her memory: thriving, bold, boot-inclined, "your line runs the coop, colleague; the alarm stays off; the farm keeps you"). Buttons. E.

W. And the evening's close, the rule said whole, three voices: "Nothing loved gets lost. The office hours never expire. The retelling is the rescue, and tonight everybody got rescued one more time, into the braid, where they ride from now on.

The gone ones come to the gold month too. They were always coming. They're braided in. They always were."
