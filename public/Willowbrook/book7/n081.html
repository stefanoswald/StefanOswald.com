Night 81

A Friendship Made of Letters

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

Olivia had exchanged letters with Jordan for four years, though they had never met in person.

"Can a friendship be made entirely of mail?" she asked Mama.

She opened the ribboned bundle and looked for the six things on her list. Jordan had written every few weeks through two moves and many seasons. The letters carried jokes, difficult news, and the sounds of two different towns. When both children stopped writing for six months, either one could have blamed the other. Instead Jordan sent: WE BOTH LOST THE THREAD. START AGAIN?

Olivia had replied: YES.

Letters even had quiet built into them. The weeks between envelopes never felt empty; they felt patient.

"I think the answer is yes," Olivia said. "A friendship can travel slowly."

Mama tied the ribbon around the bundle again. "Some of the strongest things do."

That night Olivia began her next letter: DEAR JORDAN, I HAVE BEEN TRYING TO DEFINE FRIENDSHIP. YOU ARE PART OF THE ANSWER.
