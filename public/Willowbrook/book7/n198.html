Night 198

Telling the Gate

Month 7: Making Things Right

The meadow, month's-edge, the gold path walked toward the gate by the year's rhythm, and this month, reported: "The list, Bear: the bench. The inherited feud. The mamas. The door at Sixteen. The audience-repair.

The kintsugi. The windows." The bear's assessment, delivered at the gate's sight-line, the keyhole gold in the dream-light: "The mending badges, Almost, the gate notes them all. And the bear adds this month's real entry to your meter, the one you didn't file because you can't see it from inside: seven years ago, the meadow's whole job was mending you, the freight nights, the unloading dock, the furnished dark.

This month you ran the unloading dock for a town. The meadow watched its own trade come back up the pattern, town-sized. Gates have opened for less, Almost. Gates have opened for far less.

Fifty-some nights. The account isn't just paid now. The account's got a surplus, and the gate's rules say the surplus goes with you, through. Whatever's behind the door, you arrive funded."
