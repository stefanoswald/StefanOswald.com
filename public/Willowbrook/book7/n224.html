Night 224

A Guide for New Neighbors

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

Olivia and Lily wrote a guide for families who were new to Willowbrook.

It included an ordinary map and a better one. The library lantern could be seen from almost anywhere. The sound of the brook meant the park was close. The smell of cinnamon meant the bakery, and halfway to school.

They listed useful secrets. The gap in the hedge at Number Eleven was permitted. The mitten tree offered what you needed and accepted what you could spare. The garden stand took coins, trades, and promises. The fort kept a loaner kite, the workshop lent tools, and the library never minded if you entered only to be warm.

On the last page they wrote: A NEW TOWN MAY TAKE TIME TO FEEL LIKE YOUR TOWN. KEEP SHOWING UP. LET PEOPLE LEARN YOUR NAME.

Copies went to the bakery, library, school, and town office.

Years later, other children added new paths and crossed out vanished ones. The details changed. The welcome stayed.
