Night 19

The Umpire Retires Herself

Month 1: When No One Is Watching

A retirement this week, self-ordered, that puzzled the whole court: The whistle, handed on. (The umpire post; two seasons held; the calls trusted; the crowd content. And the holder, resigning at the season's start, to the court's confusion and one fence-consultant's slow nod:) The reasons, given at the fence: "The calls got comfortable, Mateo. Nobody's disputed one in months, which sounds like winning and smells like something else: the court stopped checking me.

The chalk stopped getting looked at. 'She called it' ends arguments now, and no call should end an argument; the chalk should. A umpire the court won't check is a king wearing stripes. The post survives me or I hoarded it, and the next umpire needs the court's eyes back on the chalk, where they belong."

(The whistle went to the quiet girl from the poster project, lettering-precise, chalk-devoted, incorruptible. The court re-learned to check. The chalk resumed its reign. "The best thing a trusted person can do with too much trust..."

The notebook ruled. "is hand some back before it spoils. Trust keeps best in small containers, refrigerated by checks.")
