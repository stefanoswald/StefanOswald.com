Night 257

The Letter to the Editor

Month 9: Brave Out Loud

This month found a new venue: The town paper. (The weekly; four pages; the letters column mostly zoning complaints, and now, one properly formatted item from a new correspondent:) "To the editor: The new crossing signal went in tuesday. The town should know the whole story: Forty years of one man's vest and wave held that corner before the hardware came. The signal protects the crossing.

The guard built the trust the crossing runs on. Hardware is easy. Forty years is not. The paper prints the council votes; it should print the forty-years too. They're the realer news. O. Age 7, the corner's constituency."

(Printed. Page two. The guard, later, bought eleven copies..." One for the vest pocket; the rest for the grandkids. "and the paper's editor, meeting the correspondent at the counter, offered the only payment the column carries: "keep writing, kid.

The letters column needed somebody who saves the forty-years." She kept writing. The story has the clippings, years of them, a whole civic literature in four-page weekly installments. The venues keep growing. They never stopped.)
