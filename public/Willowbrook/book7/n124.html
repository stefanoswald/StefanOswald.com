Night 124

The Yesterday Room

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

Window two: The yesterday room. (Age five's question, from the question race: "when I remember, where do I go to look? Who lets me in?" visited now with seven years of equipment:) "The night librarians, Mama, the sleep-sorting; the brain's shelving. The memory's a library, that part I got years ago.

This month's upgrade: the library's not a recording. I checked, the remembering changes the memory; the book says; every visit, the shelvers re-shelve. The yesterday room isn't a photograph. It's a story the librarians retell, and retellings drift warm; the remembering game proves it; the snowplow's gotten better every year for seven years and nobody minds because the drift is the love, compounding.

So the answer to 'who lets me in': The teller does. I'm not visiting yesterday. I'm requesting it, and the librarians hand me the current edition, and the current edition's got all my visits in it. The memory of the boot has seven layers of missing-her in it now.

That's not corruption, Mama. That's the archive living. The records that don't change are the dead ones. Mine are alive. All the best ones are alive."
