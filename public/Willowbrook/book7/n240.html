Night 240

The Capsule Planting

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

The planting. (The capsule, steel, Petrov-sealed..." Rated for the fifty by a professional. "buried at the garden's corner, six feet from where the marble slept its seventy. The ceremony: the committee entire, the bench faculty attending, the councilman with the shovel's first honor, Gus-Senior with its last..."

The ground keeps its kids. "he said, tamping, "and now it keeps their mail too.") The lid's final additions, made at the lip: every committee member's hand-print, pressed in the fresh concrete cap. And one item added off-manifest, slipped in at the closing by the founder, witnessed only by the story and one five-year-old who keeps secrets at three-week durability now: a folded page. The notebook's own hand.

Contents undisclosed, even here; especially here. ("Some mail is for the fifty-years people only, Theo. The lock rule. The lid rule. The lid rule. You don't open other people's lids. Even when you're the one who filled them."

The lid closed. The ground took its mail. The story, which will be there at the opening; the story is always there, declines, per its whole office, to spoil the letter beyond one word: it begins "Dear.")
