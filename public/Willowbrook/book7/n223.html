The Town Anthem Project

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

The count demanded art (her counts always do): The town song. (Drafted at the piano, Papa consulting; the Gentle Brook's chords borrowed and rebuilt; the lyric assembled from the count's own tally book:) The verses, as premiered at the family table: "The water wrote the first draft / the tree said here first / three hundred years of porches / saying here till here was us..." (Verse two: the bread, the lantern, the corner, the grid. Verse three: the gone ones' wing, sung gentle.

And the chorus, built on the whole shelf's engineering:) "The town that made me / got made by whoever it made / circles, gold ones, all the way out / everybody's here gets kept, gets kept / everybody's here gets kept." (And measure twelve, as it happened with the joy it reserves for this exact move: a wrong note. Built in. On purpose, scored, permanent, "every town's got its crooked tooth, Papa, and the song should too.

The dynasty's whole law: claimed flubs become style. The town's full of claimed flubs, the leaning fence, the liar stone, the rustic rolls. The wrong note is the town. Anybody who plays this song right is playing it wrong.")
