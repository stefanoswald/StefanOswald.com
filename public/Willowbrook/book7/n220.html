Night 220

The Things Count

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

Olivia counted the things in her room that had taught her something.

Bobby Bear came first. He had listened through seven years of worries, jokes, fevers, and ordinary goodnights. The lamp came next; its click could turn an entire day toward sleep.

She listed the bicycles that had traveled to younger riders, the wheelbarrow with seven painted stars, the mending kit, the wind gauge, and Wendell's old whistle. None of them had been bought for this moment. They had gathered slowly through use, repair, and being handed on.

In the hinged box were the letter, the pressed leaf, and one space still waiting. In the treasure box were objects that no longer served their first purpose but had become better at remembering.

Last came the books, with the old bear story at the head of the shelf.

"You taught me the four parts," Olivia told it. "Lost. Sad part. Found. Goodnight."

She looked around the room. The things had not educated her by talking. They had done it by staying long enough to collect a story.
