Night 343

The Interview at Almost-Seven

Month 12: The Maybe Year

The fat notebook. The seventh interview. (The window seat this year, her choice; the questions deserve the gold view.) "Favorite color? Gold. Seven years tenured. The question can retire with me, we'll both come back next shelf as something new.

Favorite food? The harvest table, still. Plus the apple thing with no name, the half-hers one. Some foods are people. Write that. Best friend? Lily. Seven for seven. The before-memory spot, eternal. Full answer: Lily, the web, the town, the bear, the tree.

The category grew a whole atlas. What do you want to be? The keeping. It closed itself at the table this year, the looking-close-and-telling-true job found its name. A keeper. The town's got a rota somewhere with a pencil spot going to pen.

That kind. All the kinds. Scariest thing this year? The porch question. Asking it. The answer was years-likely and the asking still took everything. Best thing? The wave at Sixteen. One wave big. Counts entire.

Anything to tell Seven?... Tell Seven: the shelf ends with you. The gate opens with you. The last word comes with you. You're the year everything lands, and the whole six years before you spent every night packing your bag.

The bear says the books change shape after you, they become the ones I write. So tell Seven: Finish beautiful. The whole shelf's watching. The whole town. The whole, everybody. Finish beautiful and then... turn the page.

There's more book. The bear promised. The shelf never ends. It just changes whose hand holds the pen."
