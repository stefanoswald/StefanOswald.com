Night 91

Theo's Turn

Month 4: Theo's Big Sister

At the beginning of the month, Olivia decided it was Theo's turn to choose the brave thing.

First grade had filled his year with new doors: harder books, unfamiliar rooms, friend troubles, and fears that often arrived before he understood them.

"I can help," Olivia told him, "but I am not going to do the hard parts for you."

Theo looked suspicious. "What kind of help is that?"

"The kind where you tell me what the fear feels like first. Then we draw your map, not mine."

Olivia liked lists and steps. Theo needed to name the weather inside him before a list made sense.

"So if I feel stormy, we start with stormy?"

"Exactly. Then we find the first dry stepping-stone."

Theo considered this and nodded. He did not need a second Olivia walking half a step ahead. He needed a sister who could walk half a step behind, close enough to catch him and far enough to let him lead.
