Night 205

The Question Row Mends the Gardener

Month 7: Making Things Right

The storm's gift, delivered at the farm's question row: The resurrection fern performed. (The catalog's dare of dares, brown, curled, dead-looking for the dry weeks; the family had held law-faith: quiet-but-going; the brook rule in a plant. The storm's soak hit it, and by the Saturday visit: Green. Whole.

Unfurled. Hours, by the almanac's log. The bed's dead corner, resurrected on schedule, exactly as sworn.) The three generations at the bed, attending the miracle at porch pace: "The fern wasn't dead, Theo. The fern was waiting with its whole life packed, the cedar chest, but a plant.

The dry season took everything but the... the spark, and the spark is the whole fern, folded. One rain: unfolded." And Joe, leaning on the cane, this month's last slow addendum: "Fifty years, and I planted my first resurrection fern at seventy-some because a six-year-old's question row dared me to. Write that in both books, darlin': the old dogs plant new ferns if the young ones keep asking. The asking keeps everybody green. The asking is the rain."
