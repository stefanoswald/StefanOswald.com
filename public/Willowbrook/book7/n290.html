Night 290

The Gift Month's Hard Sum

Month 10: The Gift Month

This month's accounting session, run at the lamp with both parents (the family's counter general takes the big sums to the full board): "The sum, everybody: this month made, the scarf, the quiet book, the fair map, the brass treaty, the count of crossings, the registers and cards and birds and answered letters, the unsigned round, the perennials check, the operation-in-progress. And this month got..." The honest column; the check runs both ways or it's not a check. "the three weeks, twelve times over. The row-forty finding.

The whole-face smile, third instance. The waves at the arrow's base. The 'nobody ever counted back.' The two words at the porch. The books don't balance, Mama, the getting column's bigger, and I did all the giving!

The backwards economics ran the whole month at full tilt: every gift came back heavier than it left. The given column has no receivable side, I ruled that years ago, and the ruling holds, but this month found the footnote: the given column pays dividends anyway. Unrequested. Unreceivable.

They just... Arrive, in whole-face smiles and porch words. You can't book them. You can only... be enriched without a tally book line. The family's whole economy in one month: We're all rich in the column nobody can count."
