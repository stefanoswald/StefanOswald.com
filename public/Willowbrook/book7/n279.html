Night 279

The Gifts for the Grown Division

Month 10: The Gift Month

The grown division's gifts, made each in the recipient's trade: Mrs. Hollis: a bound blank register, spine-lettered "The friday log, volume two" ("volume one was the window's forty years, unwritten. Volume two gets written, by both of us; alternating entries; the book was always a who and now the who is us"). The librarian: a card.

Library-issue, hand-ruled, laminated at the same machine that laminated hers at four, reading: "Keeper's card. Holder: M. Good for: One former self from any shelf, anytime, forever. The building keeps yours too. A link."

Petrov: a whittled bird, basswood, thin-winged and proud of it, for the faculty shelf, tagged: "The first bird from the bench you built me. The faculty grows." Rosa: the honey-roll recipe, written fair at last, titled: "The answered letter. The correspondence of cooking, next line.

Write back." (She wrote back. A new roll appeared at the bakery that fall: "The O." Honey-crumbed. It's on the menu yet. The correspondence never closes. Not anywhere. Not ever.)
