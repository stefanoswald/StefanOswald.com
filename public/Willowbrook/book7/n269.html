Night 269

The Stand at the Shelf

Month 9: Brave Out Loud

A stand at an unexpected venue (the errand run; the next-town bookstore; foreign ground, the handles travel, by the whole law): The shooed kid. (A browser her age, clearly moneyless, deep in a chapter book, and the clerk's "buy something or move along," delivered in the tone shops use on the small and pocketless.) The customer's voice, spent from one aisle over, Mama's purchase in hand: "'Scuse me, question from a paying customer: does this shop want readers or just buyers? Because the readers become the buyers.

I'm the proof; I browsed moneyless for years at our library and my family's bought a hundred books since. The kid's mid-chapter. The chapter's free advertising. Every bookshop that ever survived knows it, the good ones keep a chair for it.

Where's your chair?" (The clerk, out-formatted, found the kid a chair. Mama bought an extra book..." The lesson's tip. "she said at the moment, deadpan. And the walk to the car held this month's traveling law, saved for all foreign grounds: "the voice works abroad, Mama.

The craft travels, the kind and honest, the staple-the-maf, the territory check. Only the geography changes. The rules came with me. They always come with me. That's what makes them rules.")
