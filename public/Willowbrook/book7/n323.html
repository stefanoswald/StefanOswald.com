Night 323

The Bears' Table

Month 11: Bobby Bear's New Kid

At the next book exchange, Olivia set up a table with a sign:

BRING THE TOY THAT HELD YOUR NIGHTS.

Thirty bears, rabbits, dolls, and blankets arrived. There was even an alarming green frog. Gus-Senior produced a knitted creature from his coat pocket.

"His name is Edgar," he said. "My wife made him. You wanted vintage?"

The children told stories about storms survived and trips taken. Then the grown-ups began admitting that many of their childhood companions still lived in drawers or on high shelves.

"I thought people threw them away when they grew up," Theo said.

"Some do," Olivia replied. "Some needs change without disappearing."

Before the table closed, everyone promised to greet one long-stored friend at home.

Bobby Bear sat in the center, patched ear forward, looking less like a toy nearing retirement than the elder of a very large and woolly profession.
