Night 212

Petrov's Story

Month 7: Making Things Right

As the month drew to a close, Stella dipped low above the Great Willow. The mending month, closing, and the watch did a thing it does perhaps once a generation: It issued an invitation. (The mechanism, the story can finally disclose: the meadow's guest rules; the dream-country's one-night visas; the wainscoting delivers the summons and the sleeper decides, and mostly they wake remembering nothing but a warm dream of a hill. Mostly.)

V. Petrov, mender, attended the session in dream, and the watch rose for him. "Forty years..." said Oliver Owl, the tally book open to a page prepared for decades. "The clocks, the zippers, the wheelbarrows, the spines of beloved books.

The weather-rates and the hug-fines and the columns no bank could check and every child's broken treasure treated like a cathedral job. The watch keeps the town's account, mender, and your column is the deepest uncounted one in it, the second grid's whole hardware division, one pair of hands, four decades. The town's things hold together. Held things hold people.

You have been steady, colleague, the entire time." And the mender, dream-easy, shop-apron even here, entirely unsurprised by talking owls in the way of men who have always suspected the world was better-made than advertised, answered the watch in his own style: "The things told me what they needed. I just listened. Anyone could.

Not anyone would. That I'll take. Now..." The professional eye, sweeping the willow's gold, the bark, the five words, the waiting space. "your tree's got one more word coming, I hear. Good joinery, that bark.

Whoever's doing your lettering..." A nod, craftsman to craftsman, at the whole listening hill. "tell them the town says the seams don't show. The town says it's the best work anybody ever did around here. The town would know.

The town's been the beneficiary. Mmm," hummed the Moon, taking the compliment for everyone. Goodnight, menders of Willowbrook, waking and dreaming, hardware and hearts. The seams don't show. The best work anybody ever did. Sleep.

(He woke remembering a warm dream of a hill. And a wheelbarrow with six stars on it got a seventh that week, unexplained, gratis. Some invoices settle in stars. In this town, most do.)
