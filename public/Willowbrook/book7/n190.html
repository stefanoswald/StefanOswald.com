Night 190

The Self-Repair Talk

Month 7: Making Things Right

This month turned inward, by the standing tradition (the mirror gets a session; the hardest customer keeps her appointment): The self-list. The check's finding. One unpaid item, excavated honest: "The Vane fight, Mama. The first week, before the mapping, before the airlock.

I saved her under wrong in seven days and I... Said things. At the lunch table. 'No-first approach.' 'The lists are her whole personality.' The web heard it. The web remembers things I say, that's the whole weight of the keeping-seat: my words get kept.

And the repair I ran was with her, the proposal, the translation, the corner she reads in now. I never repaired the audience. The kids who heard the wrong filing... some still carry it. I hear 'Vane the list-machine' at recess and it's got my fingerprints on it, a year old, still circulating.

The words outlive the take-backs if the take-back doesn't chase them all the way." The repair, run at the venue of the crime: the lunch table, addressed: "A year ago I saved Mrs. Vane wrong, out loud, here. The file was wrong.

The real file: the lists are a promise, this room never falls apart. She reads in the corner she almost didn't let me build. The airlock opens if you format proper. Update your saves. Mine's updated. The old words are recalled. That's the whole announcement. Pass the crackers."
