Night 48

The First Frost Comes Gentle

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

First frost, seventh of her records, and the tour ran gentle, veteran, this month's thesis frosted onto every surface: the tucked beds ("sleeping charged, not empty, the remembering tells them apart, and I remember everything"). The museum puddle, lidded, first exhibition of the season ("same museum, new collection, seven years running, the most reliable gallery in town"). The breath-dragons ("Theo taught his classmate, who taught her little brother, the dragons workshop now; the wonders all workshop; that's what wonders are for"). And the walk's finding, gloved hand in gloved hand: "The frost used to be the year ending, Mama, that's how I saved it at three, at four.

Now it saves under beginning: the lid era, the lantern era, the long winter's lobby, the fires. Same frost. Same me, technically. Different filing. This month keeps finding it everywhere: half of what we call endings is just...

Filing. And the filing is ours. The frost never once said what it was. We say. We always said."
