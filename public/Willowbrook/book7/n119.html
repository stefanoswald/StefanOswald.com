Night 119

The Mentor Month, Counted

Month 4: Theo's Big Sister

Month's end. At month's end, Olivia could count one hallway. One reading wall. One friend-fight (mended; the flower worked for its inventor). One solo line (eleven words to row three; the gym came down).

One green feeling. One bike lineage. One coaching mistake. One dream-wolf (asked its cargo; it was the wall; it usually is). One inverted Wednesday. One willow question (his; guarded; the tree didn't-not-say).

One rope rehearsal. One basement (his now; the furnishing kept). One soft landing, run upward. What mattered most could not be totaled: that you build the map in their order. That the shield is standing where the math changes.

That the furnished darks stay furnished for whoever comes next. That the turns are a relay, and his is built by mine, and the promise holds its date. "Good mentor-month? The handiest one, both meanings.

Mama, this month's last finding, found from the couch with a cold: the coaching seat was never a seat. It was a season. The seats dissolve when the methods go both ways, and they went both ways this month, permanently. I don't have a student anymore.

I got a colleague who's shorter than me. Temporarily shorter. The doorframe's already scheming." Remembered. A colleague, temporarily shorter. The doorframe schemes. The relay runs. On we go.
