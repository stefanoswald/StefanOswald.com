Night 140

The Fog Morning

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

This month got its weather-exam: The fog. (The thickest in years; the town, erased to twenty feet; the school walk, transformed into a philosophy field trip whether anyone ordered one or not.) The walk, run on handles: "The lampposts arrive one at a time, Theo, count them; the counting makes things visible; visible things are smaller. The cinnamon still lands at halfway, the smells don't fog.

The guard's wave comes out of the white like the white learned it. The lantern, look. The lantern burns through. Twenty feet of visibility and the lantern's glow reaches anyway, soft, exactly where it always is.

The handles hold, Theo. The whole town's erased and the handles hold, the smells, the sounds, the waves, the light. That's what handles are: the things that work when the seeing doesn't." ("The fog's like the unanswerables..."

The deputy saved, walking, his notebook thrives. "You can't see the whole. You walk it anyway. On handles. The fog's exactly like the unanswerables. This month ordered this weather, I'm pretty sure. The year provides.

It always provides." the story, which knows what the year provides and how, says nothing, per policy, and walks alongside in the white, handle to handle, the way it's walked the whole shelf.)
