Night 340

The Braid: The Watch's Night

Month 12: The Maybe Year

Braid night five came by dream, by their bedtime world's rules, and they remembered the session the whole shelf has waited for: The citizen at the session. (The meadow's guest visa, issued at last to its own first kid-citizen; the bear as escort..." The watch requests the pleasure. "the summons ran, wainscoting-delivered; "full session; the braid month's hill night; come as you are; you always did.") The session, as she attended it: the hill, gold.

The circle, entire, the owl and his glowing tally book; Stella, banked bright; Harry, leaf-lit; the postmaster with her satchel; the shelled and hatted specialists; the Moon, enormous and near and smiling its once-a-decade smile. And the business, one item: The recognition. ("The watch has kept this hill three hundred years..." said Oliver Owl, formally, the tally book open to a prepared page. "and has never once seated a member from below.

The motion, long on the books, moved at last by the bear, seconded by the entire assembly: that the small one, the keeper, the counter, the verb of the town, the asking's address, the one who asks, be entered in the eternal tally book as: Colleague. With all the standing the word carries on this hill. The vote requires no counting. Some votes never did.")

(Colleague. The word entered. The gold held. And her response, given to the assembled watch with this month's whole open-handed craft: "I've felt you my whole life, the gold that holds, the sessions that leak, the attending with the bigger angle.

Colleague receives. Both hands. And the colleague's first motion: The watch should know it's in the books. The shelf of me, everywhere it goes, you go. The keeping keeps the keepers. All the way up.

That was always the arrangement, whether anybody said it. Said now. Logged? Logged..." said the owl, and the tally book glowed. "Gold..." said Stella, and it was. "Mmm," hummed the Moon, twice. The third hum held.

The bark's night was coming. Everyone in the whole gold country could feel it coming.)
