Night 204

The Mended Month's Weather

Month 7: Making Things Right

This month got its stress-test (the months always get one; the year runs quality control): The spring storm. (The big one; the knees called it two days out; the town battened by the almanac's counsel.) The morning-after survey, run with Papa: The repairs held. (All of them: the eleven silenced gates, closed, latched, un-swung.

The east bench, dry-docked under Petrov's varnish, unbowed. The re-anchored mural bench. The fence gap's new section. The garden's young rows, staked by bench-wisdom, leaning and alive..." Bendy rides; stiff snaps; the willow's whole law, market-tested by weather.

"And the willow itself: gold and sprawling and unbothered, three hundred years of storm-craft, one blue ribbon weathering in its bark like it grew there.) ("The storm checked the whole month, Papa, and this month passed. That's what the repairs were for, not the calm days; the calm days don't need them. The mending is always a letter to the next storm.

'Dear weather: We fixed the gates. Try us.' And the weather tried us. And the gates held. The whole town, gold-seamed and storm-tested and standing, that's this month's diploma, bigger than mine. The town graduated. It graduates every storm it stands.")
