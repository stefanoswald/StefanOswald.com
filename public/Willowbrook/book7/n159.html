Night 159

The Slow Fence Walk

Month 6: Grandpa Joe's Slow Stories

Grandpa Joe walked the farm's boundary more slowly now, using his cane on the rough ground. Olivia carried the almanac. Theo carried the water.

At each fence, Grandpa told a story.

One line marked the place where Abel's barn fire had ended an old argument. Another bordered land now leased to the Petersens.

"They reset that corner post properly," Grandpa said. "Good hands. Leasing was the right choice, even on days I wish I could work it all myself."

At the north line he stopped beside a weathered maple post.

"My brother Sam set this with our father. He was eleven."

The post had stood for more than sixty years.

"You set a post straight," Grandpa said. "After that, you cannot command how long it holds. You just give it the best chance you can."

Olivia placed her hand on the wood. Once, an eleven-year-old boy she had never met had held the same place.

The slow pace had seemed like a limitation when they began. By the end, she understood that it was the only pace at which every fence had time to speak.
