Night 348

The Last Ordinary Days

Month 12: The Maybe Year

The final days before Olivia's birthday were deliberately ordinary.

She counted eggs at the farm. One bold young hen sat briefly on her boot, as if passing down the Duchess's old blessing.

She joined the park bench group. Every seat was full, and no one hurried to fill the comfortable silence.

She walked past the corner, through the permitted hedge gap at Number Eleven, and up to Lily's window seat. Their yearly friendship talk lasted nine seconds before becoming cocoa and laughter, because nothing in their agreement needed repair.

At the final dusk, Olivia stood at home with her family and watched gold light settle across Maple Lane.

"Good day," Mama said.

"Done now," Theo added.

"More coming," Olivia finished.

Three nights remained before the willow, the gate, and the handing of the bear. For this evening, no future event was allowed to steal the present one. The family watched until the last gold left the roofs.
