Night 210

The Wall's Fourth Law

Month 7: Making Things Right

This month closed at the hoop, by the family's constitutional process: The fourth stitching. (The candidates, per tradition, debated: "The gold announces the crack" (strong; kintsugi-approved). "The pause before the squeeze" (the fort's whole future). "Love things on schedule" (the board's motion).

And the winner, chosen because this month kept saying it in every voice. Petrov's, the bench's, the eggs', the fern's, the whole gold-seamed town's:) "Broken is just the first draft." (Stitched. Crooked. Gold thread, actual gold-colored, the aunts' contribution when consulted by letter: "The mending law stitches in the mending color.

Obviously. The helpers.") Hung beside its three elders: Kindest true thing first. Endings are the bottoms of slides. A friend is somebody you build a us with. And now: Broken is just the first draft.

(The kitchen wall, as it happened, is becoming legible as a single document now, the family's whole constitution, four laws deep, gold-threaded, kid-height, franchising through every guest who ever copies one down. The far pages hold nine. The story has read the whole wall. It runs out of kitchen before it runs out of true. It was always going to.)
