Night 282

The Gift for the Parents

Month 10: The Gift Month

The parents' gift, built in the family's deepest language (the keeping; the archive; the recursive love this house runs on): The keepers' Book. (Made secret across the whole month, the couch-office techniques, borrowed with attribution; the bear sworn by the eternal clearances:) The contents: seven years, from her side. The lamp, drawn at every age she can remember drawing it. The forecast, written down ("sunny with question clouds" through "gold, all evening," seven years of family weather, logged).

The doorframe's story, told from the measured one's view ("you held me flat at the baby line. I remember not-remembering it. The frame remembers for us, that's why you built everything that remembers-for-us; I know the whole design now; I've always known since I knew anything"). And the last page, the gift's whole cargo: "You are kept.

You are witnessed. The lamp will be here. You said it under every costume for seven years. This book says it back, once, plain, in my own hand: You are kept, Mama. You are witnessed, Papa.

The lamp I light someday, wherever it is, will be yours continuing. The keeping keeps the keepers. I'm the proof. Your O." (The delivery: the just-us breakfast table, off-season, unannounced. The story does not describe the parents' faces.

Some pages are for the attending. The book lives in the drawer with the letters, the drawer that waits, saved by Mama's own hands among the someday-things, which is exactly, precisely, forever where it belongs.)
