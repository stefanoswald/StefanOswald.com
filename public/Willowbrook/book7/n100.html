Night 100

Theo's Hundred-Night Review

Month 4: Theo's Big Sister

On the hundredth night of the year, Olivia let Theo lead the family's usual review.

He stood at the foot of her bed with a mug of cocoa and a page covered in crooked notes.

"First, Olivia found the wallet and told the truth about the cup," he began. "Then I got the dark hallway for my own. Boris says hello."

He remembered the repaired fight with the crane kid, the eleven words he had read at school, and the jealous feeling he had finally named.

"That one goes in because hard things count double," he said.

Olivia did not correct him. She listened as Theo decided which moments mattered and what they meant.

At the bottom of the page he had written: NEW RULES MAY BE MINE.

"Most of the rules came from you," he explained. "But I am getting old enough to make some."

"I hoped you would," Olivia said.

Theo grinned and lifted his mug. "Review finished. Pass the cocoa."

For the first time, Olivia heard a family tradition continuing in someone else's voice. It sounded different. It sounded exactly right.
