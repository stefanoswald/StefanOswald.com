Night 334

The Watch Stands Ready

Month 11: Bobby Bear's New Kid

As one month gave way to the next, the first stars appeared above the Great Willow. The bear's month, closing, and the watch assembled entire: every member, every wing, the fireflies out of season by special dispensation (the story doesn't ask how; the hill provides; the year provides), the whole three-hundred-year apparatus at post for the reading of the final schedule: "The final stories's calendar, into the eternal tally book..." Read Oliver Owl, the pages glowing faintly (the tally book does that now; it started this month; nobody comments): "The candle and the jar. The letters and the mornings.

The party of parties. The thankings. The word, the tree's last; the Moon's third hum, loaded these eleven months; the sentence completing after three hundred years of one-word-a-year patience across all its readers, six words for this one, the fullest sentence the bark ever wrote. The gate, the season arrived; the key warm; the meter surplus; the bear's walk, the one he was made for.

The handing, night 364; two operations converging on one doorway; the velvet moving down the hall at capacity. And the last page, night 365; the shelf completing; the words the whole series was built to say, said at last, and the question the owl has kept for seven years, asked at last." (The spectacles came down; the old bird looked at the sleeping town, the gold window, the chain unspooling gold.) "Colleagues: three hundred years of sessions, and the tally book's never held a week like the one loading.

All hands. Every night. The watch's finest season begins at the candle. Mmm," hummed the Moon, long, deep, and unmistakably twice; the second hum rolling into something that was almost, almost, not-yet the third; the hill held its breath; the Moon, the story swears, smiled, "...soon..." said the Moon. One word. Its first in years. "Soon.", glowing, entire, season-high. Goodnight, Willowbrook, whole and ready. All hands. The candle first. Then everything. Sleep, and gather your gold, dreamers. The finale begins.
