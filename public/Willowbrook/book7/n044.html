Night 44

The Almanac's Ending Pages

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

A discovery at the porch reading: The almanac is nearly full. (Fifty years of pages; the back cover approaching; maybe two years of entries left in it. This month's question, in binding form.) The panic, brief: "Grandpa, the book ends?!"

The answer, unhurried as everything at that farm: Joe reached under the porch bench and produced, new, cloth-bound, empty. Volume two. ("Bought it this month you became the fourth hand. Books like this don't end, darlin'.

They complete. Volume one gets a last entry, a good one, written slow. Then it goes on the shelf, done, whole, true, and volume two opens with volume one's best lessons copied onto page one, like seed stock carried to a new field. The book's a pattern too.

The volumes are its clothes.") And the porch's quiet second gift: "The last entry of volume one. I've been thinking on it two years. It's yours to witness when it comes. And the first entry of volume two..."

The old man looked at the fourth hand a long, level moment. "is yours to write. The handing, planned. The introduction, scheduled. That's how the menders do it, I hear. That's how everybody should."
