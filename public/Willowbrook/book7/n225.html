Night 225

The Debt That Can't Be Paid Back

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

The count closed its books at the lamp, and hit the arithmetic this month was always walking toward: The unpayable tally book. "The count is done, Mama. Papa. Every maker counted. And the total is... unpayable.

The town gave me: the corner's forty years, the counter's hundred, the lantern's hundred-and-twenty, the grid's aquifer, the gone ones' whole distributed everything. I can't pay it back. There's no back big enough. The tally book doesn't balance and it can't balance and, and everybody's just okay with that?!"

And Papa, from the bed's end, with the family's whole economics: "Everybody's okay with it because everybody's in it, kiddo. Ask Joe, the valley saw to his winter; he never paid it back; he paid it to forty years of chalk-marked strangers. Ask Mrs. Hollis about her forty years of Fridays, owed forward.

The tally book doesn't balance backward. It was never supposed to. The town's whole accounting runs one direction: Downstream. You pay the unpayable to the ones who come after, that's not a workaround, love. That's the design.

The debt is the engine. Every generation inherits an unpayable tally book and spends its whole life paying downstream, and the paying is the town. The debt is the town. You've been paying since the corner clinic.

You'll pay your whole life. It'll never balance. It'll always run. That's the good news, kiddo. That was always the good news."
