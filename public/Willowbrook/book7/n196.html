Night 196

The Words That Can't Be Unsaid, Taught

Month 7: Making Things Right

The clinic's lesson reached its hardest lesson, taught at the fort to the rising generation (Theo's cohort; the words are getting bigger in their mouths; the timing is the craft): The toothpaste demonstration. (The classic, run live: one tube, one plate, one volunteer..." Squeeze it all out. Good.

Now put it back. "The attempting. The impossibility. The lesson, landing the way only mess lands:) "The words are toothpaste, everybody. Out is easy. Back is never. The take-backs and the sorries, they clean the plate, they're real, they matter, but the toothpaste doesn't go back in the tube.

The person heard it. The hearing keeps. So the craft, the craft is the pause before the squeeze. Nine seconds works after. The pause works before, and before beats after in every trade there is.

The menders all say it: the best repair is the one you didn't need." (Theo's addendum, unprompted, from the sorry-flower industry's founder: "And if the squeeze already happened: Flowers. The flowers don't put it back either. The flowers just say I know it's out there and I'm sorry it's out there.

That's all the flowers ever said. That's why they work." they remembered the five-year-old's philosophy of apology beside the whole month and finds it steady. It always finds him steady.)
