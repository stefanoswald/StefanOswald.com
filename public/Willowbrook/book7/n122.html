Night 122

The Star Question, Upgraded

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

Window one, visited with Papa and a blanket: The stars. (The count question of the numbers month, grown up: "some things are for standing under," settled law. The upgrade:) "Papa. The starlight's old, the library book says.

The light left some of those stars before the willow sprouted. Before the town. We're not seeing the stars. We're seeing their mail, light-letters, posted centuries ago, arriving tonight. The sky is a post office.

The slowest mail there is, slower than the aquifer's, and it never once failed to deliver." The stillness; the blanket; the whole freight of it landing: "So when I look up... I'm reading. The oldest letters anybody ever got.

And Papa, the light that leaves the sun tonight, bounces off the town tonight, the porch lights, the lantern, the gold squares, that light's going somewhere too. The town is writing back. Every lit window is a letter, posted to whoever's standing under some other sky, centuries from now, reading us."
