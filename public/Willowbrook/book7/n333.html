Night 333

The Bear Month, Counted

Month 11: Bobby Bear's New Kid

On the last night of the bear month, Olivia sat between Mama and Papa with Bobby Bear in her lap.

She thought about what the month had held: lessons in listening, a room prepared for worry, a key measured but not yet turned, and several quiet nights when nothing needed fixing. Bobby had listened while Olivia practiced saying the hardest things plainly.

"Was it a good bear month?" Mama asked.

"The gentlest one," Olivia said.

She traced a repaired seam in Bobby's velvet. The mend was visible, and the bear was stronger for it. So were many things in the family: the old clock, the friendships that had survived mistakes, even the stories they told about grief.

Soon Bobby would move down the hall to Theo's room. Olivia could feel both truths at once. She was ready to give him, and she would miss him.

"Nothing loved gets lost," she said. "Sometimes it gets handed."

Papa wrote the sentence in the family notebook. No one added another moral beneath it. Bobby stayed in Olivia's lap until bedtime, keeping his first child for a few nights more.
