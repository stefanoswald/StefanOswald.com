Night 241

Thanking Grandpa Joe

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

Olivia saved the deepest thank-you for the farm porch.

She opened her bound gratitude book and read Grandpa Joe's page aloud: Saturdays, the almanac, the fourth hand on the farm, slow stories, careful counting, and a porch that had held every age she had been.

"Other people taught me things," she said. "You taught me to be the person who keeps them. You showed me that writing something down can be a kind of love."

Grandpa rested both hands on his cane.

"I thought I was just telling stories," he said.

"You were teaching me how to listen to them."

He tapped the book once with one finger.

"This farm has been making story keepers for four generations," he said. "You are only the first who arrived knowing the job."

They rocked without speaking for a while. Eight pages remained in the old almanac's first volume.

Olivia had worried about the day those pages ended. Now she noticed the blank new volume waiting on the shelf inside.

Keeping did not mean preventing an ending. It meant making sure the next hand knew where to begin.
