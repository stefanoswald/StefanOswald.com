Night 304

The Watch Prepares the Handing

Month 10: The Gift Month

At the month's quiet finish, Stella dipped low above the Great Willow. The gift month, closing, and the watch gathered in full for the reading of the schedule, the bear presiding from the trunk for the first time in seven years of sessions (the chair yielded gladly; it's his season now; the whole hill runs on his calendar): "The bear's month opens at dawn..." He began, velvet-formal, the job's weight worn like the honor it is. "The schedule, for the tally book: the preparation weeks, the small one and I close our seven years properly; the accounts of the needing, settled gold.

The teaching weeks, the new kid learns the bear-craft; not from me; from her; bears get handed, not trained; the handing is the training. And the last week..." The ear flopped; the hill held still. "the final stories entire: the candle, the harvest, the letters, the party, the thanking, the bark, the gate, the handing, the shelf's last page. All hands, colleagues.

Every night. The watch's whole three hundred years were, in a sense the tally book may quote me on, rehearsal for this month's minutes. And the bear himself?" asked Stella, gold and gentle, the question the hill had carried all year. "The bear himself..." said the bear. "is what he has always been: Made for this. The job was never the staying, colleagues. The job was the readiness, hers, built night by night for seven years, and now his, beginning. Bears are the scaffolding.

The building stands; the scaffolding moves to the next lot; the scaffolding is glad, write that in gold, keeper: The scaffolding is glad. And the meadow stays open, and the rumor stays false, and the needing never ends, it just changes address. I have a new address to learn. I've never been more honored in my velvet life.

Mmm," hummed the Moon, long, tide-deep, the sound of three hundred years leaning in. Entire. Goodnight, scaffolding of Willowbrook, glad, and moving to the next lot, and never once diminished. The bear's month opens at dawn.

All hands. Sleep, and dream well, dreamers. Their bedtime world's keeper is at his post. He always was.
