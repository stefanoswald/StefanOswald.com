Night 280

The Gift for the Guard

Month 10: The Gift Month

The guard's gift, built by conspiracy: The crossing count. (The project: every findable grown kid who ever crossed his corner, forty years of them; the web worked the phones and the counter and the exchange; the paper ran a notice; the names came in by the hundred:) The book, bound: "The corner's count. Forty years. Four hundred-some names and counting.

Each one a kid your corner carried to school and home again. Some are doctors now. Some are farmers. Four are teachers. One is a crossing guard, two towns over, who wrote: 'I do it because of him.'

The million crossings have names. Here they are. The constituency, with love." (The presentation: the corner, a Saturday, the deliverable delivered by the smallest current constituents. The guard read the first page. Stopped. Took off the glasses.

Read the crossing-guard-two-towns-over letter. And the story, which has a shelf for faces and a drawer for weights, saves his in both: "Forty years..." He managed, finally. "I counted crossings. Nobody ever counted back."

Somebody counted back. Four hundred names' worth. The count runs both ways. It always ran both ways. The town just needed its clerk.)
