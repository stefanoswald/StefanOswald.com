Night 54

The Snow Comes Back

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

First snow, seventh of her records, first of the shelf's last winter, and the greeting ran warm and veteran: the window vigil. The flake-catching. The morning count routes, pre-planned. The angel flock, forecast.

The lantern-building supplies, checked. And at the lamp, this month's closing observation, snow falling gold through the streetlight outside: "The snow ended, Mama, last spring, the last angel, the melt, the whole ceremony; we thanked it out the door. And here it is. Back.

Different flakes, every flake's different; the fern-confession; water can't stop telling the truth, and the same snow. The return. The pattern in its winter clothes. This month asked why do things end and the snow just delivered the answer to the whole window: Things end so they can come back.

The ending's not the opposite of the return. The ending is the return's first half. You can't come back if you never go. The going is the coming-back, started."
