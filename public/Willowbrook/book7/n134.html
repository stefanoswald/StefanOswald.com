Night 134

The Kid Answers

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

The round, run through the web (the best question at any table, asked at all of them): The kids' Cargo, collected: Lily: "Why do I remember the smell of the bakery from before I could walk? The memory library opens before the words do. What's shelved in there that I can't read?" Wren (written; her format; her right): "Where does the quiet go when the noise comes?

I think it waits. I think I'm where it waits." Noor: "Is the me who thinks in two languages one me or one-and-a-half?" June: "Do the bugs know they're small? Or is small only a thing from outside?"

Marcus: "Why do the arrows show me the pushes? Nobody taught the arrows. Who sent the arrows?" And Theo, tenderhearted, the round's deepest cut, delivered mid-snack: "When I love somebody so much it hurts a little, what's the hurting part?

The love's good. Why's there a ouch in the middle of the best thing?" ("The answers aren't the point..." The collector closed the round. "The carrying-out-loud is. Look at the bench, everybody lighter, nobody answered.

The questions wanted air, not solutions. The whole month in one recess: give the cargo air. The air is half the carrying.")
