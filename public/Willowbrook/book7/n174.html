Night 174

The Handedness of the Book

Month 6: Grandpa Joe's Slow Stories

The apprenticeship formalized this week (eleven pages left; the craft transfers while the transferring's good): The entry lessons. ("Volume two's page one is yours, darlin'. The craft should be too. Fifty years of method, porch-taught:") The lesson: "Rule one: The facts first.

Weather, counts, prices, the spine. Opinions sag; facts hold; you can hang a whole life on held facts. Rule two: The weight goes in plain. 'Margaret sick. The word the doctor used.' you don't decorate the heavy; decoration is hiding; the plain carries further.

Rule three: The different ink is sacred. The added-later lines are the book's whole soul, write today true, and leave margin for the person you'll be when the meaning lands. The margins are for the meanings. Rule four: Catch the good days live.

The rule under all the rules. You know it. You taught it back to me at six years old with a kitchen wall." And the graduation exercise: one entry, written under supervision, judged by the master, "Saturday.

Taught the entry craft to the fourth hand. She asked if the margins were for the meanings. They are. They always were. O."
