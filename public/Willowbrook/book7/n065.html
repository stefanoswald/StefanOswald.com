Night 65

The Quiet Component

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

Component five, measured at the bench with a stopwatch (she brought a stopwatch; the story loves her): The quiet test. The stories, gathered across the web all week: "Lily: Forty minutes of comfortable silence, window seat, both reading, the story. Wren: the quiet is her whole country; we picnic in it. Noor: maps side by side, no words, twenty-three minutes.

Grandpa: the porch hours run half silent, the catalog, the fields, the nothing that's everything. And the new friendships, this month checked, can't sit in quiet five minutes yet. The quiet clock starts at zero and winds up with the years. New friends fill every silence, the old finding, upgraded: the filling isn't rude; it's scaffolding.

You talk the building up. And then, floor by floor, the talk gets to rest, and the resting-together is the finished house. Part five: a friend is somebody the quiet can sit down with. The quiet-minutes are the friendship's rings, Lily. Count them and you know the age of the tree."
