Night 13

Kind and Honest

Month 1: When No One Is Watching

At bedtime Olivia asked, "What if the honest thing is not kind?"

Mama sat beside her. "Give me an example."

"If someone asks whether I like a haircut and I don't. Or if I know a surprise they are not supposed to hear yet."

They considered each case. A surprise allowed a cheerful change of subject. A haircut could be answered with something true that was not cruel: I like seeing your face, or You seem happy with it.

"Most of the time," Mama said, "honesty and kindness are not enemies. We simply have to look for a better sentence."

"And if there is no better sentence?"

"Then ask whether the truth must be said now, must be said later, or belongs to the other person alone. Waiting can be kind. Hiding something important forever is different."

Olivia wrote: FIND THE KIND TRUE SENTENCE. IF IT MUST WAIT, DO NOT LET IT DISAPPEAR.

It was not a rule for every possible moment. It was a lantern, small, useful, and bright enough for the next step.
