Night 105

The Question He Asked the Willow

Month 4: Theo's Big Sister

A first this week, watched from the hill's bottom by the family rule: Theo's solo willow walk. (His request, made formally at breakfast: "Wivy talks to the tree alone sometimes. I got a question for it. A alone one."

The grown-ups approved. The sister walked him to the bottom and held the rule line, hands pocketed, heart up, the watching-brave now fully hers to give.) The consultation, witnessed at distance: the small figure at the great trunk. The hand on the bark, low, below home, where his reach lives.

The question, asked. The listening, the long kind, the tree's kind. And the walk down, his face doing the thing faces do coming off that hill: rearranged, quiet, carrying. ("What'd it say?" asked once, by the not-digging arts, offered not pressed.) ("...

It didn't say. But it didn't not say." the exact answer, as it happened with something like awe, that she gave at four, word for word, unlearned, arrived at independently up the same hill. Some answers live in the tree. The tree hands them to whoever climbs.

"Saved as answered," the boy added, notebook-solemn, he keeps one now; of course he keeps one, and the not-digging held, and holds, and the question stays his. The story knows what it was. The story isn't telling either. Some things you guard instead of witness. The whole family knows the rule. The story is the rule, a little.)
