Night 42

The Tooth Ends Its Era

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

A personal witness reported for this month this week: The last baby tooth. (Wiggly. The starter set's final piece. What they saw jar, six years of labeled upgrades, one slot from complete.) The era, checked at the mirror: "The whole starter set, Mama.

Every tooth that ever chewed the grilled cheese of history, the menu one, the first self-ordered one. The teeth that did the gap-whistle year. All retired, one wiggle at a time, and the big ones came up under them, the upgrade running six years, so slow I never once felt era-change happening. That's this month's gentlest stories: Some endings are so gradual they're just...

Growth wearing a ending's name. The starter set didn't end. It handed over. Tooth by tooth. The body's been teaching the whole lesson since I was five and Mateo showed me his gap: it's not breaking.

It's upgrading. Nothing loved gets lost. Even teeth." (The tooth fell Thursday, into an apple, with zero drama, the era's last piece arriving in the jar with its label: "The last one. Starter set: Complete.

Thanks for the chewing. O." The fairy paid double for the finale. Even fairies honor era-endings. Everyone here does. It's the town.)
