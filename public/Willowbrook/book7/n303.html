The Gift Month, Counted

Month 10: The Gift Month

Month's end. At month's end, Some changes fit neatly on the page: twelve-plus made gifts, each in its reader's approach (the scarf's four mistakes; the blank book; the fair map; the brass treaty; the crossing count, nobody ever counted back; somebody counted back). One misread (the bracelet; the history that was loud; the rulebook that wasn't). One anonymous round.

One perennial check (plant things that give without you; the only making the dead keep doing). One workshop workshop (the making gives twice; the maker gets the realer half). One solstice succession (the doorway, taught by its second keeper to six more). One classified operation (in progress; the quartermaster's eyes bright; eighty nights, fewer now; they counted).

One ordinary week. The harder part to measure was that you can't wrap a round, the walking is the gift and the eggs are the receipts. That a handed thing knows the whole route. That the given column pays unbookable dividends and the family is rich in the column nobody can count.

"Good gift-month? The handiest and the heartiest, same month; the making's whole anatomy. And Mama, the last line before the bear's month: the gift month found out I've been giving since before I knew the word, the rounds, the systems, the perennials, the whole planted childhood. Which means this month wasn't training.

This month was recognition, the year showing me my own hands. And the hands are ready for the last two months: one bear to hand, one word to receive, one gate to walk. The hands are empty and full at once. Two coats.

The eternal two coats. On we go, the bear's month opens tomorrow, and the bear's been waiting seven years, and so, I think, have I." Remembered. The hands: empty and full at once. The bear's month opens. On we go.
