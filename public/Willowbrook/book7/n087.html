The Youngest Gear

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

This month's succession stories, observed from the bench with professional restraint: Theo and the crane kid. (Deputy and mayor. The friendship, building itself in real time, zero coaching, sure enough: she never taught him the parts. She never had to.

The house is the lesson.) the parts, observed engaging wild: attendance (the crane kid at the Wednesday corner now, waiting, part of the walk). Truth ("your bridge part is wobbly," carried; the bridge, rebuilt). Different. Repair (one fight over district borders; one sorry-flower, industrially sourced; one treaty by sundown).

Quiet (folding side by side, wordless, twenty minutes, the story timed it; the rings grow fast at five). Laughing. ("the pattern runs wild now, Mama. Nobody teaches it. The house taught me, I taught the bench, the bench taught the town, and now the five-year-olds assemble usses from ambient parts.

That's the last stage of any lesson: when it stops being taught and starts being weather. The friendship-machine is Willowbrook weather now. The kids just breathe it in.")
