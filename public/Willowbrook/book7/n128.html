Night 128

The First Question Ever, Revisited

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

The oldest window, revisited: Who asked the first question? (The owl's answer, five years kept: the look up itself was the question; the universe asking, in a small voice, with a notebook. The upgrade, worked at the lamp:) "If the owl's right, Mama, if the askers are the universe asking itself, then the whole month flips over: the unanswerables aren't questions I'm asking at the universe. They're questions the universe is asking through me.

The what-am-i-for. The why-something-not-nofing. The who-keeps-the-record. I'm not the asker. I'm the... the asking's address. The place the question lives while it's getting asked. And that means..."

The lamp burned; the equipment worked; the story leaned in. "the questions don't need me to answer them. They need me to carry them. Well. Kindly. Out loud. The way you carry anything that's bigger than you and staying: you keep it warm, you show it the world, you hand it on when your carrying's done.

The unanswerables aren't my failures, Mama. They're my cargo. The best cargo. The kind you carry glad."
