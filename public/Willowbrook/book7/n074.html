Night 74

The Sick Week Reversal

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

This month's field final: Lily went down. (The winter flu; five days; the bakery's daughter horizontal and the counter short-handed and the whole two-street world tilted.) The web, activating toward her, and someone saw six years of received care reverse its flow: the whistle, adapted (one long note at four o'clock daily, hers to Lily's window: still here, still yours, the old flu rule, returned to its co-author). The counter shift (Saturday; flour to the elbows; the apprentice program's alumna reporting for duty unasked, "the bakery is Lily's post; posts get covered; the covering is the friendship wearing a apron").

The serialized delivery (not horses, she can't draw horses. The bear book, read aloud through the door, one chapter per visit, the retired classic doing its four-thousandth night of service). And the map ("The classroom this week, so you know the territory when you get back..." Hand-drawn, every desk labeled.

Wren's old gift, franchised forward). (. "The web's whole test, Mama: can it run backwards? Six years of them carrying me, and the week asked: does the current reverse? It reversed. It reversed without thinking, the running went underground years ago.

That's the pattern's last proof: the parts turn both ways or they're not gears. They're just... Taking, with paperwork.")
