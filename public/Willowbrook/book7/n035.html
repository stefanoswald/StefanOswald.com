Night 35

The Ended Things Museum

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

Witness three: The box. (The treasure box, toured with this month's question, every relic checked: What ended, what didn't:) "The Duchess feaver: the hen ended. The truce didn't. I still walk slow-and-front at everything with dignity; the truce runs me now.

The red mitten: the winters ended, six of them. The constitution didn't, the fort's law says whoever's cold, forever, and it's colonized every fort since. The curl from the first haircut: that me ended. The... the me didn't.

(The frame keeps the lines and the girl. I checked at four. Still checks out.) The aunts' letters: each year's ended. The correspondence never, it's a pattern the family does. Earl's bus note. The first cubby tag.

The wall letter's copy. The word true in the hinged box." The finding, box-wide: "Everything in here is a ended shape, bear. That's what a treasure box is, a museum of ended shapes. And every single one has a not-ended thing still running out of it, into me, right now.

The box isn't sad. The box is the proof: the shapes end and the runnings continue. The endings are... the runnings changing clothes."
