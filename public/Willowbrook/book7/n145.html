Night 145

Olivia's Wonder Will

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

At the end of wonder month, Olivia wrote a will for the things that could not be put in a box.

I leave Theo the wonder walk, she wrote. Ask ordinary things what is miraculous about them until the street becomes new.

She left her friends the question round, in which everyone brought one mystery to the table. She left Willowbrook the monthly wonder exchange already running at the library.

To the next child who slept in her room, she left the unanswered pages at the backs of her notebooks.

Visit them, she wrote. Questions get lonely when nobody looks through them.

At the bottom she added: To anyone who helped keep my story, thank you. Please keep going.

Mama signed as witness. Olivia pressed Bobby Bear's paw to a gold ink pad and added his mark.

"Can a will leave things that are still alive?" she asked.

"Those may be the best things to leave," Mama said.

Olivia folded the paper into her wooden box. Wonder had grown larger whenever she shared it. Now she had arranged for it to travel farther than she could see.
