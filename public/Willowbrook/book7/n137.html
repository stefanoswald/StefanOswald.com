Night 137

The Wonder Relay

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

This month franchised itself, by the eternal law: The wonder table, at the exchange. ("Everybody's carrying one. The table trades them. Elders and kids. Cargo for cargo.") The trades, sampled by the story: Petrov's ("Why do things want to work?

A machine assembled right has a, a willingness. Forty years of feeling it click. What clicks?") traded for the crane kid's ("Why does folding paper feel like it already knows the shape?"). The professora's ("Which language do I dream in, and who chooses?") for Noor's one-and-a-half me.

Mrs. Hollis's. And Gus-Senior's, offered last, seventy years carried: "Why does the marble still matter? A glass sphere. A spoonful of dirt over it. Seventy years, and holding it is holding the whole yard, the whole boyhood, the whole... how does matter hold meaning?

Where does the meaning sit in it?" (The table couldn't answer. The table wasn't for answering. Forty people gave their cargo air in one afternoon, and the exchange, later, has run the wonder table monthly ever since. Some setup builds itself the moment somebody clears the ground.)
