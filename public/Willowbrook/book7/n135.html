Night 135

The Night Nothing Happened

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

The story submits, as this month's centerpiece, a night on which nothing happened, and does so under the almanac's oldest law (fixed the gate; made soup; rain at four; good day): The night, in full: the bath. The pajamas. The lamp. One chapter.

The forecast exchanged. The bear, tucked (needed same-as-ever; some nights are same-as-ever nights; the meadow's rules smile on them). The gold light. The goodnight. And at the door, Mama's pause, the old pause, seven years of it, and the exchange the story has waited the whole month to file: "Mama.

This is the answer to the whole month, isn't it. The unanswerables, the windows, the wonder, and the answer the universe actually gives, every single night, is... This. The lamp. The chapter. The goodnight. The universe declines to explain itself and offers, in exchange, tuesday.

Over and over. The owl's fair trade. I re-decide it every dawn too, Mama. I just never noticed I was deciding. And? And it's the best deal in the store. Goodnight. Good day. More coming."
