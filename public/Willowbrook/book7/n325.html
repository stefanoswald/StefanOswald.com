Night 325

The Willow Told First

Month 11: Bobby Bear's New Kid

This month's courtesy call (the carrying things get told; the oldest carrier gets told first, the family's whole rule, honored at the bark): The willow, informed. "The news, old friend, before the house makes it official: the bear moves down the hall this month. Night 364. The handing.

You watched him arrive, the crib year; you've watched the whole seven from your hill, so you get the telling first: The keeping expands. The bear goes to Theo at capacity, and the capacity includes every night you ever watched over, so really..." The hand on the bark, below the five words, beside the waiting space. "really a piece of you rides down the hall too. You're in the bear, tree.

You're in everything here. The count proved it: the town joined the tree. The bear's just... the velvet edition of the same joining." (The gold moved, the deep register; the whole-tree one. And they remembered what the hill's long grammar said back, translated per seven years of fluency: the tree watched the bear arrive.

The tree watches everything arrive, and grow, and hand, and stay-by-handing. The tree has one word left, and the word has waited eleven months, and the word, the gold said it plain, to anyone fluent, the word covers this too. The word covers everything. That was always the word's whole job. Soon.)
