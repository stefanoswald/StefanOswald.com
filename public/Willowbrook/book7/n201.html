Night 201

The Apprentice's Graduation

Month 7: Making Things Right

The apprenticeship completed at the counter, by the shop's forms: The diploma. (Petrov's hand; shop letterhead, he has letterhead; the story was as surprised as you: "Approved: The bearer has learned. The thing tells you. The loud part is not the broke part.

The windows matter. The gold announces. And the trade's last law, which cannot be taught, only confirmed: She came in already mending. Forty years at this counter and the trade's best students all arrive the same way: Pre-apprenticed by loving things that break.

V. Petrov, mender.") And the graduation gift, counter-slid: a kit. (Small. Real tools. The cut-down sizes, the whole town cuts everything to the next hands. "The first tool of your own bench, detective.

Every mender's bench starts with somebody else's spare kit and forty years of Saturdays. You've got the kit now and..." The teacher's arithmetic, warm. "more Saturdays banked than anybody I ever approved.")
