Night 266

The Youngest Chorus

Month 9: Brave Out Loud

The chorus drill's graduation stories, observed from the bench: The little district's solo deployment. (A blacktop moment, the name-wrong game, attempted on a new kid by a visiting crew, and the fist went up. Not hers. Not even Theo's.

Ruthie's, the crane kid's little sister, bench-stood-for herself last season, running the drill she'd been protected by:) The fist, held. The chorus, assembling around it. Theo, the crane kid, four more of the little district, and the three words, delivered by seven six-and-unders at once: "Use the name." (The visiting crew, out-choired, used the name.

The pattern died mid-assembly, textbook. The new kid got doored-in by lunch. The whole cycle, protected, then protector, ran its full circuit in one year, at age six, without a single senior officer on the blacktop.) ("The protected become the protectors, Mama, the chain's oldest law, but look at the speed now: Rufie was the name being wronged last season.

This season she raises the fist for somebody. The work of good runs faster every generation we drill it, the practice compound, the rules inherit, the whole blacktop teaches itself. I watched from the bench and contributed nothing. The nothing was the whole point. The nothing is the graduation. Mine.")
