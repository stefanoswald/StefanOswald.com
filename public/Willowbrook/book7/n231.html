Night 231

The Count Bound

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

The count, complete, did what her completed things do: Went to the shelf. (Bound, cloth; the aunts' overnight service again; "Counts bind in cloth. Everything important binds in cloth. The helpers," and delivered to the library as the year's civic gift: "The makers' Count of Willowbrook, taken in the seventh year of one kid, covering three hundred years of everybody.

Categories: The people, the places, the things, the gone ones, the invisible. Findings: The tally book doesn't balance backward. The kindness is older than the town. Everybody's here gets kept. Shelve under: Town. Or under: Love.

The catalog can decide. They're the same section here anyway.") (The librarian catalogued it, someone saw the decision, under both. Two cards. One book. "The catalog's first dual citizenship..." She said. "The book earned it."

It circulates yet, the story checks; steadily, decades, checked out by newcomers and old-timers and, once a year or so, by somebody who just needs to remember what towns are for. The book tells them. The book was built to tell them.)
