Night 108

The Reading Flow Arrives

Month 4: Theo's Big Sister

(the story promised this page at story 93 and the story keeps its promises:) february. The office-hours table. The truck book, mid-chapter, the miles seven weeks deep, and the boy, reading aloud by the routine, stopped narrating the words... and started narrating the truck. ("...and the truck took the mountain road because the bridge was out and. Wivy.

Wivy, I'm not sounding. The words are just... Coming. I'm thinking about the truck and the words are driving demselves...") The dinner test, passed live, mid-sentence, witnessed. The yell. The kitchen (ready; it had been ready for years; Mama arrived with the candle-roll before the yell finished, the pre-baked summit supplies; this family's whole craft in one warm pastry).

And the coach's confirmation, delivered over the crumbs: "The wall's down, Theo. The lights are all on now, the town's gonna start talking to you in marks everywhere: the signs, the menus, the cereal boxes. Welcome to their bedtime world. It never stops being good. Seven years in, I promise: it never once stops being good."
