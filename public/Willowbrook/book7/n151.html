Night 151

The Moon's Third Hum Approaches

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

At the month's quiet finish, Stella shone just above the Great Willow. And the watch, caught in this month's weather like everyone below, ran its own collection round: Stella's cargo: "Where do the set stars go when their children grow? Six above one roof now, and the lines I set decades back, over roofs whose children are grandmothers... I've never once seen a star come down.

The sky just gets richer. Is that the design? An economy of pure accumulation?" The owl's, renewed: the dawn-trade, re-decided this very dawn ("the universe declined to explain itself again this morning. It offered, in exchange, everything again.

I took the deal again. Forty-one years of taking the deal"). Harry's, sent up from the rounds: "Why does passing close help? The physics of the small warm thing. I've run the route thirty years and never found the mechanism.

Only the results." And the bear's, offered from the trunk, the year's own cargo: "What does the handing feel like? I'll know in eighty nights. First time in seven years the bear carries an unanswered-yet.

It's... invigorating. Bears understand her better every month." And the Moon, who hums twice for the biggest things and has hummed twice already this year, said nothing, hummed nothing, and simply glowed, a degree warmer than the almanac allows for the season, over the whole assembled watch and the sleeping town and one window with a chain unspooling beside it. "The third hum is loaded," Oliver Owl noted in the tally book, professionally.

"The tree is sure. The gate is read. The season approaches on schedule.", a degree warmer than the season allows. Goodnight, cargo-carriers of Willowbrook, above and below. Eighty nights. All of them counting. All of them counted. Sleep.
