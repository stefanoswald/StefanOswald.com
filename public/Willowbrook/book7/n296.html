Night 296

The Gift Nobody Wanted

Month 10: The Gift Month

This month's hard sorting, run at the winter drive's table: The unwanted bin. (The donations that arrive broken, stained, unusable, the discards wearing charity's coat; every drive gets them; the sorters know the sag of the bags before they're opened.) the rule, drafted at the sorting table for the drive's future guidance: "The drive's law, from the mitten tree's oldest teaching: The give should cost. The drawer taught me at four: the favorites, not the leftovers, if it's worth giving, it's worth giving the good one. The broken-toy bags cost nothing, they're closet-cleaning wearing a halo, and the kids who open them get the message the bag really carries: 'you get the leftovers.'

Nobody's winter needs that message. So: the drive sorts honest. The good: given, storied where possible. The broken: recycled honest, no halo. And the sorters' hard learned kindness: the leftover-givers don't get shamed, they get invited.

To the workshop. To the made-not-bought. Half of leftover-giving is just nobody ever taught the person the backwards economics. Teach them. The givers convert same as everybody: one good give, felt whole, and they never go back to the closet-cleaning. The felt-whole is the hook. It always was."
