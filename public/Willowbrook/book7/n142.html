Night 142

The Thing Olivia Is For

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

Olivia had finally found an answer to one of her oldest questions: What am I for?

Her answer was keeping.

She tested the word against her life. At the coop, she kept an honest count. At the library, she kept books findable. At the park, she helped keep agreements and friendships. Her notebooks kept questions. The family box kept the willow's words.

"Even being an umpire is a kind of keeping," she told Papa. "You keep the game fair."

Papa played a familiar wrong note on the piano. "There is one more part. The things you care for also care for you."

Olivia thought about the farm porch, the library lantern, the park bench, her family, and the friends who had carried her through hard days.

"Keepers get kept," she said.

The answer did not tell her what job she would have as a grown-up. It told her how she wanted to meet the world. That was larger, and for now it was enough.
