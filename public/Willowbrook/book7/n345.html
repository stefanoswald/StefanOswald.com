Night 345

The Aunts' Seventh Star

Month 12: The Maybe Year

The package. The twine. The early opening, seventh and last of the shelf: The seven-star sweater. (Willow gold, whole, and the seven stars arranged, as it happened before she does, in a line. A little line.

Like the one over the roof.) The letter: "Dear Olivia. Seven stars, in a line, like somebody's been setting them over your roof all along. The shelf ends this month, your Mama says. So the letter says the thing we've knit toward for seven years: The stars were never decoration.

The stars were a count. Seven years of you, accumulated, wearable. So that wherever the gate takes you, you wear the whole climb in. The constellation is complete. The constellation was always going to be complete.

Wear your years, child. All of them at once. That's what years are for. All our love, all seven of them. The aunts. P. S. The next sweater starts a new count. The knitting never ends. Neither do you."
