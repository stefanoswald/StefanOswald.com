Night 123

The Owl Consultation

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

The meadow came, and this month retained its expert: Oliver owl, consulted at the dream-willow, the tally book closed for once, spectacles down, the posture of the biggest questions. The exchange, question for question, by the trade: hers met with his ("What is the asking for? Because I've watched askers for longer than your town's stood, child, and the askers are never finished by answers. The asking is a way of touching the world.

You don't ask to close questions. You ask to hold things. Your what-am-i-for is your hand, reaching. The reaching is the for. You've been doing your for since the crib, the looking, the asking, the keeping.

The question answers itself every time you ask it. That's why it stays open. It's not a window, child. It's a door you keep walking through.") And his, offered back, the owl's finest, held forty years: "Why is there something rather than nothing?

I ask it every dawn as the town lights up. I have no answer. I have only the dawn, arriving anyway, every day, like the question's own reply: Look. Just look. Perhaps that's the deal, small one.

The universe declines to explain itself and offers, in exchange... everything. All of it. The whole gold everything. I've decided it's a fair trade. I re-decide every dawn."
