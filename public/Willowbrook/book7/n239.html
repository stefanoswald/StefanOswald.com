Night 239

The Hardest Thank-You

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

When Olivia listed the people who had made her better, one name kept being skipped: Mrs. Vane.

They had begun badly. Olivia had tried to help without listening, and Mrs. Vane had refused her careless proposals. Later, the teacher showed her how a good plan protects people, how a clear list can be a promise, and how careful formatting is a kindness to the reader.

Thanking someone after being wrong about her felt especially difficult.

Olivia stayed after class.

"Mrs. Vane, I have avoided saying this because it means admitting again that I judged you too quickly. You taught me to make ideas clear enough for other people to trust. Every proposal I write will be better because of you. Thank you."

Mrs. Vane removed her glasses.

"In thirty-one years," she said in her usual sharp voice, "that may be the best-formatted thing anyone has said to me."

Then her voice softened.

"I told the third-grade teachers that your quiet corner should continue next year. Some good work deserves to follow the child who began it."

Olivia left feeling lighter. Gratitude had cost her pride, but pride had not been worth keeping.
