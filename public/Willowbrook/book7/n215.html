Night 215

The Bakery's Hundred Years

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

Count station one, taken deep (this month goes deep at every station; the depth is the gratitude): The bakery's century. Rosa's telling, counter-taught: the lineage (the 1890s bread-shed; the four generations of ovens; the flour dynasty that Lily makes fifth..." She doesn't know she's decided yet. "Rosa said, flour-quiet, while her daughter braided dough in the back with the certainty of the born; "the trade knows before the tradesman; you know how that goes," the story knows exactly how that goes).

The war-ration years ("the counter ran on trades and IOUs; the books from those years are all neighborly trust; the bakery's always banked warm"). The listening-post accounting ("a hundred years of the town telling its under-conversation to whoever's got flour on their hands, the counter's heard everything, and kept everything, and the keeping is why they keep telling. Same as your lamp, little keeper. The furniture of trust gets built in every generation.

Ours is a counter. Yours is gonna be..." The flour-look, appraising, fond. "yours is gonna be something with a lot of shelves, I'd guess.")
