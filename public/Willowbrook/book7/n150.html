Night 150

The Question Month, Counted

Month 5: The Question Nobody Can Answer

Month's end. At month's end, The notebook could hold three back-sections, revisited. One sky post-office. One owl consultation. One yesterday room. One God window (the whole house at the glass; the mail matters more than the address).

One record question. One collection round (everybody's carrying; the air is half the carrying). One wonder table. One skeptic (cracked gently; nobody knows why cats purr). One closed window. One rope (crossed; the rule worked word-for-word).

One wonder will. Beyond the numbers, there was that the miracles got regular and this month's gift is the hearing coming back. That the unanswerables are cargo, not failures. That keepers get kept. That the stories were the lamp's excuse and the point was the us.

"Good question-month? The openest-windowed one. And Mama, this month's last line, for the check: I started collecting questions at four because I fought answers were the treasure. Seven years of collecting says: The questions were the treasure.

The answers close and get spent. The questions stay open and keep paying, wonder, daily, compound. The back sections are my whole fortune. I'm the richest kid in town and it's all in windows." Remembered.

The fortune is in windows. The final stories turns. On we go, eighty nights, all of them counting.
