Night 21

The Snack Stand Check

Month 1: When No One Is Watching

The stand's season-two check ran this week. Public, at the bench, because "honor systems owe the town their books; the trust compounds when the tally books show": The findings, read to the faculty: "Coins: some. Trades: eleven. Notes: forty-three.

Promises made: forty-three. Promises kept, forty-three. A hundred percent, two seasons running. The neighborly trust's default rate: Zero. The word banks should come study us." And Gus-Senior's new rule, from the bench's deep end, warden-wise: "Mark the reason in your book, child: the promises keep because the promisers walk past the stand every day after.

Neighborly trust runs on proximity. You can't default on a neighbor you'll see at gloaming, the seeing is the collateral. Cities lose it and invent banks. Towns keep it and invent... this."
