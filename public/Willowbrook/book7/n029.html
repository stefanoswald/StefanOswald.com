Night 29

The Month's Last Vote

Month 1: When No One Is Watching

This month's last exam came small and perfect, on the season's one lake-beach day: The shell. (A freshwater mussel shell, palm-sized, mother-of-pearl inside, the best find of the whole shore. The collection at home has a spot for it. Nobody watching.

The taking: free, harmless, legal, normal.) The vote, cast after one long look: Left. On a driftwood log, displayed, angled to catch the light. ("Not because taking's wrong, Mama, collections are keeping, and keeping's my whole trade.

But this month taught me to read my own votes, and this vote, today, wanted to be: the next kid finds it. The beach runs a lending library and I just... returned one early. The shell's more shell out there, being found over and over, than in my window being mine once. Some treasures appreciate in the wild.

The snow leopard rule, beach edition. The otter rule. The whole guarded-not-witnessed shelf of the whole kit.") (the story, which watches beaches too, confirms: a small kid found it within the hour. The yell carried down the whole shore.

The shell got found, that summer, by the story's count, six times. It's still out there, probably. Being found. Appreciating.)
