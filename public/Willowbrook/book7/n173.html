Night 173

A Quiet Sunday

Month 6: Grandpa Joe's Slow Stories

One Sunday afternoon, nobody told a story on the farm porch.

Grandpa Joe rocked with his eyes half closed. Olivia read beside him. Theo carved a piece of wood that he claimed would become a lying-down dog, though it still looked like a potato.

The catalog stayed shut. So did the almanac.

They heard hens scratching near the coop, the pump ticking, leaves turning in a light wind, and a tractor far across the valley.

Forty minutes passed without anyone trying to improve the silence.

As the fields began to turn gold, Grandpa opened his eyes.

"Well," he said.

Olivia looked up. The word did not ask a question or begin a story. It simply meant: Here we are. This is enough.

Theo held up his wooden potato. "Dog," he said.

Grandpa nodded. "Fine animal."

The three of them watched evening cross the farm. Olivia had spent years collecting Grandpa's words. That day she learned that his quiet belonged in the collection too.
