Night 342

The Quiet Day Before the Season's Heart

Month 12: The Maybe Year

The braid rested a day (as it happened the engineering: even finales breathe; the nothing-days are steady in gold months too; especially in gold months): The quiet day. Kept whole, by the masterpiece law: the route walked. The office hours held (the colleague across the table; the homework machine humming its work-song). The stand's box checked (one note: "Took radishes.

Left a poem." the poem, for the story: four lines about radishes, terrible and perfect; the box's economy expands into literature; the town writes back in every currency now). The gloaming window, attended in chorus. The lamp, the chapter, the click. ("The deep traditions start tomorrow-ish, Mama, the interviews, the doorframe, the packages, the whole gold ritual, and then the candle and everything after.

So today was the breath. The ordinary, kept whole, one more time, mid-finale, because the finale's whole point is the ordinary it protects. The word and the gate and the handing, they're not the treasure. They're the setting.

The treasure was Tuesday. The treasure was always Tuesday. The whole shelf says so and I checked every page: Tuesday, Tuesday, all the way down, gold.")
