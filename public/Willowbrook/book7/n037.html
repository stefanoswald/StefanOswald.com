Night 37

The Question Taken to Petrov

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

Witness five: The mender. ("Forty years of deciding what's over. This month needs your stories, Mr. Petrov.") The telling, given across the counter, wrench in hand: "The trade's secret, detective? Nothing is over. Things end service, the clock stops clocking, the vase stops vasing, but over?

The clock became my teacher, forty years on the faculty shelf. The vase became your garden's stepping stone. The zipper's crooked tooth became a whole philosophy, I hear, in a notebook that runs this town." (sure enough the mender tracks the notebook's laws. The whole counter-network does.

The town reads her the way she reads the town. It's mutual. It was always mutual.) "In forty years I have thrown away, truly away, ended-away, almost nothing. Things end their first service. The mender's whole trade is introducing them to their second.

Your family says it shorter: the sweater becomes the hat. So endings are... introductions that haven't happened yet. Now that..." said Petrov. "goes on my wall." (It did. Painted, over the workbench. It's there yet.)
