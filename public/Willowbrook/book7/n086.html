Night 86

The Solo Test

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

This month's control experiment, self-assigned: One saturday, deliberately alone. ("the pattern studies together. Science needs the control: what's the me without the web, for one whole rotation?") The day, logged: the office. The walk.

The drawing, the piano, the woolgathering hour. The bear (present; the witness-of-the-other-kind part; bears don't count against solitude, they're its furniture). And the findings, at the lamp: "Alone held, Mama. All day.

No lonely, the chosen-alone, the reading-tree kind; the solitude was full: the whole web was in here the whole time. Lily's rules in my games, Grandpa's methods in my hands, the wall's stitchings in my thinking. The control experiment failed, technically: there's no me-wifout-the-web to measure. The web's not around me.

The web's added. Solitude isn't the absence of your people. It's your people, running quiet, in the rooms they built in you. Lonely is when the rooms are empty. Mine are furnished. I furnished everything, Mama, the dark, the basement, the years, but the people furnished me."
