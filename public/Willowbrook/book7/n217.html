Night 217

The Crossing Guard's Count Entry

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

Count station three, taken at the corner's bench (Requested by the students, the plaque says; he sits it on breaks now; the setup serves its own maker): The guard's forty years. His count entry, given in the vocabulary of a man who counts crossings: "Forty years, miss. Call it eight crossings a kid a day, hundred-eighty days a year... I stopped doing the math at a million.

A million safe crossings. And here's the entry for your book: I remember maybe a dozen saves, the real ones, the darting kid, the sliding truck. The other nine-hundred-ninety-nine thousand were just... showing up. The vest, the sign, the wave.

The job was never the saves, miss. The job was being the certainty, the fixed point every parent's math could count on. You know what a kid's whole town needs to be brave? One corner that's always held.

Then they build out from it. I watched you build out from it, the wave, then the compliment, then the whole town. I was your first fixed point that wasn't family. That's the job. Forty years of being the first fixed point. Put it in the count under: Furniture. The steady kind."
