Night 207

The Apology Garden Blooms

Month 7: Making Things Right

This month's happiest setup report: The sorry-flower farm's spring. (Theo's bed at the lot, doubled again; the dandelion crop industrial; the drying racks, Petrov-built, egg-barter rates, full; the flower fund's first disbursement made: winter jars to three schools' worth of apology setup, via the exchange's kid-network.) The founder's state-of-the-industry, delivered from inside the bloom: "The numbers, Wivy: the farm shipped two hundred-something flowers this year. The fund's got eleven dollars and a button (somebody paid in button; the stand's economy; buttons spend here).

And the real number, the real number is the sorries that happened because the flower made the hard part easier. You can't count those. They're the dandelion fluff, they blow everywhere and land where they land and grow. The whole town's got sorry-seeds in its cracks now.

In summer you can see them come up gold." (the story, surveying the town's sidewalk cracks in summer, confirms the botany and the metaphor: gold, coming up, everywhere, in the broken places. The apology setup self-seeds. It always self-seeded. He just built it a farm.)
