Night 237

The Roads Not Here

Month 8: The Town That Made Me

The count's strangest session, run at the lamp with Papa (the what-if wing; every archive has one; most keep it locked; this month opens everything): "The near-misses, Papa. The town that almost wasn't: if the wagon picks the better-soil valley instead. If the fire of '34 wins. If the mill's whistle never shortens (Gus-Senior never leaves; the marble never buries; the garden's warden never comes home to it).

If Mama doesn't say oh on the porch. If the crackers don't match. If Lily's family picks the other street. The whole town, the whole web, the whole me, hanging off three hundred years of coin-flips that happened to land here...

And what does the count do with that?" (Papa, gently; the lamp holds the big ones; the lamp was built for them.) "... The only thing you can do with it: the gratitude gets bigger. The near-misses aren't scary, Papa, they're the price tag, showing.

Everything I love is this unlikely, and it happened anyway, all of it, every flip landing us-ward, three hundred years running. Either the luck is impossible or..." The window; the biggest one's neighbor again; everything connects to it now. "or the landing had help. Either way.

Grandma's move; undefeated; deployed at the lamp, either way: tend it. Thank it. Don't waste it. The unlikeliness is just the gratitude's exchange rate. And the rate says: everything I got is priceless. The count confirms."
