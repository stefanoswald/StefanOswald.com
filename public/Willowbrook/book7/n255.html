Night 255

The Debrief at the Bark

Month 9: Brave Out Loud

The news, delivered to its subject in person: The hill climb, town rule copy in hand, both kids. The reading, aloud, at the bark: "'... The eastern slope and watershed... Protected in perpetuity...' That means forever, in council.

The town voted. All of it. You're safe, the roots, the slope, the whole watching-spot. Six hundred names and a standing-only room. You sent the town and the town came." (The gold moved, the long, slow, whole-tree movement the story has learned across seven books to read as the tree's deepest register.)

And Theo, hand on the low bark, tenderhearted, catching what the town rule couldn't say: "It's not surprised, Wivy. It's... Thankful, but not surprised. It knew the town would come. It's known the town three hundred years.

That's why it wasn't scared." (A pause; the small hand flat on the old wood.) "That's what forever-friends are. You don't get surprised by the rescue. You just get thankful. The surprise would mean you doubted."
