Night 307

The Account of the Meadow

Month 11: Bobby Bear's New Kid

Account two, read at its own venue (the meadow came, month-faithful; the accounts travel to their grounds): The meadow years. "The dream country, bear. Our country, before it was anybody's: you stepped out of the dreams at three and became the walking-talking colleague; the only grown-up-sized magic that ever asked nothing back. The freight nights, unloaded together.

The dream-craft, the asking-the-wolf-its-cargo; you taught me the edges and I taught the edges down and the whole family runs dream-fluent now. The fence-line cow, invited. The key..." The warm shape; the three years of keeping; the gate, gold at the path's end, nearer every walk. "the key you gave at almost-five with the promise: not for the gate yet.

Yets have schedules. And the schedule's... almost here, bear. The gate reads the charge. The account of the meadow settles at the gate itself, that's the only place it can settle; the meadow's whole account was always payable at the door.

So the entry just says: To be continued. At the gate. Together. The way the whole country always ran." (The bear, at the gate's sight-line, velvet-sure: "To be continued. At the gate. Together.

The account's best entry, Almost, the only kind of settling the meadow ever wanted. Their bedtime world holds its two citizens. Soon it holds three. The meadow was always gonna grow. Growing's the whole..." the ear flopped; the gold moved; the sentence finished itself the way the meadow's sentences do: everywhere, and in her, and true.)
