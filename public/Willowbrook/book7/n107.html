Night 107

The Big Kid Shield

Month 4: Theo's Big Sister

This month's protective part invoked once. Once, the story specifies, because the part is calibrated: a bigger kid at the park, running the old deniable meanness on the first-grade district, and Theo in it, holding his own kit admirably but outweighed. The arrival, and the story describes it precisely, because the craft matters: not the swoop. The weather change.

She simply... came and stood. Adjacent. Present. Seven years of park-standing in the posture; the whole town's systems implied in one bench-calm silhouette. The bigger kid's arithmetic, recalculating in real time (this one's the umpire-emerita, the wall-taker, the kid the grown-ups consult), and the meanness, folding quietly, relocating elsewhere, the way weather-checked things fold.

("You didn't do anything," Theo said after, working it. "I attended. Attendance is part one of everything, Theo, friendship, posts, and backup. The shield isn't the fighting. The shield is the standing where the math changes.

You had the kit; you were running the kit; you just needed the numbers evened. That's the part: I don't fight your weather. I just... adjust the climate when it's rigged. The climate..." He repeated, filing it. His notebook, sure enough, now contains: "Backup = standing where the math changes.")
