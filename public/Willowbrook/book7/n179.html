The Month's Gift Back

Month 6: Grandpa Joe's Slow Stories

This month closed its accounts the keeper's way: The binding. (The slow stories, all of them, written down from this month's notes and the tape sessions, typed by Mama, bound in cloth the aunts sent overnight when they heard: "Seed stock binds in cloth. The helpers.") The title page: "The slow stories of joseph honeywell.

Told at porch pace, spring of his fifty-first farm year. Taken down by the fourth hand. The first saturday, the war years, the courting disasters, samuel, the book's birth, the best day, the town's debt, wendell and the hurt ones, the spring at the spring, and the view from the porch." The presentation: the porch, the gold, the family.

His hands on the cloth a long time. The four-sentence man's full response, all four spent at once: "Fifty years I kept the book. Nobody ever kept me back. Margaret would say this is what the listening builds.

Margaret would be right, same as always." (And the fifth thing, not a sentence, they remembered it as this month's true closing entry: he read samuel first. Alone, later, lamplight in the farm kitchen, the story attending through the window per its office. He read his brother's chapter twice.

And in the morning, the cloth volume stood on the shelf, not beside the almanac. Beside the cedar box. With the letters. With the four-sentence treasures. Saved, by its keeper, where the true weights live. There's no higher shelf in that house. There's no higher shelf anywhere.)
