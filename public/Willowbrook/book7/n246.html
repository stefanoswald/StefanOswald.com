Night 246

The Defense of the Substitute

Month 9: Brave Out Loud

Field exam two, at school: The struggling sub. (A new one; young; losing the room by inches, and the room, sensing it, beginning the old cruel arithmetic: the note, circulating; the coordinated pencil-drop planned for two o'clock; the pile-on assembling itself the way pile-ons do, out of nobody-in-particular's meanness and everybody's momentum) The voice, spent at the exact hinge (the story has watched her time these for years; the timing is the craft): not at the teacher's desk, at the room. Stood up, mid-circulation, note in hand: "This note stops with me. And the two o'clock thing is cancelled, and here's the whole why, fast: she's somebody's daughter learning her first hard room, same as we learned our first hard rooms, and nobody pencil-dropped us; they gave us the runway.

The pile-on feels like a game because everybody's holding one feaver, but she's catching the whole bird, alone, at a desk she's had for six hours. The room's better than this. I've seen the room be better than this. Prove me right by two o'clock."

(The room proved her right. The pencil-drop died unperformed. The sub survived to Friday, then thrived, the runway, granted, did its runway thing. And the room's own weather, as it happened, changed a degree permanent: the pile-ons got harder to assemble in that room, all year, because somebody had once stood up mid-circulation and named the whole machine, and named rooms don't run machines as easy. That's the brave voice's whole physics: spent once, it keeps discounting cruelty for months.)
