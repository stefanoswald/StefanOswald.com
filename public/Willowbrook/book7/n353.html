Night 353

The Maybe Night, Told Whole

Month 12: The Maybe Year

Night three-fifty-three. The night. The seventh keeping, and the founding text, told whole at last, the story setting down every word the way it set down the first ones, seven books ago: The maybe adventure, complete: The blizzard that ate the town. Papa's telling, unpolished, the fear real: the roads gone, the phones down, the car dead in the drive, and Mama nine days from Olivia with the appointment across town and the storm still coming.

The window. Mama's: every scared thing, said aloud, the actual list ("what if we can't get there. What if something's wrong. What if this baby comes into a world that's all storm..."). And Papa's four words, said into the dark of the worst night, not wisdom then; the story testifies; just a man reaching for the only handle left: "Maybe it's happening for us."

And the turning: the snowplow. Down their lane first, the lane it never took first, its lights coming up through the white like a big orange goose, and the driver leaning out: "Roads're closed everywhere but I'm headed past the clinic, you folks need anything?" The ride. The arrival.

The doctor's line, held tonight by the smallest teller, delivered with his whole feeling-first heart: "Perfect timing. The baby's got perfect timing. And then..." His part, run past its script, because feelers run past scripts. "and then the baby was wivy, and the baby grew up and taught everybody the maybe, and the maybe built the jar and the jar built the town's whole hoping, and the snowplow driver never even knew.

Somebody should find the driver. Somebody should tell him." (The candle held. The family held. And the maybes of the seventh year, spoken into the flame after, hers last: "The word comes in eight nights.

The gate after. The handing after that. Maybe it's all happening for us, and everybody, the stories's seven years deep now and the answer's in: It is. It always was. The founding text called it the first night and every night since confirmed it.

The jar rests its whole case." The flame, out on one shared breath. The night, kept. Seven keepings. A lifetime scheduled. And as it happened, for those keeping score at home: somebody did find the driver.

Next spring. Retired, two counties over, astonished. But that's the next shelf's story, the books change shape, and some of the new shapes drive plows.)
