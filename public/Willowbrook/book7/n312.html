Night 312

Lesson Three: The Talking

Month 11: Bobby Bear's New Kid

Lesson three, run at the student's own lamp (the craft moves to its future venue; the classroom follows the need): The talking. The practicum: his day, his lamp, the bear on lesson-loan, and this week, the real test arrived on schedule: a heavy Thursday. (The school kind; the friend-wobble kind; the freight a five-year-old hauls and half-hides.) The talking, run by the craft: the whole day, out loud, to the velvet, the good parts, then the heavy, then, the lesson's whole point, arriving live.

The part he hadn't told anybody: the worry under the wobble; the basement's basement; the thing that came out only because pure-ears-no-advice made the room safe enough for it. (The coach, listening from the doorway by the lesson's design, did not enter. The bear held the freight. The freight unloaded.

The boy slept easy. And the doorway got the lesson's graduation stories at breakfast: "The bear got the part I couldn't say to people yet, Wivy. The bear's the... the first draft place. You say it to the velvet till it's sayable to the hands."

The first draft place. They remembered the five-year-old's finding beside seven years of its own: the bear is where the truths practice before they perform. The craft transfers. The craft was always going to transfer. Lesson three: complete.)
