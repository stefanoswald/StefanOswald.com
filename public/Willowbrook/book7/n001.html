Night 1

The Gate Year Opens

Month 1: When No One Is Watching

On the first night of six, Olivia set three things beside the lamp: Bobby Bear, the warm brass key from the dream meadow, and the little wooden box that held the Great Willow's five words.

HOME. FAMILY. GROWING. KIND. TRUE.

One space in the box was still empty. One old gate still waited at the far end of the meadow. Bobby Bear had told her that it would open during the year she turned seven.

"So this is the last book on my first shelf," Olivia said. "One year to keep the key warm, find the willow's last word, and be ready for the gate."

Mama glanced at Bobby Bear. She knew that some bedtime truths arrived through him and did not need to be explained.

Olivia opened her notebook and added one more hope: help Theo begin his own birthday paper chain. Then she wrote the question she wanted to carry through the month:

WHO AM I WHEN NOBODY IS WATCHING?

She read it twice. The answer would not be one grand deed. It would be made from small choices, most of them quiet enough that only Olivia would ever know they had happened.
