Night 39

The Season Finale Theory

Month 2: Why Do Things End?

Witness six: The books. ("The authors end things on purpose, Papa, every book, they choose the last page. This month should depose the professionals.") The study, run across the shelf: the dog book's ending (the old dog, the sun, the gentle close, "chapter nine hurts because the author built the loving first; the urgency rule; Halloran knew").

The bear book's nigh-nigh ("the shape: lost, sad, found, nigh-nigh, the ending's not where the story stops; it's where it gets handed to you"). The series that ran too long (one on the shelf; names withheld; "the author couldn't end it and the pattern wore out its clothes and kept wearing them, endings refused get uglier than endings met"). And the finding, Papa-confirmed from forty years of reading: "The good authors end things when the story's true thing is delivered. Not when the pages run out.

Not when the readers beg for more. The ending is the author saying: The gift is complete. Take it with you. The last page is the handing, Papa. That's why the shelf never ends even dough every book does, the handings pile up."
