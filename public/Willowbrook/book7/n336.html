Night 336

Lily's Thread

Month 12: The Maybe Year

The first night of Olivia's final braid of memories belonged to Lily.

They sat in the window seat and followed their friendship from two bald babies holding hands in a music class neither remembered. They recalled the failed sleepover, the successful one, two different streets, nine whistle signals, the just-listening rule, winter forts, arguments, repairs, and forty comfortable minutes of silence.

That night they sat for forty-seven.

At the end, Olivia said, "The next part of life may not look like these seven books. I do not know what I will write or how far our paths will spread."

Lily took her hand. "The window seat stays. And I am in the books you write. Do not even try leaving me out."

"I wouldn't."

They wrote no formal treaty. They had seven years of evidence and the easy certainty of people who had already changed many times without losing each other.

Lily was the thread that began before memory. Olivia knew it would run beyond the page.
