Night 172

The Almanac Reads the Weather One More Time

Month 6: Grandpa Joe's Slow Stories

The annual jurisdictional match, run one more time because the family runs its traditions while the running's good: The forecast derby. (The radio: "clear through the weekend." The almanac plus the bones plus the cows: "storm by Saturday gloaming. Big one.

The knees say and the knees been right since the arfritis gave them tenure.") The derby's stakes, formalized by the bookkeeper: the planting schedule rides on it. The call, logged in both books. The watch, kept from the porch...

(Saturday, 5:40 pm, the gloaming barely gold before the west went the business color, and the storm took the valley exactly as the knees declared. The radio revised at 5:15, twenty-five minutes behind the arthritis. The tally book's entry, hers, gleeful: "The knees: Tenured. The radio: Still learning the valley.

Local-deep beats big-wide, forever edition." And Joe's, beneath, the different ink: "The joints take. The joints also give. Fifty years of weather in the bones now. The body becomes the almanac eventually. Everything becomes the book it kept. J.")
