The Thanking of Six

Month 12: The Maybe Year

The last night of six. The thanking, seventh annual; the shelf's deepest; the routine at full craft: "Okay, Six. Last night. Everything out loud, and Six, you get the long version; you earned it; you were the gate year: Thank you for the integrity month, the floor that held; the inner compass's graduation.

The endings school, the leaf, the corn, the real-costs-endings; the training that's about to run its finals. The parts of friendship and the us. The coaching seat, dissolved. The wonder windows, the for, closed from inside at a Tuesday table.

The slow stories. Samuel, said; the porch pace, added; the whole almanac of the heart. The mending apprenticeship. The count. The voice, fluent in every register. The gifts, given in twelve languages. The bear's month, the accounts, settled gold; the craft, taught backwards and forwards.

And the braid, Six. The braid most of all: you gavvered everything. Every thread of seven years, home, in one gold month. You were the year the whole shelf came true at once." The releasing, at the gold light's edge: "Go be a was-year, Six.

The best one. The frame keeps your line, the gate line. The folders keep your waiting. The box keeps your true. And tomorrow..." The bear, close; the sweater, chair-draped, seven stars in their line; the hill out there in the dark, sure as sure. "tomorrow the tree finishes the sentence it started when I was two.

Nigh-nigh, Six. Thank you for absolutely everything. Leave the door open on your way out. Seven's already on the porch. I can hear it. It sounds like gold."
