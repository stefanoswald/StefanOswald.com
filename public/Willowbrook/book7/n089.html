Night 89

What Friendship Month Taught

Month 3: What Makes a Friend?

On the month's last night, the gold-stitched sentence hung above Olivia's desk:

A FRIEND IS SOMEONE YOU BUILD AN US WITH.

Her notebook held the longer answer. Friends showed up. They told hard truths with care. They made room for difference, repaired hurts, shared silence, and laughed together. None of those gifts moved in only one direction.

The list had also changed while she made it. June added laughter. Mrs. Hollis explained that different parts mattered at different ages. Lily questioned every sentence that grew too certain. The definition belonged to the friendship that had built it.

"I thought I was studying my friends," Olivia told Mama. "But they were helping make the person who could write the answer."

Mama looked at the crooked gold letters. "That is what a good us does."

Before sleep, Olivia added one final note beneath the list: A DEFINITION OF FRIENDSHIP SHOULD ALWAYS LEAVE ROOM FOR A FRIEND TO CHANGE IT.

Then the lamp went dark, and Bobby Bear kept the quiet.
