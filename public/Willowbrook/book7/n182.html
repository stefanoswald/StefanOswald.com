Night 182

The Repair Shop Apprenticeship

Month 7: Making Things Right

This month opened with papers: The apprenticeship. (Petrov's counter; Saturday afternoons, post-coop; the mending trade, taught formal, "this month's about making things right, Mr. Petrov, and you're the town's teacher of it. The apprenticeship runs a month.

I sweep, I sort, I learn the bench. Payment: eggs, under the standing barter.") The teacher's opening lecture, one sentence in his school: "Rule one of repair, detective: The thing tells you. You don't bring the fix to the break, you ask the break what it needs.

Sit with the broken thing till it talks. Most menders fail by fixing too early." (The notebook, taking it whole: the just-ears rule. For objects. The rules converge, they always converged; this month is going to prove the town's whole repair-craft is one craft, and the story already knows it's right.)
