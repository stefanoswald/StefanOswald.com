Night 285

The Gift That Makes Itself

Month 10: The Gift Month

This month's botanical finding, made at the lot's planning bench: The perennial principle. ("Grandpa, the gift month's been all made things. The garden makes a whole other category: the gifts that make demselves. The dahlia couple's tulips: planted once, giving every April since, no further labor, the gift that regrows.

The shared bed. The sorry-flower farm. The bench itself. The whole lot: planted once, by a proposal, and giving daily ever since to people the planters never met...") "Now you've found the farmer's whole theology, darlin'..." said Joe, from the bench the exchange built him. "The annuals are the made gifts, beautiful, yearly, labor each time. The perennials are the institutions. You've been planting perennials since you were four and calling them systems.

The jar. The codes. The clinic. The exchange. Every one: planted once, blooming yearly, no further labor from the planter, they self-seed, they naturalize, they outlive. The gift month's last category, and you've been its whole nursery all along: Plant things that give without you.

That's the deepest making there is. It's the only one the dead keep doing." (The bench went quiet at the last line, the porch register, arrived at the lot. "Margaret's wild row..." She said. "Still giving.

Still giving..." He said. "Forty years of okra from one asking. Plant perennials, darlin'. Plant nothing but.")
