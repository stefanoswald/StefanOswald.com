Night 314

The New Kid's Room Prepared

Month 11: Bobby Bear's New Kid

This month's practical wing: The room preparation. (His room, readied for its incoming resident, the craft includes the logistics; the logistics are the love, conjugated, by the whole board:) The preparations, run joint: the pillow spot, chosen (his left side, "the feelers sleep on their worry side; the bear goes where the worry can reach him; I slept seven years with him on the right for the same reason mirrored; the craft adjusts to the sleeper"). The gold light's little brother, added (Petrov's counter; a small warm lamp; "the bear works best in gold; all the keeping things do"). The letter shelf, mounted (small; bear-height; "the annual mail needs a home, bears write to their kids; the tradition transfers whole; your first letter comes at your almost-six, and don't ask me how the bear writes; nobody asks how the bear writes; the mail just comes, and it's always true, and that's bears").

(And the room's last preparation, made by its future resident himself, unprompted, tenderhearted to the end: a drawing, taped at bear's-eye height beside the pillow spot, the two of them, boy and bear, hands-and-paw a shared scribble, captioned in his own letters: "Welcome. The worry side is yours. T." they remembered the welcome beside seven years of its finest documents. The room is ready. The rooms are always ready in this family. That's the family.)
