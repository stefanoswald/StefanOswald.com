Night 184

The Apology Check, Town Scale

Month 7: Making Things Right

The apprenticeship's eyes, aimed town-wide: The unrepaired survey. The list, bench-drafted: "Objects: the park's east bench (slats gone; the old ones stopped sitting that side; a bench out of service is a whole population out of service, the corner-bench law). The school fence gap (rusted, snaggy; one coat-tear a week, the nurse's log says. I asked the nurse; nurses keep logs; the town's full of logs nobody cross-references).

People: the four-square feud, generation three (two kids who inherited a grudge neither of them started, the Abel fence, kid edition). The two mamas who stopped talking over the parking thing (fourteen months; the whole street pays the toll of their silence). And the big one, saved respectful: the man at Number Sixteen, who came back from somewhere hard two years ago and hasn't taken a casserole from anybody, the quills, the chalk mark unread, the whole file. This month can't fix him.

This month can leave the door, the doors are half the fix; the hard case taught me at five."
