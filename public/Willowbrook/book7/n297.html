Night 297

The Time Gift

Month 10: The Gift Month

This month's scarcest-currency wing: The time gifts. ("The made things take hours. The rounds take schedules. The deepest wing takes the whole thing: Days. Given deliberate, one each, to the clocks that matter most:") The day gifts, given: Lily's day (the bakery shift taken together, start to close, "the whole day in her country; flour to the elbows; the counter's under-conversation heard side by side").

Theo's day (his plan, his rules: the ferry system's revival, the truck books, the fort, the corner race, "I lost the race by half a stride and the handicap's nearly zero, everybody; the formation's almost level; the day was his and the racing was real"). And joe's day, the deepest clock; the porch's whole gift: a full farm day, dawn to gold, no plan, the work shared at the joints' pace, the catalog read, the quiet Sunday's fluency run start to finish. ("The time gifts can't be made ahead or wrapped or stored, Grandpa. They spend now or never, the one currency with no jar.

That's why they're the deepest wing: everything else I gave this month, I could make again. The days I can't. The days spend once. So I spent them on purpose, where the clocks matter most.

Darlin'..." said the deepest clock, at the gold hour of his day. "that's the first economics lesson the porch never had to teach you. You brought that one whole.")
