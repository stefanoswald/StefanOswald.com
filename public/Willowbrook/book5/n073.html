Night 73

The Birthday Nobody Came To

Month 3: How Does Harry Feel?

Heard at pickup, secondhand, awful: a classmate, quiet kid, newer, Priya, had a birthday party Saturday. Two guests came. Two. (A sickness wave, a soccer tournament, and, be honest, the story insists, a kid not yet woven in.

The reasons don't matter to an empty table.) "The party's over..." She said at home, pacing. "You can't fix a party that already happened. The empty already got in her. So it's unfixable?" The pacing stopped. "...

No. It goes forward. The party's done but the birthday isn't, birthdays are a whole week if you believe hard. Lily. Call june." The operation: a "second Saturday." Six kids, the park, a cake Rosa donated to the cause, and a banner: "Priya week continues." Priya cried. The good kind.
