Night 26

The First Firefly Question

Month 1: The Question Collector

The year's first fireflies rose from the grass this week, the yard's oldest summer news. "They're back. Hi! Hi!" Then, newer: "Papa. The blinks. They're not just pretty, are they. They're saying something. To who?

To each other. Every blink pattern is a species saying 'here I am, anyone like me out there?' The yard's full of little lanterns all asking the same question." The stillness. The dark, blinking everywhere. "...

The whole yard is asking 'anyone like me?' Every summer. That's the firefly question. And every answering blink is a yes."
