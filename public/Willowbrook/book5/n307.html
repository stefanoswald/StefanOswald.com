Night 307

The Case of the Crossing Guard's Pockets

Month 11: The Kindness Detectives

Case two ran itself, the detective just finally saw what the route had been showing her for a year: The crossing guard's pockets. The surveillance log, one Tuesday morning, 8:00-8:20: "One band-aid, produced instantly for a scraped knee. One dog treat, for the anxious dog that crosses with the paper-route kid (the dog now sits at the corner voluntarily; the treat made the crossing safe for the whole route). One pair of spare gloves, small size, slipped to a cold-handed kid so smooth nobody saw, except a detective on assignment.

Two 'you got this', whispered, aimed, one at a test-day face, one at a Monday face. He reads faces like Harry reads feet. And the waves. Every car. Every car. Even the grumpy ones.

Especially the grumpy ones, I finally noticed. The grumpy ones need the wave more." The case conclusion, saved at the lamp: "Elias wrote 'He waves every day' and we all thought that was the important part. The waves are just the part that shows.

He's running a whole service station out of his pockets, band-aids, courage, dog diplomacy, and the uniform makes it invisible. Case finding: Some people hide their kindness inside their job. The job is the disguise."
