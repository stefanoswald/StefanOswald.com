Night 188

The Lunchbox Latch War

Month 7: Big Enough

Link six: The lunchbox. Gold, to match the bag. And guarding its contents: The latch. Press-and-slide, child-resistant, "Mama. If it resists children, and I'm a child, the box is fighting its own job."

Three days of thumb wars. The latch won rounds one through eleven. Round twelve: the shift. Not stronger thumbs. Smarter ones. "It's the button lesson. Stop fighting the latch. Negotiate with the lid, press down on the lid first, the latch goes loose, then slide.

The lid was the negotiable one. It's always the thing around the thing." The full lunch drill, run to completion Friday: box packed, latched, bagged, unlatched at the table-pretending-to-be-school, eaten, re-latched, home. Twelve minutes, start to finish, no casualties.
