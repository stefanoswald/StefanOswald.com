Night 170

The Pattern Hunter

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

One whole day, self-assigned: Hunt patterns. Findings, notebook, by dusk: "Fence posts: every fourth one is ficker. Petals: the crocus does 6, the daisy does lots-but-always-odd (checked eleven daisies, 21, 21, 34?? The big ones break the rule or have a bigger rule I can't see yet)." (the story, which knows about Fibonacci, says nothing.

Some doors she gets to open herself, years on, and the yell will be worth it.) "Sidewalk cracks: every three squares, the builders planned the cracking. Papa says they cut the weak line on purpose so it breaks neat instead of wild. They chose where the breaking goes."

That one stopped her cold, pencil hovering. "Mama. The sidewalk people knew it would crack, everything cracks, so they drew the crack a home before it came... That's the lamp. That's the whole lamp. The feelings come, everything cracks, so this family drew them a place to happen neat. The lamp is a expansion joint."
