Night 43

The Recipe Card Correction

Month 2: Mistakes Are How

Baking again, from Grandma's card this time. The old handwriting. The reader read it, and stopped: "Mama. This says three cups flour. But you always use two. The card's wrong? The card's wrong. Grandma wrote it in a hurry forty-some years ago.

Her mother caught it the first bake. Then why didn't anybody fix the card?!" Mama touched the crossed-out nowhere on the card, because there wasn't one. No correction. Just the error, standing, and next to it, in a second, younger hand.

Mama's own, age ten or so: "she means 2. Everybody knows. We kept the mistake because it's hers. In her hurry. In her hand. Fixing the card would fix her right out of it. So the card stays wrong and the family stays right, and every baker learns the story at exactly your age."
