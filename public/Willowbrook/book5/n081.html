Night 81

Grandpa's Quiet Day

Month 3: How Does Harry Feel?

Farm day. But Grandpa was... elsewhere. Present, kind, and elsewhere. Slow answers. Long looks at the middle distance. The porch swing, unmoving, mid-afternoon, a thing the story has never once logged. She didn't ask what's wrong.

(Faces defend. And some days aren't wrongs, she's learning, they're anniversaries.) She checked the almanac instead. Today's date, forty-one years back, in his careful capitals: "Margaret said yes. Under the willow. Told her the farm came with me.

She said 'Good, I wanted both.'" Grandma's yes-day. First one since, no. Forty-first since. But some years the old dates come in heavy. Freight days. So she ran Wren's rule, farm edition: near, not with.

Weeded the row alongside the swing. Read the catalog out loud, quiet, to no one in particular. Left a dandelion on the rail. At dusk he said, to the middle distance: "She'd have liked you past all sense.

I like her too. I'm the porch reader now. She keeps me company at it." The swing started moving again, slow.
