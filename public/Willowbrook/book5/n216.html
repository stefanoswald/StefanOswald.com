Night 216

The Monkey Bar Campaign

Month 8: The Settling In

The October forecast: early. (Like the sunflowers. The good years run ahead of their bears' letters.) The third bar, the yet from the playground map, fell on a Thursday. The campaign that took it: two weeks of hang-time drills ("arms are just legs that need convincing"), the swing-reach-catch pattern stolen from watching the big kids ("spying is studying when you do it with a notebook"), and chalk on the hands, Mateo-supplied, with ceremony ("the academy issues chalk at third-bar attempts.

It's tradition. Also it works"). Bar three: caught. Held. ("The catch is the whole game, the swing gives you one moment where you weigh nothing and the hand has to believe...") The full crossing, all seven bars, scoped from the landing platform, arms already lobbying for rest: "Four more bars.

The math says November. The arms say December. We'll split it: the arms get a vote but not a veto."
