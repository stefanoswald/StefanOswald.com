Night 117

The Librarian's True Story

Month 4: True Stories

The Hollis method, applied to the librarian this week: "Tell me your true story. The why-you're-a-librarian one. Everybody who loves a job this much has a story under it. Basements everywhere." The librarian, who has answered ten thousand questions and been asked about herself approximately nine times, closed the returns drawer.

"I was eight. A bad year, the kind kids don't get asked about. And a librarian, a great tall woman I never learned enough about, watched me circle the shelves for a month. Never asked what was wrong.

Just, one day, put a book in my hands and said 'this one's been waiting for you.' And it was the right book. Exactly. For exactly that year. I never found out how she knew."

A pause. "I became a librarian to learn how she knew. Forty years in: you learn the faces. The circling. You keep the right books in reach. That's the whole trade, being ready with the book before the kid can ask...

The card thing. The level-two reader with my name on it. You do it to everybody. It's been done to me. I'm just keeping the chain going."
