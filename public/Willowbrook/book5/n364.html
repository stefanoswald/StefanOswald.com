Night 364

The Year of Four, Counted

Month 12: Almost Five

The year's-end check. The big one. Twelve months, named like the old friends they are: "The question month, a hundred collected, the windows left open. The mistake month, the salt cake, the faculty of flops, wrong is right that hasn't finished.

The feelings month. Harry's feet, the basements, everybody's carrying. The truth month, lies are pets, truth weighs nothing, pay the one-time never the rent. The deep shelf, the chapter books, the underneaths, author to author.

The numbers month, the second grid of math, the layers, the standing under. The big-enough month, the pattern of me, the chain that was a mirror. The first day, the cubby that knew me, the bench, the beginning of rooms. The settling, the roots you never feel going down.

The friends month, the web, the rule, love is candles not pie. The losing month, the hot thing tamed, the bench empire, the how column. And the seeing month, the agency, the rivers, the eyes. Permanent, the eyes.

That's the whole book of Four. Three hundred sixty-five nights. Nobody skipped. Five books now, Mama. The shelf of me is five books deep." Remembered. Bound. Shelved beside its four elders. (the story, which is the book of Four, and the books of all the years, hello again, little one, from inside your own shelf, signs here: witnessed.

All of it. The year the world got rooms and the town got gold and the eyes got permanent. What a year. What a kid. One night remains. The sky's expecting us. It always is.)
