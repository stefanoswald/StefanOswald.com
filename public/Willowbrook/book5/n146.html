Night 146

The Reading Aloud Rounds

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

Mama drew it on the calendar this week, and the shape surprised even her: Monday: Theo's letter lesson (the campaign; the E is on; the M is next, "M is for moo, we're going through his interests"). Tuesday: nothing formal, but the story corner kids at the library expect her when she comes, and she comes most Tuesdays. Wednesday: the office, but Marcus climbs to the third branch now for the bed trick and b-d drills, disguised as comics. Friday: Hollis.

Tea, book club, the woolgathering file. Saturday: the farm porch. The catalog, the almanac, Joe's forty-year fence. "Look at it..." said Mama, tapping the calendar. "You know what this is?" She looked. The week.

The routes. The lamps, plural, all over town. "... It's rounds. Like Harry's. I do reading rounds. Something small and warm passing close, but it's books. I'm doing book-rounds. When did that happen? One Friday at a time, love. Same as everything."
