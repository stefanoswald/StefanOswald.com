Night 132

The Poem That Wouldn't Come

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

A craft crisis: she tried to make a poem happen this week. Sat at the window and demanded one. Rain and everything. Conditions ideal. Nothing. Forty minutes of nothing. The page: blank. The bounce: absent.

"The poems broke..." She reported, disgusted. "The first one just arrived. I stood there and it happened. Now I sit there on purpose and, nothing. Why does wanting it chase it?" Mama, folding laundry, offered the old truth: "Poems are like the barn cats.

Walk toward one, it's gone. Sit down and do something else, it's in your lap by dusk. Go set the table. Take the notebook. But set the table like the table's the whole job." She set the table.

Grudgingly. Forks, knives, the water glasses catching the gloaming light... and the pencil came out mid-napkin: "The glasses hold the gold / the gloaming pours it in / the table drinks the evening / before the soup begins. It came. I wasn't looking and it came."
