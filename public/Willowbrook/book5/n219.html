Night 219

The Class Jar Proposal

Month 8: The Settling In

The proposal went in Tuesday, delivered at the teacher's desk with the full weight of precedent: "Ms. Alvarez. At my old school we had a Maybe jar. When something goes wrong, you write it on a slip, 'maybe it's happening for us', and the jar holds it till you find out.

Miss Dana's room still runs one. I can cite the whole history: the rained-out picnic that became the puddle olympics. The broken projector that became shadow-puppet week. The jar works. Rooms need somewhere to put the wrongs while they turn into rights."

Ms. Alvarez, who kneels to talk, and who had already heard about this kid from three separate sources, said: "Bring me a jar Friday." The jar (from the family shelf; the shelf gives gladly to franchises): added. First deposit, week one, community-authored: the field trip bus broke down, "Maybe the bus is happening for us."

(It was. The replacement bus's driver turned out to do bird calls. The whole ride became an aviary. The slip came out of the jar in June, confirmed, to cheers.)
