Night 187

The Route March

Month 7: Big Enough

Link five: The route. Walked this week as a full family expedition, twice, once for learning, once for proving. The map, built aloud as they went: "Down the lane, four lampposts. Left at the green mailboxes.

The crossing with the guard, the waving one, from the compliment experiment. She remembered me. Two blocks past the bakery. And then..." The school, rising up ahead, brick and enormous and full of September. "There.

Twelve minutes. Eleven if the guard's crossing goes fast." The handles, located per law: "The lantern's visible from the corner, there's my first handle. The brook sound comes back near the bakery. And the school's flagpole, that's a new handle.

Handles work in both directions: I can find the school from home and home from the school. Home's just... the route, backwards. Home is always the route backwards." Theo, from the stroller he now rides only under protest: "I walk it too.

When I'm five." (You will, the family confirmed. The route holds. Routes keep, like stairs.)
