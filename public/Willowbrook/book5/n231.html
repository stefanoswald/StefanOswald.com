Night 231

The November Dark Adjustment

Month 8: The Settling In

The clocks fell back this week, and the dark started arriving before dinner, the annual ambush. Theo's response: outrage. ("The sun quit early. Who said.") Her response, year five of watching this particular show: stewardship.

"Fee-oh. The sun didn't quit. The sun's doing gloaming for some other kid right now, it's always gloaming somewhere, remember the globe. Our share moves to the morning for a while. The winter deal: less evening gold, more morning gold.

You just have to switch which window you catch it from." The family's dark-season adjustments, deployed like the winter things from the cedar chest: the lamps lit at four ("the house makes its own gloaming when the sky's runs short"). The homework office moved window-ward for the morning gold. The candles at dinner, resumed.

And the walk home from school rebranded: "the dusk walk now. Different animal. The town turns its lights on while you walk, you can watch the whole lane wake up its windows, one by one. The dark walk is the lane saying hi in light instead of waves."
