Night 154

Theo Counts to Twenty

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

The campaign's numbers division reported a breakthrough: Theo counted the stairs. All fourteen. Then kept going, on fingers, to twenty, with one casualty. "Nineteen-teen..." The faculty ruled. "is honestly better than nineteen. But the town won't take it.

The teens are a rough neighborhood, Fee-oh. Eleven and twelve don't even try to sound like teens, they just do their own thing, and fifteen should be five-teen but somebody smooshed it. Nobody designed the teens. They just happened, like the ough words.

Teens are ghosts?" (He knows about ghosts now. The lesson travels.) "The teens are haunted, yeah. Memorize them and move on. Twenty and up, the numbers get honest, twenty-one, twenty-two, they just say demselves.

The way things worked kicks in and you can ride it to a hundred... A hundred?!" (The look on his face: the exact look hers had at the sea-brook. There's always a bigger number. He'd just felt the edge of it.)
