Night 279

The Sore Winner Problem

Month 10: Losing Well

This month's mirror arrived via a four-square kid: he won, and he gloated. Full production: the dance, the pointing, the "Easy. Too easy. Who's next to lose?" The observation, saved from the receiving end: "Lily.

Losing has a hot thing. But winning has one too, a puffed-up thing that wants your mouth the exact same way. He's not enjoying the win. He's spending it. On us. And look at the faces, every kid he beat is planning to never play with him again.

He won the game and he's losing the whole court." The self-check, done that night with genuine alarm: "Mama. Do I gloat? The bean jar. I told four people the margin. The bars crossing.

I let the crowd count loud. Where's the line?" The line, worked out at the lamp: "Celebrating is joy at what you did. Gloating is joy at what they didn't. The crowd counting your bars: celebration, they chose to count.

Telling the beaten their margins: spending. The test: does the loser feel smaller after your joy? Then it was gloat. Joy that shrinks people was never joy. It was the puffed thing wearing joy's coat."
