Night 10

The Barber Question

Month 1: The Question Collector

First proper haircut this week. The chair: pumped up. The cape: on. The suspicion: high. "It doesn't hurt..." The barber promised. "I know that. Hair's not alive at the ends. I read it." (The barber blinked.

Adjusted his estimate of the chair's occupant.) "My question is different. The curls you cut off, they were on my head for everything. The deep end. The willow. The whole winter. Do the memories come off with them?"

The barber, to his eternal credit, took it seriously: "Memories live deeper than hair, miss. I've cut forty years of heads. Nobody's lost a thing in my chair except split ends." The cut: survived. One curl: kept.

("Not for the memories. Just, it came to everything with me. It earned a spot in the box.")
