Night 322

The Bella Return Case

Month 11: The Kindness Detectives

The season's most awaited case closed itself this week, in the garden, mid-May, right on the ancient schedule: Bella. Back. Rainbow wings over the crocus bed, then the fence post, then, the tradition holding, one long slow pass by the kitchen window, announcing. "Case: The return..."

The junior detective saved, breathless, from the yard. "She came back again. The fly came back." And the senior detective, watching the wings catch the light, saved the deeper pattern: "Fee-oh, that's the thing about the return, it's the year's most faithful kindness.

Nobody makes her. Winter's hard and the green room is dark and the wings are new every generation now, this Bella might be the daughter-Bella or the granddaughter-Bella, the book says they don't live years like us, but the return comes anyway. Every May. To this garden.

Something in the family of her keeps the appointment." A long watch, both kids still. "The town has the boots man's Saturdays and the guard's waves and the Friday knocks. The garden has the return. Everything faithful is somebody keeping a appointment nobody's checking."
