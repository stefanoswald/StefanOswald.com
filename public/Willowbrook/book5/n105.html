Night 105

What the Willow Never Says

Month 4: True Stories

Up the hill, month's-end, with the question this month saved for the top: "Willow. You only say a word a year. Everybody trusts you more than anything in town. Is that why? Because you almost never talk, the words can't go wrong?"

The gold moved. And the answer assembled, as ever, in the space between: Not quite, little one. Backwards, almost. The tree is trusted not because it says little, but because it has never once said a thing it hadn't finished being sure of.

The silence isn't the trick. The sureness is. It just takes a year to be that sure. And Oliver Owl, from the high branch, added the tally book's footnote: "Three hundred years, child. Home. Family.

Growing. Every word checked against every season before it went in the bark. That's the standard. You needn't say only one word a year. But it's worth asking, before the big ones: have I finished being sure?"
