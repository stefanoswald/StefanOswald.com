Night 159

Counting the Feeder

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

The feeder count went formal this week: a chart. Tally marks. Species columns. And by week's end, the tallies did the thing tallies do, they started talking: "Mama. Look. Chickadees come mornings, always first. The cardinal comes at gloaming, he likes the gold light, or the quiet, or both.

Rainy days: everybody doubles. And Thursday nobody came till noon and I didn't know why till I saw the hawk shape on the fence post. The chart knew about the hawk before I did. The missing birds were data."

The absence was data. (this month's biggest idea, arriving via chickadee: what doesn't happen counts too, if you're counting.) "The empty chair at story hour..." She said suddenly. "Sam. That was the same thing. I read a missing.

Counting isn't just for the there, it's for the not-there. The not-there is where the important hides."
