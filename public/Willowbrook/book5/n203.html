Night 203

The Night Before

Month 7: Big Enough

The night before. The story walks through the house and logs it all, because nights like this deserve full list: By the door: the bag, packed, checked twice, the just-in-case pocket loaded (bandage, hair tie, wrapped cracker, folded brave list, the kit, traveling). On the chair: the clothes, laid out like armor. The cardigan with its five defeated buttons.

The shoes, veteran-knotted, ready to double. On the nightstand: the alarm, set, its kind face glowing 7:00. The name tag O, practiced one final time, proud letters, standing like they mean it. And at the lamp, running long tonight, nobody needing to say why: "Tell me the snowplow story," she said.

(Not a Maybe night. But some nights need the founding text.) They told it, all four parts, Theo's line delivered from the doorway in pajamas before being herded back to bed. Then, at the very edge of sleep, the bear tucked close: "Mama.

Papa. Whatever tomorrow is... I did everything. The whole chain. The whole year. The whole almost-five of me. If tomorrow's big, I'm big enough. And if I forget I'm big enough... The list will tell you who you are," said both parents, together, the family's oldest ritual. "...

Yeah. Okay. Goodnight... Tomorrow, everything." Tomorrow, everything. Sleep, little one. The story will be there early.
