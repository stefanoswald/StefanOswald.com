Night 169

The Star Count

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

The clear dark night came, and she took the count skyward: Count the stars. (The jar method, "count a patch, count the patches, multiply. Joe's layers, but up.") The patch count began well: "That square by the roof: thirty-one... that one: twenty-eight..."

Then slower. Then slower still. Then Papa noticed the counting had stopped entirely, and the counter was just... looking. "Lost count?... No. I stopped on purpose. Papa, the patches don't stay counted. The longer I look, the more come out, little ones, behind the first ones, more and more, like the sky keeps saying 'and also... and also...'

The counting was making me look at patches. The stopping let me see the whole." A long pause, both of them under it. "Some things you count. Some things you just... stand under. The math knows which is which if you listen.

The bean jar wanted counting. This wants standing under." They stood under it a long time. The story stood with them.
