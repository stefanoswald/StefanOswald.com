Night 361

The Willow Writes: KIND

Month 12: Almost Five

The birthday. The walk. The whole family, golden hour, up the hill, fifth annual pilgrimage. Theo walking his own walk now. The five-star sweater, worn to the meeting, per rule. The tree: waiting. The hush.

The branches, parting in their private wind... And in the bark, beneath home, beneath family, beneath growing, the fourth word, fresh gold, first glow: Kind. (She read it. Stood very still. And the story heard what she whispered, before the family reached her: "...

I knew. I didn't say. You say it best.") The sentence so far, read aloud, her finger tracing air, the family gathered at the bark: "Home. Family. Growing. Kind... It's still building. Look how it builds, first the place, then the people, then the direction, and now... the how.

The tree's writing the instructions for a whole life, one word a birthday. Home, family, growing, kind. That's. Mama, that's already enough. If it stopped there, that's already a whole way to live. And it's not stopping.

There's more coming. Two more birthdays at least. The best writer in town, and it's saving its biggest words for last, that's craft, that's the cliffhanger trick, the tree knows the trick..."
