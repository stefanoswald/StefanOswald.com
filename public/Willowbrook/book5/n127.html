Night 127

The Library Sleepover Heist

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

The library announced it and the town's children lost their minds: The overnight. Sleeping bags. Between the shelves. Flashlights after lights-out, sanctioned. The trio attended in force. The night, as logged: The shelf-maze in the dark: better than any fort.

The librarian's midnight story, told by lantern light in a voice she never uses in daylight, older, riverish. ("She keeps that voice in the same drawer as the rare books," Olivia whispered. "Special occasions.") The 1 a.m.

Discovery, flashlight-lit, that the wainscoting has holes, small, regular, door-shaped if you believe, and Lily swearing something small crossed the reading room, upright, carrying what appeared to be a satchel... ("The mail goes out at moonrise," Olivia said, obscurely, and wouldn't explain. Some things you keep from even the treaty. Molly's route is watch business.)

Morning: waffles in the reading room, forty kids in sleep-creased pajamas surrounded by a million words.
