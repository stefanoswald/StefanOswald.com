Night 309

The Case Theo Cracked

Month 11: The Kindness Detectives

The agency's youngest asset (Theo; he demanded badges; the agency issued a junior magnifier and regretted nothing) cracked his own case this week: The report, delivered in crayon and at volume: "The kid at number seven. The big kid. Every day after school he gives half the snack to the dog behind the fence at number five. The dog waits for him.

The dog knows the time. I watched free days. Half the snack. Every day. The dog isn't even his dog." The senior detective's verification: confirmed, all particulars. And one addendum the junior missed: "Fee-oh.

The dog at Number Five is alone all day, the owners work long. The snack kid isn't feeding the dog. He's visiting it. The snack's just the... the ticket. The dog waits for the company.

Half a snack and three minutes of ears-scratching, every day, for a dog that isn't his, that nobody asked him to love." Case saved, junior detective's first, with senior addendum: "The snack is the ticket. The company is the kindness. Some agents are eight years old and don't know they're agents. The dog knows. Dogs always know. Dogs are the original detectives."
