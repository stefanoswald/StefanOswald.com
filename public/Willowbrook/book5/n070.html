Night 70

The Sorry That Wasn't Hers

Month 3: How Does Harry Feel?

Park incident: the fence gate left open, a scattered flowerbed, and a grown-up's fast conclusion. Marcus, nearest, got the blast. "Was it you?" It wasn't. (It was a dog. She'd seen the dog.) And Marcus, quills up, alarm on, was taking it silently, the way kids take unfair things from big voices.

The step forward cost something. She did it anyway, the hand-raise muscle, five months stronger: "'Scuse me. It was a dog. Brown one. Went that way. Marcus got here after me and I got here after the dog."

The grown-up deflated. Apologized, even, the small stiff grown-up kind. Mateo, who saw it all from the wall: "You know the best part? Marcus'll remember that longer than the yelling."
