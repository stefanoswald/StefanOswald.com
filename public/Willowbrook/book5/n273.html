Night 273

The Fireflies' Question, Answered

Month 9: Friends Old and New

As one month gave way to the next, Stella blinked once over the listening tree. The friendship month, closing, in deep winter, the fireflies long asleep in the thatch, their summer question hanging in the cold air where they left it: anyone like me? "The small one below spent this month answering it..." said Oliver Owl, reviewing the tally book.

"Shall I read the findings into the story? Read them..." said Stella. "Finding one: the answer to 'anyone like me' is never one light. It's a web of them, each different, each tied, no one hanging from a single string.

Finding two: the lights must be tended. Plucked, visited, paid-first, shown up for. An untended light doesn't go out, but it goes quiet, and quiet compounds. Finding three: ..." The owl adjusted his spectacles. "and this is the one the watch should carve somewhere: when one light went down with fever, the others burned toward it for five straight days.

Soup. Maps. Horses. One long whistle-note at four o'clock, daily. The web is not decoration. The web is how the lights survive the winter." Harry Hedgehog, in from his rounds, leaf dusted with snow, added the ground-level footnote: "I passed her window all five nights.

Never once passed it alone. The whole town's warmth was already going that direction. Nights like those, my rounds are just... confirming the town works. Log it..." said Stella. "The firefly question, winter edition: anyone like me?

Answer: no one exactly. Everyone underneath. And the tied-together lights outlast every cold there is. Mmm," hummed the Moon, tied to every tide and every window. Goodnight, tended lights of Willowbrook. Burn toward each other. Sleep.
