Night 96

The Secret vs. The Surprise

Month 4: True Stories

Theo almost spoiled Papa's surprise gift this week (a new whistle-kettle; he'd guarded the intel four days, heroic, for three). The near-miss raised this month's next distinction, drawn at the table: "What's the difference between a secret and a surprise? They're both don't-tells." Mama took it: "A surprise has an opening date.

It's a don't-tell-yet, and when the date comes, everybody's glad, including the person it was kept from. A secret with no date, that somebody tells you to carry, that would make someone sad to learn, that's different. That kind gets heavy. And here's the family rule, both of you, forever: any don't-tell that makes your stomach hurt, you bring to us.

Even if someone said not to. Especially if someone said not to. Grown-ups who ask kids to carry stomach-hurt secrets are wrong to ask. Every time. No exceptions." (as it happened: this paragraph is the most important one in this month. Some pages are for the parents. This one's for everyone.)
