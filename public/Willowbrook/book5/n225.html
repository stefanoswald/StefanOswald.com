Night 225

The October Crossing

Month 8: The Settling In

The arms' December estimate lost to the campaign's November projection, which lost to October's actual: The full crossing, all seven bars, on a cold-bright Tuesday, recess, witnessed. The story sets the scene: chalked hands. The recess crowd, gathering the way crowds sense history. Lily, at the far platform ("the catching end, every crossing needs somebody at the other side; that's not physics, that's just true").

And the count, taken up by the crowd, bar by bar: "Three... Four... Five..." (the weightless moments strung together now, each catch believing before its proof) "... Six... Seven!!" The platform. The landing. The roar. And Mateo, arriving at the fence after school with the report already ahead of him: "Seven bars at almost-five.

The academy's records are falling. What's next, the fireman pole? The fireman pole..." She confirmed, already scoping it. "And then the big kids' wall. And then..." The grin, sideways. "whatever's after the wall. The yets don't end, Mateo. I checked. They're like the numbers. The yets are a direction."
