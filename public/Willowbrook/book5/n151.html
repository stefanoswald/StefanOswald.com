Night 151

Molly's Four-Valley Library

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

As the month drew to a close, Stella's light touched the willow's highest branches. The reading month, closing, and Molly Mouse opened her satchel with ceremony. "The watch should know: the mail route carries more than letters now." She produced them: Books.

Tiny ones. Stamp-sized, seed-paper pages, scratch-script. "The four-valley lending library. Forty-one volumes in circulation. The field mice write memoirs. The barn mice prefer adventure. The literary vole has produced a trilogy, dense, but rewarding.

And the late fees are paid in seeds, which is how the library also became a granary, which is how the readers also survive the winter. Books and bread from the same building. It's the oldest arrangement there is. And the small one below?" asked Oliver Owl.

"Her month? She joined every chain there is to join..." said Molly. "The readers' chain. The tellers'. The keepers'. The rescuers of words. She reads to the tree now..." The satchel closed with a snap. "which even the four valleys have never produced.

The vole wants to interview her. I've told him she's not available for some years yet. Log it..." said Stella. "this month the reader became a librarian, in every way but the job. Mmm..." Hummed the Moon.

"The job can wait. The rounds can't. She knows which matters." Goodnight, readers of Willowbrook, stamp-sized and growing and three hundred years old. The mail goes out at moonrise. Forty-one volumes and counting. Renew your loans. Sleep.
