Night 258

The Six-Month Friendversary

Month 9: Friends Old and New

The tally book of days flagged it: six months since the first day, which means six months since the matched crackers, which means: The friendversary. (A word she built for the occasion. The sanctuary accepts domestic coinages.) The observance, lunch table: matched crackers, ceremonially traded.

The origin story, retold in duet, "you had the same crackers...and you said 'the odds of that'...and you said 'my brothers eat every other kind'...and that was the whole beginning..." (Origin stories improve with duet telling. All the good ones become responsive readings eventually. This family taught her that; the snowplow runs four-handed now.)

And the state-of-the-friendship, exchanged over the ceremonial crackers: one fight survived. One epic of horse drawings (the get-well serial, bound now, string through punched holes, "Volume one..." Optimistically). One papa-shaped quiet, still unbothered, still not dug (. "the not-digging holds. She'll say when saying's right. Or never. Both are hers").
