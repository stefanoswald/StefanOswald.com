Night 295

The Season Standings

Month 10: Losing Well

Porch, spring evening, gloaming coming on, and Papa proposed the year's strangest check: The career record. Everything competitive, all year, both columns, one honest tally. The standings, compiled: "Wins: the bean jar. Bar seven. The pole.

Three tournament games. The bench crown. Checkers (mostly, the era of mostly is ending, the trap proved it). Losses: the fun run. Musical chairs round one. The spelling ghosts. The kickball call (wrong, stood, put down.

Mostly put down). The fox trade. The frost tomatoes. Twelve-ish board games. The recital rain." The columns, weighed at the rail: "Papa. The losses column is longer. By a lot. And here's this month's final finding: the losses column taught more.

The wins are... photographs. Nice ones. The losses are the whole faculty, the tuition fox, the graduated swim, the accurate album, the bench empire, the put-down grudge. If somebody offered to trade me a all-wins year for this one, straight across...

Would you take it?... No. And a year ago I would've said yes so fast. That's the program. That's the whole month, right there in one no."
