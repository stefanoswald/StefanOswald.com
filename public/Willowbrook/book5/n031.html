Night 31

Oliver Owl and the Oldest Question

Month 1: The Question Collector

At month's end, gold light gathered in the Great Willow's branches. Stella, to the owl: "The small one's notebook asks: who asked the first question ever?" Oliver Owl polished his spectacles a long time. "The first question..."

He said. "was asked before words. Some small thing on some old night looked up, the way she looks up, and the look itself was the question. The same one. The only one, really: *what is all this, and am I part of it?*And the answer?" asked Stella.

The owl gestured with one wing at everything, the stars, the town, the tree, the small lit window on Maple Lane where a notebook slept under a pillow. "Still coming in..." He said. "That's why the watch exists.

That's why she exists, I sometimes think. The universe asking itself, in a small voice, with a notebook. Mmm," hummed the Moon, who was there for the first look up. Goodnight, askers of Willowbrook. The answer is still coming in. And yes, little one, you're part of it. That much is answered. Sleep.
