Night 362

The First Night of Five

Month 12: Almost Five

First bedtime of five. The list, from the pillow, per tradition: "The room: same. The bear: same. The drawing: there, hi, Fee-oh's scribble-hands. The gold light: same. The sweater: five stars, chair-draped, still warm-ish.

And me: five, and feeling four-plus-one-day, exactly under the annual physics. The number's a room. I furnish it. I know the drill, Mama, this is my fifth empty room. I'm a professional now.

So what goes in first, professional?" The consideration, drowsy, thorough: "... The word. Kind goes in first. The tree gave Five its first furniture before Five even started, that's never happened before; the words always summed up the old year.

But kind... Kind faces both ways. It's what Four did and what Five's for. The tree wrote a hinge." The eyes, going. "Five's the kind year. Whatever else it is. The room's got its first chair and the chair says kind and everything else gets built around... it..."

(Asleep. Mid-blueprint. Per tradition. The story tucks the thought in with her: the tree wrote a hinge. It did, little one. The story has read the tree's whole sentence, all the way to its last word, and it can report, just this once, just this small spoiler, because you're asleep and won't hear: every word is a hinge.

That's how the sentence holds a life. Sleep, Five. Build around kind. You were always going to.)
