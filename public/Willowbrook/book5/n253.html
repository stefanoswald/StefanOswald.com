Night 253

The Valentine Law, Amended

Month 9: Friends Old and New

The Valentine machine reopened (second annual; Theo promoted to co-glitter-chief, a decision the ceiling would come to regret again), and its founder introduced an new rule: "the rule of everybody, extended: the grown-up division gets cards now too. The web doesn't stop at kid height." The special editions, engineered to their readers: Mrs. Hollis: quiet colors, small words, and inside, a list: "Fridays so far: 41.

All good ones." (She reportedly kept it in the photo wall's corner, tucked into the frame of the class of 1989.) The librarian: a drawing of the lantern, and: "The light stays on. I'm in the chain.

Happy valentines from a link." Petrov: a card shaped like a wrench ("menders get tools, not hearts, he'll understand," he did; it hung in the shop window through March). The crossing guard: "He waves every day," Elias's line, credited, with a wave drawn by the whole class at her organizing. And Ms.

Alvarez's, delivered last, the hardest one to write ("teachers get a mountain of cards. Mine has to be true, not big"): "You kneel to talk. The kneeling is the whole thing. Your colleague, O."
