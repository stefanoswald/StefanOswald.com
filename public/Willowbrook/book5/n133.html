Night 133

Theo Reads His Name

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

It happened at the fridge, on an ordinary Thursday, the way the big ones do. Theo, at the magnets. The faculty had arranged them, per lesson: T-h-e-o. (The H added this week, "okay, the ghost letter.

It's in your name but it doesn't say. It just stands there being yours. Some letters are like that. Some people are like that, quiet and yours.") And the small finger, moving letter to letter, unprompted: "T...

H, that's the ghost, he's mine, he don't say... E... O... T-eo. Teo. Fee-oh. That's me. The fridge says me!" The kitchen: stopped. All four of them, around the fridge, and the faculty loudest of all: "You read it!

Your first read! The lights are on, fee-oh, the first light is on!" And Theo, drunk on power, went around the kitchen pointing: "That say?"
