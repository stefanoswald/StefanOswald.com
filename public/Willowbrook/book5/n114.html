Night 114

The Sunflower Harvest Truth

Month 4: True Stories

Farm day, and the row, the wall of faces, the fourteen giants, has gone brown. Heads bowed. Heavy. Done. "They look sad..." said Theo. "They look finished," she corrected, and then checked herself, because this month is this month: "...

No. Both true. They look sad and finished. Finished things get to look sad. That's allowed." The harvest, Joe presiding: heads cut, seeds thumbed out into buckets. Thousands. "Each head made about a thousand..." said Joe.

"Fourteen thumb-presses in, fourteen thousand seeds out. That's the farm's whole honest math, right there. It never adds. It multiplies." The count-out: next year's envelope. Bird buckets for the winter feeder. A jar for Scout's seeds, kept separate ("the volunteer's line goes on too").

And at the truck: "Grandpa, the row died, dough. That's the true part nobody's saying." Joe looked at her buckets. "Darlin', look at your own hands and say that again." She looked. Fourteen thousand seeds. "... It didn't die. It distributed."
