Night 18

The Question That Made Mama Cry

Month 1: The Question Collector

Not every question lands soft. This week, at the sink, routine voice: "Mama, what was your mama like? You know everything about babies-me. Who keeps babies-you?" And Mama went still at the sink, and then wiped her eyes with her wrist, and the collector froze, "I didn't mean a wound one...

No. No, love. Sit with me." And at the table, gently: "My mama died before you were born. You'd have loved her. She's where the letter tradition comes from, she wrote me letters. I have a drawer too...

Who keeps babies-you, then? I do. And your papa keeps some. And now..." A breath. "you can keep some too. Want one?" She got one: a story of a woman who read seed catalogs aloud on a farm porch, forty years. "... Grandma was the porch reader. Grandpa's porch reader. And now I'm, Now you're it."
