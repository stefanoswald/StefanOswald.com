Night 91

The Small Lie That Grew

Month 4: True Stories

This month opened with a small one. "Did you brush? Yes." Tiny. Two letters. And then, the physics of it, observable all evening: the lie needed guarding. Don't yawn too close to Mama. Skip the goodnight kiss angle.

Remember, at breakfast, what was said. By the lamp it weighed more than the brushing ever would have. "Mama. I didn't brush. I said yes and it wasn't. And the yes has been sitting on me all night, it's the heaviest two letters I ever carried."

The brushing: done, late, teeth and slate both. "Why do lies get heavy? The truth just... sits there. Lies need feeding. Because a lie is a pet you have to keep alive, love. Every hour it needs another little lie fed to it.

Truth needs nothing. Truth just stands there being true while you sleep." Notebook: "Lies are pets you have to feed. Truth stands alone all night free. This month's doing true. I can already tell it's a heavy-light month. Both at once."
