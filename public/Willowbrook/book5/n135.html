Night 135

The Story Under the Floorboards

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

The roof repair began this week (honestly: manageable; the money figured out, the family informed, by the new policy), and the work uncovered treasure: behind an attic wallboard, folded small, brittle-brown: a child's drawing. Old. A house, a stick family, a big tree on a hill, unmistakably the hill, the tree, and letters, faded: "My home. E.

W. Somebody us lived here before us. A kid. A drawing kid. They drew the willow, the willow was their willow too..." They investigated with town records via Mrs. Hollis (she knows everyone who ever chalked a board).

E. W: Edith Winslow. Lived in the house in the 1930s. Became. Hollis pulled the thread and her face changed..." The county's first woman veterinarian. There are farmers my age who still tell Winslow stories.

"The drawing: framed. Hung by the stairs. "The house keeps its kids..." She said. "We're not the first and we won't be the last and the willow watched all of us."
