Night 149

The Level-Three Handoff

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

A new book was waiting at the library desk with Olivia's name tucked beneath the cover.

It was a level-three book, thicker than the last one. The librarian's note said only, She's ready. M.

Olivia held the book against her chest and looked up.

"I understand the handoffs now," she said. "Someone gave you the right book when you needed it. You learned how that felt, so you became the person who gives the next right book."

The librarian folded her hands on the desk.

"And now you keep doing it for me and Sam and everyone else," Olivia continued. "Someday somebody will walk around my shelves during a hard year. I will recognize the face because you recognized mine."

She opened the cover and practiced the sentence she wanted to remember: "This one has been waiting for you."

For a moment, the librarian said nothing. Then she stamped the due-date slip.

"The chain is pleased with its newest link," she said. "Three weeks. No renewals until the waiting list is clear."

Olivia carried the book home beneath her coat, keeping it dry against the spring rain.
