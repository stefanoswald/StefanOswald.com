Night 302

The Losing Well Library

Month 10: Losing Well

This month's final setup: The donation. The losing lesson, written out on slips, one lesson each, her best printing, and distributed to the town's keeping-places: To Room 4's jar: "The hot thing is a sneeze. End on a build. The bench game is always open."

To the library, handed across the desk with full ceremony: "For the wainscoting. Or wherever the library keeps its... extra wisdoms." (The librarian accepted them without blinking: "The building has places." It does. Sure enough the slips entered the list's archive, saved under: Lesson, visitor-donated, age 5.)

And to Molly's mail route, one tiny copy, stamp-sized, her smallest-ever printing: "The dice have no memory..." Addressed to. "The four valleys, whoever's losing." (Left in the wainscoting gap at knee height. Gone by morning.

The vole's trilogy, later, quotes it in volume three.) "Why give it all away?" asked the librarian, testing, the way she tests. "The kit only keeps what it gives. That's the oldest rule I got.

Besides..." The grin. "I'm not giving away the lessons. I'm giving away copies. The originals are in me. That's how wisdoms work. They're the only treasure where giving is keeping."
