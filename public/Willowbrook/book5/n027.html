Night 27

What Was Here Before the Town?

Month 1: The Question Collector

The stone marker by the bridge, read now, because everything gets read now: "Willowbrook, founded..." and a number too big to feel. "Grandpa. What was here? Before the town. Before the founding." Joe, who is the closest thing the town has to before: "Farms before the shops.

Woods before the farms. The brook before everything, the brook's the senior resident. And the willow..." He pushed back his hat. "My grandfather said his grandfather said the willow was already old when the first wagon came over that ridge.

Town's named after it, not the other way. Folks built here because the tree made the hill look like somebody was already home." The long look, bridge to hill. "... The willow was the first home.

The first who-keeps-you. The whole town is just... everybody moving in with the tree."
