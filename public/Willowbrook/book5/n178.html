Night 178

The Twelve Days of Counting Theo

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

The counting bug bit Theo hard this week. The faculty issued him a tally notebook of his own and stood back. His findings, written down with original spelling preserved by the archive: "Cows: 1. Trucks: 7. Stairs: 14 (I counted them to wivy already but they stayed 14.

Stairs keep.)" (Stairs keep. The faculty framed that one mentally. Numbers that hold still, his first constant.) "Crackers: 6 then 5 then 3 then 0. (Crackers don't keep.)Dandelions: Lots. I stopped. Some things are lots." Some things are lots.

Three years old, and he'd hit the star-count wall, the count-or-stand-under line, in a yard full of dandelions, and made the right call, unprompted, in crayon. "He's got the whole system already..." She reported at the lamp, awed. "Stairs keep.

Crackers don't. Some things are lots. That's... that's most of it, Mama. That's honestly most of this month, in three lines, by a person who can't reach the sink."
