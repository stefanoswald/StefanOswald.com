Night 123

Theo's Letter Campaign

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

The declaration came at breakfast, from the three-year-old, fists on the table: "I want the reading. Wivy reads. Books talk to her. They don't talk to me. Teach me the talking." The faculty gathered.

(Her. She is the faculty.) The lesson, announced: "Same as mine. The letters turn on one at a time. You got the T already, that's your capital city. We build out from the T." The campaign, week one: E and O.

His name assembled on the fridge in magnets: T-e-o. ("Close enough. The H is silent anyway. The H can wait, it's a ghost letter, I'll explain ghosts later.") And the pedagogy, pure inherited method: every letter found in the wild counts double.

Stop signs. Cereal boxes. "The T! My T! On the truck!" the town turning on for its second child, sign by sign.
