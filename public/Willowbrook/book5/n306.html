Night 306

The Case of the Paid-Ahead Coffee

Month 11: The Kindness Detectives

Case one: The bakery stakeout. (Rosa's counter, Saturday morning, two detectives nursing one shared roll with professional slowness.) The catch, 9:40 a.m: a man in work boots paid for his coffee and "whatever the next person's having." Left before the next person arrived.

The next person, an old woman, counting coins at the door, got her coffee free, blinked, teared up slightly, and paid the next one forward. Chain length by stakeout's end: Four. The interrogation of the only witness with full knowledge: "Rosa. How often?"

Rosa, wiping the counter, the flour-laugh: "The boots man? Every Saturday, five years. He got his own coffee paid once, back when his work dried up. Never found out who. So now he's the who, every week, hunting the stranger that helped him by helping all the strangers."

A pause. "You're not gonna print that, detectives. Agency rule, Rosa. We witness. We don't headline." (The case notebook took it whole: "Case 1: The boots man. Five years of saturdays. He's paying back a ghost by becoming one.

The kindness chains don't end. They go underground and resurface in new people. The town runs on buried rivers of this.")
