Night 341

The Aunts' Fifth Star

Month 12: Almost Five

The package. The twine. The tradition: opened early..." Love ships when it ships. "The sweater, fifth edition: deep willow-gold this year, and on it, counted, fingers to each. Five stars. ("They went gold the year I went gold.

The aunts run intelligence somehow. The aunts always know the year's color.") The letter, read aloud, solo, start to finish, third year running, the aunts' looping hand as familiar now as print: "Dear Olivia. Five stars.

We knit all winter and talked about you the whole time. The school, the detective business (Your Mama sends reports; we are your farthest field office), the boy who beat you at checkers fair. Five things we know at our age: Wear your years. Keep company with waiting things.

Pay the one-time, never the rent. The town is better than its accounting. Every town, even ours, even at our age we keep checking and it keeps holding. And: The constellation you're becoming was always up there.

The stars just come out one year at a time. All our love, the aunts."
