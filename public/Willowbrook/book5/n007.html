Night 7

Why Do We Say Bless You?

Month 1: The Question Collector

Theo sneezed at dinner. Three people said "bless you." The collector put down her fork. "Why do we say that? The sneeze is already over. What's the blessing for?" Papa: "It's old. Hundreds of years.

People thought a sneeze needed luck after it. Nobody remembers exactly, we just kept the kindness and lost the reason. So it's a kindness with no reason left inside. Like a jar we keep filling because Grandma filled it."

(The table went quiet at that one.) "Then we should invent some. Kindnesses that'll outlive their reasons." The family workshop produced two, adopted immediately: touch the doorframe when you leave ("so the house knows you're coming back") and say "good work" to the lamp when it goes out. Both, sure enough, outlived their reasons. Both are still said.
