Night 194

A Practice School Morning

Month 7: Big Enough

Before school began, Olivia asked to practice the whole morning once.

At seven, the alarm rang. She dressed, buttoned her shirt, tied double knots, ate breakfast, packed the lunchbox, and put on her backpack.

At eight, she and Mama walked to the corner and back. At 8:15, the kitchen became a classroom. Mama was Ms. Alvarez. Theo was the other student. They tried circle time, hand raising, a worksheet, pretend recess, lunch, and dismissal.

The practice was silly enough to make everyone laugh, but it found one missing piece.

Olivia had left without their doorframe goodbye.

"I know I can go without it," she said. "I just do not want to forget it because I am watching the clock."

They added GOODBYE to the morning list.

The point of rehearsal was not to make the first day perfectly predictable. It was to keep the important things from being lost inside the hurry.
