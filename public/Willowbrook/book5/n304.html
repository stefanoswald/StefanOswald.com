Night 304

Freddy's Tournament Tale

Month 10: Losing Well

As the month drew to a close, Stella shone just above the Great Willow. The losing month, closing, and Freddy Frog, honestly hatted these two years, took the floor with a confession of a different flavor: "The Great Lily-Pad Leaping Tournament..." He announced. "Held every spring, the far shallows, all frogs eligible.

I have entered forty consecutive years." A pause, played for it. "I have never won. Never. Not once. Fortieth place, thirtieth, once, my peak, my glorious peak, fourth. The young frogs ask why I keep entering.

The watch may as well hear the answer the shallows already know:" The hat came off, held to his chest. "Because the tournament is where the frogs are. The training swims with my nephews. The bank-side commentary.

I invented the bench game before the small one was born; the story may check the shallows' minutes. The year the champion cramped mid-leap and I towed him in, and we both placed last, and it's the only result anyone remembers from that decade. Forty years of losing, and I own more of that tournament than any champion, the champions own the results. I own the tournament.

And this year?" asked Tessa, who has watched all forty from her spiral shell, ten-year pauses and all. "Thirty-first place..." said Freddy, replacing the hat with dignity. "A strong thirty-first. And my nephew took second, trained in my wake, every stroke.

So really..." And the old frog's eyes shone. "we're closing on the title. Log it..." said Stella, gold and laughing. "this month's last law, from its longest-losing master: the champions own the results. The faithful own the tournament.

Mmm," hummed the Moon, who has lost every race with the sun since time began and owns the entire night. Goodnight, faithful entrants of Willowbrook. The tournament is yours. Sleep.
