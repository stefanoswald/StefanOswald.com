Night 41

The Wrong Name Week

Month 2: Mistakes Are How

It happened at preschool, in front of everyone: she raised her hand and called Miss Dana "Mama." The class: giggled. The cheeks: five-alarm. The rest of the morning: spent wanting the floor to open.

But at pickup, Miss Dana knelt down: "Want to know a secret? Every single year, every class, everyone does it. Marcus did it in September. Lily did it last year. I called my teacher Mama in 1989 and I still remember the exact floor tile I stared at...

Everyone? It's not just... It's the most common mistake in every school on earth. You know why? Because school is where the caring happens, and 'Mama' is just the fastest word your mouth keeps for 'person who cares about me.' It's not a mistake, really. It's a compliment with the wrong address."
