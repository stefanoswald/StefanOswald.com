Night 186

The First Alarm Clock

Month 7: Big Enough

School began at 8:15 sharp, so Olivia chose a small alarm clock from Mr. Petrov's shop. It had two brass bells and, she said, the kindest face.

They set it for seven. Olivia would turn it off and get out of bed herself. Mama would still come in, but as a morning visit rather than a waking service.

On the trial morning, the bells rang. The whole family listened from the hallway.

Thump. Silence. Then Olivia opened the door upright, blinking, and proud.

"I rose because the clock said to."

"How did it feel?" Mama asked.

Olivia considered. "Bigger. And lonelier than your way."

Both feelings were true. Independence often gave something and took something at the same time.

Mama still sat on the bed for one minute, one real minute, before breakfast. The clock had changed who woke Olivia. It had not ended the part where morning began together.
