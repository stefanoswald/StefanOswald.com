Night 206

The Homework Place

Month 7: Big Enough

Olivia's first homework was one worksheet with letters and a drawing.

She cleared the window end of the kitchen table, unpacked her whole schoolbag, and put an apple beside the paper.

"Snack and work together," she decided. "Neither has to wait for the other."

Theo sat across from her with his own paper. He drew three cows and called it homework.

The worksheet took eleven minutes. The more important discovery was the place around it: afternoon light, an empty bag, a small snack, and another person working nearby.

The next day, Olivia returned to the same seat without discussion. Over time, the work grew harder and the apple changed to other snacks, but the window place remained.

On the first afternoon, she had not simply completed homework. She had built a calm beginning for the many years of work that would follow.
