Night 145

The Story Swap

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

The trio's newest institution, born under the oak: The story swap. One premise, announced. Three tellings. ("Same seed, three gardens. It's the E. W. Rule, everybody draws their own barn.") This week's premise: "A kid finds a door where no door should be."

Lily's telling: the door leads to a bakery where the rolls sing. (Warm. Everything Lily builds is warm. The door smells like Saturday.) June's: the door is bug-sized, and the kid has to shrink, and the ants have a whole city with laws and a mayor and a jail for a beetle who did nothing wrong, the story became a rescue.

(June's stories always find the wrongly jailed. As it happened it and says nothing. Yet.) Olivia's: the door is in the willow. And it doesn't lead away, it leads to the same town..." But on a Tuesday from before you were born. "and a girl is there, drawing, with the initials E.

W... Three tellings. Three worlds. One premise. "That's the proof..." She said, after. "Stories aren't in the premise. They're in the teller. You can't run out of stories any more than you can run out of people."
