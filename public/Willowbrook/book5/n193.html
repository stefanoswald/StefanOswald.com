Night 193

The Big Kid Bathroom Question

Month 7: Big Enough

The lamp caught the last one this week, the worry under the worries, surfaced at last in the low light: "Mama. The bathroom. At school. It's down a hall. You have to ask to go, and then go alone, and the stalls, and what if the lock sticks, and what if I can't find the room again after..."

The breath. "It's a baby worry. I know it's a baby worry. The uddahs are worried about friends and I'm worried about the bathroom." And Mama put down the brush and gave it the full dignity: "It is not a baby worry.

It is the worry, ask any grown-up their first-day memory and half will say the bathroom. It's the one place you go alone in a building that's still new. Of course it's the worry. Alone-in-new is the oldest worry there is."

The plan, built together: the recon map. The lock check ("try it before you need it, day one, first free minute, test the lock like Petrov would"). And the rule: "asking to go isn't a favor. It's a right.

The hand goes up. The hand's been going up for two years. The hand knows the job."
