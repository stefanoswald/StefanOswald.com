Night 162

The Estimation Game

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

The market ran a contest: Guess the beans in the jar. Win the jar. (Plus a gift basket, but the jar had her attention. The family jar shelf has standards.) The family's methods, compared: Theo: "A million!"

Papa: careful staring, lips moving. Mama: "four hundred". And Grandpa Joe, visiting for market day, taught the farmer's way: "Don't count beans. Count layers. Beans across the bottom, call it forty. Layers to the top, call it twelve.

Forty twelves is four-eighty. Knock some off for the jar's shoulders: four-fifty." Her entry, Joe-method, own adjustments: 445. The count, announced Saturday: 448. Winner. (By three beans. Mama's instinct-400 took second. "Instinct almost beat math," Olivia reported, shaken.

"Instinct is math you did without showing work..." said Joe. "Forty years of jars is in that four hundred.")
