Night 128

The Reading Slump

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

A strange affliction this week: Nothing worked. Started four books. Abandoned four books. Circled the shelves like a lost thing. ("The books all look... Closed. Even the open ones.") The alarm: real. ("What if the reading broke?

What if I used it all up?") The librarian, consulted, showed no alarm whatsoever: "Ah. The slump. Right on schedule, every real reader hits the first one about now. Your reading's not broken. It's digesting.

You took in a dog's whole death and a new author's whole toolkit in one month. The way things worked's busy." The prescription, from forty years' practice: "No new books this week. Re-read an old friend instead.

The bear book. Something your heart already knows the way home in. Re-reading isn't backwards, it's how readers rest." It worked. The bear book, night one: home. By Friday she was circling the new shelves again, appetite restored.
