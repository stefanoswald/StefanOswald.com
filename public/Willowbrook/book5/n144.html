Night 144

The Hard Word Hill

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

The sequel hit a hard stretch this week, a chapter with words like boulders: "Reluctant. Persevere. Melancholy." The old move: skip them, let the fog fill in. The reader's move, self-invented at the lamp: The hill rule.

"Hard pages are hills. You don't run hills. You gear down." Gear one: sound it out. Gear two: check the neighbors (the sentence around it, "the dog wouldn't quit the fence... persevere probably means the not-quitting").

Gear three: the dictionary (two of three confirmed by context first, "I like beating the dictionary there. It's the answer sheet, not the teacher"). Gear four, rarely: the tap signal. (Papa, on call, used once, for melancholy..."

It's the long soft sad. "he said. "The distance kind... Mama's fog," she said. Exactly, he said.) Summit: the chapter, finished, every boulder named.
