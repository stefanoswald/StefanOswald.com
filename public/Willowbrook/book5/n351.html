Night 351

The Album Night, Fifth Edition

Month 12: Almost Five

The albums, the big bed, the birthday-week tradition, and this year, a changing of the guard: She ran the tour, and Theo was the audience, and the parents got to just... watch their history be handled by its heirs. The tour: "That's me at one day old, looking at May snow. That's the duck, the first-word duck, Fee-oh, the whole talking thing starts at that duck, mine and yours, because you learned talking from me and I learned it from a duck, so technically..." ("The duck is the grandfather of all the words," Theo concluded, awed.

The story does not correct family theology.) "That's Lily and me, bald, holding hands, day eleven of the friendship. Year five of forever. That's you being born, look, there's me at the window with the gold light.

I waited so hard for you. Best waiting I ever did. That's the green room. That's Bella coming out. That's the first day of big school, look how the backpack was still stiff. That's us, Fee-oh.

The whole us so far." And Mama and Papa, at the bed's edge, watching the archive teach itself forward, and the story catches Papa's whisper, not meant for anyone: "That's the whole job, right there. Working itself out of a job."
