The Mistake Month, Counted

Month 2: Mistakes Are How

Month's end. At month's end, On paper, they could list one salt cake. One board: flipped, regretted, outvoted by do-overs. One vase: forward, not back. One burnt batch. One museum visited. One weed spared.

One wrong mailbox. One take-back: nine seconds, personal best. One brother: inventing sorry-flowers from scratch. Beyond the numbers, there was that the inside view lies and the family is each other's film. That misses are measurements.

That fix comes before blame and the table's usually the culprit. That some mistakes are heirlooms, some are mail from the Maybe, and every single one pays in weird money if the tally book stays open. "Good flop-month? The best.

Mama. I'm not scared of wrong anymore. Wrong's just... early. Wrong is right that hasn't finished." Wrong is right that hasn't finished. Remembered. Flop on, little one. The tally book's open. It stays open. That's the whole trick of it.
