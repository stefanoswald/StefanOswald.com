Night 204

The First Day

Month 7: Big Enough

Olivia woke before the alarm on her first day of big school.

She touched the old pencil mark on the kitchen doorframe, shouldered her gold backpack, and walked down Maple Lane between Mama and Papa. At the gate, they used the goodbye they had practiced: one hug, one wave, and then Olivia kept walking even though her stomach felt jumpy.

Inside, she found her cubby and printed a careful O on the label. She sat on the carpet, raised her hand twice, and asked whether Biscuit the class hamster had a middle name. He did not. The class agreed to vote on one later.

Lunch was easier after Olivia noticed that another girl had the same crackers. Recess was easier when she found Wren at the mural bench. At ten-fifteen, two familiar hands waved through the window from the younger classroom. Olivia sent the family wave back.

At pickup, her teacher spoke quietly to Mama. "She asked whether the reading corner accepts volunteers. On her first day."

Olivia heard and stood a little taller. The school no longer felt like one enormous unknown place. It had a cubby, a hamster, a bench, and a corner where she could help. That was enough for day one.
