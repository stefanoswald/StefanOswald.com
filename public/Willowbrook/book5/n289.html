Night 289

The Report Card Number

Month 10: Losing Well

The spring report card came home glowing, except one mark, lower than its siblings: Handwriting. ("The grip rebuild is behind the grade cycle, Mama. The new grip is only four months underground. The grade measured the old thumb's whole career.")

The response, run by the book. Her book, all of them: The shelf rule, applied first ("the number measures one jar; my letter-shapes jar, not my letter-love jar; the words I write aren't in the grade, just the wobble they wear"). The loss read, farmer-style ("what offered it: the late rebuild. Ms.

Alvarez caught it in fall; the grade just hasn't heard the news yet. Grades are always a little behind the kid, they measure backwards. Kids happen forwards"). And the plan, drafted without drama: ten minutes of letter-shapes at office hours, daily, "till the grade catches up to the grip."

(It caught up by June. Grades mostly do, when the plan is real. But the story keeps this entry for one line, said at the lamp, that Mrs. Hollis later asked to have copied out for her wall:) "The number is a photograph, Mama.

Of one jar. From behind. I'm a whole parade. Photographs can't even see parades, they're too slow. Let the number take its picture. The parade keeps walking."
