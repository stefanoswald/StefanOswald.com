Night 281

The Spelling Bee Bow-Out

Month 10: Losing Well

Room 4's spelling game eliminated her this week on, the story savors this, an ough word. "Through." The ghosts, collecting their ancient debt. The elimination moment, per witnesses: she spelled it "T-h-r-u," got the buzz, stood a beat, the hot thing visibly knocking, and then said, to the whole class, palms up: "The ghosts got me.

I've been hunting the ough words since I was four and they finally got their revenge. Well played, ghosts." And sat down to applause. Applause. For losing. Because the loss, held lightly and honestly, turned into the room's best moment of the day.

"The wrong-note move..." She explained at the lamp. "Papa's whole method. You can't wreck a performance you keep performing. The mistake becomes the show if you claim it fast enough. Losing's got a stagecraft to it, Mama.

The bow matters. I've been practicing the bow all month and today the bow worked."
