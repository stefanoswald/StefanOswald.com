Night 235

The Quiet Kid's Page

Month 8: The Settling In

The class book came back from the laminator this week, and Room 4 voted on its first official act: Show the crossing guard his page. The delegation: the whole class, one cold morning, assembled at his corner. The presentation: the quiet kid himself, the three-words-and-a-sun author, holding the book open to the right page, wordless, in his way. The page, for the story: a drawing of the guard, sun overhead, and the words: "He waves every day."

The guard, a large man, weatherproof, forty years of crossings, read it. Read it again. Took off his glasses. Did the thing with the barn dust. And then knelt down and said to the quiet kid: "Every day, buddy.

That's the whole job and you're the first one who ever wrote it down." The quiet kid's face. The story keeps a special shelf for faces, and this one went on it. "Three words," Olivia said at the lamp, still processing.

"I write pages, Mama. Books. And the truest thing anybody in that room ever published was three words by the kid who doesn't talk. 'He waves every day.' That's... you can't beat that. You can only witness it."
