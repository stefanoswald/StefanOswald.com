Night 342

The Hunt Invitations

Month 12: Almost Five

The invitations went out this week, fifteen of them, each engineered per reader (the craft is mature now), each carrying the party's secret payload: a folded mission card. "You are invited to the five party. There will be cake, games, and. This is the important part.

A secret. Inside this fold is your mission: a kindness to do at the party, without getting caught. At cake time, all the deeds get read out loud. No names. If nobody guesses yours, you win.

If everybody wins, everybody wins. (That's the plan. Everybody wins.)" The missions, drafted by the agency's full staff (Theo contributed four; all four involved dandelions; two were accepted): "Refill somebody's drink before they notice it's empty.

Learn one thing about a grown-up nobody asks about. Make the youngest guest laugh twice. Find whoever's at the edge and build them a door in. The party's a training exercise," Lily observed, folding cards.

"The party's a gift exercise. The new thing I got this year was the eyes. You share the new thing. Fifteen kids go home with the eyes started. That's the whole present, both directions."
