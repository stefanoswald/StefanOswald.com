Night 243

Ellie's Balloon News

Month 8: The Settling In

At the month's quiet finish, Stella's light touched the willow's highest branches. The settling season, closing, and Ellie, the young elephant, asked for the floor. (The balloon bobbed above her, tail-tied as ever, catching starlight.) "The watch has been discussing roots..." said Ellie. "so I want to testify about strings. Everyone asks about my balloon. 'Doesn't it pull? Don't you worry it'll pop, or fly off?' And for years I held the string short, wrapped tight, balloon at my ear, where I could hear it squeak.

Safe. Close. Mine." The balloon bobbed, listening to its own story. "And then I watched the small one below go through her gates this fall, the school ones. And I watched her mama at the same gates.

And I understood something about strings: her mama's been letting the string out. A little more every year. Not letting go, letting out. The balloon flies higher; the string stays tied. And the tie was never the string's length.

The tie is the tie. And so?" asked Oliver Owl, gently, tally book open. "So I let mine out. Three whole trunk-lengths this week. It found a wind up there I never let it meet, it danced, you should have seen, and every evening it comes down the string to my ear again, same as ever.

Squeaking its adventures." Ellie curled her trunk, contented. "Log it for every mother watching a gate: the string let out is not the string let go. The balloon comes down the string at evening. It always comes down the string.

Mmm," hummed the Moon, who has watched every balloon and every gate and every evening return since balloons began. Goodnight, strings and balloons of Willowbrook, held right, let out brave, tied forever. The evening return is certain. Sleep.
