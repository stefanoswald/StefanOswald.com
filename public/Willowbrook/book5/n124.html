Night 124

The Book That Made Her Cry

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

Chapter nine did it. The old farm dog, the book one, the river survivor, died. Of old age, gently, in the sun, the way book dogs do when authors are kind. And she cried at the lamp.

Real tears. For a dog made of ink. "Why am I crying? He's not real. I know he's not real. The tears didn't ask if he was real..." Mama held her and gave her the reader's oldest secret: "Because the feelings are real.

The book borrowed your real heart to love a pretend dog, and real hearts hurt real when things end. That's not a malfunction, love. That's the whole machine working perfectly." The strange discovery, after, drying: "...

I'm glad I read it. The sad chapter. I'd read it again. What kind of backwards is that, choosing the sad? Reader's backwards. It's practice sadness, with a door you can close. You're stronger at the real ones for every book one you let in."
