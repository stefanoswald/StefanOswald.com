Night 167

The Fair Race Handicap

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

The park race had a fairness problem: Mateo wins. Always. Legs like a seven-almost-eight-year-old, because that's what they are. The little kids had stopped racing..." Why run a race whose ending's already written?

"The redesign, hers, math-powered: Handicaps. "Mateo starts at the oak. I start at the bench. Fee-oh starts at the flower bed. The starts are different so the finish can be same-chance. I measured everybody's stride on the hundred chart and did the math."

Mateo's objection: "Head starts are babyish... Head starts are engineering. The fast horse carries more weight. Grandpa says they do it at real races. Being handicapped means they know you're the best. It's a compliment with math in it."

The result: a four-way photo finish, everyone screaming, Theo winning by a dandelion's width and being carried around the park like a trophy.
