Night 315

The Case Saves Review

Month 11: The Kindness Detectives

Mid-month review, the fort, both senior detectives, the saves spread on the blanket: "Look at the map, Lily." (The cases, plotted on the town map, the bilingual masterwork, borrowed for agency use, Noor consulting.) "The boots man: the bakery. The pockets: the crossing.

The weather adjustments: the lantern. The snack kid: Number Seven. The April mittens: Hollis, the window. Petrov's columns: the shop. The envelope case: [Redacted]. The Duchess: the farm. It's not dots. Connect them, it's a grid.

The whole town is covered. Every block has at least one buried river running under it. The kindness isn't random, Lily. It's setup. Unofficial, unpaid, unmapped, till now, but it's a whole second town under the first one, holding it up."

The finding, formalized: "Finding: The town has two grids. The visible one and the buried one. The visible grid gets taxes and repair crews. The buried one runs on nothing but people deciding, daily, unpaid, unthanked.

Conclusion: The second grid is the one that makes the first one a town instead of just... Buildings with plumbing."
