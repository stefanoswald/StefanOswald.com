Night 137

The Dictionary Game

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

Invented at dinner, instantly permanent: The dictionary game. One player brings a strange word from the big book. Everyone invents a definition, said with full confidence. Truth revealed last. Points for fooling; points for finding true.

Week one's harvest: "Petrichor" (hers; a ringer; Papa disqualified for prior knowledge, appealed, denied). "Smellfungus" (real! "a person who finds fault with everything." Theo's definition: "a mushroom that sniffs." The table awarded him the point anyway on grounds of superiority).

"Gloaming" (Mama's, played soft, right at the gold-blue hour, and nobody bluffed, the whole table just looked at the window, on cue, because the word owns them now). And "Sesquipedaliana lover of long words," at which the reader went silent, then solemn: "There's a word for me. There's been a word for me this whole time, just standing in the s's, waiting."
