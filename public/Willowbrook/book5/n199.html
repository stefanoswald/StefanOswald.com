Night 199

The Last Farm Week of Summer

Month 7: Big Enough

The final full week of summer belonged to Honeywell Farm.

Olivia fed the Duchess slowly and openly, maintaining their difficult truce. She circled tulips in the porch catalog and voted to grow yellow tomatoes again.

At the swimming hole, she finally jumped from the big rock after two summers of looking.

Theo collected eggs alone for the first time. "Five, and I broke zero."

In the family almanac, Olivia wrote:

SUMMER OF ALMOST FIVE. LEARNED BUTTONS, LACES, THE SCHOOL ROUTE, AND MY OWN WAY OF DOING THINGS. THE YELLOW TOMATOES CAME BACK FROM SAVED SEEDS. THE DUCHESS NODDED TWICE. SCHOOL IN NINE DAYS. THE FARM SAYS I AM READY.

Grandpa Joe read the entry. Beneath it, he printed one word in careful capitals:

CONFIRMED.

The farm did not know everything about school. It knew Olivia, which was the part she needed.
