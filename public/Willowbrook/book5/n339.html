Night 339

The Room 4 Goodbye

Month 12: Almost Five

The last day proper: Room 4, emptied of its year. The rounds, made properly (endings get care in this family; the training holds): The cubby: cleared. The proud O nameplate: peeled, kept ("second cubby tag. The box collects a school career now").

Biscuit Gravy: farewelled with a ceremonial half-scoop ("summer's at Mia's house. You'll lie to her about hunger now. Godspeed, small liar"). The somebody wall: one last deed posted, unsigned, per law ("Somebody had the best year of her life in this room").

And the reading corner: cushions straightened, the retired bear book tucked under her arm, and the corner formally handed to its next keeper: "Milo. Tuesdays are yours next year. The carpet leans at whoever's true. Do the quiet parts quieter.

You already know." At the door, the look back, the long one, the take-it-all-in one. "Thank you, room. You were the first big-school country. The flag was a jar and a wall and a hamster. Best country I ever joined."
