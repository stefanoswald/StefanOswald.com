Night 9

What Do Dreams Weigh?

Month 1: The Question Collector

The meadow came, and she brought the notebook question straight to the expert: "Bobby. What do dreams weigh? Because some mornings I wake up heavy from one and some mornings I wake up like a balloon." The bear considered it with full bear gravity.

"Dreams weigh whatever they're carrying. A dream hauling a worry comes in heavy. A dream carrying a maybe floats. The dream itself weighs nothing, it's the cargo. So my heavy mornings, the dream was doing freight.

Freight nights. Every dreamer has them. The meadow unloads what the day couldn't." And at the fence line, exactly as a letter once forecast: a small cow. Watching. Bell glinting. "That's not lost, is it.

Invited..." said the bear. "Your brother dreams louder now. Be nice." She waved. The bell rang, once, politely.
