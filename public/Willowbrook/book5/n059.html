Night 59

Tessa Turtle's Slow Confession

Month 2: Mistakes Are How

On the month's last evening, Stella's light touched the willow's highest branches. The mistake month, closing, and Tessa Turtle, slowest member of the watch, asked to speak. (Rare. Turtles testify maybe once a decade. The hill went quiet.)

"My shell..." said Tessa. "The spiral. You've all been too polite to ask. It's a whorl, a growth mistake. Shells are meant to ring evenly. Mine slipped, young, one bad season, and spiraled. The other turtles said it plain: flawed."

A pause, ten years long, the way turtle pauses are. "That spiral is how the small one below knew me from every turtle in the brook, her whole life. It's how all of you know me. The flaw became the signature.

I have never once been mistaken for anyone else. Tell me another turtle who can say that. So log it..." said Stella, gold and soft. "this month's last lesson, from the slowest and surest of us: the mistake you carry long enough becomes the mark they know you by.

Mmm," hummed the Moon, who is himself, as it happened, visibly dented, and has never once been mistaken for anything else. Goodnight, flawed and signed and unmistakable Willowbrook. Wear your spirals. Sleep.
