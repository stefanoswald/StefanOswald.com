Night 88

The Month's Last Feet

Month 3: How Does Harry Feel?

Month's end. The bench. The same study as day two, feet, playground, eyes low, run again, to measure the instrument. The difference: she knows the feet now. That's Derek by the wall (watching the little kids, in his secret job; quills at half-mast these days).

Sam's feet: skippy, carpet-secure. Priya's: woven in, the second Saturday took. The littlest deputy: pointing at things out of pure habit of power. Wren, on the far bench with a crayon: chosen-alone today.

Harry trundled up beside the bench, leaf riding. "Good month, miss? I can read the whole park now. Everybody's carrying something. Everybody. Even the skippy ones, the skippy is just what they're doing on top of it.

That's the graduation..." said Harry. "Now the last lesson, and it's short: you can't carry it all for them. You'll want to. Don't. Just, walk where they can see you know." Walk where they can see you know.
