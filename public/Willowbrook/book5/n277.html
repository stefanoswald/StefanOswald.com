Night 277

Theo Loses at Everything

Month 10: Losing Well

Theo's week: lost at Candyland, lost at checkers, lost a footrace to a kid at the park. The hot thing, age three-and-three-quarters: undefeated. The beginner lesson, delivered at floor level by the program's active trainee: "Fee-oh. I'm in losing school right now myself, so here's the first three lessons, fresh: One: the hot thing isn't you.

It's a thing that happens to you, like a sneeze. You don't have to obey a sneeze. Two: the dice got no memory. The spinner doesn't know you. Nothing chose you to lose. Three: the last number has to be a build, your own rule, the towers.

Losing's the fall. You don't stop on the fall. Rematch or bench game or new game, but you end on a build." The field test, immediate: checkers, rematch. He lost again (checkers at three is a massacre; she declined to throw it, "fake losing is lying with pieces").

And the boy sat, hot-faced, breathing his three breaths, visible effort, whole small body, and said, gritted: "... Again. Rematch. The build." (He lost the rematch too. But the again came without tears, and the family remembered it: the hot thing took its first loss in that house that week, both children, same month.)
