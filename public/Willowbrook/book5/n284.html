Night 284

The Checkers Milestone

Month 10: Losing Well

It happened at the kitchen table on an ordinary Thursday (the big ones pick Thursdays; the story has noticed): Theo won. At checkers. Legitimately. A real trap, he baited her double-jump and swept three pieces on the return, a move he'd clearly been incubating, and she stared at the board, saw it whole, and knew: beaten.

Fair. By the apprentice. Age not-quite-four. The hot thing arrived, losing to your little brother is its favorite meal, and met this month's full training: the breath. The board studied with respect. And then, the story is proud to report, the true thing, delivered across the table: "...

Fee-oh. That was a trap. You planned that. You lost fourteen games learning my double-jump habit and then you used it on me." The grin, arriving despite everything, because the craft was undeniable: "That's the best thing anybody built me all month.

Good game." And Theo, who had absorbed an entire month of losing school, did not gloat. Extended his hand across the board, solemn, ceremonial, exactly as trained: "Good game, Wivy... Rematch?" (The rematch: hers. The war: eternal.

The story has watched forty years of their board games since and rules the rivalry the healthiest institution either of them owns.)
