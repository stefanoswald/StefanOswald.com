Night 166

The Zero Question

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

The lamp got this month's deepest one: "Papa. Zero. It's a number that counts... Nothing. Who needed a number for nothing? Nothing was already doing fine without a name." Papa gave it the full treatment: "For thousands of years, nobody had it.

You could count three sheep but you couldn't write 'no sheep', you just... didn't write. And then somebody, India, mostly, long ago, realized: nothing needs a mark. Because nothing is a fact. An empty jar is full of empty.

And the moment zero got its mark, the big numbers got possible, a hundred is just one-zero-zero, zeros holding the places like... Like the ghost letter. The H in Fee-oh's name. It doesn't say anything but it holds something.

Zero is the ghost number. It stands there, silent, keeping the place, and without it the whole system falls over... Yes. Exactly that."
