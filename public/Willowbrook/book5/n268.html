Night 268

The Question Row Seed Order

Month 9: Friends Old and New

The question row's seed order went out this week, the winter mail-ritual, third annual, with its new wild line item: The selections, catalog-researched at office hours: Okra (for Grandma; non-negotiable; the fence will be wasted with honor). And three strangers, chosen by strict dragon-criteria ("never grew it, can't picture it, the description sounds like a dare"): Purple carrots. Ground cherries ("a cherry that grows in a paper lantern?? The catalog is making things up now").

And luffa ("it's a vegetable that becomes a sponge. If this is true, the garden can grow bath supplies. The question row exists for exactly this"). The envelope: addressed, stamped, kissed per postal custom ("the marks are brave; the okra is braver").

"Four unknowns..." She said at the mailbox. "The garden's dragon corner, ordered by mail. We'll know by August what the dragons were." (August's findings, for the impatient: the purple carrots were real and regal. The ground cherries were the summer's candy.

The luffa became three actual sponges and one legend. And the okra came up tall and stubborn and entirely Margaret's. The fence, wasted, never looked better.)
