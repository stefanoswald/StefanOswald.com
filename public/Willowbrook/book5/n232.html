Night 232

The Class Book Project

Month 8: The Settling In

Room 4's November project: a class book. Every kid, one page. Topic: "Our Town." The room's published author (two titles; a fan letter from a professional; the carpet knows her cv) faced her conference growth-jar squarely: this was a bridge, not a spill.

Her page was hers; the other nineteen were not. The discipline, as practiced: when asked, she consulted..." The shape thing: lost, sad part, found, nigh-nigh. It works for towns too: what the town lost, what it found. "and then sat down.

Watched nineteen pages wobble into being. The crossing guard got a page (from the kid who never talks; the guard waves at his bus; the page was three words and a sun, and it was perfect). Biscuit Gravy got two pages (a duplication the editorial board, not her; she bit her tongue nearly through, resolved by making them "volume one and volume two"). Her own page, submitted with the rest, no special treatment requested: the willow.

Of course. "The tree that was here first. The town joined it. It writes one word a year and the words so far are: Home. Family. Growing. The tree is patient. The town is lucky. The end."
