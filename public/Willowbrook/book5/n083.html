Night 83

Seven Careful Compliments

Month 3: How Does Harry Feel?

Olivia tried an experiment: one honest, specific compliment each day for a week, especially for people who did not hear many.

To Marcus she said, "Your ramps always work. You test the bottom before you build higher."

To the crossing guard: "You wave at every driver, even the grumpy ones. I counted."

To Derek: "You notice when the little children are about to fall from the wall. You keep watching until they are safe."

To Theo: "Your sorry-flowers were such a good idea that I used one myself."

Each person changed a little when the words arrived, a straighter back, a surprised smile, a quieter kind of glow.

At bedtime Olivia wrote: A GOOD COMPLIMENT COSTS ATTENTION.

"Nice shirt" could be pleasant. Telling someone exactly what goodness you had seen said something deeper: I was paying attention when you thought no one was.
