Night 317

The Family Check

Month 11: The Kindness Detectives

The agency turned its instruments home this week..." The hardest surveillance: the stuff you're too inside-of to see. "The findings, compiled over five days of in-house observation: "Papa: refills Mama's tea without being asked, he tracks the cup, I watched, he checks it when he walks past, a whole invisible tea-radar, probably fifteen years running. Mama: reads the school notes twice, once for the news, once for the between-lines, checking which kids' names stopped appearing in my stories.

(She caught the Dev moving-away quiet before I even said. The between-lines is her whole surveillance system.) Fee-oh: puts his cow's bell where I can see it when I'm sad. Never says.

Just... the bell appears. In sightlines. (I checked back: the bell has appeared at every hard day I've had for a year. The apprentice has been running a comfort operation under my own detection.) And me..."

The family got to file on her; fairness shape. "they say I leave books. The right books. On beds, before hard days. 'The handoff, home edition,' Mama called it, and I didn't know I was doing it."
