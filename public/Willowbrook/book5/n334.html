Night 334

Harry's Telling

Month 11: The Kindness Detectives

When the month's stories were told, the Great Willow caught Stella's warm light. The kindness month, closing, and Harry Hedgehog, keeper of the rounds, asked to give the closing telling. "The small one's agency found eleven rivers in one month..." said Harry.

"The watch should know: my rounds have mapped this town for thirty years, and her eleven are real, and there are..." The quills rustled, a hedgehog doing arithmetic. "sixty-some more. The night-shift ones. The ones only a low, slow, close-passing creature ever sees.

The husband who learned to cook at seventy for a wife who forgot how to. The teenager who shovels three walks and takes credit for one. The dog at Number Five, who, the story should note, comforts the boy exactly as much as the boy comforts the dog; that river runs both directions, they all run both directions, that's what makes them rivers and not pipes." A long pause.

The leaf on his back caught the starlight. "She'll find them all eventually. She has the eyes now, the permanent kind. And when her rounds and my rounds have both run long enough..." Harry looked down the hill, at the town, at all of it.

"The last thing I know, and then I'm done testifying for another decade: thirty years ago, a bad autumn, one small tired leaf needed somewhere to be, and the carrying of it taught me every round I ever walked. Every kindness anyone ever does is somebody's leaf. You carry one small thing right, and it teaches you how to carry a town. Log it..." said Stella, gold and quiet as the gloaming. "Mmm," hummed the Moon, carrying every tide. Goodnight, leaf-carriers of Willowbrook, day shift, night shift, both directions, all rivers. Carry one small thing right. Sleep.
