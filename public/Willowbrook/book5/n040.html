Night 40

The Fifth Branch Falls Short

Month 2: Mistakes Are How

First attempt at the fifth branch this week. (The yet. Scheduled for "next summer," but the arms felt long, and the day felt lucky.) Result: two inches short. A scrabble, a slide, bark-burned palms, ground.

The old script says: cry, or quit, or pretend she didn't want it. This month's script, done instead: sat at the base. Examined the palms. Looked up at the branch a long, professional moment. "Two inches.

Same two from the fourth branch last year. The tree gives one branch a summer, that's its rule, I just tested it. Okay. Saved. Saved as what?" asked Papa, spotting. "As information. A miss isn't a mistake if you catch the data on the way down. The mistake would be not measuring."
