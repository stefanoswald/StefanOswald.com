Night 230

The Trick-or-Treat Route Master

Month 8: The Settling In

Olivia planned the Halloween route with a pencil and the serious face she used for treasure maps.

"Mrs. Hollis first," she said. "She waits by the window and likes seeing the costumes. Then the bakery block. Then Number Nine, even if we already have enough candy. The knock matters there."

Theo was walking the full route for the first time. His costume was a cardboard truck with working headlights, built by Papa, and he beeped whenever anyone admired it.

Olivia added three rules to the map: look at the person who opens the door, say thank you, and compliment any grown-up who had dressed up too. "They made an effort for us," she explained.

By the time they came home, both buckets were heavy. Theo arranged his candy in rows while Olivia filled a small bag for the teachers' lounge.

"Teachers give out stickers all year," she said. "Somebody should give them chocolate."

They left the bag by the front door for morning. Outside, the last porch light clicked off, and Maple Lane put Halloween to bed.
