Night 271

The Month's Last Bench Story

Month 9: Friends Old and New

This month closed where its best work happened: The bench. Last recess of February, the sun coming thin and new, the light that means the year is turning its page. The session, as logged: Noor, adding to the joint map (the two-cartographer project has become one shared masterwork: the town, annotated in two hands, dragons and waves, "the first bilingual map in bench history"). Wren, drawing, but narrating the drawing now, unprompted, a running quiet commentary that would have been unthinkable in September ("the voice lives at the bench full-time now," Olivia noted.

"The bench was the first place it felt safe. Places hold that for people. The bench is steady"). And the question notebook, Volume Three, taking this month's last entry in the thin gold light: "Question: How do you know when a new friend has become a old friend?

Answer (Worked out at the bench, all three of us): When the silence gets comfortable. New friends fill every quiet. Old friends let the quiet sit down with them. We sat in the quiet for a whole five minutes today. Nobody filled it. It's official. All three ways."
