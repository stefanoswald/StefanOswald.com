Night 131

The Boy Who Couldn't Yet

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

At preschool: Marcus and letters are not yet friends. The B and the D switch on him. The words swim. And this week another kid said, in front of everyone, "Marcus can't even read his own name."

The room did the thing rooms do. Marcus did the thing quills do. And the intervention, she's been waiting all year without knowing it, and someone saw her reach for exactly the right tool: Grandpa Joe's. "Neither could my grandpa.

Letters came hard and late for him. He runs a whole farm. He mends fences and reads weather and knows where every bug in the county lives. Letters are one road. They're a good road, I love the road, but Marcus builds ramps nobody else can build.

His yet just has a different schedule. Everybody's got a yet. Say yours out loud, if you're so brave." (Silence from the mocker. Everyone's got one. Everyone knows it.) Then, quietly, after, to Marcus: "Also the B and the D switching.

Mateo had that. There's tricks. The bed trick, your fists make a bed, b on the left, d on the right. Want it?" He wanted it.
