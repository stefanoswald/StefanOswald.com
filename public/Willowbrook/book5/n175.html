Night 175

The Time Zones Bombshell

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

The aunts called this week, and mentioned, in passing, that it was already tomorrow where they were. The kitchen stopped. "Already tomorrow? They're in tomorrow? Right now? While we're still in today?!" Papa fetched the globe.

The lesson: the sun can't shine everywhere at once; the earth turns; noon travels around the world like a spotlight; the aunts live far enough around the curve that their clock runs half a day ahead. "So tomorrow isn't a wall everybody hits together. It's a... a wave. Going around.

Always arriving somewhere." The globe got spun, slowly, her finger riding it. "Somebody's having sunrise right now. And somebody's having gloaming right now. It's always gloaming somewhere, Papa. The gold hour never actually ends, it just travels." (the story pauses on that one.

It's always gloaming somewhere. The family used it forever after, the way other families say "it's five o'clock somewhere," but meaning: somewhere, right now, the light is perfect, and the world is being loved.)
