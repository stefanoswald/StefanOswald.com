Night 125

How Did the Author Know?

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

The question survived the night and met Papa at breakfast, still hot: "The author never met me. The book was written before I was born, the librarian said. So how did it know about Buttons? The exact feeling.

The empty-cage-shape feeling. How?" Papa gave it the full stop-everything treatment: "Because the author had their own Buttons. Everybody does, some hamster, some grandma, some moved-away friend. And here's the wire nobody shows you: hearts are all built alike underneath.

The details differ, your hamster, their dog, but the underneath-feelings are the same make and model, every human, every era. A good author writes their own underneath honestly enough... and it fits everyone. That's the whole industry, kiddo. Honest underneaths, shipped in stories."

The stillness. Then: "So when I write my underneaths honest... some kid I never meet, maybe not born yet, could read it and feel known? That's the job. That's the entire job."
