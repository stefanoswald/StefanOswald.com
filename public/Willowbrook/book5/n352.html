Night 352

The Almanac Entry at Five

Month 12: Almost Five

The almanac. The family pages. The fifth entry, written entirely solo now, Joe watching from his chair, dictating nothing: "Olivia. Year five. Lerned: The hole school (It was already in me. The school just gave it rooms).

The losing (The hot thing is a sneeze). The seeing (The town has two grids. The buried one is gold). Fed the duchess all year. She's a promise wearing fevers and I know the promise now and I'm in it too.

The question row came up: All four strangers sprouted. The okra stands like grandma's still checking on it. (She is. Through us. That's how the checking works.) The farm says I'm ready for six. The farm would know.

O." Joe read it twice, nodded once, and added his line: "Confirmed. And for the story: The porch hours are the best hours this farm has run since margaret. The arrangement continues. J." (The arrangement continues.

Two words that hold a whole religion. They remembered them with the fence rows and the gold words and everything else on this farm that outlasts weather.)
