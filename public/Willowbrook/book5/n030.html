Night 30

The Question Month, Counted

Month 1: The Question Collector

Month's end. At month's end, The easy-to-count part included One hundred questions, collected and kept. One moon: explained. One bike: ridden alone since the mailbox. One brother: three now, notebook of his own, first why-chain floored.

Fourteen sunflowers: chest-high, early, undefeated. One reference shelf: discovered. One barber: interrogated. One grandmother: met, at last, in a story at the table. One back section: open windows only. No list could quite hold that questions are the year's engine now.

That some are wounds and get answered first. That every question has a basement. That the realest one blinks in the yard every summer, anyone like me? And every light back is a yes. "Good first month of four?

The best. And Mama, four's room has furniture now. A hundred pieces. All of them questions." A room furnished in questions. They had seen libraries built worse. Remembered. Ask on.
