Night 22

The Sunflowers Pass Her

Month 1: The Question Collector

Farm check. The row: chest-high now. Gaining weekly. "They'll pass me by July..." She reported, against the stakes. "The bear's letter said August for taller-than-Papa. They're early. Good year..." said Joe. "Rain came right.

Some years it all just, lands." She looked down the row a long time. Fourteen green necks, all leaning the same direction, drinking the same light. "Grandpa. They never stop leaning at the sun. Morning to night.

Whole life... Don't they get tired of wanting it?" Joe turned his straw hat in his hands. "That's not wanting, darlin'. That's what they're for. You don't get tired of what you're for. You just get taller."
