Night 85

The Hollis Stories

Month 3: How Does Harry Feel?

Friday, Number Nine. And this week she brought this month's real gift: Questions. About her. "Mrs. Hollis. Forty years of classes. Tell me the best kid story you got. Not the best kid, the best story."

And Mrs. Hollis, who has been asked "how are you" a thousand times since retiring and asked about approximately never, opened like a book left too long shut: The boy who kept a mouse in his desk a whole winter. The girl who couldn't read till ten and then read everything, everything, angry at the lost years. The year the whole class learned sign language, secretly, over months, for the new deaf student, "they organized it themselves at recess; I only found out at the Christmas concert when thirty hands started singing."

(Thirty hands started singing. Olivia made her tell that one twice.) Two hours. Two teas. One notebook page, filled. "The old ones are full of stories nobody orders anymore. They're whole libraries with no card machine. Get a card. Ask for the stories. Fridays are for the hollis library now."
