Night 290

The Quitting Question

Month 10: Losing Well

This month's subtlest chapter: swim class hit a wall, not a fear wall, a want wall. The next level is dive-focused; she doesn't love diving; Saturdays are expensive; the question arrived at the lamp with unusual care: "I want to stop swim class. And I need to know if that's losing. Quitting's supposed to be the worst kind of losing, the kind you did to yourself.

But it doesn't feel like the hot thing. It feels... finished?" The check, built together, three questions long: "One: Are you stopping because it got hard, or because it got done? (The skills crossed the rope.

I can swim the deep, the strokes, the safe. The class was for something and the something... arrived.) Two: Would future-you thank you or blame you? (Thank, I think. Saturdays go to the bench and the books and the question row.

The trade's real.) Three: Is the hot thing anywhere in the room? (No. I checked. No sting, no shrink, no 'I don't even like swimming.' I love swimming. I'm just done with lessons. The pool stays.

The pool's mine forever.)" The answer: Finished, not quit. The last class: attended in full, thanked properly, ended on a build (one glorious cannonball, scored a nine by the old lifeguard, for tradition).
