Night 196

The Wren Pact

Month 7: Big Enough

The bench, the shade, the summit that mattered most: Wren. (Different room assignments. The news had been sitting on both of them for weeks.) Wren said it first, which itself, as it happened, is the year's whole progress in four words: "I'm scared without you.

Me too, some. So: The pact." (Prepared. Of course it was prepared.) "One: recess is shared, the bench by the mural, first recess, every day, no matter what. Mateo mapped it; it's neutral ground between our doors.

Two: the wave code. Fru the window between our rooms, one wave: hi. Two waves: good thing happened, tell you at recess. Hand flat on the glass: hard morning, come at recess with the quiet.

Three: you got a Ms. Alvarez-room friend by October or I recruit you one. I know the whole carpet from story hour; I got saves... And if I can't talk? First week? The words go away in new rooms, you know they do.

Then the crayon comes out and you're chosen-alone till they come back, and anybody who bothers you about it meets me at the mural bench. The quiet is a language, not a problem. We'll tell the whole school if we have to. It's a big school.

Somebody there already speaks it." (Somebody there already did. Her name arrives in Month 9. The story smiles and keeps its saves shut.)
