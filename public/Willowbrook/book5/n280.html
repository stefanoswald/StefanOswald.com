Night 280

The Game Night Rules

Month 10: Losing Well

Game night got its constitution this week (the family ratifies; she drafts; the way things worked is mature now): "The game night laws, posted by the shelf: One: The dice have no memory. Bad rolls are weather, not answers. Two: The hot thing gets three breaths, not the mouth. (Either hot thing, the losing one or the puffed one.)

Three: No frown games. Fake losing is lying with pieces. Everybody's real best, always, even against the free-year-old. (Especially against, respect is the real game.) Four: End on a build. The night's last game can't end mid-grudge.

Rematch, bench game, or cocoa, but the last number is a build. Five: The winner cleans up. (Winning is the energy surplus. Spend it on the box, not the beaten.) Six: The bench game is always open.

Out early = commentary rights." The document: illustrated, laminated, posted. (as it happened: house guests photographed it for years. The constitution traveled to other families' game shelves, hand-copied, like the rule before it. Her setup keeps escaping the building. The good kind always does.)
