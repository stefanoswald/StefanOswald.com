Night 86

Theo's Empathy Debut

Month 3: How Does Harry Feel?

The story has waited for this one. Park. A stranger toddler, maybe two, mid-meltdown, dropped ice cream, the full tragedy. Parents digging for wipes. And Theo, three years old, unsupervised by anything except four years of watching this family operate, walked over, sat down in range, and said: "Ice cweam fell.

That's fusstated and sad. Both at once. Two coats." (Two feelings wearing one coat, his sister's line, inherited crooked and perfect.) "You can be it. Is allowed." The toddler, astonished into silence, stared. Theo produced from his pocket: one dandelion.

(He carries them now. List. The sorry-flower system has a supply chain.) "Sowwy-flower. For the ice cweam." The toddler took it. Held it. Stopped crying entirely. His sister watched the whole operation from the slide, and reported it at the lamp with the solemnity of a proud general: "The method walks on its own legs now. Both kids. It's in both kids."
