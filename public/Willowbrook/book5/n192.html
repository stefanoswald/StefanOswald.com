Night 192

The Meet-the-Teacher Reconnaissance

Month 7: Big Enough

The open house. (The letter called it "an informal opportunity." She ran it like a reconnaissance mission. Objectives, briefed to Mama in the car: teacher, room, cubby, exits, bathroom, "and the feel. Rooms have feels.

I need the feel.") The intelligence gathered: The teacher: Ms. Alvarez. ("Alvarez, like the soup-jar Alvarez? 'Her nephew's wife,' Mama says. The town is a web. Good handle.") Warm eyes. Kneels to talk. Has read, she asked it first thing..."

Chapter books to every class, all year, even though some people say five-year-olds can't follow. They follow. "said Ms. Alvarez. "They always follow." The room: a reading corner with cushions. A hamster ("...his name's Biscuit.

Okay. Buttons taught me the whole grief already. I'll love him anyway, that's the lesson's whole point"). The cubby: her name. Already on it. Printed, official, waiting since before she arrived. "They knew me before I came..."

She said in the car, quiet. "The cubby was already keeping my spot. The whole building was already... expecting me."
