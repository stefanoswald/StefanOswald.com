Night 29

The Hundredth Question

Month 1: The Question Collector

The count reached ninety-nine this week. The hundredth: deliberated all day. ("It has to be a good one. The round numbers are doors.") Candidates, rejected: "Why is a hundred special?". "What's the biggest number?".

The final selection, entered at the lamp with both parents witnessing: "Question 100: Wen I'm old and I read this notebook, will I still be me? Will the me who asked these still be in there, or does she stay behind like the doorframe lines?" And she answered it herself, immediately, underneath, because some hundredth questions come with their answers attached: "Answer: The frame keeps the lines *and* the girl. I checked.

Signed, the me who knows the frame." (the story, reading over her shoulder from very far downstream: confirmed. She's still in there. All of them are.)
