Night 157

The Sharing Fraction War

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

Crisis at the fort: One roll, three trio members. The oldest war in mathematics. The failed solutions, attempted in order: "biggest gets it". "Youngest gets it". "Nobody gets it". And then the engineering: "We cut it.

In three. But..." The insight that makes it this month's entry. "the cutter chooses last. Whoever cuts, picks their piece after everybody else. Watch what happens to the cutting." What happened to the cutting: the most careful thirds in bakery history.

Measured by eye, adjusted twice, crumbs balanced. The cutter had invented fairness-by-shape: the rule made cheating pointless, so nobody had to be good, the way things worked was good. "That's the best trick I ever built..." She said, chewing her precisely-equal third.

"The rule does the fairness for you. Nobody has to win a argument. The knife just... behaves."
