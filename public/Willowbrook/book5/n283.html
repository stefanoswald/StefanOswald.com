Night 283

The Rained-Out Recital

Month 10: Losing Well

A different species of loss this week: the spring sing, practiced for a month, her verse assigned (a verse now, not a line; the stage career progresses), cancelled. Storm, gym flooding, no reschedule. The song: all dressed up, nowhere to go. "This one's not a game loss..."

She diagnosed at the lamp. "Nobody beat me. The rain beat the whole event. The practicing just... evaporates? A month of verse-drilling into nothing?" The family's old machine turned over: the jar. The slip: "Maybe the flooded gym is happening for us."

And the maybe, cooked by Saturday: if the song has no stage, bring the stage to the song. The living room. The family. Mrs. Hollis, invited. Petrov. Rosa-No-Relation and her horse-drawing hands for tickets.

The whole verse, delivered at full recital power to eleven people and a bear, encore demanded and supplied. "The practicing never evaporates..." She ruled, after. "The practicing lives in me. The gym was just one door for it. Doors flood. The song doesn't."
