Night 365

The Watch Sets the Fifth Star

Month 12: Almost Five

The last night. The willow. The full watch, assembled over the sleeping town for the year's final session. The minutes, read by Oliver Owl, tally book open, spectacles down: "Year five of the small one on Maple Lane.

Summary: she collected a hundred questions and left the best ones open. She learned that mistakes pay in weird money, that everybody's carrying, that truth stands free all night. She crossed into the world of rooms, the school, the cubby that expected her, the bench that became an embassy. She mapped a web and tested it with fever and it held.

She tamed the hot thing and crowned the bench and gave the losing lesson to the four valleys. And she found the second grid, the buried rivers, the gold under the streets, and came back changed: the eyes, permanent. The tree wrote kind. The watch concurs with the tree.

The watch always concurs with the tree. Motion to close the book of Four..." said Stella. "Seconded..." said Harry, in from his rounds, leaf bright. "Carried," hummed the Moon. And Stella reached into her gold and set the new star, the fifth, in the little line above a certain roof on Maple Lane.

Five now. A short bright sentence of light over a sleeping reader with a bear, a key-shaped warmth, and a letter saved with its elders. "For the new book..." said Stella. "May it fill kind."

The watch stood down. The town slept on. The willow held its four gold words up to the stars. Goodnight, Willowbrook. Goodnight, book of Four. Goodnight, seeing girl, five years old, five stars up, the eyes lit for good.

See you in the next book. The lamp will be there. It always is. The end of From Four to Five.
