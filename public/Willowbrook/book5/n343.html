Night 343

The Magnifying Glass Sky

Month 12: Almost Five

The bakery. After close. The parchment. The fifth annual consultation: "The sky's the base, tradition. This year's constellation: a magnifying glass. Made of stars. Held over, this is the new part, Rosa. The town. The whole town, tiny, in gold frosting at the bottom: the lane, the lantern, the bakery, the school, the willow on its hill.

And the star-magnifier hovering over all of it. Because that's the year: I looked close at the town and the town turned out to be made of gold. The cake should say that. Cakes are the year's summary in the only language everybody eats."

Rosa sketched, smiling the flour smile. "Five years of skies. The meadow year. The green room year. The book year. The seeing year. You know what the cakes are becoming, all in a row?... A constellation of constellations.

The years, shelved. Like the books." (The baker and the detective looked at each other with the mutual respect of two professionals who archive in different media.) "Rosa. When I'm big: the whole series. Every sky, re-baked, one table.

Promise? At your wedding," said Rosa, entirely serious. (they had seen the far page in question. Seven skies, one table. Rosa kept it. Bakers keep everything that matters, that's what baking is.)
