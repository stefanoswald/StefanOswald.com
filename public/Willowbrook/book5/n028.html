Night 28

The Question She Answered Herself

Month 1: The Question Collector

A hard moment this week: a big kid at the park said the question notebook was "babyish." The sting: real. The bench: retreated to. And then, new habit, first documented use, she opened the notebook on the problem.

Looking for she didn't know what. And found, page eleven, her own handwriting, two months old: "Some questions are really wounds. Answer those ones first." She looked at the big kid, back at the page, and did the math nobody teaches: "His question was 'why do you still like things.'

That's a wound one. Somebody made him stop liking things out loud... That's the saddest thing I learned all month." She didn't fix him. (Four-almost-five has limits.) But she didn't bleed either. The notebook had already answered it, she'd answered it, past-her, for future-her, without knowing.

Entry, that night: "The notebook works both directions. You fill it and then it fills you. Keep everything. You don't know which page is the medicine yet."
