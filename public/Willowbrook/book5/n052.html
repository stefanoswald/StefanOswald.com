Night 52

The Sunflowers Pass Papa

Month 2: Mistakes Are How

Farm day. The row: measured against the tallest available unit. Answer: the sunflowers passed Papa this week. All fourteen. Some by a hat's worth. (The bear's letter said August. It's late July. "The bear undersold it..."

She noted. "Bears are careful forecasters. They'd rader be beat early than wrong late." Saved as a life practice.) The photograph, the one the story mentioned it had seen, a book ago: the whole family under the wall of faces, dwarfed and delighted, Theo on shoulders trying to touch the lowest bloom, Scout's absence noted aloud ("he's home guarding my garden, he outgrew the photo anyway").

And Joe, surveying fourteen giants grown from fourteen thumb-presses: "Every one of these was one seed and one push. Remember that when something looks too big to have started small. Everything too big to believe started exactly that small."
