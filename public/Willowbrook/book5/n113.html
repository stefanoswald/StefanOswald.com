Night 113

The Promise Tally book

Month 4: True Stories

A realization at the lamp, mid-month: "Papa. A promise is a truth that hasn't happened yet. When I say 'I will', that's me saying something true about tomorrow. And if tomorrow comes and I don't... then I lied.

Backwards. Into the past. That's exactly what it is. Most people never see it that clearly." The response, inevitable: The promise tally book. Notebook, new section. Every "I will..." Logged. Every kept one, checked off.

This month's data: "I'll sweep the feeder" (kept, 27 of 30, the three misses logged honestly, with make-ups). "I'll teach Fee-oh the B" (kept; the B is his second letter now). "I'll come back and lose practice" (kept; four board games, three losses, one flip-free month). "The Friday knock" (kept, all four, "Mrs.

Hollis counts on it now, which makes it a steady promise"). Steady promises. Papa stopped at that one. "Where'd you get that? Petrov. Some fixes hold up other fixes. Some promises hold up other people. You have to know which of yours are steady. Those ones can't miss."
