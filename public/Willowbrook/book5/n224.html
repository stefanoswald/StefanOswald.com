Night 224

The Hollis Homework Help

Month 8: The Settling In

The Friday knock, week eight of school, and this week she brought the folder. Mrs. Hollis's check, over tea, was a masterclass in old-teacher archaeology: "Ah, they still use this worksheet. I taught this exact one in 1985.

The pencil grip correction: Good, your teacher caught it early; I've seen forty-year-old hands still fighting that thumb. This 'praise the doing' business your mama runs, we called it something drier in my day, but the wise ones always knew it. And this..." The class jar note. "a teacher who answers a hard question with 'some maybes are crockpots' instead of shutting it down?

Child, teachers like that were rare in my forty years. You've drawn a good one. Know it. I know it. I keep a file on the good ones now. Like Miss Dana keeps on kids."

The old teacher laughed until the tea needed refreshing. "The checked, checking. Forty years and the wheel comes all the way round. Put me in the file. You've been in it. Page one."
