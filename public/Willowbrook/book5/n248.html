Night 248

The Friend Who Moved Mid-Year

Month 9: Friends Old and New

Monday brought the empty desk: a classmate. Dev, the four-square diplomat, a mid-tier but real string on the map, moved away over the weekend. Family job, sudden, no goodbye. Just: desk, empty. Nameplate, gone.

The class wobbled. (She let it wobble, bridges, not spills, but this one was her wobble too.) At the lamp: "It's not the big grief. He wasn't Lily. But it's... unfinished, Mama. We were in the middle.

He was teaching me the four-square spin serve. I never said the things you say at endings because nobody knew it was a ending. The friendship just... stops mid-sentence. Like a book with the last pages torn out."

The remedies, assembled: a letter, sent via the school office's forwarding. The spin serve, practiced solo till mastered..." Because finishing his lesson is finishing something. "And the hard true thing, at the lamp: "Some friendships are chapters, not whole books.

That doesn't make them less. The dog book's chapter nine is the best thing in it and it ends. Dev was a good chapter. I'm allowed to miss the rest of the book I thought we'd get."
