Night 39

The Apology Practice

Month 2: Mistakes Are How

Not all mistakes are cakes. This week: June's special marker, the silver one, borrowed with ceremony, left capless overnight. Dead. The old her would have: hidden it, dreaded it, confessed at bedtime under duress. The now her, watch the difference a lesson makes, walked to June's the same morning, marker in hand, and ran the rule: "June.

I killed your silver. I left the cap off. It was mine to take care of and I didn't. I'm sorry. And..." The repair step, planned in advance. "my allowance has two weeks saved. We're going to the store and you're picking the new one.

Any silver they have. Even the big one." June, examining the corpse: "... It was almost dead anyway. Don't make it smaller. I'm paying the whole sorry." (I'm paying the whole sorry. June's mother, overhearing, told Rosa, who told everyone. The phrase entered the town's vocabulary. Petrov reportedly uses it with suppliers.)
