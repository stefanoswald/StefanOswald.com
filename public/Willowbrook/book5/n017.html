Night 17

Why Does the Willow Get a Word a Year?

Month 1: The Question Collector

She took a question straight to its subject this week. Up the hill, alone-ish, dusk. "Willow. Why one word a year? You're the biggest thing in town. You could write a whole book by Friday."

The tree did what trees do: moved its gold, said nothing, kept its own schedule. But standing there, in the long quiet, the answer arrived anyway, the way answers do under that tree, from somewhere between the branches and her own head: Because a word a year is a word you wait for. Because waited-for words weigh more. Because anybody can be fast; the tree's whole gift is being sure.
