Night 332

The Helpers' Notebook

Month 11: The Kindness Detectives

At the end of the helpers' first year, Olivia and Lily reviewed their notebook at the fort.

They had followed nineteen small mysteries and understood seventeen. One had been left alone because privacy mattered more than an answer. Another, the kindness of Willowbrook at dusk, was not really a case to close.

They had found eleven quiet chains of help, advised one school wall, and organized three group projects.

The numbers pleased Olivia, but they did not hold the best discovery.

"The town is kinder than it looks from the loud surface," she said. "Everywhere we searched, people were carrying things for one another without asking for credit."

The children had not invented that hidden web. They had learned to see it.

On the notebook's final page Olivia wrote: KEEP LOOKING CLOSELY. KEEP LOOKING KINDLY.

Then she opened a new notebook for the year ahead. A habit of attention did not need an official club to continue.
