Night 272

The Friends Month, Counted

Month 9: Friends Old and New

Month's end. At month's end, The notebook could hold one web, mapped. One Noor (recruited by Wren, which is the chain graduating). One Mateo string, plucked (worries found; his own lesson returned across his own fence).

One guest-list dilemma. One mid-year moving-away (Dev; the chapter grieved whole; the spin serve finished; the ripple confirmed by March mail). One fight (two days' interest paid; thirty feet crossed; markers split). One rule.

One pole. One friendversary. One founding. Some changes belonged outside the count: that love is candles, not pie. That bests come in kinds. That the web is built on good days to hold on down ones, and it held, five fevered days, soup to serialized horses.

That whoever's braver pays first, and braver just means remembered-the-worth-first. That you don't stop being from the first place; you grow the paper. "Good friend-month? The widest one. Mama. I started the year thinking friendship was a feeling.

It's not. The feeling's just the weather of it. Friendship is a practice, the Fridays, the benches, the plucking, the paying-first, the not-digging, the showing-up-at-fences. The feeling comes and goes like all weather.

The practice is the climate." The feeling is the weather. The practice is the climate. Remembered. Practice on, little climate-keeper. The web is wide and the strings all sing.
