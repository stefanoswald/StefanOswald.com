Night 120

Freddy Frog's Confession

Month 4: True Stories

As one month gave way to the next, Stella dipped low above the Great Willow. The truth month, closing, and Freddy Frog, unusually solemn, requested the floor. "The hat..." said Freddy. "The lily pad. I've told every creature on this hill I found it floating.

Lost property. Finders keepers." A long froggy breath. "I picked it. Off Tessa's favorite shallows patch. Forty seasons ago. She was napping. I've fed that little lie so long it's got moss on it." The hill turned to Tessa Turtle.

Forty seasons of stolen shade. The pause was ten years long, as her pauses are. "I know..." said Tessa. "I was awake... You knew? Forty seasons? I watched you take it, try it on, and become 20 percent more yourself immediately.

The patch grew another pad in a week. I decided the hat had found its head." A slow turtle blink. "But I'm glad you said it. You've been carrying a pad-sized weight, and pads are light, it's the carrying that's heavy.

Set it down, Freddy. It was always yours. Now it's honestly yours. Log it..." said Stella, gold and gentle. "this month's last finding: the smallest lies keep the longest. And the friends usually knew. And it's never too late to set the pad down.

Mmm," hummed the Moon, who has watched every small lie on every hill and forgiven most of them. Goodnight, honest-at-last Willowbrook. The truth weighs nothing. Sleep light.
