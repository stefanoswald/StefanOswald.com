Night 103

The Truth Goes Both Ways

Month 4: True Stories

Olivia proposed a new dinner rule.

"If children have to hear kind, honest things, grown-ups should too. Theo and I may each tell you one thing. Nobody gets in trouble for saying it carefully."

Theo went first. "Papa says one minute, but it is always ten. It is a pretend minute."

Papa winced. "Fair. From now on I will use a real number or say I do not know yet."

Olivia had prepared hers.

"Mama, when you worry about money, you say everything is fine in a voice that sounds foggy. The words and the voice disagree. That scares me more than a small piece of the truth would."

The room grew quiet.

Mama took a breath. "You noticed correctly. I can tell you when a grown-up problem exists while also telling you that it is ours to solve."

The rule was uncomfortable. That was not a reason to abandon it. A house where truth traveled only downward was not an honest house yet.
