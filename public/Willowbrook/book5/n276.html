Night 276

The Musical Chairs Massacre

Month 10: Losing Well

A party this week featured this month's perfect gym: Musical chairs. And the draw could not have been crueler: Out in round one. First out. Of everyone. Publicly. To party music. The hot thing arrived on schedule, someone saw it hit her face, and the training held: one breath.

The walk to the side. And then this month's discovery, made in real time on the spectator bench: The out-kids' bench had a whole society. The round-one veterans, commiserating. The comedy commentary on the survivors ("that kid hovers, he's not even sitting between rounds, he's pre-sitting").

The best view of the funniest game moments. By round four she was running color commentary; by the final two chairs the entire bench was chanting; the eventual winner got mobbed by the very kids she'd knocked out. "The game has two games in it..." She reported at the lamp.

"The chair game and the bench game. Everybody thinks the chair game's the whole thing. The bench game lasts longer, holds more people, and laughs harder. I lost the chair game in round one and won the whole bench."
