Night 179

The Last Link Math

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

The chain hung at seven links this week. (Thirty minus twenty-three: the tearing has been faithful, every morning, one credential read aloud on the porch. "Can read. Has A Lily. Knows the shelf rule."

The résumé, recited to the walk.) And at seven, she stopped and did the full arithmetic at the kitchen table, out loud, for the story: "Seven mornings left. The math of ready: I got dirty badges and I've re-read twenty-three. The brave list is two pages.

The question notebooks are two volumes. The reading is at level three. Lily is confirmed same-class, checked five times now. Wren's in the other room but the bench is shared at recess, Mateo mapped it.

The unknown part is... the unknown part. Maybe ten percent of the whole thing. Ninety percent is packed. And the ten percent? The ten percent is why it's called a first day and not a repeat day."

A breath. The grin, arriving sideways. "Scared plus one step. And I got..." Counting on fingers. "seven days, six badges, five friends, three... two notebooks and a Lily. The math says go."
