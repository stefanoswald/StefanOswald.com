Night 190

The Name Tag Practice

Month 7: Big Enough

The letter mentioned cubbies. Cubbies mean name tags. Name tags mean: the name, public, permanent, read by everyone who passes. The name is going professional. The practice sheets: rows of Olivia, graded by their author with ruthless standards.

("That o's an egg. That V fell over. This one..." Circling. "This is the tag one. Proud letters. Letters that stand like they mean it.") The size trials: big, medium, small. "The name has to work at every size.

Like me, park-size me, lamp-size me, school-size me. Same name. Same kid. Different letters-size." And the apprentice, demanding parity, produced his own sheet: T-e-o with the ghost H added at last, backwards, twice, then right.

"My tag. For my cubby. When I'm five." (His tag went on his bedroom door that night, by executive order of the faculty. "Practice cubby. The door's been holding your name for years anyway. Now it's official.")
