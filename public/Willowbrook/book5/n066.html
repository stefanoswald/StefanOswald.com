Night 66

The Game Nobody Was Winning

Month 3: How Does Harry Feel?

Park game this week: the big chase game, all ages, and the littlest player, June's neighbor, barely three, was it forever. Too slow to tag anyone. The game's fun ran on his stuck. The old eye sees: a game working.

This month's eye, foot-level: one pair of feet getting heavier every round while everyone else's skipped. The fix, quiet; mid-game; no announcement, because announced kindness embarrasses: "New rule: It gets a deputy. Deputy runs, it points. Pointing's the power job."

The littlest: pointing like a general within a minute. The game: better, actually, for everyone. Lily, after, who'd watched the whole maneuver: "You changed the rules so nobody noticed you changed them for him. The noticing would've cost him more than the stuck did. Kindness that shows off charges the person it helps."
