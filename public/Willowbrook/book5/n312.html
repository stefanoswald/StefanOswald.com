Night 312

The Case of the Kept Secret

Month 11: The Kindness Detectives

This entry, as it happened, is about a page that is blank, and why. The detective witnessed something this week. The case notebook shows the aftermath: "Case 9: [No entry]. Closed. Unlogged. Some kindnesses are too private even for secret saves."

What the lamp heard, carefully, names removed even there: someone at school, an grown-up someone, did something quietly enormous for a kid's family in hard weather. The detective saw the edge of it by accident: a envelope, a handshake that wasn't a handshake, two faces doing the thing faces do when dignity and need are negotiating. And she understood, at whatever cost to the agency's completeness, that witnessing it further, even logging it, would spend some of what made it work. "The agency's rule is: the unwitnessed good deserves witnesses..."

She worked out at the lamp. "But this one... the not-being-seen was part of the kindness. The dignity part. Watching it, even secret-watching, takes a piece. So the real rule underneath the rule is: the kindness comes first.

Even before the saves. Witness what wants witnesses. Guard what wants guarding. Know the difference or don't be a detective."
