Night 363

Bobby Bear's Letter to Five

Month 12: Almost Five

Under the pillow, two nights into five, right on the bear's annual schedule: The letter. "Dear five. It is me. You read the last one yourself and this one too. The letters can be longer now.

Good. Bears have more to say than they used to let on. Things I know about the year coming: First grade has the wall at the playground. Your arms already know the schedule. Trust the arms.

The kite month comes. The wind is already practicing for you. Mateo builds something this year that the whole town talks about. Be there for the first flight. Bring bandages and belief, equal amounts. Your brother turns four and gets his first chain.

Help him live a year worth chaining. You already know the requirement. The two-part stories start this year: Some things that happen won't finish in one day or one page. That's not the story breaking.

That's the story growing up with you. Let them run long. And the meadow: Open, always, rumor still false, key still warm, gate still patient. One more birthday after this one, give or take. The gate knows its season.

Love, Bobby bear. P. S. The tree wrote kind. The watch voted it the best word yet. The moon hummed twice. That's the moon's highest rating. It has only ever hummed twice for free things, and you've now caused two of them."

The letter: read twice, folded, saved with its four elders. The box: a book. The book: growing.
