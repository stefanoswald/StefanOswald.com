Night 50

The Map Drawn Wrong

Month 2: Mistakes Are How

Project this week: a town map, drawn from memory. Ambitious. Detailed. Wrong, the library placed east of the park when it lives west. The discovery: on the checking-walk (her idea, "maps have to be tested; that's what walking's for").

"It's backwards. In my head it's backwards. I've walked there a thousand times and my head still holds it wrong... Want to know something?" said Papa. "Real mapmakers, the old ones, they drew sea monsters where they hadn't checked yet.

Whole edges of the world labeled 'here be dragons.' The honest ones admitted the map had guesses in it." The revision: library moved west; and in the map's corner, her own legend, invented on the spot: a small dragon, drawn wobbly, labeled "Parts I haven't checked yet." (Every map she ever made, the far pages hold dozens, carries the dragon. Trademark. Warranty. Confession. All three.)
