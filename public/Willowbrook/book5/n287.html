Night 287

The Trade That Went Bad

Month 10: Losing Well

A trade this week, at the bench market: her duplicate fox sticker for a four-square kid's "rare" waterfall one. Done cleanly. Regretted within the hour, the waterfall, on inspection, was common as gravel (June's album had three). The fox was the actual rare one.

She'd been, the trading term arrived with the sting. Sharked. The court: "Options: one, demand the trade back. Two, tell everybody he sharked me. Three..." The long breath. "honor the deal. I agreed. He didn't lie, he just knew more than me and let my not-knowing do the work.

That's... that's legal, in trading. Sharky, but legal. The deal stands." The extraction, priced out at the lamp: "The fox cost me a lesson: Know the values before the table. Check June's album first, she's the market data.

Ask the one-day rule for big trades, sleep on it. The fox was tuition, Mama. Expensive class. One session. Never needs repeating." (It never repeated. Sure enough: she checked values first for life, stickers, then trades of every grown-up kind.

The fox, wherever he ended up, financed a forty-year discipline. Best tuition the bench ever charged.)
