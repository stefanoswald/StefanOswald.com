Night 297

The Rematch Calendar

Month 10: Losing Well

This month's closing setup (every month ends in setup now; the story has stopped pretending otherwise): The rematch calendar. Every deferred contest, logged with its season: "The fun run: next spring. Training starts March. The stride gap is one winter of legs, closeable.

The spelling ghosts: ongoing, eternal, t-h-r-o-u-g-h drilled weekly out of pure spite-respect. The memory game: after I build my own palace (June's teaching me the bug-rooms method, you put the memories in the rooms, Mama, it's shape for your head). Checkers vs. Fee-oh: continuous.

The era of mostly is over; the era of earning begins. The snow leopard: no rematch. Some things stay accurate. The kickball call: no rematch. Put down. The fifth-grade wall at the playground: two summers out, under the academy's actuarial tables.

Saved. A calendar of rematches," Mama observed, "is a calendar of hope, you know. It's both books at once, the counts and the words. Every rematch is a number with a promise inside. The losing month ends with a calendar full of again. That's the only right ending it could have."
