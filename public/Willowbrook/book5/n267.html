Night 267

The Lost Mitten Exchange

Month 9: Friends Old and New

The school lost-and-found, checked this week (she checks everything; the bin was overflowing; nobody else was going to): Forty-one single mittens. Zero pairs. "Forty-one orphans, Lily. The bin is a orphanage and nobody visits."

The matching service, launched at recess: the orphans, laid out on the bench in taxonomic rows. The service's insight, hers, and the story thinks it's this month's best: "They don't have to match their original. They have to match somebody. A red-stripe and a red-solid is a pair if the hands inside say so.

We're not finding the old pairs. We're making new ones." Results, week one: eleven new pairs adopted (mismatched, gloriously, the mismatch became, within a month, a small school fashion; the story swears this is true; check any February playground since). The remaining thirty: bagged for the mitten tree, next winter, "where orphans go to get famous."
