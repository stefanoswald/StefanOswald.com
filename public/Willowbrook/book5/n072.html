Night 72

How the Willow Feels

Month 3: How Does Harry Feel?

She took this month's question up the hill: "Willow. Do you feel things? You watch everything. The whole town's happys and sads go past you. Does any of it get in?" The tree moved its gold.

Said nothing, as ever. And the answer assembled itself in the long quiet, the way the hill's answers do: Look at the branches. They bend down. Toward everyone. A tree that felt nothing would grow straight up, all sky, no reaching.

This one has spent three hundred years leaning closer. And Harry, who had trundled up alongside, added the ground-level footnote: "Its roots hold the whole hill together, miss. Feeling things is what roots are. You can't hold ground you don't care about."
