Night 34

Theo's Tower Rule

Month 2: Mistakes Are How

Theo's tower fell again this week. Sixth time. The fusstation: building toward weather. The intervention, floor-level, veteran to recruit: "Fee-oh. Count with me. How many times did it fall?... Six. And how many times did you build it?...

Six. Right. That's the problem. The math is wrong. Falls and builds can't be even, the builds have to win by one. Six falls, seven builds. That's the whole rule of towers. The last number has to be a build."

He counted it out on his fingers, twice. Built the seventh. It fell too, and he looked at his hands, did the math, and said, thunderstruck: "Then eight. Then eight..." She confirmed. (Tower eight stood.

The rule outlived every tower. "The last number has to be a build," check the far pages; both children run their whole lives on it.)
