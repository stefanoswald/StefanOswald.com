Night 285

The Collection That Stayed Incomplete

Month 10: Losing Well

A quiet loss this week, the collector's kind: the animal sticker album, a year of trades and allowance and market-day hunts, forty-nine of fifty slots filled, hit the wall: sticker fifty is discontinued. Confirmed by the market clerk, by the company's little printed slip, by three other collectors' identical grief. The album will never be complete. Ever.

Structurally. The lamp session ran long: "Forty-nine fiftieths, Mama. The album's a 98-percent and the last two percent is unbuyable. All that collecting and it ends on a hole." And then, mid-sentence, her own turn, someone saw her make it: "...

Unless. Unless the hole is the exhibit. The museum label rule, 'The mystery is the exhibit.' The empty slot with its own story: 'Number 50: The snow leopard. Discontinued. Never caught. Somewhere out there being rare.'

That's... Mama, that's better than the sticker. Snow leopards are supposed to be uncatchable. The album's not incomplete. The album is accurate." The album is accurate. (The slot got its label. The label became the album's most-shown page. Complete collections, she ruled thereafter, are "finished." Hers was true.)
