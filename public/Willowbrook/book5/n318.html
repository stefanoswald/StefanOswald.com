Night 318

The Unkindness Case

Month 11: The Kindness Detectives

Truth page. The agency opened its first dark file this week, because instruments that find kindness find the other thing too. The pattern: a third-grader, blacktop edge, running a small meanness operation on the littlest kids. Nothing punchable, the deniable kind: the "accidental" ball-keep-aways, the name said wrong on purpose, daily, the laugh with hooks in it.

Small enough to slide under grown-up radar. Constant enough to bend three little kids' recesses flat. The agency's dilemma, at the lamp: "The rules say witness. But witnessing meanness just... saves it. The little kids don't need my saves, Mama.

And I'm not big enough to be the fix, the third-grader outranks me by a foot. So what does the detective do with a dark finding?" The answer, built with Mama, and the story stamps it as this month's most important procedure: "The dark findings get reported. To the grid that's for them.

Not tattling. Reporting. Tattling is getting somebody in trouble. Reporting is getting somebody out of it, the little kids out. The recess monitor got the pattern, the whole pattern, dates, the deniable details that only look like something in a stack.

Grown-up radar can't see one hook. It can see nine." (The stack worked. The operation ended within a week, quietly, correctly, by the people whose job it was. And the third-grader, as it happened for completeness, turned out to have his own basement, and got helped too.

The dark saves, handled right, help both directions. That's why you report to the grid instead of going to war.)
