Night 275

The Dice Have No Memory

Month 10: Losing Well

The science, run before bed: one die, a hundred rolls, tally chart. (this month gets the numbers-month's tools. All her months inherit all her months.) The finding: the six came up seventeen times. The one: fifteen.

"Basically even. The die doesn't know I needed sixes. The die doesn't know anything. It's not punishing me. It's not saving the good rolls for Papa. It has no memory, no plan, no feelings about me at all.

And how does that land? Some people find it cold." The consideration, full-length. Then: "... Three. It lands three. If the dice had feelings, every bad roll would be about me, like the die looked at me and chose.

That's the hot thing's whole story: 'the game is against you.' But the dice are just weather. Rain doesn't pick your picnic. The six doesn't skip your turn on purpose. The game isn't against me.

The game isn't for me. The game just is, and I get to play it."
