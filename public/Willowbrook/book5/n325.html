Night 325

The Question Row Comes Up

Month 11: The Kindness Detectives

Farm report, late May: The question row is up. The dragon corner, inspected sprout by sprout: the purple carrots (tops green as any carrot's, "the purple's a secret till harvest; carrots keep their cards down"). The ground cherries. The luffa (a vine with ambitions, "it's eyeing the fence, Grandpa.

The fence should know"). And the okra, Margaret's okra, standing straight-backed and stubborn in the warming dirt, exactly as advertised. Joe stood over the row a long while, hat in hands. "Forty years of safe rows.

And the wild one's the best-looking bed on the farm. The question row's just the notebook in dirt..." She said. "Open questions, planted. August answers them. That's the whole system, and Grandpa, the way things worked was Grandma's.

'The wild row.' She saved the question forty years ago. You just finally planted it. Some maybes are crockpots. Forty-year crockpots." (Joe turned to the fence awhile. The barn dust found him. And the almanac, that evening, took an entry in his careful capitals that the story reproduces in full: "Question row up.

All four strangers sprouted. Okra standing proud. You were right, margaret. Forty years late and the fence is glad to be wasted. J.")
