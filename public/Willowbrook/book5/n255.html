The Web Repair Rule

Month 9: Friends Old and New

The fight, being survived, got what everything survived gets in this family: Rule. ("The mistake pays for itself if you extract the way things worked. Petrov's shelf. The faculty of flops.") The web repair rule, as drafted at the lamp: "One: The nine-second window.

Take-backs are cheapest immediately. Use the window. Two: If the window's missed, the interest clock starts. Silence compounds. Every day of quiet costs more repair. Pay early. Three: Sorry your half only. The other half is theirs to carry, grabbing it steals their repair, same as fixing the bridge steals the tower.

Four: The sorry has 'I did' In it. Five: Repair in the same venue as the break, lunch fights get lunch repairs, public mean gets public mending. Six: After: No re-litigating. The do-over rule's last part: no holding the first try.

Repaired is repaired." Papa, reviewing the draft: "You know some married grown-ups don't have this figured out. Then somebody should give them the rule. It's not secret. It's just... nobody writes the friendship rules down.

Everybody's supposed to absorb them and half of everybody absorbs them wrong. Write. Things. Down. That's half my whole system, Papa."
