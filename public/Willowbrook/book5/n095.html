Night 95

The Exaggeration Check

Month 4: True Stories

The sled hill story had been growing. The story tracked it: eleven seconds became "So fast" became "fastest anybody ever went" became, at the fence, to Mateo, this week..." The hill record. A grown-up timed it.

"And Mateo, hill veteran, said mildly: "Huh. Cool. What grown-up?" (The silence. The specific cornered heat of an exaggeration meeting a follow-up question.) "... Nobody timed it. My papa counted eleven-ish. It felt like a record.

The feeling was record-size, so my mouth... rounded up. Yeah..." said Mateo, unbothered. "Stories do that. Mine grow too. The trick is..." He showed his scar collection. "keep the real numbers somewhere safe. Because if you round up long enough, you lose the true one, and the true one was yours. The rounded one belongs to nobody."
