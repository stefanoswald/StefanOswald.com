Night 288

The Bench Game Championship

Month 10: Losing Well

The spring fair ran musical chairs, and she arrived with an institution in her pocket: The bench game, formalized. Rules drafted, presented to the fair's game-runner (a teenager who accepted them with the shrug of the wise): the out-kids' bench elects a Commentary Chief each round; best commentary line wins "the Bench Crown"; the final chair-winner gets carried; the Bench Crown winner also gets carried. Two crowns. Two games.

One event. Her own elimination: round three. The commentary, per witnesses, peaked at: "Round five and the pre-sitter is back, ladies and gentlemen, he's evolved, he's barely walking, he's orbiting the chairs like a small polite moon..." The fair's game-runner reported the innovation up his chain.

The bench game ran at every fair game thereafter, official, printed in the program. "You lost round three," Lily observed, watching the double-carrying, "and you're the happiest person at the fair. Two-crown systems, Lily. Build enough crowns and the losing runs out of room. That's not cheating the lesson. That's shape."
