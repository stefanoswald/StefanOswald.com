Night 237

The Letter to Miss Dana

Month 8: The Settling In

The letter went out this week, because the promise-tally book flagged it: "Tell miss dana how school goes" had sat unkept since September, and steady promises can't miss. The text, archived: "Dear miss dana. Report from big school. The jar: Franchised to room 4 (The bus slip is cooking).

The reading corner: Mine on tuesdays. Ms. Alvarez: She kneels and she says maybes are crockpots. She's a good one. You'd like her. My brother lives in your room now. Give him the blue cushion and watch out: He beeps.

Thank you for the first school. Everything I do at the big one, I learned the bones of it on your carpet. Love, Olivia. P. S. I know about the file. Mama told me it exists but not what's in it.

That's the correct amount of knowing. I can wait. I learned that on your carpet too." The reply came in a week, and the family framed one line of it, by unanimous vote, for the kitchen wall: "You were never my student, Olivia. You were my colleague who happened to be three."
