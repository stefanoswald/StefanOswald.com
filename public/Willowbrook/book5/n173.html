Night 173

The Piggy Bank Meeting

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

The celebration fund got its physical check this week: coins out, sorted, stacked. Theo: assistant. (Compensation: he got to do the dumping. The dumping is the best part; everyone knows.) The towers, built by denomination: quarters, dimes ("small but they're worth two nickels, size lies, Fee-oh, value and size are different jars"), nickels, pennies ("the penny tower's the tallest and worth the least.

That's a whole lesson standing right there"). The total: $4.35. The goal (a paint set, the good one, the wind-day artist's upgrade): $7. "Three months of saving to go, at quarter-speed. Unless..." The tally book consulted. "the given column and the saved column can't trade, that's the rule, but the spent column can shrink.

No rolls for two weeks. The paint set eats my rolls." (It did. The far pages show the paint set, earned in five weeks, and the first painting made with it: a roll, painted lovingly, titled "The cost.")
