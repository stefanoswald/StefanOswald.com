Night 65

The Two Sads

Month 3: How Does Harry Feel?

A double-sad evening: hers (the trio's fort plan collapsed. June grounded, Lily at cousins') and Mama's (a phone call, the kind with long silences, about an old friend far away). At the lamp, the comparison, done by this month's scholar: "Mama. My sad is loud.

It stomps. Yours is quiet. It just makes you slower and you look out the window more. Same day, two whole different sads. Why? Sads come in sizes and speeds, love. Yours is a weather sad, big, fast, gone by Thursday.

Mine is a distance sad. It doesn't storm. It just... sits with you awhile, like a guest. Should I leave your guest alone? No. You can sit with us. That's actually the whole treatment." She sat. Quiet, for once, entirely. Twenty minutes.
