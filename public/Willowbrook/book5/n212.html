Night 212

Bobby Bear at the School Fence

Month 7: Big Enough

On the month's last evening, Stella shone just above the Great Willow. The big-enough month, closing, and Bobby Bear arrived late, dusty, having come the long way: past the school. He'd walked the fence line. All of it.

Twice. "Report..." said Oliver Owl. "The territory is sound..." said the bear. "I checked. The cubby holds her name properly, good solid printing, proud letters. The mural bench has honest sightlines to both doors. The reading corner takes volunteers, and the teacher..."

He paused, professionally. "the teacher kneels to talk. You can trust the ones who kneel. The building expected her. Buildings know when they're expecting someone. This one had the feel. And you?" asked Stella, gently, because the watch knows what school means for bears.

The needing changes. The daytime hours go to cubbies and carpets now. The bear's shift shortens, year over year, until it's nights and hard days only, and then someday... "Me..." said the bear, settling against the willow's trunk, comfortable as old velvet.

"I'm exactly where I've always been. Different is not less, she wrote that down when I told her, so it's on file twice now. The daytime work is hers and the school's. The night work is mine as long as wanting lasts.

And the meadow..." He looked up at the stars over the sleeping town, over one window with a gold backpack hanging ready beside it. "the meadow stays open. Growing up doesn't close it. That's a rumor, and bears hate that rumor, and I will personally walk every fence line in this valley for as long as it takes to keep it false.

Log it..." said the owl. "Mmm," hummed the Moon, which is how the Moon signs things. Goodnight, school kids of Willowbrook, and the bears who walk their fence lines, and the buildings that expect them, and the meadows that stay open. Everything is checked. Everything holds. Sleep.
