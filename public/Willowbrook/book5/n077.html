Night 77

The Peas Diplomacy

Month 3: How Does Harry Feel?

Dinner. Peas. Theo: refusing. Papa: invoking the try-bite rule. Standoff. And the intervention, from an unexpected quarter, his sister, who profits from all Theo prosecutions: "Wait. Look at his face. That's not the fussy no.

The fussy no is loud and looks at you. This one's quiet and looks at the plate. The peas are a real-gag for him, like mushrooms for me. He's not doing a rule-fight.

He's scared you'll make him." Papa looked. (Outside eyes. The family is each other's film.) The face was, in fact, the plate-look. "Peas: optional," Papa ruled. "Carrots in trade." Accepted instantly, with the relief of the genuinely spared.

"How'd you know?" Papa asked later. "I eat next to him three hundred times a year. I got the data."
