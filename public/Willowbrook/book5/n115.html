Night 115

The Apology to the Sky

Month 4: True Stories

It finally rained this week, the first real rain since the feeder fib. And she went out in it, on purpose, hood down, and was heard addressing the sky: "Okay. About the fib. I used you as a excuse and you weren't even there, you were off raining on some other town fair and square.

That wasn't right. Weather deserves better. You show up honest, sun when sun, rain when rain, you never once fibbed a forecast. You're maybe the most honest thing going. So: sorry. And thanks for the undersmell.

We're square now." She came in soaked and satisfied. "Did the sky accept?" asked Mama, deadpan, with the towel. "The sky sent the undersmell extra today. That's a yes. Weather holds no grudges, that's another thing it does better than people."
