Night 195

The Haircut of Readiness

Month 7: Big Enough

The pre-school haircut. Same chair, same barber, one year and one whole education since the memory question. "Big school, I hear..." said the barber, pumping the chair. "I've cut first-day heads for forty years.

Want the professional briefing?" (She did. Obviously.) "Every September, the first-day heads sit in this chair. The kids are all scared of the same three things and none of them say it. And every June, the same heads come back taller, and the school year's in the hair.

I find the paint from art class, the playground sand. Heads keep records too. My whole trade is reading and resetting them. What three things? The bathroom, the lunch table, and will-anyone-like-me."

(Her eyes went wide, the universality, confirmed from a second independent source, forty years of data.) "And the answer to the third one, miss, from every head I've ever reset: yes. Someone always does. Usually by Tuesday."

One curl kept, per tradition. ("Last curl of before-school me. The box gets it. Before-school me earned her spot.")
