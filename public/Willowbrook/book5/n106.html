Night 106

The Weather Report Fib

Month 4: True Stories

Small fib, dumb venue: "I can't do the feeder sweep, it's gonna rain, I saw the clouds." (There were no clouds. There was a chapter she wanted to finish. The fib seemed cheap at the counter.)

The sky, of course, spent the whole afternoon a flawless, accusing blue. Every window: evidence. By four o'clock the sunshine felt personal. She swept the feeder at five, unprompted, and saved the report at dinner: "I fibbed about rain to finish my chapter.

The sky spent six hours proving me wrong. Never lie about something that hangs there all day." Papa, gravely: "Rule of the trade: never tell a lie with a window in it."
