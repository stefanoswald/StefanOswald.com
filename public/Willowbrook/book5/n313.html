Night 313

The Duchess Case

Month 11: The Kindness Detectives

The agency's farm office opened one case this visit, and it was the tenderest in the saves: The duchess question. "Grandpa. The Duchess hasn't laid in two years. June's papa says hens that don't lay... don't stay.

Farm math. But she stays. The meanest chicken in the world, eating feed, laying nothing, pecking people, well, not me, for two years past her math. That's a anomaly. Anomalies are cases. What's the case?"

Joe worked at the fence rail awhile, the way he does when a thing needs the right-sized words. "Your grandma named her. Last bird your grandma ever named, she was already sick that spring; she picked the meanest chick in the batch and called her Duchess and said, 'that one's got my temper, Joe. You be patient with her.'"

The rail took some weight. "Farm math is farm math. But some birds carry a promise. The Duchess dies old and fat and mean, in her own good time, on this farm. That's the case, detective.

Case closed." (The case notebook's entry, written small: "Case 11: The duchess is a promise wearing fevers. The meanest thing on the farm is the most loved. Both true. The hard part, again, always. The agency finds that half the town's anomalies are promises in disguise. Keep pulling freds: Find love. Every time. The whole town is rigged this way.")
