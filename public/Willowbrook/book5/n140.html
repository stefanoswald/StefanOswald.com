Night 140

The Rainy Day Archive

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

Rain took the whole Saturday, and she spent it inside the treasure box, reading it, cover to cover, like the book it turned into the day she learned to read. The full archive, in order: the aunts' four letters. The bear's letters. Lily's first reply (Yu ar my best friend to, "still the best sentence anybody ever mailed me").

Earl's bus note. The Sam card's thank-you. The wanted posters. The town box notes. Vera's fast-legs horse, gifted. The curl from the barber. The red mitten. The key-shaped nothing from the dream (an empty spot she keeps anyway, "because the hand remembers the shape").

And the finding, at the bottom of the box, at the end of the rain: "Mama. The box tells one story when you read it in order. It's not stuff. It's a book that happened to me.

Everybody's got one, even people with no box got one, just unwritten... That's the saddest thing about no-box people. Their book's happening and nobody's keeping it."
