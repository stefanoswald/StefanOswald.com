Night 314

The Petrov Tally book

Month 11: The Kindness Detectives

Case twelve: The mending shop's math. (Long suspected. The "at cost" for loved books. The halved fees "for artistic merit." The wheelbarrow's free annual "maintenance." The washer sent to school. The agency finally ran the numbers.)

The investigation, done over a broken wagon wheel: "Mr. Petrov. The agency has questions. You fix the town's too-loved-to-replace things. But the fees, we've been tracking. Kids pay in hugs. Old people pay in 'next time.'

The hard-weather families pay in never. How is the shop still a shop?" Petrov turned the wheel slowly, examining spokes. "The rich pay full. The comfortable pay fair. The rest pay what the mending's worth to them, which is sometimes a drawing for the window.

The shop eats. The margin..." he shrugged, a whole philosophy in two shoulders. "A mender who only mends for money is a parts-replacer. The town keeps my lights on. I keep the town's things going.

It's the same transaction the willow runs: roots hold the hill, hill holds the roots. Forty years, nobody's checked it but you. The books balance. Different columns than a bank uses."
