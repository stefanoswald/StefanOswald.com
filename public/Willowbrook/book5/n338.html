Night 338

The Moving-Up Ceremony

Month 12: Almost Five

The school year closed this week: The moving-up ceremony. (Gym. Paper hats. Families in folding chairs. Theo on Papa's shoulders yelling "Wivy" at the procession, by the family's oldest concert tradition.) The ceremony's best moment, by unanimous family vote: Ms.

Alvarez opened the class jar, the year's maybes, read aloud to the gym, answers rendered by the class in chorus: "'Maybe the broken bus is happening for us.' Confirmed! The bird-call driver!'Maybe the rainy week is happening for us.' Confirmed!

The indoor olympics!" (Nineteen slips. Nineteen confirmations or crockpots. Zero defeats. The jar's public debut: undefeated.) And her folder, handed over with the teacher's note clipped on top, read at home, framed beside Miss Dana's line: "She came in reading books.

She leaves reading rooms. Both will serve. It was the honor of my year. A. A."
