Night 180

What the Numbers Could Hold

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

At the end of the month, Olivia counted what numbers had taught her.

She had helped count Willowbrook, learned why thirteen loaves made a baker's dozen, watched Theo reach twenty, kept three columns in a money notebook, won the bean-jar estimate, hopped across a hundred chart, and seen a car's odometer turn to a new row of zeroes.

She had also learned when counting should stop. Stars were better to stand beneath. Fairness could use numbers without being reduced to them. A zero could mean nothing, a place held open, or the beginning of a very large number.

"People say mathematics is cold," Olivia told Mama. "But every number I met had a story inside it."

The egg count protected the hens. The money columns protected promises. Measuring made a race fair. Estimating helped people share.

Numbers did not replace words. They showed a different side of the same world, and Olivia wanted to keep both.
