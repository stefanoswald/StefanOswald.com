Night 209

The Two-School Family

Month 7: Big Enough

Theo started at Miss Dana's this week. (Her room. Her Miss Dana. The circle carpet, the cubbies, the whole first school, his now.) The morning routes diverge at the corner: she goes left to big school, he goes right to little school with Mama.

And at the split, the first morning, he stood wobbling on the edge of it, the bigness of doors, arriving at three and a half, and his sister knelt down: "Fee-oh. Listen. That room is the best first room there is. The story corner has the good cushions, the blue one's the softest, claim it early.

Miss Dana keeps saves on the good ones, so be a good one. The hamster's name is whatever came after Buttons, love it anyway, that's the whole lesson early. And if you get scared..." She pressed something into his hand: a folded paper.

His own brave list. Started for him. Three entries already, in her printing: The vacuum. The big slide. The storm count. "the list tells you who you are when you forget. Add everything. I'll help Sundays."

The goodbye ceremony, invented on the spot, performed daily for years after: fists together, then fingers spread wide..." The firework. "Then both, walking backward three steps, pointing: "See you at the table." (Office hours. The colleague system. The family's whole day, rejoining at the window gold.)
