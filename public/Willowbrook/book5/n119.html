Night 119

The True Month, Counted

Month 4: True Stories

Month's end. At month's end, Some changes fit neatly on the page: one lie (two letters, one night, heaviest carry of the year, retired). One truck: confessed faster than discovered. One Earl: bussed home. One tooth: hidden two days, public since, fairy renegotiated to scale.

One report card: issued to the grown-ups. One Gate question: passed from the chair. One five-dollar question: held, not kept. Other changes resisted numbers: that lies are pets you have to feed and truth stands free all night.

That stories ask permission. That truth has doors, not walls, and 'not yet' is true, and safety answers first, and grandmas get the with. That the truest thing about this house is that truths get held here, not used. "Good true-month?

The lightest one. That's the secret nobody advertises, Mama, everybody thinks truth is heavy. Truth is the light one. I put down everything I was carrying this month and it was all lies and half-hides.

The truth weighs nothing. It just stands there. Being true. While you sleep." Remembered. Sleep light, little one. Everything you're carrying is true, and true carries itself.
