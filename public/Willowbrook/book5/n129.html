Night 129

The Word Collector's Big Catch

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

The chapter book yielded the season's biggest catch, page 61: the author described the farm dusk as "Gloaming. Papa. Gloaming. The book says it's the time when day and night trade shifts. The gold-blue time.

The willow's time has a name." Verified in the big dictionary (the answer shelf; she has privileges now): gloaming, ancient, Scottish roots, the dusk. "Old word. Barely used anymore..." said Papa. "Barely used? The best time of the whole day and people stopped saying its name?"

The outrage of a collector finding a treasure in a discount bin. "Then we're using it. This family. Starting tonight. The word's not allowed to die while we're here, words only die when everybody stops saying them.

That's a murder everybody does together by accident." And so it entered the family vocabulary, permanent: come the gold-blue hour, someone says it's gloaming, and someone else comes to the window.
