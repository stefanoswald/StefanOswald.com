Night 93

The Compliment Lie

Month 4: True Stories

Lily's cousin Vera drew a horse. It was, the story consults its notes, not a horse. And Vera asked, direct: "It's good, right?" The trap: lie kind or true mean? She found the third door, live, under pressure: "The legs are my favorite part.

They look fast." Vera beamed. Drew four more, all legs, getting better. The debrief at the table: "Was that a lie? I didn't say it's good. I said the true part loud and left the rest quiet."

Mama: "That's called kind and honest. Find the true thing worth saying and say that. You never have to say the false thing, there's always a true thing standing somewhere near it. The skill is looking till you find it.

And if there's no true good part? Then the true thing is 'you worked hard on this' or 'tell me about it.' There's always a true thing. Kindness never actually requires the lie. It just requires the search."
