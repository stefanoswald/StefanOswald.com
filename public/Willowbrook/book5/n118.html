Night 118

What True Cost This Month

Month 4: True Stories

This month's-edge tally, Papa's tally book format, applied to truth itself: what did true cost this month? "The report card to you and Papa: cost the most. My stomach hurt the whole walk to the table. Telling Lily about the scared-note fib: second most.

She looked at me different for a minute. A whole minute. The copied-drawing defense: cost standing up in front of the art table with my chest hot. The confession race with the truck: cost waking up Fee-oh."

The finding, delivered to Mama: "True always costs something. That's how you know it's true, fake never costs. But Mama, here's the thing this month taught: the cost is a one-time. You pay it and it's paid.

Lies cost rent. Forever. Every day, a little more. True is expensive once. Lies are cheap daily until you add it up." True is expensive once. Lies are cheap daily.
