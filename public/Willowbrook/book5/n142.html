Night 142

The Word Sanctuary Takes a Patient

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

The word sanctuary's fame spread to Number Nine, and this week Mrs. Hollis arrived at tea with a patient of her own: "Woolgathering. It means letting your mind wander soft, like gathering bits of wool off fences. Daydreaming, but kinder to it.

Forty years I watched children woolgather at windows and the report cards all said 'Distracted.' Wrong word. Woolgathering is where half their good ideas came from. The word for it died and the respect died with it."

The intake was formal. (The sanctuary has forms now. Of course it does.) Woolgathering: admitted, adopted, deployed within the day..." Fee-oh's not ignoring you, Mama, he's woolgathering, look at his eyes, he's miles out. "and the family calendar gained a Sunday institution: woolgathering hour, windows, no plan, minds off-leash.
