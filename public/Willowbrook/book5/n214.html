Night 214

The Reading Corner Job

Month 8: The Settling In

Ms. Alvarez made it official this week: Tuesday reading corner, she reads to the group. ("The volunteer post you applied for on day one. The position is yours. The cushions are your territory.") The first session, professionally logged: the book (the bear book, the retired one, brought from home, cleared with the teacher: "classics travel").

The grip: outward-facing, veteran. The gasp at the lost button: seven-voiced. And one kid, the smallest, a thumb-sucker from the far side of the carpet, who moved closer. Then closer. Then was simply at her elbow, following the pages like they were weather.

"That's the chain..." She reported at dinner. "The librarian to me, me to the carpet. The little elbow kid is somebody's future librarian, probably. You can tell by the leaning. Leaning is the first credential."
