Night 344

Theo's Second Masterpiece

Month 12: Almost Five

The couch-back office reopened this week. The security: upgraded ("No wooking. Is bigger this year"). The secret: held eleven days, a personal record, achieved via a new technique the story admires: he told the secret's edges out loud daily ("is not a picture.

Is not a rock. Is not a dog."), venting the pressure without breaching the vault. (Negotiating with the thing around the thing. The button lesson. It's in both kids. It's in the walls of this house by now.)

The product, revealed at the proper hour (he waited for the quiet morning this year, the courier has learned his own delivery windows): a book. Four pages, folded, stapled by Mama. Title, crayoned over dotted letters: "The wivy book. By teo."

Page one: the two figures, hands a shared scribble. Page two: a bell. Page three: a magnifying glass, drawn enormous, with two eyes behind it. Page four: "The end. You the good one. T." (The author, asked about his influences, cited: "Wivy's books. The shape: something, something, something, the end. I put the hearts in the somefings.")
