Night 251

The Sick Week Web Test

Month 9: Friends Old and New

The flu came for her properly this time: five days down, fevered, couch-harbored, the works. And the web, the story wants this documented in full. Activated: Lily: the whistle code from two streets over, adapted, one long note at four o'clock daily, meaning "still here, still yours" (heard from the couch; answered by Mama on the porch till the patient could answer herself). Wren and Noor: a map, hand-drawn, of the classroom that week..."

So you know the territory when you get back. "every desk labeled, Biscuit Gravy's mood charted daily. Rosa-No-Relation: horse drawings, one per day, slipped in the mailbox, a serialized story in pictures ("the horse is sick in picture two. The horse recovers. It's a get-well epic").

Mrs. Hollis: soup, delivered to Mama at the door, with a note: "Teachers know sick weeks. Tell her the class is unmanageable without her. It will help more than the soup." (It did. Both helped.)

Milo the elbow kid, via the Friday folder: a drawing of the reading corner, empty, captioned by Ms. Alvarez at his dictation: "It leans wrong without you. The web held..." She said at the lamp, day five, mending.

"I went down and the web held me the whole time. That's what it's for, Mama. That's what all the Fridays and the benches and the not-digging build. You build the web on the good days so it holds on the down ones."
