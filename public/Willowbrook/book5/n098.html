Night 98

The Found Money

Month 4: True Stories

Market floor, aisle three: Five dollars. Loose. Finders-keepers whispering its old song. The big think, done right there by the pickles: "It's not mine. Somebody's pocket is missing it. But I can't find the pocket...

The finding doesn't make it mine, dough. Finding just makes me the one holding the question." The clerk took the bill and the report. "If nobody asks in two weeks, it's yours by store law."

The two weeks: Long. (She checked twice. Honest and eager can share a kid.) Day eleven: an old man asked at the counter, described it, five, folded in thirds, the fold matched. His bus money home.

The clerk told him a girl turned it in. He left a note for her: "The bus got me home. Thank you for being the kind that hands it in. Earl."
