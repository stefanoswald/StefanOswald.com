Night 160

The Infinity Fight

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

The fence hosted the oldest fight in childhood this week: Mateo claimed to know the biggest number. ("A googol. Hundred zeros. My teacher wrote it.") "Googol plus one..." She returned, instantly. "Googol times googol. That plus one."

And then, mid-volley, she stopped, someone saw the vertigo arrive: "... Wait. The plus-one never runs out. Never never. There's no top. Numbers just... keep. That's not a game, Mateo, that's terrifying. And also the best thing I ever heard.

Both. Two coats. Yeah..." said Mateo, who'd hit the same wall at her age. "My teacher says infinity's not a number. It's a direction." Infinity is a direction. The fence went quiet with it. Even Theo, hanging on the rail, felt something pass overhead. "...

So when I count..." She said finally. "I'm not climbing a ladder with a top. I'm walking a road with no end. Any number I ever reach. I'm still at the beginning. Everybody is. Even the googol is at the beginning."
