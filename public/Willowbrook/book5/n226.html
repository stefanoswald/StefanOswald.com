Night 226

The Class Election

Month 8: The Settling In

Room 4's first election this week: line leader, henceforth chosen by vote, weekly. The campaigning was as you'd expect (promises of candy that didn't exist; one kid's entire platform was a backflip he could not do). And then the room's policy veteran raised the veteran hand, not to run, but to amend the way things worked: "Ms. Alvarez, if it's the same winners every week, the not-winners stop voting-caring by November.

I saw it with the park races. Proposal: the winner can't win again till everybody's had a turn. The vote picks the order, not the only. Everybody leads. The vote just says when." (The teacher's face, per witnesses, did the thing teacher faces do when the file writes itself.)

New rule: adopted. The election became a rotation with democratic seasoning, and the room's least-noticed kid, who'd never have won a straight vote all year, led the line in week three, and the story wishes everyone could have seen his shoulders.
