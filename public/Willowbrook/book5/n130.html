Night 130

The Mrs. Hollis Book Club

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

The Friday knock evolved: Mrs. Hollis, it emerged, had read the farm-dog book, taught it, in fact, for twenty years. And so was founded the town's smallest book club. Members: two. Median age: irrelevant.

The inaugural session, over tea: "The river chapter. Did you think the dog would drown, the first time you read it? The first time? I was certain. I cried in the teachers' lounge. You cried too?!

Forty years of reading and the book still got you? Child, the books don't stop getting you. That's how you know you're still alive in there. The day a good book can't reach you. That's the emergency."

The session ran two hours. Positions were taken (the ending: perfect, unanimous; the middle sister character: "underwritten," per Hollis, a word Olivia wrote down and deployed weekly thereafter).
