Night 122

The Cliffhanger Outrage

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

Chapter four ended mid-danger. The dog, the book dog, already beloved, swept downstream, and then: white space. Chapter five. "The author just stopped. The dog is in the river and the author went to get a snack.

Who does that?!" The ten-minute loophole: invoked. The dog: saved by page two of five. The outrage: converted, by lamp-out, into professional respect: "... Wait. The author did it on purpose. The stopping-place made me need the next part.

That's a trick. A mean, great trick." A pause, and the story heard the exact click of a writer leveling up: "I'm gonna do it in my next book. The bear's gonna lose the whistle right at the page turn."
