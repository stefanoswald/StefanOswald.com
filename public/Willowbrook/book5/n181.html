Night 181

Stella Counts the Watch

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

When the month's stories were told, Stella blinked once over the listening tree. The numbers month, closing, and Stella, as always, led the Count of the Watch. "Sound off. One Moon, one owl, spectacled, ledgered.

One postmaster-mouse, forty-one volumes in circulation. One hedgehog, on rounds, counted in absentia, he's passing the house with the fog tonight. One butterfly, second-generation, wintering-prep underway. One frog, one turtle, honestly hatted.

Fireflies: seasonal, uncountable, and, by the small one's finding, better stood-under than tallied. Stars: " a pause, the old joke, ", present. Count ongoing since the first night. No final figure expected. And below?" asked Oliver Owl, quill ready.

"Below: one town, secretly numerical. One family, both books balanced. Two children, no." Stella's gold flickered, the way it does at the good corrections. "Two children on the rolls. But count the layers, the way she'd tell us: one reader-becoming-librarian, one boy on block 3 learning his letters, one chain of thirty badges, one school waiting to be introduced, one tree holding three words with a fourth in the works.

Layers: past counting. The town is more than its number. Towns always are. Log it..." said the owl. "this month the smallest counter learned what the watch has always known: you count what can be counted, you stand under the rest, and you never once confuse the two.

Mmm," hummed the Moon, uncounted, uncountable, present. Goodnight, numbered and numberless Willowbrook. The count never closes. Sleep, you count. You always counted.
