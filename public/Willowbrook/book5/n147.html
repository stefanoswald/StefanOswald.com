Night 147

The Almost-Five Reading Test

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

For science, at the lamp: the first book. Level one. "Dog sits." The book that took four nights and a signal system, one autumn ago. Time to completion, this week: ninety seconds. Including dramatic voices.

Including a review ("the plot is thin, but the dog is likable. Two stars. Historically important"). And then the quiet, staring at the little book. "Mama. This was a mountain. The hardest thing I ever did.

I remember the sweat of it. And now it's... a pebble. I stepped over it without thinking." The eyes, coming up, slightly alarmed: "The sequel's boulders, persevere, melancholy, are those gonna be pebbles too? Every one.

That's what the doorframe's been telling you every year, in inches. Mountains become pebbles. It never stops being true. Then the things that are mountains right now, the big school, the fifth branch, the whole rest of everything, " She stopped. Did the math. Grinned the grin. "... Pebbles loading."
