Night 134

The Banned Book Question

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

Mrs. Hollis mentioned it in passing, a book from her teaching years that "some towns wouldn't shelve." The concept did not compute, and got taken to the source: "Books get banned? Somebody can tell a whole town what it's not allowed to read?

Who? Why? What did the book do?" The librarian's face did something the story had never logged there, quiet and fierce at once, like banked coals. "It happens, child. Always has. Books that tell uncomfortable truths, mostly, some people would rather ban the book than sit with the truth of it.

And so you'll notice..." She gestured at the lantern above them, the light that never goes out. "what this building's lamp actually means. It's not decoration. It's a promise: the light stays on. All the books.

The comfortable and the uncomfortable. As long as one librarian's standing, the whole shelf stands... The lantern's a promise. I thought it was just pretty. The best promises are also pretty. Easier to keep them lit that way."
