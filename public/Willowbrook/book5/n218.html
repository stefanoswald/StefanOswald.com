Night 218

The Pencil Grip Correction

Month 8: The Settling In

School found the flaw this week: the pencil grip. Self-taught, three years old, deeply grooved, and wrong. ("Ms. Alvarez says the thumb's crossed over. It works for now but it'll tire out at big writing.

She says rebuild it now while the habit's young.") The rebuild: Miserable. The story logs it honestly: "The old grip is underground, Mama. That's the problem! You told me underground is graduation, but now the wrong thing lives down there and getting it back out is ten times the work of putting it in.

My hand keeps doing the old way without asking me. That's the truth about underground, love, it keeps whatever you bury. Right or wrong, it doesn't judge. It just runs. Which is why...why you check what you're burying before it goes down.

Bury fast, dig slow. Ugh." Three weeks of conscious gripping. Office hours extended. The homework done slow and grumbling and correct. (The new grip went underground by November. It held for life. She was still grateful at forty, later, every long-writing day.)
