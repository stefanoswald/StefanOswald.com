Night 87

The Hard Listen

Month 3: How Does Harry Feel?

This month saved its hardest test for the window seat. Lily, quiet all visit, finally: "Can I say a thing? A real one?... Sometimes you fix too much. The game rules, the feelings, everybody's everything.

Sometimes I have a problem and I just want to say it and you're already fixing it and it feels like... like my problem got taken away from me. It was mine and you took it." Every muscle wanted to defend. This month's training held: listen.

All the way to the end. Don't drive. "... How long has the fixing been too much? Not always. Just sometimes. The sleepover joke thing. I wanted to tell you about it sad, and you went straight to okay-here's-what-we-do...

Okay. New rule, for us: you say 'just ears' and I only do ears. No hands. Just ears. Just ears," Lily agreed. And then told the whole sleepover-joke sad, start to finish, uninterrupted, unfixed.

It took four minutes. She looked lighter at the end than any fix had ever left her.
