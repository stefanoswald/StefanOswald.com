Night 263

The Compliment Ambush

Month 9: Friends Old and New

The lunch table ran an ambush this week. Coordinated. Premeditated. Rosa-No-Relation, the confessed ringleader. The operation: at the sandwich lull, every kid at the table delivered one specific-true compliment, at her. In sequence.

By plan. "You always know when somebody's day is heavy before they say. You left the ladder down at the pole." (The wavering kid. Who is at her table now. Ladders do that.) "You gave me the bed trick and my b's park straight.

The corner leans right when you read." The scientist's own physics, applied to the scientist: flustered. Pink. Attempted deflection ("the bed trick is mateo's, technically..."), overruled by the table. At the lamp, still pink: "Mama, I mapped this.

Specific-true lands every time; the undercomplimented bounce biggest. But I never checked... I never checked if I was in the undercomplimented column. Givers usually are, Rosa-No-Relation says. The givers give so much nobody thinks they need the getting.

And do they?... Everybody's in the column somewhere. Everybody. The physics has no exceptions. That's why it's physics."
