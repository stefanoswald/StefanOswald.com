Night 12

The Question Wren Asked Back

Month 1: The Question Collector

She showed Wren the notebook at preschool. (Last months of preschool now. Big school in fall. Nobody discusses it yet.) Wren read every page. Slowly. Reverently. And then, the bench, the shade, the smallest voice doing its bravest thing again: "I have one.

For your book... Why do I like watching better than doing? Everybody acts like it's wrong. Even nice people act like it's a little wrong. Is it wrong?" The collector wrote it down carefully. Then answered it faster than any question in the book: "No.

The watch is a job. The willow does it. The moon does it. I do it summers from the fourth branch. Somebody has to see everything, udderwise it happens unwitnessed, and unwitnessed is lonely. You're not not-doing.

You're keeping. That's the whole job of half the sky." Wren's whole-face smile, second recorded instance.
