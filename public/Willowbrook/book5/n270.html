Night 270

The Purim Basket Surprise

Month 9: Friends Old and New

A doorstep surprise this week: a basket. Cellophane, ribbon, hamantaschen, fruit, a small toy, from Ava's family, three doors down. "For Purim..." The tag said. "Mishloach manot, baskets to friends and neighbors. It's the mitzvah of the day."

The investigation, done at Ava's door with full question-collector badges: the holiday, the costumes, the story ("there's a queen who does the brave-scared-plus thing at the highest stakes. Esther. Add her to the brave list's hall of fame"), and the baskets, everyone gives them, friend to friend, door to door..." And also gifts to the poor, matanot la'evyonim, that part's the rule of the day, not just the custom.

"The family's return basket, assembled that evening (reciprocity is this house's reflex): rolls, the good jam, a drawing. And at the lamp: "Mama, the holiday builds the web into the calendar. Baskets to friends: the strings, plucked, annually, by law. Gifts to the poor: the whoever's-cold part, by law.

Somebody wrote the web-tending into a whole holiday, a thousand-plus years ago. Everybody's been figuring out the same good things forever."
