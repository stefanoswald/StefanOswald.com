Night 210

The Letter That Came Back

Month 7: Big Enough

It came on a Thursday. Care-of had done its patient work: an envelope, her name on it, real handwriting. From Ms. Halloran. The author. The actual one. The letter, read aloud at the table four times by request, preserved in the treasure box, quoted here by permission of the story: "Dear Olivia.

Your letter found me on a hard writing day, and I read it in the garden, and then I read it again. You asked how I knew about Buttons. Here is the truth: his name was Whistler, he was a canary, I was six, and I have been writing about that empty cage for fifty years without ever once putting a canary in a book. You are the first reader who ever wrote to tell me she found the cage anyway.

Your papa is right about underneaths. Write yours honest, and I will watch for your books, author to author. P. S. Steal the cliffhanger trick. I stole it from Dickens. That is how the trade works: we are all stealing from each other's honest underneaths, all the way back to the first fire."

(The kitchen was quiet a long time. Then: "She had a whistler. Fifty years. The cage was in chapter nine the whole time and she never said the bird, she didn't have to. The underneath carries without the details..." and the rest of the evening was lost to a four-almost-five-year-old pacing the kitchen, on fire with the confirmed physics of literature.)
