Night 228

The Wobble Practice

Month 8: The Settling In

The conference's growth-jar went into training this week: Not-fixing, practiced deliberately, like laces. The test case arrived Wednesday: the block corner, a bridge collapsing, two kids arguing the fix, both wrong (she could see it from the carpet, the span was too long for the blocks; any veteran of Marcus's ramps knows). The old reflex: up, over, solved in forty seconds. The practice: hands in pockets.

Whole heart up. Watching. The kids argued. Tried the wrong fix. Failed. Tried the other wrong fix. Failed. And then, minute six, the longest six minutes of her school year, one of them said "what if the middle had a tower under it..."

Which is the right fix, which is better-found than given, and the bridge stood, and the two of them did the builders' dance, and it was theirs. All theirs. "It took them six minutes and it took everything I had..." She reported at the lamp, drained.

"But Mama, their faces. The fix I would've handed them was forty seconds. The fix they found was... it was the whole point of blocks. I almost stole it from them. Every time I ever fixed fast, I maybe stole one of those.

And sometimes fast-fixing is right, the paint reaching the crayons. The skill is knowing which. Fix the spills. Watch the bridges... That's the rule. That might be the whole rule."
