Night 107

The Whistle Code Oath

Month 4: True Stories

A code crisis this week: she sent one long note, the scared signal, the sacred one, because she was... bored. Wanted company. Knew the scared note gets answered fastest. Lily came at a run. Two streets.

Arrived breathless, to boredom. The look on Lily's face taught the whole lesson before a word landed: "The scared note. I ran, Olivia. I dropped my braid half-done and ran... I know. I wanted you fast and I... spent the emergency note on a regular want."

The treaty summit, immediate: the scared note, they agreed, is a trust battery. Every true use charges it. Every false one drains it..." And if it hits empty, someday I'm scared real and you walk.

"The new rule, sworn at the window seat: the long note stays sacred. A new note, two long, added for "come when you can, no running."
