Night 171

The Countdown Begins

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

The calendar said it plain this week: thirty days to big school. The chain tradition, summoned for its heaviest duty yet. But this edition, engineered for the job: every link holds not a memory but a credential, one thing she already knows how to do, to be torn off and re-read on the walk toward the thing. The links, sampled as written: "Can read.

Can make friends. Can ask questions. Can do hard things scared. Knows the shelf rule. Can find the handles of any new place. Has A Lily Dirty days..." She said, hanging it. "And every morning I tear off one proof that I'm ready.

By the first day, I'll have read my whole résumé to myself dirty times. Whose idea was the credential chain?" asked Mama, already knowing. "The brave list taught me. The list tells you who you are when you forget. This is the list, but aimed."
