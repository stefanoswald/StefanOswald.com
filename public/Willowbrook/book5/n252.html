Night 252

The February Pole

Month 9: Friends Old and New

The pole's February appointment, self-scheduled, honestly deferred, came due early. (March was the forecast. February delivered. The good years keep running ahead of their letters.) the story of the crossing: the high platform. The chalked hands.

Lily at the bottom. And the commit, the all-at-once letting go, the drop, the grip finding the pole mid-air and believing... and the slide, and the landing, clean, knees bent, ta-the. "The commit muscle grew..." She reported up the pole to the platform, to the next kid wavering there.

"It grows from the other braves, the bars grew it, the deep end grew it, the hand-raising grew it. You're not scared of the pole. You're just early. Poles keep. Come back when the muscle's fed."

And Mateo, at the fence after, receiving the report: "The academy notes the story: pole at five years, one month. And the coaching at the top, that's the part the academy really logs. Any kid can slide. You stopped to leave the ladder down."
