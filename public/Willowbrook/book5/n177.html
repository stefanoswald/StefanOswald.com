Night 177

The Map's Scale Bar

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

The town map yielded its last secret this week: the little ruled bar in the corner. "One inch = one mile. Wait. The map made a deal? A inch of paper carries a mile of world?

Who agreed to this? Everyone who's ever folded a world into a pocket. That's what scale is, an honest trade, printed right on the corner so nobody's fooled. Big things ride in small things, as long as the deal's disclosed."

The applications, cascading: "The bean jar, the layers were a scale. Joe's almanac, a year rides on one page. The notebook, my whole questions year rides in one book, inch-to-mile. Books are scaled lives, Papa.

The dog book was somebody's whole heart at one-inch-to-the-mile, and when I read it, it unfolds back to full size inside me..." She stopped, hearing it. "That's why the books work. That's the deal the whole time. Authors fold. Readers unfold. The scale bar is the once-upon-a-time."
