Night 152

The Counting Town

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

This month opened the way her months do: with a walk that turned into a theory. "Papa. The lane has four lampposts. The bakery sells in twelves. The market's aisles go to nine. The porch has three steps, everybody's porch has three steps, I checked six porches...

What are you telling me? The town is secretly made of numbers. The words were hiding everywhere and then they turned on. Now the numbers are doing it. Everything's counting itself and nobody mentions it."

The walk became a count: fence posts, sidewalk squares (her stride: two per square; Papa's: one, "you walk in halves of me"), the church bell (counted at noon: twelve, obviously, "but it feels like more when you count with it"). Notebook: "The town runs on hidden numbers. The words turned on last year. The numbers are turning on now. What else is hiding? "
