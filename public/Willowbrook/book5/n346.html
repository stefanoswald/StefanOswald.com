Night 346

The Town Delivers, Fifth Round

Month 12: Almost Five

Birthday week, and the town delivered, per its five-year ritual: Monday: flowers on the step, a full jar of dandelions this year, dozens, tied with string. (Unsigned. Handwriting on the tag: suspiciously junior-detective. The supply chain honors its founder.)

Tuesday: rolls, warm, baker's-birthday-dozen ("as many as fit the bag," the bag was large this year; Rosa's scaling with the child). Wednesday: the library, a book at the desk, her name, and the note: "First grade has chapter books with maps in the front. This one's the best of those. The maps-in-front era is the golden one.

M." Thursday: at the fence, from Mateo: a whistle, carved, wooden, his work ("the academy graduates get hardware. You graduated like four things this year. Pick which this is for"). Friday: Petrov's van, briefly, at the curb: the wheelbarrow, back from "maintenance," repainted, and the star stencils now number five.

("Menders keep count too. Five years, five stars. The wheelbarrow's a almanac now.") And Mrs. Hollis's contribution, handed over at Friday tea, small and heavy: a brass magnifying glass. Real. Old. "Forty years on my desk.

Confiscated from a boy in 1979 who used it to burn leaves, kept to remember him by, he grew up to be an eye doctor, incidentally; the lens finds its people. It's yours now. The agency should have proper equipment."
