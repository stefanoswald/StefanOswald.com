Night 184

Two Ways to Tie a Shoe

Month 7: Big Enough

Olivia's school shoes had laces, and two teachers offered two methods.

Papa taught bunny ears. Mateo preferred loop, swoop, and pull.

Olivia practiced both for five days. Bunny ears worked by the second day, though slowly. Loop-swoop promised speed but came loose twice at the park.

"Which way wins?" Papa asked.

"Bunny ears for school mornings," Olivia decided. "The other way stays in practice until it works when I am in a hurry."

She added a double knot after the park failures.

"Every knot gets a friend," she said.

On the first morning of school, the familiar bunny ears held through the walk, classroom, and playground. Weeks later, loop-swoop became faster and earned its place.

Learning a new method did not require throwing away the dependable one. Olivia could choose the tool that fit the moment.
