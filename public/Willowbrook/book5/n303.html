Night 303

The Losing Month, Counted

Month 10: Losing Well

Month's end. At month's end, The month's visible changes were one program. One hundred dice rolls. One round-one elimination. One fun run (second by a stride; honor first, grief second, both whole; rematch calendared).

One sore winner. One constitution. One ghost revenge. One wrong stood call (said once, held by witnesses, put down, mostly, honestly, mostly). One flooded recital. One checkers trap (fourteen games in the building; the apprentice's masterpiece; the era of earning begun).

One snow leopard. One fox (tuition, paid once, class dismissed forever). One quit-versus-finished check (three questions; finished; the pool stays mine). One sportsmanship award. One clinic. What mattered most could not be totaled: that the hot thing is a sneeze and the game is the gym.

That every game has a bench game. That honor goes first and the sting means the wanting works. That some losses are wrong and stay wrong and you don't build on the grudge. That the losses column is the faculty, and she wouldn't trade the year, and the "no" came without hesitating.

"Good losing-month? The winningest month of the whole book, and I know exactly what I did there, and I stand by it." Remembered. The hot thing: domesticated, employed, clapping from the audience. Sixty-two nights to five.
