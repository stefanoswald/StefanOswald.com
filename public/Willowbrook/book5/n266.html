Night 266

The Grandpa Winter Visit

Month 9: Friends Old and New

Joe came to town this week, a rare deep-winter weekday visit ("the farm sleeps in February; the farmer gets parole"), and the porch arrangement gathered its indoor session: the fire, the seed catalogs, the reader and her audience of one, plus one apprentice (Theo, promoted to page-turner, a role he done with the gravity of a squire). The spring, planned by committee: the mammoth row, third generation ("Scout's line and the envelope line, we're running both bloodlines now; the fence row's his kids' territory"). The yellow tomatoes, renewed ("the experiment continues forever; some experiments just become the garden"). And a new line item, hers: "A question row.

One row we plant with something we never grew. Every year. The garden should have a dragon corner too, a parts-we-haven't-checked-yet. Gardens without dragons just repeat demselves." Joe looked at her a long moment over the catalog.

"Forty years I planted safe rows. Your grandma always wanted one wild one. I told her wild rows waste fence." He turned to the window awhile. Barn dust, February edition. "Put okra in your question row. She always said okra. We'll waste the fence."
