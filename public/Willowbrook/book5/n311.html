Night 311

The Anonymous Counterattack

Month 11: The Kindness Detectives

The agency crossed its own line this week, the inevitable line, the one the whole month was always walking toward: From witnessing to approach. "We've been catching secret kindness for two weeks..." The founder argued at the fort. "We know the tradecraft now.

The drop-and-go. The job-as-disguise. The early-restock. It would be a waste of training not to go operational." Operation warm front, done over one weekend: a jar of dandelions on Number Nine's step (Friday-adjacent, unsigned, "she'll know but she won't know-know").

The bench market's sticker corner restocked with her duplicates, free box, no note. Three "You got this" cards, Elias-style, three words, one sun, slipped into random cubbies (drawn by Theo; his suns have authority). The rolls fund: one week's spent column diverted to the boots man's counter..." For whoever's next. "making the detectives a link in case one's chain.

And the crown jewel: a new book, allowance-bought, left on the library's returns desk with a note: "For the collection. From the chain. A link." The debrief, flushed with tradecraft: "Being secret-kind is harder than being caught-kind.

The wanting-credit thing is a whole hot thing of its own, it knocks, Lily. All weekend it knocked. Anonymous costs extra. That's why it's the biggest size of warm. You pay the credit and the kindness. Double price. Double warm."
