Night 94

The Story That Wasn't a Lie

Month 4: True Stories

A troubled author this week: "Papa. I make up stories. The bear who lost his whistle never happened. Is... is my whole book a lie? Am I a liar with a library card?" Papa put down his own book for this one.

"Here's the whole difference, and it's one word: Permission. A lie says 'this is real' when it isn't, it takes something from the listener without asking. A story says 'come pretend with me', and everybody who opens the cover said yes to the game. Same made-up.

Opposite contract. Nobody's ever been robbed by a book that said 'once upon a time.' That's the storyteller's badge, it means: you know, and I know, and we're doing this together... So once-upon-a-time is a promise.

It promises the made-up is offered, not snuck. Best definition I've heard, and I've heard professors try."
