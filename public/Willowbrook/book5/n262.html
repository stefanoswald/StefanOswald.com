Night 262

The Skating Pond Hand Chain

Month 9: Friends Old and New

The pond froze proper this week, and the town's kids assembled for the year's first skate. Her second season on blades: wobbly-to-steady. Theo's first: pure wobble, ankles like question marks. And the pond's oldest technology deployed itself around him: The hand chain.

Mateo, Olivia, Lily, Theo. The chain absorbs the wobble. The wobble spreads thin across the steady. Everybody moves. "This is the web..." She announced mid-glide, loud with discovery, breath clouding. "The literal web. The new one wobbles and the chain holds and the wobble gets shared so small nobody falls.

Lily. It's the web. You can skate the web. You can also..." observed Lily. "watch where you're..." (The chain, distracted by its own philosophy, met the snowbank. All four. As it happened: the snowbank landing proved the thesis, shared wobble, shared landing, shared laughing, zero injuries. Even the crashes are better in chains.)
