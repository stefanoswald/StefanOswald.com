Night 349

The Day After

Month 12: Almost Five

The day after: streamers half-down, cake for breakfast, and the thank-you notes, fifteen, written in the year's best hand, each one specific-true by the compliment physics: "Dear mrs. Hollis. The brass glass sat on the gift table outshining everything wrapped. Forty years of desk and now it's got forty more of fieldwork coming.

O. Dear mateo. I picked what the whistle's for: The wren-and-noor bench. Free-person codes need hardware. The academy provides again. O. Dear dog from number five. You wore the bow all day.

That was your mission and nobody assigned it. Some agents are born. O." (Delivered to the fence. Read aloud by Danny. The tail, reportedly, understood.) And the last note, unplanned, written slow: "Dear four. You're done now.

You were the question year, the school year, the losing year, the seeing year. You packed more than any year yet. I'm not mailing this one anywhere. There's nowhere to send it. That's okay. Some letters are just for the saying. Five."
