Night 143

The Book Fee-oh Ruined

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

The scene: her library book, library book, open on the floor. Crayon. Across two full pages. The suspect: obvious, orange-handed, at large in the kitchen. The fury: mounting the stairs at speed. And then, the investigation's complicating evidence, examined mid-storm: The crayon marks were... a dog.

Unmistakably. Orange, wobbly, four legs and hope. Drawn on the page where the book-dog swims the river. And beside it, in the fridge-magnet letters he knows: T E O. (He'd read her the situation: the dog was in danger.

He'd sent his dog. To help. And signed the rescue.) The prosecution rested. Couldn't help it. The resolution: the library fine, paid from her allowance with Theo's dessert contribution, the librarian shown what they saw and ruling, with suspicious wetness..."

The fine is halved for artistic merit. "and the damaged book, bought outright at discount, because some evidence you keep.
