Night 101

The Fairy Negotiation

Month 4: True Stories

The wiggle progressed. The market question landed at the lamp, direct, in her standing policy: "The tooth fairy. Real answer, not the fun answer: is she real? Because Mateo says it's the parents and June says it's real real and I want the true, and I can hold it."

(The parents traded the look, the big-question look, rank three.) Mama took it: "True answer: the fairy is a game families play. The word comes from us. The game is real, the love in it, the tradition, the fun.

The wings are pretend. You get to choose now: keep playing knowing, or retire the game. Both are honorable." The big think was short: "Keep playing. Obviously. Knowing doesn't wreck games. I know the bear book's not real bears and it's still the best book...

But now that I'm inside the game: the rate. Mateo gets a dollar. What's our house paying?" (The negotiation, later, was fierce. Settled: one dollar for the first tooth, fifty cents thereafter, plus the tooth returned in a labeled jar if requested. It was requested. "Evidence. The body's first hand-me-down from me to me.")
