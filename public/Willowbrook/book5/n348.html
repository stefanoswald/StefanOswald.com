Night 348

The Five Party

Month 12: Almost Five

The five party, as kept by the story, which attended in its usual chair: The guests: everybody. The games: ran themselves, because underneath every game, the hunt was running, fifteen secret missions weaving invisibly through the afternoon, and someone saw the party grow strangely, wonderfully smooth: drinks refilled before empty. The youngest guest, laughing, twice, then constantly. Mrs.

Hollis, drawn into a twenty-minute conversation about her forty years by a seven-year-old on assignment who forgot it was an assignment. The kid at the edge, every party has one. Doored in, so gently nobody saw the hinge. Cake time: the sky revealed (the magnifier of stars over the gold town; gasps; Rosa's finest hour of a five-year run).

The candles: five, out in one. And the reading: fifteen deeds, aloud, no names, the party hearing its own secret goodness described, and the guessing (nobody guessed; everybody won; the plan held), and then the strangest, best thing: nobody wanted to claim their deeds even after. The anonymous had gotten comfortable. Fifteen kids went home owning a secret gold thing each.

"Best one yet?" asked Papa, at the wreckage, per tradition. "They're all the best one yet. But this one..." She looked at the yard, still faintly glowing with undischarged kindness. "this one keeps running. I didn't frow a party. I started something. Fifteen starters, out the door, loose in the town."
