Night 294

The Losing Well Exam

Month 10: Losing Well

This month's final exam, self-designed: The trio tournament. One afternoon, seven games (cards, checkers, the race, four-square, the memory game, jacks, and "the new one," Noor's game from her first country, taught that day, everyone equally beginner). Guaranteed: everyone loses something. That was the design.

That was the whole exam. Her results, honestly logged: won three, lost four. The four losses, checked for hot-thing activity: "The cards: lost to June, clean, no flare. The race: lost to Lily by a step, one small flare, breathed, unspent.

The memory game: lost to both of them (June's bug-brain is a memory palace), laughed the whole time. The new game: lost gloriously, beginner losses don't even count as losses, they're tuition, and Noor's game is worth every payment. It's got a move where you lose on purpose for a bigger later. Mama, her first country builds losing into the strategy.

The whole game teaches this month. I'm importing it. The bench crowns, the constitution, and now this. The losing lesson goes international." The exam answer, mutually awarded at the fort, cocoa raised: "Passed. All three.

The hot thing came four times and got breathed four times and the afternoon never once bent."
