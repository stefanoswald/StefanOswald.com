Night 141

The Sequel Problem

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

A crisis of riches at the library: the dog book has a sequel. Same farm. Same family. New dog. And she stood at the shelf, not taking it, for a long time. "What's wrong?" asked the librarian, who misses nothing.

"The ending was perfect. The old dog died right and the family was okay and the door closed clean. If I open the next one... what if it un-perfects the first one? What if the new dog is just... the old dog with the serial numbers changed?

Sequels could be the author feeding a lie to the first book." The librarian nodded slowly, professionally impressed. "That's a real risk. Readers have been burned. Here's my forty-year rule: a good sequel doesn't reopen the closed door.

It builds a new room off the same hallway. Read the first chapter, standing right here. You'll know by page three which kind this is." Page three: the new dog was not the old dog with the numbers changed.

The new dog was scared of the river, the same river, and the family had to learn a whole different shape of patience. "... New room. Same hallway." She checked it out.
