Night 32

The Backwards Cake

Month 2: Mistakes Are How

The month began with a cake. Her first solo-ish bake. Measuring: confident. Stirring: masterful. First bite, whole family assembled: ... Salt. For sugar. The whole cup. The faces. The faces. Theo drank his entire milk without breathing.

And the baker, watch her; this is this month's whole lesson in one kitchen, went from horror, to hot cheeks, to... studying the bag. "The bags look the same. Who designed that?" To: "Note for next time: Taste the white stuff first."

To, already, laughing. "The cake's a mistake..." She ruled. "but it's a funny one, and it taught me the taste rule, so it paid for itself." Mistakes that pay for themselves. Month two had its motto. (The salt cake entered family legend. Requested annually, one ceremonial bite, forever.)
