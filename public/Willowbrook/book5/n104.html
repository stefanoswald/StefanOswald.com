Night 104

The Story Hour Truth

Month 4: True Stories

Reader chair, story hour, mid-bear-book, and a very small kid, front row, urgent hand: "Is it true? The bear? The button?" (The room waited. The librarian, shelving nearby, paused to hear the call.

This is the storyteller's oldest gate, and every teller passes through it in their own style.) The answer, from the chair, age four-almost-five: "It's not fact-true. No real bear lost this button. I checked; it's a made-up bear.

But it's heart-true. The losing-something feeling? True. The looking-everywhere? True. The somebody-helps-you-find-it? The truest. Facts are true on the outside. Stories are true on the inside. That's why we need both kinds, you got a outside and a inside, right?

Right. So." The small kid absorbed this. "... Read the inside-true one again." She read it again. (In the wainscoting: "The chair passed the Gate question today. 'Facts are outside-true. Stories are inside-true.'

The lantern has waited two generations for a keeper who could say it that clean. Note: she is four. Underline: four.")
