Night 259

The Old Guard Meeting

Month 9: Friends Old and New

This month's-end summit: The old guard. Fort together, the founding three, Olivia, Lily, June, cocoa in the thermos, the mitten flag flown. The plan, as minuted: Item one: The web review. The new strings assessed by the founders: Noor ("approved.

The waves are honest"). Rosa-No-Relation ("approved. Survived a fight, which is the real credential"). Milo, Elias, the school wing entire. Item two: The worry, raised by June, quietly: "The web's getting big. The old fort only holds three.

Do we... get smaller to each other as it grows?" The answer, unanimous after discussion: "The fort holds three because the fort is the founding, not the whole country. Countries grow. Foundings stay founding-sized.

Nobody ever gets less founding." And the constitution, recited at the flag, gained its first new rule in two years: "This fort is for the three of us and whoever's cold. New rule: The three of us is permanent. The whoever's cold is infinite. Both forever."
