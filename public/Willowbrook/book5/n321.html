Night 321

The Caught-Being-Kind Wall

Month 11: The Kindness Detectives

The agency went legitimate this week, partially, carefully, on its own terms: Ms. Alvarez, who sees everything, had seen enough of the agency's work to propose institutionalizing it: a "Caught Being Kind" wall, names posted, school-wide. And the agency's founder, invited to consult, pushed back with the full weight of her saves: "The wall will catch the proud kindness fine. But the shy kindness bolts when named.

I got a whole case on it. And the biggest warms are the anonymous ones, naming them spends them. Counter-proposal: the wall posts the deeds, not the names. 'Somebody restocked the game shelf.' 'Somebody walks the new kid to the bus.'

The school sees the second grid exists, that's the point, but the somebodies stay covered. The shy ones get witnessed and protected. And the whole school starts wondering if they might be a somebody. That wondering is worth more than all the names."

Adopted. School-wide. The somebody wall. (It filled. It stayed filled. Kids stood at it doing detective math on which somebody was them, and started committing deeds just to feel the anonymous glow, which was, the founder noted in her saves, "the whole operation, scaled.

The wanting-credit converted into wanting-to-be-the-somebody. The hot thing of kindness, harnessed.")
