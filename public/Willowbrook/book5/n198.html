Night 198

The Last Preschool Day

Month 7: Big Enough

The last day of preschool came the way last days do: ordinary until suddenly not. The goodbye circle: every kid said a favorite memory. (Hers: the Maybe jar going to school. Lily's: the burnt-batch day Rosa visited with rescue rolls.

Wren's, spoken, out loud, clearly: "the bench." Three words. The room understood all of them.) The cubby: emptied. (Two years of it. The name tag peeled and kept, "first cubby. The box gets it.") And at pickup, Miss Dana handed Mama an envelope.

The file. "I keep notes on the ones who teach me..." She said. "She should have them someday. Not yet. Maybe at eighteen." (Mama put it in the drawer. With the letters. The story has read it, it reads exactly like you'd hope: the poster method, the fairness shape, the school-inside-the-school, the sub correction, dated and documented, ending: "In twenty years of teaching, I met one like her.

Tell her the file existed the whole time. Tell her she was seen.") "I'm gonna miss this room," she said at the door. Then, testing it: "... Miss. That's the right word. Not sad. Miss. Missing is what you do to things that were good."
