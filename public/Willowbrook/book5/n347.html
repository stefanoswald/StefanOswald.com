Night 347

The Remembering Game, Fifth Round

Month 12: Almost Five

The request at dinner, annual, traditional: "Tell me babies-me. All the favorites. And..." The new rule, new. "babies-fee-oh too. He's got chapters now. The archive covers everybody." The telling, greatest hits plus new material: The May snow.

The duck that earned the first word. The spaghetti helmet. The flush. And Theo's chapters, told while their subject glowed: the night he was born ("Livy waited at the window with the gold light on, for you, Fee-oh").

His first word ("Livy. You said her first. The whole town knows. She didn't stop telling people for a month"). The cow's bell. The vacuum conversion. The trap at checkers ("Already a classic. Fourteen games of patience.

The archive rates it top five all-time"). And the closing question, hers, at the lamp after: "Mama. The remembering game is the whole family keeping the whole family. But who keeps yours? The babies-you stories?

Grandma kept them and Grandma's..." The pause. The gentle press. "Somebody should be collecting yours and Papa's. Before... just before."
