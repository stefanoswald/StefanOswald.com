Night 310

The Case of Mrs. Hollis's Window

Month 11: The Kindness Detectives

Case five turned the agency's lens on its own ally, because Friday tea produced an anomaly: Mrs. Hollis knew things. The snack kid ("oh, Danny, yes, three years now; the dog's name is Biscuit, no relation to your hamster"). The boots man ("since the spring his shop closed, poor love, and look what he made of it").

The April mittens ("ah, you found the tree, did you"). The interrogation, direct, teacher to teacher: "Mrs. Hollis. You know every case in our saves. From this window. You're not a retired teacher. You're the town's original kindness detective.

How long?" The old teacher stirred her tea, headmistress-calm. "Child, what do you think forty years of playground duty is? You watch the quiet corners. You learn who shares, who hoards, who guards the little ones when nobody's assigning it.

I've been reading this town's kindness since before your mama was born. The window is just... my last classroom." A pause. The sly look. "Also: Who do you suppose does the April mittens?" (The case notebook's page for that Friday contains one line, triple-underlined: "Case zero: Solved. It was the first detective all along.")
