Night 260

The Pen Pal Assignment

Month 9: Friends Old and New

Room 4 launched a pen pal program this week, a partner class, two states away, names drawn from a hat. Hers: "Jordan. That's all I get. A name in a hat. A whole stranger." The first-letter craft, deliberated at the office hours table: "The first letter to a stranger is a map of you, but the one-inch-to-the-mile kind.

Too much and they drown. Too little and there's no handles. You give: one true big thing, one true small thing, one question, and one door." The letter, as sent: "Dear jordan. Big thing: I have a question notebook, three volumes.

Small thing: Our class hamster's middle name is gravy. Question: What's the thing everybody in your town knows that nobody wrote down? Door: If that question's too weird, tell me your favorite sandwich instead. Both answers are good doors.

Love, Olivia." (Jordan's reply, two weeks out, opened with: "Nobody ever asked me the first kind of question. The thing everybody knows: The creek behind the school sounds different before rain. Also: Turkey and pickles." A keeper, the story rules. The correspondence ran three years.)
