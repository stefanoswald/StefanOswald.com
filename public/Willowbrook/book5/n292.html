Night 292

The Puzzle With the Missing Piece

Month 10: Losing Well

The family's spring-break project, a five-hundred-piece barn scene, four evenings of table, finished this week at four hundred ninety-nine. One piece: Gone. Sky section. Hunted for three days: under the couch, the vents, Theo's pockets, the vacuum bag (Crumby was searched with dignity).

Gone. The puzzle: hole-d. The family: divided on response. Papa: "we order a replacement piece, they sell them now." Theo: "I didn't eat it." Mama: quiet, watching her daughter, knowing where this was heading...

"No replacement. The snow leopard rule. We frame it with the hole." And the case, made to the table: "Four evenings. All four of us. Fee-oh did the whole fence line himself. The hole isn't the puzzle failing, the hole is the puzzle being ours.

A replacement piece would be a stranger in the sky. The gap is the true one: five hundred pieces minus the one the couch ate, times one family, equals this puzzle. No other copy in the world has our exact hole." Framed.

Hung. The gap, small and honest, in the barn-scene sky. (Guests ask about it. That's the point. The story gets told. That was always the point. Complete things end conversations; true things start them.)
