Night 139

The Bedtime Filibuster Defense

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

Theo has discovered the bedtime filibuster: "One more story. One more. One more." And Mama, mid-negotiation, caught the eye of the filibuster's original patent-holder, observing from the hall with folded arms and deep professional recognition.

"He's using my moves. The one-more. The basement questions. The water request. The water request is mine, I invented the water request... And how did we settle yours?... The ten-minute reading rule. The loophole.

You gave me a legal door so I stopped picking the locks." A pause. The scholar, arriving at the policy: "He can't read yet. So his legal door should be... one more story, but he tells it.

To us. The teller gets tired faster than the listener. The filibuster eats itself." Instituted that night: Theo's story slot. He told a saga of a truck and a cow ("and then the truck, and then the cow, and then...") and was asleep by the third and-then, mid-sentence, the way filibusters end when the chair lets them run.
