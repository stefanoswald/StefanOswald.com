Night 299

The Sportsmanship Award Surprise

Month 10: Losing Well

The spring assembly ambushed her: Room 4'S sportsmanship award. Certificate, handshake, the gym clapping. For her. The kid who flipped a board twelve months ago. The confusion, genuine, processed at the lamp: "Mama, I lost all year.

The run, the chairs, the ghosts, the call, the fox. Since when do you get awards for the losses column? Read the certificate." She read it. Slowly. "'For how she wins, how she loses, and how she makes room for everyone playing.'...

The bench game. The handicaps. The two crowns. The treaty wall. Ms. Alvarez saw all that?" (Teachers see everything. It's the file thing. There's always a file, and the good teachers keep the good kind.)

"They didn't award the wins or the losses. They awarded the... the how. The column nobody numbers. The column nobody numbers..." said Papa. "is the one everyone remembers. Ask anybody about the best player they ever knew. They never tell you the score. They tell you the how."
