Night 211

The Big Enough Month, Counted

Month 7: Big Enough

Month's end. The check, done, for the first time in the story's history, by a school kid. Olivia could count five buttons. Two knot methods. One route. One latch. One alarm treaty. One recon. One bathroom plan (done day one; lock reported to Petrov, who sent a washer to school via Mama, fixing it, because this town is this town).

One pact. One first day (Lived). One day two. One homework office. One firework. One letter. The harder part to measure was that the chain was a mirror. That lists introduce you. That the rules travel and only the geography changes.

That day two is the real first day. That the tired is the receipt. That she was always big enough, and this month was just how she found out, it said so in Mama's handwriting, on the last link, and the story hereby enters it as fact. "Good big-enough month?

The provingest month. And Mama, the funny part? This month was called Big Enough and the whole answer was: I already was. The buttons and the laces were real. But the big was never in the fingers.

The big was in the... the showing up. And I been showing up since the first page of the first book of me." Since the first page of the first book of you. The story, which has been there since that exact page, confirms without reservation. Remembered. School kid. Carry on.
