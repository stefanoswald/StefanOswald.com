Night 217

The First School Sniffles

Month 8: The Settling In

The first school cold arrived (the classroom's welcome gift; every veteran parent knows) and took her out of job for two days. Day one: the classic sick-day comforts, soup, the couch harbor, the bear on day shift, Mama's cool hand. But day two brought a phenomenon the story wants on file: "Mama. I miss school.

Is that allowed? It's been three weeks of school total and I'm homesick for it from home. The carpet's doing circle time right now without me. Biscuit Gravy's getting fed by somebody else. The elbow kid is leaning on nobody...

That's not sick talk. That's belonging talk. You built a there there, love. Three weeks is enough when you build fast, and you've never built slow in your life." The return, day three: the carpet cheered.

(Actually cheered. Ms. Alvarez let it run a whole four seconds before settling them.) The elbow kid had saved her spot, guarded it, reportedly, with unprecedented ferocity.
