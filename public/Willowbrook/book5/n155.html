Night 155

The Allowance Tally book

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

The allowance got a tally book this week. (Papa's design, her demand, the truck repair and the marker restitution had been running on parental bookkeeping. "My money should have my math.") The columns, negotiated: Saved.

Spent. Given. ("Why given?" asked Papa, testing. "Because this month of helping said quiet help counts, and the mitten tree says the good give costs, and if the tally book doesn't have a given column, the money thinks it's all for me.

Columns are how you tell your money what you believe.") Columns are how you tell your money what you believe. (Papa wrote that one down himself. For himself, the story suspects.) Week one's entries: Saved: two quarters.

Spent: one quarter. Given: one dime, mitten-tree jar, early. ("Winter's coming eventually. The jar shouldn't have to wait for the cold to know somebody's coming.")
