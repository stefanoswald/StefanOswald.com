Night 48

Theo's Sorry Flowers

Month 2: Mistakes Are How

The crime: Theo, denied a turn with the good markers, scribbled across her wind-day drawing. The fury: hers, real, jaw-clenched, walked off per rule. And then the remarkable thing. Twenty minutes later: a knock at her door.

Theo. Behind his back: dandelions. Six. Mangled. Picked himself, from the yard, alone, nobody sent him. Nobody taught this exact move. "Wivy. I scwibbled. I was fusstated but the scwibble was wrong. Dese are sorry-flowers...

Is that a thing? I maked it a thing." (He made it a thing. Three years old, inventing repair from first principles, out of watched example and yard weeds.) "... It's a thing now..." She said, taking them. "It's a good thing." The dandelions got a jar. The jar got the windowsill.
