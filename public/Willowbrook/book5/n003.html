Night 3

The Wobble Morning

Month 1: The Question Collector

The bear's letter said spring, and now spring had arrived.

Papa carried the bicycle onto Maple Lane and removed the training wheels. Olivia watched the wrench turn. She had practiced gliding, steering, and stopping for weeks. Her balance already knew more than her worry did.

Papa held the back of the seat while she pushed off.

Down the lane. Around the chalk sun. Back toward the mailbox.

On the second trip, Papa let go.

Olivia rode half a block before she noticed.

"Papa, this is easy..."

The bicycle wobbled. Olivia straightened it and kept moving.

"I'm doing it alone!"

"You've been doing it alone since the mailbox!"

She circled back, laughing too hard to ride in a straight line. That night she wrote one question in her notebook: How long was I ready before I noticed?

The answer was half a block.

Outside, the bicycle waited on its kickstand while dusk folded gently over Maple Lane.
