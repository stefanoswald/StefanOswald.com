Night 90

Harry's Night Rounds

Month 3: How Does Harry Feel?

At the month's quiet finish, Stella watched from over the Great Willow. The feelings month, closing, and the watch heard, at last, what Harry Hedgehog actually does all night. "The rounds..." said Harry, simply. "Every night.

The whole town, garden by garden. I check the feet, the ones that dragged at dusk. The house where the swing stopped. The window where the fog was. The boy by the wall. The man with the carrier.

I don't fix anything. I'm a hedgehog. I just... pass by close, where the sad can feel something small and warm go past. You'd be surprised what that does. Something small and warm, passing close, on purpose.

That's the entire service. And the leaf?" asked Stella. "The one that never falls?" Harry was quiet a moment. "First one to ever ride my back. Years ago. A bad autumn. I was going to shake it off and something said: let it stay.

Everything since, the feet, the rounds, all of it. I learned from carrying one small tired thing that needed somewhere to be. Mmm..." Hummed the Moon. "Log this month..." said Oliver Owl, softly, for once not consulting the tally book.

"Log it: the small one below joined the rounds this month. She doesn't know it yet. She'll do them her whole life." Goodnight, heavy feet of Willowbrook. Something small and warm is passing close. On purpose. Sleep.
