Night 102

The White Lie Meeting

Month 4: True Stories

Under the oak, Olivia, Lily, and June argued about a difficult question: Is a lie ever allowed?

Lily offered the first case. "Grandma's soup is terrible. She is ninety-one. Do I have to tell her?"

They thought for a while. Olivia said, "You can tell the kind part that is still true: 'Eating lunch with you is my favorite.' The soup does not need a review."

June's case was more serious. "What if a mean older kid asks where your little sister is, and your stomach says not to answer?"

That answer came quickly. Safety came first. Nobody owed private information to a person who frightened them.

Then Olivia offered her own case. "What if Mama asks whether I'm okay, and I'm not, but I'm not ready to talk?"

Lily picked up a fallen acorn and rolled it between her palms.

"Say, 'Not yet,'" she said. "That is true. It keeps the door closed without pretending there is no door."

Olivia wrote the answer in her notebook. Honesty did not require saying everything to everyone at once. Sometimes the truest answer was a safe door with a truthful sign.
