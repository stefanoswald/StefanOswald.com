Night 183

The Button Campaign

Month 7: Big Enough

Link one: Buttons. (The school cardigan has five. Big-kid buttons, small, smooth, smug.) Day one's data: four minutes per button. Fingers like mittens. One quit threat, retracted by the one-step rule. The breakthrough came via Mama's trade secret: "Don't push the button at the hole.

Fold the hole's edge back with your thumb, then walk the button through. The hole opens like a door if you knock right. Everyone fights the button. The hole is the negotiable one." (The hole is the negotiable one.

As it happened this sentence's later career in the family, deployed for years, about buttons and everything else: when the obvious thing won't budge, negotiate with the thing around it.) Day four: all five buttons, ninety seconds, done while talking. ("The fingers learned it so hard my mouth got free. That's the graduation: when the skill goes underground and runs without you.")
