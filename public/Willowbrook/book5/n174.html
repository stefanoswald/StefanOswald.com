Night 174

The Symmetry Hunt

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

Bella landed on the fence post this week, wings open, and the counter saw it fresh: "Mama. Her left wing and her right wing are the same. Exactly. Flipped. Who folds her?" The hunt was on: Symmetry.

Findings by dusk: "Leaves: half-and-half matching. Faces: almost. I checked mine in the mirror with a paper down the middle. My left smile is bigger. I have a favorite side and never knew.

The house: the front door's in the middle, windows matching both sides, the builders folded the house on purpose. Snowflakes: six-way folded. Water can't stop folding. But: The willow..." And here the hunt stopped, up the hill, at the crooked, sprawling, gloriously unfolded tree. "the willow has no symmetry.

None. Every branch went where it wanted." The finding under the finding: "The folded things are built. The unfolded things grew. Building wants matching. Growing wants... reaching. The willow's not messy. It's just free."
