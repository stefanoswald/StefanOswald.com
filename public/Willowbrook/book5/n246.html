Night 246

The Old Friend Maintenance Call

Month 9: Friends Old and New

The web-tending check flagged it: Mateo. The fence summits had thinned, school schedules, winter, his new orbit of big-kid practices. The old string, going quiet. She plucked it: showed up at the fence, Saturday, with two of Rosa's rolls.

The summit ran long. And the intelligence surprised her: Mateo, eight now, middle school looming someday-soon on his map, had worries. Real ones, handed across the fence in trade for the roll: "The big school after this one. Everybody says you get lost.

Everybody says the old friends scatter. Everybody says...'Everybody says' is not a witness..." She returned, automatic. His lesson, from the rumor days, handed back across the same fence it came from. (He blinked. Then laughed the laugh of a teacher meeting his own homework.).

"Right. Right. Check the source. Find a kid who's there. And the scattering thing. Mateo, you taught me half my whole kit over this fence. Fences don't scatter. They just need both people to keep showing up at them. I'm showing up. That's my half. Do yours."
