Night 207

The Playground Cartographer

Month 7: Big Enough

Week two's project: The playground map. (New territory demands cartography. The pocket notebook, Volume Three, school edition, took the survey.) The chart, as drawn: "The blacktop: fast games, big kids at the far end, four-square is diplomacy (you can get invited or frozen out, watch a round before you knock).

The field: the running games, the handicap-race rules ported over already (I taught them; they took; fairness is portable). The mural bench: the embassy. Neutral ground, Wren's and mine, first recess, standing. The quiet corner by the maple: the chosen-alones read there.

Respected ground, you ask 'company or no' from five feet back. The monkey bars: a yet. (The third bar. My arms know the schedule by now. October, probably.)" And in the corner of the map, the trademark, carried over from the town map of the mistake month: one small wobbly dragon.

"Parts I haven't checked yet. Still drawing the dragons," Lily observed, over her shoulder, sketching the mural for the embassy page. "Always the dragons. The map that admits what it doesn't know is the only map you can trust.

New school, same rule. It's all the same rules, Lily, that's the secret nobody tells you about new places. The rules travel. Only the geography changes."
