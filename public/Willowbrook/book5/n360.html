Night 360

The Thanking of Four

Month 12: Almost Five

The last night. The thanking, fifth annual, the routine hers entirely now: "Okay, Four. Last night. Everything out loud: Thank you for the questions, a hundred and the unanswerables and the notebook that fills me back.

The wobble morning and the block I rode alone without knowing, that's the whole year in one bike, honestly. The mistakes that paid. The feet-reading. The truth that weighs nothing. The deep shelf and the author letter.

Author to author, Four, we got mail from the trade. The numbers and their kindnesses. The pattern of me. The school. Four, you were the year the world got rooms in it. The settling. The web that held through the flu.

The hot thing, domesticated. The eyes. The eyes most of all." The breath. The finishing: "You were the year I found out the town was gold under its streets. I'm keeping everything, the frame keeps your line, the folder keeps your pictures, the chain's bound, the box is full.

You can go be a was-year now. You earned the shelf... Nigh-nigh, Four." And at the sleep's edge, the bear tucked close, the lamp low: "... Five starts in the morning. The willow writes tomorrow.

I think I know the word. I'm not saying. Even to you, bear. Some things you let the tree say first."
