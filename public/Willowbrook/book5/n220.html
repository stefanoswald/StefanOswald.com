Night 220

The Gym Class Reckoning

Month 8: The Settling In

Gym class. (Structured. Whistled. Lined-up. The free-range park athlete meets institutional movement, and the story takes minutes.) The friction, honestly logged: "The whistle means stop even mid-fun. The lines are lines even when the fastest way is obviously not a line.

And relay rules say you run when it's your turn, not when you're ready, the turn doesn't care about ready." The discovery, mid-relay, that flipped it: her leg of the race came while she was still catching breath, turn-not-ready, exactly as feared, and she ran it anyway, and the team screamed her down the lane, and she tagged Lily's hand like passing a torch, and Lily flew... "...and the flying was mine too, Mama. That's the thing I didn't know about teams. When Lily ran, I was still in it.

My run kept going in her legs. Alone-running ends when you stop. Team-running keeps going in other people. The relay is... the chain, but web speed."
