Night 234

The Thanksgiving Table Jobs

Month 8: The Settling In

Thanksgiving, fifth edition of her attendance, first edition of her full employment: The jobs board: Papa: the bird, the heavy lifting. Mama: command, gravy, the timing miracle. Grandpa Joe: the stories and the sharpening. Theo: napkin deployment, acorn centerpiece maintenance, official taster.

And her: the table map (place cards, hand-lettered, proud o's throughout. Mrs. Hollis at the family table this year, by unanimous vote, Number Nine being no place for a teacher on a harvest holiday), the rolls (Rosa's recipe, her hands, first solo batch: eleven golden, one "rustic," the burnt wall gets its first family member), and the gratitude round, led when the moment came. Her round-opener, recorded in the almanac by request of Joe himself: "The rule is: no thank-yous for things.

Only for doings and beings. I'll start. I'm thankful that this family keeps everything, the lines on the doorframe, the letters in the drawer, the babies-me I can't carry, and the whole yous that everybody is. Somebody keeping you is the whole... it's the whole everything.

Okay. Pass the thankful. Grandpa, you're next, and no crying till the rolls." (He cried at the rolls. Barn dust. The story logged it tenderly and passed the gravy.)
