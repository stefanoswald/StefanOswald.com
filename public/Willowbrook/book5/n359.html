Night 359

The Last Days of Four

Month 12: Almost Five

Olivia spent the last ordinary days of four paying extra attention.

She walked the school route slowly: four lampposts, the crossing guard's wave, cinnamon halfway, and the Great Willow watching from its hill.

At the park fort, she read one chapter in the high branch and retied the bucket rope for whoever used it next. She sat on the bench with Lily, June, and Noor through five comfortable minutes of silence.

At Mateo's fence they shared two rolls and promised, "See you at five."

Mrs. Hollis served Friday tea and asked Olivia to return with a full report on the new age.

Nothing in the town looked different because a birthday was near. That was why Olivia wanted to see it all clearly.

The final walk home happened at dusk while windows lit one by one. Four was not ending with fireworks. It was finishing like a beloved book: every regular page read all the way through.
