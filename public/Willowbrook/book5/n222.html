Night 222

The Friday Folder Ritual

Month 8: The Settling In

The Friday folder came home and the family invented its ritual on the spot, because this family ritualizes or dies: The spread: everything out on the table, gallery-style. The tour: she presents each piece..." This worksheet: easy. This one: the grip fight, you can see where the old thumb tried to come back, there, that wobble.

This drawing: Biscuit Gravy, from life, he held still for once... "The calibration (Mama's design, and the story flags it for every family): praise aimed at the work, not the smart. "Look how you kept going on the wobbly line," not "you're so clever." ("Because clever is a thing you have and can't do anything about..."

The kid herself explained to Theo, who was absorbing the routine for future use. "but kept-going is a thing you do. They're praising the doing. The doing's the only part that's mine.") And the folder's best item, saved for last, a note from Ms.

Alvarez: "She asked the class jar's first hard question this week: 'What if a maybe takes years to turn good?' I told her some maybes are crockpots. She wrote it on the jar's lid."
