Night 55

The Mistake That Made a Friend

Month 2: Mistakes Are How

The weekly Lily-letter went in the wrong mailbox this week. (Number Nine and Number Eleven wear the same green. The story has flagged this to the town.) The retrieval mission, knock, explain, recover, found the new neighbor home: old, slow to the door, and (this is the part) lit up by the visit.

"A letter? For me? Oh, no, dear, here it is. But that's the first knock on this door in... some time." The first knock in some time. She heard the basement under that sentence. The letter got re-posted correctly.

And, new routine, self-assigned. Number Nine now gets a knock every Friday. Sometimes a drawing. Once a jar of soup-day soup, negotiated with the grown-ups. "The wrong mailbox was the only way I'd ever have knocked..."

She told Mama. "The mistake knew something I didn't." (Mrs. Hollis. Number Nine. Retired schoolteacher. She enters the far pages here, note it, and stays for three books.)
