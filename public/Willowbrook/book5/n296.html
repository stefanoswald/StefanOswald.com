Night 296

The Hardest Loss Rehearsal

Month 10: Losing Well

This month's last lamp session went deep, because she took it there, the way she takes things there: "Mama. This month trained me on games and races and stickers. But those are the practice losses, aren't they. The gym ones.

Somewhere out there are the real ones. The Buttons-size ones but bigger. Grandpa's old. The Duchess is old. Someday-losses. The kind no bench game fixes." The room held still. "I'm not asking you to say they won't come.

The true-voice rule. I'm asking: does the training even work on those?" And Mama, true-voice, by the standing law, gave it whole: "Some of it transfers. The honor-first, that works even at the biggest ones; maybe especially there.

The witnesses holding what's true. The mourn-quick-replant, though the big ones mourn much longer, and should. The end-on-a-build, the biggest losses take years to find their build, but love, they find it. The sweater becomes the hat.

The row distributes. Nothing loved gets lost, you've been learning the big-loss lesson since you were two, in every remade thing this family ever showed you. The games were never the real training. The whole shelf was...

The whole shelf was." She pulled the bear closer. "Okay. Then I'm as ready as a person gets to be, which is: not ready, plus the kit, plus you." Not ready, plus the kit, plus us.

(That's all anyone's ever been, little one. The story, which knows which far pages hold which goodbyes, confirms it gently and turns the light down slow.)
