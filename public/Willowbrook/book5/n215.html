Night 215

Rosa-No-Relation

Month 8: The Settling In

Rosa-No-Relation (the lunch-table cracker friend; the name stuck the first week and will now never unstick) earned her own page this week. The intelligence, gathered across five lunches: she's new to town entirely, moved in August, knows nobody..." The whole town is dragons on her map. "She has two older brothers ("so she knows three things about everything and how to duck").

She draws horses that actually look like horses. And, discovered gently, over crackers, she goes quiet every time the class talks about papas. (Feet first, names after; basements before questions. She didn't ask. She just noted, and moved the subject to horses, and watched Rosa-No-Relation's shoulders come down off her ears.)

"Her basement has a papa-shape in it..." She told Mama at the lamp. "I don't know what kind of papa-shape. I'm not digging. I'm just... not stepping on the spot where it's buried.

You can be a whole good friend to somebody and never dig. The not-digging is the friendship, sometimes."
