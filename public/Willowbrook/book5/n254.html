Night 254

The Friend Fight

Month 9: Friends Old and New

Truth page, this month requires one, and this month's is a fight. With Rosa-No-Relation. At lunch. Over, the story checks its notes and sighs, whose turn it was with the good markers during free draw.

It escalated: a sharp word, a sharper one back, a "Fine" apiece, and then: Two days of lunch-table silence. Two days of eating crackers at opposite ends, each waiting for the other, the silence compounding like dark-inflated worry. The lamp intervention, day two: "The nine-second take-back only works in the first nine seconds, Mama. We're at two days.

The fight's got interest on it now. So who pays first?... Whoever's braver. Or whoever remembers first that the friendship's worth more than the winning." A breath. "Same thing, probably." Day three: she crossed the lunchroom.

"Rosa. The markers were dumb and my sharp was dumber. I'm sorry for my half. The crackers taste wrong over there alone." And Rosa-No-Relation, who'd been assembling her own crossing all morning: "I was coming at recess. You beat me... My half was the sharpest half. Sorry. Split the markers?"
