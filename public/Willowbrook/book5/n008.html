Night 8

The Sunflower Check

Month 1: The Question Collector

Farm visit. First order of business, always now: The row. Fourteen seeds, thumb-pressed in spring. Current status: fourteen seedlings, knee-high, leaning sunward like a choir mid-note. "They all came. All fourteen. Nobody didn't.

Good seed. Good thumb. Good year so far..." said Joe, driving stakes. The measuring: against her leg. The notebook: "Sunflowers: Knee. Me: Still bigger. But they grow all summer and I only grow a hand a year.

By the bear's letter they win. Does it bother you? Getting beat by a plant?" asked Joe, straight-faced. "No. You planted the thing that beats you. That's not losing. That's farming." (Joe wrote that one in the almanac himself, margin of the June page. Farmers keep records too. Especially of each other.)
