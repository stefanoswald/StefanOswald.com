Night 330

The Saves Go to the Wall

Month 11: The Kindness Detectives

The roof repair's last wallboard closes this week, and the story can finally reveal what goes in the wall. The deposit, chosen after a year of considering: E. W.'s tradition, answered. A drawing, the house, the stick family, the willow on its hill, plus, folded with it, the agency's summary page, copied in her best print: "To the next kid of this house.

This town has a secret second grid. Buried rivers of unwitnessed good. The bakery, the crossing, the lantern, the mending shop, the window at number nine (Or whoever keeps that window in your time). I mapped some.

You'll find more. The rivers don't dry up. They just wait for new detectives. Also: The willow writes a word a year. Go check the bark. It knew me. It'll know you. Olivia, age almost-5, kindness detective, first of her agency.

P. S. The cracks on the ceiling of the little bedroom are named. The big one's the dragon. He's friendly. They're all friendly. I furnished everything for you already. Welcome home." The board: sealed. The wall: keeping it.
