Night 265

The Reading Corner Succession

Month 9: Friends Old and New

The growth-jar assignment, self-issued this week: The corner, delegated. Milo, elbow kid, spot-guard, leaner-since-September, got the chair for a Tuesday. She sat in the audience. (On her hands. Whole heart up.

The hardest seat in the room, by the conference, by the practice, per everything.) Milo's reading: wobbly. Slower than hers. The gasp at the story's turn came late and he had to double back for it.

And the carpet, later it plainly, leaned anyway. Leaned for him. The littlest listeners don't check fluency; they check presence, and Milo, who had guarded a spot all year, turned out to know everything about presence. "He read it different than me..."

She retold at the lamp. "Not worse. Different. He does the quiet parts quieter. I do the loud parts louder. The corner sounded like him on Tuesday, and the carpet took it, and, this is the thing, the corner's not mine, Mama.

The corner is the chair's. Whoever's in the chair, holding the book out, doing their own true version. I just got there first this year."
