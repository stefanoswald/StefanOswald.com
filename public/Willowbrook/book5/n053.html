Night 53

The Do-Over Rule

Month 2: Mistakes Are How

Agreed at dinner this week, proposed by this month's leading scholar: The do-over rule. "Anybody can call 'do-over' on a moment they wrecked. The grumpy hello. The snapped answer. The flip. You call it, you walk it back.

Literally, out the door or back to your chair, and you come in again and do it right. And everybody has to let the do-over count. No holding the first try." First invocations, logged within the week: Papa: came home tired, grunted past everyone.

Stopped. "Do-over." Went back out. Came in with the whistle and the whole hello. (Theo made him do it twice more. For joy.) Theo: snatched a truck. "Do-ober." Un-snatched. Asked. Received. Her: the bedtime stomp.

Do-over: walked back up, came down civil, negotiated the extra ten like a professional. "No holding the first try," that part, Mama observed, is the whole law.
