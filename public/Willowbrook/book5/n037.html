Night 37

The Drawing She Almost Threw Away

Month 2: Mistakes Are How

The sunflower drawing was going perfectly, which, as it happened, is always the dangerous part, when her elbow dragged green across the sky. The crumple: begun. The bin: approached. And then, the pause. The uncrumple.

The look. (The look is the skill. Everything else is decoration.) "... Wait. The smear looks like wind. If I add more that direction..." Scribble, scribble, tongue out. "it's not a ruined sky. It's a windy day.

The sunflowers should lean then..." and she bent all fourteen stems into the smear, and the picture got better than the perfect one was ever going to be. Title, written at the bottom: "The wind day. (The wind was a accident. The leaning was on purpose.)"

Mama framed it. Not for the sunflowers. For the subtitle. The whole month in nine words.
