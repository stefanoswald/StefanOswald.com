Night 238

The Snow Day Answer

Month 8: The Settling In

The year's first snow day, and her first as a school kid, which changes the whole institution: "Snow days only count when there's school to not-have. Fee-oh doesn't get it. He thinks it's Tuesday. The snow day is a school-kid sacrament."

The observance, in full: the 6:45 radio vigil (the school names read alphabetically; the held breath at the w's; the eruption). The return to bed..." The victory lap sleep, twenty minutes, just because I can. "Fort together, winter edition, raised by ten (the constitution recited, the mitten flag flown, the relic mitten, fetched from the treasure box for the ceremony and returned after, "flags visit; relics live home").

The sled hill, the whole crew, eleven runs. Cocoa at Lily's, window seat, the two-street summit reviewing the year so far ("best year yet?they're all best-yet. That's how we build them"). And at the lamp, the school-kid's new calculus: "The snow day was perfect because tomorrow the carpet comes back.

The day off is only golden because the days on are good. If school was bad, a snow day would just be... hiding. Mine was a holiday. The difference is everything."
