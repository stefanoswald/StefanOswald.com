Night 74

The Feelings Forecast

Month 3: How Does Harry Feel?

Instituted at breakfast this week, by this month's scholar: The feelings forecast. ("The sky gets one every morning. We're all skies. Where's ours?") Format: each person, one weather report, no explanations owed. "Sunny with question clouds,".

"Partly grumpy, clearing by lunch,". "Funderstorm!" (Theo, delighted, regardless of actual conditions, three-year-olds forecast for drama). And Mama's, one Tuesday, quietly: "Fog." Nobody fixed the fog. But the day adjusted around it, softer voices, an unasked-for tea, Theo's derailed tantrum ("Mama's got fog, Fee-oh, storm somewhere else").

By dinner: "Fog lifting. Thank you for driving slow." (The far pages confirm: still running, decades on. "Fog. Driving slow." Whole marriages have run on less.)
