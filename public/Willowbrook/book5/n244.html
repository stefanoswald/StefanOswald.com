Night 244

The January Roster

Month 9: Friends Old and New

Winter break's last days produced this month's founding document: The friendship map. Drawn at the window, circles and lines: "The old guard: Lily. June. Mateo. Wren. Marcus. The school wing: Rosa-No-Relation. The elbow kid (Milo, he has a name now, he told me it himself, leaning).

The quiet author of the free-word page. The grown-up division..." Yes, the map has one; the story loves that the map has one. "Mrs. Hollis. The librarian. Petrov. The crossing guard. Ms. Alvarez.

The animal branch: Biscuit Gravy, the Duchess, Bella." The finding, studying the finished map: "The circles overlap, Mama. Lily's old guard and school. The librarian connects to Sam connects to the carpet. Mrs. Hollis taught the crossing guard's daughter, it turned out.

It's not circles. It's a web. Everybody's holding somebody who's holding somebody. That's why the town holds, nobody's hanging from one string." Notebook: "The friendship map is a web, not circles. Nobody hangs from one string.

January's job: Tend the whole web. Old strings first. The old ones get quiet if you don't pluck them."
