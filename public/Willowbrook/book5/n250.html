Night 250

The Grown-Up Friend Defense

Month 9: Friends Old and New

The four-square philosopher struck again: "Grown-ups can't be your friends. They're just... grown-ups. Friends are kids." The rebuttal, delivered at dinner as a formal argument: "Evidence: Mrs. Hollis and I have a standing Friday, a book club, and a two-way file.

She tells me her true stories and I tell her mine. That's the whole definition. Petrov taught me the museum of mistakes and the steady idea and he waves off half the fee for the small, that's not a service, that's a friendship with a counter in it. The librarian runs a forty-year chain and put me in it.

The crossing guard learned my name before I learned it was learnable. Answer?" Papa, presiding: "The court finds friendship is defined by the showing-up, not the birth year. But, counterpoint for the story, the four-square kid's half-right about one thing: grown-up friends are different.

They hold more of the weight quietly. You'll even the tally book when you're grown. That's how those friendships run: long. Then I owe Mrs. Hollis about forty years of Fridays. You'll pay them to somebody. That's how it's always paid."
