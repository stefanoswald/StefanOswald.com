Night 112

The Overheard Compliment

Month 4: True Stories

The other side of the overheard-worry coin, discovered this week: through the kitchen door, late, parents' voices low, and this time, her own name. "...watched her walk that Priya thing back around all by herself. The second Saturday. Nobody taught her that shape of thing. She's going to be a person people are lucky to know.

She already is." She stood in the hallway a long time, cocoa forgotten in her hands. At the lamp, days later, she finally reported it: "I heard you say the lucky-to-know thing. Not on purpose.

And it felt... Double. Bigger than when you say nice things to me. Why bigger?" Mama considered. "Because praise to your face might be kindness. Praise behind your back can only be true. Nobody decorates an empty room."
