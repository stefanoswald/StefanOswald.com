Night 68

The Wren Translation

Month 3: How Does Harry Feel?

At preschool: Wren, upset. Her way, no tears, no words, just shutdown. Statue-still, eyes down. And the substitute: "Wren? Wren? Use your words! Can you tell me what's wrong?" Each "use your words": one brick deeper.

The translation service, deployed: "'Scuse me. She can't do words right now. Words are the first thing that goes for her, like how you can't whistle and cry. The asking is making it worse. She needs the quiet corner and one crayon and about ten minutes and no watching.

I'll sit near. Not with. Near." The sub, to her credit: stood down. The rule: ran. Minute eleven, at the quiet corner, one small voice: "... Somebody used my cubby." Fixed in thirty seconds. A cubby mix-up. Words came back when the room stopped demanding them.
