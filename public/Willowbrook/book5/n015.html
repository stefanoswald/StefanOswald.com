Night 15

What Makes a Home a Home?

Month 1: The Question Collector

A big one surfaced at the lamp: "The willow wrote home first. Before family even. Why first? What is home, the house? If we moved like Lily moved, would home come with us or stay?"

The test, done aloud: "The house? No. Lily's home moved and got a window seat. The stuff? No, the bee costume left and nothing broke. The people?... Warmer. The lamp? Warmer. The saying goodnight?" And then she built it, word by word, the way she builds everything: "Home is the place where somebody's keeping your pieces.

The town keeps pieces. The frame keeps lines. You keep babies-me. Home isn't a where. It's a who-keeps-you." (The tree, consulted later, moved its gold. Concurring, as far as anyone could tell.)
