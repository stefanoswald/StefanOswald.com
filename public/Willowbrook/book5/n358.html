Night 358

The Year in Pictures, Fifth Folder

Month 12: Almost Five

The folder. The floor. The fifth retrospective, artist-led: "September: the first-day drawing, look, the school's drawn too big and me too small. That's how it felt. Artists draw the feeling-size, not the real-size.

By October the school shrank and I grew, same building, new felt-size. The folder keeps the whole negotiation. November: the class book page. The willow, gold crayon, one word a year. December: the little gold country, the reading fort from inside, the flashlight glow drawn as a circle with two citizens.

Three citizens." (The bear. Always counted.) "February: the web map, the first draft, the circles before I knew it was a web. March: the hot thing. I drew it, Mama, look: a little red weather-cloud with sneeze-lines.

Drawing it was half the taming. April: the second grid, the town with gold rivers under it. And May..." The last one, held up. "the magnifying glass, and through the lens: everything, bigger and golder.

The year I got the eyes." The new folder, labeled five, empty, waiting. The museum of her: six folders deep now. The proof, accumulating.
