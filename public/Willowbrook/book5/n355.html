Night 355

The Just-Us Morning, Fifth Dawn

Month 12: Almost Five

Dawn. Candles. Pancakes. The original three. The letters, fifth annual reading: Mama's (excerpted; the drawer keeps the whole): "... This was the year you went out into the world, the school, the web, the whole town, and here is what I watched from the window: the world didn't dilute you.

Most people spread thinner as they spread wider. You got denser. More you, in more places. I don't know how you do it. I suspect you don't either. Don't ever figure it out, the not-knowing might be the mechanism..."

Papa's: "... Five things about five: One, you beat me at the dictionary game twice this year, legitimately, and I've never been prouder losing anything. Two, the wrong notes are yours now. I heard you convert a flubbed line at the recital-in-the-living-room without blinking.

The dynasty is secure. Three, your brother quotes you the way I quote my father. Listen sometimes; it'll teach you what you actually said. Four, the willow writes tomorrow. We never know the word. We never need to, it's been watching the same year we have.

Five: whatever it writes, it's already true. That's the tree's whole trick. It only writes what already happened in you..." The sealing. The drawer: Ten envelopes now. The someday: closer every year, patient as everything in this family.
