Night 227

The First News Home

Month 8: The Settling In

Parent-teacher conferences, week ten. She was parked at the hallway bookshelf ("browse," they said; she browsed with her ears). The fragments that carried: "...reading well past level... asks questions that reorganize my lesson plans... the jar was her idea, it's changed the room's whole weather... social instincts I've seen in few adults...one growth area: she can steer the room so well that she sometimes doesn't let it wobble. Wobble teaches.

The other children need their turns at fixing things, and she needs practice not fixing. It's her hardest skill. It may always be." The car ride home was quiet. Then: "I heard the last part.

The not-fixing. We know you heard. How's it landing?... Lily said it first. 'Just ears.' The teacher's saying the whole room needs a just-ears me sometimes." A long look out the window. "Even the good report has a one-jar rule.

The praise measures some jars and the growth-part measures another and they're both true at once. Two coats. Reports are two coats. Everything's two coats. The year keeps saying it."
