Night 264

The Snow Fort Diplomacy Crisis

Month 9: Friends Old and New

Recess built two snow forts this week, hers-and-Lily's crew, and a blacktop crew, and the ancient gravity of two forts began pulling toward the ancient conclusion: War. The scouts reported arsenals stockpiling. The rhetoric escalated ("they said our walls lean. Our walls do lean.

June's engineering leans on purpose..."). The first volley was maybe a recess away. The intervention, because fix-the-spills applies to wars; wars are spills that haven't happened: she walked out between the forts, and proposed, loudly, the treaty: "Two forts is dumb luck, you know what two forts makes? A castle, if there's a wall between.

Proposal: the wall. Both crews build it together, connecting. Snowballs stay legal, but at the targets..." She stomped a line of them into the drift. "not the builders. Castle with a shooting range. Everybody gets the war fun without the war cold-down-the-neck."

The vote: carried, both crews. The castle stood nine days, the biggest structure in playground memory, and the target range settled every dispute all month, formally, best of five throws.
