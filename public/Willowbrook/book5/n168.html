Night 168

Mrs. Hollis's Grading Math

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

Friday tea produced this month's heaviest number story. She'd asked about grading..." Forty years of putting numbers on kids. How do you number a kid? "Mrs. Hollis stirred her tea a long time. "Badly, dear.

That's the honest answer. I could number their spelling. Their sums. But the boy who fed the desk mouse better than himself, where's the score for that? The girl who read angry at ten and read everything by twelve, her third-grade numbers said 'failing.'

Failing. The numbers measured the piece that was easy to measure and everyone pretended it was the whole child. Forty years, and my one regret is every time I let a small number convince a child it was their size." The quiet.

Then: "The numbers were measuring the wrong jar... Yes. Exactly. The wrong jar. Then when I get to big school and they number me. I'll know. The number is measuring one jar. I got a whole shelf. You tell them Mrs. Hollis said so."
