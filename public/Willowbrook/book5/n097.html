Night 97

The True Apology Test

Month 4: True Stories

Preschool incident, reported at the window seat: a kid knocked June's tower, got marched over by the teacher, and delivered: "Sorry you got upset." And June, mild, bug-loving June, said: "That's not a sorry. That's a report." (The trio spent the afternoon on it.

The story submits their taxonomy in full:) "Sorry you're mad = your feelings are the problem. A report. Sorry it broke = the tower's the problem. A weather forecast. Sorry I knocked it = I'm in the sentence.

That's the only real one. The real sorry has a I did in it. And the gold one," Olivia added, from four years of family rule, "has a I did plus a next time. 'Sorry I knocked it, I'll walk wider.' The next-time is the proof you meant the sorry."
