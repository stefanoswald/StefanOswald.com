Night 189

The Supply List Ritual

Month 7: Big Enough

The supply run. (The letter's list, read so many times it's memorized. Lily's family and hers went together, "first-day supplies with your same-class person is tradition, we're deciding it's tradition, starting now.") the routine, aisle by aisle: Crayons, the 24 box ("the 64 is glory but the list says 24. The list is the rule.

The other 40 colors live at home, school colors and home colors, like school-me and home-me, same kid, different boxes"). Glue sticks, two. Safety scissors ("I've been ready for the real ones since the paper snowflakes, but fine"). The pencil box: loaded like a toolkit, each item test-fitted, the layout optimized twice.

And the unlisted purchase, negotiated at the moment: one small notebook. Pocket-sized. "Not on the list. But the list doesn't know me yet. The questions won't stop coming just because school started. Questions volume three: School edition."

Lily's unlisted item, for the story: a tiny sketchbook. ("The rolls won't draw demselves.") The two contraband notebooks were bagged together. The friendship approves of itself.
