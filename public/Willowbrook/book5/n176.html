Night 176

The Weight of Things

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

The kitchen scale came out for baking and never made it back to the cupboard. The weighing: everything portable, all afternoon, Theo ferrying specimens. The data: "The duchess feaver: so light the scale says zero. But it's one of the heaviest things in the treasure box, it's got the whole truce in it.

The red mitten: also zero-ish. Also enormous. Bobby bear: half a pound of bear, infinity of bear. A rock: heavy, means nothing. The key-shaped empty spot: unweighable. The scale doesn't even know it's there."

The finding, delivered to Mama with the scale still blinking zeros: "The scale only weighs the outside weight. The inside weight, the meaning weight, it can't see. They're different measurements and the scale only got trained on one. The feaver is light and heavy. Two scales. Everything important weighs more on the second scale."
