Night 197

The Marcus Shelf Briefing

Month 7: Big Enough

The promise from tea got kept this week: Marcus got the shelf briefing. Block corner, last preschool days, delivered like contraband intelligence: "Marcus. Listen. Big school is gonna put numbers on us. On everybody. And your letter numbers might come in low at first, the B and the D are still parking crooked sometimes, it's fine, the bed trick's working.

But here's the thing a forty-year teacher told me and now I'm telling you, and you have to keep it: the school numbers measure one jar. You got a shelf. The ramps that always work. The knowing-how-things-balance.

The patience with the little kids at the blocks. None of that gets a number. So when the letter-number comes in small, it's measuring one jar. The shelf is real. Mrs. Hollis said. Forty years of data."

Marcus built for a while, quiet. Then: "... What if the school never finds out about the shelf? Then we tell them. I'm loud enough for both of us and my hand's a veteran." A pause.

"Also your ramp jar? It's gonna be a school number someday. There's whole grown-up jobs that are just ramps. Mateo's papa builds bridges. A bridge is a ramp that graduated." (A bridge is a ramp that graduated.

Marcus repeated it twice. They had seen his far pages. Civil engineering. The sentence held.)
