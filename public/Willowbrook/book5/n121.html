Night 121

The First Chapter Book

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

The librarian struck again: a book, held at the desk, her name on it. Thicker than any yet. Chapters. And inside, she flipped, alarmed, almost no pictures. "There's been a mistake. The pictures are missing.

No mistake..." said the librarian. "Chapter books make you a deal: they bring the words, you bring the pictures. Your head does the illustrating now. Every reader draws their own." The first night's test, at the lamp, three chapters in, and she surfaced with the news, wide-eyed: "Mama.

I saw it. The farm in the book. I saw it. And here's the weird part: I built it out of grandpa's farm. The book farm has our red barn. My head used what it had.

Every reader's does. A million kids read that book, a million different barns." Notebook: "Chapter books: They bring words, you bring pictures. My book-farm is built from my real one. Every reader gets a different book. The same book. That's the deepest magic so far."
