Night 63

Mrs. Hollis's Friday

Month 3: How Does Harry Feel?

The Friday knock at Number Nine, standing appointment since the wrong mailbox. This week Mrs. Hollis showed her the classroom photographs. Forty years of them, one per year, on the stair wall. "Every face had a story.

I knew all of them, once. Do you miss it? The knowing-everybody?" The pause. (She heard it, this month has tuned her.) "Every day, dear. A teacher retires, but the caring doesn't. It just... loses its address."

The caring loses its address. She sat with that. Then: "... Mine's two streets over. My caring's address. And it comes here Fridays. So your caring could get a address too. The little kids at the library story hour never have enough grown-ups to read to them.

I checked." (Mrs. Hollis, the far pages report, read at the lantern every Tuesday for eleven years. The librarian calls her the best hire the wrong mailbox ever made.)
