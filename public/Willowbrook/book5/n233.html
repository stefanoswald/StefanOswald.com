Night 233

The First Frost Walk

Month 8: The Settling In

First frost hit the route this week, and the walk to school became, briefly, a museum tour. The reopened exhibits, greeted like returning friends: the puddle by Number Twelve, lidded in cellophane ice ("The museum's back. First window of the season, look, it caught a leaf mid-float again. Same museum, new collection").

The grass, sugared and crunching ("the undersound! The frost's the only weather you can hear with your feet"). The breath, visible ("Fee-oh's gonna love this at his corner, breath-dragons, tell him breath-dragons, he'll beep about it all day"). And the veteran's observation, delivered at the school gate, breath pluming: "Papa, this is my fifth frost season.

I know the whole lesson now, the museums, the ferns on my window coming, the icicle piano, the brook going under its lid but never stopping. Winter used to be new every year. Now it's... it's a friend coming back. I know its whole visit. The knowing doesn't make it smaller. The knowing makes it mine."
