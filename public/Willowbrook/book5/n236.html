Night 236

The Fireman Pole Yet

Month 8: The Settling In

The fireman pole campaign opened this week, and closed, honestly, the same day. The story wants this one on file because it's a postponement: The recon: the pole is a drop-and-grip, you commit in the air, then hold. Not like the bars. "The pole wants you to let go of everything at once and trust the grip you haven't made yet."

The test, from the low platform: grip strength, sufficient. The drop from the high platform: measured by eye, twice. And the answer, delivered to Mateo with no drama at all: "Not yet. The hands say yes but the... the commit muscle isn't grown yet.

The pole's a spring yet. February maybe. I'm not scared-quitting. I'm scheduling. There's a difference and I know it from the inside now." Mateo, fence-master of a hundred crashes: "Knowing the difference is the whole academy, honestly.

I got this scar, from not knowing it. The pole keeps. Poles keep like stairs."
