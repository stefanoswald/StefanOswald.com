Night 249

The Two-Best-Friends Question

Month 9: Friends Old and New

A playground philosopher posed the classic this week: "You can't have two best friends. Best means one. It's in the word." The question went, per rule, straight to the window seat: "Lily. Rosa-No-Relation is becoming... a big string.

The kid at four-square says best means one. Does the word have rules? Are you... do you ever feel the versus?" Lily, braiding, considered it with her whole eleven-days-younger wisdom: "The word's dumb.

Listen: you're my before-memory friend. Nobody else can ever be that, the spot's taken forever, it got taken before either of us could talk. Rosa-No-Relation can be your best school friend and Wren's your best bench friend and June's the best bug friend and I'm... the best best friend. Bests come in kinds.

The four-square kid thinks love is a pie, like more slices means smaller pieces. It's not a pie. What is it?... A candle thing. Like your mama's counting-candle story. You light more candles off yours, yours doesn't get smaller. The room just gets brighter."
