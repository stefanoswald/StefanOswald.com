Night 148

The Gloaming Read

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

She took the sequel up the hill this week, at the hour with the rescued name, and read a chapter to the willow. Out loud. The whole chapter. Why, asked the family, variously, after. The reasons, given at the lamp: "One: the willow watched me learn to read, it should get to hear some.

Two: Grandpa says Grandma read to him forty years and the willow's older than everybody, who ever read to it? Nobody, probably. Three hundred years of listening to the whole town and nobody ever read it a book." A pause.

"Three: it was gloaming. The book hour. Its hour. They matched." What the story can add, from the hill's side: the branches went very still for that chapter. The gold held its light a little past schedule.

And in the bark, if you'd looked close, the three words, home, family, growing, sat there like a listener leaning in. Trees keep their own counsel. But the fourth word was coming, that season. And the story has always wondered how much of it was decided during chapter eleven, read aloud, at the gold-blue hour, by the town's smallest librarian, to its oldest resident.
