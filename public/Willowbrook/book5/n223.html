Night 223

The Substitute Day

Month 8: The Settling In

Ms. Alvarez: out sick. The room, at 8:20: a substitute and nineteen five-year-olds sensing weakness like weather. The wobble: immediate. Two criers (routine-broken kids; the story knows the type; the routine is the floor for some kids and the sub had no map of the floorboards).

The deployment, as reconstructed from three witness accounts: she got up, hand raised first, permission asked, forms observed, and quietly became setup. "The schedule's on the wall, I can read it, carpet first, then centers. Biscuit Gravy gets fed at nine, half a scoop, he lies about hunger. The crying one by the cubbies is Mia, she needs the quiet corner and one crayon and no watching, I'll sit near.

The jar on the shelf, we should put this whole day in it. 'Maybe the sub day is happening for us.'" By ten, the room ran. The sub, at pickup, to Mama: "Your daughter... docked my ship. I've been doing this eleven years. Tell her thank you."
