Night 111

The Drawing She Didn't Copy

Month 4: True Stories

Art table trouble: a classmate said her sunflower drawing copied his. Loud. Public. "She took my idea." The old response: hot chest, tears, retreat. This month's response: Evidence. "I didn't copy. I can show the whole trail."

And she produced the notebook, the sketch pages: Scout in June, the row in July, the wind-day smear, fourteen sunflower studies dating back a season. "Mine come from the farm. Where's yours come from?... The cereal box..."

The classmate admitted. (His was also fine! Cereal boxes are a legitimate school!) Miss Dana turned it into the day's lesson: two kids drawing sunflowers isn't copying, it's two kids both looking at the same good world.

"Ideas aren't owned like toys. But your trail is yours, where you looked, what you saw, your particular sunflowers."
