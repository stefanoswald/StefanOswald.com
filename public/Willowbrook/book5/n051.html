Night 51

The Splinter of Blame

Month 2: Mistakes Are How

Preschool incident: the big paint jar, group table, down. Blue everywhere. And instantly, the blame circle: "Marcus bumped... June's elbow... I was nowhere..." And one voice, cutting through, with the family's oldest triage: "Stop. Wrong question.

The paint doesn't care who. The paint is reaching the crayon basket. Fix first, blame after, and I bet after, nobody even wants the blame turn." The scramble: rags, hands, the basket saved. The aftermath, breathing hard, blue to the elbows, and Miss Dana asking, curious: "Okay.

Now who spilled it?" Silence. Then Marcus: "... Kind of everybody. The table was too crowded. The table was the mistake..." said June. And that, the table, the way things worked, not the elbow, got fixed by lunchtime.

Two jars now. Two tables. (Miss Dana's file: "She'll run something someday. A school. A town. Possibly a country. Recommend: let her.")
