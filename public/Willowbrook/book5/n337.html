Night 337

The Fifth Line

Month 12: Almost Five

The doorframe. Shoes off. Heels flat. The book on the head. The pencil... The fifth line. ("Another hand? Another hand. The years keep paying a hand each. Reliable currency, years.") The frame, read bottom to top like the tree reads its rings: the baby line.

The wobbly two. The proud three. The fourth line with its whole lesson in the gap. And now five..." The school line. The line that walked itself to its own measuring. "Theo's turn: his third line, a solid thumb's gain, measured with equal ceremony ("the parade marches, both of us, no racing, all parade").

And his check of the frame, delivered gravely: "When I'm at your line, you'll be at a new line. The frame never lets anybody catch anybody. That's the frame's whole kindness, Fee-oh. Everybody climbs. Nobody catches. All parade."
