Night 202

The Last Free Wednesday

Month 7: Big Enough

The last free day. And she'd learned the lesson of the perfect-day plan, so this one got no plan. "The day gets a vote. The day always had the best ideas anyway." What the day voted for, as it unfolded: The office, one chapter, bucket-served, Marcus on branch three with comics.

The fort, rebuilt loose and fast, constitution recited from memory (whoever's cold, even in August; constitutions don't take summers off). The pond: ducks, saluted. ("Wish me luck. You started the whole talking thing. 'Duck' was word one.

Every word since is your fault.") The bakery at 4:55, looking hungry, by the ancient intelligence. (It works. It still works. Rosa's broken cookies found them, as prophesied.) The gloaming, caught from the fifth branch, gold pouring itself over the whole ready town.

And at the very end, the willow. Just to stand under. No questions this time. "No question?" the tree almost seemed to ask, gold moving. "Not today. Today I just came to say: watch me tomorrow.

Eight-fifteen. The route goes right past your hill. I'll be the one in the gold backpack, going somewhere new." A pause, hand on the bark, on home family growing. "You'll see me. You see everything. That's the whole comfort of you."
