Night 241

Halfway Through the Year

Month 8: The Settling In

The winter break began, and with it, the half-year check. September's fears, called to the stand one by one: "The bathroom: a nothing by week two. The lock got fixed because this town sends washers to school. Fear: Retired.

The lunch table: gave me Rosa-No-Relation, the horse drawings, and the not-digging friendship. Fear: Converted to asset. Will-anyone-like-me: answered by lunch, day one, beating the barber's Tuesday forecast. Retired with honors.

The wren separation: the mural bench held all sixty-some recesses. The wave code runs daily. Different rooms was geography, like I ruled. Fear: Disproven by engineering. The overwhelmed: came twice, the assembly day, the fire drill.

Named it, cornered it, breathed free. Weather, both times. Passed through, both times. Fear: Downgraded to weather." The remaining open item, entered honestly: "The not-fixing. Still hard. Maybe always hard, the conference said. But the bridge kids found their tower and the class book got nineteen wobbles and I sat on my hands with my whole heart up, mostly.

Growth-jar: Filling on schedule. Half a school year..." said Papa. "Answer? The chain said I was ready and the chain was right, but Mama's link was righter. I was always big enough. School didn't make me big. School's just... the first place that big got to live all day."
