Night 61

The Feet Study

Month 3: How Does Harry Feel?

Harry's method, field-tested: a bench, the playground, eyes low. Findings, notebook: "Skippy feet = happy or pretending. Draggy feet = tired or sad. Feet that stop and start = wants to join and can't ask. Feet pointed away from the group = already leaving, just slowly."

And one pair, spotted by the swings: stop-and-start feet. A boy she half-knew from the library. Wanting in, unable to ask. The intervention, Harry-grade: not "what's wrong". Just: "We need a fourth for the game.

You." The feet: skipped, all afternoon. "It worked..." She reported at the lamp. "I read the feet and the feet were true. What was his name?... I forgot to ask. Feet first, names after. That's tomorrow." (Sam. Library boy. Remember the name, he stays.)
