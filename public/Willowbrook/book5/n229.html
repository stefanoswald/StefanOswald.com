Night 229

The Halloween Costume Meeting

Month 8: The Settling In

Halloween approached, and with it the annual identity question: Who do you go as? The trio summit produced a group concept after real negotiation (June lobbied for "free bugs"; Lily for "bakery items"; the compromise, brokered by the usual broker, was better than both): The library. June: a bug book. Lily: a cookbook.

And Olivia: The librarian. Cardigan, spectacles, a stamp pad for "checking out" candy at each door, and the lantern, a real small one, battery, riding her shoulder on a strap. "Not a princess librarian or a scary librarian..." She briefed Mama at the sewing table.

"The real kind. The kind that knows the faces and keeps the right books in reach and stands with the shelf when the shelf needs standing with. It's not a costume, honestly. It's a forecast."

(It was a forecast. The story declines to spoil the far pages beyond noting: the lensless spectacles are in the treasure box, and they came back out, decades on, for a certain door on a certain first day of a certain job. Some costumes are rehearsals.)
