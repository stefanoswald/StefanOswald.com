Night 301

The Coach's Corner

Month 10: Losing Well

The park delivered this month's graduation exercise unrequested: a cluster of littles at the small slide, mid-meltdown over a race gone wrong, the classic pileup of first losses, three kids deep. The clinic, run on instinct (Theo assisting, he's staff now; the program employs its graduates): the kneel. The eye level. The lesson, sized down: "Hey.

Hey. The race went bad, huh. Okay, first: that hot feeling? It's a sneeze. It happens to you. You don't have to do what it says. Breathe three times with me, good. Second: the race doesn't know you.

Races got no memory. Nobody lost at being demselves, you just got to the tree second. Third: watch Fee-oh." (Theo, on cue, veteran of the whole lesson: "You go again. Or you do the bench game.

Or new game. But the last one gots to be a build.") "The last one's gotta be a build. Now: who wants again, who wants bench, who wants new?" (Two adens, one bench. The bench kid got commentary rights.

The clinic ran itself inside two minutes and the littles used the vocabulary all afternoon, "Sneeze!" one shouted at her own tears, mid-race, and kept running.)
