Night 320

Flowers for the Mothers

Month 11: The Kindness Detectives

For Mother's Day, Olivia's helper group filled small jars with dandelions before breakfast.

Papa drove while Olivia and Theo left them on the steps of Rosa's bakery, June's house, Rosa-No-Relation's home, Mrs. Hollis's porch, and Ms. Alvarez's mailbox.

"Teachers count," Olivia said. "Room Four needs mothering too."

At home, Mama received breakfast, a handmade card, and a list of quiet things the children had noticed: how she found missing mittens before anyone asked, heard worry beneath ordinary words, and knew when tea should appear.

Mama laughed and cried at the same time.

"You have been spying on me with love," she said.

"Only the good kind of spying," Theo assured her.

Across Willowbrook, small yellow jars waited on doorsteps. The flowers would last only a few days. Being noticed lasted much longer.
