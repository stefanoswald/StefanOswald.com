Night 257

The Noor Map Exchange

Month 9: Friends Old and New

The bench's two cartographers held their first formal exchange this week: maps, traded, keeper for keeper. Hers to Noor: the town map, third edition, the lane, the lantern, the willow, the routes, the dragons in the unchecked corners. Annotated for the newcomer: "The handles are marked. The lantern sees you from almost everywhere.

The bakery at 4:55, look hungry. The town takes about a year to hold you back. It's worth the year." Noor's to her: a map of two places at once, her old street, drawn from memory, every door labeled with who lived behind it; and beneath it, connected by a drawn sea full of the small honest waves: this town, this street, this bench.

"The two maps are one map," Noor explained. "You don't stop being from the first place. You just... grow the paper." (You just grow the paper. They remembered it with the year's best sentences, which is a crowded shelf, and it holds its place.)

"The waves in the middle," Olivia asked, careful, not-digging, "are they the dragon kind? The unknown kind? Some waves I know now..." said Noor. "Some I'm still crossing. The map is honest about which."
