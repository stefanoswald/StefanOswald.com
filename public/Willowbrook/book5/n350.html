Night 350

The Meadow at Five

Month 12: Almost Five

The meadow came, birthday-week faithful, gold as ever, and the bear was waiting on the path, upright, ear flopped, patchwork heart catching the light. The annual walk: the long edge. The dream flowers doing their extra colors. The small cow at the fence line (a regular now; Theo dreams loud these days; the two dream-country neighbors exchanged their usual courtesies, a wave, a bell-note).

And at the far end, at respectful distance: The far gate. Closed. Patient. The way books are closed. "The key..." She said, and held up her empty hand, which knows the shape. "Keeping warm?" asked the bear.

"Always. That's my whole job with it so far. That's the whole job till it isn't..." said the bear, comfortably. "Two more birthdays, give or take. Gates know their season. Yets have schedules. You taught the meadow that yourself."

He looked at her, the long bear look, the one that measures. "Five. You're getting big, and here's the annual report from the needing department: the needing changed shape again this year, and the meadow held, and the rumor stayed false. Growing up doesn't close the meadow.

Say it back. Growing up doesn't close the meadow. Correct. It never did. It never will. The meadow just gets... deeper in. The door moves down as the person builds up. You'll find it at every age, if you keep the key warm.

That's the whole secret of the whole place. Happy birthday, Almost. See you at the letter."
