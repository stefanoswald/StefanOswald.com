Night 256

Theo's First Friend Drama

Month 9: Friends Old and New

Sunday list time surfaced this week's freight: Theo's old Gus situation, reborn at Miss Dana's. Gus is there, in his class, and the "Milo's my best friend now" wound, a year old, reopened at the cubbies. The coaching, floor-level, from the sibling who's now survived a friend-fight of her own: "Fee-oh. The Gus thing.

Here's everything I know, and I learned some of it this month: One. Gus saying Milo didn't unsay you. Bests come in kinds; love's not a pie; a smart person told me. Two, you can't argue somebody into picking you.

Friendship's not a court. You just keep being the good colleague at the blocks and the picking sorts itself. Three, and this is the new one, if it stings, say the sting out loud to somebody safe. Me.

Mama. The list. Stings that stay inside compound. That's the interest rule. I paid two days of interest this month because I forgot my own rule... You forgot your own rule? Everybody forgets their own rules, Fee-oh.

That's why we write them down. The writing remembers when we don't. Now: the list. What's this week's brave?... I telled Gus the blocks are big enough for free builders." (The list took it. Entry eleven, his scribble-signature. The blocks were, in fact, big enough for three builders. They usually are.)
