Night 172

The Number the Willow Keeps

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

Joe came to town for errands and walked the hill with her, a rare pairing, the oldest farmer and the youngest counter, under the oldest resident. "Grandpa. The willow counts too, doesn't it. The rings.

Every year, one ring, inside, where nobody sees. Every year since before the town. Three hundred-some rings in there. Fat rings for the good years, thin ones for the hard, droughts, bad winters. The tree writes its whole diary in wood.

Never misses a year. Never fibs one either, you can't flatter a tree ring. So the willow's got... the same system as everybody. The doorframe lines. The odometer. The almanac. The brave list. Everything that lives keeps a count somewhere..."

She put her hand on the bark, over the three gold words. "but the willow keeps both kinds. The rings inside for the years. The words outside for the... for the meaning. Counting and telling.

It does both. Everything worth anything does both..." said Joe. "The almanac's got numbers in one column and 'Margaret said yes' in the other. You keep both books, darlin'. All your life. The counts and the words."
