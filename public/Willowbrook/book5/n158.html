Night 158

The Clock Conspiracy

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

The clock came under check this week and failed: "Sixty minutes? Sixty? Everything else counts in tens, money, fingers, the whole number system, and the clock does sixty and twelve like it didn't get the memo. Who built the clock math?"

Papa dug out the answer: ancient Babylonians, counting on finger-joints instead of fingers, base sixty, five thousand years back..." And it stuck. Sixty divides clean by two, three, four, five, six, tens can't do that. The old ones picked it for the sharing.

"The pause. The recalculation. "... Sixty is the baker's number. It splits fair more ways. The clock math is weird but it's weird for sharing. Half a hour, a third of a hour, a quarter, all clean.

Ten can't do a third without crumbs. Five thousand years and you checked your way to their exact reason at dinner. Somebody should check the old math sometimes. Mostly it holds up. The teens still don't."
