Night 164

The Recipe Doubling

Month 6: Numbers Are Everywhere

The block party needed double cookies, and the recipe became this month's field exam: "Two cups flour... Double is four. One cup sugar... two. Half cup butter... a whole cup. Mama, doubling makes halves whole, that's satisfying, two eggs... four eggs."

And then the pause at the salt. The long pause. The respect. "Quarter teaspoon salt. Double is... Half a teaspoon. I'm checking this one three times. Me and salt have history." (The cake of infamy stayed avenged.)

The deeper finding, mid-stir: "Doubling isn't adding, Mama. Adding would be: make it, then make it again, two whole jobs. Doubling is one job wearing a bigger coat. The math did the second batch's thinking for me.

Multiplication is... math that saves you work. Nobody said that. Everybody says it's hard. It's not hard, it's lazy, the good kind, the Petrov kind: measure once, cut once, but for thinking."
