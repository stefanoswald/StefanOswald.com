Night 185

The Backpack Fitting

Month 7: Big Enough

The backpack. (The letter's supply list called it "a school bag." The letter undersold the moment.) The selection, forty minutes, criteria rigorous: "Not the character ones, characters expire. I'll like something else by winter and then I'm carrying a stranger.

Solid gold-yellow: the willow's color. Doesn't expire. Two pockets minimum: one for the now stuff, one for the just-in-case." (The just-in-case pocket's planned list, recited: a bandage, a spare hair tie, one wrapped cracker "for whoever's day needs it..."

And the folded brave list. "The kit travels. The kit always travels.") The fitting: straps snugged by Mama..." High and tight, weight on the hips. "and then the wearing. Around the house. All evening. Empty.

To dinner. ("You break in the bag and the bag breaks in you. We're getting used to each other. By September we'll be one animal.") Theo, bereft, was issued the old red daypack as "the apprentice bag." He wore it to bed.
