Night 33

What Papa's Wrong Notes Taught

Month 2: Mistakes Are How

After the salt cake, Papa took her to the piano, the scene of the family's oldest mistakes. "You know why I never fixed my wrong notes? Truth? When I was small, I hit one in a recital and froze.

Cried on stage. And my papa said: 'The note's not wrong, it's yours. Play it again on purpose and it becomes style.'So you played it again on purpose... For thirty years. And here's what the wrong notes taught, better than the right ones ever could: the song keeps going.

Nothing stops. You can't wreck a song you keep playing, you can only make it more yours." She thought about the recital, the loud wrong note, the family-thing announcement, mid-song, age four. "... I already knew this.

You put it in me before you said it. That's the other thing about wrong notes, kiddo. They're hereditary."
