Night 274

The Losing Practice Program, Reopened

Month 10: Losing Well

This month announced its lesson at game night, because the story keeps its promises: "losing needs practice like everything needs practice, and I'm behind on it." (Her own filing, the board-flip night, a year ago. The program limped along since. This month: Full training.)

The opening assessment, honestly done: one game of Ladders, lost on the last roll. The data: no flip. But: the hot chest, the jaw, the twenty silent seconds, the "I don't even like this game" (a lie; she loves this game; losing makes liars of everyone briefly). "There it is..."

She said, catching herself mid-lie. "That's the opponent. Not you, Papa. The hot thing. The hot thing shows up every time the dice go wrong and it wants my mouth and my face and the whole evening.

So the program trains...? Not the winning. The hot thing. Losing well isn't about the game at all. The game's just... the gym." Notebook: "Month 10: Training the hot thing. The game is the gym. The hot thing makes liars of everybody briefly ('I don't even like this game'). Catching the lie is rep one. Caught it. Program: Open."
