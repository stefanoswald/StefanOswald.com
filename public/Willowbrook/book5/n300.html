Night 300

Night Three Hundred: The Standings That Matter

Month 10: Losing Well

Night three hundred, the round-number rite, fifth annual, themed by its birthday girl to this month: The true standings. Each family member's year, ranked not by scores but by growth-per-column: "Fee-oh: Most improved, all categories. Reading his name to reading sentences. Losing with tears to losing with handshakes.

The trap at checkers. The sorry-flowers gone industrial. The apprentice is closing every gap on schedule and the program is proud. Mama: The true-voice championship. The fine-voice got fixed at a dinner a year ago and never came back.

Every fog got named. The hardest column and she runs it clean. Papa: The wrong-note dynasty, defended. Thirty-one years of beloved mistakes and counting. Plus the whistle on the heavy nights, the faiffulest stat in the house.

He waves every day, basically. And me. Most games lost. Most lessons extracted. First sportsmanship award. First award that was really for the losses. The year I learned that the hot thing is a sneeze, the bench is a empire, the holes are exhibits, and the losses column is the faculty.

Standing: First place in the only league this family runs, the getting-better league. Where everybody's first place. Which is the whole point of the league. Goodnight."
