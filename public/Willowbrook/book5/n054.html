Night 54

The Unfixable Vase

Month 2: Mistakes Are How

Truth page. Not everything mends. The vase, the blue one, the shelf one, grandma's, met her elbow and the floor. Pieces. Many. Small. Petrov himself, consulted, shook his head slow: "Some breaks are past the trade.

I'm sorry." The grief: real, and doubled, the vase, and the being-the-one. Mama's grief: also real, also shown ("hiding it would be lying, and we don't"). A hard, honest week. And then, Saturday: Mama with the pieces, a bag of mortar, a stepping stone mold.

"Grandma had a garden. Her vase is going in ours. Not fixed. Changed. There's a difference, and it took me forty years to learn it: some broken things don't go back. They go forward." The stone: set by the crocuses. Blue pieces catching morning light.
