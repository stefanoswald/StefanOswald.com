Night 333

The Kindness Month, Counted

Month 11: The Kindness Detectives

Month's end. At month's end, The month's visible changes were nineteen cases (seventeen closed, one blank-on-purpose, one open forever). Eleven buried rivers, mapped. One boots man. One weather-adjustment tally book. One promise wearing feathers.

One snack that was a ticket. One shy kindness. One dark file. One somebody wall. Three operations. Five agents sworn. One wall deposit. One conspiracy. What mattered most could not be totaled: that anonymous costs double and warms double.

That the job is the disguise and the uniform hides the agent. That every smooth-running thing has a quiet somebody underneath. That you become what you hunt, and everybody's building a case about the world, so check which one. That the town is better than its own accounting.

Every town is. That's the finding under all the findings, and she'll check it in every town she ever lives in, and it will hold in every one. "Good detective-month? The seeingest month. And Mama, the agency's real product was never the saves.

It was the eyes. Nineteen cases and what I actually built was a way of looking I can't take off. The town's been this good the whole time. Five years I lived here without the eyes.

Now I got them. That's the whole birthday present, four days early: The eyes. Everything else is cake." Remembered. The eyes: permanent. The looking: lifelong. Four days to five.
