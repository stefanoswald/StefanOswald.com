Night 286

The Garden Loss Tally book

Month 10: Losing Well

A late frost took the early seedlings this week, the eager rows, planted at the first warm lie of March. The question row survived; the early tomatoes: lost. Twelve plants, black-edged, done. The farm's losing lesson, delivered over the casualty rows: "Forty years, darlin', and I've lost crops to frost, hail, drought, deer, and one memorable pig.

Want the farmer's whole losing philosophy? Three parts: Mourn quick..." He was already pulling the dead plants, even-handed, unhurried. "Replant quicker..." The seed flat was already out. "and read the loss. Every loss is the land telling you something.

This one says: March lies. I knew March lies. I got eager. The frost didn't beat me. My eager beat me, and the frost just collected. So the loss tally book has two columns too.

What took it, and what offered it. Now that's forty years in one sentence. Most losses, something offered. Not all. But most. Find your offer, log it, replant."
