Night 138

The Letter to the Author

Month 5: The Deep Shelf

The idea arrived mid-week, urgent: "The author of the dog book. Is she alive? Authors can be alive, right? Then she has a mailbox." The letter, drafted, redrafted, final text: "Dear ms. Halloran. I am 4.

Your book borrowed my real heart for your pretend dog and I cried and I'm glad. My hamster class pet died last year. Your chapter 9 knew exactly. My Papa says authors write their own underneaths honest and they fit everybody.

Yours fit me. I am a author too. I am stealing your cliffhanger trick. This letter is a thank you and a warning. Love, Olivia." Mailed care of the publisher (the librarian supplied the address and the phrase "care of..."

Which delighted her. "the mail has a carrying service for people you can't find? The mail thinks of everything"). Kissed, per postal custom. Sent. Long odds, long wait, saved under maybe. (the story permits itself one spoiler, because the story cried too: an envelope comes in Month 9. Real handwriting. The far pages hold what it says.)
