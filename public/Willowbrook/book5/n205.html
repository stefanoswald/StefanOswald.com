Night 205

The Second Day Truth

Month 7: Big Enough

Nobody warns you about day two. (the story is warning you now. That's what this page is for.) Day one runs on lightning, the whole town waving, the newness carrying you like a bucket on a rope. Day two: the lightning's gone.

Just the alarm, the buttons, the route, and the discovery that school happens every day, whole days of it, forever, and the cubby is still exciting but also just a box, and the carpet sits harder the second time, and by pickup she was quiet, and by dinner quieter, and at the lamp it came out: "Day two was heavy, Mama. Not bad. Heavy. Everything that was shiny yesterday was just...

Regular today. And regular for a whole day is more tired than shiny for a whole day. Nobody told me. The chain didn't have a link for this. Because it can't be prepared. Only weathered.

Here's the truth, love: day one is the visit. Day two is the moving in. Moving in is always heavier, you're not seeing the house anymore, you're living in it. And by day ten, the regular becomes the comfortable, and the comfortable is worth ten shiny...

Regular becomes comfortable. Like the backpack. Stiff, then broke-in, then one animal. School is a backpack. Give it this month."
