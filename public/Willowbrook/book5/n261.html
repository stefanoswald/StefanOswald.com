Night 261

The Biscuit Gravy Turn

Month 9: Friends Old and New

The rotation reached her name: Biscuit gravy, weekend guest. (The cage, ceremonially transported. The care sheet, memorized before the bus ride home. The Buttons grief, two years settled, standing quietly in the doorway of the whole thing, noted, honored, not in charge.)

The hosting, done with care: the cage on the kitchen table's window end ("the gold hour is everybody's"). The half-scoop feedings, exact ("he lies about hunger; the sheet and I both know"). The floor time in the sealed hallway, Theo stationed at one end as "the goalie..." A job he performed with the intensity of a knight.

And the Sunday-night entry, at the lamp, with the small guest wheel-running in the dark: "Mama, Buttons taught me the grief. But hosting Biscuit Gravy taught me the other end: you take care of small things knowing. Knowing they're temporary. Knowing it costs later.

And you do the care anyway, the gold window, the exact scoops. Because it's temporary. The temporary is why the honors matter. Anybody can honor a forever thing."
