Night 2

Why Does the Moon Follow the Car?

Month 1: The Question Collector

Question one got its investigation: a night drive, on purpose. "Watch..." She commanded. "The trees go backwards. The houses go backwards. The moon just... Comes." Papa gave it to her straight: "The moon is so far away that moving the car barely changes where it looks.

Near things race past. Far things keep you company. The farther something is, the longer it stays with you." Silence. Then, in the notebook, by streetlight: "Answer: The moon is too far to loose. Near things race past.

Far things stay." And underneath, her first-ever follow-up question, the collector learning her real trade: "Is that true about people too?" (The notebook holds that one unanswered for years. The far pages answer it: yes.)
