Night 92

The Broken Truck Truth

Month 4: True Stories

Theo's dump truck. Her foot. The dark hallway. Crunch. Nobody saw. Nobody heard. The classic setup, the truck could have "just been found broken." Trucks break. Three-year-olds forget. The lie sat there, pre-built, free to move in.

She woke him up. "Fee-oh. I stepped on the truck. In the dark. It's broke and it was me and I'm telling you before you find it, because finding it is worse than hearing it."

Theo, sleep-blinking, absorbing: "... You broked it? I broked it. Accident, but mine. We'll fix it or Petrov will or my allowance will... Okay. You told me." He rolled back over, then, from mostly-asleep: "You the good one, Wivy." The good one. The truck cost two weeks' allowance at Petrov's.
