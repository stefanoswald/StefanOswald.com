Night 239

The December Dark Reading

Month 8: The Settling In

December pressed its early dark against the windows, and she answered with the old remedy, upgraded: The reading fort, winter configuration. (Blankets doubled. The good flashlight. The bear in the listener's chair. The chapter book, number four of the year, and the ten-minute loophole stretched, on dark-month grounds, to fifteen.

"Seasonal adjustment. The nights are longer; the reading should get its share of the surplus.") The winter fort's discovery, logged mid-month: "The dark outside makes the light inside better. Summer reading is good but it competes with the whole gold evening.

Winter reading has no competition, the dark just... frames it. The flashlight makes a little gold country with exactly two citizens, me and the book. Three citizens." (The bear. Always counted. Needed different, counted same.)

"December's the best reading month..." She concluded. "and that's not despite the dark. That's the dark's gift. Every season hands you something. Winter hands you the frame."
