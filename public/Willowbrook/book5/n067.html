Night 67

Papa's Long Day

Month 3: How Does Harry Feel?

Papa came home with heavy feet this week. (She read them from the window. The whistle came, but late, and one note short.) The briefing, whispered to Theo before the door: "Papa's carrying a big day.

Soft landing rule." done: slippers, pre-fetched. The loud game, self-postponed. Theo's drawing delivered without the usual full-volume presentation, just left by his plate. Dinner: gentle. Nobody asked the day to explain itself.

And at the lamp, Papa came up, sat on her bed, and said: "You two ran the soft landing tonight. I felt it. Who called it? The window. Your feet said." He was quiet a moment.

"You know what it's like, walking home with a day like that and getting read before the door? It's half the fix. Being known is half the fix of everything."
