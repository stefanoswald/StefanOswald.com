Night 221

The Show and Tell Decision

Month 8: The Settling In

First show-and-tell: Friday. The big think: Wednesday through Thursday, agonized, the treasure box emptied twice. The candidates: the Duchess feather (strong story, "but you have to know the Duchess"). The author letter ("too big.

Some treasures are for the box, not the carpet, showing it might spend it"). The bean-jar victory ("braggy without the math, and the carpet can't check the math"). The red mitten ("Fort together needs three tellers minimum; the constitution's a group text"). The choice, defended at dinner: The wrong-note story.

No object at all. "Just me, telling the carpet about Papa's thirty years of wrong notes, and the recital where I hit mine loud and made it a family thing. Show and tell doesn't say the show has to be a thing. The best thing I own is a story.

I'm showing that." The result, per Friday's debrief: the carpet's first standing request for a repeat performance in reading-corner history. And two kids came up after to confess their own wrong notes, privately, like joining a club.
