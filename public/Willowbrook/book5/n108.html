Night 108

The Museum Label Problem

Month 4: True Stories

The trio runs a museum now. Exhibits, labeled by the resident writer: The duchess feaver. Scout's first leaf. A rock that looks like a duck if you believe. And one label with a problem: "Real arrowhead.

Probably a thousand years old." (It's a chipped rock. June found it. It looks arrowheady, in the right light, to the committed. "Probably" was doing enormous work on that label.) The visiting kid asked: "How do you know it's a arrowhead?"

And the curator stood at the crossroads all museums stand at, and chose: "... We don't. New label." She rewrote it live: "Rock that might be a arrowhead. Found by june. Even the library book couldn't say.

The mystery is the exhibit." And the strange result: the mystery label drew more visitors. Kids argued over it for weeks. The maybe outperformed the fake certain.
